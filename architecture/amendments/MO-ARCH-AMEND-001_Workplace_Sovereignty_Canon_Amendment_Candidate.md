# MO-ARCH-AMEND-001 — Workplace Sovereignty Canon Amendment Candidate

## Status

```text
Proposed for Owner Acceptance
Architecture Canon Amendment Candidate
```

## Purpose

This amendment reconciles the accepted MarkOrbit Orbital Architecture Canon with `MO-ADR-WSP-001`.

It corrects the constitutional meaning of Workplace, Product Installation, Product Projection, Lite, Sites and MGSN without changing the frozen Book 02 baseline or authorizing implementation.

## Amendment principle

```text
Workplace business sovereignty
+
Core semantic authority
+
Owning Service formal-state authority
+
platform-managed MGSN governance
```

These responsibilities are complementary. None absorbs the others.

## A1 — Canonical statement amendment

The Canon must preserve independent organizational orbits while clarifying that MarkOrbit may operate shared capabilities and a platform-managed service network without taking ownership of Partner business records.

Proposed canonical statement:

> MarkOrbit is the operating system for global brand professional services. Every professional-service Organization operates through one or more independent Workplaces. Each Workplace is the Organization-scoped boundary for business sovereignty, accountable operation, permissions, Product Installation and authorized projection. Core defines shared semantics, rules, capabilities, security and execution contracts; approved Owning Services control formal business-state mutation. MarkOrbit does not acquire Partner customers, Matters, internal Knowledge or commercial relationships merely by hosting data or supplying capabilities. MGSN remains a platform-owned and platform-governed global service network through which authorized Workplaces project service needs or supply capabilities under controlled routing, procurement, funds, fulfillment and Return rules.

## A2 — Workplace definition replacement

Replace the current lightweight Workplace lock with:

> Workplace is the Organization-scoped business-sovereignty, accountability, permission, Product-operation and projection boundary of the MarkOrbit ecosystem.

A Workplace carries or governs:

- Organization identity and scoped membership;
- users, roles and permissions;
- customers, contacts and relationship context;
- business records and accountable operating context;
- private Knowledge, rules, pricing, preferences and experience;
- Product Installations and entitlements;
- Product-specific configuration and data access;
- authorized external projections;
- audit, portability and withdrawal context.

```text
Workplace business sovereignty
≠ unrestricted formal-state mutation authority
```

Formal business facts remain controlled by the relevant Core-defined semantics, Owning Service and Execution rules.

## A3 — Five authority dimensions

The Canon must distinguish:

```text
Business sovereignty
Semantic authority
Formal-state authority
Physical custody
Legal / source authority
```

- Workplace or Organization normally holds business sovereignty for its customer relationships and operating context.
- Core and accepted Specifications hold semantic authority.
- Owning Services hold formal-state mutation authority.
- Platform or authorized infrastructure may hold physical custody.
- legal, privacy, evidentiary and source authority remains with the applicable customer, Organization, professional, Provider, regulator or official source.

```text
physical custody ≠ business ownership
schema authority ≠ record ownership
business sovereignty ≠ unrestricted mutation authority
```

## A4 — Product Installation

Add the following canonical relationship:

```text
Organization
→ Workplace
→ Product Installation
→ configured capability and projection
```

A Product Installation is the Workplace-scoped activation of a Product, including status, entitlement, configuration version, enabled capabilities, data-access scope, projection scope and audit context.

Product Installation does not imply separate code deployment or separate physical database.

## A5 — Workplace and Product Plane replacement

Proposed canonical plane:

```text
Organization
└── Workplace / Independent Orbit
    ├── MarkReg Installation
    ├── Lite Installation
    ├── Sites Installation
    ├── MGSN Connection / Gateway
    ├── organization-specific applications
    └── future Product Installations
```

MGSN itself is not installed into or owned by a Workplace. A Workplace installs or enables an MGSN Connection or Gateway.

## A6 — MarkReg clarification

MarkReg is the flagship trademark lifecycle Product operating through a Workplace-scoped Product Installation.

```text
Partner Organization
→ Partner Workplace
→ MarkReg Installation
```

MarkReg self-operated business must run through a distinct self-operated Organization and Workplace. Platform administration does not grant default access to Partner Workplace records.

## A7 — Lite clarification

Lite remains:

```text
a lightweight Workplace Product
for independent professionals and small teams
```

Lite may expose client-facing Intake, document, decision, payment and status projections, but Lite is not reduced to a client portal.

```text
Lite
├── professional operating experience
└── optional client-facing projection
```

## A8 — Sites clarification

Sites is the Workplace public-brand and content projection Product.

Sites may own Site content, themes, pages and publish versions within the Workplace context. Sites does not become the authoritative Customer, Order, Matter, Payment or Professional Decision service.

## A9 — MGSN definition replacement

Replace the current private-network-first definition with:

> MGSN is the platform-owned and platform-governed managed global professional-service network through which authorized Workplaces request, offer, procure, route, fund, fulfill and return professional service capability.

Canonical topology:

```text
Originating Workplace
→ authorized demand projection
→ MGSN platform network
→ controlled provider allocation / instruction
→ Execution Provider Workplace
→ governed Return
→ Originating Workplace
```

Workplace-owned or controlled MGSN context includes:

- Connection configuration;
- authorized demand and supply projections;
- private preferences and relationship history;
- external self-managed route context;
- returned results and Evidence.

Platform-owned or governed MGSN context includes:

- provider admission and supply portfolio;
- Capability Profile and verification;
- procurement terms;
- matching and routing;
- funds and settlement controls;
- fulfillment, correction and replacement;
- platform Trust and network operating data;
- controlled network interfaces.

```text
MGSN Connection ≠ MGSN Network
Workplace relationship history ≠ ownership of platform provider supply
managed collaboration ≠ participant-to-participant network edge
```

## A10 — Network and Ecosystem Plane replacement

Proposed network plane:

```text
Workplace Capability Need or Supply Projection
→ MGSN admission and eligibility
→ platform-managed supply portfolio
→ matching and bounded recommendation
→ accountable confirmation and Provider Acceptance
→ governed collaboration and fulfillment
→ typed Return
→ private and platform Trust learning
```

External self-managed provider routes may continue outside MGSN, but they do not receive MGSN procurement, funds, fulfillment or replacement guarantees and require manual Evidence return.

## A11 — Cross-Workplace collaboration

Cross-Workplace collaboration must use purpose-limited projection, Handoff and Return contracts.

```text
sender permission ≠ receiver permission
projection ≠ ownership transfer
provider access ≠ customer-relationship ownership
```

The receiving Workplace gains only the scoped context required to perform its accepted responsibility.

## A12 — Portability

A Workplace must be able to export its own customers, business records, files, Site content, Lite configuration, private Knowledge, Product configuration and its own MGSN relationship/projection history, subject to legal, contractual and third-party rights.

Portability does not include export of:

- the full MGSN provider pool;
- confidential platform procurement terms;
- platform routing weights;
- other participants' private Trust or business data;
- shared Core assets not licensed for export.

## Proposed new decisions

### ARC-029 — Workplace is the business-sovereignty and accountability boundary.

### ARC-030 — Canonical semantics, business sovereignty and formal-state authority are distinct.

### ARC-031 — Products operate through Workplace-scoped Product Installations.

### ARC-032 — MarkReg self-operated business is an isolated Workplace.

### ARC-033 — Lite is a lightweight Workplace Product and may expose client projections.

### ARC-034 — Sites is a Workplace public projection Product, not a business Owning Service.

### ARC-035 — MGSN Connection is Workplace-scoped; MGSN Network is platform-owned and governed.

### ARC-036 — Managed network collaboration does not create an unrestricted peer-to-peer network edge.

### ARC-037 — Workplace portability excludes platform-owned network supply and confidential shared infrastructure.

## Superseded interpretations

On Owner acceptance, the following interpretations are superseded:

- Workplace as merely a platform shell or employee work surface;
- `Core object definition` as proof that Core owns every concrete record;
- Lite as only a client portal;
- MGSN as a network owned separately by each Workplace;
- `Private Network first` as a constitutional requirement that users own a private provider network inside MGSN;
- cross-Workplace Handoff as automatic transfer of customer relationship or business ownership.

## Gate effect

Owner merge of this amendment accepts the amended Canon direction and authorizes:

- updating the Architecture Canon and Decision Register to active accepted wording;
- Book 04 next-version correction work;
- Book 07 Charter reconciliation and renewed review;
- implementation ADR planning consistent with the amendment.

It does not authorize:

- Book 02 semantic mutation without its Change Proposal process;
- rewriting frozen RC1 publications in place;
- database or API implementation;
- payment custody;
- autonomous provider appointment;
- production deployment;
- External Protected Action.
