# MAS-0000 — Specifications Charter

**Document ID:** MAS-0000

**Version:** 1.0.0

**Status:** Canonical

**Category:** Specifications Constitution

**Authority:** Governance Authority

---

# Purpose

This Charter establishes the constitutional foundation of the MarkOrbit Architecture Specifications.

The Architecture Canon defines architectural truth.

The Governance Framework governs architectural evolution.

The Architecture Specifications define how architectural knowledge is represented, organized and communicated.

Together they establish the normative knowledge system of the MarkOrbit Architecture Library.

---

# Scope

This Charter governs every specification published within the Architecture Specifications package.

The package includes standards governing:

- specification principles;
- specification architecture;
- specification language;
- specification dependencies;
- specification composition.

This Charter does not define:

- architectural concepts;
- governance policies;
- implementation technologies;
- operational procedures;
- product behavior.

These concerns belong to other architectural packages.

---

# Mission

The mission of the Architecture Specifications is to establish a consistent, reusable and durable representation system for architectural knowledge.

The Specifications shall:

- represent canonical architecture;
- elaborate canonical concepts;
- organize architectural knowledge;
- preserve architectural consistency;
- enable implementation without redefining architecture.

Specifications shall never replace the Architecture Canon.

---

# Principles

## Principle 1

Specifications represent architecture.

They do not define architecture.

---

## Principle 2

Every specification derives its architectural meaning from the Architecture Canon.

---

## Principle 3

Every specification shall comply with the Governance Framework.

---

## Principle 4

Architectural representation shall maximize clarity, consistency and reuse.

---

## Principle 5

Specifications shall remain independent of implementation technologies.

```text

↓
```

---

# Boundaries

The Architecture Canon defines architectural meaning.

The Governance Framework governs architectural evolution.

The Architecture Specifications represent architectural knowledge.

Frameworks organize architectural practice.

Products implement architectural capabilities.

Implementations realize products.

These responsibilities shall remain independent.

---

# Relationship to the Architecture Canon

Architectural meaning originates exclusively from the Architecture Canon.

Specifications elaborate canonical concepts.

Specifications shall not redefine canonical concepts.

---

# Relationship to Governance

Specification creation, revision and publication are governed by the Governance Framework.

The Governance Framework governs specifications.

Specifications do not govern themselves.

---

# Relationship to Frameworks

Frameworks organize specifications into reusable operational practices.

Frameworks consume specifications.

They shall not redefine them.

---

# Relationship to Products

Products implement architectural capabilities by applying architectural specifications.

Products shall remain downstream of the Specifications package.

---

# Conformance

A specification conforms to this Charter only if it:

- derives architectural meaning from the Architecture Canon;
- complies with the Governance Framework;
- preserves architectural consistency;
- follows the Architecture Specifications.

---

# Authority

MAS-0000 is the constitutional document of the Architecture Specifications.

All MAS standards derive their authority from this Charter.

In the event of inconsistency, this Charter shall prevail.

---

# Revision Policy

This Charter is expected to remain highly stable.

Changes shall occur only through constitutional revision approved by the Governance Authority.

Editorial improvements shall not modify specification semantics.
