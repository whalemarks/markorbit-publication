# Purpose

This document establishes the First Principles governing architectural specifications within the MarkOrbit Architecture Library.

Architectural specifications are the primary representation of architectural knowledge.

These principles ensure that architectural knowledge remains accurate, consistent, reusable and durable throughout its lifecycle.

Every specification shall conform to the principles established by this document.

---

# Scope

This standard applies to every architectural specification published within the Architecture Specifications package.

It governs:

- architectural representation;
- specification semantics;
- specification organization;
- architectural consistency.

It does not govern:

- architectural concepts;
- governance procedures;
- implementation technologies;
- editorial conventions.

---

# Mission

The mission of the Specification Principles is to preserve the integrity of architectural representation.

Specifications shall faithfully represent architecture without redefining it.

---

# Principles

## Principle 1

Specifications represent architecture.

They shall never redefine architecture.

Architectural meaning originates exclusively from the Architecture Canon.

---

## Principle 2

Every specification shall preserve semantic integrity.

Architectural representation shall accurately express canonical meaning.

---

## Principle 3

Every specification shall possess one primary responsibility.

Architectural responsibilities shall remain independent.

---

## Principle 4

Specifications shall maximize consistency.

Equivalent architectural knowledge shall always be represented consistently.

---

## Principle 5

Specifications shall maximize clarity.

Architectural ambiguity shall be eliminated whenever reasonably possible.

---

## Principle 6

Specifications shall maximize reuse.

Reusable architectural knowledge is preferred over duplicated descriptions.

---

## Principle 7

Specifications shall preserve traceability.

Architectural knowledge shall remain traceable to:

- the Architecture Canon;
- the Governance Framework;
- related specifications.

---

## Principle 8

Specifications shall remain implementation-independent.

Implementation realizes architecture.

Specifications represent architecture.

These responsibilities shall remain distinct.

---

## Principle 9

Specifications shall evolve through governance.

Implementation experience may inform architectural evolution.

Governance determines architectural evolution.

---

## Principle 10

Specifications shall remain durable.

Architectural representation shall outlive individual technologies, products and implementation approaches.

---

# Canonical Representation Model

Architectural representation follows the canonical progression.

```text
Architecture

↓

Representation

↓

Communication

↓

Implementation
```

Architecture defines meaning.

Specifications represent meaning.

Implementations realize meaning.

---

# Representation Characteristics

Every specification shall be:

- semantically accurate;
- internally consistent;
- explicitly traceable;
- reusable;
- reviewable;
- maintainable.

These characteristics are normative.

# Conformance

A specification conforms to this standard only if it:

- preserves canonical architectural meaning;
- satisfies these Specification Principles;
- maintains architectural consistency;
- remains governance compliant.
