# Purpose

This document defines the canonical composition model for architectural specifications within the MarkOrbit Architecture Library.

Composition governs how architectural knowledge is assembled into coherent, reusable and maintainable representations.

Composition defines relationships among architectural representations.

It does not define their internal architecture.

---

# Scope

This standard governs:

- representation composition;
- compositional relationships;
- modular composition;
- specification aggregation.

It does not govern:

- document structure;
- representation language;
- implementation architecture;
- publishing templates.

---

# Mission

The mission of Specification Composition is to establish reusable composition rules for architectural representations.

Architectural knowledge shall grow through composition rather than duplication.

---

# Composition Principles

## Principle 1

Architectural specifications shall be composed from reusable representations.

---

## Principle 2

Composition shall preserve semantic integrity.

Combining representations shall never alter canonical meaning.

---

## Principle 3

Composition shall maximize modularity.

Representations shall remain independently understandable whenever practical.

---

## Principle 4

Composition shall encourage reuse before replication.

Architectural knowledge shall be shared rather than duplicated.

---

## Principle 5

Composition shall preserve architectural consistency across the Architecture Library.

---

# Canonical Composition Model

Architectural representations are composed using a small set of canonical composition operations.

```text
Aggregation

↓

Composition

↓

Specialization

↓

Extension

↓

Reuse
```

These operations govern how architectural knowledge is assembled.

---

# Composition Operations

Every compositional relationship belongs to one canonical operation.

| Operation | Responsibility |
|-----------|----------------|
| Aggregation | Combine related representations |
| Composition | Form a coherent architectural whole |
| Specialization | Refine an existing representation |
| Extension | Add new architectural capability |
| Reuse | Share architectural knowledge without duplication |

Each operation possesses one primary architectural purpose.

---

# Composition Semantics

Composition preserves architectural authority.

Composition does not redefine architectural meaning.

Architectural meaning continues to originate from the Architecture Canon.

Composition organizes architectural knowledge into reusable structures.

---

# Composition Characteristics

Architectural composition shall be:

- modular;
- reusable;
- semantically consistent;
- implementation-independent;
- maintainable.

These characteristics are normative.

---

# Conformance

An architectural specification conforms to this standard only if it:

- follows the canonical composition model;
- preserves semantic integrity;
- maintains modularity;
- avoids duplicated architectural knowledge.

MAS-0005 is the authoritative definition of Specification Composition within the MarkOrbit Architecture Library.

All architectural specifications shall conform to this composition model.
