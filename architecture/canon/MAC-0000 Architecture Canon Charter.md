# Purpose

This Charter establishes the constitutional foundation of the MarkOrbit Architecture Canon.

The Architecture Canon defines the permanent architectural structure of the MarkOrbit Architecture Library.

It provides the normative foundation upon which all architectural specifications, frameworks, products and implementations are built.

The Canon exists to preserve architectural consistency across generations of technology, products and organizations.

---

# Scope

This Charter governs every document published within the Architecture Canon.

The Architecture Canon includes:

- Architecture Principles
- Architecture Layers
- Responsibility Matrix
- Canonical Language
- Canonical Models
- Architecture Relationships

This Charter does not define governance procedures, implementation technologies, business processes or product functionality.

Those subjects belong to other architectural packages.

---

# Canon Mission

The mission of the Architecture Canon is to establish a permanent, technology-independent and implementation-independent architectural foundation.

The Canon shall:

- define architectural principles;
- establish architectural structure;
- allocate architectural responsibilities;
- define canonical terminology;
- define canonical conceptual models;
- define canonical architectural relationships.

---

# Canon Principles

## Principle 1

The Architecture Canon defines architecture.

It does not define implementation.

---

## Principle 2

The Architecture Canon shall remain technology-independent.

Technologies evolve.

Architecture endures.

---

## Principle 3

The Architecture Canon shall remain product-independent.

Products implement architecture.

Products shall not redefine architecture.

---

## Principle 4

Architectural meaning shall remain stable.

Future publications shall extend canonical knowledge rather than reinterpret it.

---

## Principle 5

Every canonical definition shall possess one authoritative source.

Competing canonical definitions are prohibited.

The Architecture Canon defines the following classes of architectural objects.

- Principles
- Layers
- Responsibilities
- Terminology
- Models
- Relationships

These objects constitute the permanent conceptual foundation of the Architecture Library.

---

# Canon Boundaries

The Architecture Canon defines architectural meaning.

Governance governs architectural evolution.

Specifications elaborate architectural definitions.

Frameworks organize architectural practice.

Products implement architectural capabilities.

Implementations realize products.

These responsibilities shall remain independent.

---

# Relationship to Governance

The Architecture Canon and the Governance Framework are complementary.

The Architecture Canon defines architectural structure.

The Governance Framework defines how architectural structure evolves.

Neither package shall redefine the other.

---

# Relationship to Specifications

Architecture Specifications extend the Architecture Canon.

Specifications may elaborate canonical concepts.

They shall not redefine canonical concepts.

---

# Relationship to Frameworks

Frameworks organize reusable architectural practice.

Frameworks shall remain consistent with the Architecture Canon.

---

# Relationship to Products

Products implement architectural capabilities.

Products shall not become part of the Architecture Canon.

---

# Conformance

An architectural artifact conforms to the Architecture Canon only if it:

- adopts canonical principles;
- preserves canonical terminology;
- respects canonical responsibilities;
- remains consistent with canonical models;
- preserves canonical relationships.

---

# Authority

MAC-0000 is the constitutional document of the MarkOrbit Architecture Canon.

All MAC standards derive their authority from this Charter.

In the event of inconsistency, this Charter shall prevail.

---

# Revision Policy

This Charter is expected to remain highly stable.

Changes shall occur only through constitutional architectural revision approved by the Governance Authority.

Editorial improvements shall not modify canonical meaning.
