# MarkOrbit Architecture Governance

# MAG-0000 — Governance Charter

**Document ID:** MAG-0000

**Title:** Governance Charter

**Version:** 1.0.0

**Status:** Canonical

**Category:** Governance Constitution

**Authority:** Governance Authority

---

# Purpose

This Charter establishes the governance foundation of the MarkOrbit Architecture Library.

The Architecture Canon defines the architecture.

The Governance Framework defines how the architecture is governed.

Together they establish the permanent foundation of the MarkOrbit Architecture Library.

---

# Scope

This Charter governs every architectural artifact published within the Architecture Library.

The Governance Framework applies to:

- Architecture Canon
- Governance Standards
- Architecture Specifications
- Frameworks
- Reference Architectures
- Architecture Patterns

This Charter does not govern:

- software implementations;
- operational activities;
- product releases;
- business operations.

---

# Governance Mission

The mission of the Governance Framework is to preserve the long-term integrity of the Architecture Library.

Governance exists to ensure that architectural evolution remains:

- deliberate;
- transparent;
- consistent;
- traceable;
- sustainable.

---

# Governance Principles

## Principle 1

Architecture shall evolve intentionally.

Architecture shall never evolve accidentally.

---

## Principle 2

Governance shall preserve the integrity of the Architecture Canon.

The Canon is the foundation of the Architecture Library.

---

## Principle 3

Every architectural decision shall possess a clearly identified authority.

---

## Principle 4

Every significant architectural change shall remain permanently traceable.

---

## Principle 5

Long-term architectural stability shall take precedence over short-term architectural convenience.

---

# Governance Authority

Governance Authority is responsible for preserving the Architecture Library.

Its responsibilities include:

- approving constitutional changes;
- approving governance standards;
- approving major architectural revisions;
- resolving architectural conflicts;
- preserving architectural consistency.

The internal organization of the Governance Authority is implementation-specific.

This Charter defines authority.

It does not define organizational structure.

---

# Governance Framework

The Governance Framework consists of the following standards.

| Document | Responsibility |
|----------|----------------|
| MAG-0001 | Architecture Lifecycle |
| MAG-0002 | Architecture Versioning |
| MAG-0003 | Architecture Change Management |
| MAG-0004 | Architecture Extension Policy |
| MAG-0005 | Architecture Proposal Process |

Each Governance Standard owns one governance responsibility.

Governance Standards are independent.

Collectively they define the governance system of the Architecture Library.

---

# Governance Objects

The Governance Framework governs the following architectural objects.

- Proposal
- Review
- Approval
- Change
- Release
- Standard

Governance procedures operate upon these objects.

Their implementation is defined by subsequent Governance Standards.

---

# Governance Boundaries

Governance defines process.

Architecture defines structure.

Specifications define interpretation.

Frameworks define practice.

Products define implementation.

These responsibilities shall remain independent.

---

# Relationship to the Architecture Canon

The Architecture Canon defines permanent architectural structure.

The Governance Framework defines permanent governance process.

Neither system shall redefine the other.

They evolve independently while remaining mutually consistent.

---

# Conformance

A Governance Standard conforms to this Charter only if it:

- supports the Governance Mission;
- follows the Governance Principles;
- preserves architectural integrity;
- respects governance authority.

---

# Authority

MAG-0000 is the constitutional document of the MarkOrbit Governance Framework.

All Governance Standards derive their authority from this Charter.

In the event of inconsistency, this Charter shall prevail.

---

# Revision Policy

This Charter is expected to remain highly stable.

Changes shall occur only through constitutional revision.

Editorial improvements shall not modify governance meaning.
