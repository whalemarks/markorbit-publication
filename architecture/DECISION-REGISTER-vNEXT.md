# Architecture Decision Register vNext

- **Version:** vNext
- **Status:** Owner Confirmed Canonical Working Baseline
- **Effective date:** 2026-07-13
- **Authority:** Repository-level MarkOrbit architecture governance

These decisions summarize the [MarkOrbit Orbital Architecture Canon vNext](MARKORBIT-ORBITAL-ARCHITECTURE-CANON-vNEXT.md).

## ARC-001 — MarkOrbit Operating System is the total architecture.

- **Decision:** MarkOrbit Operating System is the total architecture.
- **Rationale:** MarkOrbit must remain broader than any single product, repository or capability plane.
- **Consequence:** Core, Execution, Workplace, products, network and ecosystem learning are governed as parts of one professional operating architecture rather than competing architectures.
- **Status:** Accepted for vNext working baseline.
- **Affected books/products:** Architecture Canon.

## ARC-002 — Orbital is the primary architecture metaphor.

- **Decision:** Orbital is the primary architecture metaphor.
- **Rationale:** The ecosystem connects autonomous professional organizations without absorbing them into a central marketplace.
- **Consequence:** Publication and product language must use Orbital as the primary frame; federated or network terms may describe characteristics only.
- **Status:** Accepted for vNext working baseline.
- **Affected books/products:** Architecture Canon.

## ARC-003 — Each Organization Workplace is an independent Orbit.

- **Decision:** Each Organization Workplace is an independent Orbit.
- **Rationale:** An organization must retain its own clients, data, knowledge, pricing, rules and partner relationships.
- **Consequence:** Products and shared services consume authorized Workplace context but cannot convert organizational assets into centrally owned platform assets.
- **Status:** Accepted for vNext working baseline.
- **Affected books/products:** Workplace, Products.

## ARC-004 — Federated governance is a characteristic, not the primary name.

- **Decision:** Federated governance is a characteristic, not the primary name.
- **Rationale:** Federation describes distributed authority but does not express the full independent-orbit operating model.
- **Consequence:** Architecture records may mention federated governance only as a supporting characteristic under the Orbital architecture.
- **Status:** Accepted for vNext working baseline.
- **Affected books/products:** Architecture Canon.

## ARC-005 — Core, Execution, Workplace and Products remain separate responsibilities.

- **Decision:** Core, Execution, Workplace and Products remain separate responsibilities.
- **Rationale:** Shared semantics, governed coordination, organizational context and product experience change at different rates and carry different authority.
- **Consequence:** Product specifications and implementations must not collapse these layers or let product convenience redefine Core or Execution semantics.
- **Status:** Accepted for vNext working baseline.
- **Affected books/products:** Book 02, Book 03, Book 04, Products.

## ARC-006 — MarkOrbit uses a constitutional spine plus horizontal operating planes.

- **Decision:** MarkOrbit uses a constitutional spine plus horizontal operating planes.
- **Rationale:** A single linear stack cannot represent vertical authority and cross-product shared capabilities at the same time.
- **Consequence:** Architecture work must identify whether it changes the constitutional spine, a horizontal plane or a product-specific surface.
- **Status:** Accepted for vNext working baseline.
- **Affected books/products:** Architecture Canon, Book 04.

## ARC-007 — Governance applies across all planes.

- **Decision:** Governance applies across all planes.
- **Rationale:** Identity, permission, policy, review, audit and provenance are prerequisites for safe professional-service execution.
- **Consequence:** Governance cannot be appended only at workflow completion; every plane must preserve its own governance hooks and evidence.
- **Status:** Accepted for vNext working baseline.
- **Affected books/products:** All books and products.

## ARC-008 — Workplace is the ecosystem’s primary operating unit.

- **Decision:** Workplace is the ecosystem’s primary operating unit.
- **Rationale:** The professional organization is where identity, authority, clients, knowledge, rules and accountable review converge.
- **Consequence:** Products, network routing and shared capabilities must be designed around authorized Workplace participation and context.
- **Status:** Accepted for vNext working baseline.
- **Affected books/products:** Workplace, Book 04.

## ARC-009 — Lite is a lightweight Workplace with Today as its main experience.

- **Decision:** Lite is a lightweight Workplace with Today as its main experience.
- **Rationale:** Independent professionals and small teams need an actionable operating entry rather than a full enterprise shell.
- **Consequence:** Lite should prioritize Today, recommendations, prepared actions and handoff loops instead of becoming a reduced MarkReg or content-only tool.
- **Status:** Accepted for vNext working baseline.
- **Affected books/products:** Lite, Book 06.

## ARC-010 — MarkReg is the flagship trademark lifecycle application.

- **Decision:** MarkReg is the flagship trademark lifecycle application.
- **Rationale:** International trademark service has a complete lifecycle suitable for demonstrating MarkOrbit Core, Execution, Workplace and network capabilities.
- **Consequence:** MarkReg scope remains trademark professional-service lifecycle work and must not expand into all intellectual property or shrink into traditional case management.
- **Status:** Accepted for vNext working baseline.
- **Affected books/products:** MarkReg, Book 05.

## ARC-011 — MGSN connects Workplaces through routing, capability and trust.

- **Decision:** MGSN connects Workplaces through routing, capability and trust.
- **Rationale:** Cross-organization service cooperation needs governed routing among organizational orbits rather than anonymous transaction matching.
- **Consequence:** MGSN nodes are Organizations, Workplaces or Orbits, with professionals, capabilities and evidence attached to those nodes.
- **Status:** Accepted for vNext working baseline.
- **Affected books/products:** MGSN, Book 07.

## ARC-012 — MGSN is private-first and is not an open bidding marketplace.

- **Decision:** MGSN is private-first and is not an open bidding marketplace.
- **Rationale:** Professional-service cooperation depends on established relationships, confidentiality and accumulated trust, not only public price competition.
- **Consequence:** Routing begins with private partners, then trusted extended candidates, while provider appointment continues to require human selection.
- **Status:** Accepted for vNext working baseline.
- **Affected books/products:** MGSN, Book 07.

## ARC-013 — Information, Knowledge and Value are distinct cycles.

- **Decision:** Information, Knowledge and Value are distinct cycles.
- **Rationale:** Collected information, validated knowledge and derived value candidates have different evidence, review and lifecycle requirements.
- **Consequence:** Information pipelines, knowledge governance and value-generation features must preserve candidate status and validation boundaries.
- **Status:** Accepted for vNext working baseline.
- **Affected books/products:** Information & Intelligence Architecture.

## ARC-014 — Capability and Skill are distinct.

- **Decision:** Capability and Skill are distinct.
- **Rationale:** A capability describes what the system can do; a skill is a concrete, testable and replaceable implementation.
- **Consequence:** Registries and approvals must distinguish capability definitions from enabled skill implementations and experiments.
- **Status:** Accepted for vNext working baseline.
- **Affected books/products:** Capability and Skill Governance.

## ARC-015 — AI Agents do not own formal business state.

- **Decision:** AI Agents do not own formal business state.
- **Rationale:** AI runtime roles operate under identity, permission and context but are not accountable legal or business owners.
- **Consequence:** Agents may assist, draft, recommend and coordinate within permissions, but Owning Services and accountable humans control formal state changes.
- **Status:** Accepted for vNext working baseline.
- **Affected books/products:** Agent and Assistant Architecture, Execution.

## ARC-016 — Workflow is governed execution, not UI flow or prompt chaining.

- **Decision:** Workflow is governed execution, not UI flow or prompt chaining.
- **Rationale:** Professional operations require multi-step, multi-state execution with authority, audit, review and failure handling.
- **Consequence:** Prompt chains and interface paths may support workflows but do not become executable Workflows without workflow governance and ownership.
- **Status:** Accepted for vNext working baseline.
- **Affected books/products:** Book 03, Workflow Catalog.

## ARC-017 — Artifact is cross-product and is not identical to Document.

- **Decision:** Artifact is cross-product and is not identical to Document.
- **Rationale:** Generated outcomes may include pages, video, images, reports and publication packages, while Document remains a formal Core professional-record concept.
- **Consequence:** Products may prepare Artifacts, but formal Documents remain governed by Document ownership and lifecycle rules.
- **Status:** Accepted for vNext working baseline.
- **Affected books/products:** Artifact and Delivery Architecture, Products.

## ARC-018 — Delivery and Publish are distinct.

- **Decision:** Delivery and Publish are distinct.
- **Rationale:** Controlled distribution to a recipient and public or channel publication carry different permissions, risks and audit expectations.
- **Consequence:** Delivery and Publish capabilities must have separate approval, evidence and policy gates where professional risk differs.
- **Status:** Accepted for vNext working baseline.
- **Affected books/products:** Artifact and Delivery Architecture.

## ARC-019 — Lead remains product-level until separately validated.

- **Decision:** Lead remains product-level until separately validated.
- **Rationale:** Lead behavior depends on product loops and market validation and is not yet a shared Core semantic.
- **Consequence:** Products may manage leads locally, but Lead must not be promoted into Core or cross-product canon without a future validation gate.
- **Status:** Accepted for vNext working baseline.
- **Affected books/products:** Lite, MarkReg, Products.

## ARC-020 — Formal Opportunity remains owned by its Core/Owning Service boundary.

- **Decision:** Formal Opportunity remains owned by its Core/Owning Service boundary.
- **Rationale:** Opportunity changes commercial facts and therefore needs explicit ownership, state rules and audit boundaries.
- **Consequence:** Opportunity Candidates may be recommended or prepared, but formal Opportunity creation or mutation remains with the approved Owning Service.
- **Status:** Accepted for vNext working baseline.
- **Affected books/products:** Core, Products.

## ARC-021 — Product Loop First, Shared Platform Extraction Second.

- **Decision:** Product Loop First, Shared Platform Extraction Second.
- **Rationale:** Shared architecture boundaries should be demonstrated through repeated product needs rather than speculative platform abstraction.
- **Consequence:** Lite and MarkReg product loops may use explicit local services first; reusable platform capabilities are extracted only after recurring boundaries are proven.
- **Status:** Accepted for vNext working baseline.
- **Affected books/products:** Lite, MarkReg, shared platform.

## ARC-022 — Candidate states must precede canonical or approved states.

- **Decision:** Candidate states must precede canonical or approved states.
- **Rationale:** Professional knowledge, skills, workflows and AI outputs require evidence before they become trusted operational records.
- **Consequence:** Pipelines must route candidates through validation, governance review, testing, fixtures and registration before approval or rejection.
- **Status:** Accepted for vNext working baseline.
- **Affected books/products:** All architecture specifications.

## ARC-023 — Book 04 becomes Workplace and Product Architecture.

- **Decision:** Book 04 becomes Workplace and Product Architecture.
- **Rationale:** The next publication must explain how independent organizational orbits consume Core, Execution, intelligence, capabilities, products and network services.
- **Consequence:** Book 04 planning must focus on Workplace and Product Architecture, leaving manuscript scaffold creation to the separate Book 04 task.
- **Status:** Accepted for vNext working baseline.
- **Affected books/products:** Book 04.

## ARC-024 — Book 05–07 cover MarkReg, Lite and MGSN.

- **Decision:** Book 05–07 cover MarkReg, Lite and MGSN.
- **Rationale:** The flagship domain application, lightweight Workplace and service network each need separate publication treatment after the Workplace architecture is set.
- **Consequence:** Book registry and roadmap must reserve Book 05 for MarkReg, Book 06 for Lite and Book 07 for MGSN without creating their directories in this task.
- **Status:** Accepted for vNext working baseline.
- **Affected books/products:** Books 05–07.

## ARC-025 — The publication and repository maps are not one-to-one.

- **Decision:** The publication and repository maps are not one-to-one.
- **Rationale:** Books explain architecture and governance, while repositories should follow implementation ownership and validated extraction needs.
- **Consequence:** A book or architecture concept does not automatically justify a dedicated repository, service or database.
- **Status:** Accepted for vNext working baseline.
- **Affected books/products:** Publication map, repository map.

## ARC-026 — Book 02 remains frozen.

- **Decision:** Book 02 remains frozen.
- **Rationale:** Book 02 v0.1 is the accepted Core semantic baseline.
- **Consequence:** No Architecture Canon, product specification or implementation ADR may silently add or alter Core semantics; semantic changes require the existing Book 02 Change Proposal process.
- **Status:** Accepted for vNext working baseline.
- **Affected books/products:** Book 02.

## ARC-027 — External protected action remains unauthorized.

- **Decision:** External protected action remains unauthorized.
- **Rationale:** Publication architecture does not itself approve filing, payment, provider instruction, official recordal or autonomous professional action.
- **Consequence:** Any external protected action remains deferred until separately approved by the appropriate product, execution and governance gates.
- **Status:** Accepted for vNext working baseline.
- **Affected books/products:** Execution, Products, MGSN.

## ARC-028 — “各行其道，彼此牵引，共同演进” is a canonical MarkOrbit principle.

- **Decision:** “各行其道，彼此牵引，共同演进” is a canonical MarkOrbit principle.
- **Rationale:** The phrase captures the relationship between Workplace independence, capability/service/trust connection and ecosystem learning.
- **Consequence:** Future publications and product charters should preserve the three-part principle when explaining Orbital architecture.
- **Status:** Accepted for vNext working baseline.
- **Affected books/products:** Architecture Canon, all publications.
