# Book 06 v2 Publication Reconstruction Charter

## 1. Reconstruction purpose

The existing Book 06 v2 materials establish broad Product architecture for MarkOrbit Lite, but they remain distributed across a master synopsis and an eight-part manuscript containing many short chapters. This reconstruction consolidates that coverage into a smaller number of substantial publication chapters with sustained argument, operational examples, counterarguments, Product implications and governance boundaries.

The goal is not to invent a new Lite. It is to turn the accepted Lite direction into a coherent manuscript that can be read by founders, Product leaders, engineers, trademark professionals, partners and investors without requiring them to reconstruct the architecture from fragments.

## 2. Product identity under reconstruction

Lite is the daily growth and trademark-commerce Product inside a Workplace.

Its Product promise is:

> 有内容可发，有商标可卖，有需求可接，有客户可找。

That promise is strengthened by two additional obligations:

- the Product must help the user decide what matters today;
- the Product must convert completed work into governed memory, evidence and better future decisions.

Lite is not defined by feature count. It is defined by a repeatable professional-economic loop:

```text
observe
→ prioritize
→ create or act
→ follow up
→ record outcome
→ learn
```

## 3. Authority baseline

This manuscript remains subordinate to:

- the active Architecture Canon;
- the repository Decision Register;
- frozen Book baselines;
- Core semantic authority;
- Workplace business sovereignty;
- Owning Service formal-state authority;
- Professional Authority for protected actions;
- explicit customer, asset and relationship rights.

The reconstruction does not activate candidate Core objects or controlled vocabularies. Terms are explanatory unless and until separately promoted through the required governance process.

## 4. Mandatory distinctions

The manuscript must preserve at least the following distinctions:

```text
Lite ≠ MarkReg
Product suggestion ≠ user instruction
Today item ≠ universal Task
Customer obligation ≠ commercial opportunity
Trademark ≠ Inventory
Inventory ≠ Resale Authorization
Resale Authorization ≠ Listing
Listing ≠ proof of ownership or availability
Trademark Request ≠ Buyer Inquiry
Buyer Inquiry ≠ Transaction Opportunity
Transaction Opportunity ≠ Engagement or Order
Match ≠ recommendation
Recommendation ≠ legal availability
AI-generated brand concept ≠ factual commercial operation
Content performance ≠ proof of trademark use
Handoff ≠ customer relationship transfer
Capability certification ≠ Professional Qualification
Paid Contribution ≠ disguised training
MGSN route ≠ automatic Provider appointment
```

## 5. Scope of the publication manuscript

The long-form manuscript should explain:

- why a daily operating surface is commercially stronger than a module directory;
- how Today prioritizes obligations, opportunities, risks and learning;
- how different Workplaces receive different recommendations;
- how content is generated from governed professional objects;
- how trademark assets become authorized, explainable commercial supply;
- how buyer demand becomes structured Request, Inquiry and Opportunity;
- how matching, due diligence and transfer are governed;
- how service opportunities are identified without treating uncertain leads as confirmed customers;
- how Capability Center and paid contribution support professional growth;
- how subscription, transaction and human-service revenue coexist;
- how marketplace, ranking, privacy, customer and professional boundaries are protected;
- how Lite remains close to daily professional economics over the ten-year roadmap.

## 6. Editorial requirements

A mature chapter should normally contain:

- problem origin;
- failure of the familiar model;
- MarkOrbit proposal;
- distinctions from adjacent concepts;
- normal flow;
- exceptions and failure modes;
- examples grounded in trademark work;
- Product and technical implications;
- commercial implications;
- governance limits;
- open issues where a business or legal decision remains unresolved.

The prose should avoid turning architecture labels into unexplained slogans. Repeated locks are permitted during reconstruction but should be reduced and consolidated during final editing.

## 7. Source preservation

Existing valuable content should be retained through one of four treatments:

- retained and expanded;
- revised for clarity or authority consistency;
- merged into a larger chapter;
- deferred with an explicit reason.

No existing source should be silently treated as obsolete merely because the new manuscript uses a different chapter structure.

## 8. Commercial discipline

Every major feature claim should answer at least one of these questions:

- Does it help a Workplace publish useful content?
- Does it improve authorized trademark supply?
- Does it reveal or clarify real demand?
- Does it advance a customer or transaction relationship?
- Does it generate a governed service opportunity?
- Does it improve professional capability or review quality?
- Does it increase retention through real daily value?

Features that answer none of these questions should not dominate Lite's roadmap.

## 9. Prohibited implications

This reconstruction does not authorize:

- public marketplace launch;
- unrestricted scraping or outreach;
- automatic customer solicitation;
- payment custody or escrow;
- platform-principal status;
- legal conclusions about trademark ownership or transferability;
- autonomous professional advice or filing;
- automatic Provider appointment;
- AI training on Workplace-private data;
- certification as statutory qualification;
- production deployment.

## 10. Completion standard

The first reconstruction is complete when:

- the Product thesis is coherent from daily action through commercial and capability loops;
- all major source themes have a mapped disposition;
- adjacent Product and authority boundaries remain explicit;
- normal and failed flows are explained;
- economics and governance are integrated rather than appended;
- a completeness audit identifies no major planned section missing.

Final editorial readiness requires later work on cases, diagrams, legal review, terminology harmonization, references, copy-editing and branded production.
