## Scope reviewed

- CH05 — Content Must Begin with Professional Objects

## Structural verdict

Batch 02 establishes Lite's governed content and brand-commercialization production model.

It moves beyond the generic proposition that AI can write posts or generate attractive images. The four chapters connect professional source objects, commercial authority, factual provenance, concept labeling, deterministic media production, trademark continuity, scoped professional review and correction-aware business memory.

The resulting chain is:

```text
Professional object
→ authority and purpose check
→ Content / Visual Brief
→ governed facts, recommendations and concepts
→ AI-assisted draft or source imagery
→ deterministic composition
→ scoped review
→ specific Workplace approval
→ channel projection
→ response, correction and learning
```

## CH05 review — professional-object content

The chapter correctly rejects the empty prompt as Lite's primary content model.

It establishes professional source classes across:

- trademark and legal-status objects;
- commerce objects;
- customer and service objects;
- Knowledge and Capability objects;
- audience and channel objects.

It also defines:

- source-authority checks;
- Content Brief as a governed transformation contract;
- Claim Ledger and source linkage;
- fact, inference, recommendation and concept separation;
- purpose-specific content;
- derivative version relationships;
- de-identification review;
- AI boundaries;
- risk-based review;
- correction-aware Content Memory;
- business-motion performance rather than vanity metrics.

The chapter preserves Owning Service authority and does not make Lite the legal-status authority for source objects.

## CH06 review — Trademark Marketing Pack

The chapter establishes a credible bridge from legal asset data to commercial communication.

The Marketing Pack contains coordinated layers for:

- verified trademark facts;
- commercial interpretation;
- brand story and messaging;
- visual concepts;
- transaction context;
- channel outputs.

It correctly requires authority before creativity and supports different projections for:

- public discovery;
- network partners;
- qualified buyers;
- owner or customer review.

It preserves Unknown and source conflicts, distinguishes current reality from imagined potential and requires proposed product directions to be tested against actual goods and jurisdictional scope.

The Pack is a Lite commercial-content object. It does not replace the Trademark, Resale Authorization, Listing, due diligence or formal transfer state.

## CH08 review — beautification and continuity

The chapter correctly rejects the idea of beautification as one undifferentiated operation.

It establishes four transformation levels:

```text
Level A — Restoration
Level B — Conservative Refinement
Level C — Commercial Display Variation
Level D — New-mark Concept
```

It preserves the authoritative source, protected elements, derivative relationships, intended uses and owner decisions.

The chapter separates three review perspectives:

1. visual designer;
2. trademark professional;
3. commercialization specialist.

It explicitly avoids reducing their findings to one universal score and preserves disagreement as a decision input.

The treatment of use evidence is also correct:

```text
Concept mockup
≠ actual product

Published social image
≠ qualifying use by default

Commercial variation
≠ accepted specimen without review
```

New-mark concepts are connected to search, filing and adoption decisions without automatically authorizing those actions.

## Failure-mode check

The batch directly addresses:

- generic AI copy without sources;
- unverified facts in promotional content;
- unauthorized asset promotion;
- invented founder stories and market use;
- concept visuals presented as reality;
- AI misspelling or redrawing marks;
- stale derivative content after source correction;
- uncontrolled external-model disclosure;
- ambiguous logo-version ownership;
- designer opinions being mistaken for legal conclusions;
- legal-continuity review being mistaken for market validation;
- owner approval being too broad;
- unpaid professional review disguised as training;
- platform self-preference and undisclosed paid promotion.

No blocking contradiction with Books 01 or 04 was identified.

## Editorial assessment

The chapters meet the first publication-grade reconstruction standard. They contain sustained argument, operating flows, product implications, technical consequences, commercial considerations, counterarguments, examples and failure analysis.

Final-edition work will still require:

- visual diagrams for the Content Brief, Claim Ledger and rendering pipeline;
- worked Marketing Pack examples using clearly fictional or authorized assets;
- jurisdiction-specific legal review of continuity and use-evidence examples;
- rights and licensing appendix references;
- cross-book alignment with Book 05 MarkReg handoffs and Book 03 review authority;
- reduction of repeated locks during final copyediting;
- terminology and glossary harmonization.

These are final editing and cross-book reconciliation needs, not reasons to weaken the current boundaries.
