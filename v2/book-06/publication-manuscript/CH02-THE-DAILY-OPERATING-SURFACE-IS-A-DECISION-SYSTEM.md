# Chapter 02 — The Daily Operating Surface Is a Decision System

Once Lite begins with the day, a harder question appears: how should the Product decide what to show first?

The tempting answer is to use a score. Add urgency, commercial value, likelihood of conversion and user preference, then sort the result. That approach may work for a shopping feed. It is not sufficient for professional work.

A daily operating surface must rank actions while respecting authority, evidence, customer obligations, professional risk and the possibility that the system is wrong. It is therefore not merely a recommendation engine. It is a governed decision-support system.

## 1. Ranking is an exercise of power

The item placed first receives attention. The item hidden below the fold may be delayed or forgotten. A platform that ranks actions is influencing:

- which customer is contacted;
- which trademark is promoted;
- which buyer Request receives a response;
- which service opportunity is pursued;
- which Provider or route is considered;
- which professional warning is taken seriously;
- which capability a user develops;
- which revenue opportunity the Workplace prioritizes.

This influence becomes especially sensitive when the platform earns money from some actions but not others.

If a transaction opportunity creates platform commission while a customer follow-up generates no immediate platform revenue, a purely commercial ranking model may systematically distort professional duty.

Ranking policy is therefore Product governance.

## 2. Candidate generation comes before ranking

A ranking system can only order the candidates it receives. If important obligations are never generated, no scoring formula can recover them.

Candidate actions may originate from:

- formal deadlines;
- customer commitments;
- Matter or Order state;
- document readiness;
- stale or expiring Resale Authorization;
- buyer Requests;
- new Inquiries;
- Listing performance;
- content opportunities;
- service-opportunity signals;
- Capability gaps;
- network invitations;
- security, compliance or data-quality warnings;
- user-created reminders.

Each source should produce a typed candidate rather than a generic notification.

A typed candidate identifies what kind of decision is being proposed and what authority is needed to act.

## 3. The decision record

A useful candidate should contain at least:

- candidate type;
- source object and Owning Service;
- relevant Workplace and Product Installation;
- affected customer, asset, relationship or capability;
- triggering event;
- proposed action;
- expected value or risk reduction;
- urgency and time window;
- evidence and confidence;
- known Unknowns;
- required role, permission or professional authority;
- external-effect level;
- expiry, supersession or dismissal policy;
- explanation suitable for the user.

The Product should be able to answer not only “why is this here?” but also “what would make it disappear or change priority?”

## 4. Hard gates must precede soft ranking

Some candidates should be excluded or blocked before scoring.

Examples include:

- promoting a trademark without current Resale Authorization;
- recommending outreach where contact identity is unverified or communication is not permitted;
- suggesting a filing action to a user without the required authority;
- routing work to a Provider that is ineligible, conflicted or unavailable;
- publishing customer facts without consent;
- presenting an expired buyer Request as active;
- treating an AI-generated image as proof of real commercial use.

The correct sequence is:

```text
Candidate generation
→ eligibility and authority gates
→ evidence and freshness checks
→ risk classification
→ ranking
→ explanation
→ user action or review
```

A high commercial score must never override a failed authority gate.

## 5. Several dimensions of priority

Priority should be multi-dimensional.

### Obligation urgency

How soon could delay harm a customer, legal right, contract or accepted commitment?

### Irreversibility

Can the consequence be recovered later, or would delay cause permanent loss?

### Relationship importance

Does the item concern an active customer expectation, unresolved complaint or promised response?

### Readiness

Can the proposed action be performed now, or are documents, approval, authority or evidence missing?

### Commercial value

Could the action create revenue, transaction progress, customer retention or future demand?

### Probability and confidence

How strong is the evidence that the opportunity or risk is real?

### Strategic value

Does the action improve an important asset, content channel, capability corridor or relationship?

### Effort and capacity

Can the Workplace complete the action with current time, capability and review bandwidth?

### Freshness

Is the recommendation based on current information?

### User and Workplace policy

Has the Workplace deliberately prioritized particular customers, markets, content themes, asset types or capability goals?

No single dimension should silently dominate every decision.

## 6. Obligations and opportunities require different ranking logic

A deadline should not compete with a content idea under the same scoring formula.

Lite can use separate lanes:

```text
Protect
Serve
Grow
Learn
```

- **Protect** contains deadlines, formal risks, unresolved exceptions and security concerns.
- **Serve** contains customer commitments, follow-up and active Engagement work.
- **Grow** contains content, Listings, buyer demand, transactions and service opportunities.
- **Learn** contains simulation, certification, review and paid contribution opportunities.

Within each lane, ranking can be more coherent. Across lanes, the Product can apply policy such as ensuring that critical Protect items remain visible regardless of commercial score.

This is preferable to pretending every item has one universal numerical value.

## 7. Unknown is a valid outcome

A decision system becomes dangerous when it is pressured to produce a confident answer for every candidate.

Lite should be able to say:

- buyer identity unverified;
- Listing authorization status unclear;
- customer consent missing;
- deadline source conflict unresolved;
- Provider capacity unknown;
- commercial value uncertain;
- mark status needs confirmation;
- contact information is only a candidate match.

Unknown may change the proposed action from “send offer” to “verify identity,” or from “promote asset” to “request updated authorization.”

```text
Incomplete information
≠ permission to guess
```

## 8. Explanation must expose trade-offs

A recommendation should not merely state that an item is “high priority.” It should explain why.

For example:

> This maintenance opportunity is ranked first because the statutory window closes in forty-five days, the customer relationship is active, the registration record is verified, and required use evidence has not yet been confirmed. Recommended next action: request evidence review. External filing is not yet authorized.

A buyer Request recommendation might say:

> This Request matches two authorized Class 25 Listings within budget. The first has stronger semantic fit but its authorization expires in seven days. The second has broader goods coverage but requires ownership verification before recommendation.

Such explanations let the user evaluate the Product rather than merely obey it.

## 9. User feedback should correct, not merely personalize

When a user dismisses a candidate, the Product should distinguish:

- not relevant to this Workplace;
- already completed elsewhere;
- source data is wrong;
- customer declined;
- authorization missing;
- action postponed;
- recommendation quality poor;
- permanent policy preference.

These reasons have different consequences.

A source error should trigger data correction. A customer decline may close an Opportunity. A policy preference may adjust future recommendations. A temporary postponement should preserve the item. A critical obligation may require escalation rather than disappearance.

Feedback is not just engagement data. It is evidence about the operating model.

## 10. AI may assist but not own the decision

AI can help:

- summarize source context;
- classify candidate types;
- estimate missing information;
- propose next actions;
- explain ranking;
- draft communication;
- detect contradictions;
- compare alternatives.

But AI should not autonomously:

- accept a customer instruction;
- publish customer information;
- declare a trademark legally available;
- appoint a Provider;
- initiate a protected professional action;
- change formal Matter state;
- treat repeated behavior as permanent policy without review.

The decision surface must preserve the transition from suggestion to authorized action.

## 11. External effects require stronger controls

Not all daily actions carry the same consequence.

A useful classification is:

```text
Internal observation
Internal organization
Draft creation
Internal assignment
Customer communication
Public publication
Commercial offer
Provider recommendation
Formal appointment
Protected professional action
Formal-state mutation
```

As external effect and risk increase, the Product should require stronger preview, approval, evidence and role checks.

An internal note may be created with low friction. A public trademark advertisement requires current authorization and factual review. A filing instruction may require customer approval and Professional Authority.

## 12. The platform's self-interest must be disclosed

Lite may earn revenue from:

- subscriptions;
- promoted Listings;
- transaction coordination;
- professional review;
- MarkReg service;
- capability programs;
- network fulfillment.

These interests may affect ranking.

The Product should distinguish:

- organic relevance;
- paid promotion;
- platform-owned service;
- partner commission;
- customer or Workplace preference;
- evidence-based quality recommendation.

Paid visibility must not be disguised as professional suitability. A platform-owned MarkReg route should not silently outrank a better internal or existing Provider route.

## 13. Measuring decision quality

The wrong metrics include:

- number of cards clicked;
- time spent on Today;
- notification opens;
- streaks;
- number of generated posts;
- number of routed opportunities regardless of outcome.

More meaningful measures include:

- critical obligations resolved before deadline;
- customer commitments completed;
- recommendations accepted after explanation;
- false-positive and stale-candidate rates;
- time from signal to responsible action;
- percentage of actions with sufficient evidence;
- conversion of verified opportunities;
- correction propagation;
- user trust and contestability;
- reduction in forgotten or duplicated work.

The aim is not maximum interaction. It is better professional decisions.

## 14. Failure modes

The decision system fails when:

- one opaque score governs every item;
- revenue-generating actions outrank professional duties;
- failed authority gates are treated as low scores rather than hard blocks;
- Unknown is hidden;
- explanations repeat generic language;
- feedback only trains personalization and does not correct source data;
- platform self-interest is undisclosed;
- users cannot contest or repair recommendations;
- external actions occur from a single ambiguous click;
- formal-state authority is bypassed.

## 15. Product and technical implications

The daily surface requires:

- an event and signal layer;
- typed candidate-action models;
- source and provenance references;
- policy gates;
- risk and external-effect classification;
- multiple priority lanes;
- explainable ranking;
- feedback and correction workflows;
- role and authority resolution;
- Preview and specific approval;
- outcome and supersession tracking;
- audit evidence.

The interface may be simple. The decision infrastructure cannot be simplistic.

## 16. The operating principle

Lite should help a Workplace decide without pretending to replace the Workplace's judgment.

> A trustworthy daily surface does not merely predict what the user may click. It identifies what the business should consider, explains why, blocks unsafe routes and preserves the difference between recommendation and authority.
