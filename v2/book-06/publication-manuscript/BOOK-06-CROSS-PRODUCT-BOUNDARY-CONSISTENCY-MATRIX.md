# Book 06 v2 — Cross-Product Boundary Consistency Matrix

## Purpose

This matrix verifies that the Book 06 manuscript preserves the accepted MarkOrbit authority model across Lite, MarkReg, Sites, MGSN, Core, Workplace and Owning Service.

It is a publication consistency artifact. It does not activate implementation or modify the active Canon.

## Authority baseline

```text
Core
= shared semantic authority

Workplace
= business-sovereignty and Product-operation boundary

Product Installation
= governed Product configuration inside a Workplace

Owning Service
= formal business-state authority

MGSN Connection / Gateway
= Workplace-scoped interface to the network

MGSN Network
= platform-owned managed global service network
```

## Product matrix

| Layer / Product | Primary purpose | Owns or governs | May project or hand off | Must not automatically own or imply |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Core | Shared semantics, governance primitives and interoperability | Object meaning, lifecycle rules, authority vocabulary | Canonical semantic contracts | Workplace customers, private files, Orders, Matters, pricing or relationships |
| Workplace | Business sovereignty and Product operation | Membership, policy, customer and relationship context, Product configuration | Governed external relations and Product requests | Core, Product source code, MGSN network assets or other Workplaces |
| Lite | Daily growth, supply, demand, opportunity and Capability | Lite content, Inventory relationships, Requests, Recommendations, Inquiries, Opportunities and private operating memory | Approved content and Listings to Sites; qualified service Handoffs to MarkReg; managed Needs to MGSN | Full Matter execution, professional authority, formal filing state, automatic customer transfer |
| MarkReg | Complete trademark service delivery | Intake, Engagement, Order, Matter, evidence, deadlines, Provider fulfillment and validated service state | Purpose-limited status and document Projections to Lite or Sites | Originating partner's customer relationship by default |
| Sites | Public and customer-facing Projection | Published pages, Projection configuration and interaction events | Candidate inquiries and typed submissions to Owning Services | Accepted Customer, Order, Matter, Payment or official status |
| MGSN | Managed external professional fulfillment | Network eligibility, route governance, Provider trust, appointment flow, evidence return and recovery | Governed candidate routes and fulfillment status | Participant customer relationships or unrestricted Provider contacts |

## Cross-Product contracts

### Lite → Sites

```text
Approved Content / Marketing Pack / Listing
→ Sites Projection Request
→ Sites validation
→ Published Projection
→ Inquiry or response event
→ Lite or other Owning Service
```

**Required controls:** source version, visibility, authority, concept labels, expiry, contact route, withdrawal and correction.

**Forbidden implication:** public publication does not create ownership, Resale Authorization, Customer, Order or Payment state.

### Sites → Lite

```text
Public interaction
→ typed candidate event
→ source and campaign provenance
→ Lite qualification
→ Inquiry / Request / Lead candidate
```

**Required controls:** identity uncertainty, consent, spam filtering, source page version and Relationship Owner.

**Forbidden implication:** form submission is not customer acceptance or instruction.

### Lite → MarkReg

```text
Qualified Service Opportunity
→ customer instruction
→ approved scope and terms
→ bounded Handoff
→ MarkReg Intake
→ Engagement and authorized execution
```

**Required controls:** originating Workplace, Relationship Owner, minimum necessary data, communication rules, deadline, Unknowns, commercial route and expected evidence return.

**Forbidden implication:** Handoff does not appoint a Provider, authorize filing or transfer the customer.

### MarkReg → Lite

```text
Validated MarkReg state
→ purpose-limited status Projection
→ Lite follow-up and customer-facing coordination
```

**Required controls:** Owning Service source, timestamp, evidence, freshness, visibility and revocation.

**Forbidden implication:** Lite cannot derive formal state directly from Provider email or uploaded document.

### Lite → MGSN

```text
Verified external service Need
→ Workplace MGSN Connection
→ eligibility and route recommendation
→ originating approval
→ Provider acceptance and appointment
→ fulfillment
```

**Required controls:** Relationship Owner, data scope, candidate disclosure, conflict, qualification, customer communication and recovery.

**Forbidden implication:** Need or Recommendation does not create appointment or unrestricted Provider access.

### MGSN → Lite / MarkReg

```text
Provider return
→ network evidence contract
→ relevant Owning Service validation
→ controlled status Projection
```

**Required controls:** exact expected evidence, source Provider, dates, affected object, exceptions and validation result.

**Forbidden implication:** Provider statement does not mutate formal state by itself.

### MarkReg → Sites

```text
Validated Matter state
→ customer-facing Projection request
→ Sites portal or status view
```

**Required controls:** customer identity, field scope, action scope, expiry, download and communication rules.

**Forbidden implication:** Sites does not become the Matter system of record.

## Shared-object consistency

| Object or concept | Lite treatment | Cross-Product boundary |
|---|---|---|
| Trademark | Referenced as legal or commercial source object | Formal legal status remains with authoritative source / Owning Service |
| Inventory | Workplace-scoped supply relationship | Does not migrate as ownership or Resale Authorization |
| Listing | Lite commercial Projection object | Sites may publish; publishing does not own the Listing source |
| Trademark Request | Lite demand object | May be selectively projected; buyer relationship remains governed |
| Inquiry | Lite or receiving Product interaction object | Must preserve source and Relationship Owners |
| Transaction Opportunity | Lite commercial opportunity | Does not become MarkReg Engagement automatically |
| Service Opportunity | Lite qualified candidate | Requires customer instruction and Handoff before MarkReg Intake |
| Capability | Person/Workplace evidence object | Does not create MGSN eligibility, Assignment or qualification alone |
| Content / Visual Asset | Lite-governed production object | Sites may project; MarkReg may receive evidence-relevant assets through typed Handoff |
| Payment reference | May be displayed or reconciled | Does not authorize custody or prove completion |
| Provider | May appear as recommended or appointed role | Full network identity and trust remain governed by MGSN / relevant service route |

## Memory and AI consistency

```text
Lite operating memory
≠ MarkReg Matter memory
≠ Sites public content
≠ MGSN network learning
≠ platform model training consent
```

Cross-Product AI use requires:

- a declared purpose;
- authorized source context;
- minimum necessary fields;
- retention and memory rules;
- model-provider policy;
- source and output provenance;
- correction propagation;
- no silent cross-Workplace reuse.

## Relationship consistency

The following roles remain independently recordable across all Product flows:

- Demand Originator;
- Relationship Owner;
- Contracting Party;
- Payment Receiver;
- Delivery Owner;
- Professional Authority;
- Provider;
- Contributor;
- Owning Service.

```text
Product that displays the relationship
≠ Product or platform that owns the relationship
```

## Revenue consistency

| Revenue type | Primary basis | Boundary requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Lite subscription | Maintained operating capability | Does not create professional authority |
| Sites fee | Publishing, hosting, media or Projection capability | Does not create Customer / Order / Payment authority |
| MarkReg fee | Complete trademark service delivery | Must identify Contracting Party, Delivery Owner and professional route |
| MGSN fee | Managed routing, trust, evidence and recovery | Does not sell unrestricted Provider contacts |
| Transaction fee | Referral, brokerage, coordination or due diligence role | Must preserve provenance, commission and relationship rules |
| Contribution payment | Accepted bounded Work Package | Does not grant customer relationship or unrestricted reuse |

## Failure ownership

Every cross-Product failure should identify:

- source Product;
- receiving Product;
- source Workplace;
- current Owning Service;
- expected event or evidence;
- responsible actor;
- customer communication owner;
- deadline impact;
- recovery route;
- commercial consequence.

A seamless interface must not hide responsibility.
