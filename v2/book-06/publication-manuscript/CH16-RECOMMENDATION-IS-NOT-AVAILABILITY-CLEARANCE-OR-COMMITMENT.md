# Chapter 16 — Recommendation Is Not Availability, Clearance or Commitment

A recommendation is easy to overread.

The buyer may see a polished card, a strong match explanation, an asking price and an attractive Marketing Pack. The seller may receive an alert that a buyer Request matches the Listing. The platform may calculate a high confidence score.

None of these events proves that the trademark is still available, legally clear, commercially acceptable or ready for transfer.

Lite should make recommendation useful without turning it into false certainty.

```text
Recommendation
≠ availability confirmation
≠ legal clearance
≠ seller acceptance
≠ buyer commitment
≠ reservation
≠ transaction agreement
≠ ownership transfer
```

The Recommendation is a governed decision aid. It identifies a possible route and the work still required to validate it.

## 1. Recommendation sits between matching and action

A complete chain may be:

```text
Qualified Request
→ eligible candidate set
→ matching run
→ Recommendation
→ buyer presentation
→ buyer feedback
→ Inquiry
→ seller confirmation
→ Transaction Opportunity
→ Engagement
→ due diligence and negotiation
→ transfer execution
```

The Recommendation should not collapse later stages merely because the candidate looks promising.

## 2. Availability has several meanings

The word “available” is dangerously ambiguous.

It may mean:

- the Listing is visible;
- the Resale Authorization is active;
- the seller has not withdrawn the mark;
- the mark is not reserved;
- the seller is willing to receive inquiries;
- the asking price is current;
- the seller is ready to negotiate;
- the seller is ready to accept a particular offer;
- the legal right remains valid;
- the transfer route is operationally feasible.

Lite should use specific states instead of one green “Available” badge.

Possible states include:

```text
Visible for discovery
Available for inquiry
Seller reconfirmation required
Price reconfirmation required
Private negotiation only
Reserved
Transaction pending
Authorization revalidation pending
Withdrawn
Sold subject to recordal
Transfer completed
```

The Recommendation should display the relevant state and verification date.

## 3. Listing visibility does not confirm seller intent today

A Listing may remain active while:

- the owner changes strategy;
- price expectations change;
- the authorization expires;
- the mark enters another negotiation;
- the owner stops responding;
- the legal status changes;
- an exclusivity conflict appears;
- the seller decides to keep the mark.

For material buyer action, Lite should obtain seller or authorized intermediary reconfirmation.

```text
Listing active
≠ seller currently ready
```

The amount of reconfirmation should be proportionate. A recent and evidence-rich Listing may require a simple availability check. A stale or high-value asset may require full authority and status refresh.

## 4. Recommendation is not legal clearance

A purchased registration can still create legal risk.

Possible issues include:

- earlier third-party rights;
- restricted goods coverage;
- non-use vulnerability;
- ownership defects;
- pending disputes;
- misleading or descriptive significance;
- conflicts in expansion jurisdictions;
- unsuitable actual use;
- differences between the registered mark and intended brand version;
- transfer or assignment restrictions.

Matching compares the candidate with the buyer’s Request. Clearance evaluates whether the buyer’s intended use and acquisition route are legally acceptable under the relevant scope.

```text
Good fit
≠ safe use
≠ registrable expansion
≠ enforceable right
```

Where the buyer relies on a legal conclusion, the Recommendation should call for the appropriate professional review rather than simulate certainty.

## 5. Recommendation is not due diligence

A Recommendation may use summarized and recently verified data. Due diligence can require deeper evidence, such as:

- official status records;
- certificate and file history;
- ownership chain;
- assignment documents;
- identity and authority of the seller;
- maintenance and use evidence;
- disputes, licenses and security interests;
- co-existence or settlement agreements;
- financial and tax considerations;
- included assets;
- territorial restrictions;
- transfer formalities.

The Recommendation should identify known due-diligence gaps.

It should not present “no issue found in matching data” as “no issue exists.”

## 6. Recommendation is not buyer commitment

A buyer may:

- request more details;
- save a candidate;
- compare several options;
- ask for a lower price;
- seek internal approval;
- ask for professional review;
- withdraw the Request;
- choose a new-filing route.

These actions should have distinct states.

```text
Viewed
Saved
Shortlisted
More Information Requested
Professional Review Requested
Inquiry Opened
Offer Considered
Offer Submitted
Rejected
```

A saved or shortlisted mark should not be represented to the seller as a confirmed buyer.

## 7. Recommendation is not seller commitment

Seller-side events should also remain distinct:

```text
Inquiry Accepted
Information Supplied
Price Confirmed
Negotiation Authorized
Offer Considered
Offer Accepted Subject to Contract
Binding Agreement Signed
```

A seller’s willingness to discuss is not acceptance. An intermediary’s response is not necessarily owner approval. An accepted headline price may remain subject to documents, payment, due diligence and formal agreement.

## 8. Recommendation is not reservation

A Recommendation does not remove the asset from other buyers.

Reservation should be an explicit event with:

- the identified buyer or originating Workplace;
- approving seller or authorized actor;
- start and expiry;
- conditions;
- exclusivity scope;
- whether other inquiries may continue;
- deposit or consideration where applicable;
- cancellation rules;
- attribution protection.

```text
Recommended
≠ shortlisted
≠ reserved
```

The interface should make this clear when several buyers may be viewing the same Listing.

## 9. Recommendation is not a quotation

Matching may display:

- asking price;
- owner net price where authorized;
- estimated transaction costs;
- estimated professional fees;
- expected official fees;
- possible expansion costs.

These values may have different status:

```text
Confirmed seller price
Indicative price
Price on request
Professional quotation
Estimated official fee
Historical estimate
Unknown
```

A Recommendation should preserve these labels and dates.

The platform should not combine them into a false “all-inclusive final price” unless the responsible parties have issued a binding or clearly scoped quotation.

## 10. Recommendation is not an instruction to contact

Buyer and seller relationships may be protected.

The Recommendation should specify the permitted next route:

- contact originating Relationship Owner;
- open an in-platform Inquiry;
- request seller reconfirmation through a partner;
- ask for professional review;
- request access to a qualified buyer room;
- do not contact the owner directly;
- owner-direct contact permitted after disclosure.

```text
Candidate identity visible
≠ contact permission
```

This protects legitimate relationship and confidentiality interests without making the platform the permanent owner of every future relationship.

## 11. Recommendation should include a readiness statement

A useful Recommendation may summarize readiness across dimensions.

Example:

```text
Request fit: strong
Legal-status evidence: recently verified
Resale Authorization: active
Seller availability: reconfirmation required
Price: indicative
Goods fit: partial — professional review recommended
Cross-jurisdiction expansion: unknown
Buyer identity disclosure: not yet permitted
Next step: open private Inquiry through originating Workplace
```

This is more honest than a single “92% match” label.

## 12. Recommendations should have expiry

A Recommendation becomes stale when:

- the Request changes;
- the Listing changes;
- the Authorization expires;
- the price changes;
- the status changes;
- another buyer reserves the asset;
- seller response history worsens;
- new evidence appears;
- matching rules or jurisdiction knowledge changes materially.

Each Recommendation should record:

- Request version;
- candidate and Listing version;
- evidence snapshot;
- generated date;
- validity or refresh trigger;
- responsible Product and Workplace context.

A stale Recommendation should be marked, refreshed or withdrawn rather than silently remaining active.

## 13. Recommendation correction must propagate

Suppose a Recommendation said that a mark covered coffee machines, but later review shows the specification covers only coffee roasters.

The system should:

1. correct the source interpretation;
2. identify affected Recommendations;
3. notify the responsible Workplace;
4. update or withdraw buyer-facing views;
5. preserve the original version and correction evidence;
6. reassess other candidates and Requests using the same normalization rule;
7. avoid silently changing formal transaction records.

Correction is an operating responsibility, not merely an editorial edit.

## 14. AI confidence should not determine legal or commercial state

AI may estimate:

- name fit;
- likely goods alignment;
- brand potential;
- likelihood that the buyer will respond;
- possible seller responsiveness.

These estimates can prioritize human attention. They cannot create:

- legal validity;
- resale authority;
- seller availability;
- buyer intent;
- reservation;
- acceptance;
- transfer completion.

```text
Prediction
≠ state mutation
```

Formal state should change through typed events from the appropriate actor or Owning Service.

## 15. Professional endorsements must remain scoped

A professional may review a Recommendation for:

- goods coverage;
- legal-status evidence;
- transaction feasibility;
- brand commercialization;
- local-language perception;
- likely transfer requirements.

The review record should state what was and was not reviewed.

For example:

> Reviewed for apparent goods alignment in the United States based on the current official specification. Ownership chain, use evidence, infringement risk and transfer documents not reviewed.

A professional badge without scope can cause the buyer to rely on conclusions that were never given.

## 16. The buyer should see meaningful uncertainty

Uncertainty should be specific and actionable.

Weak disclosure:

> Subject to verification.

Stronger disclosure:

- seller must reconfirm current willingness to sell;
- owner identity differs between two data sources;
- goods coverage appears partial for the buyer’s core product;
- Chinese filing route has not been evaluated;
- price excludes recordal and professional fees;
- Marketing Pack contains concept visuals, not existing products;
- use evidence has not been reviewed.

The user should understand what decision can safely be made now.

## 17. Recommendation routes should include “do not proceed”

A matching system should be able to recommend:

- proceed to Inquiry;
- request seller reconfirmation;
- request professional review;
- ask buyer to clarify;
- retain as a secondary option;
- use only as naming inspiration;
- pursue new filing instead;
- reject the candidate;
- pause because evidence is insufficient.

The commercial model should not reward only forward movement.

A refusal or pause can protect the buyer, seller and platform.

## 18. Example: a strong match with weak availability

A buyer seeks a registered US mark for air fryers and small kitchen appliances.

Candidate X has:

- strong name fit;
- useful Class 11 specification;
- attractive Marketing Pack;
- price within budget;
- Listing last verified eight months ago;
- Resale Authorization expired last month;
- seller response unknown.

The system should not label Candidate X “available.”

A responsible Recommendation may state:

```text
Fit: strong
Legal-status evidence: refresh required
Authorization: expired
Seller availability: unknown
Commercial route: reconfirmation through source Workplace
Buyer action now: save as candidate; do not rely on price or availability
```

The candidate may become viable after reauthorization. Until then, it is not transaction-ready supply.

## 19. Example: available asset with weak legal fit

Candidate Y has:

- active Authorization;
- confirmed seller;
- clear price;
- fast transfer documents;
- registration for lighting products rather than kitchen appliances.

A responsible Recommendation may state:

```text
Commercial availability: strong
Name and brand fit: strong
Current goods fit: weak
Possible route: acquire for brand value plus new filing for core goods
Professional review: required before relying on existing registration
```

The system should not downgrade every such candidate to zero. It should explain the acquisition-plus-filing route and cost.

## 20. Handoff to Inquiry

When the buyer chooses to proceed, Lite should create an Inquiry linked to:

- Request version;
- Recommendation version;
- Listing and Authorization;
- originating Workplace;
- Relationship Owner;
- buyer-disclosure permissions;
- questions asked;
- documents requested;
- known uncertainty;
- expected response deadline.

The Inquiry is not a free-form message detached from provenance.

## 21. Handoff to MarkReg or professional services

A Recommendation may require:

- legal-status refresh;
- goods and class review;
- clearance search;
- due diligence;
- agreement preparation;
- recordal;
- new filing;
- use-evidence review;
- logo continuity review.

Lite should create a governed Handoff with the source Request, candidate, evidence and user-approved scope.

The Handoff does not transfer the customer relationship by default. It also does not appoint a Provider automatically.

## 22. Conflict and self-preference controls

A platform may benefit when a Recommendation leads to:

- a platform-owned Listing;
- MarkReg work;
- MGSN delivery;
- paid review;
- higher commission;
- sponsored placement.

The system should disclose material interests and preserve alternatives.

A recommended next step should be based on the buyer’s route and risk, not only platform revenue.

```text
Platform can provide the next service
≠ platform is the only permitted route
```

## 23. Failure modes

The Recommendation model fails when:

- a high match score becomes an “available” badge;
- seller willingness is inferred from an old Listing;
- legal clearance is implied by goods and name fit;
- price estimates are shown as final quotations;
- buyer saves are reported as commitments;
- recommendations create reservations automatically;
- seller messages are treated as binding acceptance;
- concept visuals are presented as actual use;
- AI confidence mutates formal state;
- recommendation expiry is absent;
- corrections do not propagate;
- contact rights are ignored;
- professional reviews lack scope;
- platform services are preferred without disclosure;
- “subject to verification” hides material unknowns.

## 24. Product and technical implications

Lite requires Recommendation objects with:

- Request and candidate version linkage;
- matching-dimension explanations;
- verified and unknown fields;
- availability state;
- Authorization state;
- price status;
- professional-review scope;
- allowed next actions;
- relationship and contact rules;
- expiry and refresh triggers;
- conflict and sponsorship disclosure;
- buyer feedback;
- correction history;
- Inquiry Handoff;
- MarkReg and professional-service Handoff.

Typed events should control transitions from Recommendation to Inquiry, reservation, offer, agreement and transfer.

## 25. Commercial implications

Clear boundaries may reduce impulsive conversion because the product refuses to imply certainty. They improve long-term trust and reduce:

- wasted buyer time;
- seller disputes;
- false availability claims;
- price surprises;
- unauthorized contact;
- professional-liability confusion;
- stale Listing reputational damage.

The commercial opportunity lies in helping participants complete the necessary next work, not in pretending that matching has already completed it.

## 26. Metrics that matter

Useful measures include:

- Recommendation-to-Inquiry conversion;
- seller reconfirmation success;
- stale Recommendation rate;
- price correction rate;
- goods-fit correction rate;
- professional-review escalation;
- buyer misunderstanding incidents;
- unauthorized-contact incidents;
- Inquiry closure reasons;
- time from Recommendation to reliable availability confirmation;
- correction propagation time.

A high click-through rate can coexist with a misleading system.

## 27. The operating principle

```text
Recommend a possible route
State the evidence
Name the uncertainty
Separate buyer and seller intent
Require explicit transitions
Expire stale conclusions
Escalate professional questions
Do not convert prediction into authority
```

> A Recommendation is valuable when it helps the buyer decide what to investigate next—not when it makes an incomplete commercial possibility look like a completed legal and transactional fact.
