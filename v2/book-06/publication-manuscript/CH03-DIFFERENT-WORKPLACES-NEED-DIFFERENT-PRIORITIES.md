# Chapter 03 — Different Workplaces Need Different Priorities

A daily operating Product cannot assume that every user is trying to run the same business.

One Workplace may rely on content to generate demand. Another may hold a large portfolio of trademarks for resale. A third may focus on international filing and lifecycle service. A fourth may be a single professional developing capability through supervised contribution. A fifth may combine all of these activities but face a temporary shortage of reviewer capacity.

If Lite gives each Workplace the same homepage, the Product will either become generic or force every business toward the platform's preferred model.

The daily surface must therefore adapt to the Workplace's real resources, needs, obligations and strategy.

## 1. Personalization is not merely interface preference

Ordinary personalization changes:

- color;
- layout;
- language;
- favorite module;
- notification frequency;
- recently opened records.

Workplace adaptation is deeper. It changes which actions are relevant because the business has different:

- customer relationships;
- trademark assets;
- Resale Authorizations;
- content channels;
- capabilities;
- professional qualifications;
- current capacity;
- buyer demand;
- service pipeline;
- commercial policies;
- risk exposure;
- Product Installations.

The Product should not infer a complete business strategy from clicks alone. It should use governed business context.

## 2. Resource Profile and Need Profile serve different purposes

A Resource Profile describes what a Workplace has or can access.

It may include:

- active customer relationships;
- verified trademark Inventory;
- authorized Listings;
- content and audience channels;
- internal capabilities;
- reviewer capacity;
- professional qualifications;
- Provider relationships;
- language and jurisdiction coverage;
- private data and knowledge;
- available budget or transaction capacity.

A Need Profile describes unresolved demand or weakness.

It may include:

- insufficient content output;
- stale Inventory;
- buyer demand without matching supply;
- customer follow-up backlog;
- missing professional authority;
- service opportunities requiring verification;
- capability gaps;
- insufficient review capacity;
- overdependence on one Provider;
- weak inquiry conversion;
- poor customer retention.

```text
Resource Profile
≠ Need Profile
```

A Workplace may possess a resource but still need more current capacity. It may lack an internal capability but already have a trusted external route. It may own trademarks but lack authority to sell them. It may have a large audience but no consent to use customer data for promotion.

## 3. Four illustrative Workplace patterns

### Marketing-led Workplace

This Workplace attracts customers through educational and commercial content.

Today may emphasize:

- verified topics derived from current professional objects;
- content gaps by jurisdiction or service;
- underused case knowledge that can be safely generalized;
- trademarks suitable for concept commercialization;
- channel performance;
- inquiries generated from previous content;
- customer follow-up linked to published topics.

The Product should not reward content volume alone. It should prioritize usefulness, factual grounding, audience fit and downstream relationship quality.

### Asset-led Workplace

This Workplace manages owned or entrusted trademarks.

Today may emphasize:

- expiring Resale Authorization;
- stale Listings;
- incomplete asset evidence;
- buyer Requests with strong fit;
- price-review needs;
- authorization restrictions;
- unanswered Inquiries;
- transaction due-diligence gaps.

The Product must not treat every trademark record as sellable Inventory.

### Service-led Workplace

This Workplace earns from professional service and lifecycle management.

Today may emphasize:

- customer obligations;
- deadlines;
- maintenance candidates;
- ownership-change signals;
- missing documents;
- unresolved Provider returns;
- customer communication;
- MarkReg or MGSN route preparation.

Content and commerce may support growth, but they should not displace active service responsibility.

### Capability-led Person or team

This Workplace or Personal Workplace emphasizes professional development and bounded contribution.

Today may emphasize:

- relevant simulation;
- expiring capability evidence;
- supervised production opportunities;
- paid Work Packages;
- review feedback;
- mentor or reviewer availability;
- tasks blocked by missing Production Authorization.

The Product must distinguish genuine training from paid production work.

## 4. Most Workplaces are hybrids

The patterns above are not permanent organization types.

A small agency may be marketing-led during customer acquisition, service-led during a filing season and asset-led when a buyer campaign begins. One team member may use Lite primarily for content, another for customer follow-up and another for capability review.

Adaptation therefore requires multiple levels:

```text
Organization strategy
+ Workplace configuration
+ Product Installation policy
+ active pipeline
+ current capacity
+ Person role and Membership
= current daily context
```

No single profile should permanently label the business.

## 5. Priorities must change with operating state

A Workplace may deliberately prioritize content growth, but a sudden deadline incident should change the day.

Dynamic factors include:

- approaching statutory deadlines;
- customer complaints;
- Provider silence;
- new buyer Requests;
- authorization expiry;
- seasonal demand;
- staff absence;
- reviewer overload;
- cash-flow constraints;
- campaign launch;
- regulatory or platform changes;
- security incidents.

The Product should distinguish stable strategy from temporary operating state.

A strategy preference may guide ranking. A critical incident may override it.

## 6. Capacity changes what is useful

A recommendation can be relevant but unusable.

A Workplace may have ten content opportunities but no design capacity. It may see a buyer Request but lack time to verify ownership. It may identify maintenance leads but have no staff authorized to contact customers. It may have internal capability but insufficient review bandwidth.

Lite should consider:

- current workload;
- role availability;
- reviewer capacity;
- external Provider capacity;
- expected effort;
- deadline and recovery time;
- dependency on missing documents;
- ability to complete the next step.

```text
Relevant opportunity
≠ executable opportunity
```

Where readiness is incomplete, Today should propose the missing enabling action rather than the final action.

## 7. Workplace policy must remain explicit

A Workplace may choose policies such as:

- never publicly list customer-owned trademarks;
- promote only assets with exclusive authorization;
- require professional review for jurisdiction-specific content;
- avoid cold outreach;
- prioritize existing customers over new leads;
- restrict AI image generation for certain brands;
- use MarkReg only in white-label mode;
- route particular jurisdictions through existing Providers;
- reserve specific capabilities for senior reviewers.

These policies should be visible, versioned and explainable.

The Product should not silently infer sensitive commercial policy from behavior and treat it as permanent authority.

## 8. The Person's context matters, but cannot override the Workplace

Within the same Workplace, different Persons may see different actions based on:

- Membership role;
- Assignment;
- capability evidence;
- language;
- availability;
- authority;
- customer responsibility;
- review duties.

A junior contributor may see a suggested Work Package but not customer pricing. A relationship manager may see follow-up obligations but not privileged professional analysis. A qualified reviewer may see evidence requiring validation.

Personal relevance is constrained by Workplace policy and data scope.

```text
Personally relevant
≠ organizationally authorized
```

## 9. The danger of platform-shaped businesses

A platform may prefer behaviors that increase its own revenue:

- more public Listings;
- more routed transactions;
- more MarkReg handoffs;
- more promoted content;
- more paid capability tasks;
- more MGSN use.

If Lite adapts Workplaces only toward these behaviors, it is not supporting business sovereignty. It is reshaping independent firms into distribution channels for the platform.

The Product must preserve the Workplace's right to:

- keep Inventory private;
- serve customers internally;
- use existing Providers;
- decline public marketplace participation;
- avoid unsolicited outreach;
- choose slower but trusted routes;
- ignore opportunities that conflict with its strategy.

## 10. Cold-start adaptation

A new Workplace has limited history. The Product should not wait months before becoming useful, nor should it pretend to know the business immediately.

A responsible cold-start flow may ask a small number of high-value questions:

- What is the Workplace's primary business today?
- Which customers or matters require immediate attention?
- Does it manage trademarks for resale?
- Which channels does it use for content?
- Which jurisdictions and services are common?
- Does it permit public promotion or cold outreach?
- Which professional and review capabilities are available?
- Which existing tools or Providers should remain in use?

The Product can then offer provisional priorities labeled as such and improve them through explicit feedback and outcome evidence.

## 11. Adaptation should be contestable

Users should be able to understand why Lite believes their Workplace is content-led, asset-constrained or overloaded.

They should be able to correct:

- inaccurate resource assumptions;
- stale capacity;
- wrongly inferred policy;
- misclassified opportunities;
- missing customer obligations;
- outdated strategic priorities.

A Workplace should also be able to reset or review adaptation history.

Without contestability, personalization becomes an invisible governance layer.

## 12. Example: one signal, three different recommendations

Suppose the system detects that a United States registration is entering a maintenance window.

For a service-led agency with an active customer relationship, Today may recommend:

> Verify current ownership and use evidence, then prepare customer follow-up.

For an asset-led Workplace authorized to sell the mark but not manage maintenance, Today may recommend:

> Confirm whether the owner intends to maintain the registration; update Listing status and disclose the approaching deadline to qualified buyers.

For a capability-led contributor without customer authority, Today may recommend:

> Review a de-identified maintenance evidence simulation or accept a bounded verification task if assigned.

The underlying signal is the same. The lawful and commercially useful action is different.

## 13. Metrics for adaptive value

Useful measures include:

- percentage of recommendations relevant to the current Workplace strategy;
- percentage executable with current readiness and capacity;
- correction rate for inferred policy;
- time to useful cold-start recommendations;
- reduction in irrelevant notifications;
- coverage of critical obligations;
- conversion of verified opportunities;
- user understanding of why an item appears;
- preservation of customer and asset boundaries.

A high click-through rate alone does not prove correct adaptation.

## 14. Failure modes

Workplace adaptation fails when:

- a permanent business type is inferred from temporary behavior;
- user clicks outweigh formal obligations;
- the platform optimizes every Workplace toward public marketplace activity;
- capacity is ignored;
- resource presence is treated as authority to use;
- personal preferences override organizational policy;
- cold-start questions are excessive or collect sensitive data too early;
- policy inference is invisible and irreversible;
- one Person's behavior trains recommendations for unrelated Workplaces;
- cross-Workplace data is reused without rights or purpose.

## 15. Product and technical implications

Adaptive Today requires:

- Resource and Need Profiles;
- stable and dynamic priority layers;
- Workplace policy records;
- current capacity and readiness;
- Person role and Membership context;
- Product Installation configuration;
- explicit cold-start answers;
- provisional inference with confidence;
- correction and reset;
- source and rights boundaries;
- explainable ranking.

The system should store not only the recommendation but the context under which it was made.

## 16. The operating principle

Lite should adapt to the business the Workplace is actually operating, not the business model the platform would prefer it to become.

> Different Workplaces need different mornings because professional value depends on resources, obligations, authority, capacity and strategy—not on a universal feed.
