# Chapter 24 — Lite-to-MarkReg Service Handoff Without Customer Transfer

Lite discovers and qualifies service opportunity. MarkReg may deliver the complete trademark lifecycle.

That distinction becomes difficult at the moment of handoff. A user may click “proceed,” a customer may approve a quotation, or a partner may send an instruction sheet. If the system treats that event as an informal transfer of the entire customer record, relationship ownership, professional authority and business state can become confused.

The handoff should instead be explicit and typed.

```text
Qualified service opportunity
→ customer instruction
→ service scope and commercial approval
→ Lite-to-MarkReg Handoff
→ MarkReg intake and readiness
→ Engagement and professional route
→ authorized execution
→ evidence return
→ customer-facing outcome
```

The customer relationship can remain with the originating Workplace while MarkReg performs the bounded service.

## 1. Lite and MarkReg solve different problems

Lite primarily helps a Workplace:

- discover service needs;
- resolve entities and contacts;
- communicate responsibly;
- explain options;
- prepare quotations;
- obtain customer instruction;
- organize commercial opportunity;
- follow up outcomes.

MarkReg may:

- create and govern Matter and Order state;
- collect filing information and documents;
- apply jurisdictional workflow;
- coordinate professional review;
- appoint or instruct Providers through authorized routes;
- manage deadlines, official fees and evidence;
- validate Provider returns;
- maintain formal lifecycle records.

```text
Opportunity authority
≠ formal Matter authority
```

## 2. Handoff is a contract between product contexts

A Handoff should contain a structured package rather than a link to an unbounded customer workspace.

The package may include:

- originating Workplace;
- Product Installation;
- customer and Relationship Owner;
- represented Organization;
- service opportunity and source;
- customer instruction evidence;
- approved service scope;
- jurisdiction and mark references;
- requested outcome;
- required deadline;
- known facts and Unknowns;
- documents and data permitted for transfer;
- quotation and accepted commercial terms;
- communication rules;
- partner or white-label context;
- professional and Provider constraints;
- expected return evidence;
- unresolved exceptions;
- Handoff version and expiry.

MarkReg should accept, reject or request correction rather than silently importing an incomplete package.

## 3. Customer relationship and delivery responsibility remain separable

Possible structures include:

### Originating Workplace retains relationship

The originating Workplace communicates with the customer while MarkReg performs backend workflow and professional coordination.

### MarkReg disclosed as service operator

The customer knows MarkReg is coordinating the service, but the original Workplace remains the commercial Relationship Owner.

### Co-delivery

Both parties have defined communication and delivery roles.

### Direct MarkReg engagement

The customer explicitly establishes a direct relationship with MarkReg. This requires an express transition, not an implied effect of Handoff.

```text
MarkReg receives service data
≠ MarkReg owns future customer relationship
```

## 4. Customer instruction must be evidenced

Acceptable instruction evidence may include:

- signed instruction sheet;
- approved quotation;
- confirmed portal action;
- authenticated email;
- recorded customer approval;
- partner-provided instruction under an existing agreement;
- signed POA where relevant.

The system should distinguish:

```text
Interest
Request for quotation
Approval in principle
Customer instruction
Engagement acceptance
Execution authorization
```

A request to “send more information” should not create a filing Matter.

## 5. Scope must be explicit

The Handoff should state exactly what service is requested, for example:

- renewal review only;
- Section 8 declaration preparation;
- combined Section 8 and 9 filing;
- ownership-change recordal;
- certificate retrieval;
- abandoned-mark re-filing;
- status investigation;
- local counsel quotation;
- full filing through registration;
- evidence review without filing.

Scope should also identify exclusions and dependencies.

```text
Customer instructed maintenance review
≠ customer instructed every available declaration
```

## 6. Commercial terms should not be inferred from the lead

The package should distinguish:

- official fees;
- professional fees;
- originating Workplace fee;
- MarkReg service fee;
- Provider fee;
- taxes and disbursements;
- currency and exchange assumptions;
- payment timing;
- refund or additional-cost rules;
- commission or white-label margin;
- who invoices whom;
- who bears fee changes.

No Product should silently rewrite these terms during Handoff.

## 7. Data transfer should be minimum necessary

MarkReg may need:

- customer identity;
- owner or applicant details;
- mark and registration information;
- goods and services;
- relevant documents;
- deadlines;
- contact and instruction evidence;
- prior official correspondence.

It may not need:

- the originating Workplace’s full customer portfolio;
- unrelated communications;
- private pricing strategy;
- all marketing history;
- unrelated AI memory;
- other customer matters.

```text
Handoff access
≠ full Workplace access
```

## 8. MarkReg intake should revalidate readiness

Even a qualified Lite opportunity may be incomplete for execution.

MarkReg should check:

- correct jurisdiction and procedure;
- official status and dates;
- customer and owner identity;
- signing authority;
- required POA or originals;
- goods, classes and mark data;
- professional review requirements;
- payment readiness;
- Provider eligibility;
- conflicts;
- document quality;
- deadline feasibility.

Possible intake outcomes include:

```text
Accepted for Preparation
Additional Information Required
Professional Review Required
Payment Required
Provider Route Required
Customer Reconfirmation Required
Rejected — No Viable Route
Returned for Correction
```

Handoff completion does not mean execution readiness.

## 9. Engagement and appointment remain separate

MarkReg may require:

- customer Service Engagement;
- internal Work Package;
- qualified professional Assignment;
- Provider acceptance;
- local-agent appointment;
- conflict check;
- POA;
- payment authorization;
- filing approval.

Continue to preserve:

```text
Handoff Accepted
≠ Provider Appointed
≠ Filing Authorized
≠ External Protected Action Permitted
```

## 10. White-label delivery requires explicit communication rules

A white-label Handoff should record:

- customer-facing brand;
- originating Relationship Owner;
- whether MarkReg is disclosed;
- legally required professional disclosures;
- who sends updates;
- who approves quotations and additional fees;
- who receives customer documents;
- who handles complaints;
- who explains professional outcomes;
- whether direct customer contact is permitted;
- what happens if urgent direct contact is necessary.

White-label does not justify hiding a legally required attorney or agent identity.

## 11. Evidence return is part of the Handoff contract

Before execution, the originating Workplace should know what MarkReg will return.

Possible evidence includes:

- intake confirmation;
- accepted instruction summary;
- document checklist;
- Provider acceptance;
- official filing receipt;
- payment receipt;
- filed document copy;
- official notice;
- status update;
- certificate;
- completion report;
- exception and recovery record.

```text
MarkReg says “completed”
≠ evidence-return contract satisfied
```

## 12. Formal state must be validated before projection back to Lite

A Provider email or uploaded receipt is evidence input. MarkReg’s Owning Service should validate it before the result becomes formal Matter state.

Lite may then receive a controlled projection such as:

```text
Instruction Accepted
Filing Prepared
Customer Approval Required
Filed — Receipt Validated
Official Review Pending
Additional Action Required
Completed — Evidence Available
Closed with Exception
```

Lite should not derive formal status directly from an email summary.

## 13. Communication loops must stay aligned

A common failure occurs when MarkReg communicates directly with one participant while Lite continues showing outdated information to another.

The Handoff should define:

- authoritative update source;
- customer-facing sender;
- update frequency;
- urgent exception route;
- partner notification rules;
- who approves additional costs;
- who records customer decisions;
- how corrections propagate.

The same customer instruction should not be interpreted differently by the two Products without an explicit conflict state.

## 14. Changes require amendment, not silent scope growth

During execution, circumstances may change:

- official fees increase;
- original documents become necessary;
- goods need amendment;
- another class is recommended;
- a refusal appears;
- restoration is unavailable;
- re-filing becomes necessary;
- a Provider changes;
- the deadline becomes infeasible.

MarkReg should create a typed change or exception requiring the appropriate approval.

```text
New recommendation
≠ amended customer instruction

Additional fee identified
≠ fee approved
```

## 15. Cancellation and return should preserve responsibility

If the customer withdraws, the Provider declines or the route becomes impossible, the Handoff should close with:

- current state;
- work completed;
- fees incurred;
- documents held;
- evidence returned;
- pending deadlines;
- refund or balance treatment;
- customer communication responsibility;
- suppression or future follow-up rules.

The opportunity should not disappear merely because execution failed.

## 16. Data and memory after completion

After completion:

- formal Matter history remains in the Owning Service;
- the originating Workplace receives the agreed evidence and customer-facing result;
- customer relationship memory remains governed by the originating Workplace context;
- MarkReg retains records required for delivery, audit and law;
- learning candidates require separate rights and de-identification review;
- private pricing and partner terms do not become shared model training data automatically.

## 17. Example: US maintenance instruction

Lite identifies a verified maintenance need for an existing customer.

The originating Workplace:

1. confirms the official window;
2. explains available filing options;
3. receives customer instruction for a specified declaration;
4. records accepted fees and communication rules;
5. creates the Handoff package.

MarkReg then:

1. revalidates registration data;
2. checks evidence and signer requirements;
3. prepares the filing;
4. requests customer approval where required;
5. routes the protected action through an authorized professional;
6. validates the official receipt;
7. returns the receipt and status projection.

The original Workplace remains the customer-facing relationship owner unless a direct transition was expressly agreed.

## 18. Example: partner white-label filing

A partner submits a customer instruction under its own brand.

The Handoff records:

- partner as Relationship Owner;
- MarkReg as backend service orchestrator;
- local attorney as Professional Authority where required;
- no unrestricted direct marketing;
- partner approval for extra fees;
- evidence returned to partner;
- customer-facing update prepared in partner branding;
- legally required professional identity still disclosed.

## 19. Failure modes

This model fails when:

- clicking “proceed” transfers the entire customer record;
- incomplete lead data creates a formal Matter automatically;
- customer interest is treated as filing authority;
- Handoff gives MarkReg access to unrelated Workplace data;
- Provider appointment is assumed;
- white-label hides required professional identity;
- extra work and fees are added without amendment;
- Provider messages update formal state without validation;
- evidence return is optional;
- customer and partner communications diverge;
- completion data becomes shared AI memory without rights review;
- failed execution erases responsibility and deadline history.

## 20. Product and technical implications

The integration requires:

- versioned Handoff objects;
- typed source opportunity and customer instruction;
- scope, exclusion and deadline fields;
- data-scope and document grants;
- relationship and communication policy;
- commercial-term package;
- intake validation;
- Engagement and appointment gates;
- amendment and exception workflow;
- evidence-return contract;
- formal-state projection from MarkReg to Lite;
- cancellation and closure states;
- audit, retention and learning boundaries.

The interface should be reusable for other Product-to-Product handoffs without erasing each Product’s authority boundary.

## 21. Commercial implications

A disciplined Handoff enables:

- Lite-led customer discovery;
- MarkReg lifecycle revenue;
- partner white-label service;
- co-delivery;
- professional Provider coordination;
- clear margin and commission treatment;
- improved evidence and customer trust;
- repeatable cross-border delivery.

The platform should not increase conversion by obscuring who serves the customer, who is professionally responsible or what has been authorized.

## 22. The operating principle

```text
Lite discovers and qualifies
The customer instructs
The Handoff limits context
MarkReg validates and orchestrates
Professionals act under authority
Evidence returns
The originating relationship remains protected
```

> A Product handoff should move the work that must be done, not silently move the customer, the relationship or the authority to act.
