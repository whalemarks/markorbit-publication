# Chapter 30 — Transaction and Human-service Revenue Must Remain Explicit

Subscription does not cover every kind of value created through Lite.

A trademark transaction, professional review, certification assessment or MarkReg delivery can involve human judgment, external fees, legal responsibility, negotiated commissions and failure risk. These should not be hidden inside a vague platform charge.

```text
Subscription revenue
≠ transaction revenue
≠ professional-service revenue
≠ official or Provider cost
≠ contributor compensation
```

The distinctions protect customers, Workplaces, professionals and the platform itself.

## 1. Revenue follows a defined role

Before charging, the system should identify the role that creates the charge.

Possible roles include:

- Product provider;
- Listing promoter;
- Demand or seller introducer;
- transaction coordinator;
- co-broker;
- Relationship Owner;
- Contracting Party;
- professional reviewer;
- Capability assessor;
- contributor;
- MarkReg service operator;
- MGSN Provider;
- official authority or external vendor.

A payment description should not imply a role the recipient does not actually hold.

## 2. Revenue categories

### Subscription

Pays for ongoing Lite operating capability.

### Promotion and media

May pay for disclosed enhanced visibility, premium production or campaign services.

### Transaction-related revenue

May include:

- referral fee;
- seller-side commission;
- buyer-side advisory fee;
- co-brokerage share;
- transaction coordination fee;
- transfer-support fee;
- due-diligence fee.

### Human professional service

May include:

- factual review;
- trademark-continuity review;
- commercialization review;
- legal or professional opinion where permitted;
- translation review;
- assessment and certification;
- specialist consultation.

### Paid Contribution

Compensates a person or Workplace for a bounded Work Package.

### External and official cost

May include:

- official fees;
- local Provider fees;
- verification fees;
- courier, notarization or legalization;
- licensed data or media;
- payment-provider costs.

Each category needs its own basis, recipient, trigger and evidence.

## 3. Displayed price must be decomposable

A customer or partner may see a combined commercial quotation, but the system should still understand its components:

```text
Asset price
+ seller-side or buyer-side service
+ professional review
+ transfer or filing service
+ official fees
+ external Provider cost
+ tax or payment cost
```

This does not require exposing every confidential margin to every participant. It requires that the responsible Workplace can explain what is being charged and why.

## 4. Commission must preserve provenance

Transaction revenue depends on who introduced what.

The system should retain:

- asset source;
- seller relationship;
- buyer source;
- Demand Originator;
- matching contribution;
- Inquiry source;
- protected buyer or campaign;
- agreed commission basis;
- term and survival period;
- amendments;
- payment trigger.

```text
Platform visibility
≠ platform ownership of every commission
```

A shared system should not erase the business contribution of the originating Workplace.

## 5. Platform self-interest must be disclosed

Lite may recommend:

- a paid Listing;
- MarkReg transfer support;
- a professional reviewer;
- an MGSN route;
- certification;
- premium media production.

Where the platform, its self-operated Workplace or an affiliated party earns from a recommended route, that interest should be visible at an appropriate decision point.

```text
Recommended
≠ commercially neutral by default
```

Disclosure does not make an unsuitable route acceptable. Eligibility, fit and customer choice still govern.

## 6. Paid promotion cannot purchase factual status

A seller may pay for greater visibility. Payment cannot create:

- ownership;
- valid Resale Authorization;
- current legal status;
- transaction readiness;
- professional endorsement;
- buyer fit;
- higher trust evidence.

Promoted placement should be labeled and remain subject to the same eligibility and correction rules.

## 7. Human service should state who is responsible

A paid human review should identify:

- reviewer or service organization;
- qualification and Capability scope;
- represented Workplace;
- review purpose;
- material exclusions;
- expected deliverable;
- whether the result is advice, preparation, quality review or formal professional work;
- revision and complaint route.

The phrase “expert review” is insufficient when the user cannot tell what was actually reviewed.

## 8. Contributor compensation and platform revenue are different

A customer may pay the Workplace or platform for an Outcome. A contributor may receive payment for one Work Package. The difference should be explicit.

```text
Customer price
≠ contributor compensation
```

The platform may charge for orchestration, infrastructure, review and recovery. It should not hide the contributor's agreed compensation or change it after acceptance without governed amendment.

## 9. Certification revenue needs independence

Payment for instruction, assessment or certification administration must not influence the result.

Controls may include:

- published criteria;
- separate assessor role;
- evidence retention;
- appeal;
- no automatic pass for subscription level;
- no sales commission based on pass rate.

```text
Revenue from certification
≠ authority to sell passing outcomes
```

## 10. MarkReg revenue follows the service model

When Lite hands work to MarkReg, the commercial route may be:

- originating Workplace white-label delivery;
- referral;
- co-delivery;
- direct customer transition by explicit agreement;
- self-operated MarkReg Workplace service;
- MGSN-assisted fulfillment.

Each route should define:

- Contracting Party;
- Relationship Owner;
- Payment Receiver;
- Delivery Owner;
- Professional Authority;
- Provider cost;
- platform or MarkReg fee;
- customer communication;
- refund and failure responsibility.

Payment to MarkReg does not automatically transfer the customer relationship.

## 11. MGSN revenue must not imply Provider ownership

MGSN may create revenue through managed routing, orchestration, trust, evidence return or fulfillment support. The network remains platform-managed, but participant customer relationships and Provider organizations do not become platform property.

A Workplace accesses MGSN through a governed Connection. It does not buy unrestricted access to the full Provider pool or direct contacts.

## 12. Payment milestones must not collapse business state

The system may record:

- quotation issued;
- deposit requested;
- payment initiated;
- payment confirmed;
- fee allocated;
- Provider payable;
- contributor payable;
- refund pending;
- settlement completed.

These events do not prove:

- customer instruction;
- service completion;
- official filing;
- transfer of ownership;
- professional acceptance.

```text
Paid
≠ completed
```

## 13. This architecture does not authorize custody

Lite may display and reconcile payment references. This manuscript does not authorize MarkOrbit to hold customer funds, operate escrow, net unrelated obligations or act as unrestricted payment principal.

Any future custody or escrow model would require separate legal, financial, operational and canonical decisions.

## 14. Refund and failure responsibility must follow the route

Failures may arise from:

- seller withdrawal;
- buyer withdrawal;
- invalid authority;
- professional refusal;
- official rejection;
- Provider non-performance;
- missing originals;
- incorrect source data;
- platform or rendering failure.

The responsible party, refundable component, non-refundable work and dispute route should be established before the failure occurs.

## 15. Product and technical implications

Lite requires:

- typed revenue and fee records;
- role and relationship provenance;
- quotation versioning;
- commission ledger;
- promoted-placement labels;
- related-party disclosure;
- contributor compensation records;
- professional service scope;
- milestone and evidence linkage;
- refund and dispute workflow;
- separation from formal business state.

## 16. Commercial implications

Multiple revenue sources can strengthen Lite's economics, but only when users understand the difference between software, promotion, transaction support, professional work and external costs.

The final rates, percentages and service bundles remain open Product decisions. The architectural requirement is transparency of role, basis, recipient, trigger and responsibility.

## 17. Operating principle

> Revenue should follow an identifiable contribution and an accountable role. Lite must never use one opaque fee to hide commercial interest, professional responsibility, external cost or the compensation owed to the people who perform the work.
