# Chapter 20 — Transfer Handoff, Evidence and Completion

A trademark transaction does not end when the parties agree on a price.

It does not necessarily end when they sign an agreement, when money moves, when an assignment document is executed or when an agent files a recordal request. Each event matters, but none should be used as a shortcut for every other state.

Lite should coordinate the commercial transaction while handing formal legal execution to MarkReg or another qualified and authorized service route.

```text
Commercial agreement
→ execution plan
→ payment and condition milestones
→ signed transfer documents
→ official recordal submission
→ evidence return
→ formal-state validation
→ completion and post-transfer obligations
```

## 1. Completion must be decomposed

The system should distinguish:

```text
Commercial terms agreed
Agreement signed
Payment initiated
Payment settled
Assignment document signed
Assets delivered
Recordal filed
Recordal accepted
Official owner updated
Transaction closed
Post-closing obligations completed
```

These events may occur in different orders and may be governed by different parties.

```text
Agreement Signed ≠ Transfer Recorded
Payment Made ≠ Title Validated
Recordal Filed ≠ Recordal Accepted
Seller Email ≠ Official Completion Evidence
```

## 2. Lite-to-MarkReg Handoff

Lite may originate the Opportunity and preserve commercial relationships. MarkReg may receive a governed Handoff for:

- final party and asset details;
- due-diligence findings;
- approved transaction structure;
- signed or required documents;
- jurisdictional recordal work;
- official fee handling;
- deadline and evidence tracking;
- exception recovery;
- final official-state validation.

The Handoff should state:

- originating Workplace;
- Relationship Owners;
- contracting parties;
- represented roles;
- selected professional route;
- scope and exclusions;
- data and document access;
- agreed commercial terms relevant to execution;
- required approvals;
- open risks and conditions;
- return evidence;
- customer communication rules.

```text
Handoff
≠ Customer Relationship Transfer
```

## 3. Engagement and appointment remain explicit

The receiving Product or Provider should not begin protected professional work merely because an Opportunity exists.

Required events may include:

- service Engagement;
- Provider acceptance;
- conflict clearance;
- professional appointment;
- power of attorney;
- payment authorization;
- instruction approval;
- data-access grant.

The exact requirements vary by jurisdiction and service model.

## 4. Payment milestones are separate from legal state

Possible payment structures include:

- full payment before signing;
- deposit and balance;
- escrow;
- staged release;
- holdback pending recordal;
- direct buyer-to-seller payment;
- separate service-fee payments.

Lite should record the intended structure without implying that the platform is authorized to hold funds.

Payment events should show:

- payer;
- payee;
- amount and currency;
- purpose;
- milestone;
- payment provider;
- status;
- evidence;
- refund or release condition.

```text
Payment instruction ≠ payment custody
Payment confirmation ≠ legal transfer completion
```

## 5. Document execution

The transfer package may require:

- assignment agreement;
- assignment deed or form;
- powers of attorney;
- corporate authority evidence;
- signatures and notarization;
- legalization or apostille;
- translations;
- certificates;
- tax or stamp documents;
- declarations concerning goodwill or use;
- local-agent forms.

Lite can coordinate readiness, but jurisdictional requirements should come from governed Knowledge and qualified professional review.

## 6. Evidence-return contract

Every execution route should define what evidence must return.

Examples include:

- signed agreement;
- payment receipt;
- executed assignment;
- courier tracking;
- filing acknowledgment;
- official receipt;
- recordal application number;
- office notice;
- updated registry extract;
- updated certificate;
- professional completion report.

A Provider message saying “done” is not sufficient where stronger evidence is expected.

```text
Provider Return
≠ Validated Formal State
```

## 7. Owning Service validation

The Owning Service should evaluate returned evidence and update formal business state only when the relevant criteria are met.

Possible validations include:

- correct parties and mark;
- correct jurisdiction and registration number;
- correct execution date;
- signature completeness;
- official receipt authenticity and relation;
- recordal acceptance;
- official owner update;
- unresolved office requirement;
- final certificate or extract.

Lite may display progress, but should not become the authoritative source of official transfer status merely because it initiated the deal.

## 8. Exception states

Transfer workflows often encounter:

- missing original documents;
- incorrect names or addresses;
- insufficient signing authority;
- rejected notarization;
- expired power of attorney;
- fee increase;
- registry delay;
- office objection;
- owner mismatch;
- payment dispute;
- lost courier;
- seller or buyer withdrawal;
- provider non-response.

The system should record:

- exception type;
- affected milestone;
- responsibility;
- deadline;
- proposed remedy;
- approval and cost impact;
- evidence;
- escalation and recovery route.

## 9. Transaction closing is a governed decision

Closing should require a defined evidence set, which may include:

- commercial obligations satisfied;
- required payments settled or released;
- required assets delivered;
- transfer documents executed;
- recordal filed or completed according to agreed closing definition;
- customer notified;
- commissions calculated;
- service invoices resolved;
- open risks accepted or transferred to post-closing obligations.

The parties may agree that commercial closing occurs before official recordal completes. If so, Lite should show both states separately.

## 10. Post-closing obligations

After closing, the system may track:

- official recordal completion;
- certificate delivery;
- domain or account transfer;
- brand asset delivery;
- seller transition support;
- additional country filings;
- renewals or declarations;
- removal of old Listings;
- public ownership updates;
- commission settlement;
- retained confidentiality and non-circumvention obligations.

A sale should not leave stale public Listings or active Resale Authorizations.

## 11. Commission settlement

Commission and service fees should be calculated from the accepted agreement and attribution ledger, not reconstructed from memory after closing.

The settlement record may include:

- source commission;
- buyer-side fee;
- seller-side fee;
- co-broker shares;
- transaction support fee;
- professional service fees;
- platform fee;
- taxes and payment costs;
- trigger and settlement evidence.

A commission may be payable on agreement, payment, official recordal or another milestone. The trigger must be explicit.

## 12. Relationship continuity after transfer

The transaction may lead to:

- maintenance services;
- additional filings;
- brand development;
- monitoring;
- licensing;
- enforcement;
- website and content work.

These opportunities do not automatically belong to the platform or execution Provider.

The original Relationship Owner, customer instructions and new Engagements should govern follow-on services.

## 13. Example

The parties agree to transfer a US trademark for USD 10,000. They sign an agreement, the buyer pays into an external escrow service, and the seller signs an assignment. A US attorney files the assignment recordal.

Lite should show:

- agreement signed;
- escrow funded;
- assignment executed;
- recordal filed with receipt;
- official owner update pending;
- commercial closing condition defined in the agreement;
- final commission release condition;
- post-closing certificate or registry-extract task.

It should not show “Transfer Completed” solely because the attorney filed the recordal.

## 14. AI assistance and limits

AI may assist with:

- milestone planning;
- document completeness checks;
- extraction and comparison;
- reminder generation;
- evidence classification;
- exception summarization;
- communication drafts;
- post-closing task generation.

AI should not:

- determine that signatures are legally effective without review;
- authorize fund release;
- appoint a Provider;
- file protected actions autonomously;
- mark official recordal complete from an unverified message;
- alter contractual terms;
- decide commission disputes.

## 15. Failure modes

The model fails when:

- “sold” replaces all transaction states;
- payment is treated as title transfer;
- filing is treated as acceptance;
- Lite silently becomes payment custodian;
- Handoff transfers the customer relationship;
- execution begins without Engagement or appointment;
- Providers return messages without evidence;
- formal state is updated without validation;
- exceptions are handled outside the record;
- stale Listings remain visible after sale;
- commission triggers remain ambiguous;
- post-closing obligations disappear.

## 16. Product and technical implications

The architecture requires:

- typed Handoff objects;
- Engagement and appointment gates;
- transaction milestone model;
- payment references without assumed custody;
- document-readiness and execution tracking;
- evidence-return contracts;
- Provider-return validation;
- formal-state authority separation;
- exception and recovery workflows;
- closing criteria;
- post-closing obligations;
- commission settlement;
- source Listing and Authorization closure.

## 17. Commercial implications

A reliable transfer workflow creates value through:

- reduced failure and delay;
- professional service revenue;
- transaction coordination;
- transparent commission settlement;
- better partner trust;
- repeat customer services;
- stronger evidence for disputes and recovery.

The platform should not overstate completion to accelerate revenue recognition or commission release.

## 18. The operating principle

```text
Agreement is not recordal
Payment is not title
Filing is not acceptance
Provider return is not formal state
Handoff is not relationship transfer
Completion requires defined evidence
```

> A trustworthy transaction system does not ask everyone to believe that the deal is done. It shows exactly which commercial, documentary, payment and official milestones have actually been completed.
