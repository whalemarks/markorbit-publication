# Chapter 11 — Listing as a Controlled Commercial Projection

A Listing is not the trademark and not the sale itself.

It is a purpose-limited commercial projection built from governed supply, current authority and selected disclosure.

```text
Trademark
→ Trademark Inventory
→ Resale Authorization
→ Listing
→ Inquiry
→ Transaction Opportunity
```

Collapsing these objects creates false availability, unclear relationship ownership and uncontrolled disclosure. Keeping them separate allows Lite to expose enough information to test demand without surrendering the owner, source Workplace or transaction route.

## 1. What a Listing actually represents

A Listing answers:

- Which asset is being presented?
- Under which active authorization?
- For which audience and channel?
- What facts and concepts may be shown?
- What price or inquiry rule applies?
- Who receives and qualifies inquiries?
- What happens when the source changes?
- When does the Listing expire?

A Listing should not answer questions that belong to due diligence or formal transfer with invented certainty.

## 2. The Listing is a projection, not a duplicate legal record

The underlying Trademark remains governed by its authoritative source. The Listing selects and explains only the fields necessary for the commercial purpose.

A Listing may include:

- mark image and name;
- jurisdiction;
- selected status information;
- classes and simplified goods summary;
- verified source date;
- asking price or price-on-request;
- seller or intermediary context;
- brand concept and Marketing Pack assets;
- suitable industries;
- known restrictions;
- availability status;
- inquiry route;
- concept and verification labels.

It should retain links to the full governed source rather than copying facts into an independent truth.

## 3. Listing creation requires an active route

Before activation, Lite should verify:

- Inventory is current;
- Resale Authorization is active;
- the proposed audience and channel are permitted;
- price presentation is authorized;
- owner identity treatment is permitted;
- Marketing Pack claims are reviewed;
- legal-status freshness meets policy;
- inquiry routing and Relationship Owner are defined;
- expiry and revalidation are scheduled.

```text
Complete Marketing Pack
≠ Listing may be activated
```

## 4. Listing states should be explicit

A useful lifecycle is:

```text
Draft
→ authority review
→ content review
→ approved
→ active
→ paused
→ reserved
→ transaction pending
→ sold / withdrawn / expired
→ archived
```

A Listing may also be rejected before activation.

Paused should preserve history and public references while preventing new inquiries or clearly displaying temporary unavailability, according to policy.

## 5. Availability needs precise language

Common marketplace labels such as “available” hide several states.

Lite may need:

- available for inquiry;
- seller confirmation required;
- price confirmation required;
- private negotiation only;
- reserved for named buyer;
- transaction pending;
- temporarily paused;
- authority revalidation pending;
- withdrawn;
- sold subject to recordal;
- transfer completed.

```text
Buyer and seller agreed terms
≠ legal transfer completed
```

## 6. Price presentation is a controlled field

A Listing may use:

- fixed asking price;
- negotiable asking price;
- price range;
- price on request;
- owner net price hidden;
- buyer-side service fee disclosed separately;
- campaign price with expiry;
- no price during private demand testing.

The public number should not silently combine owner price, commission, transfer fee and platform fee without explanation to authorized participants.

Suggested pricing should be labeled as an estimate or presentation recommendation, not an objective asset valuation.

## 7. The Listing must identify the commercial route

The visible seller context may be:

- owner-direct;
- exclusive representative;
- non-exclusive representative;
- network-sourced;
- co-brokered;
- platform-managed;
- confidential owner represented by named Workplace.

The public interface may simplify this language, but the internal record must preserve the actual role chain.

The Listing should also define:

- Inquiry Receiver;
- Relationship Owner;
- who may contact the owner;
- who qualifies the buyer;
- who may discuss price;
- who may open due diligence;
- who may create a Transaction Opportunity.

## 8. Marketing concepts need visible boundaries

A Listing may show product, packaging or store concepts to make the mark understandable.

These should remain distinguishable from:

- existing products;
- current business operations;
- proof of use;
- included physical assets;
- guaranteed legal coverage.

The Listing should state whether visual concepts are included in the transaction or merely promotional demonstrations.

## 9. Goods summaries must not distort the legal specification

Public users may need a concise goods summary. Simplification is useful but risky.

Lite should preserve:

- the source specification;
- the simplified public summary;
- reviewer or rule used for simplification;
- exclusions or limitations;
- link to fuller information where appropriate.

```text
“Clothing brand”
≠ every clothing good is necessarily covered
```

## 10. Inquiry is a new object, not a message count

When a person responds, Lite should create an Inquiry with:

- source Listing and version;
- source channel;
- buyer identity state;
- represented Workplace;
- requested asset;
- message and questions;
- contact consent;
- relationship provenance;
- attribution;
- qualification status;
- next action;
- spam or fraud indicators.

A social comment or click is not automatically a qualified Inquiry.

## 11. Listing version matters to later disputes

A buyer may rely on:

- the price shown;
- the status described;
- the included assets;
- an exclusivity statement;
- the owner context;
- a concept or factual claim.

Lite should preserve the Listing version visible when the Inquiry was created.

Later amendments should not erase what was represented earlier.

## 12. Source change propagation

Changes that may require pausing or revising a Listing include:

- legal status;
- owner identity;
- Authorization scope;
- price;
- commission;
- included assets;
- identity disclosure;
- transaction restriction;
- known dispute;
- Marketing Pack correction.

The dependency chain should identify every active output and channel affected.

## 13. Duplicate Listings require provenance, not blind deduplication

The same Trademark may have multiple legitimate Listings through non-exclusive or segmented authority.

Lite should compare:

- authorization chain;
- source Workplace;
- exclusivity;
- territory and channel;
- price;
- commission;
- owner identity treatment;
- active period;
- prior buyer introduction.

Possible outcomes include:

- technical duplicate merge;
- legitimate parallel Listings;
- conflict review;
- superseded Listing;
- fraudulent or unsupported Listing.

## 14. Ranking is a governance decision

Listing order can determine commercial success.

Ranking may consider:

- buyer-request fit;
- legal and authority freshness;
- seller responsiveness;
- price fit;
- Marketing Pack quality;
- historical inquiry quality;
- channel relevance;
- Workplace preference;
- paid promotion.

Paid promotion must be disclosed and must not override basic eligibility or authority requirements.

Platform-owned inventory should not receive hidden preference.

## 15. Listing quality should measure trust and usefulness

Useful quality dimensions include:

- source verification;
- active authority;
- price readiness;
- media and concept clarity;
- owner responsiveness;
- goods clarity;
- transaction restriction disclosure;
- correction history;
- inquiry response history.

A polished Listing with weak authority is low quality.

## 16. Reservation should not become ownership or completion

A Listing may be reserved when:

- an owner grants temporary exclusivity to a buyer;
- a deposit or agreed checkpoint is reached;
- due diligence begins;
- the seller pauses competing discussions.

Reservation should record:

- buyer or Opportunity;
- start and expiry;
- conditions;
- effect on other inquiries;
- approving party;
- cancellation route.

```text
Reserved
≠ sold
≠ transferred
```

## 17. Sold status should be staged

A transaction can progress through:

```text
Commercial terms agreed
→ agreement signed
→ funds checkpoint satisfied
→ assignment documents executed
→ recordal filed
→ recordal accepted where applicable
→ transaction closed
```

The Listing should not announce completed transfer at the first commercial agreement unless the label is qualified.

## 18. Withdrawal and archive

A Listing may be withdrawn because:

- owner changed strategy;
- authority ended;
- price changed materially;
- legal status changed;
- conflict arose;
- transaction completed elsewhere;
- owner became unresponsive;
- quality threshold was not met.

Archival records should preserve inquiries, attribution, versions and reasons without continuing public promotion.

## 19. Counterargument: Listings should be simple

A marketplace may argue that users only need a photo, name and price.

That interface can remain simple while the underlying object remains governed.

The buyer-facing page does not need to display every internal role and evidence field. The platform needs those fields to prevent stale, unauthorized and misleading supply.

Simplicity in presentation should come from structured complexity underneath, not from ignoring it.

## 20. Failure modes

The Listing system fails when:

- Listing is treated as the Trademark itself;
- activation ignores Authorization scope;
- “available” hides pending confirmation;
- price components are opaque;
- the inquiry receiver is undefined;
- concept images imply existing business;
- goods summaries expand legal coverage;
- Listing versions are overwritten;
- source changes do not pause derivatives;
- duplicates are merged without authority provenance;
- paid ranking is undisclosed;
- reservation is treated as sale;
- transaction agreement is treated as completed transfer.

## 21. Product and technical implications

Lite needs a Listing object with:

- source Inventory and Authorization;
- Marketing Pack and selected assets;
- audience and channel policy;
- disclosure fields;
- price presentation;
- commercial roles;
- inquiry routing;
- state and lifecycle;
- version history;
- expiry and revalidation;
- source dependency monitoring;
- ranking and promotion metadata;
- duplicate and conflict handling;
- reservation and transaction linkage;
- archive and correction controls.

## 22. Commercial implications

Governed Listings support:

- public or private marketplace activity;
- paid disclosed promotion;
- partner distribution;
- buyer-request matching;
- transaction coordination;
- premium Marketing Packs;
- seller services;
- MarkReg transfer support.

The commercial objective is qualified demand and reliable progression, not maximum Listing count.

## 23. The operating principle

```text
A Listing is a controlled statement of current commercial availability,
not a claim of ownership transfer,
not proof of legal availability,
and not the transaction itself.
```

> Lite should make supply visible without allowing visibility to erase authority, provenance, uncertainty or the stages still required before transfer.
