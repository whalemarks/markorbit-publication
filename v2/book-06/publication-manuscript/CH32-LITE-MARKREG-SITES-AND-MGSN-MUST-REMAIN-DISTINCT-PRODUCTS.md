# Chapter 32 — Lite, MarkReg, Sites and MGSN Must Remain Distinct Products

MarkOrbit becomes harder to govern when every useful capability is pulled into one product.

Lite, MarkReg, Sites and MGSN may share Core semantics and connect inside a Workplace. They do not become one application, one database or one authority merely because the user experiences continuity.

```text
Core provides shared semantic authority.
Workplace preserves business sovereignty.
Product Installation provides a governed operating boundary.
Owning Service preserves formal business state.
Typed Projection and Handoff connect products.
```

## 1. Lite is the daily opportunity and growth product

Lite is designed for independent trademark professionals and small teams whose daily economics depend on:

- credible content;
- governed trademark supply;
- buyer demand;
- matching and Inquiry;
- service opportunities;
- Capability growth;
- controlled access to deeper products and networks.

Lite answers:

> What deserves attention today, and what is the next safe action?

It is not a smaller MarkReg, a generic content generator, a public website or unrestricted Provider marketplace.

Lite may prepare, recommend, coordinate and project. It does not become formal authority for every Matter, transaction, payment or professional action it helps initiate.

## 2. MarkReg is the complete trademark service product

MarkReg handles complete service delivery and lifecycle work, including appropriate combinations of:

- customer intake;
- professional scope;
- Order and Matter management;
- documents and evidence;
- deadlines and official actions;
- Provider fulfillment;
- exceptions and recovery;
- formal-state evidence;
- service completion.

When MarkReg directly serves customers and bears commercial or professional responsibility, that operation belongs in a self-operated Workplace. It should not be hidden as a special tenant inside another Workplace or as an invisible platform role.

```text
Lite opportunity
≠ MarkReg Matter

Lite Handoff
≠ accepted MarkReg Engagement
```

MarkReg performs its own Intake, authority checks and Owning Service validation.

## 3. Sites is the public Projection product

Sites publishes governed external experiences for a Workplace, including:

- brand and service pages;
- professional content;
- controlled Listings;
- forms and inquiries;
- customer-facing status or document views where authorized;
- campaign and landing pages.

Sites is not the Workplace itself. It is a Product Projection generated from selected Workplace and Product objects.

```text
Public form submitted
≠ Customer accepted
≠ Order created
≠ Matter opened
≠ Payment confirmed
```

A public interaction should produce a typed candidate event for the relevant Owning Service or Product workflow.

## 4. MGSN is the managed global service network

MGSN is the platform-owned and governed network for external professional service routing and fulfillment.

A Workplace accesses it through an MGSN Connection or Gateway. The Workplace does not receive the entire Provider pool, unrestricted contacts or ownership of network trust and routing assets.

```text
MGSN Connection / Gateway
≠ MGSN Network
```

MGSN may support:

- Provider eligibility;
- candidate route generation;
- recommendation;
- acceptance and appointment flow;
- instruction package delivery;
- evidence return;
- exception and recovery;
- network trust and governance.

The network does not automatically own the originating Workplace's customer relationship.

## 5. Core is not a central business database

Core defines shared semantic authority, including object meanings, lifecycle rules, governance primitives and interoperability expectations.

Core should not absorb every Workplace's:

- customers;
- Orders;
- Matters;
- files;
- pricing;
- private Knowledge;
- communication;
- relationship history;
- Product configuration.

Formal data remains with the applicable Owning Service under Core semantics.

```text
Shared semantics
≠ centralized ownership of business state
```

## 6. Product Installation matters

A Product is installed, enabled and configured within a specific Workplace context.

The installation may define:

- edition and entitlement;
- roles and permissions;
- active workflows;
- brand settings;
- data scope;
- AI and external-model policies;
- integrations;
- Projection routes;
- retention and audit rules.

The same Person may use Lite in several Workplaces with different roles, memories and permissions. Product access in one Workplace does not transfer to another.

## 7. Cross-product continuity should use typed contracts

Products should connect through explicit objects and events rather than silent shared-table mutation.

Examples include:

### Lite to Sites

```text
Approved Content or Listing
→ Sites Projection Request
→ Sites validation
→ published Projection
→ response event back to Lite
```

### Lite to MarkReg

```text
Qualified Service Opportunity
→ customer instruction
→ Handoff
→ MarkReg Intake
→ Engagement and authorized execution
→ evidence-backed status Projection to Lite
```

### Lite to MGSN

```text
Verified service Need
→ Workplace MGSN Connection
→ governed route recommendation
→ originating approval
→ Provider acceptance and appointment
→ fulfillment and evidence return
```

### MarkReg to Sites

```text
Owning Service state
→ purpose-limited customer-facing Projection
```

Each contract should define source, scope, authority, version, expected response and failure handling.

## 8. Projection is not state ownership

A Product may display information without owning the formal state.

Lite may show that a transfer is under official review. Sites may show that an application was filed. The authority remains with the relevant Owning Service and validated evidence.

```text
Displayed state
≠ authoritative state
```

The Projection should retain source, timestamp, freshness and revocation behavior.

## 9. Handoff is not transfer of the customer

When Lite sends a qualified opportunity to MarkReg, the default structure should preserve:

- originating Workplace;
- Relationship Owner;
- customer communication rules;
- commercial attribution;
- selected scope;
- minimum necessary data;
- return obligations.

A direct customer transition is possible only through explicit agreement.

The same rule applies when a Workplace uses MGSN or another collaborating Workplace.

## 10. White-label does not erase real responsibility

Lite, Sites or MarkReg may support white-label delivery. The customer-facing brand may remain with the originating Workplace.

White-label cannot conceal:

- legally required professional identity;
- Contracting Party;
- who handles customer funds;
- who bears Delivery responsibility;
- who provides formal professional service;
- material related-party routing;
- complaint and recovery routes.

## 11. Sites should not become a shadow CRM or Order authority

It may be convenient for public forms, pages and portals to store their own copies of customer or transaction state. Without boundaries, Sites can become an uncontrolled second CRM.

Sites should retain Projection and interaction state needed for its function, while accepted Customers, Orders, Matters and Payments are created or validated by their Owning Services.

## 12. Lite should not become a shadow MarkReg

Lite may identify a deadline, prepare a Handoff or show progress. It should not quietly duplicate full Matter execution merely because the interface is convenient.

When work requires:

- complete lifecycle ownership;
- formal professional responsibility;
- Provider appointment;
- official filing;
- evidence validation;
- exception recovery;
- service completion;

the route belongs to MarkReg or another qualified Owning Service.

## 13. MGSN should not become an open directory

A full searchable Provider directory with direct contact can undermine:

- eligibility governance;
- customer relationship protection;
- staged disclosure;
- routing quality;
- conflict management;
- evidence return;
- failure recovery.

The default should remain a governed recommendation: one recommended route and no more than two materially different alternatives where useful.

No candidate is a valid and safe result when the network cannot support the Need.

## 14. Product revenue must follow product responsibility

Subscription revenue belongs to maintained Product capability. Sites may charge for publishing, hosting or media services. MarkReg may charge for service delivery. MGSN may charge for managed routing and fulfillment support.

Cross-product commercial flows should not obscure:

- which Product provides what;
- which Workplace contracts;
- who owns the relationship;
- who receives payment;
- who bears failure responsibility.

## 15. Cross-product AI must respect memory boundaries

The same model may technically assist several Products. That does not make all context interchangeable.

```text
Lite memory
≠ MarkReg Matter memory
≠ Sites public content
≠ MGSN network learning
```

Any transfer of context should be purpose-limited, authorized, minimized and traceable. Access for inference does not create training consent or cross-Workplace memory.

## 16. Failure should remain attributable

When a cross-product workflow fails, the system should identify:

- source Product;
- receiving Product;
- current Owning Service;
- expected event or evidence;
- responsible Workplace or Provider;
- customer communication owner;
- recovery route.

A seamless user interface must not create responsibility ambiguity.

## 17. Product and technical implications

The architecture requires:

- Product-local data ownership;
- shared Core identifiers and semantics;
- typed Handoffs and Projections;
- event provenance;
- version and freshness controls;
- contextual authorization;
- minimum necessary data packages;
- independent Intake and validation;
- failure and retry contracts;
- audit across Product boundaries;
- portability without collapsing Products.

## 18. Commercial implications

Distinct Products allow MarkOrbit to serve different operating needs without creating one ungovernable super-application.

The commercial portfolio can grow through:

- Lite subscription;
- Sites publishing and projection;
- MarkReg service delivery;
- MGSN network fulfillment;
- human review and Contributions.

The products reinforce each other through governed continuity, not forced bundling or hidden ownership transfer.

## 19. The boundary matrix

| Product / layer | Primary purpose | May prepare or project | Does not automatically own |
|---|---|---|---|
| Core | Shared semantics and governance | Rules, object meaning, interoperability | Workplace customers and formal business state |
| Workplace | Business-sovereignty boundary | Product configuration and controlled external relations | Platform-owned Products or MGSN network assets |
| Lite | Daily growth, supply, demand and capability | Content, Recommendations, opportunities and Handoffs | Full Matter execution or professional authority |
| MarkReg | Complete trademark service delivery | Formal service workflows and evidence-backed state | Originating partner customer relationship by default |
| Sites | Public and customer-facing Projection | Pages, content, forms and controlled portals | Accepted Customer, Order, Matter or Payment state |
| MGSN | Managed external professional fulfillment | Governed routes, appointments and evidence return | Participant customer relationships or unrestricted Provider contacts |

## 20. Operating principle

> MarkOrbit should feel continuous without becoming centralized. Lite, MarkReg, Sites and MGSN create more value when each Product knows what it owns, what it may project, what it must hand off and which authority it must never imply.
