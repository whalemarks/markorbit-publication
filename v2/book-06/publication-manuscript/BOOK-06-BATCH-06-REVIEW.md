# Book 06 v2 Publication Reconstruction — Batch 06 Review

## Scope reviewed

- CH21 — Maintenance Leads Are Candidates, Not Customer Instructions

## Structural verdict

Batch 06 establishes Lite's service-opportunity and customer-resolution layer.

It connects raw trademark and operational signals to verified leads, evidence-backed entity resolution, permission-aware outreach and typed Product-to-Product Handoff. It does so without turning public records into marketing consent, lead assignment into filing authority, or MarkReg delivery into customer relationship transfer.

The resulting chain is:

```text
Observed Signal
→ Candidate Lead
→ Status, Entity and Relationship Verification
→ Outreach Eligibility
→ Qualified Service Opportunity
→ Customer Instruction
→ Lite-to-MarkReg Handoff
→ MarkReg Intake and Engagement
→ Authorized Execution
→ Validated Evidence Return
```

## Accepted distinctions

```text

```

The chapter correctly distinguishes raw signals, Candidate Leads, qualified service opportunities and customer instructions.

It covers:

- maintenance and renewal windows;
- declarations and grace periods;
- abandonment and re-filing signals;
- ownership and status anomalies;
- recordal and certificate exceptions;
- evidence, source freshness and calculation provenance;
- existing-customer obligations;

The chapter correctly treats accepted customer responsibility as an obligation and exception workflow, not merely a high-value lead.

## CH23 review — governed outreach

The chapter establishes Outreach as a controlled business action rather than a generic campaign function.

It covers:

- relationship-context classification;
- purpose-, channel- and sender-scoped consent;
- jurisdictional policy;
- truthful use of official facts and calculated dates;
- Outreach Briefs;
- operational versus marketing communications;
- frequency, quiet-hour and suppression controls;
- duplicate outreach across Workplaces;
- partner-owned customer protection;
- AI-assisted drafting and response interpretation;

- transparent public-record prospecting.

The chapter preserves the rule that a response such as “send details” does not itself become a filing instruction.

## CH24 review — Lite-to-MarkReg Handoff

The chapter gives the previously stated Lite/MarkReg boundary an operational contract.

The Handoff includes:

- originating Workplace and Relationship Owner;
- customer instruction evidence;
- service scope and exclusions;
- deadlines and Unknowns;
- commercial terms;
- minimum necessary data and documents;
- communication and white-label rules;
- professional and Provider constraints;
- expected return evidence;
- amendments and exceptions;
- cancellation and closure.

It correctly requires MarkReg intake revalidation before Matter execution and preserves separate gates for Engagement, conflict review, professional Assignment, Provider appointment, POA, payment authorization and protected action.

The chapter also establishes evidence-return and formal-state projection back to Lite rather than allowing Lite to infer completion from emails.
