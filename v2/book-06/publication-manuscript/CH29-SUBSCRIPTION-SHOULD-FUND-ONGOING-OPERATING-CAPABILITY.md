# Chapter 29 — Subscription Should Fund Ongoing Operating Capability

Lite is not a vending machine that dispenses isolated AI outputs. Its value comes from remaining available every day as a governed operating environment.

A subscription should therefore fund persistent capability rather than meter every ordinary invocation.

```text
Subscription
→ continuing Product Installation
→ governed data and memory
→ operating workflows
→ updates, security and support
→ compounding Workplace value
```

## 1. The wrong unit is the prompt

Charging or rationing every routine prompt encourages the user to avoid the very system that should become their daily operating surface.

A professional may use Lite repeatedly to:

- inspect Today;
- clarify a buyer request;
- prepare content;
- review an Inventory item;
- compare candidates;
- follow an Inquiry;
- inspect a service lead;
- continue a learning path;
- coordinate a bounded Handoff.

These actions belong to one operating relationship. They should not feel like repeated purchases from unrelated tools.

## 2. What the subscription sustains

A Lite subscription may fund:

- Product Installation and configuration;
- Workplace-scoped data and settings;
- Today ranking and explanation;
- private operating memory;
- governed AI assistance;
- templates and deterministic rendering;
- content and asset versioning;
- Request, Recommendation, Inquiry and Opportunity workflows;
- Capability Map and learning continuity;
- permissions, audit and security;
- updates and maintained Knowledge connections;
- ordinary support and recovery.

The user is paying for a maintained operating capability, not ownership of Core, shared models or Product source code.

## 3. Ordinary use and variable-cost services should be separated

Some activities create material external or variable cost. Examples may include:

- premium image or video generation;
- unusually large media rendering;
- third-party licensed data;
- identity or company verification;
- professional human review;
- official searches or documents;
- external Provider services;
- high-volume outreach infrastructure.

These may require allowances, pass-through cost or separate purchase. The distinction should be visible before use.

```text
Ordinary operating use
≠ unlimited external cost
```

The system should not advertise unlimited use and later hide severe throttling. Nor should it convert every inexpensive internal operation into a separate charge.

## 4. Subscription must not buy authority

A paid edition may provide better workflow, scale and collaboration. It does not create:

- professional qualification;
- filing authority;
- Provider appointment;
- customer instruction;
- resale authority;
- transaction commitment;
- higher legal confidence merely because the user pays more.

```text
Paid subscription
≠ professional authority
```

Safety, provenance and authority checks should not disappear in higher editions.

## 5. The retained value is continuity

Lite becomes more valuable when it remembers, within governed boundaries:

- what the Workplace is trying to achieve;
- which customers and relationships it is responsible for;
- what content has already been produced;
- which assets are authorized for promotion;
- which buyer preferences have been confirmed;
- which Recommendations were rejected and why;
- what work is pending;
- which capabilities are current;
- which rules or facts were corrected.

This continuity is a core subscription value. It must remain portable and correctable under Workplace sovereignty rules.

## 6. Cancellation should not erase the business

When a subscription ends, the Product may restrict future active capability according to the commercial terms. It should not trap the Workplace's own business records or erase evidentiary history.

A governed offboarding route should cover:

- data export;
- active obligations;
- open Inquiries and Opportunities;
- pending Handoffs;
- published projections;
- retained audit evidence;
- AI memory treatment;
- deletion and legal retention;
- reactivation conditions.

```text
Subscription ended
≠ Workplace business history forfeited
```

## 7. Fair use must be operationally intelligible

Where limits are needed, users should understand what is constrained:

- storage;
- seats;
- active Workplaces;
- rendering volume;
- external-model cost;
- public channels;
- automation frequency;
- verification services;
- support level.

Vague “reasonable use” language should not become arbitrary denial. The Product should warn before material limits, explain the reason and offer a predictable route.

## 8. Subscription should align with customer success

Healthy subscription value appears when Lite helps a Workplace:

- publish more credible content;
- prepare trustworthy trademark supply;
- answer buyer demand faster;
- protect customer relationships;
- convert qualified opportunities;
- reduce missed work and avoidable errors;
- improve capability;
- coordinate with MarkReg, Sites and MGSN safely.

Daily activity alone is not success. A system can generate many clicks while producing little business value.

## 9. Do not subsidize harmful growth loops

Subscription economics should not encourage:

- bulk unauthorized Listing creation;
- indiscriminate cold outreach;
- unnecessary AI generation;
- misleading concept content;
- hidden partner bypass;
- low-quality matching for engagement metrics;
- withholding correction or export to preserve retention.

Retention should come from compounding usefulness, not lock-in.

## 10. Workplace autonomy includes commercial configuration

Each Workplace may configure:

- its own services and prices;
- internal allocation rules;
- preferred content channels;
- public or private inventory strategy;
- MarkReg and MGSN routes;
- brand and communication policy;
- user roles and approval levels.

The subscription does not make the platform the owner of those commercial choices.

## 11. Product and technical implications

Lite requires:

- edition-aware entitlements;
- transparent usage accounting;
- separation of ordinary and variable-cost services;
- warnings and graceful degradation;
- billing and entitlement history;
- export and offboarding;
- reactivation;
- retention rules;
- non-destructive downgrade paths;
- protection of audit and formal evidence.

Entitlement checks must remain separate from professional and business authority checks.

## 12. Commercial implications

Subscription can create predictable recurring revenue because Lite supports ongoing operating work. Additional revenue may exist, but it should not make the subscription intentionally incomplete.

The final price, allowances and packaging remain Product decisions. The architectural lock is narrower:

```text
Subscription funds maintained operating capability.
Ordinary use is not reduced to prompt-by-prompt purchasing.
Material external cost and human service remain explicit.
```

## 13. Operating principle

> Lite should earn renewal because the Workplace becomes more organized, capable and commercially effective over time—not because its data, customers or professional continuity become difficult to leave.
