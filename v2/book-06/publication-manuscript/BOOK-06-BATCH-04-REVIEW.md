# Book 06 v2 Publication Reconstruction — Batch 04 Review

## Scope reviewed

- CH13 — Trademark Requests Are Governed Demand Objects
- CH14 — Clarification with Fewer Questions and More Inference
- CH15 — Explainable Matching Across Name, Goods, Jurisdiction and Price
- CH16 — Recommendation Is Not Availability, Clearance or Commitment

## Structural verdict

Batch 04 establishes Lite's governed demand and matching architecture.

It converts incomplete buyer conversations into versioned Trademark Requests, uses AI-assisted clarification without disguising inference as user instruction, separates retrieval from eligibility and recommendation, and defines Recommendation as a bounded decision aid rather than proof of availability, legal clearance or transaction commitment.

The resulting chain is:

```text
Demand signal
→ governed Trademark Request
→ clarification and qualification
→ permitted retrieval
→ candidate eligibility
→ multidimensional matching
→ explained Recommendation
→ buyer feedback
→ Inquiry or closure
```

## CH13 review — governed demand object

The chapter correctly establishes Trademark Request as the demand-side operating object.

It covers:

- Demand Originator and Relationship Owner;
- represented buyer context;
- demand provenance;
- explicit, inferred, flexible and unknown field states;
- naming, business, legal, commercial, preference and governance dimensions;
- Request Submitter versus Ultimate Buyer and Decision Maker;
- proportionate qualification;
- private, selected, network and public request modes;
- relationship protection without buyer captivity;
- versioning and Demand Portfolios;
- AI-extracted drafts awaiting confirmation;
- negative demand signals;
- Request lifecycle and varied fulfilment outcomes.

The Request remains a Lite object and does not become an Order, Engagement or transaction state automatically.

## CH14 review — fewer questions and more inference

The chapter correctly rejects the idea that conversational intake means asking a long form one question at a time.

It separates:

```text
Extraction
Inference
Recommendation
```

It also introduces:

- confidence plus consequence analysis;
- route-impact ranking for unknowns;
- trade-off choices instead of technical forms;
- progressive clarification stages;
- concise explanations of why a question matters;
- granular confirmation;
- the rule that silence is not informed confirmation;
- contradiction detection and alternative routes;
- original-language preservation;
- professional escalation based on consequence;
- feedback learning without automatic overfitting;
- clarification history as evidence.

The chapter preserves the user's right to understand and correct the system while reducing unnecessary intake burden.

## CH15 review — multidimensional matching

The chapter establishes four separate stages:

```text
Retrieval
→ Eligibility
→ Matching
→ Recommendation
```

It covers matching across:

- exact and normalized name;
- pronunciation and transliteration;
- semantic and cultural interpretation;
- actual goods and services;
- class and jurisdiction;
- legal-status evidence;
- Resale Authorization and Listing state;
- asking price and total expected cost;
- speed and route complexity;
- visual and brand potential;
- seller restrictions;
- hard constraints, strong preferences, flexible preferences and Unknowns.

It rejects one opaque score and supports explained dimensions, negative matching reasons and Pareto-style recommendation sets.

The chapter also addresses professional contribution, sponsorship and platform self-preference.

## CH16 review — recommendation boundaries

The chapter correctly treats Recommendation as a governed decision aid.

It separates multiple meanings of availability and requires specific states such as:

- visible for discovery;
- available for inquiry;
- seller reconfirmation required;
- price reconfirmation required;
- reserved;
- transaction pending;
- Authorization revalidation pending;
- sold subject to recordal;
- transfer completed.

It further distinguishes:

- matching from legal clearance;
- Recommendation from due diligence;
- buyer saves and shortlists from commitment;
- seller discussion from acceptance;
- Recommendation from reservation;
- estimated costs from quotations;
- candidate visibility from permission to contact;
- AI confidence from formal-state mutation.

The chapter requires expiry, correction propagation, scoped professional review and typed Handoffs to Inquiry, MarkReg or external professional work.

- buyer relationship does not transfer through Request visibility;

## Failure-mode check

The batch directly addresses:

- free-text demand becoming an Order automatically;
- long forms reproduced as chat;
- AI silently inventing classes, goods or buyer intent;
- buyer identity exposed before permission;
- public Request visibility transferring the relationship;
- retrieved records presented as sellable supply;
- class equality treated as useful goods coverage;
- one opaque score hiding hard failures;
- stale status, Authorization or price;
- paid promotion changing fit claims;
- Recommendation implying availability or legal safety;
- buyer saves being reported as commitment;
- automatic reservation;
- seller discussion being treated as acceptance;
- vague “subject to verification” language hiding material gaps;
- platform-owned services receiving undisclosed preference.

No blocking contradiction with Books 01 or 04, or with Book 06 Batches 01–03, was identified.

## Editorial assessment

The chapters meet the first publication-grade reconstruction standard. They contain sustained argument, object distinctions, normal and exceptional flows, examples, counterarguments, product and technical implications, commercial consequences and failure analysis.

Final-edition work will still require:

- diagrams for Request lifecycle, clarification states and matching stages;
- worked examples across several languages and jurisdictions;
- jurisdiction-specific professional review of goods and clearance examples;
- terminology alignment with Book 05 transaction and MarkReg Handoffs;
- formal alignment with Book 03 Execution review and protected-action boundaries;
- controlled glossary treatment for Request, Inquiry and Opportunity;
- copyediting to reduce repeated boundary statements while preserving meaning.
