# Book 06 v2 Publication Reconstruction — Batch 03 Review

## Scope reviewed

- CH09 — Trademark Inventory Is a Governed Supply Object
- CH10 — Resale Authorization Before Visibility
- CH11 — Listing as a Controlled Commercial Projection
- CH12 — Public, Network, Private and Selected Visibility

## Structural verdict

Batch 03 establishes Lite's governed trademark-supply architecture.

The four chapters define a controlled chain from legal record to commercial visibility:

```text
Trademark
→ Trademark Inventory
→ Resale Authorization
→ Listing
→ Visibility Projection
→ Inquiry
→ Transaction Opportunity
```

Each stage has a separate purpose, authority basis, lifecycle and evidence requirement.

## Accepted distinctions

```text
Trademark record ≠ commercial supply
Trademark ≠ Trademark Inventory
Inventory ≠ Resale Authorization
Resale Authorization ≠ Listing
Listing ≠ transfer
Listing ≠ transaction
Visibility ≠ authority
View ≠ promote
Promote ≠ negotiate
Negotiate ≠ accept
Reserved ≠ sold
Commercial agreement ≠ completed legal transfer
Public ≠ externally indexed by default
Selected access ≠ onward-sharing permission
```

## CH09 review — governed Inventory

The chapter correctly defines Inventory as a Workplace-scoped commercial relationship rather than a duplicate legal record.

Coverage includes:

- owned, customer-authorized, entrusted, co-brokered, network-visible, platform-managed and candidate supply;
- Relationship Owner preservation;
- evidence-based inventory quality;
- purpose-specific readiness;
- candidate-to-archive lifecycle;
- legal-status change propagation;
- duplicate Trademark identity versus multiple legitimate Inventory relationships;
- provenance, pricing readiness and supply health;
- anti-financialization and anti-vanity-volume controls.

It preserves the formal legal-state authority of the underlying Trademark source.

## CH10 review — Resale Authorization

The chapter establishes Authorization as an evidence-bearing mandate rather than a free-text note or Boolean.

It covers:

- granting party and authority basis;
- covered marks, jurisdictions, channels and assets;
- multidimensional exclusivity;
- promotion, negotiation and acceptance authority separation;
- owner identity and document disclosure;
- sub-agency and network rights;
- price, commission and attribution;
- versioning, expiry, revocation and survival terms;
- conflicting appointments and fraud risk;
- progressive evidence strength;
- AI extraction without AI-created authority.

The central lock is correct:

```text
Authority to market
≠ authority to bind
```

## CH11 review — controlled Listing

The chapter defines a Listing as a versioned commercial Projection sourced from active Inventory and Authorization.

It correctly separates:

- source legal facts from selected presentation;
- availability states;
- price presentation from internal economics;
- seller context from relationship ownership;
- Marketing Pack concepts from existing business reality;
- Inquiry from clicks or messages;
- reservation from sale;
- commercial agreement from recordal and closure.

It also addresses:

- activation gates;
- Listing lifecycle;
- inquiry routing;
- source-change propagation;
- duplicate Listing provenance;
- ranking and paid-promotion disclosure;
- archive and withdrawal.

## CH12 review — visibility architecture

The chapter replaces the public/private Boolean with layered visibility:

```text
Private
Selected Workplaces
Authorized Network
Qualified Recipients
Public
```

It adds:

- purpose-based visibility;
- reusable visibility profiles;
- field-specific disclosure;
- onward-sharing controls;
- tracked downloads and expiring links;
- revocation propagation;
- search-indexing policy;
- social derivative tracking;
- demand-led matching of private Inventory;
- ranking-scope enforcement;
- confidentiality and platform-operator access boundaries.

It correctly states that minimum disclosure must not hide facts needed for a competent transaction or professional decision.

## Authority locks preserved

- no active Canon modification;
- no frozen Book modification;
- no Core semantic activation;
- Lite remains distinct from MarkReg;
- Inventory does not create ownership or customer marketing rights;
- Resale Authorization does not create legal transfer;

## Failure-mode check

The batch directly addresses:

- imported database records labeled as available supply;
- stale, unreachable and unauthorized Listings;
- Boolean exclusivity;
- hidden commission and attribution;
- silent owner-identity disclosure;
- expired mandates leaving active promotion;
- duplicate-record merging that erases commercial provenance;
- concepts represented as actual businesses;
- goods summaries expanding legal scope;
- reservations treated as sales;
- private inventory leaking into public recommendations;
- platform customer-data prospecting;
- public indexing without owner awareness.

No blocking contradiction with Books 01 or 04 was identified.
