# Chapter 31 — Free, Pro and Business Editions Without Artificial Professional Scarcity

Product editions are necessary. Individuals, small teams and larger Workplaces do not need the same scale, collaboration, governance or support.

The danger begins when edition design intentionally withholds basic professional safety, knowledge or correction in order to create fear-driven upgrades.

```text
Edition difference
should reflect operating scale and governance,
not artificial scarcity of professional truth.
```

## 1. What should not be paywalled as a safety tactic

Lite should not reserve the following only for the most expensive edition merely to force conversion:

- clear distinction between fact and concept;
- source provenance;
- authority warnings;
- correction of known errors;
- basic privacy and security;
- visibility of professional limits;
- access to one's own business records;
- required concept labels;
- suppression and consent controls;
- export of entitled Workplace data;
- notice of material deadlines already accepted as responsibility.

A free user should not receive unsafe professional guidance by design.

## 2. Edition differences can be legitimate

Reasonable differences may concern:

- number of users;
- number of Workplaces or Product Installations;
- storage and media volume;
- active Inventory, Requests or Opportunities;
- collaboration and approval routes;
- custom roles and policies;
- brand and channel projections;
- private rendering templates;
- external integrations;
- audit depth and retention;
- API and automation capacity;
- support and recovery commitments;
- organization-wide Capability management;
- data-region or security options.

These represent real differences in cost and operational complexity.

## 3. Free should prove the operating model

A Free edition may help an individual experience the core Lite loop:

```text
Today
→ one governed action
→ follow-up
→ memory
→ next recommendation
```

It may support bounded use of:

- personal Today;
- professional-object content drafts;
- a limited private Inventory;
- a limited Trademark Request;
- learning and Capability exploration;
- basic export;
- clear upgrade preview.

Free should be useful enough to demonstrate value, not a broken interface designed only to collect data.

## 4. Pro should support the independent professional

A Pro edition may focus on one professional or a very small operating unit that needs:

- persistent private memory;
- larger content and asset workflows;
- governed rendering;
- broader Inventory and Request handling;
- Inquiries and Opportunity tracking;
- service leads;
- deeper Capability development;
- paid review and Contribution participation;
- selected channel or Sites projections;
- structured Handoff to MarkReg.

Pro does not mean the user becomes a Provider, lawyer or transaction principal.

## 5. Business should support organizational governance

A Business edition may add:

- multiple Members;
- custom roles;
- assignment and approval chains;
- relationship protection;
- shared and private operating memory;
- organization-wide Inventory and demand;
- advanced audit;
- brand and channel governance;
- team Capability maps;
- policy configuration;
- SSO or stronger identity controls;
- integration and export administration;
- multiple Product Installations;
- service-level and recovery options.

Business value comes from governed collaboration, not simply increasing every numerical limit.

## 6. Editions belong to a Workplace context

The Product subscription and edition should be attached to the relevant Workplace and Product Installation.

A Person may belong to several Workplaces with different editions and roles. Their access derives from the current context, not from one global personal badge.

```text
Person has Pro access in one Workplace
≠ Person has Pro authority in every Workplace
```

## 7. Downgrade must be non-destructive

When a Workplace moves to a lower edition, Lite may restrict:

- new automation;
- new public projections;
- additional seats;
- future high-volume generation;
- selected premium integrations.

It should not silently delete:

- customer history;
- evidence;
- accepted Contributions;
- active obligations;
- audit records;
- source provenance.

The Product should provide a resolution period for over-limit resources and explain which capabilities become read-only, paused or exportable.

## 8. Professional learning should not be artificially blocked

Edition differences may affect advanced simulations, assessor time, certification or premium curricula. They should not prevent users from seeing why a task exceeds their Capability or authority.

A basic safety message such as “professional review is required” should not itself be a premium feature.

Paid learning should offer additional structure, practice, review and evidence—not exclusive access to necessary warnings.

## 9. Usage limits must not distort professional decisions

A user approaching a generation limit should not be encouraged to:

- omit verification;
- skip review;
- publish without a concept label;
- ignore a customer obligation;
- use one unsafe bulk action instead of several controlled steps.

The Product should degrade convenience or volume, not integrity.

## 10. Collaboration features should preserve sovereignty

Business collaboration should not mean that every Member sees everything.

Edition capability should still honor:

- Membership scope;
- Product roles;
- Matter and customer boundaries;
- Assignment-scoped access;
- minimum necessary disclosure;
- relationship ownership;
- private Person memory where appropriate.

More seats do not justify weaker permissions.

## 11. Paid visibility requires separate disclosure

An edition may include promotion credits or public Listing capacity. That does not create eligibility or factual status.

```text
Promotion allowance
≠ Resale Authorization
≠ Listing approval
≠ Recommendation priority without disclosure
```

## 12. Edition naming should not overclaim

Words such as Pro, Business, Certified, Enterprise or Expert may create expectations.

The Product should avoid implying:

- a Pro subscriber is professionally qualified;
- a Business edition is legally compliant in every jurisdiction;
- an Enterprise customer receives ownership of platform infrastructure;
- a Certified user is government-licensed.

Edition names describe Product packaging, not professional status.

## 13. Metrics for healthy edition design

Useful measures include:

- time to first governed value;
- upgrade reasons;
- downgrade recovery;
- retention linked to business outcomes;
- frequency of limit surprises;
- export success;
- support burden;
- collaboration adoption;
- safety incidents by edition;
- whether Free users can understand the product honestly.

Conversion alone is not sufficient. A high conversion rate caused by artificial lock-in may damage long-term trust.

## 14. Product and technical implications

Lite requires:

- Workplace-scoped entitlements;
- edition-aware limits;
- role-aware feature access;
- transparent usage views;
- upgrade and downgrade previews;
- read-only and paused states;
- export and retention protection;
- non-bypassable safety controls;
- separation of Product entitlement from professional authority.

## 15. Commercial implications

Free, Pro and Business can create a coherent adoption ladder:

```text
Free proves the loop.
Pro supports independent daily economics.
Business governs organizational collaboration.
```

Final prices, limits and feature allocations remain open. The governing rule is that editions monetize scale, continuity, collaboration and governance without manufacturing professional danger or data captivity.

## 16. Operating principle

> A higher Lite edition should let a Workplace do more, coordinate more and govern more. It should not make truth, safety, correction or ownership of the Workplace's own business history privileges reserved for those who pay the most.
