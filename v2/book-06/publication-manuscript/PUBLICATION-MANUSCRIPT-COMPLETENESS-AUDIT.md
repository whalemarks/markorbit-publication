## Thesis coverage

The manuscript adequately establishes the following primary theses:

1. Lite is the daily growth and trademark-commerce Product for independent professionals and small Workplaces.
2. Today is a governed decision surface rather than an activity feed.
3. Content must derive from professional objects and preserve factual provenance.
4. AI imagery requires deterministic identity and factual control.
5. Trademark supply requires Inventory relationship, Resale Authorization and controlled Listing.
6. Buyer demand should be structured with minimal high-value clarification.
7. Matching must be explainable and must not imply availability or legal clearance.
8. Inquiry and Transaction Opportunity require provenance and relationship protection.
9. Due diligence and transfer completion require typed evidence and formal-state validation.
10. Maintenance leads require verification, consent and customer instruction before execution.
11. Lite-to-MarkReg Handoff transfers bounded work context, not customer ownership.
12. Capability is evidence-backed, scoped and perishable.
13. Learning, Simulation, Assessment, Certification and production authority remain distinct.
14. Paid Contributions require fair terms, review, compensation and rights governance.
15. Subscription funds maintained operating capability.
16. Transaction and human-service revenue remain explicit.
17. Editions monetize scale and governance rather than truth or safety.
18. Lite, MarkReg, Sites and MGSN remain distinct Products connected by typed contracts.

## Repetition audit

The manuscript intentionally repeats critical distinctions because the chapters were reconstructed in batches and each needed to remain safe in isolation.

Final editing should reduce repetitive formulations around:

- Recommendation ≠ instruction;
- Handoff ≠ customer transfer;
- Provider return ≠ formal state;
- AI inference ≠ authority;
- payment ≠ completion;
- visibility ≠ ownership.

The concepts should remain; redundant wording should be leveled.
