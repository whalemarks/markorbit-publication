# Book 06 v2 Publication Reconstruction — Batch 08 Review

## Scope reviewed

- CH29 — Subscription Should Fund Ongoing Operating Capability
- CH30 — Transaction and Human-service Revenue Must Remain Explicit
- CH31 — Free, Pro and Business Editions Without Artificial Professional Scarcity
- CH32 — Lite, MarkReg, Sites and MGSN Must Remain Distinct Products

## Structural verdict

Batch 08 establishes Lite's business-model principles and closes the main Product-boundary argument of the first manuscript pass.

The chapters define recurring Product revenue, explicit non-subscription revenue, edition design and cross-Product continuity without setting final prices, activating payment custody or collapsing Lite into MarkReg, Sites or MGSN.

The governing chain is:

```text
Workplace need
→ appropriate Product Installation
→ edition and entitlement
→ governed Product action
→ typed Projection or Handoff where required
→ accountable revenue and responsibility
```

## CH29 review — subscription

The chapter correctly defines subscription as funding maintained operating capability rather than prompt-by-prompt purchasing.

It covers:

- Product Installation and configuration;
- Workplace-scoped memory and data;
- Today and operating workflows;
- AI, rendering, versioning and audit;
- ordinary versus variable-cost use;
- transparent limits;
- cancellation, export and non-destructive offboarding;
- retention based on useful continuity rather than captivity.

The chapter does not promise unlimited external cost or fix final packaging.

Important locks are preserved:

```text
Subscription
≠ professional authority
≠ ownership of Core
≠ ownership of Product source code
```

## CH30 review — transaction and human-service revenue

The chapter separates:

- subscription;
- paid promotion and media;
- transaction revenue;
- professional human service;
- paid Contribution;
- official and external cost.

It requires role, basis, recipient, trigger and evidence for each charge. It also requires related-party and platform-interest disclosure where recommendations create platform or affiliated revenue.

The chapter protects:

- commission provenance;
- customer and partner relationships;
- contributor compensation;
- certification independence;
- MarkReg and MGSN responsibility boundaries;
- payment-state separation from business and formal state.

No escrow, unrestricted custody, automatic netting or platform-principal authority is granted.

## CH31 review — editions

The chapter establishes a coherent edition principle:

```text
Free proves the operating loop.
Pro supports independent daily economics.
Business governs organizational collaboration.
```

Legitimate differences concern scale, seats, collaboration, storage, audit, automation, integrations, support and governance.

The following are not treated as premium safety features:

- source provenance;
- correction of known errors;
- authority warnings;
- basic privacy and security;
- concept labeling;
- access to entitled Workplace records;
- data export;
- notice of accepted obligations.

The chapter also requires Workplace-scoped entitlements, non-destructive downgrade and separation between Product edition and professional status.

## CH32 review — Product boundaries

The chapter correctly establishes:

```text
Lite
= daily growth, supply, demand, opportunity and Capability Product

MarkReg
= complete trademark service-delivery Product

Sites
= public and customer-facing Projection Product

MGSN
= platform-owned managed global service network
```

It also preserves:

```text
Core
= shared semantic authority

Workplace
= business-sovereignty boundary

Owning Service
= formal business-state authority
```

The chapter explicitly rejects:

- Core as a central business database;
- Sites as a shadow CRM or Order authority;
- Lite as a shadow MarkReg;
- MGSN as an open Provider directory;
- silent cross-Product shared-table mutation;
- Handoff as customer relationship transfer;
- Projection as formal state ownership.

The typed continuity examples for Lite–Sites, Lite–MarkReg, Lite–MGSN and MarkReg–Sites align with the accepted Workplace sovereignty correction.
