# Chapter 23 — Outreach Requires Relationship, Consent and Jurisdictional Rules

Finding a plausible contact does not create the right to contact them.

Lite may help a Workplace discover maintenance needs, re-filing candidates, ownership anomalies and other service opportunities. The same capability can easily become indiscriminate cold outreach if relationship, consent, jurisdiction and message truthfulness are not governed separately.

Outreach should therefore be treated as a controlled business action.

```text
Qualified lead
→ relationship and consent review
→ jurisdiction and channel rules
→ approved message purpose
→ authorized sender
→ outreach event
→ response, suppression or escalation
```

The purpose of the model is not to prevent legitimate business development. It is to make the basis for communication visible and defensible.

## 1. Different relationship contexts create different communication rights

Lite should distinguish:

- active customer;
- former customer;
- prospective customer who requested information;
- referred contact;
- partner-owned customer;
- existing network participant;
- public-record prospect;
- unrelated third party;
- person who opted out;
- person whose relationship is disputed or unknown.

An active customer may expect operational deadline communication. A former customer may be eligible for a limited reactivation message. A public-record prospect may require a stronger compliance review and a more transparent explanation of how the contact information was found.

```text
Relevant service need
≠ same outreach entitlement in every relationship context
```

## 2. Purpose should be explicit

Possible outreach purposes include:

- operational notice;
- deadline reminder;
- requested quotation;
- customer-service follow-up;
- reactivation;
- public-record prospecting;
- partner-introduced contact;
- transaction inquiry;
- event or educational content;
- satisfaction or evidence request.

The same contact point may be permitted for one purpose and prohibited for another.

For example, an email provided for an active matter should not automatically be reused for unrelated bulk marketing.

## 3. Consent is not one universal Boolean

Consent or permission may vary by:

- channel;
- purpose;
- brand or sending entity;
- frequency;
- territory;
- relationship;
- term;
- withdrawal status.

Lite may record:

```text
Operational communication permitted
Requested commercial response permitted
General marketing consented
Partner referral permitted
No marketing — service notices only
Do not contact
Unknown — review required
```

Silence, data availability or prior correspondence should not automatically become broad marketing consent.

## 4. Jurisdiction affects the route

Outreach rules can differ across countries and channels. Relevant factors may include:

- business-to-business versus consumer communication;
- email, telephone, SMS, messaging app or social media;
- existing customer relationship;
- direct marketing consent rules;
- sender identification requirements;
- unsubscribe or opt-out requirements;
- data-source transparency;
- time-of-day restrictions;
- professional advertising rules;
- solicitation restrictions;
- cross-border data use;
- retention and suppression duties.

The manuscript does not define universal legal rules. Lite should use jurisdiction-scoped policy and route uncertain cases to qualified review.

## 5. Sender identity must be clear

Every outreach event should identify:

- sending Person or Workplace;
- represented Organization;
- relationship owner;
- service or commercial purpose;
- source of authority;
- reply route;
- opt-out route where applicable;
- whether the communication is automated or AI-assisted where disclosure is required.

White-label arrangements should not create deceptive sender identity. The recipient should be able to understand who is communicating and on whose behalf.

## 6. Message truthfulness begins with source facts

An outreach message should distinguish:

- verified official fact;
- calculated or inferred deadline;
- possible issue requiring review;
- service recommendation;
- quotation;
- promotional claim.

Avoid statements such as:

- “Your trademark will definitely expire tomorrow” when the date is unverified;
- “We are your appointed agent” without an active relationship;
- “Only our firm can restore the mark” without evidence;
- “Your application has been rejected” when the record is merely delayed;
- “Immediate payment is required” before a valid quotation and instruction.

Urgency should reflect actual risk, not a sales tactic.

## 7. Outreach content should be generated from an approved brief

The Outreach Brief may include:

- lead and source facts;
- recipient and relationship context;
- purpose;
- channel;
- jurisdictional policy;
- sender identity;
- approved claims;
- prohibited claims;
- call to action;
- deadline wording;
- pricing status;
- required disclaimer;
- opt-out treatment;
- review level;
- valid period.

AI may draft within this boundary. It should not freely assemble customer and public data into a persuasive message without policy checks.

## 8. Operational notices and marketing should remain distinct

A customer may opt out of general marketing but still need essential operational notices for an active matter.

Lite should maintain separate communication categories and suppression rules.

```text
Marketing opt-out
≠ suppression of necessary matter communication

Operational relationship
≠ unlimited promotional permission
```

The system should prevent users from disguising sales messages as operational notices.

## 9. Frequency and contact pressure matter

Even a lawful individual message can become abusive through repetition.

Policy may control:

- maximum attempts;
- minimum interval;
- channel escalation;
- quiet hours;
- response-based suspension;
- opt-out handling;
- bounced or invalid contact suppression;
- duplicate outreach across Workplaces;
- ongoing matter exceptions.

A Contact Suppression record should prevent repeated contact after refusal, complaint or invalidity.

## 10. Cross-Workplace duplication requires coordination

The same owner may appear in multiple datasets or partner portfolios. Without relationship-aware suppression, several Workplaces could contact the same person about the same deadline.

Lite should detect possible duplication while preserving Workplace sovereignty.

Possible actions include:

- warn the current Workplace;
- identify an existing Relationship Owner;
- route through the partner;
- suppress duplicate public-record outreach;
- require manual coordination;
- preserve separate lawful relationships where they genuinely exist.

The platform should not expose one Workplace's customer list to another merely to deduplicate outreach.

## 11. Partner-owned relationships require communication discipline

Where a lead belongs to a partner relationship, Lite should define:

- whether the originating Workplace may contact the customer directly;
- whether communication must pass through the partner;
- whether white-label communication is allowed;
- who approves content and quotation;
- whose branding is used;
- who owns future follow-up;
- attribution and fee rules;
- what happens if the customer requests direct contact.

```text
Access to customer information
≠ permission to bypass the partner
```

## 12. AI-assisted outreach remains human-governed

AI may:

- draft messages;
- adapt language and tone;
- summarize the verified issue;
- suggest the minimum high-value call to action;
- translate;
- identify unsupported claims;
- compare the message with policy;
- schedule an approved sequence;
- summarize replies.

AI should not:

- choose a recipient solely because they are commercially valuable;
- infer broad consent;
- send protected or high-risk messages without approval;
- continue after opt-out;
- invent urgency or official status;
- impersonate a professional;
- create a customer instruction from a positive-sounding reply;
- appoint a Provider or initiate filing.

## 13. Replies require typed interpretation

A recipient response may mean:

```text
Wrong person
No longer owner
Already handled
Interested in information
Request quotation
Call requested
Do not contact
Complaint
Customer instruction candidate
Partner referral
```

An AI summary can assist, but material responses should remain linked to the original message and be confirmed before state changes.

“Please send details” does not necessarily mean “proceed with filing.”

## 14. Outreach should create an auditable event

An Outreach Event should record:

- lead and recipient;
- contact point;
- sender and represented Workplace;
- purpose and policy basis;
- message version;
- facts used;
- approval;
- sent time;
- delivery result;
- reply;
- suppression or next-step state;
- related Customer, Opportunity or Matter.

This supports complaint handling, relationship protection and continuous improvement.

## 15. Public-record prospecting should be transparent

Where appropriate, the message may explain that the sender identified a possible issue through public trademark information and has not yet confirmed the recipient's current representation.

Transparency can reduce confusion and false authority.

The message should not imply that the platform, government office or existing attorney instructed the contact.

## 16. Example: former customer renewal opportunity

A Workplace previously filed a trademark but has no active maintenance Engagement.

The safe route is:

1. verify the mark and deadline;
2. confirm the former customer identity and prior relationship;
3. check current representative and opt-out history;
4. classify the message as reactivation, not active-matter notice;
5. identify the sender and prior relationship honestly;
6. explain the verified window and offer review;
7. obtain explicit instruction before creating the MarkReg matter.

The system should not imply ongoing appointment merely because the Workplace handled the original filing.

## 17. Example: partner portfolio

A partner provides a list of marks requiring maintenance review. The originating Workplace may prepare deadline reports and white-label drafts, but the partner retains customer communication.

The system should preserve:

- partner as Relationship Owner;
- originating Workplace as bounded contributor or service orchestrator;
- no direct contact permission;
- partner approval of quotations;
- Handoff only after partner-provided customer instruction.

## 18. Failure modes

This model fails when:

- public data is treated as consent;
- operational and marketing messages are mixed;
- sender identity is misleading;
- AI sends messages using unverified deadlines;
- several Workplaces contact the same owner without coordination;
- opt-outs are stored only in one campaign tool;
- partner customers are contacted directly without authority;
- positive replies become customer instructions automatically;
- message versions and approvals are not retained;
- the platform ranks outreach by revenue while ignoring complaints and trust.

## 19. Product and technical implications

Lite needs:

- relationship-context classification;
- purpose- and channel-scoped consent;
- jurisdictional outreach policy;
- sender and representation context;
- Outreach Briefs;
- claim and source validation;
- approval and send permissions;
- frequency and quiet-hour controls;
- central suppression within the applicable Workplace scope;
- duplicate-contact detection without sovereignty leakage;
- partner communication rules;
- reply classification;
- audit and complaint workflow;
- typed conversion to Opportunity or Customer Instruction candidate.

## 20. Commercial implications

Responsible outreach can support:

- customer retention;
- maintenance revenue;
- former-customer reactivation;
- partner portfolio service;
- qualified public-record prospecting;
- more accurate service forecasting.

The commercial model should measure not only response and conversion, but also:

- complaint rate;
- opt-out rate;
- false-urgency corrections;
- duplicate-contact incidents;
- partner relationship breaches;
- long-term retention.

## 21. The operating principle

```text
A contact point is data
A relationship creates context
Consent and law create permission
An approved brief creates the message boundary
A reply creates evidence, not automatic instruction
```

> Lite should make legitimate professional outreach easier while making unauthorized, deceptive and repetitive outreach harder.
