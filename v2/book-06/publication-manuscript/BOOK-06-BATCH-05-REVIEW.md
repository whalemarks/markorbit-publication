## Structural verdict

Batch 05 establishes the governed transition from buyer-seller contact to transaction formation, verification, execution and completion.

The four chapters preserve the difference between communication, commercial viability, professional work, contractual agreement, payment, official recordal and final evidence.

```text
Inquiry
→ qualification
→ Transaction Opportunity
→ due diligence
→ Engagement and execution Handoff
→ agreement and milestones
→ evidence return
→ formal-state validation
→ closing and post-closing obligations
```

## Accepted distinctions

```text
Listing view ≠ Inquiry
Inquiry ≠ Offer
Inquiry ≠ Transaction Opportunity
```

## CH17 review — Inquiry provenance

The chapter correctly models Inquiry as a governed object rather than an unstructured message thread.

It preserves:

- source Request, Listing and Recommendation versions;
- source channel and campaign;
- Demand Originator and Relationship Owners;
- referring Workplace;
- represented buyer and seller context;
- contact and disclosure permissions;
- visible price and terms at the time;
- commission and attribution rules;
- communication evidence;
- qualification state;
- progressive disclosure.

It also prevents Inquiry creation from producing buyer commitment, exclusivity, direct-contact authority or relationship transfer.

## CH18 review — Transaction Opportunity

The chapter establishes an explicit conversion threshold from Inquiry to a plausible transaction route.

A Transaction Opportunity requires a specific buyer context, governed asset, current seller route and identifiable next-stage responsibilities. It records:

- participants and represented roles;
- transaction model;
- term versions;
- attribution and commission ledger;
- risk and Unknown register;
- Reservation status;
- Engagement requirements;
- conversion probability as a contestable estimate.

The chapter correctly avoids treating every lead as pipeline revenue.

## CH19 review — Due diligence

The chapter covers:

- seller identity and authority;
- chain of title;
- legal status and deadlines;
- disputes, limitations and vulnerability;
- included and excluded assets;
- buyer-use and goods fit;
- evidence provenance;
- typed findings and consequence levels;
- Unknown and conditional progression;
- seller representations versus independent verification;
- controlled due-diligence room;
- privacy and minimum necessary access;
- explicit proceed, remediate, pause or reject decisions.

It preserves the distinction between asset due diligence and broader use clearance or freedom-to-operate advice.

## CH20 review — Handoff and completion

The chapter decomposes transaction completion across:

- commercial terms;
- agreement execution;
- payment milestones;
- assignment documents;
- asset delivery;
- official filing;
- official acceptance;
- owner-record update;
- closing;
- post-closing work.

It correctly positions Lite as the commercial coordination Product and MarkReg or qualified Providers as potential formal execution routes. No Handoff creates Provider appointment or customer relationship transfer automatically.

Evidence-return contracts and Owning Service validation preserve formal-state authority.

## Authority locks preserved

- no active Canon or frozen Book modification;
- no Core semantic activation;
- Lite remains distinct from MarkReg;
- Inquiry access does not create Relationship Ownership;
- direct contact remains permission-scoped;
- Transaction Opportunity does not create Engagement, Reservation or commitment automatically;
- AI probability does not mutate state;
- due diligence does not guarantee legal availability, enforceability or safe use;
- uploaded evidence does not become verified automatically;
- Handoff does not appoint a Provider or transfer the customer;
- Lite does not become official trademark-record authority;
- payment references do not authorize platform custody;
- Provider messages do not update formal state without evidence validation;
- no autonomous protected professional action is authorized.

## Commercial decisions deliberately left open

The manuscript does not determine:

- whether Lite or another entity will ever become a contracting party;
- whether any MarkOrbit Product will hold funds or provide escrow-like services;
- default commission percentages;
- commission payment triggers;
- mandatory due-diligence scope for each jurisdiction;
- specific document-authentication methods;
- platform responsibility for buyer or seller default;
- insurance, warranty or indemnity products;
- final transaction fee model;
- exact legal definition of closing for every transaction.

These require commercial, financial, regulatory and legal decisions outside this batch.

## Failure-mode check

The batch directly addresses:

- loss of Listing and Request provenance;
- premature buyer or seller identity disclosure;
- ambiguous relationship and commission ownership;
- every Inquiry being treated as a sales Opportunity;
- stale seller availability;
- professional work beginning without Engagement;
- payment and legal state being collapsed;
- generic or post-payment due diligence;
- seller statements being presented as verified facts;
- unrestricted data-room access;
- provider completion messages without evidence;
- recordal filing being described as accepted transfer;
- stale Listings and Authorizations after sale;
- post-closing obligations disappearing.

No blocking contradiction with the active Canon, Book 04 Workplace sovereignty or prior Book 06 batches was identified.
