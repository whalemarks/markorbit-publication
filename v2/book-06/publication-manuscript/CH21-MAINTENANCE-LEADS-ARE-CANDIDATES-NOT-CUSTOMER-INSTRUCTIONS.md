# Chapter 21 — Maintenance Leads Are Candidates, Not Customer Instructions

Trademark databases contain recurring commercial signals: a declaration window is opening, a renewal deadline is approaching, an owner name appears inconsistent, a mark has become abandoned, or a new filing may be needed after a failed case.

These signals can create valuable service opportunities. They can also create spam, false urgency and unauthorized professional action when the system mistakes a data pattern for a customer instruction.

Lite should therefore treat every maintenance lead as a governed candidate.

```text
Observed signal
→ candidate lead
→ identity and status verification
→ relationship and outreach eligibility
→ qualified service opportunity
→ customer instruction
→ MarkReg engagement and execution
```

The lead begins the investigation. It does not authorize the service.

## 1. A maintenance signal is not yet a lead

Possible signals include:

- Section 8, Section 9, Section 15 or Section 71 windows;
- renewal or declaration deadlines;
- grace-period entry;
- ownership or address inconsistency;
- correspondence-address failure;
- status anomaly;
- abandonment after an Office Action;
- missed publication or registration fee;
- recordal not completed;
- certificate not delivered;
- goods or class mismatch;
- apparent re-filing need;
- possible non-use exposure;
- foreign associate delay;
- unclosed task or missing evidence.

A raw data signal may be wrong because:

- the official source has not refreshed;
- the mark has already been maintained;
- another representative handled the matter;
- a filing is pending but not yet visible;
- the record belongs to a different entity with a similar name;
- the owner has changed;
- the deadline rule was calculated incorrectly;
- the mark is intentionally being abandoned;
- the customer is no longer represented by the Workplace.

```text
Data pattern
≠ verified maintenance need
```

## 2. Candidate Lead should preserve its source

A Candidate Lead should record:

- source Trademark or Matter;
- originating dataset and version;
- detected signal;
- calculation method;
- jurisdiction;
- relevant date or window;
- confidence;
- known conflicts;
- source freshness;
- affected owner or applicant candidate;
- related Workplace records;
- prior actions and communications;
- review status;
- expiry or recheck date.

The system must be able to explain why the candidate appeared.

## 3. Verification has several layers

Lead qualification should separate:

### Status verification

- current official status;
- last official event;
- maintenance or renewal record;
- pending submissions;
- grace-period availability;
- abandonment or cancellation basis;
- known restoration or re-filing routes.

### Identity verification

- recorded owner or applicant;
- current legal entity;
- name and address changes;
- parent or affiliate relationship;
- possible duplicate entities;
- authorized representatives.

### Relationship verification

- whether the Workplace currently represents the customer;
- whether the matter came through a partner;
- whether another Workplace owns the relationship;
- whether the customer asked not to receive marketing;
- whether there is an active Engagement;
- whether prior work was completed or disputed.

### Service-route verification

- whether the task is still legally and operationally possible;
- what documents are required;
- whether a Provider or attorney is needed;
- expected official and service costs;
- deadlines and uncertainty;
- whether re-filing is the only viable route.

No single verification result should silently imply the others.

## 4. Lead categories should remain distinct

Lite should distinguish at least:

```text
Existing-customer obligation
Existing-customer opportunity
Former-customer reactivation candidate
Partner-owned opportunity
Public-record prospect
Internal exception-recovery lead
Unknown-relationship candidate
```

An existing-customer obligation may require urgent operational attention. A public-record prospect may require marketing-law and consent review before any contact. A partner-owned opportunity may require communication through the partner rather than the ultimate owner.

Treating all categories as one sales queue damages both service quality and relationships.

## 5. Existing obligations outrank speculative prospecting

If the Workplace already accepted responsibility for a matter, Lite should not present the deadline merely as a sales opportunity.

```text
Accepted responsibility
→ obligation, risk and workflow

No accepted responsibility
→ possible service opportunity
```

The distinction affects:

- priority;
- escalation;
- required communication;
- liability;
- evidence retention;
- internal assignment;
- customer expectation.

A missed deadline in an active matter is not “a high-value lead.” It is an operational exception requiring recovery and accountability.

## 6. Customer instruction remains a separate event

Even where the need is genuine, the customer must normally decide whether to proceed.

Possible states include:

```text
Candidate
Verified
Eligible for Internal Follow-up
Eligible for Outreach
Customer Contacted
Customer Interested
Scope Explained
Quotation Issued
Customer Instructed
Engagement Accepted
Execution Ready
Closed / Declined / Expired
```

Continue to preserve:

```text
Customer Interested
≠ Customer Instructed

Quotation Sent
≠ Quotation Accepted

Customer Instruction
≠ Provider Appointment
```

## 7. Deadline communication must be exact about certainty

A maintenance communication should distinguish:

- confirmed official deadline;
- estimated deadline pending source verification;
- ordinary period;
- grace period;
- final known date;
- restoration uncertainty;
- professional review requirement;
- documents not yet available.

The system should not manufacture urgency by presenting an estimated date as a confirmed final deadline.

Where rules are uncertain or jurisdiction-specific, the communication should state the uncertainty and route the matter for qualified review.

## 8. Lead scoring must not override eligibility

Commercial factors may include:

- expected revenue;
- customer relationship strength;
- urgency;
- service fit;
- probability of response;
- document readiness;
- likelihood of successful completion.

But eligibility gates come first:

```text
relationship and consent
→ legal and factual eligibility
→ conflict and capacity
→ prioritization
```

A profitable opportunity should not be ranked for outreach when the Workplace has no lawful or relationship basis to contact the owner.

## 9. Public-record leads require stronger restraint

Public trademark data may identify owners and addresses. That does not automatically create permission to send repeated marketing communications.

Before outreach, Lite should consider:

- applicable marketing and privacy rules;
- whether the contact channel is professional or personal;
- whether the owner is represented;
- whether a partner or attorney relationship is visible;
- do-not-contact status;
- frequency limits;
- source transparency;
- opt-out mechanism;
- jurisdictional restrictions;
- sensitivity of the matter.

```text
Publicly accessible data
≠ unrestricted marketing permission
```

## 10. AI may help qualify, not invent certainty

AI may:

- detect candidate windows;
- summarize official event history;
- identify inconsistencies;
- compare owner names;
- suggest likely service routes;
- draft a review checklist;
- prepare a customer-friendly explanation;
- identify missing evidence;
- prioritize re-verification.

AI may not:

- create a customer instruction;
- state that restoration is available without evidence;
- infer current representation from an old record;
- claim that a mark is abandoned for a particular reason without verification;
- contact the customer automatically where approval is required;
- convert a probable owner match into a confirmed entity;
- mutate formal Matter state.

## 11. Lead ownership must be explicit

A service lead may originate from:

- a Workplace customer portfolio;
- a partner relationship;
- public data;
- a Data Refinery signal;
- a network referral;
- a prior Inquiry;
- an internal exception;
- a MarkReg history.

The system should record:

- Lead Originator;
- Relationship Owner;
- represented customer context;
- permitted assignees;
- attribution and commission rules;
- whether direct contact is allowed;
- expiry and reassignment rules.

Discovering the lead does not automatically transfer the customer relationship.

## 12. Allocation should follow policy and capacity

Workplace policy may route verified leads according to:

- existing account owner;
- jurisdiction expertise;
- language;
- service type;
- workload;
- conflict status;
- customer preference;
- partner restrictions;
- supervision requirements.

```text
Lead Assigned
≠ Customer Instruction
≠ Professional Authorization
```

Assignment here means responsibility for qualification or follow-up, not authority to file or act externally.

## 13. Lead quality should be measured by outcomes

Useful metrics include:

- signal-to-verified-lead rate;
- false-positive rate;
- status freshness;
- owner-resolution rate;
- relationship-resolution rate;
- eligible-outreach rate;
- response rate;
- instruction conversion;
- completion rate;
- missed-deadline prevention;
- complaint and opt-out rate;
- reasons for closure;
- recovery success.

Raw lead count is a weak metric. A smaller verified set that prevents customer loss or generates legitimate instructions is more valuable.

## 14. Example: an apparent Section 8 opportunity

Lite detects that a US registration has entered a maintenance period.

The correct route is:

1. confirm registration and maintenance dates;
2. check whether a declaration is already pending or filed;
3. identify the recorded owner;
4. resolve the owner against Workplace customer records;
5. determine whether the Workplace currently represents the owner;
6. review contact and relationship rules;
7. create either an existing-customer obligation or an eligible service lead;
8. draft an accurate explanation and quotation;
9. obtain customer instruction;
10. create the appropriate MarkReg Engagement and execution route.

The wrong route is to email the owner immediately with “your trademark is about to expire” based only on a stale database calculation.

## 15. Failure modes

This model fails when:

- every approaching date becomes a sales lead;
- existing customer obligations are treated as optional opportunities;
- old representative data is treated as current relationship authority;
- public records are used for unrestricted outreach;
- AI-generated deadlines are stated as official facts;
- lead scoring hides legal or relationship ineligibility;
- assignment is mistaken for authority to file;
- customer interest is mistaken for instruction;
- a closed or declined lead continues to trigger messages;
- false positives are not recorded and corrected;
- the platform prioritizes its own MarkReg revenue over the Workplace's chosen route.

## 16. Product and technical implications

Lite needs:

- signal ingestion and provenance;
- jurisdictional date-rule versioning;
- candidate lead objects;
- official-status revalidation;
- entity and relationship resolution;
- consent and outreach eligibility;
- lead ownership and attribution;
- policy-based allocation;
- versioned customer communications;
- instruction and Engagement gates;
- closure and suppression rules;
- correction feedback to Data Refinery and Brain;
- typed Handoff to MarkReg.

Formal trademark and Matter states remain under their Owning Services. Lite coordinates discovery, qualification and follow-up.

## 17. Commercial implications

Maintenance leads can create recurring revenue and improve retention, but only when the system protects trust.

Legitimate value includes:

- preventing missed obligations;
- reactivating former customers responsibly;
- identifying re-filing needs;
- resolving data anomalies;
- preparing qualified service opportunities;
- reducing manual portfolio review;
- routing matters to appropriate providers.

A model based on exaggerated urgency, indiscriminate scraping or relationship bypass may produce short-term responses while destroying long-term credibility.

## 18. The operating principle

```text
Signal is evidence to investigate
Lead is a governed candidate
Opportunity is a qualified business route
Instruction is a customer decision
Execution requires separate authority
```

> Lite should help professionals discover service work early without pretending that a database signal is already a customer request.
