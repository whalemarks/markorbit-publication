# Book 06 v2 — Chapter and Thesis Map

## Purpose

This map records the first long-form manuscript structure and the principal thesis advanced by each chapter. It is an editorial and consistency artifact, not a replacement for the chapters.

## Part I — Today as the operating surface

### CH01 — Why Lite Must Begin with the Day

**Thesis:** Lite should begin with the user's present operating condition and next safe action, not with a module directory or generic dashboard.

**Primary lock:** Today ≠ activity feed.

### CH02 — The Daily Operating Surface Is a Decision System

**Thesis:** daily prioritization must pass through eligibility, evidence, risk and explanation before ranking.

**Primary lock:** high commercial value ≠ permission to bypass authority.

### CH03 — Different Workplaces Need Different Priorities

**Thesis:** priorities derive from Workplace strategy, Product configuration, active pipeline, capacity and Person role rather than permanent organization labels.

**Primary lock:** relevant opportunity ≠ executable opportunity.

### CH04 — From Daily Action to Compounding Business Memory

**Thesis:** daily work becomes durable Product value only when actions, evidence, outcomes, corrections and learning form governed memory.

**Primary lock:** AI inference ≠ memory ≠ shared learning.

## Part II — Content and brand commercialization

### CH05 — Content Must Begin with Professional Objects

**Thesis:** professional content should begin from governed source objects, Content Briefs and Claim Ledgers rather than empty prompts.

**Primary lock:** object existence ≠ publication authority.

### CH06 — From Trademark Record to Marketing Pack

**Thesis:** a Marketing Pack can translate verified trademark facts into bounded commercial imagination without becoming due diligence or sale authority.

**Primary lock:** Trademark ≠ Inventory ≠ Resale Authorization ≠ Listing ≠ Marketing Pack.

### CH07 — AI Visuals, Deterministic Rendering and Factual Control

**Thesis:** AI should generate visual possibility while deterministic composition protects exact marks, text, facts, labels and reproducibility.

**Primary lock:** AI source imagery ≠ controlled final asset.

### CH08 — Beautification, Continuity and Multi-professional Review

**Thesis:** restoration, conservative refinement, commercial variation and new-mark design must be distinguished and reviewed across visual, trademark and commercial dimensions.

**Primary lock:** visual quality ≠ trademark continuity ≠ commercialization fit.

## Part III — Governed trademark supply

### CH09 — Trademark Inventory Is a Governed Supply Object

**Thesis:** Inventory is a Workplace-scoped commercial relationship to a Trademark, not another legal status or proof of ownership.

**Primary lock:** Trademark record ≠ sellable supply.

### CH10 — Resale Authorization Before Visibility

**Thesis:** promotion, disclosure, negotiation and commission rights require structured, evidence-bearing Authorization before external visibility.

**Primary lock:** may promote ≠ may negotiate ≠ may accept ≠ may bind owner.

### CH11 — Listing as a Controlled Commercial Projection

**Thesis:** a Listing is a versioned commercial Projection with status, price, story, media and inquiry rules; it does not prove legal availability or transfer.

**Primary lock:** Listing ≠ transaction ≠ transfer.

### CH12 — Public, Network, Private and Selected Visibility

**Thesis:** visibility must be layered, field-scoped and purpose-limited rather than reduced to public/private.

**Primary lock:** can view ≠ can promote ≠ can contact ≠ owns relationship.

## Part IV — Buyer demand and matching

### CH13 — Trademark Requests Are Governed Demand Objects

**Thesis:** a Trademark Request is a versioned demand object preserving buyer context, Relationship Ownership, confidentiality and matching scope.

**Primary lock:** Request ≠ search query ≠ Order ≠ buyer commitment.

### CH14 — Clarification with Fewer Questions and More Inference

**Thesis:** Lite should extract and infer structure, ask only route-changing questions and preserve Unknown rather than force a long form.

**Primary lock:** silence ≠ informed confirmation.

### CH15 — Explainable Matching Across Name, Goods, Jurisdiction and Price

**Thesis:** matching must separate retrieval, eligibility and recommendation while exposing trade-offs across multiple dimensions.

**Primary lock:** retrieved ≠ eligible ≠ matched ≠ recommended.

### CH16 — Recommendation Is Not Availability, Clearance or Commitment

**Thesis:** a Recommendation is a time-bound, explainable candidate view requiring seller reconfirmation and professional review before substantive action.

**Primary lock:** Recommendation ≠ availability ≠ clearance ≠ reservation ≠ commitment.

## Part V — Inquiry and transaction formation

### CH17 — Inquiry Preserves Provenance and Relationship Ownership

**Thesis:** Inquiry must retain source versions, represented roles, contact permissions and Relationship Owners rather than become an unstructured message thread.

**Primary lock:** message access ≠ Relationship Ownership.

### CH18 — From Inquiry to Transaction Opportunity

**Thesis:** a Transaction Opportunity should be created only after qualification, authority reconfirmation, commercial fit and participant approval.

**Primary lock:** Inquiry ≠ Transaction Opportunity ≠ Engagement.

### CH19 — Due Diligence Before Commercial Commitment

**Thesis:** evidence, provenance, Unknowns and typed findings must be evaluated before material commercial commitment.

**Primary lock:** due diligence ≠ legal clearance or guarantee.

### CH20 — Transfer Handoff, Evidence and Completion

**Thesis:** agreement, payment, assignment execution, recordal, official acceptance and closing are separate evidence-backed events coordinated through typed Handoff.

**Primary lock:** payment or Provider statement ≠ title transfer or formal completion.

## Part VI — Service opportunity and customer resolution

### CH21 — Maintenance Leads Are Candidates, Not Customer Instructions

**Thesis:** data signals can create Candidate Leads, but verified need, relationship, outreach eligibility and customer instruction remain separate stages.

**Primary lock:** signal ≠ verified need ≠ customer instruction.

### CH22 — Entity and Contact Resolution with Evidence and Confidence

**Thesis:** legal entity, Organization, Customer relationship, Contact Person, role and contact point require purpose-scoped, reversible resolution.

**Primary lock:** contact found ≠ verified identity or permission.

### CH23 — Outreach Requires Relationship, Consent and Jurisdictional Rules

**Thesis:** outreach is a governed action requiring sender identity, purpose, relationship, consent, channel and jurisdictional policy.

**Primary lock:** public data ≠ marketing consent.

### CH24 — Lite-to-MarkReg Service Handoff Without Customer Transfer

**Thesis:** Lite can transfer bounded work context to MarkReg while preserving the originating Workplace's customer relationship and requiring independent Intake.

**Primary lock:** Handoff ≠ customer transfer ≠ Provider appointment ≠ filing authority.

## Part VII — Capability Center and paid Contributions

### CH25 — A Capability Map Must Be Evidence-backed and Perishable

**Thesis:** Capability must be scoped, evidence-backed, current and appealable rather than a permanent profile badge.

**Primary lock:** Capability ≠ qualification ≠ Assignment.

### CH26 — Learning in Work Without Confusing Guidance and Authority

**Thesis:** guided work can build capability inside real operations when modes, supervision, risk and escalation remain visible.

**Primary lock:** guidance ≠ authorization.

### CH27 — Simulation, Assessment and Certification Boundaries

**Thesis:** Simulation provides safe practice, Assessment tests a bounded claim and Certification recognizes evidence without creating statutory authority.

**Primary lock:** Certification ≠ professional license or task Assignment.

### CH28 — Paid Tasks Require Eligibility, Scope, Review and Fair Compensation

**Thesis:** paid professional work requires a transparent Work Package, eligibility, explicit Assignment, limited access, review, compensation and rights governance.

**Primary lock:** learning opportunity ≠ substitute for fair compensation.

## Part VIII — Business model and Product boundaries

### CH29 — Subscription Should Fund Ongoing Operating Capability

**Thesis:** subscription should fund maintained daily operating capability and governed continuity rather than meter every ordinary prompt.

**Primary lock:** subscription ≠ professional authority or data captivity.

### CH30 — Transaction and Human-service Revenue Must Remain Explicit

**Thesis:** subscription, promotion, transaction, professional service, Contribution and external cost require separate accountable roles and evidence.

**Primary lock:** payment ≠ completion; customer price ≠ contributor compensation.

### CH31 — Free, Pro and Business Editions Without Artificial Professional Scarcity

**Thesis:** editions should monetize scale, continuity, collaboration and governance without paywalling truth, safety, correction or portability.

**Primary lock:** Product edition ≠ professional status.

### CH32 — Lite, MarkReg, Sites and MGSN Must Remain Distinct Products

**Thesis:** seamless cross-Product experience should be created through typed Projections and Handoffs while each Product and Owning Service retains its authority.

**Primary lock:** continuity ≠ Product collapse or centralized business-state ownership.

## Closing thesis

Book 06 argues that Lite can become the daily economic operating surface for trademark professionals and small Workplaces only when commercial growth is joined to evidence, authority, relationship protection, product boundaries and correction-aware memory.

The manuscript does not propose growth at any cost. It proposes governed compounding:

```text
more credible visibility
+ trustworthy supply
+ structured demand
+ explainable matching
+ accountable opportunity
+ safer service conversion
+ evidence-backed capability
= durable Workplace growth
```
