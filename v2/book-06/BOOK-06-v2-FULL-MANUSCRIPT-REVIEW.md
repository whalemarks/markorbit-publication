## Product thesis test

The manuscript supports the intended dual core:

```text
content growth
+ trademark commerce
```

It also connects the dual core to:

```text
service opportunities
+ customer retention
+ capability growth
```

Lite is therefore not reduced to either a content tool or a trademark marketplace.

## Semantic locks preserved

- Lite is not a smaller MarkReg.
- Listing is not Trademark.
- Trademark Request is not Order.
- Opportunity is not confirmed customer consent.
- Handoff does not automatically transfer the customer relationship.
- AI-generated brand concepts are not legal facts or use evidence.
- Platform certification is not professional qualification.
- MGSN appointment remains governed.
- Existing active Canon and frozen Book 06 remain unchanged.

## Risks requiring later product decisions

1. The initial boundary between brokerage, transaction support and contract-party status.
2. Whether and when Lite may hold or route transaction funds.
3. The minimum evidence required for resale authorization.
4. Commission attribution when several Workplaces participate.
5. Public marketplace launch timing relative to private and network liquidity.
6. AI image and video cost controls under a non-metered capability promise.
7. Jurisdiction-specific advertising, privacy and outreach restrictions.

## Editorial judgment

The 103 short chapters provide strong concept coverage and clear implementation cues, but a publication edition should later consolidate them into approximately 35–45 longer chapters. Consolidation must preserve the object distinctions and product locks established here.
