# Book 06 v2
# MarkOrbit Lite — Growth, Trademark Commerce and Capability Workplace

## Purpose

Lite is the daily operating Product for independent trademark professionals and small teams.

Its commercial value comes from combining content, trademark assets, buyer demand, business opportunities and Capability growth inside one Workplace experience.

Lite is not a smaller MarkReg, a content-only AI tool, a generic learning platform, a Founder OS or a client portal.

## Product promise

> 有内容可发，有商标可卖，有需求可接，有客户可找，有能力可成长。

```text
Content creates attention.
Trademark assets create supply.
Requests create demand.
Matching creates opportunity.
Transactions create income.
Capability creates long-term professional growth.
```

## Part I — Today as an operating surface

### Chapter 01 — Today

The home experience answers:

- What should I publish today?
- What trademark should I promote today?
- Which buyer requests should I respond to?
- Which inquiries and service opportunities need follow-up?
- Which Capability should I practice or strengthen next?
- Which Reflection or Evidence needs confirmation?

Today should connect work, opportunity and learning rather than place Capability growth in a separate course library.

### Chapter 02 — Dynamic Workplace priorities

A marketing-led Workplace, asset-led Workplace and service-led Workplace may see different recommendations based on its Resource Profile, Need Profile, Capability Profile and current pipeline.

Lite may adapt guidance depth and review requirements according to evidenced proficiency, task risk and Production Authorization.

```text
Personalized Guidance
≠ Automatic Authority
```

## Part II — Content Studio

### Chapter 03 — Daily topic generation

Lite uses market Data, Knowledge, trademark inventory, service opportunities, Source Capabilities and prior performance to propose useful topics rather than generic copy.

A Source Capability may provide a disclosed lens. MO Mode may combine a Primary Capability with limited Supporting and Critic Capabilities to produce one coherent content plan.

### Chapter 04 — Trademark marketing pack

For a selected trademark, Lite may generate:

- positioning;
- brand story;
- suitable products;
- visual directions;
- product and store concepts;
- social posts;
- sales cards;
- video scripts;
- buyer recommendation reasons.

Legal facts, commercial concepts, Source-derived viewpoints and AI-generated visuals remain clearly separated.

### Chapter 05 — Static and motion rendering

Parameterized HTML / CSS templates, Playwright rendering, Remotion and FFmpeg produce consistent multi-channel assets. AI imagery supplies source visuals; deterministic templates protect text, logo and factual accuracy.

The rendering Skill implements a Capability. It is not itself the full Capability contract.

### Chapter 06 — Professional review

Users may request content, visual, trademark-continuity or commercial review from certified contributors. Human work is compensated separately from ordinary Capability use.

Review Evidence may support the user’s Capability Ledger when the review scope, contributor authority and user contribution are attributable.

## Part III — Trademark inventory and marketplace

### Chapter 07 — Trademark Inventory

Lite distinguishes:

- owned trademarks;
- client-authorized trademarks;
- entrusted resale assets;
- shared network listings;
- platform or MarkReg inventory.

### Chapter 08 — Resale Authorization

Every sellable asset records authorization basis, exclusivity, term, price constraints, commission, permitted channels, update obligation and Evidence.

### Chapter 09 — Listing

A Listing is not the Trademark itself. It is a sales projection with visibility, price, story, media, status, seller context and inquiry rules.

### Chapter 10 — Visibility

Listings may be public, network-only, private or shared with selected Workplaces.

Capability Evidence and user profile information are governed separately from Listing visibility.

## Part IV — Trademark Request and matching

### Chapter 11 — 寻标栏 / Trademark Request Board

A buyer request records name direction, industry, goods, class, jurisdiction, budget, acceptable variation, preferred style and expiry.

### Chapter 12 — Intelligent matching

Matching considers semantic fit, goods, class, jurisdiction, price, mark type, brand potential, legal status, authorization and seller constraints.

The matching Capability may use several Skills, but the user should receive one explainable route rather than a pile of ungoverned model outputs.

### Chapter 13 — Recommendation

Lite produces a short, explainable recommendation set rather than a large unranked list. Each Recommendation states fit, trade-offs, missing verification and which Source or MO Capability materially shaped the result.

```text
Recommendation
≠ Transaction Commitment
≠ Legal Conclusion
```

## Part V — Inquiry and transaction opportunity

### Chapter 14 — Buyer Inquiry

An Inquiry preserves source, buyer context, represented Workplace, requested asset, communication history and Relationship Ownership.

### Chapter 15 — Transaction Opportunity

When a buyer and asset show viable fit, Lite creates a Transaction Opportunity with participants, proposed price, commission logic, next steps and required due diligence.

### Chapter 16 — Engagement

The parties agree whether the transaction is referral, co-brokerage, white-label service, direct owner sale or MarkReg-assisted transfer.

### Chapter 17 — Due diligence and transfer

Lite may hand off due diligence, agreement preparation, payment milestones and transfer recordal to MarkReg or qualified Providers.

A successful transaction may become a Case Candidate only after customer data, commercial rights and participant permissions are reviewed.

## Part VI — Business opportunities

### Chapter 18 — Maintenance leads

Data Refinery and Brain identify renewal, declaration, status anomaly, ownership-change and re-filing opportunities. Leads remain Candidates until verified.

### Chapter 19 — Applicant and contact resolution

Entity and contact discovery preserves Evidence, confidence and privacy. Candidate contact data is not treated as confirmed identity.

### Chapter 20 — Allocation and follow-up

Workplaces may claim, assign or schedule opportunities under internal policy. Outreach content is generated from verified facts and approved communication rules.

Opportunity work can support Capability growth only when the user’s actual contribution, review result and Outcome are attributable.

## Part VII — Capability Center

### Chapter 21 — My Capability Profile

Lite presents a Product-local Capability Profile for the trademark professional.

It may show:

- Capabilities discovered or learned;
- Skills available for guided use;
- practiced and independently performed work;
- accepted Cases and Evidence;
- certification, expiry and Production Authorization;
- Professional Qualification references;
- current gaps and recommended progression;
- recent Capability versions used.

The experience may use a map, tree or orbit view, but the visualization does not create semantic truth.

```text
Capability Profile
≠ Public Ranking
≠ Professional Qualification
≠ Permanent Judgment of the User
```

### Chapter 22 — Agent Capability Twin

The Agent Capability Twin is Lite’s evidence-bounded projection of how the user currently works and develops as a trademark professional.

It may support:

- task-specific guidance;
- choice of Lens, Council or MO Mode;
- stronger review for unfamiliar work;
- suggestions for simulation or practice;
- identification of relevant Cases and Principles;
- explanation of why a task remains outside current Production Authorization.

It is not a personality clone and should not pretend to know the user beyond the authorized Evidence.

```text
Agent Capability Twin
≠ Personality Replica
≠ Autonomous Agent Acting as the User
≠ Universal Competence Score
```

### Chapter 23 — Learning in work

When a user meets an unfamiliar task, Lite may switch to guided mode, recommend a Source Skill, create a controlled Capability Composition, suggest simulation or require stronger review.

After a meaningful invocation, Execution may return a Session Receipt containing Capability and Skill versions, Outcome, Evidence and user disposition.

Lite may propose a Reflection Candidate asking:

- What did you adopt?
- What did you change or reject?
- What happened?
- What would you do differently?
- May this remain private, enter the team, or become an anonymized learning candidate?

The user or authorized Workplace reviews the Reflection before it enters the Capability Ledger.

```text
AI-generated Reflection Draft
≠ User-confirmed Reflection
≠ Platform Principle
```

### Chapter 24 — AI Mentor

AI Mentor connects work with the next useful Capability step.

It may recommend:

- one Capability to strengthen;
- one relevant Case or Source Capability;
- a simulation before real work;
- a Supporting or Critic Capability missing from the current approach;
- professional review for a risky decision;
- evidence needed to substantiate progression.

The Mentor should explain its basis, uncertainty and limits.

```text
Mentor Recommendation
≠ Assessment Decision
≠ Certification
≠ Professional Authority
```

It should not tell a user that one business decision proves success or failure from a single Outcome.

### Chapter 25 — Simulation and certification

Lite provides entry to de-identified Cases, simulated clients, assessments and certification paths such as trademark commerce, content growth and US maintenance.

The progression path may be:

```text
Learn
→ Guided Practice
→ Simulation
→ Shadow Work
→ Supervised Production
→ Bounded Independent Work
→ Advanced Review
```

Certification proves assessed MarkOrbit work competence. Production Authorization and Professional Qualification remain separate.

### Chapter 26 — Capability Ledger and growth evidence

The Lite Capability Ledger records authorized Product-local references to:

- practice and invocation;
- Session Receipts;
- confirmed Reflection;
- accepted Cases;
- simulation and assessment;
- certification;
- paid-task contribution;
- Capability version exposure.

XP or progression indicators may motivate learning, but they should represent defined Product events rather than a universal measure of intelligence or professional worth.

Private Reflection remains private unless the user or Workplace authorizes broader use.

### Chapter 27 — Paid tasks

Qualified users may receive bounded verification, search, visual, content, assessment or MarkReg production tasks.

Task Eligibility depends on Capability Evidence, current authorization, risk, capacity and review policy. It is separate from Product subscription.

A paid task can create new Evidence, but completion alone does not automatically upgrade the user or the underlying Capability.

```text
Paid Task Completed
≠ Capability Verified
≠ Professional Qualification Granted
```

## Part VIII — Business model

### Chapter 28 — Subscription

Lite subscription provides ongoing operating Capabilities and is not metered by ordinary invocation count.

Capability guidance, basic Reflection and the user’s own Capability Profile should not be artificially crippled to force transactional payment.

### Chapter 29 — Training, certification and human-service revenue

Separate revenue may arise from training, simulation, certification, expert assessment, professional review and paid human contribution.

Users pay for structured development, governed review and real human work—not for the platform silently claiming ownership of their Capability Profile.

### Chapter 30 — Transaction revenue

Additional revenue may arise from brokerage, transaction service, transfer support and MarkReg-assisted delivery.

Transaction economics and Capability-growth evidence remain connected but separately governed.

### Chapter 31 — Free, Pro and Business

Edition differences should reflect Workplace scale, collaboration, data scope, Capability governance, brand projection and operating controls—not artificial restriction of basic professional learning.

## Part IX — Product boundaries

### Chapter 32 — Lite and MarkReg

Lite discovers, grows and organizes opportunity; MarkReg may perform complete lifecycle delivery.

MarkReg Outcomes and official Evidence may return to Lite through governed Session Receipt, Reflection and Case pathways. Handoff does not automatically transfer the customer relationship.

### Chapter 33 — Lite and Sites

Sites publishes controlled brand, service, content, Listing and selected Capability projections. It does not become Customer, Order, Capability-assessment or Payment authority.

### Chapter 34 — Lite and MGSN

Lite may initiate a managed professional-service Need through the Workplace’s MGSN Connection, but it does not directly allocate unrestricted Providers.

Provider Supply Capability is distinct from the user’s Agent Capability Profile.

### Chapter 35 — Lite and Founder OS

Lite remains a trademark-professional Capability Workplace.

A future Founder-facing Product may reuse Capability, Skill, Reflection, Case, Principle, Ledger and Twin semantics across Vision, Brand, Product, Market, Sales, Operations, Finance, People and Expansion.

```text
Founder OS
≠ Lite Rebranding
≠ New Capability Domain
```
