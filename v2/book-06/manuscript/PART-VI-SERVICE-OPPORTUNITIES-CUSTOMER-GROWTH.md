# Part VI — Service Opportunities and Customer Growth

## Chapter 56 — Lite should discover business, not only organize it

A daily operating product becomes more valuable when it helps identify which customers or trademark records may require action.

Potential opportunities include:

- renewal and declaration windows;
- ownership or address changes;
- status anomalies;
- abandoned marks that may need re-filing;
- missing classes or jurisdictions;
- trademark transfer needs;
- evidence and use-risk concerns;
- buyer demand related to a customer's portfolio;
- content topics linked to current customer interests.

Every opportunity remains a candidate until verified.

## Chapter 57 — Opportunity provenance

A service opportunity should record:

- source dataset;
- source record and version;
- rule or reasoning path;
- confidence;
- relevant dates;
- customer or entity match confidence;
- missing verification;
- permitted use and outreach policy.

An opportunity without provenance should not drive automated customer contact.

## Chapter 58 — Customer and entity resolution

Official records, internal customers and public contact data may not align.

Lite may propose candidate links using:

- names;
- addresses;
- company identifiers;
- historical filings;
- agent mentions;
- domains and contact evidence.

The system must distinguish:

```text
candidate entity match
confirmed customer link
public business contact
private customer contact
```

## Chapter 59 — Opportunity qualification

A good opportunity is not merely technically valid.

It should be evaluated for:

- actual customer relevance;
- timing;
- expected service value;
- relationship ownership;
- professional capability;
- likelihood of response;
- contact legitimacy;
- risk of misleading outreach.

## Chapter 60 — Outreach preparation

Lite may prepare:

- internal opportunity summary;
- customer-facing explanation;
- deadline and risk statement;
- service options;
- quotation inputs;
- follow-up schedule;
- required documents.

Communications must be reviewed according to Workplace policy before sending.

## Chapter 61 — Relationship-aware allocation

Within a team, opportunities may be allocated based on:

- existing customer owner;
- industry knowledge;
- jurisdiction capability;
- current workload;
- language;
- prior communication;
- conflict and permission.

Allocation should not silently remove the original relationship owner.

## Chapter 62 — Lead is not Order

Lite must preserve the distinction:

```text
Opportunity
→ verified customer interest
→ Engagement or service proposal
→ Order
```

A predicted need is not customer consent. A generated quotation is not an accepted order.

## Chapter 63 — Lite and MarkReg service handoff

When a customer accepts a complete trademark service, Lite may create a Handoff to MarkReg.

The Handoff should preserve:

- originating Workplace;
- Relationship Owner;
- customer context;
- verified need;
- approved service scope;
- pricing and brand mode;
- communication policy;
- expected status and result Return.

## Chapter 64 — White-label growth

A small agency may use MarkReg production while retaining its customer-facing brand and relationship.

Lite should let the agency:

- prepare and approve the solution;
- control customer communication;
- see delivery status;
- receive structured questions;
- approve changes;
- deliver the result under the agreed model.

White-label does not mean hidden responsibility. Internal responsibility and professional authority must remain recorded.

## Chapter 65 — Customer memory

Lite should retain a governed memory of:

- customer industries;
- trademark portfolio;
- prior services;
- communication preferences;
- price sensitivity;
- unresolved needs;
- declined recommendations;
- consent and outreach restrictions.

Customer memory belongs to the Workplace business context.

## Chapter 66 — Growth without spam

The product should refuse to optimize solely for outbound volume.

Good growth is based on:

- verified relevance;
- legitimate relationship or contact basis;
- clear source;
- useful explanation;
- controlled frequency;
- easy correction and opt-out;
- honest uncertainty.

## Chapter 67 — Opportunity outcomes

Outcomes may include:

- contacted;
- interested;
- not relevant;
- already handled;
- postponed;
- converted;
- no response;
- incorrect entity match;
- prohibited outreach.

These outcomes improve future opportunity quality.

## Chapter 68 — Growth metrics

Lite should measure:

- verified opportunities;
- response quality;
- conversion to Engagement;
- conversion to Order;
- customer retention;
- repeat services;
- false-positive rate;
- time from detection to action;
- revenue by opportunity source;
- customer complaints or opt-outs.
