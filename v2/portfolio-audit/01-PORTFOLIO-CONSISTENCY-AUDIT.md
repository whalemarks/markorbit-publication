# Portfolio Consistency Audit

## 1. Executive conclusion

Books 01–07 v2 now share one defensible architecture:

```text
Data
→ Knowledge
→ Brain
→ Capability
→ Execution
→ Workplace
→ Product Installations
→ Professional Contribution Network / MGSN
→ Outcome, Evidence and Learning
```

The corpus is no longer centered on a single product, database, workflow engine or marketplace. Capability is consistently treated as the durable outcome contract; Workplace is consistently treated as the business-sovereignty boundary; Execution is consistently treated as the trust engine; Products remain distinct operating experiences; MGSN remains a governed external professional network.

The rewrite therefore passes the strategic-coherence gate.

It does not yet pass the publication-authority gate because several candidate shared semantics remain unactivated and the manuscripts retain duplicated short-form chapters, provisional terminology and unresolved implementation ownership.

## 2. Strongest areas of consistency

### 2.1 Platform identity

Book 01 defines MarkOrbit as a capability-centered operating system for global brand professional services. Books 03–07 consistently implement that identity rather than reverting to a generic SaaS, agency marketplace or content generator.

### 2.2 Workplace sovereignty

Books 01, 04, 05, 06 and 07 consistently preserve:

- the originating customer relationship;
- engagement-specific roles;
- minimum necessary disclosure;
- Product Installation boundaries;
- separation between platform custody and business ownership.

### 2.3 Capability and qualification

Across the corpus:

```text
Capability Certification
≠ Production Authorization
≠ Professional Qualification
```

This distinction is preserved in Lite, MarkReg, Execution and MGSN.

### 2.4 Formal-state authority

Books 02 and 03 consistently state:

```text
Core defines meaning.
Execution governs action.
Owning Services mutate formal business state.
```

Products and Brain do not silently acquire formal-state authority.

### 2.5 Network boundaries

The Professional Contribution Network and MGSN remain distinct:

- bounded contribution, review, verification, simulation and MarkReg production belong to the contribution network;
- accountable external professional fulfillment belongs to MGSN.

### 2.6 Lite commercial thesis

Book 06 is consistent with Book 01’s economic argument:

```text
content creates attention
trademark assets create supply
requests create demand
matching creates opportunity
transactions and services create income
capability creates long-term growth
```

Lite is no longer dependent on content generation alone.

## 3. Material inconsistencies or unresolved tensions

## 3.1 Capability Fabric versus Capability System

The corpus uses both terms.

Recommendation:

- `Capability System` for the whole human, AI, learning, certification and dispatch system;
- `Capability Fabric` for the runtime composition and binding layer;
- `Capability Definition` for the stable shared contract;
- `Capability Unit` for a taxonomy entry.

## 3.2 Professional Contribution Network scope

Some passages include MGSN under the broad contribution-network diagram; others define it as separate.

Canonical resolution:

```text
MarkOrbit Professional Network
├── Professional Contribution Network
└── MGSN Professional Service Network
```

MGSN should not be a child pool inside the bounded contribution network.

## 3.3 Need and Request

Lite uses `Trademark Request`; MGSN uses `Capability Need Projection`; the Core reconciliation introduces candidate `Need`.

Canonical resolution:

```text
Need = shared candidate semantic origin
Trademark Request = Lite specialization of Need
Capability Need Projection = MGSN projection of an authorized Need or Matter requirement
```

Until Core activation, Products may maintain local records with explicit mapping.

## 3.4 Engagement and Contract

Engagement is central in Books 04, 05, 06 and 07, but its relation to legal Contract remains underdefined.

Canonical resolution:

- Engagement is the operational collaboration boundary;
- Contract is a legal or commercial instrument where applicable;
- one Engagement may reference zero, one or multiple Contracts;
- Contract does not replace engagement-specific roles, disclosure or delivery responsibility.

## 3.5 Outcome versus Delivery

Books use `Outcome`, `Delivery`, `Return` and `formal state` in related but different ways.

Canonical resolution:

```text
Contribution = submitted work
Outcome = reviewed result accepted for a declared purpose
Delivery = customer- or participant-facing transmission of an Outcome or artifact
Return = typed result/evidence sent back across a Handoff
Formal State = accepted business fact owned by an Owning Service
```

## 3.6 Provider and Contributor

The distinction is coherent conceptually but some product prose risks using “professional” generically.

Canonical resolution:

- Contributor is an engagement or assignment role for bounded work;
- Provider is an accountable Organization profile for external service fulfillment;
- Professional is a broad human descriptor and never sufficient to infer authority.

## 3.7 MarkReg’s platform role

MarkReg is described as flagship product, self-operated service, white-label production system and proving ground. This is coherent, but the commercial center-of-gravity risk remains.

Required lock:

> MarkReg may be the strongest reference implementation and demand source without becoming the compulsory contracting party, relationship owner or fulfillment route for other Workplaces.

## 3.8 Book 08 question

The capability and collaboration material is currently distributed across Books 01–07. This avoids premature portfolio expansion, but Book 04 and Book 03 carry very large capability-network responsibilities.

Recommendation:

Do not create Book 08 during the current authority cycle. First stabilize the shared contracts and pilot implementations. Reassess Book 08 after the first two operating loops generate evidence.

## 5. Ten-year investment assessment

The rewrite now identifies five compounding assets:

1. governed global trademark and entity data;
2. sourced and versioned professional knowledge;
3. reusable Capability Definitions and evidence;
4. protected Workplace relationships and transaction demand;
5. provider, contributor and outcome trust evidence.

These assets reinforce each other and justify the operating-system thesis.

The main investment danger is now not lack of vision but simultaneous execution across too many layers. The portfolio should therefore remain locked to two initial loops:

```text
Lite trademark-commerce loop
US maintenance capability and production loop
```
