# Chapter 17 — Two Professional Networks, Two Kinds of Responsibility

MarkOrbit needs more than one professional network because professional work does not have one uniform authority model.

Some work is bounded platform production: checking data, reviewing a search result, preparing content, validating a visual, assessing a simulation or completing a standard MarkReg work package.

Other work is external professional fulfillment: filing before an authority, issuing a legal opinion, acting as local representative, notarizing a document, conducting an investigation or performing another service for which an Organization bears formal responsibility.

Treating both categories as one marketplace would blur qualification, liability, payment and customer relationships.

## 1. The Professional Contribution Network

The Professional Contribution Network enables qualified people and Workplaces to perform bounded units of platform work.

Typical contributions include:

- data verification;
- entity-resolution review;
- search-result analysis;
- goods and services classification review;
- document preparation;
- content and visual judgment;
- simulation assessment;
- quality review;
- standard MarkReg production;
- specialist opinion that will be reviewed before use.

The central object is not a provider listing. It is a governed Work Package.

```text
Work Package
→ eligibility
→ Assignment
→ Contribution
→ Review
→ Outcome
→ compensation and evidence
```

The contributor receives only the minimum information required for the work. The contributor does not automatically acquire the full customer file, the originating relationship or the authority to perform an external protected action.

## 2. MGSN

MGSN handles formal external professional fulfillment.

The accountable actor is usually a Provider Organization. The Provider may rely on named professionals, but the engagement is accepted under an organizational and professional responsibility structure.

Examples include:

- local trademark filing;
- official representation;
- legal response or opinion;
- notarization or legalization;
- investigation;
- certified translation where required;
- local payment or procedural handling;
- certificate procurement or official record action.

MGSN therefore requires provider admission, professional qualification, appointment, Instruction Package, funds controls, evidence Return and recovery.

## 3. Why one person may appear in both networks

A trademark lawyer may review a simulated office action as a paid contribution and separately act through a law firm as the formally appointed local representative.

These are not the same engagement.

In the first role, the lawyer may be a Contributor or Reviewer under a bounded Assignment. In the second, the lawyer or firm acts as a Provider under local professional authority.

The platform must preserve separate:

- identities and represented Organizations;
- authority sources;
- data scopes;
- commercial terms;
- insurance or liability context;
- appointment status;
- evidence obligations.

## 4. Certification is not qualification

MarkOrbit may certify that a person can perform a capability to a demonstrated standard.

That certification can improve eligibility for platform work. It cannot create a license granted by a government, bar, professional body or other competent authority.

```text
Capability Certification
≠ Production Authorization
≠ Professional Qualification
```

Production Authorization determines whether the platform permits a person to perform a category of real work under specified supervision and review.

Professional Qualification determines whether the person or Organization may lawfully perform reserved work.

## 5. Different trust models

Contribution trust focuses on:

- capability evidence;
- assessment results;
- review quality;
- error and revision patterns;
- scope discipline;
- timeliness;
- tool and source use;
- evidence completeness.

Provider trust additionally focuses on:

- legal identity;
- current qualification;
- conflicts handling;
- acceptance behavior;
- official submission evidence;
- funds handling;
- communication;
- correction and dispute history;
- active-matter continuity.

A universal star score cannot represent these differences.

## 6. Different commercial models

A contribution may be compensated by task fee, hourly reference, review fee, revenue share or other bounded arrangement.

A Provider engagement may include:

- official fees;
- professional fees;
- taxes;
- disbursements;
- staged payments;
- contingency items;
- refund or replacement policy.

Combining both into one price column would hide the nature of the commitment.

## 7. Customer relationship boundaries

A Contributor normally should not receive customer contact details unless the Assignment requires direct communication.

A Provider may need customer identity, documents and direct professional communication. That access still does not transfer the customer relationship.

The engagement should identify:

- Relationship Owner;
- Contracting Party;
- Payment Receiver;
- Delivery Owner;
- Professional Authority;
- permitted communication route.

## 8. White-label does not erase responsibility

A partner may use MarkReg under a white-label model while work is completed by contributors and local providers.

The customer-facing presentation may remain under the partner’s brand. But legal and professional disclosures must remain accurate. The system must not imply that an unqualified party performed a reserved action.

White-label changes presentation and relationship structure. It does not change reality.

## 9. No hidden labor arbitrage

A professional contribution network can easily become exploitative if the platform uses training language to obtain free production or routes work only to the cheapest participant.

MarkOrbit should require:

- explicit distinction between practice and production;
- compensation for accepted production contributions;
- transparent review expectations;
- no retroactive expansion of scope;
- appeal or dispute path;
- evidence portability where permitted;
- progression based on quality, not volume alone.

## 10. Why the distinction matters strategically

The Professional Contribution Network expands productive capacity inside the operating system.

MGSN extends the operating system into jurisdictions and reserved professional environments that the platform cannot own directly.

Together they create a broader MarkOrbit Professional Network, but they must remain separately governed.

```text
Professional Contribution Network
= bounded platform work

MGSN
= accountable external professional fulfillment
```

This distinction lets MarkOrbit scale without pretending that every worker is a provider, every provider is a contributor or every professional action belongs to the platform.
