## Material editorial risks

### 1. Repetition

Core distinctions recur across chapters because they are load-bearing. Final editing should reduce unnecessary repetition while retaining the important locks.

### 2. Terminology density

Readers unfamiliar with the architecture may encounter many defined terms. A glossary, diagrams and reader apparatus will be required.

### 3. Insufficient external evidence

The manuscript is primarily a platform thesis, not an academic industry study. A final public edition should add carefully sourced market evidence and selected external cases without allowing those cases to dominate the architecture.

### 4. Prior-edition reconciliation

No valuable concept from the previous Book 01 should be lost merely because it is absent from the reconstructed narrative. A source-by-source disposition register remains mandatory.

### 5. Product proof

The manuscript may sound more mature than the implemented Products. Publication language must clearly distinguish architecture, proposed operating model, controlled pilot and proven production.
