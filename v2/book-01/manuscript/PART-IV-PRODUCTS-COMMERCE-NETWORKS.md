# Part IV — Products, Commerce and Networks

## Chapter 27 — Products are capability arrangements

A MarkOrbit product is not defined only by its pages or codebase.

It is a governed arrangement of capabilities, data access, user roles, workflows, projections and commercial promises for a specific audience.

The same Capability may appear differently in different products.

For example, a trademark-maintenance assessment may appear as:

- a professional workflow in Lite;
- a full service order in MarkReg;
- a client-facing status explanation;
- a training simulation;
- a bounded review task for a certified contributor;
- an instruction package for an MGSN provider.

The Product Installation determines which form is enabled in a particular Workplace.

## Chapter 28 — Lite is a growth and commerce product

Lite serves independent trademark professionals and small teams whose daily challenge is not only case administration. They need visibility, supply, opportunities, customer follow-up and a way to turn professional activity into revenue.

Lite should answer four questions every day:

```text
What should I publish today?
What can I sell today?
Which buyer or customer demand should I respond to?
Which service opportunity should I follow up?
```

Its durable core is:

- Content Studio;
- trademark inventory and resale authorization;
- trademark marketplace views;
- buyer request board;
- matching and recommendation;
- inquiry and transaction opportunity management;
- business opportunity discovery;
- capability learning and contribution access.

Lite is not a smaller MarkReg. It is the operating surface for growth, commerce and professional leverage.

## Chapter 29 — Content becomes valuable when it is connected

Content Studio should not generate generic posts detached from the user's business.

It should begin from controlled objects:

- a trademark listing;
- a trademark request;
- a maintenance opportunity;
- a jurisdiction change;
- a professional capability;
- an anonymized success story;
- a service package.

For a trademark asset, the system may combine legal facts with clearly labeled concept material:

```text
verified trademark facts
+ proposed brand positioning
+ AI-generated product and visual concepts
+ channel-specific copy
+ professional review
→ publishable sales assets
```

The distinction between fact and concept must remain visible. A concept product, store or packaging image is not evidence that the trademark is used or that a business exists.

## Chapter 30 — Trademark commerce is a managed process

A trademark database record is not the same as a sellable asset.

The commerce chain requires distinct objects:

```text
Trademark
→ Trademark Inventory
→ Resale Authorization
→ Listing
→ Buyer Request
→ Match Candidate
→ Inquiry
→ Transaction Opportunity
→ Due Diligence
→ Transfer Service
→ Settlement
```

A Workplace may expose a listing publicly, only to an approved network, or only to selected collaborators.

The system must preserve:

- ownership evidence;
- authorization scope;
- price and commission rules;
- listing freshness;
- buyer-source attribution;
- relationship protection;
- transaction status;
- professional transfer requirements.

## Chapter 31 — Demand is more valuable than passive inventory

Large listing volume is not sufficient to create a liquid market.

The buyer request board should allow a professional to describe:

- desired name characteristics;
- industry and goods;
- classes;
- jurisdiction;
- budget;
- acceptable variation;
- urgency;
- preference for word or device marks;
- need for brand concepts or transfer support.

Brain can convert the request into structured matching criteria and retrieve candidates from private, network and public inventory.

The professional remains responsible for deciding which candidates should be shown and how the recommendation is explained.

## Chapter 32 — MarkReg is the flagship lifecycle product

MarkReg is where the full international trademark service promise is organized.

It supports:

- intake and strategy;
- search and filing preparation;
- application and prosecution;
- publication, registration and maintenance;
- transfer, change and recovery;
- customer communication;
- provider instruction;
- evidence and delivery.

MarkReg can operate as a self-managed customer-facing service, a white-label production service for another Workplace, or a governed component in a co-delivery engagement.

It must not automatically take ownership of a partner Workplace's customer relationship.

## Chapter 33 — MarkReg can become a production proving ground

As MarkReg order volume grows, it will require more human capacity.

The correct response is not uncontrolled outsourcing. It is controlled decomposition.

An order may be divided into Work Packages such as:

- status verification;
- ownership reconciliation;
- goods-use questionnaire review;
- evidence screening;
- customer follow-up draft;
- attorney instruction preparation;
- filing receipt verification;
- final delivery report.

Certified users with appropriate production authorization may complete bounded tasks for compensation. Higher-risk conclusions and official actions remain subject to qualified review and legal authority.

This creates a progression path from simulation to paid professional work while expanding MarkReg capacity.

## Chapter 34 — The Professional Contribution Network

Not every contribution requires a full order or external provider relationship.

The Professional Contribution Network supports small or specialized contributions across the platform:

```text
Data Verification Pool
Search and Analysis Pool
Visual and Brand Review Pool
Content Review Pool
Knowledge Contribution Pool
Simulation and Assessment Pool
MarkReg Production Pool
Expert Review Panel
```

The network should match by capability, level, jurisdiction, risk authorization, language, capacity, quality history and conflict status.

It is not an open gig marketplace.

## Chapter 35 — MGSN is the formal service network

MGSN serves work that requires an accountable external provider, such as a local trademark agency, law firm, translator, investigator or other professional service organization.

The canonical path is:

```text
Originating Workplace
→ authorized capability need projection
→ MGSN Connection
→ managed matching and route recommendation
→ provider acceptance
→ governed instruction
→ provider execution
→ evidence and typed Return
→ originating validation
```

MGSN differs from the Professional Contribution Network because the provider may assume formal local responsibility, maintain its own Workplace and operate under procurement, qualification and fulfillment controls.

## Chapter 36 — Collaboration must preserve the customer relationship

A single engagement may involve:

- the party who originated demand;
- the party who owns the customer relationship;
- the contracting party;
- the solution orchestrator;
- several capability contributors;
- the professional authority;
- the delivery owner;
- the payment receiver.

These roles must be explicit.

Use of MarkOrbit, MarkReg, a certified contributor or MGSN does not automatically transfer the customer relationship.

A local agency should be able to retain its customer while using MarkReg white-label delivery and an MGSN provider. A specialist should be able to contribute expertise without acquiring unrestricted access to the customer. A marketer should be able to originate demand without pretending to hold professional authority.

## Chapter 37 — The platform is not a free-for-all marketplace

MarkOrbit should not optimize for unrestricted contact, public customer data or lowest-price bidding.

Professional collaboration requires controlled disclosure and clear responsibility.

The platform should use:

```text
bounded projection
→ eligibility filtering
→ engagement formation
→ minimum necessary access
→ governed execution
→ review and return
→ settlement and evidence
```

This approach may grow more slowly than an open directory, but it builds trust and protects the conditions required for long-term participation.
