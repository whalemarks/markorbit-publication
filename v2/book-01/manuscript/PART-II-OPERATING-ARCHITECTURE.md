# Part II — The Operating Architecture

## Chapter 07 — Orbital architecture

MarkOrbit uses the orbital metaphor because the platform must preserve independence while enabling coordinated movement.

```text
Each in its own orbit.
Connected by capability.
Evolving together.
```

An Organization operates through one or more accountable Workplaces. A Workplace preserves its customer relationships, private knowledge, commercial policies, internal permissions and product context. It may project a bounded need, capability, asset or result outward, but it does not surrender its entire business context.

The orbital model rejects two extremes:

- isolated software islands that cannot cooperate;
- a platform monolith that claims ownership of every relationship and record.

Connection occurs through typed contracts, permissions, execution and evidence.

## Chapter 08 — The constitutional spine

The long-term architecture is expressed as a responsibility spine:

```text
Industry and professional philosophy
→ Core semantic authority
→ Data and Knowledge factories
→ Brain intelligence
→ Capability fabric
→ Execution governance
→ Workplace sovereignty
→ Product Installations
→ Contribution and service networks
→ Evidence-driven evolution
```

This is not a mandatory physical deployment sequence. It explains who defines meaning, who supplies information, who performs reasoning, who packages competence, who governs action and who bears business responsibility.

## Chapter 09 — Five forms of authority

The word owner is too imprecise for a professional operating system.

MarkOrbit separates at least five forms of authority.

### Business sovereignty

Who controls the customer relationship, private pricing, commercial policy, internal context and accountable business decision.

The default holder is the relevant Organization through its Workplace.

### Semantic authority

Who defines the controlled meaning of objects, fields, states and contracts.

The default holder is Core and the accepted specification chain.

### Formal-state authority

Who may create or mutate an accepted business fact.

The default holder is the applicable Owning Service acting through governed Execution.

### Physical custody

Who stores, transmits, indexes or backs up data.

A platform may physically host data without acquiring the customer relationship or commercial sovereignty.

### Legal and source authority

Who possesses official, evidentiary, privacy or professional authority.

This may be a government office, customer, lawyer, agent, provider, author or other source holder.

Every major MarkOrbit workflow must preserve these distinctions.

## Chapter 10 — The two factories

MarkOrbit needs two independent information factories because structured facts and professional knowledge behave differently.

### Data Refinery

The Data Refinery transforms official and market data into canonical, jurisdiction-aware data products.

```text
source registration
→ download or capture
→ immutable raw archive
→ parsing
→ normalization
→ validation
→ deduplication
→ entity resolution
→ event reconstruction
→ publication
```

Its products include trademark records, lifecycle events, applicant and owner entities, agent mentions, opportunity datasets, transaction inventory and goods semantics.

### Knowledge Refinery

The Knowledge Refinery transforms laws, regulations, official guidance, professional articles, questions, correspondence and internal practice into sourced Markdown knowledge.

```text
source registration
→ crawl or import
→ raw capture
→ extraction
→ classification
→ distillation
→ Markdown
→ version comparison
→ review
→ vault publication
```

Obsidian may be a human workspace for the resulting vault, but it is not the sole database or source of truth.

## Chapter 11 — Brain is intelligence, not authority

Mo Brain combines structured retrieval, semantic retrieval, relationship graphs and provenance.

It may:

- connect a registration date with a maintenance rule;
- identify likely service opportunities;
- explain why a trademark matches a buyer request;
- recommend content topics;
- compare jurisdiction practices;
- retrieve relevant cases and professional guidance.

It may not silently convert a suggestion into formal truth.

```text
AI conclusion
≠ official fact

retrieved document
≠ current applicable rule without validation

recommended action
≠ authorized professional decision
```

Brain produces intelligence. Capability turns intelligence into a defined professional outcome.

## Chapter 12 — Capability as the durable unit

A Capability is a stable outcome contract.

It defines:

- the user outcome;
- accepted inputs;
- required data and knowledge;
- permitted implementation modes;
- human and AI responsibilities;
- review requirements;
- output artifacts;
- evidence expectations;
- legal or professional restrictions.

The implementation may change from one model, tool or provider to another. The user-facing outcome and governance contract remain stable.

For example, `GenerateTrademarkMarketingPack` may use a language model, image model, template renderer, trademark database and professional reviewer. None of those components alone is the Capability.

## Chapter 13 — Execution is the discipline of action

Execution converts capability into governed work.

It manages:

```text
request
→ eligibility
→ preview
→ assignment
→ execution
→ contribution
→ review
→ acceptance or revision
→ formal mutation where authorized
→ outcome
→ evidence
```

Execution must preserve failure, retry and unknown states. A network request that times out is not automatically failed or successful. A returned result is not automatically accepted as formal truth. A professional contribution is not automatically canonical knowledge.

## Chapter 14 — Workplace is the business orbit

Workplace is the Organization-scoped boundary for:

- membership and permissions;
- customers and relationships;
- private pricing and margin;
- internal knowledge and documents;
- professional decisions;
- Product Installations;
- authorized projections;
- audit and accountability.

Workplace is not merely a dashboard, tenant label or product theme.

A person may hold an independent identity and capability record while participating in several Workplaces. Every action must state which Workplace is represented and which authority is being exercised.

## Chapter 15 — Product Installations and projections

A Product participates in a Workplace through a Product Installation.

A Product Installation defines:

- edition and lifecycle;
- enabled capabilities;
- entitlement;
- configuration;
- permission mappings;
- data scope;
- projection scope;
- connector policy.

A projection exposes a purpose-limited view beyond the originating context. It does not transfer ownership by default.

Examples include:

- a public trademark listing;
- a buyer request shared with an approved network;
- a client status view;
- an MGSN capability need;
- a bounded work package for a certified contributor.

The architecture must always ask: what is being projected, for what purpose, to whom, for how long and under whose authority?
