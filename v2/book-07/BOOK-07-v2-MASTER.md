# Book 07 v2
# Mark Global Service Network — Managed Professional Fulfillment

## Purpose

MGSN is the platform-owned and platform-governed global professional service network. It supplies formally accountable external fulfillment when a Workplace or Product needs local lawyers, agents, translators, investigators, notaries or other professional Organizations.

MGSN is not an open Provider directory, free participant graph, lowest-price bidding market or substitute for Professional Qualification.

MGSN also produces valuable operating Evidence: Acceptance behavior, Milestones, Provider Returns, Corrections, Incidents, recovery and verified Outcomes. This Evidence may support controlled Capability evolution, but the Network does not automatically convert Provider performance into shared Capability canon.

```text
MGSN Engagement Evidence
≠ Capability Verification
≠ Automatic Capability Upgrade
```

## Part I — Network constitution

### Chapter 01 — Why a managed network

Global trademark work fails not only because information is missing, but because Provider identity, Qualification, price, Instruction, funds, deadline, Evidence and recovery are poorly governed. MGSN converts informal overseas-agent relationships into managed fulfillment.

### Chapter 02 — Connection versus Network

A Workplace uses an MGSN Connection or Gateway to project authorized demand and receive governed Returns. The platform-owned Network controls Provider admission, supply, procurement terms, matching, Trust, fulfillment and settlement.

### Chapter 03 — Network authority

MGSN may manage routing, Package terms, platform Trust and fulfillment Evidence. It does not automatically own the Originating Workplace’s customer relationship or private commercial context.

MGSN may contribute attributed evidence candidates to Capability governance. It does not own the user’s Capability Profile, private Reflection or the Core Capability Definition.

## Part II — Provider supply

### Chapter 04 — Provider Organization

A Provider is an accountable Organization operating through a Provider Workplace. Individual professionals, Qualifications and authorized representatives are linked but not substituted for the Organization’s responsibility.

### Chapter 05 — Admission

Admission considers identity, legal existence, Professional Qualification, jurisdiction, insurance where relevant, conflicts, service scope, language, security, funds capability and performance Evidence.

### Chapter 06 — Capability Supply Portfolio

Providers project governed service Capabilities with jurisdiction, Qualification, service scope, Package terms, turnaround, exclusions, capacity and Evidence requirements.

The following concepts must remain separate:

```text
Provider Supply Capability
≠ User Capability Profile
≠ Source Capability
≠ Verified MO Capability
```

- **Provider Supply Capability** describes what a Provider Organization may be able and willing to deliver through MGSN under stated conditions.
- **User Capability Profile** describes what a Person or Workplace has practiced, evidenced or is developing.
- **Source Capability** preserves a provenance-bound method from a book, author, course or practitioner.
- **Verified MO Capability** is a governed platform Capability version supported by broader evidence and review.

A Provider’s service offer may implement or evidence a shared Capability. It is not the shared Capability itself.

### Chapter 07 — Qualification and certification

MarkOrbit Capability Certification may support operational confidence, but MGSN Eligibility for reserved services requires verified Professional Qualification and Provider Authority.

```text
Capability Certification
≠ Professional Qualification
≠ Provider Admission
≠ Current Eligibility
```

## Part III — Demand and routing

### Chapter 08 — Capability Need Projection

The Originating Workplace projects only the context required to identify a lawful and suitable route. Customer identity may remain masked until engagement requires disclosure.

A Capability Need expresses the outcome required from external supply. It does not imply that the requesting user lacks the Capability personally or that the selected Provider owns the Capability Definition.

### Chapter 09 — Eligibility

Eligibility filters by jurisdiction, Capability, Qualification, conflict, language, capacity, Package availability, data policy and risk.

Historical success may support Trust, but does not replace current Eligibility.

### Chapter 10 — Candidate Route Set

MGSN produces a bounded set of Eligible Routes. A candidate is not automatically appointed.

### Chapter 11 — Recommendation

Recommendation explains fit, price structure, turnaround, Evidence, known risk and meaningful alternatives. User confirmation remains separate from Provider appointment.

## Part IV — Engagement and instruction

### Chapter 12 — Provider allocation

Allocation creates a controlled pending state. The Provider must accept scope, authority, price, deadline and data obligations.

### Chapter 13 — Instruction Package

Instruction preserves source, purpose, customer authority, requested action, documents, deadlines, professional assumptions, funds status and expected Return.

### Chapter 14 — Communication

Participants may communicate where Execution requires it, but communications remain engagement-scoped. Access does not create an unrestricted participant network.

### Chapter 15 — Rebrokering

Undisclosed rebrokering is prohibited. Additional local representatives must be disclosed and governed to the extent required by law, professional practice and Evidence integrity.

## Part V — Funds and commercial governance

### Chapter 16 — Procurement terms

MGSN separates official fees, Provider professional fees, taxes, disbursements, platform fees and contingency items.

### Chapter 17 — Funds checkpoints

Payment authorization, receipt, Provider release, official-fee Evidence, refund and exception states are explicit. Payment does not prove performance.

### Chapter 18 — Settlement

Settlement follows accepted Milestones and Evidence. Unknown official Outcomes, disputed disbursements and incomplete Returns prevent silent closure.

Commercial success, Provider revenue and transaction volume do not constitute Capability quality by themselves.

## Part VI — Fulfillment

### Chapter 19 — Fulfillment monitoring

MGSN monitors Acceptance, prerequisites, deadlines, submission, acknowledgement, follow-up, official decision and Return obligations.

The resulting history may show how a Service Package and Provider Supply Capability performed in context. It does not directly score every individual involved.

### Chapter 20 — Evidence Return

Providers return typed status, official receipts, filings, correspondence, decisions, certificates, invoices and professional explanations with provenance.

A Provider Return should preserve:

- Provider Organization and responsible professional;
- Service Package and Capability references;
- Instruction version;
- Milestone and action context;
- assertions and attached Evidence;
- official and non-official source distinction;
- known Unknowns;
- Correction or next-action requirements.

```text
Provider Return
≠ Official Truth
≠ Capability Verification
```

### Chapter 21 — Return validation

The Originating Workplace or Owning Service validates a Return before treating it as formal truth. Delivered files may be incomplete, inconsistent or outside scope.

For Capability learning, Return validation is only one stage. The Evidence must also be rights-cleared, attributed, contextualized and evaluated for relevance.

### Chapter 22 — Correction, replacement and escalation

MGSN defines Correction requests, Provider replacement, expert escalation, deadline emergency, refund consideration, suspension and network retirement.

Corrections, replacements and recovery may reveal important Capability boundaries, Package weaknesses and execution failure modes.

```text
Correction Required
≠ Provider Incapable

Replacement Succeeded
≠ Original Failure Erased
```

## Part VII — Trust and network intelligence

### Chapter 23 — Trust evidence

Trust is based on Qualification, Acceptance behavior, deadline performance, Evidence completeness, Correction behavior, communication, Dispute history and verified Outcomes.

Trust remains service-specific and contextual.

```text
MGSN Trust
≠ Capability Verification
≠ Professional Qualification
≠ Universal Provider Score
```

Trust may influence routing and control depth. It does not canonize a Provider’s method as the platform’s preferred Capability implementation.

### Chapter 24 — No public popularity shortcut

Public star ratings alone are insufficient for professional routing. Trust views are role-appropriate, contextual and resistant to retaliation or superficial volume gaming.

### Chapter 25 — Capacity and scarcity

The Network measures Capability supply, jurisdiction scarcity, review bottlenecks, demand concentration and Provider dependency. Recruitment follows Evidence, not only geographic coverage claims.

Supply scarcity may identify investment needs for recruitment, training, Package design or Capability development. Scarcity does not prove that a Capability Definition is incomplete.

### Chapter 26 — Network learning

Provider Outcomes may improve routing, Package design, checklists, risk models and Capability candidates. Private Provider terms, customer data and professional work remain subject to rights and confidentiality.

The controlled learning path is:

```text
MGSN Engagement Evidence
→ Rights and Confidentiality Review
→ De-identification where required
→ Attributed Case Candidate
→ Capability Evaluation
→ Capability Change Proposal where justified
```

Relevant Evidence may include:

- Allocation and Acceptance behavior;
- Capability, Package and jurisdiction context;
- Milestone performance;
- Provider Returns and official Evidence;
- Corrections and repeated clarification;
- Incidents and Disputes;
- replacement and recovery performance;
- settlement and reconciliation;
- residual Unknowns.

The following boundaries remain fixed:

```text
One Provider Outcome
≠ Platform Capability Canon

Provider Performance Evidence
≠ User Capability Evidence

Provider Return
≠ Official Truth

MGSN Trust Adjustment
≠ Capability Version Decision
```

MGSN may submit evidence-backed candidates. Capability governance decides whether broader synthesis, testing or a new version is justified.

## Part VIII — Relationship with other networks

### Chapter 27 — MGSN and Professional Contribution Network

The Professional Contribution Network handles bounded platform work such as verification, review, content, visual judgment, simulation and MarkReg production. MGSN handles formal external professional fulfillment. The same Person may participate in both under different authority.

Evidence from the two networks must retain its original contract and authority context. A Provider Outcome cannot be silently credited as a contributor’s personal Capability event, and a bounded Contribution cannot be treated as Provider Organization performance.

### Chapter 28 — External self-managed route

A Workplace may use an external Provider outside MGSN. It remains responsible for negotiation, Instruction, payment, follow-up and recovery. Platform continuity resumes through source-qualified Evidence.

External-route Evidence may support learning only when provenance, rights, roles and reliability are sufficiently clear.

### Chapter 29 — MarkReg route

MarkReg may procure MGSN services for direct, co-delivered or white-label Matters. The originating relationship and contracting model remain explicit.

MarkReg formal-state and service Outcome records may enrich the MGSN evidence chain, but neither Product automatically updates the shared Capability Definition.

## Part IX — Ten-year network strategy

### Chapter 30 — 2026–2028: controlled corridors

Start with high-demand jurisdictions and Service Packages where price, Qualification, documents and Returns can be standardized.

### Chapter 31 — 2028–2031: evidence-based routing

Expand Provider supply, Package depth, replacement policy, funds controls and Capability-aware matching. Use accumulated evidence to improve routing and submit bounded Capability-improvement candidates without collapsing Trust into canon.

### Chapter 32 — 2031–2036: professional infrastructure

MGSN should become trusted infrastructure for cross-border professional fulfillment without becoming an opaque central broker that erases local professional responsibility.

Its long-term contribution to Capability evolution is an evidence source, not a monopoly on professional truth.

## Ten-year lock

The Network’s moat is not the number of listed Providers. It is the density of qualified supply, controlled procurement, reliable fulfillment, Evidence quality, recovery mechanisms and accumulated Trust.

MGSN may help MarkOrbit learn which Capabilities, Packages and controls work in real professional delivery. It must never convert commercial volume, Provider assertion or one successful engagement into automatic Capability canon.
