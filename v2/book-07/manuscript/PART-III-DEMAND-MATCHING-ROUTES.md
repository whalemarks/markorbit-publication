# Part III — Demand, Matching and Route Formation

## Chapter 21 — Capability Need Projection

The originating Workplace projects only what is required to identify a lawful and suitable route.

A need projection may include:

- jurisdiction;
- requested service;
- applicant or owner type;
- mark and class summary;
- deadline;
- language;
- document readiness;
- risk level;
- customer-contact restrictions;
- preferred commercial model.

Customer identity may remain masked until disclosure becomes necessary.

## Chapter 22 — Demand is not an order

A projected need is not yet a binding provider instruction.

The route must pass through eligibility, recommendation, originating approval, provider acceptance and Engagement formation.

## Chapter 23 — Eligibility filters

Eligibility evaluates:

- jurisdiction;
- service scope;
- professional qualification;
- conflict;
- language;
- capacity;
- package availability;
- document prerequisites;
- data policy;
- funds readiness;
- risk ceiling.

An ineligible provider should not appear merely because it has high historic volume.

## Chapter 24 — Candidate Route Set

MGSN should return a bounded set of viable routes rather than an unranked provider list.

A route may include one provider or a controlled chain, such as translator plus local counsel, provided responsibility and evidence remain explicit.

## Chapter 25 — Recommendation is not appointment

A recommendation explains why a route fits. It does not authorize disclosure, payment or instruction.

The originating Workplace must make or delegate the appointment decision under policy.

## Chapter 26 — Explainable route factors

A recommendation should state:

- mandatory qualification match;
- scope match;
- price structure;
- expected timing;
- document assumptions;
- known exclusions;
- evidence quality;
- recovery options;
- uncertainty.

## Chapter 27 — Price cannot dominate routing

Lowest price may correlate with missing stages, weak evidence, long response time or hidden rebrokering.

Routing should balance total expected cost, reliability, speed, professional fit, evidence and recovery.

## Chapter 28 — Relationship constraints

The originating Workplace may require:

- no direct customer contact;
- white-label communication;
- designated communication channel;
- masked pricing;
- no reuse of customer details;
- no cross-selling;
- no undisclosed delegation.

These constraints become part of Engagement eligibility.

## Chapter 29 — Conflict checking

Conflict checks may require staged disclosure. A provider can first assess based on parties and marks under protected access before receiving broader case details.

Conflict outcome and verifier become part of the route record.

## Chapter 30 — Route freshness

Provider qualification, price, capacity and government requirements can change quickly.

Every recommendation requires an as-of time and revalidation trigger. A route should expire rather than remain indefinitely reusable.
