# Part VI — Fulfillment, Return and Recovery

## Chapter 51 — Fulfillment monitoring

MGSN monitors:

- provider acceptance;
- document prerequisites;
- funds readiness;
- deadline source;
- submission or service action;
- acknowledgement;
- follow-up;
- official decision;
- evidence Return;
- correction obligations.

## Chapter 52 — Milestones and events

Milestones should correspond to observable evidence rather than vague labels such as “in process.”

## Chapter 53 — Evidence Return

Providers return typed evidence, including:

- status statements;
- official receipts;
- filed documents;
- office correspondence;
- decisions;
- certificates;
- invoices;
- professional explanations;
- unresolved questions.

Each item retains source, date, provider and relation to the Instruction Package.

## Chapter 54 — Return is not automatic truth

The originating Workplace or applicable Owning Service validates whether the Return is complete, consistent and within scope before changing formal business state.

## Chapter 55 — Missing and inconsistent evidence

A certificate may contain an incorrect address; a receipt may omit a class; a provider summary may conflict with the official record. The system records the inconsistency rather than forcing premature acceptance.

## Chapter 56 — Correction request

Correction identifies the defect, requested remedy, deadline, responsibility and effect on settlement.

## Chapter 57 — Provider silence

Silence triggers escalating follow-up, alternate contacts, management review, deadline protection and possible replacement. It must not remain an informal inbox problem.

## Chapter 58 — Unknown official outcome

Where the provider claims submission but official confirmation is absent, the state remains Unknown pending reconciliation. Duplicate filing should not be attempted without risk assessment.

## Chapter 59 — Replacement

Replacement may require transfer of authority, documents, funds, prior correspondence and deadlines. The original provider's evidence remains preserved.

## Chapter 60 — Emergency route

Urgent deadlines may activate expedited qualification, limited disclosure and provisional instructions, but emergency handling does not remove professional or approval requirements.

## Chapter 61 — Complaint and dispute

Complaints distinguish service quality, billing, professional conduct, data misuse, missed deadline and relationship violation. Each requires a different remedy path.

## Chapter 62 — Network incident review

Serious failures produce a structured incident review covering causes, detection, customer impact, provider responsibility, platform control gaps and preventive action.
