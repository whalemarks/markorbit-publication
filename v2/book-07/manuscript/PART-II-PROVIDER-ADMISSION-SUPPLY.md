# Part II — Provider Admission and Supply

## Chapter 11 — Provider Organization

The accountable network participant is normally a Provider Organization operating through a Provider Workplace. Individual professionals, signatories and contacts attach to that Organization but do not replace its commercial and professional responsibility.

## Chapter 12 — Identity and legal existence

Admission begins with evidence of legal existence, registered name, address, ownership or control, authorized contacts, banking identity and relevant professional registration.

The platform must preserve source, verification date, verifier and expiry.

## Chapter 13 — Professional qualification

Qualification evidence is jurisdiction- and service-specific. A firm may be eligible for filing but not litigation, translation but not legal opinion, or administrative recordal but not reserved representation.

Eligibility must be narrower than a generic “trademark agent” label.

## Chapter 14 — Service scope

Each provider defines a Capability Supply Portfolio containing:

- jurisdiction;
- service type;
- professional restrictions;
- accepted applicant types;
- document prerequisites;
- filing channels;
- language support;
- exclusions;
- expected evidence;
- turnaround;
- package terms.

## Chapter 15 — Capacity is dynamic

A qualified provider may still be unsuitable because of workload, holiday closure, internal transition, office-system outage or temporary inability to accept funds.

Capacity therefore has freshness and expiry. Stale capacity must not be treated as available supply.

## Chapter 16 — Insurance, security and operational readiness

Where appropriate, admission may examine professional insurance, confidentiality controls, data handling, business continuity, secure communication and funds-handling capability.

The standard should be proportionate to service risk.

## Chapter 17 — Pricing and package evidence

Provider pricing should distinguish:

- official fees;
- professional fees;
- taxes;
- publication and registration stages;
- disbursements;
- certificate or courier costs;
- response or objection exclusions;
- currency and validity period.

A package is not accepted merely because a total number is provided.

## Chapter 18 — Trial, probation and staged trust

New providers may begin with limited corridors, capped value, mandatory review or enhanced evidence requirements.

Trust should expand through accepted outcomes rather than presentation quality alone.

## Chapter 19 — Ongoing verification

Admission is not permanent.

Qualification, contacts, fees, service scope, capacity and professional status require periodic revalidation and event-driven review.

## Chapter 20 — Suspension and retirement

Providers may be restricted, suspended or retired because of expired qualification, repeated silence, missing evidence, undisclosed rebrokering, funds disputes, confidentiality breach, quality failure or unresolved conflict.

Suspension should preserve active-matter recovery and historical evidence.
