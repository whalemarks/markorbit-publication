# Part V — Procurement, Funds and Settlement

## Chapter 41 — Procurement terms must be explicit

MGSN procurement distinguishes:

- official fees;
- provider professional fees;
- taxes;
- publication or registration stages;
- disbursements;
- courier or certificate costs;
- contingency items;
- platform fees;
- currency and validity period.

A bundled total without stage and inclusion clarity is not sufficient for governed procurement.

## Chapter 42 — Quote comparability

Different providers may quote different scopes. The platform should normalize packages before comparison and expose missing stages or assumptions.

## Chapter 43 — Funds readiness

A provider should not begin a fee-dependent action until the relevant funds checkpoint is satisfied or an approved credit arrangement exists.

Funds readiness is separate from customer payment status and provider performance.

## Chapter 44 — Payment authorization

Payment requires explicit payer, payee, amount, currency, purpose, milestone and approval authority.

## Chapter 45 — Official-fee evidence

Provider invoices do not automatically prove payment to an office. Where available and appropriate, official receipts, filing references or other evidence should be returned.

## Chapter 46 — Milestone release

Provider payment or platform settlement may follow accepted milestones such as instruction acceptance, filing acknowledgement, publication, registration or final evidence return.

## Chapter 47 — Payment does not prove performance

A paid invoice is commercial evidence, not proof that the requested legal or administrative action occurred.

## Chapter 48 — Refund and credit states

Refunds, credits, unused official fees, duplicate payment and failed remittance require explicit states and reconciliation.

## Chapter 49 — Disputed disbursements

Unexpected government surcharges, publication costs or third-party expenses must be supported, explained and approved according to Engagement policy.

## Chapter 50 — Settlement closure

Settlement closes only after required milestones, evidence, corrections and disputes are resolved or explicitly accepted as exceptions.
