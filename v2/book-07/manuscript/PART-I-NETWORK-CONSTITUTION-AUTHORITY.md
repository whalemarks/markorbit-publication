# Part I — Network Constitution and Authority

## Chapter 01 — Why a managed network exists

Cross-border trademark work often depends on external firms that the originating agency or customer cannot directly supervise. Informal email relationships may work for years, but they are fragile when staff change, deadlines become urgent, fees change, files are incomplete or a provider stops responding.

MGSN exists to convert informal overseas-agent dependency into governed professional fulfillment.

Its value is not merely discovering a provider. It organizes:

- verified provider identity;
- jurisdiction and professional qualification;
- service scope and exclusions;
- price structure;
- instruction quality;
- acceptance and deadline control;
- funds checkpoints;
- evidence return;
- correction, replacement and escalation;
- long-term trust evidence.

## Chapter 02 — Why a directory is insufficient

A directory answers who claims to provide a service. It does not answer:

- whether the provider is currently qualified;
- whether the quoted service includes publication, registration or certificate delivery;
- whether the provider has capacity;
- whether the provider accepts the requested documents;
- whether the provider will return official evidence;
- what happens if the provider disappears;
- who owns the customer relationship;
- how disputes are resolved.

MGSN is therefore a managed network rather than a listing product.

## Chapter 03 — Connection and Network are different objects

A Workplace accesses MGSN through a Workplace-scoped Connection or Gateway.

The Connection holds:

- originating Workplace context;
- authorized demand projection;
- disclosure policy;
- product context;
- relationship constraints;
- active engagements;
- returned evidence references.

The platform-owned Network holds:

- Provider Organizations;
- qualifications;
- capability supply;
- procurement packages;
- routing rules;
- trust evidence;
- network capacity;
- suspension and replacement policies.

The Connection is not a copy of the Network and does not grant unrestricted browsing or contact.

## Chapter 04 — MGSN authority

MGSN may govern:

- provider admission;
- route eligibility;
- package standards;
- procurement terms;
- service matching;
- engagement formation;
- fulfillment monitoring;
- evidence requirements;
- correction and replacement;
- settlement conditions;
- provider trust status.

MGSN does not automatically acquire:

- the originating customer relationship;
- the originating Workplace's private pricing;
- authority to alter formal case records;
- authority to perform reserved professional actions without qualification;
- ownership of provider work product beyond agreed rights.

## Chapter 05 — The originating Workplace remains visible in responsibility

A provider engagement may be initiated by Lite, MarkReg or another Product, but the originating Workplace and its Engagement role remain explicit.

A white-label matter may hide MarkReg from the end customer while preserving MarkReg as Delivery Owner internally. A direct MarkReg matter may identify MarkReg as Relationship Owner and Contracting Party. A co-delivery matter may allocate those roles differently.

MGSN must not flatten these commercial arrangements into a generic order.

## Chapter 06 — Professional responsibility remains local

The network does not replace local professional rules.

Where law or practice requires a locally qualified lawyer or agent, the Provider Organization and named professional remain responsible for the reserved work they perform.

Platform workflow, templates, certification or AI assistance cannot substitute for that qualification.

## Chapter 07 — Network participation is role-specific

The same person may appear as:

- an employee or representative of a Provider Organization;
- a MarkOrbit-certified capability holder;
- a contributor in the Professional Contribution Network;
- a reviewer or assessor;
- an MGSN professional contact.

Each action must state the represented Organization, Workplace, authority and engagement.

## Chapter 08 — MGSN is not an open participant graph

Providers do not automatically receive access to one another, to all demand or to all customer identities.

Network visibility is purpose-limited. This protects:

- customer confidentiality;
- provider pricing;
- commercial relationships;
- conflict information;
- capacity data;
- performance evidence.

## Chapter 09 — Managed does not mean opaque

A managed network must explain its decisions.

Route recommendations should disclose:

- qualification fit;
- service-scope fit;
- price structure;
- turnaround;
- capacity signal;
- evidence quality;
- known limitations;
- meaningful alternatives.

The platform may protect sensitive internal scoring while still giving the originating Workplace enough information to make an accountable choice.

## Chapter 10 — The constitutional promise

MGSN should make cross-border professional fulfillment more reliable without erasing local responsibility or the originating business relationship.

Its constitutional promise is:

```text
qualified supply
+ controlled demand projection
+ explainable routing
+ explicit engagement
+ governed funds
+ evidence-based fulfillment
+ recovery mechanisms
= managed global service
```
