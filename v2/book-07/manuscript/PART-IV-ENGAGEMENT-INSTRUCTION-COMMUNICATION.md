# Part IV — Engagement, Instruction and Communication

## Chapter 31 — Pending allocation

Provider allocation begins as a pending state. The provider must confirm scope, authority, price, deadline, document readiness, communication model and data obligations before work begins.

## Chapter 32 — Engagement formation

The Engagement records:

- originating Workplace;
- provider Organization;
- Relationship Owner;
- Contracting Party;
- Delivery Owner;
- Professional Authority;
- service scope;
- commercial terms;
- disclosure rights;
- milestones;
- correction and termination rules.

## Chapter 33 — Instruction Package

A complete Instruction Package preserves:

- source and purpose;
- customer authority;
- requested action;
- verified facts;
- assumptions;
- documents and versions;
- deadlines and source;
- funds status;
- expected Return;
- prohibited actions.

## Chapter 34 — Version control

A provider must be able to distinguish current instructions from superseded drafts. Material changes require an updated package and acknowledgement.

## Chapter 35 — Customer approval

Where customer approval is required, the approved version and scope must be specific. General consent does not authorize later changes to goods, owner details, evidence or strategy.

## Chapter 36 — Communication channels

Communication may occur through platform messaging, email capture, structured forms or approved external channels. Material commitments and decisions must return to the Engagement record.

## Chapter 37 — Direct customer contact

Direct contact is allowed only when the Engagement permits it and defines purpose, visibility, branding and relationship constraints.

Contact access does not create independent customer rights.

## Chapter 38 — White-label execution

In white-label delivery, the provider may remain invisible to the customer while still being fully visible in internal responsibility and evidence records.

## Chapter 39 — Rebrokering and local associates

Undisclosed rebrokering is prohibited. Additional local representatives must be identified where required, with their authority, role and evidence path recorded.

## Chapter 40 — Change, cancellation and termination

The Engagement defines how scope changes, deadline changes, customer cancellation, provider refusal, replacement and unfinished work are handled without losing evidence or creating contradictory instructions.
