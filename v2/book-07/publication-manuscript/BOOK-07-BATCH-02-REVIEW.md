## 4. Chapter review

## CH05 — Provider Organization and Accountable Supply

### Purpose

Moves the supply model from informal contacts to accountable Organizations, Provider Network Profiles, bounded Capability contexts and matter-specific Provider Acceptance.

### Main distinctions

```text
known contact
≠ accountable Organization
≠ admitted Provider representation
≠ qualified Capability
≠ accepted engagement
```

```text
Provider Organization
≠ responsible professional
≠ operating contact
≠ billing contact
≠ local subcontractor
```

```text
prior relationship
≠ current qualification
≠ permanent exclusivity
```

### Contribution

- preserves Organization as stable identity;
- treats Demand and Supply as contextual roles;
- separates shared identity from role-specific permissions and financial positions;
- defines the Provider Network Profile as a platform-governed representation rather than provider-owned internal records;
- makes Capability granular by service, jurisdiction, stage, authority and limitation;
- keeps the responsible professional distinct from Organization accountability;
- preserves Relationship Provenance without transferring customer or provider ownership;
- requires Provider Acceptance before engagement responsibility exists;
- keeps exit from erasing open obligations or historical evidence.

### Review result

PASS.

The chapter advances the argument from constitutional topology to an operationally accountable supply identity. It does not create a new permanent Core Provider type or imply that a contact record establishes authority.

- limits MarkOrbit certification to assessed platform capability;
