## 4. Chapter review

## CH13 — Provider Allocation Is a Pending State

### Purpose

Separates a user’s route choice from the Provider Organization’s acceptance of exact engagement responsibility.

### Main distinctions

```text
Route confirmed
≠ Provider allocated
≠ Provider accepted
≠ engagement active
≠ protected action authorized
```

```text
message delivered
≠ message read
≠ Allocation acknowledged
≠ Provider accepted
```

### Contribution

- defines Allocation as a version-bound request for Provider commitment;
- requires exact Provider, Capability, Package, Need, price, deadline, conflict and disclosure context;
- gives the Provider meaningful accept, conditional, clarification, change and decline outcomes;
- prevents Provider silence or historical practice from becoming implied Acceptance;
- treats decline as a legitimate and sometimes responsible network outcome;
- defines expiry, emergency handling and revalidation;
- prevents replacement from inheriting Acceptance or customer mandate;
- separates Product-side Allocation from Book 03 Execution Assignment.

### Review result

PASS.

The chapter makes the transition from Recommendation to accountable external responsibility explicit without creating automatic Provider appointment.

## CH14 — The Instruction Package Preserves Authority and Version

### Purpose

Converts scattered emails, attachments, documents, approvals and fee instructions into one attributable and versioned handoff.

### Main distinctions

```text
Provider Acceptance
≠ complete Instruction

Instruction sent
≠ authority proven

latest attachment
≠ approved version
```

```text
Document present
≠ Document valid
≠ Document approved for this use
≠ Action authorized
```

### Contribution

- defines the minimum components of an Instruction Package;
- separates Workplace, customer, professional, financial and protected-action authority;
- binds customer approval to exact content and version;
- preserves source conflicts rather than silently normalizing them;
- distinguishes document presence, validity and authorized use;
- separates fee approval, payment and professional performance;
- records official, internal, customer, document and Provider deadlines separately;
- introduces readiness states and permitted preparatory work;
- requires Provider acknowledgement;
- governs changes, revocation and cancellation;
- limits AI to extraction, comparison and drafting support.

### Review result

PASS.

The chapter supports practical international filing and professional-service work without treating payment, document upload or informal wording as action authority.

```

≠ Workflow
```

```text

```

### Contribution

- treats material communication as execution evidence;
- defines engagement-scoped participants, purpose, data, channel and closure;
- supports managed relay, direct professional, joint and restricted confidential channels;
- preserves professional independence and Workplace relationship ownership simultaneously;
- uses progressive contact disclosure;
- records sender, represented Organization, role, version and confidentiality provenance;
- separates original, machine, operator, professional and approved translation;
- captures material off-platform communication without pretending an operator note is confirmed instruction;

### Review result

PASS.

## CH16 — Undisclosed Rebrokering Breaks Accountability

### Purpose

Defines how additional professionals and subcontractors may participate without allowing the accepted responsibility chain to disappear.

### Main distinctions

```text
Provider Acceptance
≠ permission for undisclosed substitution
```

```text
disclosed supporting participant
≠ undisclosed substitute Provider
```

```text
general subcontracting clause
≠ approval of every material route change
```

### Contribution

- distinguishes supporting work from material professional substitution;
- defines disclosure of identity, role, qualification, conflict, data, fees, evidence, supervision and responsibility;
- requires specific approval where the route materially changes;
- preserves both Organization-level accountability and applicable local professional responsibility;
- treats a new beneficiary or invoice entity as a material funds signal;
- applies minimum necessary disclosure and confidentiality to substitutes;
- extends conflict checks to the real performer;
- governs emergency substitution and anomaly detection;
- defines correction, replacement, refund, dispute and suspension responses;
- prevents unlimited downstream Provider chains.

### Review result

PASS.

The chapter does not prohibit legitimate local representation or specialist support. It requires transparency and accountability proportional to materiality.
