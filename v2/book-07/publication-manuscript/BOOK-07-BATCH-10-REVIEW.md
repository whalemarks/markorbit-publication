## Editorial purpose

The batch addresses the principal gap identified in the whole-book continuity audit: the manuscript had a complete argument but lacked sufficient reader-facing transitions between its eight Parts.

The new material performs four functions:

1. frames the practical problem before Product terminology appears;
2. explains the book’s reader promise and reading path;
3. bridges the argument at each four-chapter Part boundary;
4. closes the book by returning to the opening operational question.

## Introduction review

The Introduction begins from the ordinary professional question, “Who can handle this for us?”, then shows why contact discovery alone does not create operating capability.

It establishes the book’s central standard:

```text
Global professional capability
is the ability to form, verify, complete,
correct and recover a service route.
```

It introduces the full operating chain without attempting to reteach the detailed chapter content. It also explains the sovereignty distribution among Originating Workplace, Provider, MGSN, Core, Execution and Owning Service.

Result: PASS.

Each Part introduction explains why the next four chapters are grouped together and states the question carried into the following Part.

Result: PASS.

## Conclusion review

The Conclusion returns to the opening question and expands it into the final infrastructure test.

It reinforces six whole-book boundaries:

```text
Recommendation ≠ Appointment
Provider Acceptance ≠ Customer Instruction
Payment ≠ Performance
Provider Return ≠ Official Truth
Return Accepted ≠ Formal State Updated
Data Portability ≠ Authority Portability
```

It summarizes MGSN as accountable route infrastructure without presenting the Product as implemented, legally approved or production-ready.

Result: PASS.
