# Core Figure and Table Specifications

This document converts the Book 07 visual inventory into production-ready editorial specifications. It defines content, labels and boundaries. It does not prescribe final graphic style or imply deployed software.

## Visual notation

Use one notation throughout the book:

```text
solid arrow   = governed progression
broken arrow  = conditional or review-dependent progression
return arrow  = Evidence, correction or recovery path
boundary box  = ownership or authority boundary
warning mark  = unsafe shortcut or prohibited collapse
```

All visual labels must use the manuscript’s preferred terms.

## Figure 1 — From Contact to Global Capability

**Purpose:** Show why a contact is only the beginning of an accountable route.

```text
Private Contact
→ Accountable Provider Organization
→ Admitted Provider Supply Capability
→ Current Eligible Route
→ Accepted Engagement
→ Evidence-backed Completion
→ Correctable and Recoverable Route
```

**Boundary notes:**

- relationship history does not create current Eligibility;
- admitted supply does not create Provider Acceptance;
- completion claimed does not equal completion evidenced;
- recovery capability is part of the route, not an optional add-on.

**Placement:** Introduction or CH01.

# Priority Tables

## Table 1 — Participant Responsibilities

| Participant | Owns or controls | May decide | May not decide by default |
|---|---|---|---|
| Originating Workplace | customer relationship, Matter context, internal Knowledge | whether to project, choose or leave a route | Provider professional judgment or official state |
| MGSN | network representation and managed-route governance | admission, routing controls, Trust and recovery process | Professional Qualification, customer mandate or formal state |
| Provider Organization | delivery organization and accepted service responsibility | accept, condition or decline work | originating customer ownership or Owning Service state |
| Responsible professional | professional judgment within valid authority | professional act within scope | customer commercial decisions outside mandate |
| Execution | governed runtime coordination | permitted transitions under policy | create authority or truth |
| Owning Service | formal business state | validate accepted Returns and update state | external official state |
| Official Authority | official record | official acceptance, registration or other official action | Workplace business state outside its authority |

## Table 2 — Capability and Authority Distinctions

| Layer | Central question | Evidence or source | Does not create |
|---|---|---|---|
| Capability Claim | What does the Provider say it can supply? | Provider assertion | admitted supply |
| Provider Supply Capability | What supply has MGSN admitted? | Organization, people, process and Evidence | current Eligibility |
| Professional Qualification | Is recognized professional status current? | external authoritative source | Provider Acceptance |
| Capability Certification | Has a Capability-related assessment been passed? | governed assessment Evidence | legal qualification |
| MGSN Qualification | May the Provider offer this supply through MGSN? | network review | Matter-specific authority |
| Eligibility | May this exact route be considered now? | current Need, conflict, capacity, package and risk | Acceptance |
| Provider Acceptance | Has the Provider committed to this exact engagement? | attributable response | customer instruction |
| Matter-specific Authority | Is the actor authorized for this Matter action? | mandate, appointment and applicable rules | protected-action approval by itself |

## Table 3 — Route Types

| Route | Formation | Provider relationship | MGSN governance | Recovery support |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| R1 External Self-managed | Workplace chooses external route | Workplace-managed | limited or none | Workplace-managed |
| R2 MGSN Recommended Managed | MGSN recommends eligible alternatives | relationship remains attributable | route and fulfillment governance | MGSN-supported |
| R3 MGSN Managed Preferred-provider | preferred relationship preserved subject to controls | provenance protected | full managed-route controls | MGSN-supported with replacement path |

## Table 4 — Provider Return Types

| Return type | Purpose | Typical Evidence | Possible next state |
|---|---|---|---|
| Acknowledgement | confirm receipt and understanding | version acknowledgement | ready or clarification required |
| Progress Return | evidence milestone movement | receipt, draft, official communication | accepted, correction or Unknown |
| Exception Return | report blocker or changed condition | conflict, rejection, fee or document evidence | change control or safe-stop |
| Correction Return | prove corrective work | corrected document and provenance | accepted or further correction |
| Closure Return | support reconciliation | final deliverables and outstanding items | closure review |

## Table 5 — Incident Severity and Response

| Severity | Typical concern | Immediate response | Final decision authority |
|---|---|---|---|
| Low | minor process deviation without material harm | record and correct | MGSN operating review |
| Moderate | repeated delay, incomplete Evidence or bounded exposure | contain, investigate and remediate | applicable MGSN governance role |
| High | deadline, authority, funds, confidentiality or customer harm risk | safe-stop, preserve Evidence, restrict route | appropriate professional, contractual or external forum |
| Critical | likely severe or continuing harm | urgent containment and external escalation where required | competent external authority where applicable |

## Table 6 — Dispute Forums

| Dispute type | Primary forum | MGSN role | MGSN limit |
|---|---|---|---|
| Operational | MGSN route governance | preserve Evidence, coordinate correction | does not decide legal liability |
| Commercial | contracting parties and agreed process | maintain records and bounded holds | does not create universal payment adjudication |
| Professional | competent professional or regulatory process | refer and preserve relevant Evidence | does not discipline professionals |
| Official-state | official authority or competent court | support Evidence handoff | does not alter official state |
| Platform-governance | MGSN governance and appeal process | decide network participation controls | does not replace external legal rights |
