# CH27 — Disputes Need Bounded Forums and Evidence

A Dispute begins when ordinary clarification, correction or Incident handling no longer produces agreement.

The parties may disagree about scope, version, milestone completion, Evidence, fees, funds, responsibility, data disclosure or customer-relationship boundaries.

A managed network needs a place to hold those disagreements without pretending that the platform can decide every legal, professional or financial question.

```text
MGSN Dispute Process
≠ Court
≠ Regulator
≠ Professional Disciplinary Body
≠ Universal Arbitration Authority
```

## 1. A Dispute needs a defined object

“The Provider failed” is too broad.

The record should identify exactly what is contested, such as:

- Scope or Instruction version;
- Milestone or Return;
- Invoice or price change;
- Official fee or refund;
- Provider substitution;
- Professional responsibility;
- Customer authority;
- Data disclosure;
- Relationship circumvention;
- Termination.

A defined object prevents the entire engagement from becoming one undifferentiated conflict.

## 2. The forum must match the question

Different issues belong in different forums.

### Operational forum

Suitable for missing documents, Return completeness, version history, milestone Evidence, status and replacement coordination.

### Commercial forum

Suitable for invoice scope, fee change, refund, credit, holdback, settlement allocation and cancellation charges.

### Professional review forum

Suitable for professional work quality, reserved judgment, Qualification, Conflict, supervision and professional correction.

### External legal or regulatory forum

May be required for legal liability, discipline, fraud, ownership, enforceable money claims, injunctions or mandatory reporting.

MGSN should route the issue to the correct forum rather than silently expanding its own authority.

## 3. Evidence must remain attributable

A Dispute package may include:

- accepted Service Package;
- Recommendation and User Disposition;
- Allocation and Provider Acceptance;
- Instruction Package versions;
- approvals and communications;
- Milestones and Returns;
- Official Evidence;
- invoices and Funds Checkpoints;
- Correction and Incident records.

Each item should preserve source, date, version, author, confidentiality and whether it is original or summarized.

```text
Operator Summary
≠ Original Evidence
```

## 4. Separate agreed facts from contested claims

A useful record distinguishes:

```text
Agreed Facts
Contested Facts
Missing Evidence
Professional or Legal Interpretation
Requested Remedy
```

The parties may agree that a filing occurred while disagreeing about whether the correct goods were used. They may agree that an invoice was issued while disputing whether the work was in scope.

This separation makes partial resolution possible.

## 5. Protect the Matter while the Dispute continues

An unresolved Dispute should not expose an official deadline or critical document.

MGSN may need to:

- preserve Evidence;
- hold a contested amount;
- protect originals;
- prepare replacement review;
- restrict new Allocation;
- maintain a controlled communication channel;
- separate contested and uncontested obligations.

```text
Funds Hold
≠ Final Entitlement Decision
```

The network should separate:

```text
Matter Protection Track
from
Responsibility and Remedy Track
```

A corrective filing, extension or replacement may require new authority and cost approval, but it should not wait for a final blame decision when delay is unsafe.

## 6. Remedies must stay within authority

Platform-level outcomes may include:

- Correction or Evidence supplementation;
- partial Milestone acceptance;
- revised settlement recommendation;
- contractually supported credit or refund process;
- replacement assistance;
- Package restriction;
- Trust adjustment;
- Incident escalation;
- referral to an external forum;
- closure for insufficient Evidence.

MGSN should not award damages, make disciplinary findings or impose externally enforceable remedies unless valid authority exists.

## 7. Partial settlement can continue

A Dispute over one amount should not automatically block every uncontested obligation.

The engagement may separate:

```text
Accepted Amount
Contested Amount
Holdback
Refund Claim
Official Fee Already Consumed
Work Not Yet Started
```

Partial settlement can reduce pressure while preserving the disputed issue.

## 8. Neutrality cannot be assumed

A decision maker should not review a Dispute where they have a material interest in the Provider, Workplace, commercial margin, original Recommendation or Incident finding.

```text
Platform Operated the Route
≠ Platform Automatically Neutral
```

Relevant conflicts should be disclosed and independent review used where necessary.

## 9. Confidentiality and closure

A Dispute may involve professional advice, customer instructions, bank details and sensitive Evidence.

Access should be limited to the forum’s purpose. A party does not gain broad access to another participant’s private records merely because a Dispute exists.

Possible closure states include agreement, Correction, partial resolution, commercial settlement, external referral, withdrawal, insufficient Evidence or closure without agreement.

Closure should preserve the history, remedies and remaining obligations.

## 10. Product principle

A Dispute process is credible when it knows both what it can decide and what it cannot.

```text
Bounded Forum
+ Attributable Evidence
+ Interim Protection
+ Authority-appropriate Remedies
+ External Referral Where Required
= Governed Dispute Handling
```
