# CH27 — Disputes Need Bounded Forums and Evidence

A dispute begins when ordinary clarification, correction or incident handling no longer produces agreement.

The parties may disagree about:

- what was instructed;
- which version was approved;
- whether the Provider accepted the work;
- whether a milestone was completed;
- whether Evidence is sufficient;
- whether an official fee was paid;
- who caused a missed deadline;
- whether additional fees are justified;
- whether a refund or credit is due;
- whether a Provider may retain documents or funds;
- whether a customer relationship boundary was crossed.

A managed network needs a place to hold these disagreements without pretending that the platform can decide every legal, professional or financial question.

```text
MGSN dispute process
≠ court
≠ regulator
≠ professional disciplinary body
≠ universal arbitration authority
```

## 1. A dispute must have a defined object

The dispute record should identify exactly what is contested.

Possible dispute objects include:

```text
scope
price
invoice
milestone
Evidence
Return
funds transfer
official fee
refund
professional responsibility
customer authority
provider substitution
data disclosure
relationship circumvention
termination
```

A broad statement such as “the Provider failed” is not enough. The forum must know which obligation, event, amount or piece of Evidence is being challenged.

## 2. The forum must be appropriate to the question

Different questions belong in different forums.

### Operational forum

Suitable for:

- missing documents;
- status disagreement;
- Return completeness;
- milestone evidence;
- communication or version history;
- package-scope comparison;
- replacement coordination.

### Commercial forum

Suitable for:

- invoice scope;
- fee change;
- refund or credit;
- holdback;
- settlement allocation;
- cancellation charges.

### Professional review forum

Suitable for:

- professional work quality;
- reserved judgment;
- qualification or supervision concern;
- conflict handling;
- professional correction.

### External legal or regulatory forum

May be required for:

- legal liability;
- professional discipline;
- fraud;
- ownership disputes;
- enforceable money claims;
- injunctions;
- mandatory reporting;
- rights that cannot be decided by contract or platform policy.

MGSN should route the issue to the correct forum rather than expanding its own authority.

## 3. Evidence must remain attributable

The dispute package may include:

- accepted Service Package;
- Recommendation and user disposition;
- Allocation and Provider Acceptance;
- Instruction Package versions;
- approvals;
- communications;
- milestones;
- Provider Returns;
- official Evidence;
- invoices and funds checkpoints;
- correction records;
- incident findings;
- relevant policies and terms.

Each item should preserve:

- source;
- date;
- version;
- author or actor;
- whether it is original or summarized;
- confidentiality level;
- whether another participant confirmed it.

```text
operator summary
≠ original evidence
```

## 4. Undisputed facts should be separated from contested claims

A useful dispute record distinguishes:

```text
agreed facts
contested facts
missing evidence
legal or professional interpretation
requested remedy
```

This prevents the entire engagement from becoming one undifferentiated conflict.

The parties may agree that a filing occurred while disagreeing about whether it used the correct goods. They may agree that an invoice was issued while disagreeing about whether the work was within scope.

## 5. Interim protection may be necessary

While a dispute is unresolved, MGSN may need to:

- preserve a deadline;
- authorize replacement review;
- hold settlement;
- protect original documents;
- restrict new allocation;
- separate contested and uncontested amounts;
- maintain a communication channel;
- require evidence preservation;
- notify affected Workplaces.

Interim action should avoid deciding the final dispute prematurely.

```text
funds hold
≠ final entitlement decision
```

## 6. Remedies must match authority

Possible platform-level outcomes may include:

- correction required;
- Return or Evidence supplementation;
- partial milestone acceptance;
- revised settlement recommendation;
- credit or refund process where contractually supported;
- replacement assistance;
- package restriction;
- Trust adjustment;
- incident escalation;
- referral to an external forum;
- closure for insufficient evidence.

MGSN should not award damages, make disciplinary findings or impose externally enforceable remedies unless valid authority exists.

## 7. Settlement and dispute may proceed in parts

A dispute over one amount should not automatically block every uncontested obligation.

The engagement may distinguish:

```text
accepted amount
contested amount
holdback
refund claim
official fee already consumed
work not yet started
```

Partial settlement can reduce pressure while preserving the disputed issue.

## 8. Time and deadline governance

Dispute procedures should not allow an approaching official deadline to expire while participants argue about historical responsibility.

The network may separate:

```text
Matter protection track
from
responsibility and remedy track
```

A replacement filing, extension request or corrective action may require new authority and cost approval. Those decisions should be made explicitly without waiting for a final blame decision where delay is unsafe.

## 9. Neutrality and conflicts in the forum

A decision maker should not review a dispute where they have a material interest in:

- the Provider;
- the Originating Workplace;
- the commercial margin;
- the disputed transaction;
- the original Recommendation;
- the incident finding.

The forum should disclose relevant conflicts and assign independent review where necessary.

```text
platform operated the route
≠ platform automatically neutral
```

## 10. Confidentiality and privilege

A dispute may involve professional advice, customer instructions, pricing, bank details and sensitive evidence.

Access should be limited to the forum’s purpose. A party should not gain broad access to another participant’s private records merely because a dispute exists.

Where privilege or legally protected confidentiality applies, the platform should record the existence and handling of the material without assuming unrestricted access.

## 11. Closure states

A dispute may close as:

```text
resolved by agreement
resolved by correction
partially resolved
settled commercially
referred externally
withdrawn
closed for insufficient evidence
closed without agreement
superseded by official or legal decision
```

Closure should preserve the history, remedies and remaining obligations.

## 12. AI support and limits

AI may assist with:

- organizing dispute evidence;
- comparing package and instruction versions;
- separating agreed and contested facts;
- calculating disputed amounts;
- detecting missing records;
- drafting neutral timelines;
- identifying possible forum routes.

AI must not:

- decide liability;
- determine professional negligence;
- issue binding awards;
- waive rights;
- settle on behalf of a party;
- infer consent;
- expose privileged or restricted material.

## 13. Failure modes

### Platform decides everything

An operational team makes a final legal-liability decision beyond its authority.

Correct response: resolve operational facts and refer the legal issue to the proper forum.

### Entire settlement is frozen

A small disputed fee blocks payment of all accepted milestones.

Correct response: separate contested and uncontested amounts.

### Urgent Matter waits for dispute closure

The official deadline expires while parties argue about responsibility.

Correct response: create a protected recovery track with renewed authority.

### Public pressure replaces evidence

A participant threatens public accusations to force settlement.

Correct response: preserve evidence, confidentiality and the bounded forum.

## 14. Product principle

A dispute process is credible when it knows both what it can decide and what it cannot.

```text
bounded forum
+ attributable evidence
+ interim protection
+ authority-appropriate remedies
+ external referral where required
= governed dispute handling
```
