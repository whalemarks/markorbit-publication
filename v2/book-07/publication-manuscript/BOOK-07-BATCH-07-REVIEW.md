
## 4. Chapter review

## CH25 — Trust Must Be Service-specific and Evidence-backed

### Main distinctions

```text
Provider reputation
≠ service-specific Trust

high Trust
≠ Qualification
≠ Eligibility
≠ authority
```

### Contribution

- treats Trust as bounded confidence for a defined service, jurisdiction, stage and period;
- separates identity, qualification, response, deadline, Evidence, correction, funds, data and relationship dimensions;
- distinguishes Workplace Relationship Trust from MGSN Network Trust;
- treats recency, evidence quality, severity, recurrence and correction behavior separately;
- preserves pilot and limited-evidence outcomes for new Providers;
- prevents commercial volume, paid promotion and personal preference from becoming Trust;
- recognizes safe refusal and authority discipline as potentially positive behavior;
- requires review and due process for material negative consequences.

### Review result

PASS.

The chapter does not create a universal Provider score or imply that Trust can replace professional qualification, current eligibility or Matter authority.

## CH26 — Incidents Need Severity, Containment and Due Process

### Main distinctions

```text
complaint
≠ verified incident

incident opened
≠ responsibility decided

protective action
≠ final sanction
```

### Contribution

- defines incident scope across deadline, authority, Evidence, data, funds, substitution and relationship risks;
- preserves allegation, verified fact and final finding as separate states;
- introduces severity as an impact and urgency control rather than a blame score;
- places containment before final responsibility where active harm exists;
- reconstructs the engagement chain from Need through Return;
- distinguishes provider, Workplace, customer, official, shared and control-design causes;
- provides typed findings, remediation, bounded scope, confidentiality and appeal;
- prevents revenue and volume from weakening protective action.

### Review result

PASS.

The chapter supports immediate safety controls without claiming that MGSN is a regulator, court or professional disciplinary body.

## CH27 — Disputes Need Bounded Forums and Evidence

### Main distinctions

```text
MGSN dispute process
≠ court
≠ regulator
≠ professional disciplinary body
≠ universal arbitration authority
```

### Contribution

- requires a defined dispute object rather than broad accusations;
- separates operational, commercial, professional and external legal forums;
- preserves attributable source, version and confidentiality for dispute Evidence;
- separates agreed facts, contested facts, missing Evidence and requested remedy;
- allows interim Matter protection without prejudging final responsibility;
- limits remedies to the authority of the relevant forum;
- supports partial settlement of uncontested amounts;
- separates Matter protection from responsibility and remedy;
- requires conflict review for decision makers;
- defines closure states and external referral.

### Review result

PASS.

The chapter does not grant MGSN authority to award damages, impose discipline or make binding legal findings without a valid external basis.

## CH28 — Network Learning Must Not Become a Public Star Rating

### Main distinctions

```text
network learning
≠ public popularity ranking
≠ universal Provider score
≠ commercial leaderboard
```

### Contribution

- defines supply, routing, fulfillment and governance learning;
- attaches learning to bounded units such as Capability, package, jurisdiction and milestone;
- explains why consumer-style ratings distort professional quality and authority discipline;
- preserves internal evidence-backed, service-specific and reviewable classifications;
- incorporates positive performance, failure, recovery and control-design learning;
- structures feedback by actor and question rather than free-text popularity;
- identifies volume, selection, language and service-complexity bias;
- applies privacy, purpose limitation and contestability;
- directs learning toward improved packages, milestones, eligibility, replacement and training.

### Review result

PASS.

The chapter advances MGSN as a learning institution without turning it into an open marketplace or public provider-rating platform.

- safe professional refusal being punished;

- commercial importance weakening restrictions;
- platform teams deciding legal questions beyond authority;
- all settlement being frozen by one disputed amount;
- deadlines expiring while disputes continue;
- unresolved disputes becoming public punishment;
- transaction volume becoming quality;
- product-design failure being attributed only to Providers.
