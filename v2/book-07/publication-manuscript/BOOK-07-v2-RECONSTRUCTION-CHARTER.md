# Book 07 v2 — Reconstruction Charter

## 1. Book identity

```text
Book: 07
Product: MGSN
Working title: Mark Global Service Network — Managed Professional Fulfillment
Primary subject: governed access to external professional capability
Primary reader: professional-service operators, Product leaders and institutional partners
```

## 2. Central thesis

A global professional network becomes valuable only when it governs more than discovery.

MGSN must preserve the complete chain from bounded demand projection to qualified supply, explainable route choice, Provider Acceptance, fulfillment evidence, correction and typed Return. Its advantage is not a larger list of foreign agents. Its advantage is that participants can expand global capability without surrendering their customers, professional responsibility or ability to recover when delivery fails.

## 3. Main narrative problem

Traditional international service networks are usually held together by private spreadsheets, email threads, introductions and personal memory. They may appear relational and flexible, but they create hidden operational risk:

- provider identity and qualification become stale;
- prices and official fees are mixed together;
- customer authority and provider appointment are unclear;
- instructions lose version and provenance;
- deadlines are monitored inconsistently;
- payment is separated from evidence of performance;
- provider silence produces manual reconstruction;
- relationship ownership becomes contested;
- successful history becomes an unexamined proxy for current suitability.

Book 07 must show how a managed network addresses these risks without becoming an opaque central broker.

## 4. Constitutional boundaries

The manuscript must preserve:

```text
Originating Workplace sovereignty

MGSN matching and Recommendation

Provider network admission

Professional qualification
```

The manuscript follows a seven-stage argument:

```text
why the network exists

→ how demand becomes a route
→ how fulfillment is governed
→ how Trust and recovery work

```

The architecture is currently expressed as 32 substantive chapters in `BOOK-07-v2-MASTER.md`.

## 7. Editorial rules

- Prefer operating examples over abstract taxonomy.
- Explain every important state distinction in terms of participant risk.
- Do not treat “AI”, “platform” or “network” as magical actors.
- State who knows, decides, accepts, pays, performs, reviews and updates formal state.
- Distinguish what exists as accepted architecture from what remains an implementation prerequisite.
- Avoid marketing claims such as “best provider”, “guaranteed result” or “global coverage” without evidence and scope.
- Do not normalize lowest-price routing as efficiency.
- Treat failure and recovery as part of the Product, not an appendix.

## 8. Reconstruction method

Each batch should integrate:

1. frozen RC1 narrative;
2. active cross-book architecture;
3. practical international-service operating experience;
4. authority, evidence and recovery boundaries;
5. a whole-book forward transition.

The reconstruction should compress obsolete repetition but retain important operational distinctions. New prose may clarify accepted architecture; it must not claim that an unimplemented mechanism is already production-ready.

## 9. Completion standard

The first long-form reconstruction pass is complete only when:

- all planned chapters exist;
- every Part has a coherent beginning, development and close;
- Product and cross-book boundaries are consistent;
- provider, route, commercial, fulfillment and Trust chains are complete;
- failure, correction and recovery receive substantive treatment;
- examples remain legally and operationally bounded;
- a whole-book thesis and completeness review is recorded.

## 10. Non-goals

This manuscript does not:

- activate a Provider network;
- verify any real provider;
- authorize payment custody or escrow;
- create professional qualification;
- appoint a professional;
- authorize filing or another protected action;
- define every jurisdiction-specific legal requirement;
- replace the frozen RC1 by implication;
- serve as a software implementation specification.
