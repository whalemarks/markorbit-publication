# CH17 — Procurement Terms Must Separate Cost Layers

A service quote is not a number.

It is a layered statement about who will do what, which costs are known, which costs belong to an external authority, which assumptions may change and who carries the risk when they do.

International professional-service pricing becomes dangerous when every layer is compressed into one total.

```text
headline price
≠ complete route economics
```

MGSN therefore treats procurement terms as a structured operating record rather than a loose quotation attachment.

## 1. Why totals become misleading

A Provider may quote “filing cost” while including only professional fees. Another may include official fees but exclude publication, registration, translation, courier and tax. A third may include later-stage work but only if no refusal arises.

All three may appear to quote the same service.

They do not.

A managed route must distinguish at least:

```text
Provider Procurement Cost
Official Fees
Taxes
Disbursements
Third-party Costs
Platform-managed Service Layer
Risk and Support Layer
Demand-side Margin where permitted
Customer-facing Offer
```

The layers can be commercially combined for presentation, but they should not become semantically indistinguishable.

## 2. Provider procurement cost

Provider procurement cost is the amount and conditions under which the Provider agrees to perform the admitted professional or operational work.

It may include:

- ordinary professional work;
- local representation;
- drafting or filing;
- routine correspondence;
- standard reporting;
- an agreed number of items, classes or pages;
- a defined period of monitoring;
- correction within the admitted scope.

It may exclude:

- substantive refusal responses;
- hearings or appeals;
- legalization;
- translation beyond a limit;
- courier;
- excess official charges;
- emergency work;
- third-party investigation;
- registration-stage services.

Procurement cost must be linked to a Service Package version. A number without scope is not a usable procurement term.

```text
Provider price
+ undefined scope
= ungoverned liability
```

## 3. Official fees belong to an external authority

Official fees are not Provider revenue and not platform service fees.

They may be:

- fixed;
- class-based;
- item-based;
- stage-based;
- applicant-based;
- currency-sensitive;
- subject to official change;
- dependent on filing route or payment channel.

The route should record:

- authority;
- fee type;
- amount and currency;
- effective or checked date;
- source or supporting Evidence;
- whether the amount is exact, estimated or pending;
- who will pay;
- whether pre-funding is required;
- what evidence of payment is expected.

```text
official fee quoted
≠ official fee paid
≠ official action completed
```

A Provider may collect funds for an official fee, but the collection record must remain distinct from official payment evidence.

## 4. Taxes and disbursements

Taxes and disbursements frequently create hidden variance.

Relevant items may include:

- VAT or sales tax;
- withholding tax;
- bank charges;
- payment processor charges;
- currency conversion;
- courier;
- notarization;
- legalization;
- translation;
- official publication charges;
- local third-party expenses.

The route should identify whether each item is:

```text
included
excluded
estimated
passed through at cost
subject to cap
subject to prior approval
unknown until incurred
```

A generic statement that “expenses are extra” is not enough for a managed offer.

## 5. Platform-managed service layer

MGSN may add value beyond the Provider’s professional work.

The platform-managed layer may include:

- intake standardization;
- route analysis;
- Provider admission and qualification controls;
- communication coordination;
- deadline monitoring;
- document completeness checks;
- funds coordination where authorized;
- exception handling;
- correction and replacement support;
- typed Return;
- network Trust evidence.

This layer must be described honestly.

```text
platform service fee
≠ official fee
≠ Provider professional fee
≠ legal result guarantee
```

The customer should understand whether a charge pays for professional work, platform coordination, risk support or another service.

## 6. Demand-side margin and Workplace sovereignty

An Originating Workplace may add its own service and commercial margin where its business model and applicable rules permit.

That margin may reflect:

- customer acquisition;
- relationship management;
- internal advice;
- strategy;
- translation;
- document preparation;
- billing and credit risk;
- coordination;
- after-service support.

MGSN should not automatically expose the Workplace’s private procurement economics to the customer or Provider.

```text
Provider procurement cost
≠ customer-facing price
```

But the platform must still prevent ambiguity about included service, official fees, taxes and later-stage exposure.

Commercial confidentiality does not justify misleading scope.

## 7. The customer-facing offer

A customer-facing offer should present a usable commercial decision, not a raw internal ledger.

It should state:

- route and Provider context where disclosure is appropriate;
- service scope;
- included stages;
- excluded stages;
- known professional and official costs;
- estimates and variables;
- taxes and disbursements;
- validity period;
- currency and conversion assumptions;
- payment milestones;
- refund or cancellation rules;
- change-control triggers;
- likely later-stage costs;
- unresolved Unknowns.

The offer may use a total, but the total should preserve the underlying structure.

## 8. Fixed, estimated, conditional and unknown

Every amount should have a type.

### Fixed

The amount is committed for the stated scope and validity period, subject only to explicit exceptions.

### Estimated

The amount is a reasonable current estimate but may change based on a named external variable.

### Conditional

The amount applies only if a specified event occurs.

### Unknown

The amount cannot yet be determined without further facts, official information or Provider confirmation.

```text
estimated
≠ fixed

unknown
≠ zero
```

The system should not use zero to represent an unresolved amount.

## 9. Later-stage exposure

Many services have predictable future stages.

A filing route may later require:

- publication fee;
- registration fee;
- response to refusal;
- use evidence;
- legalization;
- opposition defense;
- renewal or maintenance;
- certificate courier.

The current offer may not include those stages. It should still disclose their likely existence where known.

```text
not currently payable
≠ commercially irrelevant
```

A route that appears cheaper only because later-stage exposure is hidden should not be recommended as economically superior.

## 10. Price validity and stale terms

Procurement terms need:

- issue date;
- validity period;
- currency;
- official-fee check date;
- package version;
- assumptions;
- Provider confirmation status.

A historical quote may remain useful evidence. It should not become current truth without revalidation.

```text
previous price
≠ current price
```

Urgent work should not bypass price confirmation where material uncertainty remains. It may require a bounded estimate, emergency approval or explicit commercial risk acceptance.

## 11. Currency and conversion

Cross-border routes may involve multiple currencies.

The commercial record should identify:

- source currency;
- customer currency;
- conversion basis;
- conversion time;
- buffer or tolerance;
- who carries exchange-rate variance;
- whether unused funds are reconciled;
- whether bank charges are included.

The platform should not silently profit from an unexplained exchange-rate spread while describing the amount as an official or Provider cost.

## 12. Procurement confidentiality and auditability

MGSN may protect:

- Provider procurement rates;
- negotiated discounts;
- volume arrangements;
- route economics;
- internal risk pricing;
- Workplace margin.

Protection does not remove the need for internal auditability.

The network should be able to explain:

- which price version was used;
- who confirmed it;
- which layers formed the offer;
- why a change occurred;
- whether a commercial conflict affected Recommendation;
- whether the customer approved the revised amount.

## 13. Commercial neutrality

Recommendation must not be secretly determined by the route that yields the highest margin.

```text
higher platform or Workplace margin
≠ stronger route fit
```

Commercial factors may be relevant, but they must remain subordinate to:

- Eligibility;
- professional fit;
- scope;
- customer interest;
- risk;
- deadline;
- evidence and recovery.

Paid placement cannot convert an ineligible Provider into a Candidate Route.

## 14. Procurement change events

A procurement term may change because:

- official fees increase;
- the Provider discovers additional work;
- the customer changes scope;
- the deadline becomes urgent;
- exchange rates move;
- tax treatment changes;
- a third party becomes necessary;
- a Package expires;
- a new stage begins.

Each change should identify:

- trigger;
- old and new amount;
- affected scope;
- source Evidence;
- decision authority;
- whether customer approval is required;
- whether work must pause;
- whether the route remains preferable.

## 15. Failure modes

### 15.1 One total hides scope differences

Two Providers appear comparable, but one excludes registration and certificate return.

Correct response: normalize cost and scope layers before comparison.

### 15.2 Official fee is treated as Provider revenue

The customer cannot tell whether a charge was paid to the authority.

Correct response: require official-fee classification and payment Evidence.

### 15.3 Historical quote remains active

A two-year-old quotation is reused after official fees change.

Correct response: mark it stale and obtain current confirmation.

### 15.4 Unknown is displayed as zero

The interface shows no amount for a likely later-stage fee.

Correct response: display Unknown or conditional exposure explicitly.

### 15.5 Margin drives recommendation

A less suitable route is promoted because its spread is higher.

Correct response: review recommendation integrity and commercial conflict.

## 16. Product principle

MGSN can simplify pricing for the user only after it has separated the economics internally.

```text
structured procurement layers
+ versioned Service Package
+ transparent assumptions
+ bounded commercial confidentiality
+ governed change control
= trustworthy customer-facing offer
```
