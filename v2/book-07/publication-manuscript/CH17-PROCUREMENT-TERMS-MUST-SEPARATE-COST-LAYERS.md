# CH17 — Procurement Terms Must Separate Cost Layers

A service quote is not a number.

It is a compact promise about scope, responsibility, external charges, uncertainty and change. International work becomes commercially unsafe when those layers are collapsed into one reassuring total.

```text
headline price
≠ complete route economics
```

Two Providers may both quote “trademark filing.” One includes classification review, official fees and filing evidence. Another includes only professional submission work. A third includes later registration work but excludes courier and legalization. The totals look comparable. The routes are not.

MGSN therefore treats procurement terms as a structured part of the route, not as an attachment that sits beside it.

## 1. Start with the accepted Service Package

A price is meaningful only when tied to a defined Service Package version.

The package establishes:

- the service and procedural stage;
- included professional work;
- excluded work;
- assumed volume, classes, pages or applicants;
- expected deliverables and Evidence;
- timing assumptions;
- correction obligations;
- validity period.

```text
Provider price
+ undefined scope
= ungoverned liability
```

A low quotation may be entirely reasonable for a narrow package. It becomes misleading only when the package boundary is hidden.

## 2. Separate the economic layers

A managed route may contain several distinct layers:

```text
Provider Procurement Cost
Official Fees
Taxes and Disbursements
Third-party Costs
Platform-managed Service Layer
Workplace Service and Margin
Customer-facing Offer
```

The layers may be combined for presentation, but they should remain distinguishable in the governed record.

### Provider Procurement Cost

This is what the Provider charges for the admitted work it has agreed to perform. It may cover local representation, drafting, filing, routine correspondence and reporting. It may exclude refusals, hearings, legalization, excess translation, urgent work or later stages.

### Official Fees

Official fees belong to an external authority. They are not Provider revenue and not platform service fees.

The route should record the authority, fee type, amount, currency, source date, confidence, payer and expected payment Evidence.

```text
official fee quoted
≠ official fee paid
≠ official action completed
```

### Taxes, Disbursements and Third-party Costs

These may include tax, bank charges, courier, translation, notarization, legalization, investigation or another external service. Each item should be identified as included, excluded, estimated, capped, passed through or Unknown.

A generic statement that “expenses are extra” is not enough for a managed offer.

### Platform-managed Service Layer

MGSN may charge for work such as standardized intake, route formation, communication coordination, deadline monitoring, Evidence handling, exception management and recovery support.

```text
platform service fee
≠ Provider professional fee
≠ official fee
≠ result guarantee
```

### Workplace Service and Margin

The Originating Workplace may add its own service value and margin where permitted. That may reflect customer acquisition, advice, strategy, document work, billing risk, coordination and after-service support.

```text
Provider procurement cost
≠ customer-facing price
```

MGSN should not automatically expose private procurement economics. Commercial confidentiality, however, does not justify misleading scope or hidden official charges.

## 3. Every amount needs a type

The customer-facing offer should distinguish:

```text
Fixed
Estimated
Conditional
Unknown
```

A fixed amount is committed for a defined scope and validity period. An estimate depends on a named variable. A conditional amount becomes payable only if an event occurs. An Unknown cannot yet be determined.

```text
estimated
≠ fixed

unknown
≠ zero
```

Using zero for an unresolved fee produces false certainty.

## 4. Show later-stage exposure

Many professional services have predictable later stages. A filing may later require publication, registration, a refusal response, evidence of use, opposition work, renewal or certificate handling.

Those stages do not need to be priced as if already due. Their likely existence should still be visible.

```text
not currently payable
≠ commercially irrelevant
```

A route should not appear cheaper merely because foreseeable later-stage exposure has been omitted.

## 5. Price validity and currency

Procurement terms need an issue date, validity period, package version, currency, official-fee check date and named assumptions.

```text
previous price
≠ current price
```

Cross-border routes may involve multiple currencies. The record should identify the conversion basis, timing, tolerance, bank charges and who carries exchange-rate variance. An unexplained spread should not be described as an official or Provider cost.

## 6. Customer-facing simplification

The customer should receive a usable commercial decision, not a raw internal ledger.

A clear offer can summarize:

- what is included now;
- what is excluded;
- which amounts are fixed or estimated;
- which official fees are known;
- which later stages may arise;
- when the offer expires;
- which payment and change rules apply.

The route may display one total, provided the underlying structure remains auditable.

## 7. Commercial neutrality

MGSN and the Originating Workplace may have legitimate commercial interests. Those interests must not distort Eligibility or Recommendation.

```text
higher platform or Workplace margin
≠ stronger route fit
```

A Provider cannot buy entry into the Candidate Route Set. Commercial comparison begins only after qualification and current Eligibility have been established.

## 8. When procurement terms change

A price may change because official fees increase, scope expands, urgency rises, tax changes, currency moves or a third party becomes necessary.

The change should identify:

- what changed;
- why it changed;
- which cost layer is affected;
- the old and new amount;
- the supporting Evidence;
- who must approve;
- whether work must pause.

The original quotation and accepted engagement should remain visible. CH20 develops the full change-control process.

## 9. Representative failure patterns

### One total hides different scope

One route excludes registration and certificate Return while another includes both. The correction is to normalize package and cost layers before comparison.

### Official fees are treated as Provider revenue

The customer cannot tell whether money reached the authority. The correction is to separate the official-fee amount from the Provider fee and require payment Evidence.

### Historical price remains active

An old quotation is reused after fees or package terms changed. The correction is to mark the price stale and obtain current confirmation.

### Margin drives Recommendation

A weaker route is promoted because its commercial spread is larger. The correction is to review recommendation integrity and keep commercial incentives subordinate to route fit.

## 10. Product principle

MGSN can simplify pricing only after it has separated the economics internally.

```text
versioned Service Package
+ separated cost layers
+ typed uncertainty
+ bounded confidentiality
+ governed change control
= trustworthy customer-facing offer
```
