# Book 07 v2 Publication Reconstruction — Batch 12 Review

## Scope

Batch 12 performs controlled editorial consolidation of Part II:

```text
CH05 — Provider Organization and Accountable Supply
CH06 — Admission Is a Governed Lifecycle
CH07 — Capability Supply Portfolios and Service Packages
CH08 — Qualification, Certification and Professional Authority
```

The objective is not to add new Product architecture. It is to reduce repetition, shorten long inventories, clarify each chapter’s primary responsibility and preserve every material authority boundary.

## Editorial result

Part II now follows a cleaner four-step argument:

```text
CH05
Who is the accountable supply participant?

→ CH06
How does that participant become bounded network supply?

→ CH07
How are admitted Capabilities organized into resilient and comparable routes?

→ CH08
What has been verified, qualified, permitted and authorized?
```

The chapters no longer re-teach the complete identity–qualification–Eligibility–Acceptance chain in full each time.

## CH05 review

CH05 now focuses on the accountable participant structure:

- Organization as stable identity;
- Demand and Supply as contextual roles;
- Provider Network Profile as a governed representation;
- bounded Capability as the scope of supply;
- responsible professional distinct from Organization accountability;
- Relationship Provenance without monopoly;
- Provider Acceptance as the point where engagement responsibility begins;
- exit without deletion of obligations.

Long profile-field and failure-mode inventories were reduced. Qualification details were moved to their primary home in CH08, and admission lifecycle details remain in CH06.

Core lock preserved:

```text
known contact
≠ accountable Provider Organization
≠ accepted engagement
```

## CH06 review

CH06 was reduced from an eleven-stage and many-state specification-like presentation to six reader-facing lifecycle stages:

```text
candidate and independent participation
→ identity and connection
→ Provider Network Profile and Capability Claim
→ Evidence, Verification and Qualification
→ Service Package and operational readiness
→ bounded activation
```

Conditional admission, freshness, suspension, exit, correction and incentive logic remain intact.

The chapter now explains why states exist rather than presenting an exhaustive state catalog as the main narrative.

Core lock preserved:

```text
known
≠ invited
≠ admitted
≠ qualified
≠ active for every Need
```

## CH07 review

CH07 now has two clear halves:

1. Capability Supply Portfolio as the resilience structure;
2. Service Package as the operable and comparable offer.

It preserves:

- service-specific rather than country-only coverage;
- contextual portfolio roles;
- multiple routes without auction logic;
- relationship-preserved supply without monopoly;
- honest no-route outcomes;
- Capability distinct from Service Package and Instruction Package;
- explicit scope, exclusions, cost layers, Evidence and Return;
- package versioning;
- concentration and dependency visibility.

Long lists of services, package fields, exclusions and governance inputs were condensed into grouped reader-facing explanations.

Core locks preserved:

```text
country contact list
≠ service-specific supply portfolio
```

```text
Capability
≠ Service Package
≠ engagement-specific instruction
```
