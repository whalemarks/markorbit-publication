## 3. Chapter review

## CH17 — Procurement Terms Must Separate Cost Layers

### Purpose

Separates Provider procurement cost, official fees, taxes, disbursements, platform service, risk support, Workplace margin and customer-facing offer.

### Main distinctions

```text
headline price
≠ complete route economics
```

```text
Provider procurement cost
≠ official fee
≠ platform service fee
≠ customer-facing price
```

```text
estimated
≠ fixed

unknown
≠ zero
```

### Contribution

- binds Provider price to Service Package scope and version;
- treats official fees as external-authority costs with source, freshness and payment Evidence;
- makes taxes, disbursements and third-party costs typed;
- explains the platform-managed service layer without claiming professional-performance authority;
- preserves private Workplace margin while requiring honest customer-facing scope;
- separates fixed, estimated, conditional and Unknown amounts;
- includes later-stage exposure and currency assumptions;
- preserves procurement confidentiality with internal auditability;
- blocks margin or paid placement from bypassing Eligibility.

### Review result

PASS.

## CH18 — Funds Checkpoints Do Not Prove Performance

### Purpose

Prevents payment states from being interpreted as Provider Acceptance, work completion, official payment or protected action.

### Main distinction

```text
funds received
≠ Provider accepted
≠ work started
≠ milestone completed
≠ official fee paid
≠ protected action performed
```

### Contribution

- defines typed funds checkpoints from offer and invoice through settlement, refund and reconciliation;
- keeps MGSN checkpoints distinct from Finance ledger truth;
- separates recording funds from authority to hold, transmit or release money;
- distinguishes customer payment, Provider funding and official-fee funding;
- adds beneficiary-change controls;
- links funds to milestones without treating payment as Evidence of performance;
- supports partial performance, holds, refunds and closure reconciliation;
- improves interface terminology by requiring paid-by-whom, to-whom and for-what context.

### Review result

PASS.

No universal escrow, trust-account, payment-institution or client-money authority is claimed.

## CH19 — Settlement Follows Accepted Milestones and Evidence

### Purpose

Defines settlement as the commercial conclusion of a governed milestone and Evidence review rather than an automatic consequence of invoice receipt.

### Main distinction

```text
invoice received
≠ milestone completed
≠ Evidence accepted
≠ settlement authorized
≠ settlement executed
```

### Contribution

- requires milestones to define output, Evidence, review authority, correction and settlement basis;
- distinguishes Provider statements from official acknowledgements;
- supports Accepted, Conditional, Partial, Correction Required, Disputed and Unknown outcomes;
- allows partial settlement and holdbacks where justified;
- separates MGSN Return acceptance from Owning Service formal-state update;
- explains differences between invoiced and accepted amounts;
- establishes a controlled settlement sequence;
- preserves disputes, correction history and service-specific Trust evidence;
- rejects automatic settlement from silence or elapsed time.

### Review result

PASS.

## CH20 — Commercial Exceptions Need Explicit Change Control

### Purpose

Governs fee increases, scope changes, urgent costs, substitutions, beneficiary changes, cancellation, credits and refunds through explicit versioned change control.

### Main distinction

```text
commercial exception
≠ permission to rewrite the engagement silently
```

```text
Engagement v1
→ Change Request
→ Review and Approval
→ Engagement v2
```

### Contribution

- defines material commercial exceptions;
- requires trigger, old/new scope, old/new price, Evidence, authority and consequences;
- separates Provider fee, official-fee, tax, platform and customer-price changes;
- updates Need, Eligibility, Package, Instruction and Acceptance where scope expands;
- allows emergency decisions without erasing Unknowns or authority requirements;
- distinguishes Provider-caused correction from customer-caused change;
- governs substitution, new beneficiaries and payment paths;
- separates commercial cancellation from official withdrawal;
- preserves credits and refunds without hiding Trust impact;
- requires explicit approval and supersession rather than inferred consent.

### Review result

PASS.
