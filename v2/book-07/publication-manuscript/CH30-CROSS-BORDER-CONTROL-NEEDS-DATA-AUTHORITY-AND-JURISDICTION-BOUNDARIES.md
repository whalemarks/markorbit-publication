# CH30 — Cross-border Control Needs Data, Authority and Jurisdiction Boundaries

MGSN exists because professional work crosses borders.

A customer may be in China, the Originating Workplace in another jurisdiction, the Provider in the United States, the responsible professional in a third office, the payment beneficiary elsewhere and the official authority in yet another country.

The network cannot safely treat this as one undifferentiated transaction.

```text
One Engagement
≠ One Jurisdiction
≠ One Data Rule
≠ One Authority Source
≠ One Funds Regime
```

Cross-border control begins by making the relevant boundaries visible.

## 1. Every route has several jurisdictional dimensions

A single engagement may involve:

- customer jurisdiction;
- Originating Workplace jurisdiction;
- Provider Organization jurisdiction;
- responsible professional’s qualification jurisdiction;
- service-performance jurisdiction;
- official authority jurisdiction;
- data-storage and processing locations;
- payment-origin and beneficiary jurisdictions;
- dispute forum;
- governing-law clauses;
- document execution, notarization or legalization jurisdiction.

These dimensions may align. Often they do not.

A route should therefore carry a jurisdiction map rather than one country label.

```text
Service Jurisdiction
≠ Provider Incorporation Jurisdiction
≠ Data Processing Location
≠ Payment Destination
```

## 2. Data projection must remain purpose-limited

The Capability Need may cross the MGSN Connection before the customer’s full identity or file does.

That separation supports minimum necessary disclosure.

For each disclosure stage, MGSN should identify:

- purpose;
- data categories;
- source;
- recipient;
- jurisdiction;
- legal or contractual basis where required;
- retention period;
- onward-transfer restrictions;
- confidentiality level;
- whether the data is masked, pseudonymous or identified;
- whether professional privilege or another protection may apply;
- whether customer or Workplace approval is required.

```text
Provider Needs Context
≠ Provider Needs Entire Customer File
```

## 3. Data categories have different risk

Cross-border data is not one category.

An engagement may involve:

- ordinary business contact details;
- customer identity;
- company registration documents;
- personal identity documents;
- signatures;
- bank details;
- powers of attorney;
- confidential strategy;
- legal advice;
- evidence of use;
- employee or witness information;
- investigation material;
- dispute or disciplinary information;
- official records;
- health, criminal or other sensitive data in exceptional matters.

The route must not assume that permission to share one category authorizes all others.

```text
Matter Access
≠ Unrestricted Data Access
```

## 4. Conflict checking needs a controlled disclosure path

Conflict review often requires identity before the Provider can accept the engagement.

The network should support progressive conflict disclosure:

```text
Masked Need
→ Preliminary Capability Fit
→ Limited Identity Disclosure for Conflict
→ Conflict Result
→ Further Engagement Disclosure
```

The conflict recipient, purpose and result should be recorded.

A Provider should not reuse conflict-check information for marketing, unrelated relationship development or another service without an appropriate basis.

```text
Identity Disclosed for Conflict
≠ Permission to Solicit the Customer
```

## 5. Professional confidentiality and platform visibility differ

MGSN may need operational visibility to manage milestones, deadlines and Returns.

That does not mean the platform is entitled to read all professional communications or privileged materials.

A route may distinguish:

- platform-visible operational metadata;
- Workplace-visible customer and delivery information;
- Provider-visible engagement information;
- restricted professional content;
- customer-only or professional-only communication;
- Evidence that can be referenced but not broadly copied.

```text
Platform Coordinates the Engagement
≠ Platform Owns Professional Confidentiality
```

Where MGSN cannot inspect content, it may still record:

- that a restricted exchange occurred;
- participants;
- time;
- applicable purpose;
- required next step;
- whether an Evidence artifact exists;
- whether a qualified reviewer confirmed completion.

## 6. Authority must be sourced by jurisdiction and action

Professional authority cannot be inferred from a global Provider profile.

The route must identify:

- action to be performed;
- jurisdiction and authority;
- required professional qualification;
- responsible person or Organization;
- appointment or representation requirement;
- customer mandate;
- document form;
- signature, notarization, legalization or original-document requirement;
- expiry or continuing validity;
- whether authority covers preparation, filing, withdrawal, settlement or another protected act.

```text
Qualified Somewhere
≠ Authorized Here

Authorized for Filing
≠ Authorized for Withdrawal or Settlement
```

Where authority evidence is incomplete, the route should remain conditional, Unknown or blocked.

## 7. Original documents need custody and chain of control

Cross-border professional work may still depend on original documents.

The network should record:

- document identity and version;
- owner;
- sender;
- recipient;
- courier reference;
- dispatch and receipt dates;
- condition;
- intended use;
- whether return is required;
- retention period;
- replacement possibility;
- loss or delay response;
- whether a scan and original have different legal significance.

```text
Scanned Copy Received
≠ Original Received
≠ Original Accepted by Authority
```

A lost original can create legal, deadline and customer risk. Custody should not disappear into an email thread.

## 8. Payment routes require jurisdictional review

The customer, Workplace, platform, Provider and official authority may use different currencies and banking jurisdictions.

The route should distinguish:

- payer;
- invoice issuer;
- contractual payee;
- payment processor;
- beneficiary;
- Provider Organization;
- official-fee recipient;
- currency;
- taxes;
- exchange-rate basis;
- sanctions and banking restrictions;
- refund destination;
- evidence of receipt.

```text
Provider Requests Payment Abroad
≠ Beneficiary Automatically Verified
```

A change of country, beneficiary or account should trigger review rather than administrative replacement.

## 9. Cross-border subcontracting compounds risk

When a Provider uses another Organization or professional in a different jurisdiction, the route may gain additional:

- data transfers;
- professional authority checks;
- conflict reviews;
- funds beneficiaries;
- confidentiality duties;
- dispute forums;
- tax and sanctions questions;
- document custody points.

The network must know the real delivery chain.

```text
Provider A Accepted
≠ Every Downstream Participant Authorized
```

Material downstream participants should be disclosed and governed according to their role.

## 10. Jurisdiction clauses do not solve every dispute

A contract may name governing law or a dispute forum.

That does not necessarily determine:

- professional discipline;
- official authority procedure;
- data-regulatory jurisdiction;
- local representation validity;
- criminal or fraud reporting;
- client-money obligations;
- mandatory consumer or employment rules.

MGSN should record contractual forums without claiming that one clause overrides every external regime.

```text
Contractual Forum
≠ Universal Jurisdiction
```

## 11. Cross-border incident response

A data, funds, authority or deadline incident may require action in several jurisdictions.

The incident record should identify:

- affected data and participants;
- where the event occurred;
- where harm may occur;
- immediate containment;
- notification obligations requiring professional review;
- official deadlines;
- funds holds;
- evidence preservation;
- external counsel or specialist review;
- customer communication;
- replacement-route implications.

MGSN can coordinate evidence and safe-stop actions. It should not claim to make legal notification determinations without the appropriate authority and review.

## 12. Retention, deletion and audit are role-specific

Different records may require different treatment.

For example:

- operational messages may have one retention period;
- official evidence another;
- financial records another;
- professional files another;
- conflict data another;
- incident evidence another;
- customer identity data another.

Deletion by one participant should not erase evidence another party must legally or professionally retain.

At the same time, retention should not become a pretext for indefinite unrestricted use.

```text
Must Retain
≠ May Reuse for Any Purpose
```

## 13. AI and cross-border data

AI may assist with:

- classifying data categories;
- identifying likely sensitive content;
- mapping recipients and jurisdictions;
- checking whether the requested disclosure exceeds the current purpose;
- detecting missing authority documents;
- comparing beneficiary information;
- drafting a disclosure or transfer summary.

AI must not:

- invent a legal basis;
- waive confidentiality;
- decide privilege;
- determine professional authority from incomplete data;
- approve cross-border transfer by itself;
- send sensitive records without human and policy authorization;
- treat model training as an implied secondary purpose.

## 14. Failure modes

### 14.1 Full customer file sent before conflict clearance

The Provider receives sensitive strategy before confirming it can act.

Correct response: use progressive disclosure and record the conflict-check purpose.

### 14.2 Responsible professional qualified in the wrong jurisdiction

A global firm profile obscures that the assigned person lacks the required local authority.

Correct response: bind qualification and authority to the exact service and jurisdiction.

### 14.3 Original POA lost in transit

The parties know a document was mailed but cannot prove receipt or custody.

Correct response: preserve courier evidence, chain of custody, deadline impact and replacement plan.

### 14.4 New overseas beneficiary accepted from one email

Funds are sent without independent verification.

Correct response: pause payment and verify Organization relationship, account change and sanctions context.

### 14.5 Platform retains privileged content for unrestricted learning

Restricted material is reused beyond engagement purpose.

Correct response: separate operational learning from protected content and apply purpose limitation.

## 15. Product principle

Cross-border capability is not created by moving information and money faster.

It is created by moving the right information, authority, funds and evidence through the right jurisdictional boundaries with visible responsibility.

```text
Cross-border Control
= Purpose-limited Data
+ Sourced Authority
+ Verified Participants
+ Governed Funds
+ Accountable Documents
+ Bounded Jurisdiction
```
