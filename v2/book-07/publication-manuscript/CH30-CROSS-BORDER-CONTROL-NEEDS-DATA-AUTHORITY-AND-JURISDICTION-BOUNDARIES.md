# CH30 — Cross-border Control Needs Data, Authority and Jurisdiction Boundaries

MGSN exists because professional work crosses borders.

A customer may be in one country, the Originating Workplace in another, the Provider in a third, the responsible professional elsewhere, the payment beneficiary in another jurisdiction and the official authority somewhere else again.

The network cannot safely treat this as one undifferentiated transaction.

```text
One Engagement
≠ One Jurisdiction
≠ One Data Rule
≠ One Authority Source
≠ One Funds Regime
```

## 1. Every route has a jurisdiction map

A route may involve:

- customer and Workplace jurisdictions;
- Provider and responsible-professional jurisdictions;
- service-performance and official-authority jurisdictions;
- data-storage and processing locations;
- payment origin and beneficiary jurisdictions;
- dispute forum and governing-law clauses;
- document execution, notarization or legalization jurisdictions.

```text
Service Jurisdiction
≠ Provider Incorporation Jurisdiction
≠ Data Processing Location
≠ Payment Destination
```

A single country label cannot represent the full control surface.

## 2. Data disclosure remains purpose-limited

The Capability Need may cross the MGSN Connection before the customer’s full identity or file does.

For each disclosure stage, the network should identify purpose, recipient, data category, jurisdiction, retention, confidentiality and onward-transfer restrictions.

```text
Provider Needs Context
≠ Provider Needs Entire Customer File
```

Permission to share ordinary business details does not automatically authorize transfer of identity documents, signatures, bank information, legal advice, investigation materials or privileged content.

```text
Matter Access
≠ Unrestricted Data Access
```

## 3. Conflict disclosure must be controlled

Conflict review often requires identity before Provider Acceptance.

A safer sequence is:

```text
Masked Need
→ Preliminary Capability Fit
→ Limited Identity Disclosure for Conflict
→ Conflict Result
→ Further Engagement Disclosure
```

The recipient, purpose and result should be recorded.

```text
Identity Disclosed for Conflict
≠ Permission to Solicit the Customer
```

## 4. Platform visibility is not ownership of confidentiality

MGSN may need operational visibility into Milestones, deadlines and Returns. That does not entitle the platform to read every professional or privileged communication.

```text
Platform Coordinates the Engagement
≠ Platform Owns Professional Confidentiality
```

Where content is restricted, the platform may still record that an exchange occurred, who participated, what next step is due and whether a qualified reviewer confirmed completion.

## 5. Authority must be tied to action and jurisdiction

A global Provider profile cannot establish authority for every country or act.

The route should identify:

- the exact action;
- the relevant jurisdiction and authority;
- required qualification;
- responsible person or Organization;
- customer mandate and appointment;
- document, signature, original, notarization or legalization requirements;
- whether authority covers preparation, filing, withdrawal, settlement or another Protected Action.

```text
Qualified Somewhere
≠ Authorized Here

Authorized for Filing
≠ Authorized for Withdrawal or Settlement
```

Where Evidence is incomplete, the route remains conditional, Unknown or blocked.

## 6. Original documents need custody control

Cross-border work may still depend on original POAs, legalized documents, signed declarations or certificates.

The network should record ownership, sender, recipient, courier, dispatch, receipt, intended use, retention and return requirements.

```text
Scanned Copy Received
≠ Original Received
≠ Original Accepted by Authority
```

A lost original can create deadline and rights risk. Its custody should not disappear into an email thread.

## 7. Payment routes require separate verification

The payer, invoice issuer, contractual payee, processor, beneficiary, Provider Organization and official-fee recipient may be different entities.

```text
Provider Requests Payment Abroad
≠ Beneficiary Automatically Verified
```

A new country, beneficiary or account should trigger review rather than administrative replacement.

Relevant checks include Organization relationship, currency, taxes, sanctions, exchange-rate basis, refund destination and Evidence of receipt.

## 8. Downstream delivery compounds cross-border risk

When a Provider uses another Organization or professional, the route may gain new data transfers, authority checks, Conflict reviews, beneficiaries and document custody points.

```text
Provider A Accepted
≠ Every Downstream Participant Authorized
```

Material downstream participants should be disclosed and governed according to their actual role.

## 9. Contract clauses do not create universal jurisdiction

A contract may name governing law or a Dispute forum. That clause does not necessarily control professional discipline, official procedure, data regulation, local representation, criminal reporting or client-money obligations.

```text
Contractual Forum
≠ Universal Jurisdiction
```

MGSN should record contractual forums without claiming that one clause overrides every external regime.

## 10. Retention and reuse remain distinct

Different records may require different retention periods: operational messages, Official Evidence, financial records, professional files, Conflict data and Incident records.

Deletion by one participant should not erase Evidence another must retain. Retention should also not become a pretext for unrestricted reuse.

```text
Must Retain
≠ May Reuse for Any Purpose
```

## 11. Product principle

Cross-border capability is not created by moving information and money faster.

It is created by moving the right information, authority, funds and Evidence through visible jurisdictional boundaries with attributable responsibility.

```text
Cross-border Control
= Purpose-limited Data
+ Sourced Authority
+ Verified Participants
+ Governed Funds
+ Accountable Documents
+ Bounded Jurisdiction
```
