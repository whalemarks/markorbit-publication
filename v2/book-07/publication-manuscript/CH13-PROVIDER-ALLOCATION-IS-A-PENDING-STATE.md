# CH13 — Provider Allocation Is a Pending State

A user has selected a route. The Provider is qualified, currently eligible and appears able to meet the deadline.

Work has still not begun.

The route decision belongs to the Originating Workplace. Responsibility for delivery begins only when the Provider Organization reviews the exact engagement and accepts it.

```text
User Disposition
≠ Provider Acceptance
```

MGSN therefore treats Provider Allocation as a controlled pending state between route choice and accountable commitment.

## 1. Why route selection cannot create an engagement

A Recommendation is built from current evidence, but conditions may change before the Provider responds. Capacity may disappear, a conflict may emerge, a responsible professional may become unavailable, the customer may alter the scope or a document may reveal work outside the quoted Service Package.

A route selection means:

> Ask this Provider to consider this exact engagement under these exact conditions.

It does not mean:

> Treat the Provider as already instructed and responsible.

The operating chain is:

```text
Recommended Route
→ User Disposition
→ Provider Allocation
→ Provider Response
→ Provider Acceptance
```

## 2. Allocation is a bounded request for commitment

The Allocation should identify the route the network is proposing to activate. It should bind the current Need version, Provider Organization, Capability, Service Package, expected scope, price basis, deadline, conflict state, data-disclosure stage and response deadline.

It should also show material conditions and Unknowns. The Provider must be able to see what it is being asked to accept without reconstructing the request from an email chain.

Allocation remains a Product-side record. It does not appoint the Provider under professional law, create a customer mandate or authorize a filing.

```text
Allocation
= request for bounded Provider commitment

Allocation
≠ legal appointment
≠ customer instruction
```

## 3. Allocation must be version-bound

The Provider should accept a defined version, not a moving target.

A changed applicant, jurisdiction, class, deadline, contentious issue, price, responsible professional or conflict context may require a revised Allocation. The previous version should remain visible because it explains what the Provider originally reviewed.

```text
Same Provider
≠ Same Allocation

Same Matter
≠ Same Scope Version
```

Minor clarification may not require a new route. A material change that alters eligibility, scope, price, timing or authority does.

## 4. Provider responses must be meaningful

The Provider should be able to:

- accept;
- accept with conditions;
- request clarification or a governed change;
- require further conflict or professional review;
- decline;
- allow the Allocation to expire without response.

The response must be attributable to an authorized representative of the Provider Organization. A casual “looks fine” should not silently become formal Acceptance when the scope, deadline or conditions remain unclear.

## 5. Provider Acceptance

Provider Acceptance is the Provider Organization’s explicit commitment to perform the defined engagement.

It should confirm the accepting Organization, authorized person, responsible professional where required, accepted Allocation and Package version, scope, exclusions, price basis, deadline assumptions, conflict result, document requirements, communication route and expected Evidence and Return.

This creates engagement responsibility. It does not prove that every prerequisite has been satisfied or that the customer has approved a protected action.

```text
Provider Acceptance
= Provider accepts engagement responsibility

Provider Acceptance
≠ Customer Instruction
```

## 6. Conditional Acceptance must remain visibly conditional

A Provider may accept subject to an original Power of Attorney arriving, funds being available, customer identity passing conflict review, a translation being approved or a responsible professional confirming a difficult issue.

The condition should identify:

- who owns it;
- when it must be resolved;
- what Evidence proves resolution;
- whether preparation may begin;
- what happens if it remains unmet.

A conditional Acceptance should not be displayed as fully ready.

```text
Conditionally Accepted
≠ Ready for Every Action
```

## 7. Decline is a legitimate result

A qualified Provider may decline because of conflict, capacity, deadline, professional concern, data restriction, funds path or inability to return the required Evidence.

A safe decline can be evidence of professional discipline. It should not automatically reduce Trust.

The reason should guide the next step: conflict may require another route; capacity may require a backup; scope mismatch may require a specialist or a revised Need.

## 8. Silence is not Acceptance

International service failures often begin with an unanswered email that one side assumes has been accepted.

```text
Message Delivered
≠ Message Read
≠ Allocation Acknowledged
≠ Provider Accepted
```

Every Allocation needs a response deadline, escalation path, expiry and backup-route rule. Without explicit Acceptance, the state remains pending or expires.

Historical practice does not cure the problem. “They always act when we email them” is not current Acceptance tied to current scope and deadline.

## 9. Data access remains purpose-limited

Conflict review or Provider evaluation may require customer identity and selected documents. Allocation does not justify unrestricted access to the customer file.

If the Provider declines, further engagement access should stop, while necessary conflict and audit records remain protected.

The Originating Workplace also remains the Relationship Owner unless a separate contracting model says otherwise.

```text
Provider Receives Engagement Access
≠ Provider Acquires Customer Relationship
```

## 10. Emergency Allocation compresses time, not authority

An urgent deadline may justify rapid availability confirmation, narrow preliminary disclosure, immediate escalation and a short Acceptance window.

It should still preserve:

```text
Who accepted?
What scope?
Which deadline?
Which conditions?
What remains Unknown?
Which action is authorized?
```

Urgency cannot create missing professional authority or customer instruction.

## 11. Replacement requires a new commitment

A pending or accepted route may fail because of silence, conflict, lost capacity, suspension or a material scope change.

The replacement Provider must receive its own Allocation and complete its own conflict, Eligibility and Acceptance checks.

```text
Replacement Provider
≠ Transferred Acceptance
≠ Transferred Customer Mandate
```

The original Allocation, disclosed data, funds state, documents, deadline and reason for replacement must remain visible for recovery.

## 12. Product principle

Provider Allocation is the bridge between a user’s choice and a Provider’s accountable commitment.

```text
Route Choice
→ Pending Allocation
→ Explicit Provider Response
→ Provider Acceptance
→ Versioned Instruction
```
