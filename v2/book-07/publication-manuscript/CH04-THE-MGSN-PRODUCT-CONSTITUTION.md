# Chapter 04 — The MGSN Product Constitution

MGSN can coordinate a global service chain only if its boundaries are stronger than its convenience features.

Without a Constitution, the Product will drift toward whichever pressure is strongest. Growth will ask for more visible Providers. Procurement will ask for lower prices. Participants will ask for unrestricted contact. Operations will ask to bypass acceptance. Finance will be tempted to treat payment as progress. AI systems will be asked to automate route selection. A serious incident may tempt the platform to take authority it never held.

Each shortcut can look reasonable in isolation. Together they can turn a managed professional network into an opaque broker.

The Constitution defines what MGSN may optimize and what it must preserve even when preservation slows the transaction.

## The constitutional topology

The basic chain is:

```text
Originating Workplace
→ authorized Capability Need Projection
→ MGSN Connection
→ platform-governed MGSN Network
→ eligible Candidate Route Set
→ Recommendation and user disposition
→ Provider Allocation and Acceptance
→ governed fulfillment
→ typed Return
→ validation by the Originating Workplace and Owning Service
```

These systems cooperate. They are not one actor.

### Originating Workplace

The Originating Workplace ordinarily retains the customer relationship, Matter context, private pricing, internal Knowledge and the business decision to seek outside capability. It decides whether to project a Need and whether to accept, reject or rematch a proposed route.

Projection does not transfer the customer or the Workplace’s business sovereignty to MGSN.

### MGSN Connection

The Connection is the Workplace-scoped boundary through which authorized demand enters the Network and governed Returns come back.

It controls purpose, disclosure and approval. It may mask customer identity, limit documents, attach restrictions and require internal review before information crosses the boundary.

The Connection is not the shared Network. Operating a Connection does not give a participant ownership of Provider supply, procurement terms, route logic or Trust evidence.

### MGSN Network

The platform-governed Network manages shared resources that cannot be governed independently by every participant:

- Provider admission and network Profiles;
- Capability Claims, Evidence and qualification records;
- Supply Portfolios and Service Packages;
- Eligibility and route formation;
- Recommendation presentation;
- Allocation and Provider Acceptance records;
- network fulfillment, correction and replacement pathways;
- Trust evidence, restrictions and learning.

The platform owns the mechanism, not the participant’s customer or the Provider’s professional practice.

### Execution Provider Workplace

The Provider Workplace retains its Organization identity, responsible professionals, work methods, professional judgment and the right to accept or decline work. It remains responsible for actions performed within its accepted scope and for applicable correction duties.

Admission to MGSN does not make a Provider the employee, agent or unrestricted subcontractor of every participant.

### Core, Execution and Owning Services

Core provides shared semantic authority so terms such as Organization, Matter, Evidence, Permission and Event remain consistent.

Execution governs how authorized work moves through validation, Assignment, Contribution, Review, Approval, Apply, Return, reconciliation and recovery.

Owning Services retain formal-state authority for the records they own. MGSN may return Evidence about a filing, payment, decision or certificate. A structurally valid Return does not automatically change another Product’s formal state.

### Official and professional authority

Official institutions determine official state. Qualified professionals exercise reserved judgment and action under applicable authority.

MGSN may verify Evidence, represent appointments and control its own permissions. It cannot create a professional licence, invent official acceptance or turn Recommendation into authority to act.

## Seven constitutional locks

Every material feature must preserve seven boundaries.

### 1. Workplace sovereignty

The Workplace retains its customer relationship, private context and business decisions unless another arrangement is explicit.

```text
Need Projection
≠ customer transfer
```

Information disclosed for routing cannot become unrestricted prospecting inventory.

### 2. Platform network governance

Shared network resources remain platform-governed.

```text
Access to a Provider
≠ ownership of the Network
```

This prevents a participant from exporting the Provider graph while continuing to claim MGSN protection. Platform governance must also remain reviewable enough to prevent arbitrary exclusion and self-preference.

### 3. Provider professional responsibility

The Provider remains responsible for professional work it accepts and performs.

```text
Platform Recommendation
≠ platform performance of the professional service
```

The network may govern route quality and recovery. It must still show who advised, signed, submitted or represented the customer.

### 4. Authority separation

Identity, Capability, admission, qualification, Eligibility, appointment and instruction remain separate stages.

A Provider admitted to the Network is not qualified for every service. A route that is eligible has not yet been accepted. Provider Acceptance does not prove that the customer authorized a protected action.

The closer a decision moves toward official or reserved professional consequence, the stronger the authority Evidence must become.

### 5. Meaningful user disposition and Provider Acceptance

MGSN may automate classification, filtering and Recommendation drafting. Ordinary professional routing still requires meaningful user disposition and explicit Provider Acceptance.

```text
Automatic matching
≠ automatic appointment
```

Greater automation is possible only inside declared authority, risk and exception controls.

### 6. Evidence-backed fulfillment and typed Return

Completion cannot be inferred from silence, payment or a generic “done” message.

A Return should identify what the Provider says occurred, the relevant stage, supporting documents or official sources, outstanding issues, fees, deadlines and any required correction.

```text
Return received
≠ Return accepted
≠ formal state updated
```

### 7. Separate authorization for funds and protected actions

Commercial coordination belongs in the Product. Regulated custody and protected external action are not assumed.

```text
Payment requirement recorded
≠ funds custody authorized

Customer paid
≠ Provider performed

Provider prepared
≠ filing authorized
```

Escrow, money transmission, payment release, legal representation, filing and signature each require their own gates.

## The three route families

The Constitution recognizes three broad routes.

### R1 — External self-managed route

The Workplace uses a Provider outside MGSN or manages the relationship independently. The platform should not imply procurement, replacement or fulfillment protection it did not provide. Any result returning to MarkOrbit enters through source and validation controls.

### R2 — MGSN recommended managed route

MGSN forms an eligible Candidate Route Set, explains a Recommendation and manages the route accepted by both user and Provider.

### R3 — Managed preferred-provider route

The Workplace asks to use an existing Provider relationship. MGSN honors that preference only after current admission, qualification, conflict, availability, package and risk checks.

Preference influences route formation. It does not bypass safety.

## What MGSN may decide

Within its own Product authority, MGSN may determine whether:

- an Organization may participate in the Network;
- a Provider Profile has sufficient Evidence;
- a Service Package meets comparison requirements;
- a route is eligible under declared rules;
- new allocations should be restricted or suspended;
- network correction or appeal procedures are satisfied;
- network Evidence may influence future routing.

These decisions may still require notice, human review and appeal.

## What MGSN may not decide alone

MGSN cannot unilaterally determine that:

- a customer appointed a professional;
- a Provider has no legal conflict;
- a person holds current professional authority without Evidence;
- an official submission succeeded or official rights changed;
- a disputed professional fee is legally owed;
- participant data may be reused outside purpose;
- an adverse official result proves Provider failure;
- a high Trust result overrides jurisdiction or authority requirements.

Those conclusions belong to other authorities or require Evidence and review beyond platform preference.

## The shortcuts that break the model

The Constitution is tested most clearly by attractive shortcuts.

Auto-appointing the top-ranked Provider collapses Recommendation, conflict, appointment and Acceptance. Showing every Provider’s full contact details turns controlled access into an open graph. Routing by platform margin disguises self-interest as suitability. Treating payment as service commencement lets funds state override readiness and authority. Marking a Matter complete when a file is uploaded bypasses Return validation and Owning Service authority. Silent rebrokering hides the responsible Organization and professional. One public rating erases context and contestability.

These shortcuts may increase transaction volume. They would weaken the Product’s purpose.

## The constitutional review

Before approving a material Product decision, reviewers should be able to answer:

1. Which Organization retains the customer relationship?
2. Which data crosses the Connection, for what purpose and under whose authority?
3. Which Provider Organization and professional are responsible?
4. Which decision belongs to the platform, Workplace, Provider, Owning Service or official authority?
5. Has the user made the required disposition?
6. Has the Provider accepted the exact scope?
7. What Evidence proves progress or completion?
8. What remains Unknown?
9. What happens if the route fails?
10. Does any financial or protected action require separate authorization?

A feature that cannot answer these questions is not ready merely because its interface is clear.

The final lock of Part I is:

```text
Managed coordination
≠ unlimited platform authority
```

MGSN becomes trustworthy not by claiming control over every outcome, but by making the limits and transitions of control explicit.
