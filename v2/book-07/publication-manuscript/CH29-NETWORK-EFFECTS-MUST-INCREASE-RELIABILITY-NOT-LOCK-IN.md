# CH29 — Network Effects Must Increase Reliability, Not Lock-in

A professional network becomes more valuable when each completed engagement improves the next one.

That improvement may come from better Capability coverage, clearer Service Packages, stronger Evidence requirements, more reliable replacement routes and faster recovery.

But growth can also create dependency: relationships become opaque, records become trapped and participants remain only because leaving would destroy operating continuity.

MGSN must distinguish reliability-producing network effects from dependency-producing lock-in.

```text
More Participants
≠ Better Network

More Transactions
≠ Greater Reliability

More Dependence
≠ Durable Value
```

## 1. The right network effect

A healthy network effect should improve:

- qualified service-specific supply;
- visibility of coverage gaps and concentration;
- Package clarity and price comparability;
- Evidence quality;
- replacement readiness;
- deadline and handoff reliability;
- recovery with less loss of context.

```text
Reliable Network Effect
= Better Supply
+ Better Decisions
+ Better Evidence
+ Better Recovery
```

The value should come from accumulated operating knowledge, not restricted exit.

## 2. Growth should reduce hidden dependency

Ten visible Providers may still depend on one responsible professional, one subcontractor or one banking route.

```text
Provider Count
≠ Independent Supply
```

For each critical Capability, MGSN should understand whether alternatives are genuinely independent, how quickly another route can accept work and what records must transfer.

Network growth should expose single points of failure rather than conceal them.

## 3. Relationships are not platform property

The Originating Workplace retains its customer relationship. The Provider retains its organizational and professional identity. MGSN may preserve Relationship Provenance and enforce bounded protections around introduced business or confidential procurement terms.

```text
Platform-mediated Introduction
≠ Platform Ownership of the Relationship

Network Access
≠ Permanent Exclusivity
```

Non-circumvention may be legitimate when it protects a real, time-bound interest. It should not prevent use of a pre-existing Provider, claim unrelated customers or hide the party actually performing the work.

## 4. Portability protects continuity

Matter Portability means a participant can obtain the records reasonably necessary to continue its own Matter, subject to privacy, professional duties and network confidentiality.

Relevant records may include accepted Package versions, Allocation and Acceptance, Instructions, Milestones, Returns, Evidence, deadlines, Funds Checkpoints and open Correction or Dispute history.

It does not mean unrestricted export of the Provider pool, unrelated pricing or another participant’s private Knowledge.

```text
Matter Portability
≠ Provider Directory Export

Relationship Continuity
≠ Network Data Extraction
```

## 5. Data portability does not transfer authority

Exporting a file or Instruction Package preserves context. It does not appoint a new Provider or authorize an action.

```text
Data Ported
≠ Authority Transferred

History Available
≠ Engagement Continued
```

A changed route may still require new Conflict review, Provider Acceptance, professional appointment, POA, customer approval, Funds Authority and communication permissions.

## 6. Anti-lock-in by design

Artificial dependence appears when the platform:

- withholds original Evidence;
- prevents Matter-level export;
- hides accountable Provider identity;
- makes replacement impossible without platform discretion;
- traps original documents or unreconciled funds;
- destroys access immediately on exit;
- stores only opaque summaries with no usable source record.

Anti-lock-in design requires attributable records, version history, role-scoped export, visible obligations, controlled wind-down and no destruction of audit history.

## 7. Exit is an operating event

Exit should identify open engagements, deadlines, funds, original documents, incomplete Returns, unresolved Corrections, professional appointments and continuing confidentiality obligations.

Possible outcomes include immediate exit with no open work, controlled wind-down, Matter transfer, Capability retirement or partial role exit.

Demand and Supply roles should not be terminated together where only one role is affected.

## 8. Real switching cost versus artificial friction

A replacement Provider may need to review records, repeat Conflict checks, verify authority, reconstruct deadlines and charge for duplicated professional work.

```text
Real Transition Work
≠ Platform-created Exit Penalty
```

The network should expose transition effort, duplicated fees, deadline risk, document availability and unresolved Provider obligations without pretending that switching is free.

## 9. Retention by value

A platform may be tempted to optimize captured communication, exclusive participation and switching friction.

A better model values successful completion, Evidence quality, replacement readiness, low unresolved exit burden and participant renewal by choice.

```text
Retention by Value
≠ Retention by Friction
```

Data retained for audit, dispute or legal obligations must remain purpose-limited.

```text
Data Retained for Accountability
≠ Data Free for Any Future Use
```

## 10. Product principle

The strongest network is not the one participants cannot leave.

It is the one they choose to remain in because it creates better routes, stronger Evidence and safer recovery while respecting sovereignty.

```text
Sustainable MGSN
= Reliability
+ Portability
+ Relationship Integrity
− Artificial Lock-in
```
