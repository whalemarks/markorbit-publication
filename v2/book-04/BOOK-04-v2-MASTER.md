# Book 04 v2
# MarkOrbit Workplace and Collaboration Architecture

## Purpose

Workplace is the Organization-scoped boundary for business sovereignty, accountability, permission, Product operation and controlled external projection.

It is also the primary context in which a Person or Organization practices, evidences and develops Capability without surrendering private customers, private Reflection, operating memory or professional responsibility to the platform.

A Workplace is not a generic dashboard, tenant label, universal profile or owner of every platform Capability it uses.

## Part I — Identity, sovereignty and represented capability

### Chapter 01 — Person, Organization and Workplace

A Person holds identity, personal Capability Evidence and professional credentials. An Organization bears legal, commercial, professional or administrative accountability. A Workplace is the Organization-scoped operating boundary. Membership connects a Person to a Workplace with explicit role and authority.

A Person may practice a Capability in several Workplaces, but the context, Evidence, customer authority and permitted reuse may differ in each.

```text
Identity Continuity
≠ Cross-Workplace Data Authority
≠ Universal Capability Claim
```

### Chapter 02 — Personal and organizational Workplaces

An independent professional may operate a personal Workplace and also participate in one or more organizational Workplaces. Every action, Reflection and Capability event must identify the represented Workplace.

A practice result created for an employer, a client or a private business does not automatically become portable personal Evidence. Rights, confidentiality, professional duties and organizational policy remain attached to the originating context.

### Chapter 03 — Business sovereignty

Workplace sovereignty covers:

- customer relationships;
- private pricing and commercial policy;
- internal Knowledge and operating memory;
- private Reflection and Case material;
- configuration and Product priorities;
- accountable decisions;
- permission to project Needs, Capabilities, assets and capacity.

It does not authorize bypassing Core semantics, Owning Services, Professional Qualification, legal restrictions or protected-action controls.

## Part II — Resource, Need and Capability Profile

### Chapter 04 — Resource Profile

A Workplace may possess:

- customer relationships and audience;
- marketing channels;
- trademark assets;
- staff and delivery capacity;
- local Professional Qualification;
- Provider relationships;
- Cases, Knowledge and tools;
- practiced, evidenced or developing Capabilities.

Resource Profile describes what the Workplace can responsibly use. It does not convert every asset into network-visible supply.

### Chapter 05 — Need Profile

A Workplace may need customers, promotion, search, classification, local counsel, visual design, temporary capacity, expert review, trademark supply, transaction support, Capability development or formal fulfillment.

A Need may concern business resources, professional delivery or Capability growth. These should remain distinct because they require different disclosure, eligibility and authority rules.

### Chapter 06 — Capability Profile and Capability Twin

A Workplace Capability Profile is a governed projection of Capabilities that a Person or Organization has practiced, evidenced, is authorized to perform or is currently developing.

It may contain:

- Capability and version references;
- practice and invocation history;
- accepted Cases and Evidence;
- simulation and assessment results;
- certification and expiry;
- Production Authorization;
- Professional Qualification references;
- current development goals;
- confidence and Evidence limitations.

A Capability Twin is a Product- or Workplace-held representation derived from this Evidence. It may support guidance, staffing, delegation, learning and risk-aware assistance.

```text
Capability Twin
≠ Personality Replica
≠ Universal Judgment of a Person
≠ Professional Qualification
≠ Automatic Production Authorization
```

The Twin should represent what the available Evidence supports, including uncertainty and context limits.

### Chapter 07 — Asymmetry as normal

The platform must not assume every Workplace is a full-service firm or every Person has the same Capability pattern.

One Workplace may possess customer relationships but need local counsel. Another may have deep prosecution expertise but weak marketing. A third may have strong content and trademark-commerce Capability but require professional review.

A useful network arises because participants hold different resources, Capabilities and gaps.

## Part III — Controlled projection

### Chapter 08 — Demand Projection

A Workplace may project a bounded Need without exposing its full customer record.

Projection specifies:

- purpose;
- required Capability;
- disclosure level;
- expiry;
- relationship constraints;
- authority and review conditions.

A Capability-growth Need should not be presented as a production assignment unless the user is eligible and authorized for real work.

### Chapter 09 — Capability Projection

A Workplace or Person may project verified or bounded Capability information, including jurisdiction, language, capacity, Service Package, turnaround and participation preference.

Projection should distinguish:

```text
Practiced
Evidenced
Certified
Production-authorized
Professionally qualified
Currently available
```

These states may overlap but are not interchangeable.

Private Reflection, raw conversations, customer details and unsupported self-assessment remain outside the projection by default.

### Chapter 10 — Asset, Audience and Capacity Projection

Trademark assets, reseller authorization, marketing reach and available delivery capacity may be projected under separate rights and visibility policies.

Capability projection does not authorize disclosure of unrelated Cases, customer records or private pricing.

### Chapter 11 — Projection is not transfer

```text
Platform Visibility
≠ Network Visibility

Projection
≠ Ownership Transfer

Need Publication
≠ Customer Transfer

Capability Projection
≠ Professional Appointment

Provider Access
≠ Relationship Ownership
```

## Part IV — Engagement architecture

### Chapter 12 — From Need to Engagement

Need becomes Opportunity when a viable route is identified. Engagement begins only after participants accept role, scope, disclosure, responsibility, commercial terms and termination conditions.

A Capability request, learning recommendation or simulation invitation is not itself a production Engagement.

### Chapter 13 — Engagement roles

A single engagement may include:

- Demand Originator;
- Relationship Owner;
- Contracting Party;
- Payment Receiver;
- Solution Orchestrator;
- Capability Contributor;
- Professional Authority;
- Delivery Owner;
- Reviewer or Assessor.

Roles are engagement-specific, not permanent Workplace types.

### Chapter 14 — Collaboration modes

Supported modes include referral, co-delivery, white-label delivery, bounded WorkPackage contribution, expert review, managed network fulfillment, simulation and supervised production.

The same Person may participate under different contracts in different contexts. A simulated role or bounded contribution never silently expands into professional representation.

### Chapter 15 — Customer relationship protection

The default Relationship Owner remains the originating party unless a recorded agreement changes it. Other participants receive only the information and communication rights necessary for their role.

Capability learning, assessment and Reflection do not create customer ownership for MarkOrbit or another participant.

## Part V — Product architecture and adaptive experience

### Chapter 16 — Product Installation

A Product Installation records edition, entitlement, enabled Capabilities, configuration, roles, data scope, connector policy, projection policy, audit reference and lifecycle.

It may also record which Capability experiences are enabled, such as guided execution, simulation, AI Mentor, Capability Profile views or paid-task eligibility.

### Chapter 17 — Dynamic Workplace experience

Lite, MarkReg and other Products may adapt the home experience according to Resource Profile, Need Profile, Capability Profile and current pipeline.

A marketing-oriented Workplace, capability-oriented Workplace and asset-oriented Workplace may see different priorities while sharing the same semantic foundation.

Adaptation may control guidance depth, recommended practice, review requirements and opportunity visibility. It must not infer Professional Authority from interface behavior.

### Chapter 18 — Capability guidance and AI Mentor

A Product may use the Capability Profile to recommend:

- the next useful practice;
- a relevant Source Capability or Skill;
- simulation before production;
- stronger professional review;
- a missing Supporting or Critic Capability;
- a Case or Principle relevant to the current task.

The AI Mentor should explain the Evidence and uncertainty behind its guidance.

```text
AI Mentor Recommendation
≠ Final Judgment of the User
≠ Certification
≠ Professional Qualification
```

### Chapter 19 — Product Projection

Client portals, public Sites, Provider instruction views, trademark listing pages, Capability cards and network requests are purpose-limited projections.

They are not separate sovereignty domains unless an explicit Workplace exists.

## Part VI — Reflection, Evidence, permissions and portability

### Chapter 20 — Private Reflection

Reflection begins in the originating Workplace or Product context.

A Reflection Candidate may record:

- what was attempted;
- which Capability and Skill versions were used;
- what the user adopted, changed or rejected;
- what Evidence and Outcome resulted;
- what the user believes was learned;
- privacy and reuse permissions.

```text
Reflection Candidate
≠ Raw Conversation Archive
≠ Shared Platform Knowledge
≠ Canonical Principle
```

The user or authorized Workplace should be able to review, correct, keep private, share with a team, anonymize or decline a proposed Reflection.

### Chapter 21 — Capability Ledger projection

The Workplace may maintain a Capability Ledger projection containing authorized references to:

- invocation and practice;
- Session Receipts;
- Reflection Candidates;
- accepted Cases;
- simulation and assessment;
- certification;
- Production Authorization;
- version exposure and expiry.

The Ledger supports traceability and growth. It is not a universal ranking of people.

### Chapter 22 — Minimum necessary access

Assignments, reviews, mentoring and Capability Composition expose only the Data, Knowledge, Cases, Principles and customer context required for the authorized purpose.

Access expires or is revoked when the assignment or purpose ends.

### Chapter 23 — Cross-Workplace AI

AI access granted in one Workplace does not authorize learning from or exposing its private data in another Workplace.

Training, memory, retrieval, Reflection reuse and Capability evolution require explicit rights, purpose and governance.

```text
Available to One Execution
≠ Available for Model Training
≠ Available to Another Workplace
```

### Chapter 24 — Capability portability and exit

A Workplace may export its authorized business records, documents, Evidence, private Knowledge, Capability Profile, Ledger references and configuration subject to rights.

It does not gain ownership of:

- platform Capability definitions or implementations;
- shared routing models;
- MGSN Provider pools;
- another participant’s private Cases;
- protected assessment content;
- platform-wide Trust or benchmarking data.

```text
Capability Profile Portability
≠ Capability Ownership
≠ Authority Portability
```

Certification, Production Authorization and Professional Qualification retain issuer, scope, status and expiry.

## Part VII — Commercial and operating models

### Chapter 25 — Internal delivery

A Workplace may use its own team and Capabilities without projecting demand externally.

Capability Profile information may assist staffing and review, but the Workplace remains accountable for assignment and authority.

### Chapter 26 — MarkReg white-label and co-delivery

A Workplace may retain the customer relationship while MarkReg supplies some or all delivery under a defined Engagement.

MarkReg Outcomes and official Evidence may support Capability learning only through authorized Session Receipt, Reflection and Case pathways.

### Chapter 27 — Contribution network

Bounded tasks such as search review, data verification, visual judgment and content review may be assigned to certified contributors under scoped access and compensation.

Contribution Evidence may enter a Capability Ledger after review. Paid-task completion does not automatically prove broad professional competence.

### Chapter 28 — MGSN connection

Formal external professional services are accessed through a Workplace-scoped MGSN Connection. The connection is not the MGSN Network itself.

Provider supply Capability, user Capability Profile and Source Capability remain distinct.

## Part VIII — Governance and organizational learning

### Chapter 29 — Conflict and non-circumvention

Conflict checks, Relationship Provenance, communication controls and Engagement history protect participants without pretending that contractual prohibition alone creates loyalty.

Capability Reflection and learning must not expose another participant’s customer, confidential method or professional material without authority.

### Chapter 30 — Workplace health

A Workplace should understand:

- Capability coverage and Evidence quality;
- current capacity and active Needs;
- customer and Provider concentration;
- collaboration dependency;
- transaction pipeline;
- stale certifications or authorizations;
- repeated Correction or review patterns;
- Capability development priorities.

A health view should explain its sources and should not become a single opaque score.

### Chapter 31 — Organizational learning

Workplace Outcomes improve private operating memory first.

The governed path is:

```text
Outcome
→ Private Reflection Candidate
→ Workplace Review
→ Private or Team Case
→ Authorized De-identification
→ Shared Learning Candidate
→ Platform Evaluation
```

Only authorized, de-identified and reviewed candidates may contribute to shared Knowledge or Capability evolution.

```text
Workplace Learning
≠ Automatic Platform Learning
```

## Ten-year lock

Workplace is the unit through which MarkOrbit scales without erasing independent businesses or reducing people to platform-owned scores.

The platform grows by strengthening and connecting Workplaces, preserving private Reflection and customer sovereignty, and making Capability growth portable and evidence-backed without absorbing every relationship, Case or decision into one central operator.
