# Book 04 v2
# MarkOrbit Workplace and Collaboration Architecture

## Purpose

Workplace is the Organization-scoped boundary for business sovereignty, accountability, permission, Product operation and controlled external projection. It is not a generic dashboard, tenant label or owner of every platform capability it uses.

## Part I — Identity and sovereignty

### Chapter 01 — Person, Organization and Workplace

A Person holds identity, personal capability evidence and professional credentials. An Organization bears legal, commercial, professional or administrative accountability. A Workplace is the Organization-scoped operating boundary. Membership connects a Person to a Workplace with explicit role and authority.

### Chapter 02 — Personal and organizational Workplaces

An independent professional may operate a personal Workplace and also participate in one or more organizational Workplaces. Every action must declare the represented Workplace. Identity continuity does not authorize cross-Workplace data reuse.

### Chapter 03 — Business sovereignty

Workplace sovereignty covers customer relationships, private pricing, internal knowledge, commercial policy, business history, configuration and accountable decisions. It does not authorize bypassing Core semantics, Owning Services or professional restrictions.

## Part II — Resource and need model

### Chapter 04 — Resource Profile

A Workplace may possess customer relationships, audience, marketing channels, professional capability, trademark assets, staff capacity, review authority, local qualification, provider relationships, cases or knowledge.

### Chapter 05 — Need Profile

A Workplace may need customers, promotion, search, classification, local counsel, visual design, temporary capacity, expert review, trademark supply, transaction support or formal fulfillment.

### Chapter 06 — Asymmetry as normal

The platform must not assume every Workplace is a full-service firm. A useful network arises because participants hold different resources and gaps.

## Part III — Controlled projection

### Chapter 07 — Demand Projection

A Workplace may project a bounded need without exposing its full customer record. Projection specifies purpose, capability requirements, disclosure level, expiry and relationship constraints.

### Chapter 08 — Capability Projection

A Workplace or Person may project verified capability, jurisdiction, language, capacity, turnaround and participation preference without exposing all private performance data.

### Chapter 09 — Asset, Audience and Capacity Projection

Trademark assets, reseller authorization, marketing reach and available capacity may be projected under separate rights and visibility policies.

### Chapter 10 — Projection is not transfer

```text
platform visibility ≠ network visibility
projection ≠ ownership transfer
need publication ≠ customer transfer
provider access ≠ relationship ownership
```

## Part IV — Engagement architecture

### Chapter 11 — From Need to Engagement

Need becomes Opportunity when a viable route is identified. Engagement begins only after participants accept role, scope, disclosure, responsibility, commercial terms and termination conditions.

### Chapter 12 — Engagement roles

A single engagement may include:

- Demand Originator;
- Relationship Owner;
- Contracting Party;
- Payment Receiver;
- Solution Orchestrator;
- Capability Contributor;
- Professional Authority;
- Delivery Owner.

Roles are engagement-specific, not permanent Workplace types.

### Chapter 13 — Collaboration modes

Supported modes include referral, co-delivery, white-label delivery, bounded work-package contribution, expert review and managed network fulfillment.

### Chapter 14 — Customer relationship protection

The default Relationship Owner remains the originating party unless a recorded agreement changes it. Other participants receive only the information and communication rights necessary for their role.

## Part V — Product architecture

### Chapter 15 — Product Installation

A Product Installation records edition, entitlement, enabled capabilities, configuration, roles, data scope, connector policy, projection policy, audit reference and lifecycle.

### Chapter 16 — Dynamic Workplace experience

Lite, MarkReg and other Products may adapt the home experience according to resource and need profile. A marketing-oriented Workplace, capability-oriented Workplace and asset-oriented Workplace may see different priorities while sharing the same semantic foundation.

### Chapter 17 — Product Projection

Client portals, public Sites, provider instruction views, trademark listing pages and network requests are purpose-limited projections. They are not separate sovereignty domains unless an explicit Workplace exists.

## Part VI — Data, permissions and portability

### Chapter 18 — Minimum necessary access

Assignments expose only the data required to perform the authorized work. Access expires or is revoked when the assignment ends.

### Chapter 19 — Cross-Workplace AI

AI access granted in one Workplace does not authorize learning from or exposing its private data in another Workplace. Training, memory and retrieval require explicit policy and rights.

### Chapter 20 — Portability and exit

A Workplace may export its concrete business records, documents, evidence, private knowledge and configuration subject to rights. It does not gain ownership of platform routing models, shared capability code, MGSN provider pools or other participants’ private data.

## Part VII — Commercial and operating models

### Chapter 21 — Internal delivery

A Workplace may use its own team and capabilities without projecting demand externally.

### Chapter 22 — MarkReg white-label and co-delivery

A Workplace may retain the customer relationship while MarkReg supplies some or all delivery under a defined engagement.

### Chapter 23 — Contribution network

Bounded tasks such as search review, data verification, visual judgment and content review may be assigned to certified contributors under scoped access and compensation.

### Chapter 24 — MGSN connection

Formal external professional services are accessed through a Workplace-scoped MGSN connection. The connection is not the MGSN Network itself.

## Part VIII — Governance

### Chapter 25 — Conflict and non-circumvention

Conflict checks, relationship provenance, communication controls and engagement history protect participants without pretending that contractual prohibition alone creates loyalty.

### Chapter 26 — Workplace health

A Workplace should understand its capability coverage, capacity, active needs, customer concentration, collaboration dependency, transaction pipeline and evidence quality.

### Chapter 27 — Organizational learning

Workplace outcomes improve private operating memory first. Only authorized, de-identified and reviewed candidates may contribute to shared platform learning.

## Ten-year lock

Workplace is the unit through which MarkOrbit scales without erasing independent businesses. The platform grows by strengthening and connecting Workplaces, not by absorbing every relationship into one central operator.
