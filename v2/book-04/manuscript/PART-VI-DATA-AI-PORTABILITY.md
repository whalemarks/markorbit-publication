# Part VI — Data, AI, Permissions, Portability and Exit

## Chapter 73 — Minimum necessary access

Assignments, reviews and provider instructions should expose only the information required for the authorized purpose.

A visual reviewer may need:

- the mark image;
- intended goods;
- target audience;
- required deliverable.

The reviewer may not need:

- customer identity;
- payment history;
- unrelated trademarks;
- internal margin;
- full correspondence.

Minimum necessary access reduces harm without preventing useful work.

## Chapter 74 — Permission is contextual

Permission should be evaluated from several dimensions:

```text
Person
+ Membership
+ Workplace
+ Product Installation
+ record scope
+ action
+ purpose
+ time
+ Engagement or Assignment
```

A person authorized to review a task in one Workplace is not automatically authorized to retrieve the same customer's data elsewhere.

## Chapter 75 — Access lifecycle

Scoped access should have a lifecycle:

```text
requested
→ approved
→ active
→ restricted
→ expired
→ revoked
→ audited
```

Access may expire automatically when an Assignment or Engagement ends.

Downloaded or exported material may require additional controls because revoking platform access does not retract a local copy.

## Chapter 76 — Cross-Workplace AI boundaries

AI access in one Workplace does not authorize use of its private data in another.

The platform should separate:

- operational inference for the current task;
- private Workplace memory;
- shared model improvement;
- de-identified analytics;
- public knowledge indexing;
- human training material.

Each use requires an appropriate policy and rights basis.

## Chapter 77 — Workplace memory

A Workplace may maintain private operational memory such as:

- customer preferences;
- internal pricing logic;
- preferred providers;
- writing style;
- review decisions;
- previous exceptions;
- business strategy.

Private memory improves continuity but should not become an invisible source of formal fact.

The user should be able to inspect, correct and retire important memory.

## Chapter 78 — Shared learning requires promotion

A private outcome may contribute to shared learning only through a governed path.

```text
private observation
→ authorized candidate
→ de-identification and rights review
→ professional validation
→ shared knowledge or capability evidence
```

The platform should not assume that removing a customer name makes all reuse lawful or appropriate.

## Chapter 79 — Data classification

Workplace data should be classified by sensitivity and authority.

Possible classes include:

- public;
- customer confidential;
- internal commercial;
- professional privileged or restricted;
- personal data;
- payment data;
- credential data;
- licensed source data;
- platform trust data;
- simulation data.

Classification guides storage, retrieval, projection, retention and export.

## Chapter 80 — Data locality and residency

Some Workplaces may require data to remain in a particular region or provider environment.

Product Installation and connector policy should support:

- residency requirements;
- regional processing limits;
- approved subprocessors;
- encryption and key policy;
- backup location;
- cross-border transfer basis.

Workplace sovereignty does not guarantee that every desired residency configuration is technically available, but the actual arrangement must be transparent.

## Chapter 81 — Audit without surveillance excess

The platform needs evidence of significant actions:

- permission changes;
- projection approval;
- data export;
- task acceptance;
- professional review;
- formal mutation;
- provider instruction;
- settlement milestone.

Audit should not become indiscriminate employee surveillance.

The system should record what is necessary for accountability, security and quality, with retention proportional to purpose.

## Chapter 82 — Communication retention

Not every conversation needs permanent retention, but material instructions and decisions must be recoverable.

A useful model distinguishes:

- ephemeral collaboration;
- operational messages;
- formal customer instruction;
- professional decision;
- contractual commitment;
- evidence-bearing communication.

The system may summarize conversations into formal records while preserving a source link where lawful and necessary.

## Chapter 83 — Portability is part of sovereignty

A Workplace should be able to export or transfer its own concrete business records, subject to rights and technical feasibility.

Portable material may include:

- customers and contacts;
- Matters, Orders and Tasks;
- documents and evidence;
- private knowledge;
- relationship history;
- Product Installation configuration;
- owned or licensed content;
- accepted outcomes.

Portability should use documented formats and retain provenance.

## Chapter 84 — What is not automatically portable

Workplace sovereignty does not imply ownership of:

- MGSN provider pools;
- confidential procurement terms;
- platform routing models;
- other participants' private records;
- shared capability source code;
- platform trust calculations;
- restricted licensed data;
- model weights;
- aggregate anti-fraud intelligence.

The Workplace may receive authorized views or evidence without acquiring the underlying platform asset.

## Chapter 85 — Exit planning

A Workplace may leave the platform, retire a Product or end a collaboration.

Exit should address:

- pending customer work;
- active Engagements;
- external provider commitments;
- unpaid settlements;
- data export;
- public projection withdrawal;
- connector shutdown;
- retention obligations;
- credential and evidence continuity;
- future contact restrictions.

## Chapter 86 — Suspension is not deletion

A Workplace may be suspended for security, payment, professional or governance concerns.

Suspension may restrict new actions while preserving:

- customer access to necessary outcomes;
- evidence;
- dispute handling;
- legal retention;
- controlled export.

Immediate deletion can destroy accountability and harm customers.

## Chapter 87 — Identity and capability continuity after exit

A Person's legitimate capability evidence and professional credentials may outlive Membership in a particular Workplace.

However, the Person cannot take:

- customer records;
- private case material;
- internal reviews;
- employer-owned work products;
- confidential business history;

merely because the Person participated in producing them.

The platform needs clear evidence ownership and portability rules.

## Chapter 88 — Security is a shared responsibility

The platform secures infrastructure and product boundaries. The Workplace secures membership, internal process, customer consent and endpoint behavior. Users secure credentials and follow policy.

Security failures often arise at these boundaries rather than inside one component.

The architecture should make responsibility visible instead of assuming the platform alone can eliminate all risk.
