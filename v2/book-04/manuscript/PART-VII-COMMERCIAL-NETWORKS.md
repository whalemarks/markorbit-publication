# Part VII — Commercial Models, Contribution and MGSN

## Chapter 89 — A Workplace may operate several commercial models

A Workplace may simultaneously:

- serve its own customers;
- use MarkReg white-label delivery;
- contribute bounded tasks;
- sell or resell trademarks;
- refer opportunities;
- participate in MGSN;
- provide expert review;
- offer training or assessment.

The platform should not force the Organization into one permanent business category.

## Chapter 90 — Internal delivery

Internal delivery keeps the work within the originating Workplace.

The Workplace may still use shared:

- data;
- knowledge;
- Brain;
- Capability workflows;
- templates;
- quality controls;
- audit;
- official connectors.

Internal delivery is appropriate when capability, capacity, authority and commercial preference align.

## Chapter 91 — MarkReg direct service

In direct service, the MarkReg operating Workplace becomes the primary contracting and delivery party.

It may manage:

- customer intake;
- quotation;
- professional service routing;
- lifecycle communication;
- payment;
- final delivery.

This model should remain isolated from partner Workplace records and policies.

## Chapter 92 — MarkReg white-label service

In white-label service, another Workplace normally remains the Relationship Owner and may remain the Contracting Party.

MarkReg provides a defined production or delivery package.

The Engagement must define:

- service scope;
- customer-facing identity;
- communication route;
- pricing confidentiality;
- professional disclosures;
- approval responsibilities;
- complaint handling;
- data retention;
- renewal and repeat-business rules.

## Chapter 93 — MarkReg co-delivery

Co-delivery divides responsibility visibly or operationally.

A partner Workplace may handle:

- customer development;
- local communication;
- collection of business information;
- final relationship management.

MarkReg may handle:

- international strategy workflow;
- provider coordination;
- production quality;
- lifecycle tracking;
- structured delivery.

Neither side should assume the other's responsibilities without explicit acceptance.

## Chapter 94 — Certified production contribution

MarkReg and other Products may offer bounded Work Packages to certified and authorized contributors.

A task offer should state:

- Capability requirement;
- production level;
- risk tier;
- deadline;
- compensation;
- permitted tools;
- review route;
- customer-contact restriction;
- revision expectation;
- evidence generated.

A contributor does not become the Delivery Owner merely by completing a task.

## Chapter 95 — Visual and brand contribution

AI-assisted trademark beautification may involve several contributors:

- visual designer;
- trademark professional;
- brand commercialization adviser;
- final reviewer.

Each opinion addresses a different dimension:

```text
visual coherence
trademark continuity and registration risk
commercial suitability
```

The platform should preserve disagreement rather than average unlike judgments into one unexplained score.

## Chapter 96 — Search and data contribution

Search and data work can be decomposed into:

- query interpretation;
- search-strategy review;
- result verification;
- relevance screening;
- entity reconciliation;
- risk interpretation;
- report quality review.

Lower-risk components may be completed by certified contributors. Formal legal opinion remains subject to the appropriate Professional Authority.

## Chapter 97 — Knowledge contribution

A contributor may:

- verify an official update;
- distill a professional article;
- compare guidance versions;
- convert correspondence into a candidate practice note;
- prepare a de-identified simulation;
- review translation;
- identify outdated content.

Knowledge contribution requires source, permission, attribution and review.

## Chapter 98 — Simulation and assessment contribution

Certified assessors may act as:

- simulated customers;
- reviewers;
- interviewers;
- scenario controllers;
- appeal reviewers;
- rubric calibrators.

Assessment work should be compensated and itself subject to quality evidence.

## Chapter 99 — Contribution Network eligibility

Eligibility may consider:

- Capability certification;
- production authorization;
- jurisdiction;
- language;
- quality history;
- capacity;
- conflict status;
- current agreement;
- data-security readiness;
- assessor or reviewer status.

Ranking should not rely only on completed task count, or new qualified contributors will never progress.

## Chapter 100 — Opportunity access for new contributors

The network should provide a controlled advancement route:

```text
simulation
→ shadow task
→ supervised task
→ low-risk independent task
→ standard task
→ complex task
→ review authority
```

A portion of suitable tasks may be reserved for supervised progression.

## Chapter 101 — Compensation models

Compensation may include:

- fixed task fee;
- review fee;
- urgent-task premium;
- accepted-case fee;
- referral commission;
- transaction commission;
- professional service fee;
- licensed knowledge revenue;
- training and assessment fee.

The basis should be explicit before acceptance.

## Chapter 102 — Contribution evidence

A Contribution record should preserve:

- contributor;
- represented Workplace;
- Capability;
- assignment;
- input scope;
- output;
- AI assistance mode;
- review;
- revisions;
- acceptance;
- later outcome;
- compensation reference.

Payment does not make a contribution correct, and rejection does not erase the fact that it occurred.

## Chapter 103 — MGSN Connection

An MGSN Connection is installed within a Workplace.

It holds:

- participation configuration;
- demand and supply projection policy;
- disclosure settings;
- relationship provenance view;
- preferred routes;
- returned results;
- audit references.

It does not own the MGSN provider pool or platform procurement logic.

## Chapter 104 — MGSN Network

MGSN is the platform-owned and platform-governed managed global professional service network.

It governs:

- provider recruitment;
- qualification;
- service portfolios;
- procurement terms;
- routing;
- acceptance;
- fulfillment monitoring;
- funds checkpoints;
- correction and replacement;
- provider performance;
- typed Return.

## Chapter 105 — External self-managed routes

A Workplace may choose its own external provider outside MGSN.

The Workplace remains responsible for:

- negotiation;
- verification;
- instruction;
- payment;
- follow-up;
- replacement;
- evidence entry.

The platform may record the route without granting MGSN guarantees.

## Chapter 106 — No undisclosed rebrokering

A provider or contributor must not silently pass work to another party when identity, qualification, confidentiality or responsibility matter.

Where further professionals are required, the Engagement should govern:

- identity;
- role;
- authority;
- data access;
- fees;
- Return path.

## Chapter 107 — Commerce and service networks intersect

A trademark transaction may require:

- asset listing;
- buyer matching;
- due diligence contribution;
- transaction negotiation;
- transfer documentation;
- MGSN local recordal;
- settlement;
- final evidence.

The Workplace model allows these stages to involve different participants without losing role clarity.

## Chapter 108 — Network participation is optional and revocable

A Workplace may choose which resources to project and which networks to join.

Participation may be suspended or withdrawn, subject to completing active commitments and preserving required records.

MarkOrbit should create strong reasons to participate, not depend on forced exclusivity.
