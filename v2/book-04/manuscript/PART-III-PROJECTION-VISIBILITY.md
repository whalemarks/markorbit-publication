# Part III — Controlled Projection and Visibility

## Chapter 27 — Projection is a governed disclosure

A Projection is a purpose-limited representation of Workplace data, capability, demand, asset or status beyond its originating private context.

A Projection should define:

- source Workplace;
- purpose;
- audience;
- visible fields;
- legal and business basis;
- expiry;
- update policy;
- interaction rights;
- withdrawal conditions;
- provenance.

Projection is not replication without limits.

## Chapter 28 — Demand Projection

Demand Projection allows a Workplace to seek help without revealing its full customer record.

A demand may describe:

- required outcome;
- Capability IDs;
- jurisdiction;
- language;
- risk level;
- deadline;
- acceptable collaboration modes;
- commercial range;
- disclosure stage;
- relationship constraints.

The first projection may be anonymous or abstract. More information is disclosed only after eligibility and Engagement progress.

## Chapter 29 — Capability Projection

Capability Projection allows a Person or Workplace to state what it can offer.

It may include:

- verified Capability Unit;
- level;
- production authorization;
- professional qualification;
- jurisdiction;
- language;
- typical turnaround;
- capacity;
- preferred task type;
- visibility;
- validity period.

Private performance details and failed assessments need not be exposed to all participants.

## Chapter 30 — Asset Projection

Asset Projection makes a trademark, template, case, content asset or service package visible for an authorized purpose.

For a trademark listing, the projection may expose:

- mark representation;
- jurisdiction;
- class;
- goods or services summary;
- current status;
- asking-price policy;
- resale authorization status;
- commission policy;
- concept materials;
- listing freshness.

The full ownership evidence and confidential minimum price may remain private until due diligence.

## Chapter 31 — Audience Projection

Audience Projection describes market reach without exposing individual contacts.

Examples include:

- Chinese consumer-goods exporters;
- local startup founders;
- a social account audience interested in brand naming;
- an agency's client industries;
- a regional partner network.

Audience Projection must remain aggregate, consent-aware and purpose-limited.

## Chapter 32 — Capacity Projection

Capacity Projection communicates temporary availability.

It may state:

- number of task slots;
- normal or urgent availability;
- applicable capability;
- review capacity;
- date range;
- service window;
- participation conditions.

Because capacity changes quickly, stale projections should expire automatically.

## Chapter 33 — Visibility levels

A common visibility model should include at least:

```text
Private
Designated Participants
Workplace Network
Managed Platform Network
Authenticated Platform Users
Public
```

Visibility does not itself grant mutation, download, contact or reuse rights.

## Chapter 34 — Platform visibility is not participant visibility

The platform may process private information for matching, fraud prevention or governance without displaying it to other participants.

```text
platform processing permission
≠ disclosure permission

matching eligibility
≠ access to source record
```

The distinction is critical for customer protection and confidential pricing.

## Chapter 35 — Progressive disclosure

Collaboration should disclose information in stages.

```text
Stage 1 — abstract need or offer
Stage 2 — eligibility-confirmed summary
Stage 3 — Engagement negotiation context
Stage 4 — assignment-specific data
Stage 5 — formal instruction and evidence
```

Each stage should reveal only what the next decision requires.

## Chapter 36 — Projection versioning

A Projection represents a source at a point in time.

It should record:

- source version;
- projection version;
- generated time;
- expiry;
- transformation;
- approval;
- withdrawal;
- later supersession.

A withdrawn listing may remain in audit history while disappearing from discovery.

## Chapter 37 — Projection is not transfer

The following locks must remain explicit:

```text
projection ≠ ownership transfer
publication ≠ customer transfer
provider access ≠ relationship ownership
listing ≠ authority to sell
match ≠ engagement
technical delivery ≠ unrestricted reuse
```

## Chapter 38 — Public projection carries higher duties

Public pages may be indexed, copied, misunderstood or remain cached.

Public projection therefore requires stronger controls for:

- verified facts;
- rights to images and logos;
- privacy;
- concept disclaimers;
- stale-status warnings;
- correction and withdrawal;
- public contact routing.

## Chapter 39 — AI-generated projection content

AI may generate summaries, recommendations, images or sales material for a Projection.

The system must distinguish:

```text
verified source fact
AI interpretation
concept illustration
professional opinion
customer-supplied claim
```

An AI-generated store, product or packaging concept must never be presented as real use evidence or an existing business fact.

## Chapter 40 — Projection policy belongs to the Workplace

Platform defaults may protect users, but each Workplace needs explicit policy for:

- which resource classes may be projected;
- who may approve projection;
- permitted audiences;
- retention;
- public-brand rules;
- customer-consent requirements;
- AI processing;
- withdrawal.

Product-specific policy may narrow Workplace policy but should not silently expand it.
