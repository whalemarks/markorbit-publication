# Part I — Identity, Organization and Sovereignty

## Chapter 01 — Person is not Workplace

A Person is the durable identity of a human participant.

The Person may hold:

- verified identity;
- languages;
- professional credentials;
- personal capability evidence;
- certifications;
- assessor or mentor status;
- conflict declarations;
- participation history.

A Person does not automatically own the customers, documents or commercial records of every Workplace in which that person participates.

Identity continuity supports accountability. It does not erase organizational boundaries.

## Chapter 02 — Organization is the accountable participant

An Organization is the legal, commercial, professional or administrative participant that can bear responsibility.

Examples include:

- a trademark agency;
- a law firm;
- an independent practice;
- an enterprise legal department;
- a brand owner;
- a MarkReg operating entity;
- an MGSN provider;
- the platform operator.

The Organization may sign contracts, receive revenue, employ or engage people, hold professional responsibility and operate one or more Workplaces.

## Chapter 03 — Workplace is the operating boundary

A Workplace is the Organization-scoped boundary for:

- business sovereignty;
- customer and relationship context;
- commercial accountability;
- membership and permission;
- Product Installations;
- private knowledge and documents;
- configuration;
- authorized projection;
- audit history.

```text
Organization
→ Workplace
→ Product Installation
→ enabled operating capability
```

Workplace is not merely a tenant identifier. It is the business context in which an action becomes meaningful.

## Chapter 04 — One Organization may need several Workplaces

An Organization may operate more than one Workplace when separation is legitimate.

Examples include:

- separate brands or business units;
- self-operated MarkReg business isolated from partner services;
- regional operations with distinct customer and data policies;
- training and simulation environments separated from production;
- regulated professional entities separated from general commercial activity.

Multiple Workplaces should not be created only to evade governance, fragment accountability or duplicate customer records without purpose.

## Chapter 05 — Personal Workplace

An independent professional may operate through a personal Workplace where law and commercial policy allow.

The personal Workplace can hold:

- private customers;
- service offers;
- personal content and brand assets;
- capability development;
- task participation;
- transaction opportunities;
- earnings and evidence.

A personal Workplace is still an accountable operating context. It is not the same as the Person identity itself.

## Chapter 06 — Membership connects Person and Workplace

Membership defines how a Person participates in a Workplace.

It should state:

- role;
- status;
- permission set;
- represented capacity;
- start and end dates;
- supervision requirement;
- data-access scope;
- external communication authority;
- financial authority;
- conflict and confidentiality obligations.

The same Person may hold different roles in different Workplaces.

## Chapter 07 — Every action declares representation

When a Person performs an action, the system must know:

```text
who acted
which Workplace was represented
which role was active
which permission was used
which capability was invoked
which record or projection was affected
```

A user logged into one account may switch between Workplaces, but the represented context must remain explicit.

## Chapter 08 — Business sovereignty

Business sovereignty includes the legitimate control of:

- customer relationships;
- private pricing and margin;
- commercial policy;
- internal workflow preference;
- private knowledge;
- business memory;
- customer communication;
- service positioning;
- authorized collaboration choices.

Business sovereignty does not imply unrestricted power.

A Workplace cannot redefine Core semantics, mutate records outside the applicable Owning Service, ignore professional restrictions or use data without rights.

## Chapter 09 — Four roles often confused as ownership

A customer engagement may involve different holders of authority:

```text
Customer Data Sovereignty
Relationship Owner
Contracting Party
Delivery Owner
```

These roles may belong to one party, but they should not be assumed to do so.

A white-label engagement may preserve the partner agency as Relationship Owner and Contracting Party while MarkReg acts as Delivery Owner.

## Chapter 10 — Professional Authority is separate

Professional Authority identifies the person or Organization that is qualified and accountable for a restricted professional act.

It may include:

- a locally admitted lawyer;
- an authorized trademark agent;
- a qualified signatory;
- an accredited assessor;
- an expert reviewer.

A Workplace may orchestrate a service without holding every required professional qualification internally.

## Chapter 11 — Platform operation is not business ownership

The platform may host data, execute workflows, route tasks, calculate settlement and enforce policy.

Those functions do not automatically grant the platform:

- customer relationship ownership;
- freedom to reuse private knowledge;
- authority to contact customers outside the engagement;
- power to make professional decisions;
- ownership of participant-created business records.

Physical custody and operational control must remain distinct from business sovereignty.

## Chapter 12 — Sovereignty requires accountability

A Workplace that claims sovereignty must also accept responsibility for:

- membership management;
- customer permissions;
- commercial decisions;
- internal approvals;
- lawful use of data;
- correct projection;
- engagement commitments;
- retention and exit decisions.

Sovereignty without accountability becomes concealment. Accountability without sovereignty becomes dependency.
