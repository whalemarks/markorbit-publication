## Structural verdict

Batch 05 establishes the Product, access, AI and exit boundaries of the Workplace. It explains how external experiences remain governed projections, how data access follows accountable responsibility, how AI is prevented from dissolving Workplace sovereignty and how meaningful portability coexists with third-party and platform rights.

## Accepted distinctions

```text
External portal ≠ new Workplace
Product Projection ≠ duplicated formal state
View access ≠ write authority
Upload ≠ validation
Assignment ≠ broad project membership
AI inference ≠ memory
AI memory ≠ shared learning
Visible data ≠ freely exportable data
Person exit ≠ Workplace exit
Portability ≠ ownership of platform assets
```

- branding without authority distortion;

## AI boundaries covered

- inference, retrieval, memory and learning separation;
- Workplace-private memory;
- Person versus Organization memory;

- de-identification and re-identification risk;

## Portability boundaries covered

- structured export of concrete business records;
- rights and restriction labeling;
- active-obligation handover;
- member departure versus Workplace exit;
- customer-directed transfer;
- lawful retention without unrelated reuse;
- manifest, checksums and exclusion report;
- non-portable Core, Product, routing and network assets;
- rejection of hostage economics.

## Authority locks preserved

- no active Canon or frozen Book mutation;
- no Core semantic activation;
- external experience does not create a new sovereignty domain;
- Product Projection does not own source state;
- external write-back does not bypass the Owning Service;
- Assignment-scoped access does not create relationship ownership;
- AI access does not imply memory, training or cross-Workplace reuse;
- platform certification does not create professional authority;
- portability does not transfer other participants' private data, MGSN pools, shared Capability code or platform routing models;
- customer choice is not obstructed;
- no payment custody or platform-principal authority is introduced.
