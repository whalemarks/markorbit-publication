# Chapter 12 — Projection Is Not Transfer

The most dangerous misunderstanding in a collaboration platform is to treat visibility as ownership. Once a need, capability, asset or audience becomes visible outside its source Workplace, participants may assume that the underlying relationship, authority or commercial right has also moved. MarkOrbit must reject that assumption.

Projection is a controlled representation. Transfer is a change in rights, responsibility or control. They are different events.

## 1. The boundary

The core rule is:

```text
platform visibility ≠ network visibility
network visibility ≠ recipient ownership
Projection ≠ transfer
access ≠ authority
participation ≠ relationship ownership
```

A Projection may enable another participant to evaluate, quote, recommend, review or perform a bounded task. None of those actions silently changes the source of business sovereignty.

## 2. What may be transferred

Some collaborations do require transfer, delegation or appointment. These changes must be explicit and typed.

Examples include:

- assigning a Work Package;
- appointing a local professional representative;
- transferring ownership of a trademark;
- licensing use of a brand asset;
- transferring a customer relationship by agreement;
- delegating operational authority;
- assigning payment collection responsibility;
- granting marketing rights;
- transferring a Matter between service providers.

Each event has different legal, commercial and evidentiary requirements. A generic “share” or “accept” action cannot represent all of them.

## 3. Customer relationship transfer

Customer relationships are especially vulnerable to silent appropriation.

A provider may communicate directly with a customer because professional rules, local filing practice or operational efficiency require it. Direct communication does not itself transfer the relationship. A provider may receive customer identity for a conflict check. That does not create marketing rights. A Product may store the customer's record. That does not make the Product or platform the Relationship Owner.

A genuine relationship transfer should identify:

- transferring party;
- receiving party;
- customer knowledge or consent where required;
- scope of transferred relationship;
- retained rights;
- communication rights;
- future service rights;
- commercial consideration;
- effective date;
- termination or reversal conditions.

Without such evidence, the originating Relationship Owner remains unchanged.

## 4. Professional appointment

Professional appointment is another transfer-like event that must not be inferred from discovery.

A local attorney may be:

- visible as a candidate;
- eligible;
- recommended;
- approved internally;
- asked to confirm availability;
- accepted as a provider;
- formally appointed;
- authorized to act;
- actively executing.

These states are not interchangeable. Formal appointment may require a Power of Attorney, engagement terms, conflict clearance, fee approval or official filing record.

Projection supports the early stages. It does not complete the appointment.

## 5. Asset transfer

A trademark Asset Projection may show that an asset is available for discussion. It does not prove ownership, resale authorization or completed transfer.

The chain must remain explicit:

```text
Asset Projection
→ Inquiry
→ authority verification
→ due diligence
→ commercial agreement
→ transfer documents
→ official recordal where applicable
→ accepted Outcome
```

A marketplace-style “sold” label cannot replace this chain.

## 6. Data and knowledge transfer

Providing access to data does not necessarily transfer title, licence or reuse rights. The recipient may receive a purpose-limited right to process specific data. The source Workplace may retain sovereignty over private knowledge and internal history. Shared evidence may remain subject to customer, professional or legal restrictions.

Similarly, a Contributor's work product may be delivered to a Workplace under defined commercial terms, but the Contributor's general professional capability and unrelated methods do not become Workplace property.

## 7. Revocation does not erase history

A Projection may be revoked or expire. That should end future authorized access, but it does not erase the fact that the Projection existed or that recipients acted under it.

The system should preserve an audit record containing:

- what was projected;
- to whom;
- for what purpose;
- under which policy;
- what action followed;
- when access ended.

Historical accountability and current access are different concerns.

## 8. Derived records and downstream use

Recipients may create notes, quotations, reviews or Contributions based on a Projection. These derived records need provenance and policy.

A quotation may remain part of the originating Workplace's route analysis after the Projection expires. A provider may retain records required by professional law. A contributor may retain evidence of completed work but not the customer's full confidential materials. The correct treatment depends on the object's purpose and governing rights.

The platform must therefore avoid both extremes: uncontrolled downstream copying and unrealistic claims that all traces can always be deleted.

## 9. Product design implications

The interface should use distinct actions and language:

- Project;
- invite to evaluate;
- request quotation;
- recommend;
- approve;
- appoint;
- assign;
- delegate;
- license;
- transfer;
- revoke.

These verbs should trigger different checks and evidence requirements. A user should never complete a legal or commercial transfer merely by using an ambiguous collaboration control.

## 10. Failure modes

The boundary fails when:

- a provider treats customer access as prospecting permission;
- the platform reuses projected demand for its own sales;
- a listing is treated as proof of resale authority;
- a match is treated as appointment;
- a Product copies private data into another Workplace;
- an expired Projection remains accessible;
- an engagement ends but future marketing rights are assumed;
- a platform fee is interpreted as ownership of the transaction.

These are not minor privacy defects. They undermine the business reason independent Workplaces would join the platform.

## 11. The operating principle

MarkOrbit must make collaboration possible without turning every act of coordination into appropriation.

> Projection creates a governed window into a resource or need. Transfer occurs only through an explicit, authorized and evidenced change of rights or responsibility.
