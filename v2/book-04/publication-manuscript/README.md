## Purpose

Book 04 explains how MarkOrbit can connect independent professionals, agencies, organizations, Products and service networks without absorbing their customers, pricing, knowledge or decision authority into one central operator.

Its central object is the Workplace: the Organization-scoped boundary for business sovereignty, accountable action, Product operation, private context and controlled collaboration.

## Editorial standard

Each chapter must explain:

1. the operational problem;
2. why common SaaS or marketplace models are insufficient;
3. the MarkOrbit distinction;
4. the relevant objects and boundaries;
5. normal and exceptional operating flows;
6. consequences for Products, permissions, data and commercial responsibility;
7. failure modes and unresolved governance questions.

## Planned structure

The first reconstruction is expected to contain approximately 26–32 substantial chapters across:

- identity and sovereignty;
- resources and needs;
- controlled projection;
- engagement and collaboration;
- Product Installation;
- permissions, AI and portability;
- commercial operating modes;
- governance, health and organizational learning.

Chapter count is not a completion metric. The test is whether the book can guide Product design, implementation, partnership policy and dispute analysis without depending on hidden assumptions.
