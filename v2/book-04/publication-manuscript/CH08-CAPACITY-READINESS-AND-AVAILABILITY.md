# Chapter 08 — Capacity, Readiness and the Difference Between Having and Being Available

A Workplace may appear capable on paper and still be unable to accept work. It may employ qualified people who are already committed, possess a workflow that has not been validated for the current jurisdiction, or maintain a provider relationship that is temporarily suspended. Professional routing fails when the system treats historical possession as present availability.

MarkOrbit must therefore distinguish Capability, Capacity, Readiness and Availability.

## 1. Four different questions

```text
Capability: can this outcome be produced at the required standard?
Capacity: is there sufficient time and operational bandwidth?
Readiness: are prerequisites, evidence, permissions and inputs in place?
Availability: is the resource willing and eligible to participate now?
```

A provider can be capable but unavailable. A member can have capacity but lack Production Authorization. A Workplace can be ready except for a missing POA. A Product can support a workflow while the current data is not ready for execution.

## 2. Capacity is time-bound

Capacity should never be represented as a permanent attribute.

It may depend on:

- current assignments;
- deadline distribution;
- reviewer availability;
- jurisdiction operating hours;
- holidays and office closures;
- language support;
- payment or account status;
- document readiness;
- external-provider response time;
- risk tolerance.

Useful capacity expressions may include bands rather than false precision:

```text
available now
available with delay
limited
reserved
fully committed
unavailable
unknown
```

The system should record the observation time and expiry.

## 3. Individual and organizational capacity

An individual may have spare hours while the Workplace lacks review capacity. A Workplace may have many staff but only one person authorized to approve a high-risk outcome. Organizational capacity is therefore constrained by the narrowest required dependency.

For a filing route, capacity may require:

- intake preparation;
- classification review;
- applicant verification;
- local professional acceptance;
- funds;
- submission access;
- post-filing validation.

The route is not available merely because one step has capacity.

## 4. Readiness dimensions

Workplace readiness may be evaluated across multiple dimensions:

- identity readiness;
- customer instruction readiness;
- evidence readiness;
- document readiness;
- professional-authority readiness;
- commercial readiness;
- payment readiness;
- data readiness;
- workflow readiness;
- provider readiness;
- delivery readiness.

A single overall status should be explainable through these dimensions. “Not ready” is useful only when the blocking condition is visible.

## 5. Readiness is purpose-specific

A trademark may be ready for publication content but not ready for resale. A matter may be ready for customer review but not for filing. A provider may be ready for quotation but not yet appointed. A contributor may be ready for supervised work but not independent production.

Readiness must therefore be evaluated against a proposed action, not assigned globally to an object.

## 6. Availability includes willingness

Eligibility and spare capacity do not create an obligation to accept work.

A participant may decline because of:

- conflict;
- pricing;
- workload;
- relationship concerns;
- strategic preference;
- jurisdiction risk;
- insufficient information;
- unacceptable delivery mode;
- sanctions or payment constraints.

The system should separate recommendation from acceptance and acceptance from appointment.

## 7. Reservations and commitments

Once a resource is provisionally selected, the platform may need a reservation state. Reservation should have:

- purpose;
- expected duration;
- capacity amount or band;
- expiry;
- responsible party;
- release condition.

A reservation is not an Assignment and does not authorize data access or production. It prevents the same scarce capacity from being promised repeatedly while negotiation continues.

Commitment begins only after the relevant Engagement, Order or Assignment is accepted.

## 8. Capacity evidence

Self-declared availability can be useful, but higher-risk routing should consider evidence such as:

- recent completion time;
- missed deadlines;
- active workload;
- acceptance-to-completion ratios;
- reviewer bottlenecks;
- jurisdiction-specific performance;
- current operating status.

These signals should be contextual, not converted into a universal public score. A participant may perform well in one capability and poorly in another.

## 9. Readiness gates

For controlled work, the system should use explicit gates:

```text
captured
→ prerequisites checked
→ authority confirmed
→ resources reserved
→ participant accepted
→ data disclosed
→ execution authorized
```

Each gate should have a responsible actor and evidence. Skipping gates should require an explicit exception rather than disappearing into informal communication.

## 10. Failure and recovery

Capacity and readiness can change after work begins. A provider may become unavailable, a document may be rejected, or a reviewer may identify a missing fact.

Recovery requires:

- preserving completed evidence;
- identifying the failed dependency;
- revoking unnecessary access;
- releasing or reallocating reservations;
- selecting a substitute route;
- communicating deadline impact;
- avoiding duplicate formal action.

The system must support replacement without rewriting history.

## 11. Product implications

A Workplace Product should not merely display a list of people and providers. It should help answer:

```text
Who is capable?
Who is authorized?
Who has current capacity?
What is not ready?
What must happen before disclosure?
What can be safely promised?
What is the recovery route?
```

This distinction prevents a common platform failure: selling an outcome because a profile exists, then discovering during production that no eligible and available route exists.

Having a resource is a statement about the Workplace. Being ready and available is a statement about a specific purpose at a specific time.
