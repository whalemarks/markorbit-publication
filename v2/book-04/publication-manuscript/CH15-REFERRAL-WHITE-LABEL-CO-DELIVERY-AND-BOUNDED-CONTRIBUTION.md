# Chapter 15 — Referral, White-label, Co-delivery and Bounded Contribution

Collaboration is not one generic relationship. The same Need can be solved through materially different commercial and operating modes, each with different rules for customer contact, pricing, authority, disclosure, accountability and revenue distribution.

A platform that offers only “assign provider” will eventually misrepresent the real arrangement. MarkOrbit therefore treats collaboration mode as an explicit part of the Engagement.

## 1. Referral

A Referral transfers or introduces the commercial opportunity to another participant, while the originating party may retain a defined referral interest.

A valid Referral should clarify:

- whether the customer consents to the introduction;
- whether the receiving party contracts directly with the customer;
- whether the originating party retains any relationship rights;
- referral fee or other consideration;
- permitted follow-up communication;
- whether future unrelated work is included;
- what happens if the customer does not proceed.

Referral is not automatically customer transfer. The scope of the introduction must be recorded.

## 2. White-label delivery

In white-label delivery, the originating Workplace normally retains the customer-facing relationship while another participant performs some or all delivery behind the originating brand or interface.

A white-label Engagement should identify:

- the Relationship Owner;
- the customer-facing contracting structure;
- the actual Professional Authority where disclosure is legally or professionally required;
- communication protocol;
- branding and document rules;
- pricing and margin boundaries;
- service-level commitments;
- escalation rights;
- customer complaint and recovery handling;
- whether the delivery participant may contact the customer directly.

White-label delivery must not become deceptive concealment. A platform may preserve the commercial front while still disclosing the legally required professional or filing representative.

## 3. Co-delivery

Co-delivery applies when multiple Workplaces jointly produce an Outcome and each retains an accountable scope.

Examples include:

- an originating agency manages the customer while MarkReg prepares and coordinates the matter;
- a local attorney handles formal filing while a specialist prepares evidence and arguments;
- a brand strategist develops positioning while a trademark professional validates legal constraints;
- one Workplace manages transaction due diligence while another handles official assignment recordal.

Co-delivery requires a work breakdown, not a vague statement that parties will cooperate.

The Engagement should identify:

- overall Delivery Owner;
- work-package owners;
- review points;
- handoff conditions;
- shared and restricted data;
- customer communication rights;
- dependency and delay rules;
- consolidated acceptance process.

## 4. Bounded Contribution

Bounded Contribution allows a certified or otherwise eligible Person or Workplace to perform a discrete task without becoming the Provider Organization or Delivery Owner.

Typical tasks include:

- search-result review;
- document verification;
- goods and services normalization;
- translation review;
- evidence assessment;
- visual similarity judgment;
- structured data correction;
- content or design review.

The Assignment should specify:

- exact input;
- expected output;
- applicable Capability;
- evidence requirements;
- deadline;
- permitted tools;
- confidentiality and reuse limits;
- review and acceptance criteria;
- compensation.

```text
Contribution
≠ Provider appointment
≠ professional representation
≠ ownership of the customer relationship
```

## 5. Expert Review

Expert Review is distinct from ordinary contribution because its purpose is to validate, challenge or approve another output.

The reviewer should be independent where the risk requires it. The Product must prevent a contributor from silently approving their own work unless a documented exception applies.

Review should record:

- reviewed object and version;
- review standard;
- findings;
- material exceptions;
- approval, conditional approval or rejection;
- reviewer qualification and authority;
- required remediation.

## 6. Managed network fulfillment

MGSN fulfillment is used when formal external professional delivery is required through a Provider Organization.

It includes a controlled sequence from eligibility and recommendation through approval, provider acceptance, appointment, instruction, Return, validation and settlement.

It is not equivalent to referral or bounded contribution. The Provider may hold formal professional responsibility and external authority that contributors do not.

## 7. Internal delivery

A Workplace may solve the Need with its own Members and installed Products.

Internal delivery still requires authority and evidence. “Internal” does not eliminate Production Authorization, review, segregation of duties or formal professional qualification.

Internal delivery should remain visible as a route so the platform does not create unnecessary external transactions merely to monetize coordination.

## 8. Choosing the correct mode

Selection depends on questions such as:

- who should own the customer relationship;
- who contracts with whom;
- whether formal professional authority is required;
- who is accountable for the final Outcome;
- whether the task is divisible;
- whether customer-facing branding matters;
- what data must be disclosed;
- whether direct customer communication is necessary;
- how price and responsibility should be distributed.

A low-risk data-verification task may suit Bounded Contribution. A foreign legal filing may require MGSN fulfillment. A complete service supplied under the originating agency's brand may suit white-label delivery.

## 9. Hybrid engagements

Real work may combine modes.

A MarkReg matter could include:

- white-label customer-facing delivery;
- co-delivery between the originating Workplace and MarkReg;
- bounded contributions for search or evidence review;
- expert review for quality control;
- MGSN appointment for local filing.

The system should allow a controlled composition of modes while preserving each role and authority boundary.

## 10. Mode changes are material changes

A white-label engagement should not silently turn into a referral. A bounded contribution should not expand into direct customer representation. A provider should not subcontract formal work without the required approval.

Changing collaboration mode may alter:

- customer consent;
- pricing;
- liability;
- disclosure;
- professional authority;
- relationship rights;
- settlement.

It therefore requires recorded amendment and approval.

## 11. Failure modes

Common failures include:

- calling all outsourcing “white-label”;
- hiding the professional provider where disclosure is required;
- allowing contributors to contact customers for unrelated solicitation;
- treating a referral fee as permanent ownership of the customer;
- unclear co-delivery responsibility;
- unpaid real production disguised as training;
- lowest-price routing that ignores qualification and recovery capacity;
- silent subcontracting.

## 12. The operating principle

A collaboration mode is a compact statement of how value, authority, communication and accountability are arranged.

> MarkOrbit does not merely connect supply and demand. It makes the operating relationship explicit enough that independent businesses can cooperate without pretending they have become one organization.
