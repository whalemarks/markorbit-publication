# Book 04 Publication Reconstruction Charter

## 1. Reconstruction objective

The existing v2 master correctly identifies Workplace as the business-sovereignty and Product-operation boundary, but it remains an architectural outline. This reconstruction turns that outline into a book capable of supporting product management, implementation, partnership design and governance decisions.

The manuscript must answer a difficult question:

> How can one platform coordinate work across independent businesses without treating independence as a temporary inconvenience to be removed?

## 2. Central thesis

A durable professional platform cannot rely on the ordinary tenant model alone.

A tenant normally answers where data and users are partitioned. A Workplace must additionally answer:

- which Organization is represented;
- who may act for it;
- which customer relationships belong to it;
- which commercial policies remain private;
- which Products are installed;
- which records are authoritative elsewhere;
- what may be projected externally;
- which external actors may see or perform what;
- how rights end when collaboration ends.

## 3. Required distinctions

The manuscript must preserve:

```text
Person ≠ Organization ≠ Workplace ≠ Membership
business sovereignty ≠ record ownership ≠ physical custody
access ≠ authority
visibility ≠ projection
projection ≠ transfer
participation ≠ relationship ownership
Product Installation ≠ Product ownership
Provider access ≠ Provider appointment
AI assistance ≠ cross-Workplace learning permission
```

## 4. Source integration

The reconstruction draws from:

- the frozen and active Portfolio baseline;
- `BOOK-04-v2-MASTER.md`;
- the earlier Book 04 v2 architectural manuscript;
- the completed Book 01 publication reconstruction;
- Book 02 semantic reconciliation;
- Books 03, 05, 06 and 07 operating requirements.

No later Product manuscript may silently redefine Workplace semantics.

## 5. Manuscript tests

A chapter is not complete merely because it defines a term. It should include operating consequences and realistic tensions.

Examples include:

- a person belonging to several agencies;
- an agency operating Lite and MarkReg with different permissions;
- a customer introduced by one Workplace and served by another;
- a provider needing limited case access;
- an employee leaving an Organization;
- an independent professional carrying personal capability evidence between Workplaces;
- a Workplace exporting its records while platform models remain shared assets;
- AI being denied access to another Workplace’s private memory.

## 6. Authority locks

This manuscript does not:

- activate new Core objects;
- make Workplace a legal person;
- make the platform owner of customer relationships;
- allow a Workplace to mutate every record visible inside it;
- grant cross-Workplace AI training rights;
- make white-label delivery permission to misstate professional responsibility;
- convert MGSN Connection into ownership of the MGSN Network;
- authorize payment custody or platform-principal status.

## 7. Reconstruction sequence

The first batch establishes the Workplace foundation. Later batches expand:

```text
identity and representation
→ resources and needs
→ projection
→ engagement and collaboration
→ Product Installation
→ permissions and portability
→ operating models
→ governance and organizational learning
```
