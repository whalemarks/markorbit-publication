# Chapter 23 — The Professional Contribution Network

Not every piece of professional work requires a full external Provider engagement. Many Outcomes contain bounded tasks that can be assigned, reviewed and accepted separately.

Examples include search review, evidence verification, classification analysis, translation review, image comparison, data correction, content review and structured research.

MarkOrbit needs a Professional Contribution Network for this class of work.

## 1. Contribution is narrower than service delivery

A Contributor performs a defined Work Package under an Assignment. The Contributor does not automatically become the customer representative, Delivery Owner or formal Provider.

```text
Contribution
≠ complete professional service
≠ Provider Appointment
≠ customer relationship ownership
≠ formal-state authority
```

This distinction allows distributed expertise without creating false authority.

## 2. The unit of participation is the Work Package

A useful Work Package should define:

- source Engagement, Order or Matter;
- target Contribution;
- required Capability;
- input package;
- allowed tools and data scope;
- acceptance criteria;
- deadline;
- compensation basis;
- review route;
- confidentiality and reuse restrictions.

The task should be bounded enough that responsibility can be evaluated, but not fragmented so aggressively that professional context disappears.

## 3. Capability evidence matters

A profile claim is not enough. Eligibility may depend on:

- verified assessment;
- relevant production evidence;
- recency;
- language and jurisdiction fit;
- quality history;
- current Production Authorization;
- conflict and availability status.

Certification may support selection, but it does not create statutory qualification or authority to perform regulated acts.

## 4. Production Authorization remains scoped

A Person may be capable of a task but not yet authorized to perform it independently in production.

A maturity route may include:

```text
Simulation
→ supervised contribution
→ shadow production
→ bounded production
→ independent contribution
→ review authority
```

Authorization should identify the Capability, Product, task class, risk level and review requirement.

## 5. Review is part of production

The network must not treat review as optional overhead. A Contribution may be:

- accepted;
- accepted with correction;
- returned for revision;
- escalated;
- rejected;
- superseded.

The reviewer should evaluate against disclosed criteria and preserve enough evidence to explain the decision.

A contributor should be able to contest a quality decision through a governed route rather than an opaque score.

## 6. Compensation must reflect real work

Paid production must not be disguised as training. Where a Contribution creates business value, compensation should be explicit.

The commercial record should distinguish:

- training exercise;
- assessment;
- unpaid voluntary contribution, where lawfully and knowingly agreed;
- paid production Contribution;
- expert review;
- correction or rework responsibility.

The platform should not create a lowest-price labor market or use learners as invisible production capacity.

## 7. Data access remains Assignment-scoped

Contributors receive only the information required for the Work Package. They do not gain access to the full customer relationship or unrelated Matter history.

Material facts necessary for competent judgment must still be included. Redaction cannot turn the task into a misleading fragment.

Access should expire when the Assignment ends, subject to lawful evidence and retention duties.

## 8. Contributor identity and represented Workplace

A Person may contribute through:

- their own Personal Workplace;
- an employer's Workplace;
- another authorized Organization context.

Every Contribution must declare which Workplace the Person represents. Personal identity continuity does not permit private data or work products to move between Workplaces without rights.

## 9. Network health requires more than ratings

Simple star ratings reward visibility and volume rather than professional reliability.

Health indicators may include:

- acceptance quality;
- revision rate;
- response reliability;
- evidence completeness;
- deadline performance;
- task difficulty;
- review consistency;
- recency;
- unresolved disputes;
- workload and concentration.

Metrics must be interpreted in context and must not become permanent punishment for a single error.

## 10. Adverse selection and concentration risk

A contribution network may degrade when:

- difficult tasks are priced too low;
- experienced contributors leave;
- high-volume actors dominate ranking;
- reviewers become bottlenecks;
- contributors optimize for metrics rather than Outcomes;
- one Workplace captures most work;
- unqualified work is silently escalated into formal advice.

Governance should monitor the quality and distribution of opportunities, not only transaction volume.

## 11. Relationship to MGSN

The Professional Contribution Network and MGSN are distinct.

```text
Contribution Network
= bounded internal-platform professional work

MGSN
= formal external fulfillment by Provider Organizations
```

A lawyer may participate in both, but each role requires separate scope, authority and evidence.

## 12. The operating principle

Distributed professional work becomes valuable when the unit of contribution is clear, access is bounded, quality is reviewed and value is shared fairly.

> The Contribution Network expands production capacity without pretending that every contributor is the Provider, every task is a complete service or every certificate is professional authority.
