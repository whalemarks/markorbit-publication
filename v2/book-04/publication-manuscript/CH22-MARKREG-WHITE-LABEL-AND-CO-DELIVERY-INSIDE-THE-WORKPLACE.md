# Chapter 22 — MarkReg White-label and Co-delivery Inside the Workplace

MarkReg is not valuable because it replaces every agency. Its value is that it can supply structured Capability, workflow, evidence and coordination inside a Workplace without requiring the originating business to surrender its customer relationship.

That makes white-label and co-delivery central operating models.

## 1. The originating Workplace remains visible to the architecture

In a white-label arrangement, the customer-facing experience may use the originating Workplace's brand. The architecture must still preserve the real participants and responsibilities.

A typical route may include:

- originating Workplace as Relationship Owner;
- originating Organization as Contracting Party or customer-facing commercial party;
- MarkReg as Solution Orchestrator or Delivery Owner for a defined scope;
- internal staff as contributors;
- an MGSN Provider as local Professional Authority;
- an Owning Service holding formal Matter state.

White-label presentation does not erase this structure.

## 2. White-label is not silent forwarding

A weak white-label model merely forwards emails and hides the actual work chain. A governed model defines:

- service scope;
- customer communication rights;
- branding rules;
- professional disclosure duties;
- pricing and settlement;
- provider appointment;
- evidence return;
- exception handling;
- future relationship rights.

```text
White-label
≠ anonymous subcontracting
≠ concealed professional authority
≠ automatic customer transfer
```

## 3. Co-delivery divides the Outcome deliberately

Co-delivery is appropriate when the originating Workplace retains meaningful delivery responsibility while MarkReg or another participant supplies specific capabilities.

Examples include:

- originating Workplace collects customer facts while MarkReg performs readiness review;
- originating Workplace recommends strategy while MarkReg coordinates filing preparation;
- MarkReg generates a structured draft while a qualified professional reviews it;
- local Provider performs the formal act while the originating Workplace manages the customer;
- both parties contribute separate Work Packages to one Outcome.

Co-delivery requires a visible responsibility map. “We are working together” is not enough.

## 4. The customer-facing promise must match the operating model

A Workplace should not promise complete control while depending on undisclosed external actors for every critical step. Nor should MarkReg claim ownership of a relationship it only supports.

The customer-facing promise should explain, at the appropriate level:

- who manages the relationship;
- who is responsible for the overall Outcome;
- when a local professional is required;
- what remains subject to official action;
- what information may be shared;
- how delays and exceptions are handled.

Commercial simplicity must not become factual misrepresentation.

## 5. Product Installation governs the arrangement

The Workplace's MarkReg Product Installation should record:

- enabled service corridors;
- white-label or co-delivery mode;
- branding configuration;
- customer portal policy;
- roles and approval thresholds;
- pricing and quotation rules;
- data and AI policy;
- MGSN connection policy;
- evidence and retention requirements;
- escalation contacts.

This makes the operating model repeatable without turning it into an inflexible global rule.

## 6. Handoffs must be typed

A governed co-delivery route uses explicit handoffs:

```text
Customer Intake
→ Readiness Review
→ Route Approval
→ Work Package Assignment
→ Professional Review
→ Provider Appointment
→ Formal Execution
→ Evidence Return
→ Customer Delivery
```

Each handoff should identify the object transferred, the receiving role, the permitted action and the acceptance condition.

An email copied to another party is not a sufficient handoff record.

## 7. Pricing and margin remain bounded commercial information

The originating Workplace may set its own customer price. MarkReg and external participants may have separate service, coordination and provider charges.

The system must distinguish:

- customer-facing price;
- official fees;
- provider fees;
- contributor compensation;
- MarkReg service or coordination fee;
- originating Workplace margin;
- taxes and disbursements.

```text
Payment Receiver
≠ sole value creator
```

Visibility into internal margin should follow the agreed role and purpose, not platform curiosity.

## 8. Exceptions reveal the real Delivery Owner

Routine work can make responsibility appear clear until something fails. A rejected document, missed deadline, unavailable Provider or unexpected office action exposes whether anyone truly owns recovery.

The Engagement must identify who:

- informs the customer;
- obtains new instructions;
- appoints a replacement;
- approves extra cost;
- updates formal state;
- validates the recovered Outcome.

White-label must not become a mechanism for passing failure invisibly between parties.

## 9. Relationship protection is reciprocal

The originating Workplace should not be bypassed by MarkReg or a Provider. At the same time, the originating Workplace must not misuse MarkReg's network, knowledge or contributors outside agreed terms.

Protection may include:

- purpose-limited customer contact;
- provider identity and reuse policy;
- private pricing protection;
- source and relationship provenance;
- customer choice;
- termination and post-engagement rules;
- dispute and remedy procedures.

## 10. The operating principle

MarkReg should increase the operating capacity of a Workplace without dissolving it.

> White-label and co-delivery are successful when the customer receives a coherent Outcome, every participant's authority remains accurate, and the originating Workplace grows stronger rather than becoming a disposable lead source.
