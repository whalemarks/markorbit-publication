# Chapter 06 — Need Profiles and Unresolved Demand

A Workplace does not only have resources. It also has gaps, pressures, ambitions and unresolved demand. Those Needs are often hidden inside inboxes, spreadsheets, informal conversations and individual memory. When they remain unstructured, the organization reacts late, buys the wrong service or exposes too much information too early.

MarkOrbit treats Need as a first-class operating concept because collaboration starts with an unresolved outcome, not with a predetermined provider.

## 1. A Need is not yet a solution

A Need describes a desired result or unresolved condition before the route is fixed.

Examples include:

- determine where a brand should file;
- identify suitable classes and goods;
- obtain local professional representation;
- review a search result;
- create temporary production capacity;
- find a trademark available for acquisition;
- reach prospective buyers;
- verify ownership or resale authority;
- recover an abandoned application;
- obtain expert review of a difficult issue.

A Need should not be prematurely rewritten as “hire Provider X” or “buy Product Y.” That collapses problem discovery into procurement and prevents the platform from comparing routes.

```text
Need
≠ Request for a named provider
≠ Order
≠ Matter
≠ Assignment
≠ Transaction Opportunity
```

## 2. Where Needs come from

Needs may originate from:

- a customer instruction;
- an internal deadline;
- a detected portfolio risk;
- a capacity shortfall;
- a missing qualification;
- a Product recommendation;
- a regulatory change;
- an asset or sales opportunity;
- a failed workflow;
- a learning or quality gap.

The origin matters. A customer-originated Need may carry relationship and disclosure constraints. A system-detected Need may require human confirmation. A network-originated opportunity may not yet justify customer contact. An internal capacity Need may be solved without any external projection.

## 3. Need Profile

A useful Need Profile should capture at least:

- desired outcome;
- originating Workplace;
- relationship context;
- jurisdiction;
- relevant capability;
- urgency and deadline;
- risk level;
- budget or commercial constraints;
- required professional authority;
- preferred delivery mode;
- permitted disclosure;
- evidence available;
- dependencies;
- expiry or review date;
- known unknowns.

The profile should be proportional. A simple visual-review Need should not require the same intake as a multi-country filing strategy. But even a lightweight Need must preserve enough context to prevent unsafe routing.

## 4. Unknown is a legitimate state

Professional work frequently begins with incomplete information. The system must not force false certainty merely to complete a form.

A Need may have unknown:

- jurisdiction;
- applicant structure;
- commercial launch date;
- budget;
- exact goods and services;
- evidence quality;
- responsible professional;
- preferred route.

Unknown should trigger clarification, research or staged recommendation. It should not be silently replaced with defaults that later appear authoritative.

## 5. Need versus Intake

Intake is the process of obtaining enough information to handle a Need. It is not the Need itself.

A strong AI-assisted intake should:

- ask only questions that materially change the route;
- infer where evidence supports inference;
- explain assumptions;
- present alternatives;
- identify blocking unknowns;
- defer unnecessary detail;
- preserve customer choice.

The goal is not to maximize completed fields. The goal is to reach a decision-quality Need Profile with minimum burden.

## 6. Need maturity

Needs can mature through controlled states:

```text
signal
→ captured Need
→ clarified Need
→ route candidate
→ approved route
→ Engagement or Order
```

A signal may be a deadline alert or buyer inquiry. A captured Need records the unresolved outcome. Clarification adds necessary facts. A route candidate identifies possible ways forward. Approval selects a route. Only then should the system create the appropriate Engagement, Order, Matter or Transaction Opportunity.

This separation prevents every alert from becoming a case and every inquiry from becoming a sale.

## 7. Internal resolution before external projection

The Workplace should first ask whether the Need can be resolved internally.

Possible routes include:

- use an existing member;
- activate a Product capability;
- reuse approved knowledge;
- assign supervised work;
- schedule capacity later;
- request internal review;
- connect to an existing provider relationship;
- project externally.

External projection is one route, not the default response.

## 8. Relationship constraints

Needs involving customers require explicit relationship context.

The system should know:

- who owns the relationship;
- who may contact the customer;
- whether white-label delivery is expected;
- what identity must be disclosed;
- what information may be shared;
- whether future solicitation is prohibited;
- who contracts and receives payment.

These constraints belong in the Need Profile before candidate routing. Adding them after a provider receives customer information is too late.

## 9. Need aggregation without exposure

A platform can learn from aggregated Needs without publishing private customer demand.

For example, it may detect:

- rising demand for a jurisdiction;
- repeated shortages of a capability;
- common intake failures;
- unmet trademark acquisition demand;
- seasonal maintenance workloads.

Such analysis should use authorized, minimized and appropriately de-identified information. Aggregate learning must not allow the platform or another Workplace to reconstruct private customer pipelines.

## 10. Commercial significance

Needs are commercially important because they reveal where value is currently blocked.

A resource directory shows supply. A Need system shows demand. Sustainable collaboration requires both.

For Lite, Needs can become content, search, trademark acquisition or service opportunities. For MarkReg, Needs can become strategy, filing, maintenance or recovery routes. For MGSN, Needs can become controlled professional-service projections. For the Contribution Network, Needs can become bounded paid work.

But the conversion must remain governed:

```text
Need visibility
≠ permission to solicit
Need match
≠ professional recommendation
Need route
≠ appointment
Need acceptance
≠ formal-state change
```

## 11. Product behavior

A Product should help the Workplace understand:

- which Needs are unresolved;
- which are blocked by missing facts;
- which can be handled internally;
- which require external capability;
- which are approaching expiry;
- which carry relationship or authority constraints;
- which should not proceed.

The value of a Need Profile is not that it creates more work objects. It makes unresolved demand legible before the organization commits money, authority or customer information.
