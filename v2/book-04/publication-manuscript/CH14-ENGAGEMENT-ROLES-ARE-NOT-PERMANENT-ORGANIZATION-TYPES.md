# Chapter 14 — Engagement Roles Are Not Permanent Organization Types

Professional collaboration becomes distorted when temporary responsibilities are turned into fixed identities. A firm that originates one matter is labeled a “sales agent.” A local counsel becomes “the provider.” A platform product becomes “the service owner.” A person who receives payment is assumed to own the client. These labels appear convenient, but they erase the fact that responsibility changes from engagement to engagement.

MarkOrbit therefore models roles as engagement-specific assignments, not permanent organization types.

## 1. Why fixed categories fail

A single Organization may act differently across matters:

- relationship owner for one customer;
- capability contributor for another Workplace;
- local professional authority in its home jurisdiction;
- buyer of a trademark asset;
- seller or authorized reseller of another asset;
- reviewer for a bounded work package;
- originating Workplace in one Engagement and delivery participant in another.

If the system stores one permanent label such as “agent,” “provider,” “customer” or “partner,” it will eventually grant the wrong rights or allocate value incorrectly.

## 2. The principal engagement roles

A complex Engagement may include several distinct roles.

### Demand Originator

The party that identifies and introduces the Need. This may be the originating Workplace, a customer-facing professional or a Product-generated lifecycle signal.

Demand origination does not by itself create customer ownership or delivery responsibility.

### Relationship Owner

The party that holds the ongoing commercial relationship with the customer for the defined scope.

Relationship ownership may include communication expectations, pricing history, future-service opportunity and non-circumvention protections. It does not automatically include professional authority or the right to control formal legal facts.

### Contracting Party

The legal or commercial party that enters the agreement for the relevant service or transaction.

The Contracting Party may be different from the Relationship Owner, especially in white-label, group-company, marketplace or managed-network arrangements.

### Payment Receiver

The party that receives funds from the customer or another participant.

```text
Payment Receiver
≠ Relationship Owner
≠ Delivery Owner
≠ Professional Authority
```

Payment receipt creates accounting and settlement obligations, but not automatic entitlement to all value.

### Solution Orchestrator

The party that designs or coordinates the route across capabilities, Workplaces, Products and Providers.

Orchestration may involve readiness review, sequencing, escalation and communication. It does not necessarily make the orchestrator the professional decision-maker or the formal state owner.

### Capability Contributor

A Person or Workplace performing a bounded piece of work under an Assignment.

The Contributor may prepare, verify, research, review, translate, classify, design or analyze. Contribution does not imply authority to represent the customer or control the entire Outcome.

### Professional Authority

The qualified Person or Organization whose legal, regulated or professional status is required for a formal act or opinion.

Professional Authority must be grounded in actual qualification, jurisdiction and appointment. Platform certification cannot create it.

### Delivery Owner

The party accountable for producing the agreed customer-facing Outcome across all work packages and dependencies.

Delivery ownership is broader than performing one task. It includes coordination, exception handling, acceptance and recovery.

### Owning Service

The service that holds the formal business state for a Matter, Order, Transaction or other governed object.

Owning Service is an architectural authority, not necessarily a commercial party.

## 3. One participant may hold several roles

A small agency may be Relationship Owner, Contracting Party, Payment Receiver and Delivery Owner in one matter, while a foreign law firm holds Professional Authority.

A MarkReg white-label arrangement may place:

- customer relationship with the originating Workplace;
- commercial contract with either the originating Workplace or MarkReg, depending on the agreed model;
- orchestration with MarkReg;
- local professional authority with an MGSN Provider;
- formal Matter state with the Owning Service.

Combining roles is legitimate. Hiding the combination is not.

## 4. One role may also be shared

Co-delivery may require multiple Delivery Owners for separate scopes, or a primary Delivery Owner plus accountable work-package owners.

Professional Authority may involve several professionals where jurisdictions or legal acts differ. Relationship management may be shared between a group headquarters and local affiliate.

Shared roles require explicit boundaries. “Joint responsibility” without scope often means no one knows who must act.

## 5. Roles must carry rights and obligations

A role is not merely a display label. It should determine:

- permitted access;
- required approvals;
- communication rights;
- evidence obligations;
- liability or accountability scope;
- commercial entitlement;
- termination effects;
- escalation duties.

For example, a Capability Contributor may view only the assigned evidence package and submit a Contribution. The Delivery Owner may view broader status and exceptions. The Professional Authority may require access to all material facts necessary for competent action.

## 6. Role assignment must be evidenced

The system should record:

- who assigned or accepted the role;
- represented Workplace and Organization;
- effective date;
- scope;
- required qualification or authorization;
- related Engagement, Order, Matter or Work Package;
- compensation basis where relevant;
- revocation or replacement conditions.

A role inferred from behavior may be useful as a warning, but it should not silently become authoritative.

## 7. Role conflicts need explicit handling

Certain combinations create conflicts or concentration risk:

- the route recommender also receives undisclosed commission;
- the reviewer is reviewing their own contribution;
- the platform ranks its own service without disclosure;
- the Payment Receiver controls acceptance without representing the customer;
- the Relationship Owner withholds facts needed by the Professional Authority;
- the Delivery Owner lacks the ability to replace a failed provider.

The Product should disclose, prevent or require approval for material conflicts.

## 8. Role changes during execution

A provider may withdraw. A staff member may leave. The originating Workplace may transfer a customer relationship by agreement. A reviewer may take over delivery after escalation.

Role changes should not rewrite history. The system should preserve:

- prior holder;
- effective period;
- reason for change;
- outstanding obligations;
- access revocation;
- handover evidence;
- customer and participant approvals.

## 9. Commercial value follows contribution and responsibility

Because roles are separate, revenue distribution should not be based solely on who issued the invoice.

A settlement model may recognize:

- customer acquisition and relationship maintenance;
- solution design;
- production contribution;
- professional responsibility;
- local filing or formal representation;
- quality review;
- platform coordination;
- payment and foreign-exchange handling.

The allocation must be defined by the Engagement rather than guessed afterward.

## 10. The operating principle

Organizations are persistent. Engagement roles are contextual.

> MarkOrbit does not force every participant into a permanent marketplace category. It records who is responsible for what, in this Engagement, for this scope, during this period.
