# Chapter 09 — Why Collaboration Requires Projection

Professional collaboration begins with a paradox. A Workplace cannot solve every need internally, yet it cannot expose its full private operating context merely to discover whether another participant may be able to help.

Traditional systems usually offer only two bad choices. Either the Workplace keeps everything private and remains isolated, or it publishes too much and risks losing customer relationships, pricing logic, internal judgment, evidence, strategic intent or negotiating leverage. MarkOrbit requires a third mechanism: controlled Projection.

## 1. The problem before the solution

A Workplace may know that it needs local counsel, a search reviewer, design capacity, a buyer for a trademark asset, a filing partner, additional production capacity or expert validation. At the Need stage, however, many questions remain unresolved:

- whether the need can be solved internally;
- which Capability is actually required;
- whether professional authority is necessary;
- what information a candidate must see;
- whether the customer may be identified;
- whether commercial terms may be disclosed;
- whether the opportunity should be visible to one participant, a bounded network or a broader audience;
- how long the request should remain valid.

A raw database share cannot answer these questions. A public marketplace listing usually answers them too broadly. An email forwarded to several providers answers them inconsistently and leaves little auditable control.

Projection exists to convert a private resource or need into a purpose-limited external representation.

## 2. Projection as a governed view

A Projection is not a duplicate of the source record. It is a governed view generated from an authorized source context for a declared purpose.

Every Projection should identify at least:

- source Workplace;
- source object or object set;
- Projection class;
- declared purpose;
- intended recipients or audience class;
- disclosure level;
- omitted or transformed fields;
- relationship restrictions;
- allowed actions;
- expiry or review date;
- revocation policy;
- audit reference.

This means the external participant does not receive unrestricted access to the Workplace. The participant receives only the representation needed to evaluate or perform a bounded activity.

## 3. Why ordinary sharing is insufficient

Ordinary file sharing assumes that the file is the thing being shared. Professional collaboration is more complex. The relevant object may be a Need, Capability, trademark asset, evidence package, audience, capacity window or provider instruction.

The recipient may need to know:

- that a United States Section 8 filing is required;
- the registration number and deadline;
- the applicant type;
- whether use evidence exists;
- whether the originating Workplace retains the customer relationship.

The recipient may not need to know:

- the customer's entire historical portfolio;
- unrelated invoices;
- private pricing margins;
- internal staff discussions;
- future marketing plans;
- other providers under consideration.

Projection allows these two truths to coexist.

## 4. Projection is a stage, not an engagement

A Projection may support discovery, qualification, recommendation, approval or instruction. It does not itself create an Engagement, Order, Assignment or provider appointment.

```text
Need
→ Projection
→ Candidate discovery
→ Qualification
→ Route recommendation
→ Approval
→ Engagement
```

Collapsing these stages creates serious risk. A participant who merely views a Projection must not be treated as appointed. A provider who expresses interest must not gain access to full customer records. A match score must not become a recommendation without the required evidence and governance.

## 5. Projection and professional authority

Projection never creates authority that does not already exist. A person may project verified capability evidence, but the Projection does not create Professional Qualification. A Workplace may project a need for local counsel, but the Projection does not authorize an unqualified participant to provide legal representation.

The system must therefore preserve distinctions among:

- visibility;
- eligibility;
- recommendation;
- acceptance;
- appointment;
- authorization;
- execution.

Each transition requires its own conditions and evidence.

## 6. Projection as commercial infrastructure

Projection is also a commercial primitive. It allows Workplaces to expose bounded demand, capacity, assets and audience without surrendering their business position.

Examples include:

- a private trademark request visible only to selected inventory holders;
- a capability need visible to certified contributors;
- a provider instruction visible only after appointment;
- a content opportunity visible to a marketing partner without exposing the customer database;
- a resale listing that hides owner identity until due diligence;
- an available-capacity window that exposes no unrelated performance history.

This makes collaboration possible without requiring MarkOrbit to centralize every relationship.

## 7. Failure modes

Projection can fail when:

- the purpose is vague;
- too much information is copied by default;
- the audience is broader than necessary;
- expiry is missing;
- a projection remains active after the underlying Need changes;
- the recipient can reuse information for unrelated solicitation;
- transformed or redacted data obscures facts necessary for professional judgment;
- the platform treats visibility as consent to transact.

A trustworthy system must therefore support revocation, supersession, recipient logging, staged disclosure and explicit transition into engagement.

## 8. Product implications

Products should not implement Projection as a generic “share” button. They should offer purpose-specific Projection flows with clear preview and policy checks.

Before publication or delivery, the user should see:

- what will be disclosed;
- what will remain hidden;
- who may receive it;
- what recipients may do;
- when it expires;
- what later stage will require further approval.

The user should be able to compare a human-readable preview with the source context before release.

## 9. The operating principle

Collaboration requires selective visibility, but selective visibility must not become silent transfer.

The purpose of Projection is not to make all resources public. It is to let independent Workplaces coordinate around real needs while preserving authority, accountability, privacy and relationship sovereignty.

> MarkOrbit connects Workplaces by projecting only what collaboration requires, not by dissolving the boundaries that make each Workplace accountable.
