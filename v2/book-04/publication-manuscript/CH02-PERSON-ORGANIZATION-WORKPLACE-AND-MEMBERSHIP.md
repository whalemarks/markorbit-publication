# Chapter 02 — Person, Organization, Workplace and Membership

Professional platforms often collapse four different things into one account: the human user, the business entity, the operating environment and the permission record. That shortcut works until a person changes jobs, represents several businesses, acts independently or carries personal qualifications that do not belong to any employer.

MarkOrbit separates four concepts:

```text
Person
Organization
Workplace
Membership
```

## 1. Person: continuity of human identity

A Person represents the continuing human actor.

Personal attributes may include:

- identity and contact references;
- languages;
- professional history;
- personal capability evidence;
- credentials and qualifications;
- participation preferences;
- consent and privacy settings.

A Person does not automatically own every customer record they have touched. Nor does personal identity alone authorize action for an Organization.

## 2. Organization: the accountable actor

An Organization may be a company, agency, law firm, professional practice, nonprofit body or other accountable entity.

It may bear:

- contractual responsibility;
- invoicing and tax responsibility;
- employment or membership relationships;
- professional or regulatory obligations;
- ownership of private business assets;
- customer relationship responsibility.

An Organization can continue after individual members leave. It may operate several teams or brands while remaining one accountable entity.

## 3. Workplace: the operating boundary

A Workplace is the Organization-scoped environment in which people, Products, data access, permissions, private knowledge and business processes are assembled.

An Organization may have one Workplace or several, depending on governance needs. Separate Workplaces may be justified for subsidiaries, regulated practices, distinct business units or legally separated operations. They should not be created merely to mimic folders.

A Workplace is not itself a legal person. It represents an operating context for an Organization.

## 4. Membership: authority to participate

Membership connects a Person to a Workplace under explicit conditions.

A useful Membership records:

- status;
- role or role set;
- effective dates;
- Product access;
- permission scope;
- authority limits;
- invitation and approval provenance;
- supervision requirements;
- suspension or termination reason.

Membership answers not only whether a person can enter a Workplace, but how they may act there.

## 5. One Person, several Memberships

A professional may be:

- owner of a personal consulting practice;
- employee of a trademark agency;
- reviewer in a professional contribution network;
- named practitioner for a Provider Organization;
- learner in a Simulation Workplace.

These contexts may coexist. The platform should preserve one Person identity while preventing silent movement of private data and authority between Memberships.

The interface must make representation visible before consequential actions. Filing, quoting, inviting a provider, approving payment or contacting a customer should never rely on an invisible default Workplace.

## 6. Personal Workplace

An independent professional needs a place to manage their own customers, work, content, assets and subscriptions.

A Personal Workplace may be implemented as an Organization-backed sole-practice context rather than a Person becoming an Organization. The semantic decision remains subject to Core reconciliation, but the operating rule is clear: personal professional activity still needs an accountable business context.

Personal capability evidence may remain attached to the Person, while customer records and commercial policy belong to the Personal Workplace.

## 7. Joining does not transfer prior relationships

When a professional joins an Organization, their personal customer relationships do not automatically become organizational property. Conversely, customers served through an employer do not automatically leave with the professional.

The platform must record relationship provenance and the applicable agreement rather than guessing from who entered the contact.

This matters during:

- onboarding;
- team transfers;
- resignations;
- agency mergers;
- referral relationships;
- temporary project participation.

## 8. Leaving a Workplace

Termination of Membership should trigger controlled changes:

- revoke future access;
- terminate active sessions and connectors where appropriate;
- preserve historical attribution;
- reassign open responsibilities;
- review personal exports;
- protect Organization records;
- preserve the Person’s legitimate portable evidence and credentials.

Historical actions must not be rewritten as if performed by a generic account.

## 9. Delegation and supervision

Roles alone are often too broad. A junior professional may prepare an application but not approve filing. A finance member may confirm payment but not alter legal instructions. A contributor may view a single Work Package without accessing the full customer portfolio.

Membership therefore provides the standing relationship, while Assignment, Product permissions and professional authorization provide narrower authority.

```text
Membership
≠ Assignment
≠ Production Authorization
≠ Professional Qualification
```

## 10. Why the separation matters

This four-part model allows MarkOrbit to preserve:

- identity continuity without universal access;
- organizational accountability without owning personal credentials;
- multi-Product operation without duplicating Organizations;
- portable professional evidence without portable customer theft;
- historical attribution after Membership ends.

The model is more demanding than a conventional user table, but the complexity already exists in the real world. The choice is whether the platform represents it explicitly or hides it until a dispute exposes the omission.
