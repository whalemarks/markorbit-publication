# Chapter 03 — Business Sovereignty Is Not Data Ownership

The phrase business sovereignty can easily be misunderstood.

It does not mean a Workplace owns every record it can access. It does not mean an Organization may override legal restrictions, platform contracts or professional authority. It does not mean all data connected to a customer may be copied, exported or reused without limit.

Business sovereignty means something narrower and more useful:

> A Workplace retains legitimate control over its customer relationships, private commercial context, internal operating choices and accountable decisions, except where law, contract or delegated authority establishes otherwise.

## 1. Five different forms of control

A single record may involve several forms of control:

```text
business sovereignty
semantic authority
formal-state authority
physical custody
legal or evidentiary authority
```

Consider a trademark registration record.

The customer relationship may belong to Agency A. The official registry remains the legal source. MarkReg may own the internal Matter state. A cloud service may physically store a copy. Core defines the shared meaning of fields and references. None of those facts should be collapsed into one word such as owner.

## 2. Business sovereignty

A Workplace may legitimately control:

- whether and how it serves a customer;
- its private pricing and margin policy;
- internal notes and operating knowledge;
- which Products and providers it uses;
- whether a demand is projected externally;
- who may communicate with its customer;
- whether a collaboration continues;
- how internal work is assigned, subject to professional limits.

This sovereignty is the foundation of platform trust. Participants will not contribute customers, knowledge or demand to a system that silently treats those resources as platform inventory.

## 3. Record ownership is contextual

The idea that every record has one owner is often too crude.

A Workplace may own or control a private customer note. It may have licensed access to a data product. It may possess a copy of an official certificate without owning the official legal fact. It may display a provider invoice without having authority to validate the underlying government fee.

The platform should therefore record authority and provenance rather than relying on one universal ownership field.

## 4. Formal-state authority

Formal-state authority answers who may create or mutate an authoritative business record inside MarkOrbit.

For example:

- a MarkReg Owning Service may maintain Matter status;
- a billing service may maintain invoice state;
- an MGSN service may maintain provider appointment state;
- a Product Installation service may maintain entitlement state.

A Workplace may request, approve or consume these changes, but visibility does not grant direct mutation rights.

## 5. Physical custody

Data may be physically stored by a platform service, a connected storage provider or the Workplace itself.

Custody creates duties around security, retention, backup and deletion. It does not automatically create business sovereignty or legal authority.

This distinction matters when a customer leaves. A platform may be required to delete or return stored material while still preserving limited audit evidence or shared system integrity under applicable rules.

## 6. Legal and evidentiary authority

Some facts derive authority from external institutions or qualified professionals.

Examples include:

- official filing receipts;
- registry status;
- notarized or legalized documents;
- professional opinions;
- qualification records;
- government payment receipts.

A Workplace cannot make these facts true merely by approving them internally. It can decide how to act on them, but not replace their source authority.

## 7. Customer relationship sovereignty

Customer relationship sovereignty is especially sensitive.

It includes more than contact details. It may include:

- acquisition provenance;
- communication history;
- trust and expectations;
- pricing arrangements;
- future service rights;
- marketing consent;
- referral or non-circumvention obligations.

A provider who receives a customer name to perform one filing does not automatically gain permission to market renewal services. A Product that displays the customer does not become the Relationship Owner. A platform employee who supports an incident does not gain commercial rights.

## 8. Limits on sovereignty

Business sovereignty is not absolute.

It may be limited by:

- law and regulation;
- customer instructions;
- professional duties;
- contractual commitments;
- evidence preservation;
- sanctions and security requirements;
- valid platform governance;
- rights of other participants.

An agency cannot invoke sovereignty to hide a material filing failure from the customer or to misrepresent the identity of a required local professional.

## 9. Portability does not mean total extraction

A Workplace should be able to export concrete business records, documents, private knowledge and configuration it has rights to use.

It does not automatically gain ownership of:

- shared capability definitions;
- platform routing models;
- other Workplaces’ private data;
- MGSN provider pools;
- platform security intelligence;
- licensed third-party data beyond the license.

A credible exit policy distinguishes portable business assets from shared infrastructure.

## 10. The practical rule

Every consequential object should answer:

```text
who may see it?
who may use it?
who may change it?
who may disclose it?
who may export it?
who provides its authority?
```

Business sovereignty is therefore not a slogan of ownership. It is a disciplined model for preserving legitimate control while allowing shared systems to operate.
