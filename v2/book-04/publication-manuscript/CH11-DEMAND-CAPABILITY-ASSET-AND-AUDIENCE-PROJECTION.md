# Chapter 11 — Demand, Capability, Asset and Audience Projection

Projection is not one generic object. Different things are projected for different purposes, under different rights and different risks. A professional operating system must therefore distinguish Projection classes instead of treating every external representation as a listing.

Four classes are especially important to Workplace collaboration: Demand, Capability, Asset and Audience Projection.

## 1. Demand Projection

A Demand Projection represents an unresolved need that may require an external route.

It can describe:

- target Outcome;
- jurisdiction or market;
- required Capability;
- timing and urgency;
- language;
- evidence or document readiness;
- preferred collaboration mode;
- budget structure or quotation requirements;
- professional-authority requirements;
- relationship and communication constraints.

A Demand Projection should not automatically reveal the customer, full Matter, private pricing or all source documents.

Its purpose may be discovery, route testing, quotation, capability screening or provider recommendation. Those purposes require different audiences and disclosure levels.

```text
Demand Projection
≠ Order
≠ open tender
≠ provider appointment
≠ permission to solicit the customer
```

A projected demand may also remain private to selected candidates. “Projection” does not mean public publication.

## 2. Capability Projection

A Capability Projection represents what a Person or Workplace can potentially contribute.

It may include:

- defined Capability units;
- evidence level;
- Production Authorization where relevant;
- jurisdiction;
- language;
- professional qualification;
- permitted participation modes;
- current availability or capacity window;
- turnaround range;
- review or supervision requirements;
- commercial preference.

The Projection should not expose all private performance evidence, customer history, internal rates or disciplinary detail merely because a participant is discoverable.

Capability Projection must also preserve scope. A person who is qualified for one legal service is not thereby qualified for all work in that jurisdiction. A Workplace that completed one successful task does not gain universal authority.

The product must distinguish verified facts from self-declared claims and derived estimates.

## 3. Asset Projection

An Asset Projection represents a resource that may support commercial, operational or professional collaboration.

Examples include:

- a trademark available for resale;
- a brand concept available for licensing;
- a content asset available for distribution;
- a dataset available under a defined licence;
- a document template available for bounded use;
- available translation or design inventory.

For trademark commerce, the system must separate:

```text
Trademark record
→ asserted inventory
→ verified control or authority
→ resale authorization
→ Asset Projection
→ inquiry
→ due diligence
→ transaction route
```

An Asset Projection must disclose its authority state. A person may possess information about an asset without owning it or having authority to sell it. The platform must not convert data presence into commercial entitlement.

Visibility may also be tiered:

- public summary;
- network-only inventory;
- selected-buyer view;
- due-diligence package;
- execution package.

## 4. Audience Projection

An Audience Projection represents controlled access to distribution, attention or a customer-facing channel.

A Workplace may possess:

- a social-media account;
- an email audience;
- a professional community;
- a regional agent network;
- website traffic;
- client-education channels;
- offline events or association access.

Audience is not the same as a transferable customer list. A Workplace may agree to distribute content or promote an opportunity without revealing audience identities or granting another party direct solicitation rights.

An Audience Projection may specify:

- audience characteristics;
- channel type;
- estimated reach;
- content restrictions;
- brand and compliance requirements;
- tracking method;
- compensation model;
- whether leads remain with the originating Workplace;
- whether direct contact is permitted.

This allows content, trademark assets and professional services to reach demand without turning every audience into platform-owned prospecting inventory.

## 5. The classes may interact

A commercial route often connects more than one Projection class.

For example:

```text
Buyer Demand Projection
+ Trademark Asset Projection
+ Professional Capability Projection
+ Audience Projection
→ Match Candidate
→ controlled inquiry
→ due diligence
→ professional execution
```

Or:

```text
Workplace Demand Projection
+ Contributor Capability Projection
→ bounded contribution opportunity
→ acceptance
→ Assignment
```

The system should preserve each Projection’s source and policy rather than flattening all information into one opportunity record.

## 6. Projection state and lifecycle

Each Projection should have an explicit lifecycle, such as:

```text
Draft
→ Reviewed
→ Active
→ Paused
→ Matched or Routed
→ Expired
→ Revoked
→ Superseded
→ Closed
```

The underlying source can change while a Projection remains active. The system must detect material divergence and require refresh or suspension.

Examples include:

- a deadline changes;
- capacity is consumed;
- resale authorization expires;
- a customer withdraws consent;
- a provider loses qualification;
- an audience channel becomes unavailable;
- pricing conditions change.

## 7. Ranking and recommendation risk

Projection enables discovery, but ranking introduces power. A platform may be tempted to rank by payment, margin or convenience while presenting the result as objective suitability.

The system should distinguish:

- eligible candidates;
- match candidates;
- ranked candidates;
- recommended routes;
- approved participants.

Ranking factors should be explainable and should not hide conflicts of interest. Paid visibility must not be confused with professional recommendation.

## 8. Commercial implications

Projection classes create several legitimate revenue paths:

- Product subscription for managing resources and needs;
- coordination fees for governed matching or routing;
- content and distribution services;
- transaction support;
- professional delivery;
- capability development.

But a fee does not create authority. Payment cannot prove resale authorization, professional qualification, customer consent or appointment.

## 9. Product implications

Products should provide class-specific creation flows and previews.

A Demand Projection should ask about Outcome, urgency and relationship constraints. A Capability Projection should ask about evidence, authorization and capacity. An Asset Projection should ask about control and resale rights. An Audience Projection should ask about channel rights and lead handling.

A generic form would erase the very distinctions needed to govern collaboration.

## 10. The operating principle

Demand, Capability, Asset and Audience are different economic resources. They can be connected, but they must not be semantically merged.

> Projection makes resources discoverable without pretending that discoverability creates ownership, authority, appointment or transfer.
