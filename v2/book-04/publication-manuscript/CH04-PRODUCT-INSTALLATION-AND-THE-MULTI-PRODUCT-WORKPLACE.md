# Chapter 04 — Product Installation and the Multi-Product Workplace

A Workplace is not identical to any one Product.

The same agency may use Lite for content, customers and commercial opportunities; MarkReg for formal trademark matters; a Simulation Product for training; and MGSN-connected views for external professional fulfillment. If each Product creates its own isolated account, identity, customer model and permission system, the platform reproduces fragmentation inside itself.

MarkOrbit therefore separates Workplace from Product Installation.

## 1. Product versus Product Installation

A Product is a governed arrangement of capabilities, interfaces, workflows, commercial promises and evidence obligations.

A Product Installation is the Workplace-scoped record that defines how that Product participates in one Workplace.

It may include:

- edition and entitlement;
- enabled capabilities;
- configuration;
- assigned roles;
- data scope;
- connector policy;
- AI and memory policy;
- projection policy;
- audit references;
- activation, suspension and termination state.

The Product remains platform infrastructure. The Installation is the Workplace-specific operating contract.

## 2. Shared identity, bounded Product access

A Person may be a Workplace administrator in Lite but only a viewer in MarkReg. Another member may manage MarkReg production without permission to export the full customer list from Lite.

Membership establishes participation in the Workplace. Product Installation permissions define how that participation applies within a Product.

```text
Workplace Membership
+ Product role
+ object permission
+ Assignment or authorization
= effective action boundary
```

No single broad role should silently grant all Product access.

## 3. Shared objects without a universal database owner

Products may reference the same Customer, Organization, Matter, Trademark or Evidence object while different Owning Services maintain formal state.

The Workplace should present continuity without copying every object into every Product.

For example:

- Lite may show that a customer has an active MarkReg Matter;
- MarkReg may consume relationship and contact references from the Workplace;
- MGSN may receive a purpose-limited instruction projection;
- neither Lite nor MGSN gains authority to mutate the MarkReg Matter directly.

## 4. Product-specific experience

The same Workplace may present different priorities depending on installed Products and current needs.

A commerce-oriented Workplace may emphasize:

- content pipeline;
- trademark inventory;
- buyer requests;
- customer follow-up;
- transaction opportunities.

A production-oriented Workplace may emphasize:

- filing readiness;
- deadlines;
- blocked matters;
- review queues;
- provider Returns.

Personalization may change ordering and emphasis. It must not change semantic meaning, hide material risk or grant authority.

## 5. Installation lifecycle

A Product Installation may move through:

```text
proposed
→ configured
→ trial or shadow
→ active
→ restricted
→ suspended
→ terminated
```

Activation should confirm:

- authorized Workplace administrator;
- commercial entitlement;
- role mapping;
- initial data scope;
- connector permissions;
- retention and export policy;
- material governance notices.

Termination should revoke future access while preserving required evidence and export rights.

## 6. Shadow mode

A Product should often begin in shadow mode.

MarkReg may generate readiness findings without controlling production. Lite may suggest commercial actions without publishing them. A new AI assistant may draft responses without sending or applying them.

Shadow mode allows the Workplace to compare the Product’s behavior with existing work before delegating authority.

## 7. Connectors are part of the Installation contract

Email, calendars, storage, accounting systems and external databases can expand Product value and risk.

Connector policy should specify:

- which Workplace authorized it;
- which Product may use it;
- permitted data categories;
- read and write scope;
- credential custody;
- synchronization behavior;
- revocation and incident handling.

A connector enabled for one Product must not become a universal platform permission.

## 8. AI memory and Product boundaries

AI assistants may use Workplace-private memory, Product context and shared Knowledge under different rules.

An Installation must make clear whether the Product may:

- retrieve private Workplace documents;
- retain conversational memory;
- use outcomes for local improvement;
- submit de-identified candidates for shared learning;
- call external models or tools.

Installing a Product is not blanket consent to train on all Workplace data.

## 9. Commercial promises remain Product-specific

Lite may promise daily commercial leverage. MarkReg may promise governed lifecycle production. Simulation may promise safe practice and assessment.

The Workplace unifies context, but it should not blur those promises. Product metrics, responsibilities and remedies remain distinguishable.

## 10. The multi-Product principle

The long-term architecture is:

```text
one Workplace sovereignty boundary
+ multiple governed Product Installations
+ shared semantics and references
+ explicit cross-Product Handoffs
```

This provides continuity without creating a monolith. It allows Products to evolve independently while preserving customer relationships, authority and evidence at the Workplace level.
