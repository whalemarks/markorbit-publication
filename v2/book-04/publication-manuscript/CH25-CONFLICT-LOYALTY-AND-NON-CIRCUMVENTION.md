# Chapter 25 — Conflict, Loyalty and Non-circumvention

Collaboration creates value because independent Workplaces can combine customers, capability, authority, assets, audience and delivery capacity. The same connection also creates conflict risk. A participant may see a customer it would like to approach directly, recommend a route from which it secretly benefits, reuse another Workplace's pricing, or place its own service ahead of a better alternative.

The solution is not to pretend that every participant is neutral. It is to make interests, loyalties, access and remedies explicit enough that collaboration remains trustworthy.

## 1. Conflict is broader than legal conflict

A legal conflict check is necessary where professional rules require it, but Workplace conflict also includes commercial, relational, informational and platform conflicts.

Examples include:

- representing opposing parties in a dispute;
- recommending a Provider that pays an undisclosed commission;
- reviewing one's own Contribution;
- using a customer's confidential launch plan to help another customer;
- approaching a customer introduced for one Matter with unrelated services;
- routing demand to a platform-owned service without disclosing that interest;
- using private provider pricing to negotiate against that provider elsewhere;
- favoring volume over quality because the platform earns from completed routing.

A trustworthy system must therefore ask not only, “Is this legally prohibited?” but also, “Could this interest distort judgment, disclosure, recommendation or loyalty?”

## 2. Loyalty is scope-specific

Loyalty should not be modeled as permanent allegiance to one Organization. A Person may belong to several Workplaces and owe different duties in different contexts. A Provider may serve many originating Workplaces. A contributor may review work for multiple parties.

The relevant question is:

```text
For this Engagement,
who is represented,
for what scope,
under which duty,
and during which period?
```

Scope-specific loyalty may include duties to:

- protect customer confidentiality;
- avoid adverse representation;
- disclose material interests;
- follow the agreed communication route;
- preserve pricing confidentiality;
- avoid unauthorized solicitation;
- prioritize the accepted Outcome within agreed capacity;
- report circumstances that impair independent judgment.

This does not create ownership over a Person, Provider or customer. It creates bounded obligations arising from a defined Engagement.

## 3. Conflict checking must occur at several transitions

A single check at onboarding is insufficient. Relevant facts may become known only after customer identity, mark details, transaction parties or dispute positions are disclosed.

Conflict review may be required at:

- candidate qualification;
- disclosure expansion;
- Opportunity recommendation;
- provider acceptance;
- formal appointment;
- Assignment;
- material scope change;
- party substitution;
- dispute escalation;
- merger, departure or organizational change.

The system should support outcomes such as Cleared, Cleared with Conditions, Potential Conflict, Waiver Required, Declined and Unknown.

Unknown must remain legitimate when the necessary facts have not yet been disclosed.

## 4. Non-circumvention protects contribution, not captivity

Non-circumvention is justified when one party introduces a customer, Provider, asset, buyer, seller or opportunity and another participant exploits that introduction outside the agreed route without permission.

It is not justified as a claim of ownership over all future dealings.

A reasonable non-circumvention rule should identify:

- protected relationship or opportunity;
- originating party;
- protected scope;
- protected participants;
- effective period;
- permitted direct communication;
- customer-choice exception;
- pre-existing relationship exception;
- required attribution or compensation;
- complaint and remedy process.

```text
Introduction
≠ perpetual ownership

Direct communication
≠ automatic circumvention

Customer choice
≠ breach by default
```

## 5. Customer choice remains decisive

A customer may decide to change provider, appoint local counsel directly, move to another Workplace or terminate the originating relationship. The platform must not use non-circumvention to trap the customer.

The correct response is to preserve evidence of:

- who originated the relationship;
- what protection was agreed;
- whether the customer independently requested the change;
- whether confidential information was misused;
- whether compensation or notice is due;
- whether professional handover duties apply.

A customer-directed transfer may alter Relationship Owner, Contracting Party or Delivery Owner through explicit events. It must not be silently blocked or silently assumed.

## 6. Direct professional contact may be necessary

White-label and co-delivery arrangements often require direct contact between a customer and a lawyer, agent or qualified professional. Such contact may be legally required or operationally necessary.

The system should distinguish:

- identity verification;
- conflict checking;
- professional advice or confirmation;
- signature or authorization;
- formal filing communication;
- unrelated marketing;
- future-service solicitation.

The first five may be permitted within scope. The last two require separate authority or consent.

## 7. Platform conflicts must be visible

MarkOrbit itself may occupy several positions:

- Product operator;
- route recommender;
- network manager;
- subscription provider;
- transaction coordinator;
- MarkReg delivery participant;
- owner of platform-operated services.

These positions can create self-preference. The platform should therefore disclose material interests when:

- a recommended route includes a platform-owned service;
- ranking is affected by commercial payment;
- the platform receives a transaction or coordination fee;
- an affiliated Provider is considered;
- the platform benefits from a longer or more expensive route;
- data from one role could advantage another.

The objective is not an impossible claim of perfect neutrality. It is governed self-interest that can be inspected and challenged.

## 8. Ranking and recommendation require conflict controls

A ranking system can distort behavior even when no person acts dishonestly. Optimizing for response rate may favor superficial providers. Optimizing for low price may reduce quality. Optimizing for platform revenue may favor costly routes.

Recommendations should therefore expose:

- eligibility basis;
- evidence used;
- known limitations;
- commercial relationships;
- uncertainty;
- alternative routes;
- reasons for exclusion;
- human or professional review where required.

A Provider should not be able to buy invisible authority.

## 9. Complaint and remedy process

Conflict and circumvention disputes require evidence rather than informal accusation.

A complaint record should identify:

- disputed relationship or opportunity;
- relevant Engagement and Projection;
- communication and access history;
- alleged unauthorized action;
- customer instruction;
- prior relationship evidence;
- material commercial impact;
- response from affected participants;
- interim protection;
- decision and remedy.

Possible remedies include:

- access restriction;
- communication limitation;
- correction of role or attribution;
- compensation under agreed terms;
- removal from an Engagement;
- Provider or contributor review;
- suspension from a route;
- customer-directed handover;
- escalation to contractual or legal process.

The platform must not decide professional liability merely because it stores the evidence.

## 10. Product implications

Products should provide:

- conflict declarations and checks;
- relationship provenance;
- commercial-interest disclosure;
- purpose-limited communication channels;
- customer-choice and transfer records;
- protected-scope and expiry controls;
- pre-existing relationship evidence;
- complaint and remedy workflow;
- auditable ranking explanations;
- role separation where self-review or self-recommendation is unsafe.

> Collaboration becomes durable when loyalty is explicit, conflicts are visible and non-circumvention protects legitimate contribution without turning customers or professionals into captive assets.
