# v2 Governance and Rewrite Roadmap

## Status rule

The files under `v2/` are working manuscripts. They do not replace the active vNext.1 Canon or frozen Book baselines until explicit Owner approval.

## Why this rewrite is necessary

The first seven-book cycle established substantial architecture but accumulated strategic drift:

- Capability remained present but lost visible first-class status;
- Workplace sovereignty advanced faster than the product and collaboration model;
- Data, Knowledge and Brain did not become a complete platform topology;
- Lite evolved from a broad growth tool into a content-and-commerce product;
- certification, simulation, production authorization and paid contribution became core platform mechanisms;
- MarkReg emerged as both flagship product and capability-production proving ground;
- platform contributors and MGSN providers required a clear separation;
- Need, Engagement, WorkPackage and Contribution became necessary to explain multi-party delivery.

## Ten-year portfolio priorities

### 2026–2027 — Foundation and first revenue loops

- Data Refinery and Knowledge Refinery foundations;
- Brain retrieval with provenance;
- Capability Registry v1;
- Lite trademark inventory, content studio, request matching and inquiry pipeline;
- US maintenance capability simulation and supervised production pilot;
- MarkReg standardized lifecycle and MGSN controlled corridors.

### 2027–2029 — Capability and commerce density

- expand capability taxonomy and certification paths;
- transaction engagement and transfer services;
- professional contribution pools;
- Workplace resource and need profiles;
- white-label MarkReg;
- jurisdiction and provider coverage expansion.

### 2029–2032 — Network operating leverage

- evidence-based routing;
- institution-level capability analytics;
- deeper applicant and opportunity intelligence;
- standardized expert panels;
- cross-product asset and opportunity projections;
- stronger funds and settlement controls.

### 2032–2036 — Industry infrastructure

- recognized professional capability credentials;
- broad global data and knowledge coverage;
- high-density verified professional network;
- interoperable Workplace ecosystem;
- trusted global fulfillment and transaction infrastructure;
- learning loops that improve capability without violating rights or responsibility.

## Investment discipline

A proposed repository, Product or network layer must satisfy at least one durable moat:

- proprietary structured data;
- governed professional knowledge;
- capability evidence;
- transaction liquidity;
- verified professional supply;
- customer workflow lock-in created by value, not obstruction;
- accumulated trust and outcome evidence.

Projects that only duplicate a generic AI feature without deepening these assets should remain experiments, adapters or optional integrations.
