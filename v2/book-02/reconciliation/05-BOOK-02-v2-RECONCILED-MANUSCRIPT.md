# MarkOrbit Core Specification — Shared Semantics for a Capability-Centered Platform

## Status

- Edition: v2 reconciled working manuscript
- Authority: draft explanatory text
- Frozen Core: unchanged
- Candidate additions: explicitly labeled

## Part I — Core constitution

### Chapter 01 — Core defines the shared language

Core defines stable identifiers, controlled meanings, public contracts, lifecycle vocabulary, validation expectations, evidence structure and compatibility rules shared across MarkOrbit.

Core does not become the central database, workflow engine, product UI, commercial operator, knowledge store or owner of every record.

### Chapter 02 — Authority remains separated

Every implementation must preserve:

```text
business sovereignty
semantic authority
formal-state authority
physical custody
legal, evidentiary and professional authority
```

Core owns semantic authority. Owning Services retain formal-state authority. Workplaces retain legitimate business sovereignty. Source and professional authority remain with the applicable actor or institution.

### Chapter 03 — Candidate-before-Canonical

```text
observation ≠ fact
candidate ≠ approved record
AI output ≠ professional decision
Contribution ≠ accepted Outcome
accepted Outcome ≠ formal-state mutation
Provider Return ≠ accepted truth
certification ≠ legal qualification
projection ≠ ownership transfer
```

## Part II — Identity and context

### Chapter 04 — Person, Organization, Workplace and Membership

Person represents identity continuity. Organization represents legal, commercial, professional or administrative accountability. Workplace is the Organization-scoped business-sovereignty and Product-operation boundary. Membership connects a Person to a Workplace under explicit role and authority.

These are active shared semantics.

### Chapter 05 — Relationship semantics

Customer, Contact and Relationship remain shared concepts. Relationship Owner is treated in this manuscript as a candidate engagement-role reference, not an activated new root object.

Customer data stored or displayed by the platform does not transfer relationship ownership.

### Chapter 06 — Product Installation

Product Installation remains the Workplace-scoped record of edition, entitlement, enabled capabilities, configuration, data scope, permission mapping, projection policy and lifecycle.

## Part III — Capability semantics

### Chapter 07 — Capability Definition

Capability Definition is the durable outcome contract. It includes identity, version, domain, outcome, inputs, outputs, implementation modes, data and knowledge dependencies, review policy, risk, restrictions, evidence and compatibility.

### Chapter 08 — Skill, Tool, Service and Workflow remain distinct

```text
Capability = stable professional outcome contract
Skill = agent or procedural implementation
Tool = execution engine
Service = runtime or owning component
Workflow = orchestration
Product = user and commercial experience
```

Core standardizes the distinctions without absorbing every implementation.

### Chapter 09 — Levels and collaboration modes

L1–L5 proficiency and M0–M5 human–AI collaboration are candidate controlled vocabularies. They may be referenced by Capability profiles before becoming activated Core value sets.

### Chapter 10 — Credentials and authorization

Capability Certification, Professional Qualification and Assessor Authorization should share a common credential envelope where possible.

Production Authorization is a stronger candidate because it controls access to real work and may require a governed lifecycle.

No credential or authorization is activated by this manuscript.

## Part IV — Demand and collaboration

### Chapter 11 — Need

Need is a candidate shared object representing a required outcome before a commercial or delivery route is fixed.

Until approved, Products may implement Need as a versioned profile over existing intake, request or opportunity semantics.

### Chapter 12 — Opportunity

Opportunity remains the potential route by which demand, capability, asset, audience, capacity or provider supply may create value.

Product specializations such as Transaction Opportunity do not require a parallel Core root.

### Chapter 13 — Engagement

Engagement is a candidate shared collaboration boundary between Opportunity and execution or commercial commitment.

Its minimum proposed meaning includes:

- participating actors;
- engagement-specific roles;
- purpose and scope;
- disclosure boundary;
- responsibility allocation;
- customer-relationship constraint;
- professional-authority reference;
- commercial reference;
- termination and continuity.

Engagement must not duplicate Matter, Order, Contract or Assignment.

### Chapter 14 — Engagement roles

Demand Originator, Relationship Owner, Contracting Party, Payment Receiver, Solution Orchestrator, Capability Contributor, Professional Authority and Delivery Owner are candidate controlled roles.

They are not permanent Person or Workplace types.

## Part V — Work and outcome semantics

### Chapter 15 — Matter and Order

Matter remains the long-lived professional subject. Order remains the customer-facing commercial commitment.

Neither should be used as a universal container for all collaboration or work.

### Chapter 16 — Work Package

Work Package is a candidate bounded work contract. It is distinct from Workflow, Step and Product Task.

A proposed Work Package includes:

- objective;
- input reference;
- required output;
- eligibility;
- authority boundary;
- visible data;
- deadline;
- review policy;
- compensation reference;
- acceptance criteria.

### Chapter 17 — Assignment

Assignment is the candidate authorization that binds a Work Package to a Person or Workplace under scoped terms.

It is not proof that work was completed.

### Chapter 18 — Contribution

Contribution is the candidate submitted work, artifact, verification, opinion or professional input produced under an Assignment or other authorized context.

It retains actor, capability, authority, evidence, tool and provenance references.

### Chapter 19 — Review and Outcome

Review remains an active shared governance concept.

Outcome is a candidate accepted result assembled from one or more Contributions for a declared purpose.

Formal business-state mutation remains separate and must occur through the Owning Service and governed Apply path.

### Chapter 20 — Settlement and Evidence

Core may define neutral references for compensation, commission, professional fees and settlement status while detailed commercial policy remains with the applicable Owning Service.

Evidence remains the cross-cutting lineage of source, actor, time, method, authority, version and disposition.

## Part VI — Data, knowledge and Brain

### Chapter 21 — Data references

Core defines references and contracts needed for interoperability. Data Refinery owns transformation and publication of structured data products.

Core must not absorb every jurisdiction field or data-processing pipeline.

### Chapter 22 — Knowledge references

Knowledge Source, Document, Claim, Version, Citation, Link and Review State are candidate or active contracts depending on the frozen baseline mapping.

Markdown is a representation, not semantic authority.

### Chapter 23 — Brain results

Brain outputs must be typed as retrieval, inference, recommendation, candidate, explanation or other governed result class.

Brain may cite formal facts but cannot silently mutate them.

## Part VII — Projection and networks

### Chapter 24 — Projection

Projection remains the purpose-limited exposure of information, capability, asset or demand from an authorized source context.

Demand Projection, Capability Projection, Asset Projection, Audience Projection and Capacity Projection are profiles of the same semantic mechanism.

### Chapter 25 — Handoff and Return

Handoff carries typed context, purpose, permission, minimum necessary information, version and acceptance state.

Return carries evidence, status or result to the origin. The origin validates Return before formal acceptance.

### Chapter 26 — Contributor and Provider

Contributor is an engagement or assignment role held by a Person or Workplace performing bounded work.

Provider Organization is an Organization profile bearing external professional responsibility.

The same person may participate in both contexts, but the authority contract is different.

### Chapter 27 — Product-local commerce and network objects

Trademark Inventory, Listing, Buyer Inquiry, Transaction Opportunity, Candidate Route Set, Provider Package, Case Fixture and Commission Claim remain outside Core for now.

They should reference shared Core semantics rather than redefine them.

## Part VIII — Compatibility and change governance

### Chapter 28 — Public, shared and local contracts

Core should contain only concepts whose shared meaning must remain stable across Products or layers.

Product-local records remain legitimate and should not be promoted merely for architectural symmetry.

### Chapter 29 — Versioning

Every public contract and controlled value set requires identifier, version, compatibility rule, deprecation policy and migration evidence.

### Chapter 30 — Change Proposal gate

Any activation of Need, Engagement, Work Package, Assignment, Contribution, Outcome, Production Authorization or new engagement roles requires:

```text
frozen-baseline field mapping
→ lifecycle and authority analysis
→ cross-book impact analysis
→ formal Change Proposal
→ conformance fixtures
→ implementation migration plan
→ Owner approval
```

## Ten-year lock

Core must remain smaller than the platform.

Its long-term value comes from preserving a stable professional language while allowing Products, Workplaces, Data, Knowledge, Brain, Execution and Networks to evolve independently.
