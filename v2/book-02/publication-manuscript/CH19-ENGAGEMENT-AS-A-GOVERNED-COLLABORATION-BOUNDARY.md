# CH19 — Engagement as a Governed Collaboration Boundary

## Collaboration needs an explicit boundary

A professional platform can connect customers, partner Workplaces, contributors, providers, reviewers, delivery teams and AI-enabled implementations.

Without a governed collaboration boundary, these actors begin sharing data, making commitments and performing work through informal messages and implicit assumptions.

Engagement is the candidate shared semantic intended to solve that problem.

It answers:

> Which actors are collaborating, for what purpose, under which scope, authority, disclosure and continuity rules?

```text
Engagement
≠ Opportunity
≠ Order
≠ Matter
≠ Contract
≠ Assignment
≠ Workflow
```

Engagement is not activated by this chapter. It remains an F3/F4 candidate requiring the formal Core Change Proposal process.

## Why existing records are not enough

Opportunity describes a potential route. Order describes a commercial commitment. Matter preserves a long-lived professional subject. Assignment authorizes bounded work. Contract records legal terms.

None alone fully describes a multi-party collaboration boundary.

For example, a white-label trademark filing may involve:

- an originating agency that owns the customer relationship;
- MarkReg as Delivery Owner;
- a local Provider with professional authority;
- a customer signatory;
- contributors preparing documents;
- a payment receiver different from the provider;
- an external office establishing official state.

Trying to represent this entire arrangement as one Order, Matter or Provider field obscures responsibility.

## Minimum Engagement meaning

A proposed Engagement should preserve:

- Engagement identifier and version;
- purpose;
- participating actors and represented organizations;
- engagement-specific roles;
- scope and exclusions;
- related Need, Opportunity, Order, Matter and Product references;
- customer-relationship constraint;
- contracting-party references;
- professional-authority references;
- disclosure and data-use boundary;
- communication rules;
- commercial and settlement references;
- delivery and review responsibilities;
- effective period;
- amendment, suspension and termination;
- continuity and recovery plan;
- Evidence and audit references.

```text
actor participates
≠ actor has every role
≠ actor owns every record
```

## Engagement begins only through governed acceptance

A platform may recommend an actor or route, but recommendation does not create participation.

```text
Actor discovered
≠ Actor selected
≠ Actor accepted Engagement
```

Each participating actor should know:

- why they are involved;
- what they are expected to do;
- which data they may access;
- who may contact the customer;
- who can approve cost or action;
- how they may exit;
- what evidence they must return.

A Provider email exchange or internal chat is not a substitute for Engagement acceptance where the collaboration has consequential scope.

## Engagement and customer relationship

One of the most important Engagement duties is preserving relationship ownership.

A partner Workplace may retain the customer relationship while another team performs delivery. Direct communication may be allowed for a limited professional purpose without transferring the relationship.

```text
Direct customer contact
≠ Relationship transfer

Delivery responsibility
≠ customer ownership
```

The Engagement should specify:

- Relationship Owner;
- permitted contact channels;
- whether communication is mediated;
- branding and disclosure rules;
- who may make commercial commitments;
- whether follow-up marketing is prohibited;
- how customer context is returned or deleted at termination.

## Engagement and commercial commitment

An Engagement may reference one or more Orders or Contracts, but it should not duplicate them.

Commercial terms may be distributed across:

- customer Order;
- provider service agreement;
- contributor compensation terms;
- platform subscription;
- revenue-share arrangement;
- official fee approval.

```text
Engagement exists
≠ every commercial term accepted
≠ funds movement authorized
```

Engagement records which commercial references govern the collaboration and which party holds each obligation.

## Engagement and professional authority

An Engagement may identify a Professional Authority role, but it cannot create legal qualification.

```text
Engagement names a lawyer
≠ lawyer qualified in the jurisdiction
≠ conflict cleared
≠ matter appointment accepted
```

The platform must separately preserve:

- qualification evidence;
- jurisdiction;
- current status;
- appointment;
- scope;
- acceptance;
- reserved action boundary.

## Engagement and Matter

A Matter can exist before, during and after an Engagement. One Engagement can support several Matters. A Matter can involve successive Engagements as providers, Workplaces or delivery arrangements change.

```text
Engagement terminated
≠ Matter terminated

Matter transferred
≠ customer relationship automatically transferred
```

Continuity rules should determine what happens to open Work Packages, Assignments, documents, deadlines, approvals and external effects.

## Engagement and Assignment

Engagement defines the collaboration boundary. Assignment authorizes one actor to perform one bounded Work Package.

```text
Engagement participant
≠ assigned performer
```

A reviewer, Relationship Owner or payment receiver may participate in the Engagement without holding an Assignment. A contributor may have several Assignments under one Engagement.

The Engagement can constrain the types of Assignment permitted, but Book 03 owns task-specific eligibility and runtime assignment behavior.

## Disclosure boundary

The Engagement should define the maximum collaboration-wide disclosure policy. Each Projection, Handoff and Assignment must still apply minimum-necessary access.

```text
Engagement permits collaboration
≠ all participants may see all data
```

The boundary may specify:

- customer identity visibility;
- document categories;
- financial information;
- privileged or sensitive materials;
- download and onward-sharing rules;
- retention;
- AI-use and training restrictions;
- jurisdictional transfer limits.

## White-label and co-delivery patterns

A white-label Engagement may preserve:

```text
Originating Workplace = Relationship Owner and Contracting Party
MarkReg team = Delivery Owner
Local Provider = Professional Authority and External Actor
Customer = decision and signature authority where applicable
```

A co-delivery Engagement may instead disclose several participating organizations to the customer and divide responsibilities openly.

The same Product capability can support both patterns because Engagement makes the roles and disclosure model explicit.

## Amendment and versioning

Engagement scope can change when:

- a provider is replaced;
- a new jurisdiction is added;
- direct customer contact is allowed;
- commercial terms change;
- data scope expands;
- a Professional Authority changes;
- a deadline incident requires emergency coordination.

Material changes require a versioned amendment and actor acceptance where their obligations or rights change.

```text
new participant added
≠ earlier participant authority revoked automatically
```

## Suspension and termination

Termination must address more than access removal.

The platform should identify:

- open Matters;
- active Work Packages and Assignments;
- pending external actions;
- customer communications;
- held documents and credentials;
- outstanding fees and compensation;
- data return or deletion;
- continuity owner;
- unresolved incidents and Evidence.

```text
Engagement ended
≠ historical responsibility erased
```

## Engagement Evidence

A governed Engagement should be reconstructable through:

- acceptance records;
- role and authority references;
- scope versions;
- disclosure decisions;
- commercial references;
- Handoffs;
- Assignments and Returns;
- amendments;
- suspension or termination events.

This evidence allows the platform to explain why an actor had access or responsibility at a particular time.

## Candidate status and admission risk

Engagement is attractive because it connects concepts that recur across Books 03, 04, 05 and 06. It is also dangerous because it could become an uncontrolled universal container.

Formal admission must prove that Engagement:

- does not duplicate Order, Matter, Contract or Assignment;
- has a stable minimum lifecycle;
- improves cross-product interoperability;
- preserves Workplace sovereignty;
- does not centralize all collaboration records in Core;
- supports local profiles without semantic drift.

## Constitutional rule

```text
Engagement defines the governed boundary of collaboration.
It identifies purpose, participants, roles, disclosure,
responsibility and continuity without replacing
Order, Matter, Contract, Assignment or professional authority.
```
