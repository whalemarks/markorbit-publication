# Book 02 — Candidate Semantic Register

## 1. Purpose

This register records concepts that appear useful across multiple Products or platform layers but are not automatically active Core semantics.

The register is governed by:

```text
F0 — Already Covered
F1 — Composition
F2 — Explanatory Profile
F3 — Candidate Shared Addition
F4 — Formal Core Change Proposal Required
```

Publication inclusion does not activate a candidate.

## 2. Strongest candidates

| Candidate | Current classification | Reason for candidacy | Main risks before admission | Required next gate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Need | F3 | Distinct cross-product demand origin before Product or route selection | duplication with Opportunity or Product intake; unclear lifecycle | frozen mapping, lifecycle and authority proposal |
| Engagement | F3/F4 | Stable multi-party collaboration boundary across Workplaces and Providers | becoming a universal case container; overlap with Order, Matter and Contract | field-level Change Proposal and lifecycle fixtures |
| Work Package | F3 | Bounded production contract needed across Execution, MarkReg and contribution systems | overlap with Workflow Step or Product task | exact contract, versioning and acceptance fixtures |
| Assignment | F3 | Scoped authorization binding a Work Package to an actor or implementation | accidental permanent role or professional authority | authority ceiling, expiry and revocation proposal |
| Contribution | F3 | Attributable submitted work distinct from accepted Outcome | duplication with Document or task completion | provenance, revision and review contract |
| Outcome | F3 | Accepted purpose-specific result assembled from Contributions | confusion with Decision or Formal State | acceptance, version and formal-state boundary proposal |
| Professional Authority | F3 | Needed to gate reserved actions across Execution, MarkReg and MGSN | platform-created licence or universal actor status | credential, appointment and jurisdiction profile |
| Relationship Owner | F3 | Needed to preserve customer relationship across Handoffs and white-label delivery | treating customer as owned property; conflict with Workplace sovereignty | role scope, transfer and termination rules |
| Delivery Owner | F3 | Needed for multi-party production continuity and recovery | implied universal approval or formal-state ownership | responsibility, escalation and handoff profile |
| Capability Certification | F3 | Portable evidence of assessed capability | confusion with legal qualification or production permission | issuer, evidence, expiry and revocation contract |
| Production Authorization | F3/F4 | Grants real-work participation within controlled scope | broad access, hidden autonomy or professional-authority leakage | strict lifecycle, policy and conformance suite |
| Resale Authorization | F3 | Needed to prove authority to list or broker an asset | confusion with ownership, title or general commercial authority | rights source, scope, expiry and evidence contract |

## 3. Controlled vocabulary candidates

| Vocabulary | Classification | Intended use | Lock |
|---|---|---|---|
| L1–L5 | F2/F3 | Capability-specific proficiency reference | not a universal person rank |
| M0–M5 | F2/F3 | Human–AI collaboration mode | not an autonomy or maturity score |
| R0–R4 | F2/F3 | Required control tier for a proposed transition | not inherent risk of a whole Product |
| Engagement Roles | F2/F3 | Contextual roles such as Relationship Owner, Delivery Owner and Professional Authority | not permanent Person or Organization types |
| Brain Result Types | F2/F3 | Retrieval, Inference, Recommendation, Candidate and Explanation | not canonical truth statuses |

These value sets require versioning, definition, compatibility and deprecation rules before activation.

## 5. Product-local concepts that remain outside Core

Unless cross-product interchange proves otherwise, the following remain Product-local:

```text
Trademark Inventory
Candidate Route Set
Provider Package
Case Fixture
Commission Claim
Content Brief
Campaign Asset
Marketplace Ranking
Pricing Policy
Country Checklist
Prompt or Tool Binding
```

Their importance to a Product does not justify Core admission.

## 6. Admission evidence required

An F3 or F4 proposal should include:

1. frozen-baseline field and lifecycle mapping;
2. at least two independent consumers with one stable shared meaning;
3. proof that composition or profile treatment is insufficient;
4. authority, ownership and lifecycle design;
5. identifier and versioning rules;
6. validation and Evidence requirements;
7. compatibility and migration impact;
8. conformance fixtures;
9. rollback and deprecation plan;
10. explicit Owner approval.
