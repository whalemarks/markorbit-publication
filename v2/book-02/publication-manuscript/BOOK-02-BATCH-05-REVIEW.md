## Main decisions

### 1. Need precedes product and commercial routing

```text
Need
≠ Opportunity
≠ Order
≠ Matter
≠ Capability Request
```

Need represents a required outcome before a Product, Capability or commercial route is selected.

Need remains a candidate shared semantic. Detection of a Need does not create a Customer, marketing consent or execution instruction.

### 2. Opportunity remains potential value

```text
Opportunity
≠ Customer Instruction
≠ Order
≠ Engagement
≠ Assignment
```

Opportunity represents a possible route through which a Need, Capability, asset, audience, capacity or provider supply may create value.

Product-local service, transaction, maintenance, content and partnership Opportunities should remain profiles rather than separate Core roots by default.

Opportunity scores remain Product-local decision aids and do not establish consent, legal priority or verified demand.

### 3. Engagement is a collaboration boundary, not a universal container

```text
Engagement
≠ Opportunity
≠ Order
≠ Matter
≠ Contract
≠ Assignment
≠ Workflow
```

The proposed Engagement meaning preserves:

- participating actors;
- contextual roles;
- purpose, scope and exclusions;
- customer-relationship constraints;
- disclosure boundaries;
- professional-authority references;
- commercial references;
- continuity and termination.

Engagement remains a high-priority candidate and was not activated.

### 4. Roles remain contextual

```text
actor identity
≠ permanent Engagement role
```

The batch distinguishes:

- Demand Originator;
- Relationship Owner;
- Contracting Party;
- Payment Receiver;
- Solution Orchestrator;
- Capability Contributor;
- Professional Authority;
- Delivery Owner;
- Reviewer and Approver;
- Provider.

A role must be bound to an exact Engagement or other governed context, represented actor, scope, authority source and effective period.

### 5. Relationship ownership stays protected

```text
Need detected
≠ customer relationship created

Opportunity projected
≠ relationship transferred

Direct customer contact
≠ relationship transferred

Delivery Owner
≠ Relationship Owner
```

White-label and co-delivery patterns remain possible without collapsing relationship, delivery and professional authority.
