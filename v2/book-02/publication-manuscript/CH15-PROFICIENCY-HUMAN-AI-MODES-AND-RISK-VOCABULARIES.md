# CH15 — Proficiency, Human–AI Modes and Risk Vocabularies

## Three vocabularies answer three different questions

MarkOrbit needs controlled vocabularies for proficiency, human–AI collaboration and execution risk.

They must remain distinct because they answer different questions:

```text
Proficiency level
→ how capable is the performer?

M-mode
→ how is human and AI work combined?

R-tier
→ what control is required before reliance or action?
```

Therefore:

```text
L-level
≠ M-mode
≠ R-tier
```

A highly proficient professional can still use M2 assistance. An M4 implementation can still be restricted to R0 actions. A novice under supervision may participate in an R2 route without holding final authority.

## Proficiency levels

The v2 architecture proposes an L1–L5 vocabulary as a candidate shared scale.

A useful interpretation is:

```text
L1 — foundational awareness
L2 — bounded standard production
L3 — independent standard production
L4 — complex and supervisory capability
L5 — system, authority or method leadership
```

A level should always be scoped to:

- Capability;
- jurisdiction;
- language;
- work class;
- implementation mode;
- date and evidence period.

```text
L4 in one Capability
≠ L4 in every domain
```

The scale should not become a universal ranking of people.

## Evidence behind proficiency

A proficiency assessment may consider:

- scenario performance;
- supervised production;
- accepted Contributions;
- defect severity;
- source judgment;
- escalation quality;
- recovery behavior;
- communication;
- review outcomes;
- recent case diversity.

Task volume alone is insufficient.

```text
many completed tasks
≠ demonstrated complex capability
```

## Human–AI collaboration modes

Book 03 defines M0–M5 operationally. Book 02's role is to preserve the controlled meaning and compatibility boundary.

```text
M0 — Human-only
M1 — AI Reference
M2 — AI Assist
M3 — AI Draft, Human Approve
M4 — AI Execute under Governance
M5 — Hybrid Professional Network
```

The mode belongs to a Capability invocation or implementation route, not permanently to a Product, person or model.

```text
Product uses AI
≠ every request is M4
```

## M-mode does not express quality

Higher mode numbers do not mean better results.

M0 may be appropriate for sensitive or reserved work. M2 may outperform M3 when human judgment is primary. M4 may be safe only for tightly bounded deterministic-like actions. M5 may be necessary for complex multi-party delivery.

```text
more automated
≠ more mature
≠ more trustworthy
```

## Risk and control tiers

R0–R4 describe required control before an output is relied upon or an action occurs.

```text
R0 — deterministic and low-consequence control
R1 — standardized production with governed sampling
R2 — complete professional review
R3 — senior or cross-professional review
R4 — qualified final-authority control
```

Risk is tied to the specific transition, not to the entire Product or actor.

```text
Draft email
≠ Send binding email

Calculate fee
≠ Approve payment
```

## Combining the vocabularies

Examples include:

```text
L2 / M2 / R1
AI-assisted standard data preparation with sampled review

L3 / M3 / R2
AI-generated filing draft with complete professional review

L4 / M5 / R3
complex cross-jurisdiction strategy assembled by a hybrid network

qualified authority / M0 / R4
human final approval for a reserved professional action

M4 / R0
bounded automated internal validation with no protected external effect
```

The combination makes the control path explicit without collapsing skill, automation and authority.

## Unknown increases control

When the platform encounters:

- conflicting sources;
- unfamiliar jurisdiction;
- missing evidence;
- model drift;
- external timeout;
- unclear authority;
- sensitive data;

the route may require a higher R-tier, a lower-autonomy M-mode or a more qualified performer.

```text
Unknown
→ reduce autonomy or increase review
```

Unknown should not be converted into a lower confidence score and ignored.

## Vocabulary versioning

Controlled vocabularies need:

- identifier;
- version;
- definitions;
- allowed transitions;
- compatibility rules;
- deprecation policy;
- conformance examples.

A change in the meaning of M3 or R2 could affect Product promises, assessment records and production authorization. It therefore cannot be made casually in publication prose.

## Candidate status

L1–L5 and M0–M5 remain candidate controlled vocabularies unless and until their frozen-baseline mapping and formal activation are complete. R0–R4 likewise requires exact mapping and governance review.

Their use in explanatory text does not activate new Core value sets.

## Constitutional rule

```text
Proficiency describes demonstrated capability.
M-mode describes human–AI composition.
R-tier describes required control.

None creates professional qualification,
production authorization or action authority by itself.
```
