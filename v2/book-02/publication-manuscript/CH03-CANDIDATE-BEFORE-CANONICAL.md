# CH03 — Candidate Before Canonical

## The platform must preserve uncertainty without becoming unusable

Professional systems constantly receive incomplete, conflicting and provisional information.

A customer states a new company address. A provider says an application was filed. An AI model extracts a deadline. A contributor proposes that two organizations are the same entity. A public page reports a fee. A document looks official but is several years old.

The dangerous shortcut is to treat every useful observation as a fact.

Book 02 establishes a different progression:

```text
Observation
→ Candidate
→ Review or Validation
→ Accepted Record or Outcome
→ Formal-state Mutation where authorized
```

Each transition requires its own authority and evidence.

## The permanent distinctions

```text
Observation ≠ fact
Candidate ≠ approved record
AI output ≠ professional decision
Contribution ≠ accepted Outcome
Accepted Outcome ≠ formal-state mutation
Provider Return ≠ accepted truth
Certification ≠ legal qualification
Projection ≠ ownership transfer
```

These are not defensive disclaimers. They are structural requirements.

## Observation

An Observation records that something was encountered or reported.

Examples include:

- an email stating that a certificate was issued;
- a website displaying a new fee;
- an uploaded company registration;
- a model detecting a possible owner match;
- a courier record showing delivery;
- a user reporting that a mark is in use.

An Observation should preserve source, actor, time, method and purpose. It does not yet settle the formal meaning.

## Candidate

A Candidate is a structured proposition suitable for review.

Examples include:

- `DeadlineCandidate` derived from an official event;
- `EntityResolutionCandidate` linking two names;
- a proposed applicant address;
- a candidate Knowledge Claim;
- a candidate trademark status;
- a candidate Work Package route.

Candidates can be compared, scored, rejected, superseded or left unresolved. That flexibility is precisely why they must not be written directly into formal state.

## Validation and Review

Validation asks whether a record satisfies defined structural or authoritative requirements.

Review asks whether a qualified actor accepts the object for a declared purpose.

The same item may be accepted for one purpose and rejected for another.

```text
accepted for triage
≠ accepted for filing
≠ accepted as legal conclusion
```

A low-resolution image may be sufficient to identify a trademark but insufficient for an official filing. A provider email may justify a follow-up but not a registered-status update. A customer statement may support intake while still requiring official identity verification.

## Outcome

An Outcome is an accepted result for a declared purpose, potentially assembled from several Contributions.

It must preserve:

- purpose;
- input and source versions;
- contributing actors;
- review disposition;
- limitations;
- evidence;
- authority boundary.

An Outcome can be decision-ready without becoming formal business truth.

For example, a filing package may be accepted as ready for customer approval. It has not yet been submitted and does not create an application number.

## Formal-state mutation

Formal-state mutation occurs only through the applicable Owning Service and governed authority path.

```text
Accepted Outcome
→ specific Approval
→ governed Apply where required
→ external or internal Evidence
→ Owning Service validation
→ formal-state transition
```

This separation prevents a workflow engine, AI model, user interface or provider message from silently rewriting authoritative state.

## Unknown is not a defect in the model

Some Candidates cannot immediately be accepted or rejected.

The system may have conflicting sources, missing evidence or an external timeout. In those cases, `Unknown` is the truthful state.

```text
Unknown
≠ Failure
≠ Success
≠ Permission to Guess
```

Unknown should trigger clarification, additional evidence, increased review, reduced autonomy or reconciliation.

## Correction and supersession

Canonical does not mean immutable forever. It means accepted under an identified authority and version.

When new evidence arrives, the system should not overwrite history. It should preserve:

- prior record;
- new Candidate;
- correction reason;
- approving authority;
- effective time;
- affected projections and decisions.

```text
Correction
≠ historical erasure
```

## Example: provider reports filing

A provider returns an email saying “filed successfully.”

The correct semantic path is:

```text
Provider Return received
→ filing-status Candidate
→ Evidence Contract check
→ official receipt or authoritative reference validation
→ accepted filing Outcome
→ Matter Owning Service updates formal state
```

If the provider message lacks the official number or receipt, the system should preserve the Return and mark the formal result as pending or Unknown, not filed.

## Example: AI entity resolution

AI suggests that `ABC Trading Ltd.` and `ABC Trading Limited` are the same owner.

That output should become an `EntityResolutionCandidate` with:

- matched names;
- addresses;
- registration numbers if available;
- source records;
- confidence and conflicts;
- purpose of the resolution.

It may be sufficient for research grouping but insufficient for transfer documents or applicant correction.

## Cross-book boundary

Book 03 governs how Candidates move through Review, Approval, Apply and Reconciliation.

Book 05 uses the pattern for applicants, filings, certificates, deadlines, maintenance and transactions.

Book 06 uses it for content claims, marketplace records, buyer requests, leads and capability evidence.

Knowledge systems use it for Sources, Claims and Versions.

Book 02 keeps the semantic progression stable across all of them.

## Constitutional rule

```text
No observation, AI output, Contribution,
Provider Return or projected view becomes canonical
merely because it is convenient, confident or visible.
```

Candidate-before-Canonical allows MarkOrbit to move quickly without pretending that uncertainty has disappeared.
