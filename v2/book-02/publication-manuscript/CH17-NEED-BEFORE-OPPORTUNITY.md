# CH17 — Need Before Opportunity

## Demand should be understood before it is commercialized

A professional platform often sees demand through sales records: leads, inquiries, opportunities, quotes and orders.

That view is incomplete.

A person or organization may require an outcome before the platform knows:

- which Product should serve it;
- whether a commercial route exists;
- which professional authority is required;
- whether the request is even safe or feasible;
- whether the user should buy anything at all.

Book 02 therefore preserves a distinction between a required outcome and a potential value route:

```text
Need
≠ Opportunity
≠ Order
≠ Matter
≠ Capability Request
```

Need is a candidate shared semantic. Until formally admitted, Products may implement it as a versioned profile over existing intake, request or opportunity semantics.

## What a Need represents

Need answers:

> What outcome is required, for whom, in what context and under what constraints, before a delivery or commercial route is selected?

A proposed Need record may include:

- requesting actor or represented organization;
- affected customer, owner, applicant or audience;
- desired outcome;
- jurisdiction and language;
- relevant assets or Matters;
- urgency and deadline;
- known facts;
- assumptions and Unknowns;
- budget or commercial constraints;
- confidentiality and data restrictions;
- professional-authority requirements;
- success conditions;
- unacceptable outcomes;
- current discovery and validation state.

The Need should be outcome-oriented rather than framed as a preselected product purchase.

```text
“I need a trademark filing”
may actually mean
“I need a defensible protection route for this brand.”
```

The eventual answer may be filing, searching first, changing scope, delaying, using another right, or concluding that no safe route is currently available.

## Need is not a sales lead

A sales lead indicates potential commercial interest. A Need records an outcome requirement.

The difference matters because:

- a public-data signal may suggest a Need without identifying a customer;
- a customer may have a Need but no intention to buy immediately;
- several Products may compete or cooperate to address the same Need;
- the safest response may be guidance rather than a paid service;
- the Need may remain unresolved after an Opportunity is closed.

```text
Need exists
≠ customer consented to sales contact
≠ Opportunity should be created
```

Lite may detect that a trademark appears close to a maintenance deadline. That can form a `Need Candidate` or service signal. It does not establish that the owner is a Customer, that the deadline calculation is accepted, or that marketing contact is authorized.

## Need Candidate before accepted Need

The Candidate-before-Canonical rule applies.

Possible sources include:

- customer statement;
- professional observation;
- public-data event;
- AI inference;
- partner referral;
- existing Matter event;
- compliance or risk trigger.

These sources have different authority.

```text
public-data inference
≠ customer-confirmed Need

customer statement
≠ professionally validated route
```

A Need Candidate should preserve source, purpose, Evidence, confidence or epistemic status, and the conditions required for acceptance.

## Need and Capability Request

Need states the outcome problem. Capability Request asks the execution system to invoke a particular Capability under governed context.

```text
Need
→ route analysis
→ selected Capability or Product path
→ Capability Request
```

A single Need may generate several Capability Requests, such as:

- entity verification;
- trademark search;
- classification planning;
- document readiness;
- provider selection;
- filing preparation.

Conversely, one Capability Request may support several Needs where purpose, authority and reuse conditions permit.

```text
Need
≠ executable instruction
```

Book 03 governs Capability Request validation and execution. Book 02 preserves the shared semantic distinction.

## Need and Guide

Guide may help a user articulate the Need by:

- asking minimal clarifying questions;
- identifying missing facts;
- separating legal necessity from commercial preference;
- comparing routes;
- explaining constraints and costs;
- marking Unknowns;
- recommending professional review.

But:

```text
Guide conversation
≠ accepted Need automatically
≠ customer instruction
```

A conversational statement must be converted into an attributable, versioned record before consequential execution relies on it.

## Need and Opportunity

An Opportunity is a potential route by which value may be created from a Need, Capability, asset, audience, provider supply or capacity.

A Need may produce:

- no Opportunity;
- one direct service Opportunity;
- several competing Opportunities;
- a self-service route;
- a partner referral route;
- a content or education route;
- a deferred route;
- a `No Safe Route` result.

```text
Need validated
≠ commercial Opportunity accepted
```

Commercial attractiveness must not rewrite the Need.

For example, a higher-priced multi-country filing package may be commercially attractive, but the Need may only require one mandatory jurisdiction and one optional expansion route. The system should preserve that difference.

## Need versioning

Needs change when:

- the customer clarifies objectives;
- new Evidence appears;
- deadlines move;
- ownership changes;
- budget changes;
- professional advice narrows the route;
- an external event resolves part of the problem.

A material change should create a new version rather than overwrite prior context.

Each version should preserve:

- changed fields;
- change source;
- effective time;
- affected Opportunities, Orders, Capability Requests and Matters;
- whether prior approvals or routes remain valid.

```text
Need changed
≠ existing Opportunity remains valid automatically
```

## Need closure

Need status should not be reduced to `won` or `lost`.

Possible dispositions include:

- clarified;
- validated;
- partially satisfied;
- satisfied;
- deferred;
- redirected;
- withdrawn;
- no safe route;
- unresolved;
- superseded.

A commercial Opportunity may be lost while the Need remains unresolved. An Order may complete only part of the Need. A Need may be satisfied through a route outside MarkOrbit.

## Relationship and consent boundary

Need discovery does not create a customer relationship.

```text
Need detected
≠ Customer created
≠ Relationship transferred
≠ marketing consent granted
```

The source Workplace and applicable privacy or communication policy determine whether the Need may be projected, routed or used for outreach.

## Candidate status and admission test

Need is a strong F3 candidate because several Products require a stable distinction between outcome demand and commercial route.

Formal admission still requires:

```text
frozen-baseline mapping
→ lifecycle and authority analysis
→ overlap test with Request and Opportunity
→ cross-book impact analysis
→ Change Proposal
→ conformance fixtures
→ migration plan
→ Owner approval
```

This chapter explains the intended meaning. It does not activate a new Core root object.

## Constitutional rule

```text
Need records the required outcome before the platform
selects a Product, Opportunity, Order or execution route.

Demand must not be rewritten merely to fit
what the platform currently wants to sell.
```
