# CH31 — Provider and Contributor Must Remain Distinct

## Similar people may carry different contracts

The same individual or Organization may appear in MarkOrbit as a contributor, provider, reviewer, professional authority, delivery partner or relationship owner.

Those roles may overlap in practice. They do not have the same meaning.

```text
Contributor
≠ Provider
≠ Professional Authority
≠ Employee
≠ Reviewer
≠ Delivery Owner
```

The distinction matters because each role carries different responsibility, appointment, disclosure, compensation and evidence requirements.

## Contributor is a bounded production role

Contributor answers:

> Who submitted a defined piece of work, analysis, verification, artifact or professional input under a governed Assignment or collaboration context?

A Contributor may be:

- a Person;
- a Workplace;
- an external specialist;
- an AI implementation;
- a deterministic system;
- a provider professional acting in a bounded contribution role.

A Contribution should preserve:

- contributor identity;
- represented Organization or Workplace;
- Work Package and Assignment;
- scope;
- input and output versions;
- sources and Evidence;
- tools and AI involvement;
- limitations and Unknowns;
- compensation reference;
- review and disposition.

```text
Contributor submitted work
≠ Provider accepted external responsibility
```

## Provider is an Organization profile with external responsibility

Provider answers:

> Which Organization has been selected, appointed or accepted to deliver an external professional or operational service under a declared contract and authority boundary?

Provider normally requires:

- Organization identity;
- service and jurisdiction scope;
- eligibility and capacity;
- professional personnel where required;
- conflict and independence checks;
- appointment or engagement;
- fee and commercial terms;
- customer-contact rules;
- document and data custody rules;
- service-level expectations;
- Evidence Return contract;
- complaint, replacement and termination routes.

```text
Provider listed
≠ Provider selected
≠ Provider appointed
≠ Provider accepted
```

Provider should remain a profile over Organization rather than a separate identity universe.

## Provider Organization and provider professional are different

An Organization may provide the commercial and operational service, while a specific professional holds the relevant qualification or authority.

```text
Provider Organization eligible
≠ every employee professionally qualified

Qualified professional employed by Provider
≠ appointed for this Matter
```

The platform should preserve:

- Provider Organization;
- responsible professional;
- qualification and jurisdiction;
- appointment;
- scope;
- supervision;
- substitutions;
- signature or filing authority.

## Contribution does not create external responsibility

A contributor may prepare:

- a translation;
- a classification proposal;
- a search analysis;
- a document checklist;
- a content draft;
- an identity-resolution candidate.

That contribution may be accepted as an input without making the contributor responsible for the complete external service.

```text
Contribution accepted
≠ Contributor becomes Provider
≠ Contributor becomes Delivery Owner
```

The Delivery Owner remains responsible for assembling the complete Outcome and ensuring required reviews, approvals and handoffs occur.

## Provider may also contribute

A provider can submit one or more Contributions, such as:

- local legal advice;
- filing review;
- official submission;
- office-action analysis;
- certified translation;
- status verification.

The records should preserve both roles:

```text
Organization acts as Provider
+ professional submits Contribution
```

This avoids treating the provider's entire service as one opaque result.

## Appointment and Assignment remain separate

```text
Provider Appointment
≠ Assignment
```

Provider Appointment establishes the external service relationship or professional route. Assignment authorizes a bounded Work Package to an actor or implementation.

A Provider may accept an Engagement but still require separate Assignments or instruction packages for each Matter. A contributor may accept an Assignment without any external provider appointment.

## Professional Authority is not implied

A Provider may offer administrative, technical, translation or commercial services that do not require reserved professional authority.

Where professional authority is required, it must be independently verified and bound to the action.

```text
Provider
≠ Professional Authority automatically

Contributor expertise
≠ Professional Qualification
```

Platform ratings, successful history and Certification cannot replace external qualification or appointment requirements.

## Relationship ownership remains separate

Providers and contributors may receive customer context or communicate directly for delivery.

```text
Direct Contact Permission
≠ Relationship Ownership
```

A white-label arrangement may preserve:

```text
Originating Workplace = Relationship Owner
MarkReg or delivery team = Delivery Owner
Local firm = Provider
Qualified agent = Professional Authority
```

The provider must not infer unrestricted marketing or commercial rights from delivery access.

## Compensation differs by role

Contributor compensation may be tied to:

- accepted Work Package;
- milestone;
- review outcome;
- revision policy;
- time or fixed fee;
- reuse rights.

Provider fees may include:

- professional service fee;
- official-fee handling;
- translation;
- courier;
- local tax;
- disbursement.

```text
Contributor Compensation
≠ Provider Invoice
≠ Official Fee
```

Payment and Settlement records should preserve the basis and payee rather than combine all amounts into one service total.

## Evidence expectations differ

Contributor Evidence may include:

- sources;
- work product;
- method;
- assumptions;
- version;
- review status.

Provider Return Evidence may additionally require:

- appointment acceptance;
- external reference;
- official receipt;
- fee receipt;
- professional identity;
- custody or submission proof;
- deviations and unresolved status.

```text
Contribution complete
≠ Provider service complete
```

## Provider recommendation and selection

A provider network may produce a Candidate Route Set or recommendation based on jurisdiction, capability, cost, capacity and past performance.

```text
Recommended
≠ Selected
≠ Appointed
≠ Accepted
```

Selection should consider:

- professional eligibility;
- conflicts;
- customer or Workplace constraints;
- price and currency;
- capacity and deadline;
- communication policy;
- data and document requirements;
- Evidence Return capability;
- recovery route.

## Replacement and continuity

Contributor replacement primarily affects the Work Package and Assignment. Provider replacement may affect:

- external appointment;
- official authority;
- documents and original custody;
- funds;
- deadlines;
- customer communication;
- possible duplicate action;
- formal representation records.

```text
Replace Contributor
≠ Replace Provider
```

A provider transition requires continuity and reconciliation controls beyond ordinary reassignment.

## Conflicts and independence

A contributor may require conflict checks depending on the work. A Provider or professional authority may have stronger legal or professional conflict obligations.

The platform should not apply one generic `conflict_checked` flag to every role.

```text
Conflict Check Passed for Contribution
≠ Provider Conflict Cleared
≠ Professional Independence Established
```

## AI can be Contributor, not Provider

AI may submit a bounded Contribution under a governed implementation profile.

It cannot become the accountable Provider Organization, hold a professional licence, accept contractual responsibility or receive a human professional appointment.

```text
AI Contribution
≠ AI Provider
≠ AI Professional Authority
```

Accountability remains with the relevant Workplace, Delivery Owner, reviewer, approver, Provider and Owning Service.

## Public representation must be accurate

Customer-facing material should accurately distinguish:

- platform or Product operator;
- Relationship Owner;
- Delivery Owner;
- external Provider;
- responsible professional;
- contributor or AI assistance.

```text
Delivered through MarkOrbit
≠ every service performed by MarkOrbit itself
```

White-label presentation may simplify the experience but must not misrepresent professional responsibility or contracting parties.

## Candidate status

Provider remains an Organization profile. Contributor remains a contextual role connected to Assignment or Engagement. Provider Appointment may be a profile or candidate contract specialization.

This chapter does not create new permanent Person or Organization types or activate a universal Provider Appointment object.

## Constitutional rule

```text
Contributor supplies bounded work.
Provider accepts an external service responsibility.
Professional Authority comes from valid qualification and appointment.
Delivery Owner coordinates the complete route.
Relationship Owner preserves the customer relationship.

Overlap of actors never erases the distinctions among these contracts.
```
