## Main decisions

### 1. Identity is contextual rather than account-shaped

```text
Person
≠ Organization
≠ Workplace
≠ Membership
```

A login, Membership or organizational association does not create task authority, professional qualification or Provider appointment.

### 2. Customer and Contact remain distinct

```text
Customer
≠ Applicant
≠ Owner
≠ Payer
≠ Instructor
≠ Signatory
```

Contact availability does not create unrestricted communication permission. Relationship Owner preserves stewardship but does not own the customer as a person or acquire formal-state authority.

### 3. Product activation is Workplace-scoped

```text
Product
≠ Product Installation
≠ Entitlement
≠ Assignment
≠ Approval
```

Installation binds a Product to one Workplace and policy context. Payment, installation or Capability availability does not authorize professional or protected action.

### 4. References preserve ownership boundaries

```text
Reference
≠ duplicated authoritative record
≠ permission to mutate source state
```

Identifiers remain namespace-bound. Source Records preserve observed values. Resolution Candidates preserve uncertainty. Canonical identity requires accepted evidence and purpose-specific authority.

### 5. Identity resolution remains reversible

A probable match may be sufficient for retrieval or analytics while remaining insufficient for filing, transfer, payment or other consequential use.

```text
similarity
≠ verified identity

merged view
≠ source history destroyed
```
