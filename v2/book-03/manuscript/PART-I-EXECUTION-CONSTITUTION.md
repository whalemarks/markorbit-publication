# Part I — Execution Constitution and Request Model

## Chapter 01 — Work is a governed transition

A professional operating system cannot treat work as a sequence of button clicks. Every meaningful action changes context, creates reliance or moves a business object toward a new state.

Execution therefore records:

- who initiated the work;
- which Workplace and Product context applies;
- what Capability is requested;
- which authority permits the action;
- what inputs and versions are used;
- what outcome is expected;
- what review is required;
- how failure and uncertainty are represented;
- what evidence must remain afterward.

## Chapter 02 — Execution is not ownership

Execution coordinates work but does not acquire customer relationships, commercial sovereignty or formal record ownership.

```text
orchestration ≠ business ownership
execution state ≠ formal business truth
technical access ≠ professional authority
completed task ≠ accepted outcome
```

The applicable Owning Service remains responsible for formal mutation. The relevant Workplace remains responsible for its business context.

## Chapter 03 — Capability Request

A Capability Request is the entry contract for governed work.

It binds:

- Capability identifier and version;
- requester and represented Workplace;
- Product Installation;
- purpose and expected outcome;
- input references and versions;
- risk level;
- entitlement;
- disclosure scope;
- review policy;
- allowed implementation bindings;
- expiry and cancellation policy.

A vague prompt is not sufficient for high-impact work.

## Chapter 04 — Request validation

Before work begins, Execution validates:

- required inputs exist;
- the requester has authority;
- the Product Installation enables the Capability;
- the request fits the Workplace data scope;
- the requested action is not prohibited;
- dependencies and versions are available;
- risk and review requirements are resolved.

Invalid requests fail before assignment rather than becoming hidden exceptions later.

## Chapter 05 — Eligibility

Eligibility determines who or what may perform the work.

The system may evaluate:

```text
Capability Certification
Production Authorization
Professional Qualification
Jurisdiction
Language
Conflict
Capacity
Quality history
Data-access scope
Commercial terms
```

Eligibility is task-specific. A person qualified for one jurisdiction or one production level is not automatically eligible for another.

## Chapter 06 — Implementation binding

A Capability may be fulfilled by:

- deterministic software;
- AI under a defined collaboration mode;
- a Workplace member;
- a certified contributor;
- an expert reviewer;
- a MarkReg internal team;
- an MGSN provider;
- a hybrid combination.

The calling Product requests an outcome. Execution selects or validates the implementation binding under policy.

## Chapter 07 — Preview before Apply

Actions that may spend funds, expose protected information, appoint a provider, communicate externally or change formal state require a previewable plan.

A Preview should show:

- intended action;
- affected objects;
- selected provider or contributor;
- data to be disclosed;
- expected cost;
- review path;
- irreversible consequences;
- uncertainty and alternatives.

Approval applies to the previewed version, not to an unspecified future mutation.

## Chapter 08 — Specific approval

Approval must identify:

- approver;
- authority represented;
- exact version approved;
- scope;
- time;
- conditions;
- whether delegation is allowed.

Silence, prior approval of a similar case or general platform consent does not equal approval of a protected action.

## Chapter 09 — Execution state model

At minimum, work may move through:

```text
Draft
Validated
Awaiting Eligibility
Ready
Assigned
In Progress
Submitted
Under Review
Revision Required
Accepted
Apply Pending
Applied
Completed
Cancelled
Failed
Unknown
Reconciliation Required
```

Products may present simpler views, but the underlying distinctions must remain.

## Chapter 10 — Unknown is first-class

External systems, providers and payments do not always return clear outcomes.

A timeout after submission may mean:

- nothing was sent;
- the request was accepted but the response was lost;
- the action was duplicated;
- the official system remains processing.

Execution must preserve Unknown and initiate reconciliation. Guessing success or failure can create duplicate filings, payments or instructions.

## Chapter 11 — Cancellation and revocation

Cancellation is not always immediate.

Execution must determine:

- whether work has started;
- whether external action has occurred;
- whether compensation is earned;
- whether disclosed access must be revoked;
- whether a provider can still be recalled;
- whether the originating object requires correction.

Revocation ends future authority but does not erase completed evidence.
