# Book 03 v2
# MarkOrbit Execution System — Governed Human–AI Work

## Purpose

Execution governs how Capabilities become controlled work. It coordinates people, AI, deterministic tools, Products, Owning Services and external Providers without confusing orchestration with formal-state authority.

Execution also governs how runtime Outcomes produce learning candidates without silently changing Capability canon.

## Part I — Execution constitution

### Chapter 01 — Work is a governed state transition

Every meaningful action has an initiator, context, authority, input, expected Outcome, review policy, failure model and Evidence trail.

### Chapter 02 — Preview before Apply

Actions that may change formal state, expose protected data, spend funds, appoint Providers or communicate externally require a previewable plan and a governed approval path.

Capability Composition and Context Compilation must also be previewable where they materially affect authority, privacy, cost or professional judgment.

### Chapter 03 — Unknown is a first-class state

Timeout, duplicate request, external silence, unresolved Capability conflict and ambiguous Provider response must not be simplified into success or failure. Unknown Outcome requires reconciliation.

## Part II — Capability invocation and composition

### Chapter 04 — Capability Request

A Capability Request binds:

- requested Capability and version;
- Workplace and Product context;
- requester;
- purpose;
- input contract;
- risk level;
- entitlement;
- review policy;
- permitted implementation bindings;
- whether composition is allowed or required.

### Chapter 05 — Eligibility

Before composition, assignment or execution, the system evaluates certification, production authorization, Professional Qualification, jurisdiction, Conflict, language, capacity, data-access scope and Product entitlement.

Eligibility may apply separately to a Capability, Skill, implementation binding, Person, Workplace or Provider route.

### Chapter 06 — Capability Composition

Complex tasks may require several Capabilities, but the system must not load every relevant method into one undifferentiated context.

The default composition contract is:

```text
Primary Capability: exactly one
Supporting Capabilities: zero to three by default
Critic Capability: zero or one
Capability Budget
Decision-node responsibilities
Conflict Set
Resolution Rationale
Unified Action Plan
```

The Primary Capability owns the output structure and action ordering. Supporting Capabilities answer bounded questions. The Critic Capability examines a defined risk.

### Chapter 07 — Capability Budget

A Capability Budget limits composition by relevance, necessity, evidence quality, historical usefulness, conflict cost, privacy cost and context cost.

The central question is not whether another Skill is related. It is whether excluding it would materially weaken the decision.

A Budget may constrain:

- number of Capabilities;
- Context size;
- model cost;
- maximum unresolved conflicts;
- professional review depth;
- allowed external tools;
- maximum disclosure scope.

### Chapter 08 — Capability Conflict and resolution

When Source Capabilities or professional methods disagree, Execution records:

- issue;
- positions and provenance;
- assumptions;
- current context;
- material trade-offs;
- selected resolution;
- reviewer or policy basis;
- residual uncertainty.

Execution must not silently average materially conflicting methods into generic advice.

### Chapter 09 — Context Compiler

The Context Compiler creates a purpose-limited execution context from authorized Data, Knowledge, Memory, Cases, Principles, preferences and Matter facts.

It performs:

- relevance selection;
- version selection;
- permission checks;
- privacy and de-identification controls;
- token and Capability budgeting;
- provenance preservation;
- conflict surfacing;
- omission of irrelevant material.

```text
Context Compilation
≠ unrestricted retrieval
≠ ingestion of the user’s entire history
≠ authority expansion
```

### Chapter 10 — Implementation binding

A Capability may be implemented by AI, deterministic software, a certified Contributor, an internal MarkReg team, an expert reviewer or an MGSN Provider.

Binding is selected under policy, Capability version, risk, authority and Evidence requirements—not assumed by the calling Product.

A Capability Composition may use several bindings, but each binding retains a bounded responsibility.

## Part III — Workflow and collaboration

### Chapter 11 — Workflow, Step and Work Package

Workflow coordinates a goal. WorkPackage defines a bounded contribution unit. Step is an executable stage. The three must not be collapsed.

Capability Composition defines why and how Capabilities cooperate; Workflow defines how the authorized work progresses.

### Chapter 12 — Assignment

Assignments specify recipient, deadline, compensation basis, visible data, allowed actions, review requirement, relevant Capability and Skill versions, and revocation policy.

### Chapter 13 — Contribution

A Contribution records what was submitted, by whom, under which Capability, Skill and authority, using what Evidence and tools.

It remains pending until reviewed or accepted by policy. It may later support a Reflection or Case Candidate, but Contribution is not itself proof that a Capability should change.

### Chapter 14 — Multi-professional work

Some Outcomes require distinct perspectives: visual design, trademark continuity, commercial fit, legal qualification or jurisdiction practice.

Execution supports parallel Contributions and typed synthesis rather than undifferentiated voting. One professional cannot inherit another’s authority merely because their work appears in the same composition.

## Part IV — Human–AI collaboration

### Chapter 15 — M0–M5 modes

Execution enforces the selected collaboration mode:

- M0 human-only;
- M1 AI reference;
- M2 AI assist;
- M3 AI draft, human approve;
- M4 AI execute under governance;
- M5 hybrid professional network.

The mode applies to each relevant Step or binding, not automatically to the entire Capability Composition.

### Chapter 16 — AI disclosure and responsibility

AI-generated material must retain model provenance, instruction reference where appropriate, source citations, uncertainty, relevant Capability and Skill versions, and human disposition.

Approval cannot be inferred from silence. AI may assist Conflict analysis but cannot create Professional Qualification, customer consent or Protected-action Authority.

### Chapter 17 — Dynamic assistance by Capability Profile

The same Capability may expose more guidance and stronger review for early-stage users and more autonomy for users with accepted Evidence.

Assistance level is governed policy, not merely UI preference or an AI-generated score.

A Capability Profile may inform guidance but cannot replace current Eligibility, Professional Qualification or Matter-specific Authority.

### Chapter 18 — Lens, Council and MO modes

Products may expose three user experiences:

- Lens Mode — one Source Capability provides a distinctive perspective;
- Council Mode — several Source Capabilities expose different assumptions and positions;
- MO Mode — governed composition produces one unified action plan.

These are Product experience modes. Execution still applies the same authority, provenance, privacy and review controls.

## Part V — Simulation and assessment

### Chapter 19 — Simulation Workplace

Simulation uses isolated customers, trademarks, documents, communications, deadlines and events. It prohibits external submission, real payment and accidental production mutation.

### Chapter 20 — Scenario engine

Cases support hidden facts, staged document release, customer objections, incorrect Evidence, deadline pressure, Capability conflicts and branching consequences.

### Chapter 21 — Examiner interaction

AI and human assessors may play customer, supervisor or external actor roles. Their interactions are part of the assessment record.

### Chapter 22 — Assessment Evidence

Assessment considers facts, rules, risk, communication, service design, MarkOrbit operation, human–AI judgment and delivery quality—not only the final answer.

Assessment Evidence may update a Capability Profile or Certification record under policy. It does not create Professional Qualification.

### Chapter 23 — From simulation to production

```text
simulation
→ shadow task
→ supervised production
→ bounded independent production
→ review authority
```

Every transition requires Evidence, Eligibility and the applicable production or professional authorization.

## Part VI — Risk, review and protected action

### Chapter 24 — R0–R4 execution risk

- R0 automated;
- R1 sampled review;
- R2 single professional review;
- R3 cross-professional review;
- R4 legally qualified or expert final review.

Composition does not lower the highest applicable risk or authority requirement.

### Chapter 25 — Professional Authority

Execution prevents an operationally capable but legally unqualified actor from performing reserved actions. Certification, Source Capability or Verified MO Capability never bypasses jurisdictional professional requirements.

### Chapter 26 — External Protected Action

Official filing, binding legal communication, Provider appointment, payment custody and other Protected Actions require explicit authority and idempotent execution boundaries.

A unified MO action plan is still only a plan until the relevant approvals exist.

## Part VII — Return, Reflection and Capability learning

### Chapter 27 — Outcome and Return

Execution distinguishes:

- generated output;
- submitted Contribution;
- reviewed Outcome;
- accepted Return;
- formal-state update.

The origin validates the Return before accepting it as Product or business state.

### Chapter 28 — Session Receipt

A Session Receipt is the typed Return for one Capability invocation or composed session.

It may include:

- session and invocation references;
- Product and Workplace context;
- Capability, Skill and implementation versions;
- input-scope reference;
- composition and Conflict summary;
- Outcome summary;
- user disposition and modifications;
- Evidence references;
- privacy and rights state;
- Reflection Candidate reference.

```text
Session Receipt
≠ complete raw conversation
≠ approved Reflection
≠ automatic Capability upgrade
```

### Chapter 29 — Reflection Candidate

A Reflection Candidate captures a possible learning from real work after privacy, purpose and rights controls.

Execution may propose:

- what problem occurred;
- which Capability and Skill versions were used;
- what the user changed or adopted;
- what Outcome occurred;
- what Evidence exists;
- what lesson or Principle might be reusable;
- what uncertainty remains.

The user, Workplace or authorized reviewer may confirm, revise, reject or restrict it.

### Chapter 30 — Case and Principle candidate formation

Accepted Reflection Candidates may contribute to Case Candidates and Principle Candidates.

Execution links evidence and provenance but does not decide that one Case proves a general Principle.

Candidate promotion requires the Capability governance process defined by Core and the relevant Capability registry.

### Chapter 31 — Capability learning handoff

The governed path is:

```text
Outcome
→ Session Receipt
→ Reflection Candidate
→ de-identification and rights review
→ Case / Principle Candidate
→ Capability Change Proposal
```

Execution produces the evidence package and handoff. It does not publish the new Capability version by itself.

## Part VIII — Reliability and evidence

### Chapter 32 — Retry and duplicate safety

Retries must preserve idempotency keys, prior attempts, external references, Capability and Skill versions, and reconciliation state.

### Chapter 33 — Failure and recovery

Failures are classified as validation, authorization, dependency, Provider, external system, Data, review, composition, conflict, privacy or commercial failures, with defined recovery routes.

### Chapter 34 — Audit and event Evidence

Execution produces immutable Evidence of Requests, compositions, context compilation, Assignments, decisions, modifications, approvals, Returns, settlement triggers, Reflection proposals and final Outcomes.

### Chapter 35 — Learning without silent canonization

Execution Outcomes may generate learning candidates. They enter Data, Knowledge or Capability improvement only after:

- rights checks;
- privacy and de-identification review;
- source and version preservation;
- quality validation;
- repeated or otherwise sufficient Evidence;
- human or governed policy review;
- explicit Change Proposal and release decision.

```text
Real work can improve Capability.
Real work cannot silently rewrite Capability.
```

## Ten-year lock

Execution is the platform’s Trust engine and the bridge between Capability runtime and Capability evolution.

Its success is measured not by automation rate or volume of stored conversation, but by reliable Outcomes, accountable decisions, reversible mistakes, protected customers, attributable Evidence and governed learning.
