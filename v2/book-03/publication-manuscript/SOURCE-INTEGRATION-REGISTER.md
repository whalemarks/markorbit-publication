## 2. Principal conflicts and reconciliation rules

### Workflow versus Guide

Guide helps a user understand, decide and prepare. Workflow coordinates a goal after or around that preparation. Guide output may become a Capability Request or Workflow input, but Guide itself is not the execution authority.

### Workflow versus Work Package

Workflow coordinates the end-to-end goal. Work Package defines a bounded outcome that may be assigned or contributed. Step is an executable stage. The reconstruction will retain all three.

### Recommendation versus Approval

AI, routing policy or a professional may recommend. Approval is a specific authority-bearing disposition of an exact version. Recommendation ranking cannot mutate approval state.

### Review versus Approval

Review produces findings and disposition candidates. Approval authorizes a defined next boundary. The same person may perform both roles only when policy permits and the two records remain distinct.

### Apply versus External Protected Action

Apply is the controlled transition from an approved preparation state toward effect. An External Protected Action is a class of consequential external action requiring additional authority and reliability controls. Not every Apply is external; not every external action has the same professional or legal consequences.

### Execution state versus formal state

Execution owns coordination evidence about attempts, assignments, reviews and returns. The relevant Owning Service owns Customer, Order, Matter, Payment, Document or other formal business state. Official truth remains grounded in authoritative external evidence.
