# CH13 — Multi-professional Contributions Need Typed Synthesis

## 1. Professional complexity does not disappear when many people participate

Complex work often requires more than one discipline. Trademark matters may require legal reasoning, visual comparison, commercial judgment, language review, evidence assessment, provider knowledge and customer communication. Brand commercialization may add design, packaging, content, licensing, pricing and transaction expertise.

A weak system responds by placing everyone in one discussion and asking for consensus. MarkOrbit requires a stronger model.

```text
Many Participants
≠ One Undifferentiated Opinion
```

The system must preserve who contributed what, under which Capability, authority, source and review standard.

## 2. Contributions must remain typed

Examples of Contribution types include:

- factual research;
- jurisdiction interpretation;
- professional legal analysis;
- visual similarity assessment;
- trademark continuity assessment;
- commercial feasibility;
- evidence sufficiency;
- translation and transliteration;
- customer-communication review;
- provider-practice confirmation;
- pricing and delivery assessment;
- privacy and disclosure review.

```text
Visual Assessment
≠ Legal Clearance

Commercial Recommendation
≠ Professional Authority

Provider Practice
≠ Official Rule
```

A typed Contribution makes clear what conclusion it can and cannot support.

## 3. Synthesis is an accountable operation

Synthesis combines accepted Contributions into a coherent decision-ready Outcome.

It should identify:

- the question being answered;
- required disciplines;
- accepted Contributions;
- conflicting findings;
- missing perspectives;
- authority required for resolution;
- customer choices;
- residual Unknowns;
- final recommendation and limitations.

```text
Synthesis
≠ Averaging
≠ Majority Vote
≠ Hiding Disagreement
```

## 4. Contradictions must remain visible

A legal reviewer may consider a route supportable while a commercial reviewer considers it uneconomic. A visual specialist may see strong continuity while a trademark professional identifies a material legal difference. A local provider may report a practical filing preference that differs from a published rule.

The system should record:

```text
Agreement
Partial Agreement
Scope Difference
Source Conflict
Professional Conflict
Commercial Trade-off
Unresolved Conflict
```

The Delivery Owner or qualified authority then determines what must be resolved, disclosed or presented as options.

## 5. Different disciplines may use different evidence standards

A packaging mockup may be sufficient for visual review but not for use evidence. A provider email may be useful for operational planning but insufficient as official authority. A marketplace listing may support a commercial assessment but not prove legal ownership.

```text
Evidence Sufficient for One Review
≠ Evidence Sufficient for Every Review
```

Each Contribution should state the purpose for which its evidence is accepted.

## 6. Professional authority cannot be synthesized into existence

Combining several unqualified opinions does not create a qualified legal judgment.

```text
Multiple Non-authoritative Contributions
≠ Professional Authority
```

Where law or professional regulation reserves the final decision, the qualified actor must remain identifiable and must review the relevant synthesis.

## 7. AI can support synthesis without becoming the final authority

AI may:

- organize Contributions;
- compare terminology;
- detect contradictions;
- map evidence to findings;
- draft option sets;
- identify missing reviews;
- summarize residual risk.

But:

```text
AI-generated Synthesis
≠ Approved Synthesis
≠ Professional Final Judgment
```

The system must preserve model provenance and human disposition.

## 8. Parallel work requires version discipline

Contributors may work simultaneously. Their Contributions must identify the exact input version.

```text
Contribution A on Package v2
+ Contribution B on Package v3
≠ Automatically Compatible
```

Before synthesis, Execution must reconcile versions or request rework.

## 9. The synthesis record

A Synthesis Record should contain:

- originating Work Package or Workflow;
- synthesis owner;
- included Contribution identifiers;
- excluded Contributions and reasons;
- version compatibility;
- conflicts;
- resolved decisions;
- unresolved questions;
- required Approval;
- expiry conditions;
- downstream use.

## 10. Multi-professional review must protect minority findings

A critical finding should not disappear because most reviewers did not share it.

```text
Minority Finding
≠ Irrelevant Finding
```

High-severity findings may trigger escalation even when other Contributions are positive.

## 11. Synthesis should produce options when values conflict

Some conflicts are not factual errors but legitimate trade-offs.

Example:

```text
Option A — narrower scope, lower risk, lower cost
Option B — broader scope, higher cost, professional review required
Option C — delay pending evidence
Option D — no safe route yet
```

This preserves customer agency without presenting every option as equally recommended.

## 12. One customer-facing answer may have many internal sources

The user experience may present one coherent explanation, but the audit trail must retain the internal Contributions, reviewers and authority boundaries.

```text
One Customer Experience
≠ One Hidden Actor
```

## 13. Synthesis does not own the underlying records

Execution coordinates synthesis. It does not acquire ownership of Customer, Matter, Payment, official status or professional relationships.

```text
Synthesis Outcome
≠ Formal-state Mutation
```

The Owning Service validates any resulting formal update.

## 14. Failure modes

Common failures include:

- collapsing disciplines into one score;
- using majority vote for reserved professional judgment;
- mixing versions;
- hiding source conflicts;
- treating Provider practice as official rule;
- allowing commercial preference to silence legal risk;
- allowing legal caution to erase legitimate customer choices;
- presenting AI summary as original evidence.

## 15. The governing model

```text
Typed Contributions
→ Version Reconciliation
→ Conflict Mapping
→ Authority-aware Synthesis
→ Decision-ready Options
→ Required Review and Approval
→ Outcome
```

Multi-professional work becomes reliable only when difference is preserved long enough to be governed.
