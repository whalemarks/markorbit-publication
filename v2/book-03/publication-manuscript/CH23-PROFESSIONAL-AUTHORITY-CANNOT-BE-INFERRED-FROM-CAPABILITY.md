# CH23 — Professional Authority Cannot Be Inferred from Capability

## 1. Capability and authority solve different problems

A person, AI system or organization may be capable of producing excellent work without possessing the authority to make the final professional decision or perform a reserved action.

```text
Capability
→ Can this actor perform the work competently?

Professional Authority
→ May this actor make or perform the reserved decision or action?
```

The distinction is foundational:

```text
Capability
≠ Certification
≠ Production Authorization
≠ Professional Qualification
≠ Appointment
≠ Professional Authority
```

MarkOrbit must never infer legal or professional authority from quality scores, platform status, historical performance or technical access.

## 2. Professional Authority is contextual

Professional Authority may depend on:

- profession;
- jurisdiction;
- current qualification;
- organization;
- appointment;
- client or customer relationship;
- matter scope;
- conflict clearance;
- supervision requirement;
- reserved-action rule;
- signature or filing method;
- effective date.

```text
Qualified Somewhere
≠ Authorized Here
```

A lawyer qualified in one jurisdiction may not be authorized to represent a customer in another. A local trademark agent may be authorized to file but not to give broader legal advice. A corporate signatory may approve commercial terms but not provide professional legal approval.

## 3. Capability Evidence cannot create a professional license

Capability Evidence may show that an actor:

- identifies issues accurately;
- uses sources well;
- produces reliable drafts;
- escalates appropriately;
- communicates clearly;
- performs consistently.

That evidence can support Certification and Production Authorization, but it cannot create:

- a law license;
- a trademark-agent registration;
- a regulated professional status;
- a customer appointment;
- a statutory signature authority.

```text
High Assessment Score
≠ Professional Qualification
```

## 4. Certification is a platform or program decision

Certification confirms that a person or system met a defined assessment standard.

It should state:

- issuer;
- Capability;
- jurisdiction or scope;
- assessment version;
- M-mode;
- R-tier limit;
- validity period;
- limitations;
- renewal rule.

Certification may be useful evidence, but it remains different from an external professional credential.

```text
MarkOrbit Certified
≠ Government Licensed
```

## 5. Production Authorization is permission within a governed system

Production Authorization allows an actor to perform defined real work within MarkOrbit or a participating Workplace.

It may permit:

- document preparation;
- source verification;
- standardized analysis;
- supervised customer communication;
- bounded independent production;
- Review of specified work.

It does not automatically permit:

- final legal approval;
- customer representation;
- official submission;
- provider appointment;
- signature;
- formal-state mutation.

```text
Production Authorized
≠ Professionally Authorized
```

## 6. Professional Qualification must be verified as current

A qualification record should identify:

- issuing authority;
- credential type;
- credential number where appropriate;
- jurisdiction;
- status;
- effective and expiry dates;
- restrictions;
- source of verification;
- last verification time;
- disciplinary or suspension status where lawfully available.

```text
Credential Uploaded
≠ Credential Verified
≠ Credential Current
```

The system should recheck before high-impact assignment and Apply.

## 7. Appointment connects qualification to a specific matter

A professionally qualified provider may still need formal appointment or engagement before acting.

The chain may be:

```text
Qualified Provider
→ Conflict Check
→ Commercial Selection
→ Customer or Workplace Appointment
→ Provider Acceptance
→ Matter-specific Instruction
→ Professional Action
```

Continue to freeze:

```text
Provider Recommended
≠ Provider Appointed

Provider Appointed
≠ Provider Accepted

Provider Accepted
≠ Action Authorized
```

## 8. Professional Authority may be non-delegable

Some decisions or acts must be performed personally by the qualified or appointed professional.

Examples may include:

- signing a declaration;
- submitting under professional credentials;
- issuing a formal legal opinion;
- certifying a document;
- approving a reserved legal strategy.

The system must distinguish:

```text
Preparation Delegable
Review Delegable
Approval Delegable
Apply Delegable
Non-delegable Final Authority
```

```text
Can Supervise
≠ Can Delegate Every Reserved Act
```

## 9. Supervision does not automatically transfer authority

A trainee or Contributor may work under supervision, but the supervisor's authority does not silently pass to the performer.

The supervisor must know:

- what was produced;
- which sources were used;
- what changed;
- which risks remain;
- whether the work is within the supervised scope;
- what final act requires the supervisor personally.

```text
Under Supervision
≠ Holds Supervisor Authority
```

## 10. Professional Review and Professional Authority remain distinct

A qualified professional may review an artifact without approving the next external action.

Conversely, a professional authority may approve an action only after relying on Reviews by others.

```text
Professional Review
≠ Final Professional Approval
≠ External Apply
```

Records should state whether the professional acted as:

- contributor;
- reviewer;
- senior reviewer;
- final approver;
- signatory;
- submitting representative;
- provider organization contact.

## 11. Customer authority is not professional authority

The customer may decide objectives, budget and acceptable business risk. The customer does not necessarily have authority to waive professional or legal requirements.

```text
Customer Instructs
≠ Professional Requirement Waived
```

Similarly, a professional authority cannot decide the customer's commercial priorities without authorization.

```text
Professional Authority
≠ Customer Commercial Authority
```

## 12. Workplace role is not external qualification

A role such as `Senior Trademark Specialist`, `Administrator` or `Delivery Owner` may carry internal permissions. It does not establish external professional status.

```text
Workplace Role
≠ Jurisdictional Professional Qualification
```

Internal role design must not use names that mislead customers about regulated authority.

## 13. AI cannot hold professional authority by performance

AI may produce high-quality analysis, but it does not become the licensed or appointed professional of record merely because its output is accurate.

```text
AI Capability
≠ Professional Qualification
≠ Professional Appointment
≠ Professional Responsibility
```

At R4, AI can support preparation, source comparison, conflict detection and drafting. The qualified authority remains the identifiable final actor.

## 14. Provider organizations and individual professionals must be distinguished

A Provider Organization may contract for services, but a specific individual may hold the professional credential or signature authority.

The system should record:

- Provider Organization;
- responsible professional;
- credential;
- jurisdiction;
- appointment;
- reviewer or signatory role;
- substitution rule;
- organization accountability.

```text
Firm Appointed
≠ Every Firm Member Authorized
```

## 15. Professional Authority can expire or be suspended

Triggers include:

- credential expiry;
- suspension;
- role termination;
- provider replacement;
- conflict discovery;
- appointment withdrawal;
- customer termination;
- jurisdiction change;
- scope expansion beyond qualification.

```text
Previously Authorized
≠ Currently Authorized
```

Active Assignments and pending Apply operations must be reassessed when authority changes.

## 16. Authority evidence must survive audit

For each R4 decision or Protected Action, the audit trail should reconstruct:

- qualified actor;
- qualification source;
- jurisdiction;
- appointment;
- conflict status;
- exact object version;
- Review relied upon;
- Approval;
- signature or Apply event;
- external evidence;
- formal-state validation.

A display badge is not sufficient evidence.

## 17. Apparent authority must be treated carefully

Customer-facing interfaces, email signatures and branding can create an impression of authority.

MarkOrbit should prevent:

- Contributors being presented as lawyers or agents without qualification;
- AI being presented as the final professional decision-maker;
- white-label production obscuring required professional disclosure;
- provider credentials being attributed to the platform itself;
- internal titles implying official appointment.

```text
Professional-looking Interface
≠ Professional Authority
```

## 18. Authority gaps should produce explicit states

Possible states include:

```text
Professional Authority Confirmed
Qualification Verification Pending
Appointment Required
Provider Acceptance Pending
Conflict Review Required
Authority Expired
Authority Suspended
No Qualified Route Available
```

The correct Outcome may be:

> No safe route is available until an eligible professional is appointed.

The system should not fill the gap with AI or an unqualified Contributor.

## 19. Commercial incentives must not distort authority claims

A platform may earn more from a preferred provider or internal route. That commercial interest cannot create or exaggerate professional authority.

```text
Preferred Commercial Route
≠ Qualified Professional Route
```

Where material, provider relationship and commercial interest should be disclosed at the decision point.

## 20. Cross-border execution requires layered authority

A multi-country service may involve:

- customer or originating agent;
- MarkReg or another Delivery Owner;
- Contributor;
- central reviewer;
- local Provider Organization;
- local qualified professional;
- official office.

Each actor's authority and responsibility should remain visible.

```text
One Customer Experience
≠ One Universal Professional Authority
```

## 21. The governing chain

```text
Capability Evidence
→ Certification
→ Production Authorization
→ Task-specific Eligibility
→ Professional Qualification Verification
→ Appointment and Acceptance
→ R4 Approval or Protected Action
```

Professional Authority is not a reward for being good at the work. It is a legally, professionally and contextually grounded permission that must be independently verified.
