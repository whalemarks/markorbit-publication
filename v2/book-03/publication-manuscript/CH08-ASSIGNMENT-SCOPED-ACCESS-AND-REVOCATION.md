# CH08 — Assignment, Scoped Access and Revocation

## 1. Assignment creates bounded responsibility

An Assignment is the governed connection between a Work Package and a performer.

It should answer:

- who or what is responsible;
- for which Work Package;
- under which Capability and authority;
- with access to which data;
- allowed to perform which actions;
- subject to which Review;
- by what deadline;
- for what compensation or service obligation;
- under what cancellation and revocation rules.

```text
Assignment
≠ General Membership
≠ Permanent Role
≠ Customer Relationship
≠ Professional Appointment in Every Case
```

## 2. Assignment follows eligibility and acceptance

The safe sequence is:

```text
Validated Work Package
→ Eligibility
→ Candidate Recommendation
→ Selection
→ Assignment Offer
→ Acceptance
→ Access Grant
→ Active Work
```

A candidate should not receive protected data merely because they appeared in a recommendation list.

```text
Eligible Candidate
≠ Assigned Performer

Assignment Offered
≠ Assignment Accepted
```

## 3. Acceptance is a meaningful execution event

Acceptance should confirm:

- the exact Work Package version;
- scope and exclusions;
- deadline;
- expected Outcome;
- Review requirement;
- allowed tools;
- data and confidentiality conditions;
- compensation terms;
- conflict status;
- current capacity;
- escalation path.

A casual acknowledgment such as “received” may not be sufficient where the Assignment creates professional or commercial responsibility.

## 4. Assignment scope must be explicit

A performer assigned to prepare a translation review is not automatically authorized to:

- contact the customer;
- alter the Mark;
- change the Goods;
- approve the filing;
- instruct a Provider;
- submit externally;
- reuse the case for training.

```text
Assigned to Contribute
≠ Authorized to Decide
≠ Authorized to Apply
```

## 5. Access follows Assignment, not organizational curiosity

The default execution chain is:

```text
Work Package
→ Assignment
→ Data Scope
→ Allowed Action
→ Contribution
```

Access should be limited to what is necessary for the assigned Outcome.

This may include:

- specific Customer fields;
- one Trademark record;
- selected Matter history;
- defined documents;
- relevant communications;
- an approved source pack;
- a limited Provider profile;
- the exact template or rubric.

It should not automatically include the full Workplace database, complete customer portfolio or unrelated AI memory.

## 6. Visibility and action are separate permissions

A person may be allowed to view a document but not download it. They may annotate but not replace it. They may upload a candidate but not validate it. They may draft a message but not send it.

```text
View
≠ Download
≠ Edit
≠ Share
≠ Approve
≠ Apply
```

Access control should therefore be action-specific, not merely object-specific.

## 7. Purpose limitation remains attached to access

The same field may be visible for one purpose but prohibited for another.

Examples:

- a customer email may support matter clarification but not marketing;
- a price may support quotation review but not external disclosure;
- a specimen may support evidence review but not public publication;
- a Provider contact may support an active appointment but not personal solicitation.

```text
Access Granted for Delivery
≠ Reuse Granted for Marketing, Training or Networking
```

## 8. Access should be time-bounded

Assignment access should have a lifecycle:

```text
Prepared
→ Active
→ Suspended
→ Expired
→ Revoked
→ Archived Evidence Only
```

Triggers may include:

- Assignment completion;
- deadline expiry;
- reassignment;
- conflict discovery;
- security concern;
- cancellation;
- employment or Membership change;
- professional qualification expiry;
- customer withdrawal;
- Workplace exit.

## 9. Revocation stops future authority

Revocation should remove future access and action authority without deleting historical evidence.

```text
Revocation
≠ Historical Erasure
```

The system should preserve:

- what the performer accessed;
- what they submitted;
- what decisions were made;
- whether data was downloaded;
- whether external communication occurred;
- what compensation was earned;
- why authority ended.

## 10. Revocation cannot undo external effects

If a message was sent, a Provider was instructed or a filing was submitted, revoking the Assignment does not reverse the external event.

Execution must determine:

- whether recall is possible;
- whether correction is required;
- who owns recovery;
- whether the customer must be informed;
- whether a second action would create duplication;
- what formal state remains Unknown.

```text
Authority Revoked
≠ External Action Reversed
```

## 11. Suspension differs from revocation

Suspension temporarily stops work while preserving the possibility of return.

Reasons may include:

- source conflict;
- awaiting customer decision;
- security review;
- possible conflict;
- payment checkpoint;
- quality investigation;
- external Unknown state.

Revocation ends the future authority under that Assignment.

```text
Suspended
≠ Revoked
≠ Completed
```

## 12. Reassignment requires controlled handoff

When work moves to a new performer, Execution should:

- close, suspend or revoke the prior Assignment;
- preserve prior Contributions;
- identify unresolved questions;
- determine whether prior Review remains valid;
- grant only necessary access to the new performer;
- record the responsibility transfer;
- avoid duplicate external action.

```text
New Assignee Added
≠ Old Authority Automatically Removed
```

Both sides must be handled explicitly.

## 13. Assignment amendments must be visible

Scope may change because:

- new documents arrive;
- the customer changes instructions;
- an official issue appears;
- the deadline changes;
- the Work Package expands;
- a new jurisdiction is added;
- Review requirements increase.

A material change should create an amendment or replacement Assignment.

It should identify:

- changed scope;
- changed deadline;
- changed compensation;
- changed access;
- invalidated prior work;
- renewed acceptance.

```text
Assignment v1 Accepted
≠ Expanded Assignment v2 Accepted
```

## 14. Assignment to AI still needs boundaries

An AI invocation may function as an assignment-like implementation record.

It should define:

- model and version;
- permitted inputs;
- tools;
- purpose;
- output type;
- collaboration mode;
- prohibited actions;
- Review requirement;
- memory policy;
- retention;
- correction path.

AI should not inherit all accessible Workplace context merely because a Product can technically retrieve it.

## 15. Assignment to deterministic systems also needs boundaries

A script or connector should receive:

- exact inputs;
- operation type;
- idempotency key;
- environment;
- permitted side effects;
- expected Return;
- timeout and retry policy;
- reconciliation rules.

```text
Machine Assignment
≠ Unlimited Service Account Authority
```

## 16. Provider Appointment is not an ordinary Assignment

A Provider Organization may require a formal appointment or service acceptance beyond a Work Package Assignment.

The route may include:

```text
Provider Candidate
→ Eligibility
→ Conflict Check
→ Commercial Approval
→ Selection
→ Appointment Authority
→ Instruction Package
→ Provider Acceptance
```

A local lawyer or agent should not be treated as merely another internal assignee when they carry separate professional responsibility.

## 17. Customer contact requires specific authority

An Assignment may define one of several communication modes:

```text
No Customer Contact
Draft for Relationship Owner
Platform-mediated Communication
Supervised Direct Communication
Authorized Direct Professional Communication
Emergency Contact under Defined Rule
```

Access to a customer name or email does not create permission to contact them.

```text
Customer Data Access
≠ Customer Contact Permission
≠ Customer Relationship Ownership
```

## 18. Relationship protection survives participation

A Contributor, Reviewer or Provider may need limited access to serve a customer. That participation does not create entitlement to:

- future customer work;
- independent marketing;
- direct billing;
- unrelated service offers;
- retention of customer lists;
- bypassing the originating Workplace.

Non-circumvention rules should remain scoped, time-bounded and compatible with customer choice.

## 19. Assignment and compensation are linked but distinct

An Assignment should disclose the compensation basis before acceptance where the work is paid.

Possible states include:

```text
Offer Pending
Accepted
Work Started
Contribution Submitted
Revision Required
Accepted for Payment
Disputed
Cancelled with Earned Compensation
Settled
```

```text
Assignment Revoked
≠ No Compensation Earned
```

Completed or partially completed work may still require payment.

## 20. Quality concerns do not justify silent access expansion

A Reviewer may need more context than the original performer, but the system should still grant it deliberately.

Likewise, a quality investigation may require audit access, but that access should be:

- purpose-specific;
- logged;
- time-limited;
- proportionate;
- reviewable.

## 21. Emergency access requires exceptional governance

Urgent deadlines or incidents may justify temporary elevated access.

Emergency access should record:

- reason;
- authorizer;
- scope;
- duration;
- actions taken;
- evidence;
- post-event review.

```text
Urgency
≠ Unlimited Authority
```

## 22. Assignment evidence supports reconstruction

For consequential work, MarkOrbit should be able to reconstruct:

- why the performer was eligible;
- what they accepted;
- what data they could see;
- what they were allowed to do;
- which tools they used;
- what they returned;
- who reviewed it;
- when access ended;
- whether any external effect occurred.
