# CH14 — Delivery Owner Coordinates Without Owning Every Authority

## 1. Complex execution needs one accountable coordinator

When work spans several Contributors, reviewers, providers, systems and customer decisions, somebody must remain accountable for the coherence of delivery.

MarkOrbit calls this role the `Delivery Owner`.

The Delivery Owner is responsible for ensuring that the accepted scope becomes a complete, explainable and governed Outcome.

```text
Many Performers
≠ No Accountable Coordinator
```

But the Delivery Owner must not be confused with every other form of ownership or authority.

## 2. Delivery ownership is a coordination responsibility

The Delivery Owner may be responsible for:

- decomposing accepted scope into Work Packages;
- confirming dependencies;
- ensuring Assignment acceptance;
- monitoring deadlines;
- securing reviewers;
- identifying missing customer decisions;
- reconciling versions;
- assembling Outcomes;
- exposing Unknowns;
- coordinating Provider Returns;
- triggering escalation and recovery;
- preparing customer delivery;
- confirming Evidence Contract completion.

```text
Delivery Owner
= accountable coordinator of the delivery path
```

## 3. Delivery Owner is not Relationship Owner

The Relationship Owner governs the customer relationship, represented Workplace and communication expectations.

```text
Delivery Owner
≠ Relationship Owner
```

In a direct MarkReg service, one person or Workplace may hold both roles. In white-label or co-delivery work, the original agent may remain Relationship Owner while MarkReg or another production team acts as Delivery Owner.

The delivery role does not create permission to market to, solicit or retain the customer.

## 4. Delivery Owner is not Professional Authority

The Delivery Owner may coordinate legal, design, evidence and commercial work without being qualified to make every reserved judgment.

```text
Delivery Ownership
≠ Professional Authority
```

Where a jurisdiction-specific legal conclusion, signature or filing approval is required, the qualified professional remains responsible for that judgment.

## 5. Delivery Owner is not formal-state owner

Execution coordinates work, while Owning Services govern formal records.

```text
Delivery Owner Acceptance
≠ Matter Formal State Updated
```

The Delivery Owner can submit a validated Outcome to the Owning Service, but cannot silently rewrite Customer, Order, Matter, Payment, Right or official status.

## 6. Delivery Owner is not the universal approver

A Delivery Owner may approve internal coordination choices within policy, but other approvals may still be required:

- customer strategy approval;
- commercial scope approval;
- professional approval;
- data-disclosure approval;
- funds approval;
- provider appointment approval;
- External Protected Action approval.

```text
Coordinates the Decision Path
≠ Holds Every Decision Authority
```

## 7. Appointment of the Delivery Owner must be explicit

The role record should identify:

- person or team;
- represented Workplace;
- Matter, Workflow or delivery scope;
- start and end time;
- decision rights;
- excluded authorities;
- escalation route;
- deputy or continuity arrangement;
- data scope;
- conflict status;
- revocation conditions.

A generic manager title is not enough.

## 8. The Delivery Plan is the main coordination artifact

A Delivery Plan may include:

- accepted Outcome;
- Work Packages;
- dependencies;
- Implementation Bindings;
- Assignments;
- Review requirements;
- Approval requirements;
- deadlines and safety dates;
- Provider appointments;
- customer decisions;
- funds checkpoints;
- Evidence Contracts;
- recovery routes;
- completion criteria.

```text
Delivery Plan
≠ Workflow Template
≠ Customer Contract
```

It is an execution projection of the accepted scope, not a replacement for commercial or formal records.

## 9. The Delivery Owner protects boundary integrity

The role must actively prevent status leakage, such as:

```text
Work Package Complete
→ Matter Complete
```

without evidence, or:

```text
Provider Says Filed
→ Official Status = Filed
```

without verification.

It must also prevent customer silence, payment or historical precedent from being treated as Approval.

## 10. Delivery Owner manages dependency truth

Dependencies should be typed:

```text
Awaiting Input
Awaiting Customer Decision
Awaiting Professional Review
Awaiting Approval
Awaiting Provider Acceptance
Awaiting Funds
Awaiting External Evidence
Awaiting Reconciliation
```

The Delivery Owner ensures that “blocked” is not used to hide who or what is actually needed.

## 11. Capacity and deadline responsibility

The Delivery Owner should assess whether the route is feasible before accepting or continuing it.

This includes:

- performer capacity;
- reviewer availability;
- provider response time;
- document lead time;
- original-document courier time;
- customer decision time;
- official deadline and timezone;
- recovery margin.

```text
Task Assigned
≠ Deadline Feasible
```

## 12. Customer communication remains role-aware

The Delivery Owner may prepare status updates or decision requests, but the represented sender and Relationship Owner must remain clear.

Possible modes include:

- internal draft only;
- draft for Relationship Owner;
- platform-mediated communication;
- supervised direct communication;
- authorized direct professional communication.

```text
Delivery Responsibility
≠ Automatic Customer Contact Permission
```

## 13. The Delivery Owner coordinates revisions without assigning blame prematurely

When Review requests revision, the Delivery Owner determines whether the cause is:

- performer error;
- incomplete instruction;
- missing customer fact;
- source change;
- scope expansion;
- reviewer inconsistency;
- provider error;
- system defect.

```text
Revision
≠ Contributor Fault by Default
```

This affects compensation, capability evidence, deadlines and customer communication.

## 14. Recovery ownership must be clear

When an incident occurs, the Delivery Owner may become or appoint the Recovery Owner.

Responsibilities include:

- containment;
- evidence preservation;
- authority check;
- duplicate-risk control;
- provider follow-up;
- customer notification coordination;
- alternative-route analysis;
- official-state reconciliation.

```text
Delivery Owner
≠ Unlimited Emergency Authority
```

Protected actions still require the applicable authority.

## 15. Handover requires an explicit transfer record

A Delivery Owner may change because of absence, conflict, workload or escalation.

Handover should preserve:

- current scope;
- open Work Packages;
- deadlines;
- accepted and pending Contributions;
- Review and Approval status;
- Provider status;
- customer commitments;
- Unknowns;
- access changes;
- recovery risks.

```text
New Delivery Owner Added
≠ Previous Authority Automatically Ended
```

Access and responsibility must be revoked or closed deliberately.

## 16. AI may assist delivery coordination

AI can:

- identify missing dependencies;
- summarize execution state;
- detect version mismatch;
- forecast deadline risk;
- draft status communication;
- suggest routing or escalation;
- assemble evidence checklists.

But:

```text
AI Delivery Assistant
≠ Delivery Owner of Record
```

Unless a future policy explicitly creates a bounded machine-owned execution mode, accountable human or organizational responsibility must remain identifiable.

## 17. Delivery metrics should measure outcome integrity

Useful measures include:

- accepted Outcome rate;
- deadline reliability;
- rework;
- Review latency;
- Provider evidence completeness;
- Unknown duration;
- recovery cost;
- customer decision delay;
- formal-state discrepancy;
- customer delivery completeness.

```text
More Tasks Closed
≠ Better Delivery
```

## 18. One Delivery Owner may coordinate several production lanes

A delivery can combine:

```text
Deterministic Tool
+ AI Assistance
+ Contributor Production
+ Expert Review
+ Provider Execution
```

The Delivery Owner ensures that each lane keeps its own authority, evidence and responsibility.

## 19. The governing model

```text
Accepted Scope
→ Delivery Owner
→ Delivery Plan
→ Work Packages and Bindings
→ Contributions and Reviews
→ Decisions and Approvals
→ Apply and Return
→ Outcome Assembly
→ Customer Delivery and Formal Handoff
```

The role exists to preserve one accountable delivery path without pretending that one person owns every relationship, fact, decision and authority.
