## Simulation isolation review

The Simulation Workplace is defined as a technically and semantically isolated environment with:

- simulation-only identities and credentials;
- blocked or stubbed official connectors;
- controlled recipients;
- synthetic funds;
- separate storage and event streams;
- no production formal-state mutation.

The chapter requires verifiable isolation rather than relying only on visual warnings.

## Rights and privacy review

The batch preserves:

```text
Operational Record
≠ Simulation Fixture
≠ Training Permission

Assessment Participation
≠ Unlimited Analytics or Model-training Consent
```

Real Matter data requires rights, privacy, confidentiality and re-identification review before transformation into training material. Synthetic reconstruction is preferred where risk remains.

## Scenario-quality review

The Scenario Engine tests:

- fact classification;
- source judgment;
- authority;
- route selection;
- communication;
- Human–AI governance;
- Review and Approval;
- Unknown and reconciliation;
- stopping behavior;
- recovery.

It explicitly allows multiple defensible routes and rejects arbitrary hidden traps.

## Assessment evidence review

Assessment is modeled as a multidimensional evidence profile rather than only a total score.

Dimensions include:

- facts;
- rules and sources;
- risk;
- communication;
- service design;
- MarkOrbit operation;
- Human–AI judgment;
- delivery;
- recovery.

Blocking integrity or authority failures remain distinguishable from ordinary skill gaps.

## Human–AI review

M0–M5 applies inside simulation and assessment. The batch requires assessment of whether AI was used within the permitted mode, with proper source grounding, data control, human disposition and autonomy reduction.

```text
Good AI Draft
≠ Good Human–AI Execution
```

AI systems themselves follow a staged path from offline evaluation to Simulation, shadow recommendation, supervised production and bounded M4 execution.

## Professional Authority review

No simulation, assessment, certification or production history bypasses jurisdiction-specific Professional Authority.

```text
Strong Capability Evidence
≠ Legal Qualification
```

Early live work is intentionally bounded and reviewable. External Protected Actions remain subject to later dedicated authority chapters.

## Labor and compensation boundary

The batch distinguishes genuine simulation and assessment from real value-producing work.

```text
Learning Opportunity
≠ Substitute for Compensation
```

Shadow or supervised Contributions used in customer delivery require clear compensation terms.

## Reversibility review

Production progression is explicitly reversible through:

- additional Review;
- scope restriction;
- refresher learning;
- reassessment;
- return to supervision;
- suspension;
- revocation.

Reversal preserves evidence, active-work handoff, fairness and appeal routes.

## Publication-quality assessment

### Strengths

1. Simulation is treated as a real execution environment with hard production isolation.
2. Scenarios test decisions, communication and recovery rather than terminology recall.
3. Assessment observes the entire execution path.
4. Capability Evidence, Certification, Authorization, Eligibility and Assignment remain distinct.
5. Human and AI progression use the same trust logic without pretending they are identical actors.
6. Production progression is supervised, compensated and reversible.

1. R0–R4 risk tiers need dedicated long-form treatment.
2. Professional Authority and External Protected Action need deeper chapters.
3. Retry, duplicate safety, Handoff, provider silence and reconciliation remain for later batches.
4. Exact frozen-RC1 source trace remains incomplete.
5. Final Core object decisions remain outside Book 03 publication authority.
