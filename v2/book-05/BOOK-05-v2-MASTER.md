# MarkReg — Full-Lifecycle International Trademark Product

## Purpose

MarkReg is MarkOrbit’s flagship full-lifecycle trademark product and self-operated service model. It converts professional capabilities into reliable customer outcomes across search, filing, prosecution, maintenance, transactions and cross-border fulfillment.

## Part I — Product promise

### Chapter 01 — One accountable lifecycle

Customers should not need to understand fragmented databases, forms, jurisdictions, agents, deadlines and evidence standards. MarkReg organizes them into one governed lifecycle while preserving the authority of each professional and office.

### Chapter 02 — Product, service and operating model

MarkReg is simultaneously:

- a Product Installation;
- a standardized service catalog;
- an orchestration and quality model;
- a self-operated Workplace;
- a source of real demand, evidence and cases.

It is not the mandatory commercial counterparty for every MarkOrbit Workplace.

### Chapter 03 — Customer relationship modes

MarkReg supports direct self-operated service, co-delivery, white-label delivery and bounded capability fulfillment. Each mode records Relationship Owner, Contracting Party, Delivery Owner and Professional Authority.

## Part II — Lifecycle architecture

### Chapter 04 — Intake and decision readiness

AI assists clients and professionals in identifying country, class, goods, applicant, use basis, document and provider requirements with as few questions as possible. Recommendations remain explainable and reviewable.

### Chapter 05 — Search and risk

Search combines structured data, semantic retrieval, AI candidate generation and professional review. Search output must distinguish factual matches, inferred relevance, professional opinion and legally reserved advice.

### Chapter 06 — Filing preparation

The product governs applicant data, address, entity type, mark representation, translation, transliteration, mark description, goods, priority, POA and documentary requirements.

### Chapter 07 — External filing

Where local qualification is required, MarkReg creates a governed Handoff to MGSN or an approved external route. Formal submission remains with the authorized professional.

### Chapter 08 — Prosecution and response

Office actions, formalities, objections, oppositions and disputes are classified by risk, capability and qualification. AI may organize issues and drafts; professional authority controls final legal strategy and submission.

### Chapter 09 — Registration and maintenance

Certificates, declarations of use, renewals, ownership changes, address changes, assignments, restoration and re-filing opportunities form a continuous post-registration lifecycle.

### Chapter 10 — Transaction and asset services

MarkReg may provide due diligence, transfer preparation, recordal, payment milestones and final delivery for trademark commerce initiated through Lite or other Workplaces.

## Part III — Production architecture

### Chapter 11 — Order to Work Package

A customer Order is decomposed into WorkPackages such as status verification, evidence screening, customer questions, search review, filing preparation, provider instruction, receipt checking and delivery reporting.

### Chapter 12 — Internal team, contributors and providers

WorkPackages may be fulfilled by:

- MarkReg internal staff;
- certified contributors;
- expert reviewers;
- deterministic systems;
- AI under governance;
- MGSN providers.

The Delivery Owner remains explicit.

### Chapter 13 — Certified Production Pool

Certification alone does not grant production access. Contributors require identity verification, confidentiality, conflict control, production authorization, scoped access and quality history.

### Chapter 14 — Progression into real work

```text
simulation
→ shadow production
→ supervised production
→ bounded independent work
→ review authority
```

Paid work creates capability evidence and a path to higher-value tasks.

### Chapter 15 — Professional Contribution Network

MarkReg may source search verification, data cleanup, evidence screening, content checking, visual review and other bounded work without treating every contributor as an external provider Organization.

## Part IV — Quality and responsibility

### Chapter 16 — Risk-based review

Low-risk standardized work may be automated or sampled. High-risk analysis, reserved advice and formal actions require qualified review. Review depth follows risk, value, contributor history and jurisdiction.

### Chapter 17 — Customer communication

MarkReg presents a unified service team and controlled communication history. Contributors do not acquire customer rights merely because they see a task or draft a message.

### Chapter 18 — Evidence and audit

Every filing instruction, evidence choice, deletion decision, provider Return, customer approval and delivery result is traceable.

### Chapter 19 — Failure and recovery

The product handles missed responses, rejected documents, provider silence, payment failure, official-system ambiguity and incorrect status as governed exception paths, not informal email chaos.

## Part V — Economics

### Chapter 20 — Revenue

MarkReg earns through professional service margin, managed fulfillment, lifecycle subscription or monitoring, transaction service, premium review and related service packages.

### Chapter 21 — Contributor compensation

Bounded work uses transparent fixed prices, complexity premiums, urgency premiums, review compensation and quality incentives. It does not use lowest-price bidding.

### Chapter 22 — Provider economics

MGSN procurement, official fees, professional fees, funds checkpoints and replacement policy remain distinct from internal production compensation.

## Part VI — Learning and platform value

### Chapter 23 — Case distillation

Eligible completed matters may be de-identified or synthesized into training cases only after rights, privacy and professional-responsibility review.

### Chapter 24 — Capability feedback

Repeated errors, successful decisions, provider outcomes and customer questions improve rules, training, checklists and capability definitions, but do not automatically become canonical knowledge.

### Chapter 25 — Market signal

MarkReg demand reveals which capabilities, jurisdictions, providers and maintenance opportunities require investment. It is a platform sensor as well as a revenue product.

## Ten-year lock

MarkReg succeeds when it can scale reliable delivery without centralizing every customer relationship or lowering professional standards. It should become the proving ground for MarkOrbit capabilities, not the owner of the entire ecosystem.
