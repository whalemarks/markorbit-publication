# Book 05 v2
# MarkReg — Full-Lifecycle International Trademark Growth and Service Product

## Purpose

MarkReg is MarkOrbit’s flagship full-lifecycle international trademark Product. It connects source-backed service discovery, decision-ready conversion, professional delivery, continuing-right management and trademark transactions in one accountable customer journey.

MarkReg converts professional Capabilities, governed Knowledge and real operating Evidence into reliable customer outcomes across:

- SEO and generative-engine discovery;
- public professional content;
- automated Need identification and conversion;
- search, filing and prosecution;
- registration, maintenance, renewal and recordal;
- trademark transactions;
- cross-border fulfillment;
- lifecycle monitoring, learning and service improvement.

```text
MarkReg Product
≠ MarkReg-operated Workplace
```

A MarkReg-operated Workplace may use the Product for direct self-operated service. The Product itself does not automatically become every customer’s Relationship Owner, Contracting Party, Payment Receiver, Delivery Owner or Professional Authority.

MarkReg is also an important Capability proving ground because it produces attributable service Outcomes, official Evidence, Corrections and recovery records. Those records may support controlled Capability evaluation. They do not automatically rewrite Capability canon.

```text
Formal Service Outcome
≠ Capability Verification
≠ Automatic Capability Upgrade
```

## Part I — Product promise

### Chapter 01 — One accountable lifecycle

Customers should not need to understand fragmented content, databases, forms, jurisdictions, agents, deadlines and Evidence standards. MarkReg organizes them into one governed lifecycle while preserving the authority of each professional, Workplace, Owning Service, Provider and office.

### Chapter 02 — Product, service and operating model

MarkReg is:

- a Product that may be installed and operated inside a Workplace;
- a standardized service catalog;
- an orchestration and quality model;
- a source-backed discovery and conversion Capability;
- a source of real demand, Evidence, Cases and Capability-evaluation candidates.

A self-operated MarkReg Workplace is one commercial mode using the Product. MarkReg is not the mandatory counterparty or delivery route for every MarkOrbit Workplace.

### Chapter 03 — Customer relationship modes

MarkReg supports direct self-operated service, co-delivery, white-label delivery and bounded Capability fulfillment. Each mode records Relationship Owner, Contracting Party, Payment Receiver, Delivery Owner and Professional Authority.

## Part II — Lifecycle architecture

### Chapter 04 — Intake and decision readiness

AI assists clients and professionals in identifying country, class, goods, applicant, use basis, document and Provider requirements with as few questions as possible. Recommendations remain explainable, reviewable and distinct from customer instruction or professional approval.

A completed Intake may also show which Capabilities were required, which assumptions were material and where stronger professional review was needed. It does not prove that the user mastered those Capabilities.

### Chapter 05 — Search and risk

Search combines structured Data, semantic retrieval, AI candidate generation and professional review. Search output distinguishes factual records, retrieved candidates, inferred relevance, professional opinion, customer risk decisions and legally reserved advice.

### Chapter 06 — Filing preparation

The Product governs applicant data, address, entity type, mark representation, translation, transliteration, mark description, goods, priority, POA and documentary requirements through versioned Filing Package Candidates.

### Chapter 07 — External filing

Where local qualification is required, MarkReg creates a governed Handoff to MGSN or another approved external route. Provider discovery, Eligibility, Recommendation, selection, appointment, Instruction, Acceptance and official result remain distinct. Formal submission remains with the authorized professional or approved official route.

### Chapter 08 — Prosecution and response

Office actions, formalities, objections, oppositions and disputes are classified by source, risk, deadline, Capability and qualification. AI may organize issues and drafts; Professional Authority controls final strategy, approval and protected submission.

### Chapter 09 — Registration is a verified transition

Registration begins with sourced official Evidence. Official records, certificates, certificate files, Registration Outcome Records, Right Baselines and Product projections remain distinct. The registered scope is compared with the approved and officially acknowledged scope.

```text
Official Record
≠ Service Interpretation
≠ Capability Evidence by Itself
```

An official result becomes useful Capability Evidence only when it is linked to the relevant context, Capability version, actor, decision, authority, Work Package and review history.

### Chapter 10 — Continuing rights, maintenance and recovery

Declarations of use, Evidence, renewals, ownership and address changes, assignments, recordals, restoration and re-filing opportunities form a continuing-right lifecycle. Each obligation retains its source, deadline, responsibility, Package, approval, execution and official result.

### Chapter 11 — Transaction and asset services

MarkReg may provide due diligence, transfer preparation, assignment, recordal coordination, payment-milestone records and final delivery for trademark commerce initiated through Lite or another Workplace. Commercial closing, payment, contractual transfer and official recordal remain distinct.

## Part III — Production architecture

### Chapter 12 — Order, Matter and Work Package

A customer Order may support multiple Matters. Each Matter may be decomposed into Work Packages such as status verification, Evidence screening, customer questions, search review, filing preparation, Provider Instruction, receipt checking and delivery reporting.

```text
Order
≠ Matter
≠ Work Package
≠ Assignment
≠ External Protected Action
```

Capability Evidence must be attributed at the smallest reliable unit. Completion of an entire Matter does not prove that every participant performed every Capability involved.

### Chapter 13 — Internal teams, systems, contributors and Providers

Work Packages may be fulfilled by:

- MarkReg internal staff;
- deterministic systems;
- AI under governance;
- certified contributors;
- expert reviewers;
- MGSN Providers;
- approved private or direct external routes.

The Delivery Owner, reviewer, approver and Professional Authority remain explicit. Evidence should identify which actor or implementation produced which Contribution or Outcome.

### Chapter 14 — Certified Production Pool

Certification alone does not grant production access. Contributors require identity verification, confidentiality, conflict control, current Capability Evidence, Eligibility, Production Authorization, scoped access and quality history.

```text
Capability Certification
≠ Production Authorization
≠ Professional Qualification
```

### Chapter 15 — Progression into real work

```text
simulation
→ shadow production
→ supervised production
→ bounded independent work
→ review authority
```

Progression is evidence-backed, current, reversible and subject to risk and authority. Paid work may create Capability Evidence and a route to higher-value Contributions.

```text
Paid Work Completed
≠ Capability Verified
```

A single successful task may support a Practice event or Case Candidate. Higher confidence requires suitable review, repeated performance, risk context and current Evidence.

### Chapter 16 — Professional Contribution Network

MarkReg may source search verification, data cleanup, Evidence screening, content checking, visual review and other bounded production without treating every contributor as an external Provider Organization. Scope, review, compensation, confidentiality and customer-relationship protection remain governed.

## Part IV — Quality and responsibility

### Chapter 17 — Risk-based review

Low-risk standardized work may be automated or sampled. High-risk analysis, reserved advice and formal actions require qualified review. Review depth follows consequence, value, contributor history, source quality and jurisdiction.

Capability evaluation must also consider the risk and difficulty of the work. Ten routine completions and one complex corrected Matter are not interchangeable evidence units.

### Chapter 18 — Customer communication

MarkReg presents a coherent service experience and controlled communication history. Contributors and Providers do not acquire customer rights merely because they see a task, draft a message or communicate within an approved scope.

### Chapter 19 — Evidence and audit

Every Filing Instruction, Evidence choice, deletion decision, Provider Return, customer approval, content claim, conversion event and delivery result is traceable to source, version, actor and authority.

MarkReg distinguishes five evidence layers:

```text
Official Fact
Service Outcome
Execution Evidence
Reflection / Case Candidate
Capability Evaluation
```

- **Official Fact** records what an official authority or source establishes.
- **Service Outcome** records what happened in the customer journey or Matter.
- **Execution Evidence** attributes work, decisions, review and implementation.
- **Reflection or Case Candidate** captures bounded learning from the experience.
- **Capability Evaluation** determines whether the evidence supports practice, proficiency or a Capability Change Proposal.

No layer silently replaces another.

### Chapter 20 — Failure and recovery

The Product handles missed responses, rejected documents, Provider silence, payment failure, official-system ambiguity, incorrect status, stale public content, failed conversion routing and other exceptions as governed recovery paths rather than informal email or marketing chaos.

Failure, Correction and recovery may be more informative than uncomplicated success. They must still be interpreted in context before affecting Capability status.

```text
Correction Required
≠ Actor Incapable

Recovery Succeeded
≠ Original Error Erased
```

## Part V — Growth, discovery and conversion

### Chapter 21 — Service discovery must be source-backed

MarkReg supplies governed service Knowledge and structured professional objects for SEO, generative-engine optimization and public service discovery. Sites provides public publishing, indexing, schema, technical SEO and page performance. Lite supports content production and distribution. Knowledge supplies source, freshness and provenance.

```text
Published Service Page
≠ Verified Legal Rule

Search or AI Visibility
≠ Professional Accuracy
```

### Chapter 22 — Automated conversion must move from intent to decision-ready intake

MarkReg connects content and service discovery to governed intent detection, progressive clarification, explainable options, route and fee comparison, decision-ready Intake and formal commercial Handoff.

```text
Content Interest
≠ Customer Instruction

Recommendation
≠ Professional Approval

Quote Viewed
≠ Order Created
```

The process should ask fewer high-value questions while preserving assumptions, Unknowns, relationship ownership, consent and communication boundaries.

### Chapter 23 — Content quality testing must separate truth, usefulness and conversion

Content and conversion surfaces are evaluated across:

- factual and professional quality;
- source freshness and exception coverage;
- SEO quality;
- GEO and answer-engine usability;
- user comprehension;
- qualified conversion;
- correction and withdrawal performance.

A/B testing may optimize presentation, sequence and calls to action. It must not experiment with official fees, legal deadlines, professional conclusions, filing requirements or material risk disclosure.

```text
High Traffic
≠ High-quality Content

High Conversion
≠ Correct Conversion

Conversion Experiment
≠ Permission to Experiment with Legal Truth
```

Conversion and content results may become Product-learning evidence. They do not establish legal or professional Capability without the relevant review context.

## Part VI — Economics

### Chapter 24 — Revenue

MarkReg earns through professional service margin, managed fulfillment, lifecycle subscription or monitoring, transaction service, premium review, growth and conversion Capability, and related Service Packages. Revenue attribution must remain distinct from customer relationship and Professional Authority.

### Chapter 25 — Contributor compensation

Bounded work uses transparent fixed prices, complexity premiums, urgency premiums, review compensation and quality incentives. It does not use lowest-price bidding or disguise production as unpaid learning.

Compensation amount is not a Capability score. Paid Evidence is evaluated under the same attribution, review and rights rules as unpaid simulation or internal practice.

### Chapter 26 — Provider economics

MGSN procurement, official fees, professional fees, funds checkpoints, replacement policy and external payment routes remain distinct from internal production compensation and MarkReg-operated Workplace revenue.

## Part VII — Learning and platform value

### Chapter 27 — Case distillation

Eligible completed Matters, content Outcomes and conversion journeys may be de-identified or synthesized into training Cases only after rights, privacy, relationship and professional-responsibility review.

The preferred path is:

```text
Official Fact or Service Outcome
→ Attributed Execution Evidence
→ Reflection Candidate
→ Case Candidate
→ Capability Evaluation
→ Capability Change Proposal where justified
```

A Case should preserve relevant context, Capability and Skill versions, actor roles, authority, Evidence, Outcome, Corrections and known limits.

### Chapter 28 — Capability feedback

Repeated errors, successful decisions, Provider Outcomes, customer questions, content Corrections and conversion results may improve rules, training, checklists and Capability Definitions.

They enter controlled evolution through:

```text
Outcome
→ Evidence Qualification
→ Reflection / Case Candidate
→ Review
→ Capability Evaluation
→ Change Proposal
→ Version Decision
```

The following boundaries remain fixed:

```text
Official Fact
≠ Capability Proof by Itself

One Successful Filing
≠ User Mastery

MarkReg Formal State
≠ Capability Canon

Service Outcome
≠ Automatic Principle
```

MarkReg may submit evidence-backed change candidates. It does not unilaterally mutate shared Capability Definitions.

### Chapter 29 — Market and demand signal

MarkReg demand reveals which services, countries, questions, Capabilities, Providers, maintenance opportunities and public Knowledge gaps require investment. SEO and GEO performance may contribute signals, but visibility, correlation and conversion do not become legal truth or mandatory Product priority automatically.

## Ten-year lock

MarkReg succeeds when it can connect trusted discovery, intelligent conversion and reliable full-lifecycle delivery without centralizing every customer relationship, absorbing the roles of Lite or Sites, or lowering professional standards.

It should become the proving ground for MarkOrbit service Capabilities and accountable growth—not the owner of the entire ecosystem or an automatic author of Capability canon.
