## Structural verdict

Batch 06 successfully adds the accepted MarkReg Growth & Conversion Layer without turning MarkReg into a generic CMS, CRM, advertising platform or replacement for Lite, Sites or Knowledge.

The integrated lifecycle is now:

```text
Question, service need or commercial intent
→ source-backed discovery
→ trustworthy professional content
→ provisional intent understanding
→ progressive clarification
→ explainable options
→ Decision-ready Intake
→ formal commercial and professional Handoff
→ full-lifecycle delivery
→ continuing-right management
→ content and knowledge feedback
```

The chapter establishes public discovery as an upstream part of the service lifecycle while preserving Product boundaries.

It correctly defines:

```text

= service meaning, requirements, decision logic and conversion route

Sites
= publication, indexing, schema and performance

Lite
= content production, campaigns and distribution

Knowledge / Brain
```

The chapter introduces governed page families, typed claims, source and freshness states, contradiction handling, fee decomposition, publication gates and correction propagation.

Important locks include:

```text

≠ Verified Legal Rule

AI Engine Citation
≠ Professional Approval

```

## CH23 review — content quality testing

The chapter rejects a single content score and establishes independent dimensions:

```text
Professional Truth
User Usefulness
SEO Discoverability
GEO Answerability
Conversion Quality
Operational Safety
```

It correctly requires professional truth to remain a blocking gate. High traffic, citation or conversion cannot compensate for stale or unsupported professional claims.

The chapter also integrates content work into the governed production model of Chapters 13–16. Professional review, SEO review, GEO evaluation and conversion review are distinct Work Packages and roles.

It adds:

- immutable truth layers for A/B testing;
- experiment eligibility and stopping rules;
- answer-engine representation testing;
- downstream Matter feedback;
- derivative-content correction propagation;
- incident severity;
- white-label separation;
- maintenance budgets;
- net content value including correction and support cost.
