# Chapter 04 — Intake and Decision Readiness

Customers should not need to understand international trademark procedure before MarkReg can help them.

They may know the brand, products, launch date and markets. They may not know the correct applicant, classes, goods wording, filing route, use basis, documentary requirements or local Provider structure.

A conventional form responds by asking the customer to supply the professional structure themselves.

MarkReg should do something different:

```text
Incomplete business objective
→ structured understanding
→ candidate interpretation
→ material clarification
→ comparable options
→ accountable customer and professional Decisions
→ Formal Intake
→ readiness assessment
```

The Product should ask fewer questions because it does more disciplined reasoning, not because it accepts less truth.

## 1. Intake begins with the objective

The opening interaction should establish why protection is being considered.

Useful questions include:

- What name, logo or other sign is involved?
- What products or services does the business currently offer?
- What will be launched next?
- Where is the business selling or preparing to sell?
- Which markets are essential, recommended or optional?
- Is the priority speed, budget, coverage, defensibility or investor readiness?
- Who currently owns or controls the brand?
- Has the mark already been used, filed or registered anywhere?
- Is there a deadline, dispute, launch or transaction driving urgency?

The answers create a Business Context Snapshot and Need Brief. They do not yet create a filing instruction.

## 2. The customer should not be asked to solve the classification system

An inexperienced customer may be asked to select a Nice class and choose goods from a long list.

That interface appears precise. It often produces weak scope because the customer cannot see:

- which goods are commercially central;
- which descriptions are acceptable in a jurisdiction;
- which additional classes protect adjacent risk;
- which item limits affect cost;
- which broad wording creates examination problems;
- which goods require use or later evidence;
- which descriptions omit the actual business.

MarkReg should begin with the real business and then propose structured options.

```text
Customer products and services
→ normalized commercial activities
→ candidate classes and goods
→ jurisdiction-specific constraints
→ package options
→ professional and customer confirmation
```

The customer decides commercial scope with understandable consequences. The professional reviews legal and procedural suitability.

## 3. Fewer questions require visible inference

The Product may reduce repetition by using:

- authorized Workplace and customer records;
- existing trademark and portfolio data;
- company registration information;
- prior accepted applicant details;
- verified product catalogs;
- uploaded documents;
- earlier MarkReg sessions;
- official records;
- AI extraction and comparison.

Every reused or inferred value should remain typed.

A field may be:

```text
Verified fact
Customer-confirmed fact
Source-derived candidate
AI inference
Professional recommendation
Default assumption
Unknown
Not applicable
Requires professional advice
```

The interface should show material uncertainty without forcing the customer to inspect every technical source.

## 4. Silent assumptions are not intelligent intake

A short form can appear efficient because it hides assumptions.

For example, the system may assume:

- the customer is the applicant;
- the business address is the official address;
- the logo should be filed in color;
- all planned products belong in one class;
- the customer has already used the mark;
- the listed owner still controls the right;
- a scan of the POA will be accepted;
- a Provider can file without additional documents.

Those assumptions may be wrong and consequential.

```text
Fewer questions
+ hidden assumptions
= faster uncertainty
```

The Product should expose the assumptions that materially affect scope, cost, ownership, documents, authority, deadline or legal route.

## 5. Clarification should follow decision impact

MarkReg should ask a question when the answer materially changes the route.

High-value clarification may concern:

- applicant identity;
- ownership and signatory authority;
- mark version;
- essential jurisdictions;
- current or intended use;
- priority claim;
- critical goods and services;
- search tolerance;
- budget ceiling;
- filing deadline;
- availability of original or legalized documents.

Low-value questions may be deferred when they do not affect the current Decision.

For each material question, the Product should be able to explain:

- why the answer matters;
- which option changes;
- what assumption applies if unanswered;
- when the question becomes mandatory;
- who should answer or verify it.

## 6. Decision readiness is not formal completeness

Decision readiness means the customer and professional have enough structured context to compare the relevant options and choose a route.

It does not mean every formal field and document is already complete.

A decision-ready output may contain:

```text
Minimum required plan
Recommended plan
Broader strategic plan
Cost-saving alternative
Deferred-market option
Known risks
Material assumptions
Missing information
Professional-review requirements
```

For example, a customer may decide to file a word mark in the United States now and defer the logo and EU filing. The exact US goods wording and attorney filing basis may still require later preparation.

## 7. Options should make trade-offs visible

A useful option is more than a price.

It should explain:

- jurisdictions;
- filing routes;
- mark units;
- classes and goods strategy;
- search level;
- documentary requirements;
- expected professional route;
- immediate and later-stage cost;
- timing;
- principal risks;
- what remains excluded.

An option set may look like:

### Option A — Minimum launch protection

- essential jurisdiction only;
- word mark;
- primary class and goods;
- focused search;
- lowest immediate cost;
- narrower protection.

### Option B — Recommended commercial protection

- essential and priority expansion jurisdictions;
- word and selected device filing;
- primary and adjacent classes;
- stronger search and document preparation;
- higher cost and broader operational coverage.

### Option C — Staged portfolio plan

- immediate filing where ready;
- delayed markets linked to launch milestones;
- document preparation in parallel;
- future classes and maintenance mapped;
- preserves budget while avoiding rushed incomplete filings.

The Product should not manipulate the customer toward the option that creates the highest platform revenue.

## 8. Country recommendation requires business context

Country choice should consider:

- current sales;
- launch plan;
- manufacturing and sourcing;
- ecommerce reach;
- distributor or investor requirements;
- counterfeiting and filing risk;
- local use requirements;
- cost and document burden;
- regional or international routes;
- existing rights;
- enforcement and business importance.

The result may classify jurisdictions as:

```text
Essential now
Recommended next
Optional expansion
Monitor only
Defer pending evidence or budget
Not presently justified
```

A country recommendation is strategic guidance. It is not a guarantee of registration, enforcement or commercial success.

## 9. Applicant and ownership readiness

Applicant errors can persist for years and create later recordal or enforcement problems.

MarkReg should distinguish:

- customer;
- instructor;
- applicant;
- official owner;
- business operator;
- beneficial or economic controller where relevant;
- payer;
- signatory;
- acting Workplace.

The intake may reuse an existing entity record while requiring revalidation before a consequential boundary.

Applicant readiness may require:

- exact legal name;
- entity type;
- registration number;
- official address;
- country of organization;
- prior or alternative names;
- relationship to the brand;
- signatory identity and authority;
- consistency with existing rights;
- supporting document.

```text
Similar company name
≠ verified applicant identity
```

## 10. Mark identity readiness

A customer may provide several versions:

- plain word;
- stylized word;
- logo;
- color logo;
- black-and-white logo;
- Chinese characters;
- transliteration;
- packaging label;
- old and new commercial versions.

MarkReg should help determine the intended filing units and preserve the relationship between official and commercial assets.

Readiness may require:

- exact mark image;
- high-resolution source;
- color treatment;
- wording;
- non-Latin characters;
- translation;
- transliteration;
- mark description;
- disclaimer candidates;
- continuity with actual intended use.

AI may extract or draft. A qualified professional or governed review determines whether the formal representation is suitable for the jurisdiction and service.

## 11. Goods and services readiness

The Product should distinguish:

- actual current goods;
- near-term planned goods;
- strategically adjacent goods;
- defensive or speculative scope;
- goods outside current legal coverage;
- jurisdiction-specific acceptable wording;
- item or fee limits;
- evidence implications.

A customer may choose commercial breadth. The system should explain cost, examination and use consequences.

```text
Class selected
≠ goods scope ready

Broad business ambition
≠ acceptable filing specification
```

## 12. Search readiness

Search is not one binary step.

Possible scopes include:

- exact identity check;
- preliminary similarity search;
- jurisdiction-specific clearance search;
- visual or phonetic review;
- owner or series-mark investigation;
- professional opinion;
- multi-country staged search.

The intake should determine:

- the Decision the search supports;
- jurisdictions and classes;
- mark versions;
- goods context;
- timing;
- budget;
- acceptable risk;
- whether the result may change naming, filing or launch.

A search option should state what it cannot conclude.

## 13. Document readiness is jurisdiction- and stage-specific

Documents may include:

- Power of Attorney;
- business registration;
- identity document;
- priority document;
- translation;
- notarization or legalization;
- use evidence;
- consent;
- assignment or ownership evidence.

For each document, MarkReg should identify:

```text
Required before filing
May be filed later
Scan accepted for current stage
Original required
Notarization required
Legalization required
Provider confirmation pending
Validity or expiry condition
```

The system should avoid presenting a generic global document checklist as universal truth.

## 14. Formal Intake begins after a route is chosen

Formal Intake converts the accepted service scope into the structured facts and documents required for preparation and execution.

It should preserve:

- selected option and its version;
- accepted commercial scope;
- customer instruction;
- applicant and authority context;
- mark units;
- countries and routes;
- classes and goods;
- filing basis;
- documents;
- deadlines;
- Provider requirements;
- unresolved items;
- required Reviews and approvals.

```text
Decision-ready
≠ Formal Intake complete

Formal Intake complete
≠ execution ready
```

## 15. Readiness is multi-dimensional

One readiness status cannot represent every condition.

MarkReg should separately assess:

### Commercial readiness

- quotation valid;
- scope accepted;
- payment or credit condition satisfied;
- later-stage fees understood.

### Customer-decision readiness

- material options understood;
- assumptions visible;
- scope selected;
- risk acceptance recorded where needed.

### Identity and authority readiness

- customer and applicant identified;
- signatory authority sufficient;
- Relationship Owner and acting Workplace known.

### Documentary readiness

- required documents present;
- format and certification acceptable;
- originals or later-filed items tracked.

### Professional readiness

- relevant facts sufficient;
- search and risk reviewed;
- specification suitable;
- qualified Reviewer or Professional Authority available.

### Execution readiness

- exact package version approved;
- Provider or connector route accepted;
- Permission and idempotency controls present;
- deadline and expected Return defined.

A journey can be ready in one dimension and blocked in another.

## 16. Consequence increases require revalidation

Context that was valid during exploration may become stale.

Revalidation should occur before:

- final quotation or Customer Instruction;
- Formal Intake completion;
- professional Review;
- package approval;
- Provider instruction;
- payment;
- official submission;
- recordal or transaction completion.

Possible changes include:

- company name or address;
- ownership;
- signatory authority;
- mark version;
- goods scope;
- official fee;
- document requirement;
- Provider conflict or availability;
- deadline source;
- customer preference.

Reuse reduces repeated work. It does not create permanent validity.

## 17. Material change requires impact assessment

A material change may affect:

- recommendation;
- option set;
- quotation;
- Customer Instruction;
- Formal Intake;
- package;
- Review;
- approval;
- Provider instruction;
- deadline;
- cost.

The system should identify what was invalidated and what must be repeated.

For example, changing the applicant after a package is approved may require:

- new identity evidence;
- new POA;
- revised filing basis;
- repricing;
- new professional Review;
- new approval;
- Provider reconfirmation.

The old version remains in the audit trail.

## 18. Customer confirmation must be specific

A customer may confirm:

- factual identity;
- commercial preference;
- applicant choice;
- mark version;
- country plan;
- class and goods scope;
- document content;
- risk acceptance;
- price;
- filing instruction.

These confirmations have different consequences.

```text
Customer confirmed facts
≠ customer approved filing

Customer accepted price
≠ customer accepted every later fee

Customer uploaded document
≠ document validated
```

Silence should not become a filing, abandonment, settlement or payment instruction unless an applicable engagement and lawful rule explicitly support that consequence.

## 19. AI's role in intake

AI may help:

- extract business activities from a website or catalog;
- identify candidate classes and goods;
- compare applicant names and addresses;
- detect non-Latin characters;
- draft translation, transliteration or mark descriptions;
- summarize uploaded documents;
- identify missing information;
- propose country and scope options;
- explain trade-offs;
- detect inconsistent versions.

AI may not:

- invent applicant authority;
- convert inference into verified fact;
- select the final legal strategy autonomously;
- accept commercial terms for the customer;
- create Professional Review;
- authorize filing;
- suppress uncertainty to make the form appear complete.

## 20. A practical guided-intake example

A customer says:

> We are launching a smart home appliance brand in the United States and Europe. The logo contains three Chinese characters and an English name. We want to file quickly and keep the first-year cost under control.

MarkReg can initially infer and present:

### Known

- target markets include the US and EU;
- the mark has English and non-Latin elements;
- the business concerns smart home appliances;
- budget and speed matter.

### Candidate interpretation

- likely classes include relevant appliance and software or retail classes;
- separate word and device filings may be worth comparing;
- translation and transliteration will probably be required;
- US filing basis and evidence need confirmation;
- EU scope may differ from the US specification.

### High-value questions

1. Which products will launch in the next twelve months?
2. Has the English name or logo already been used in US commerce?
3. Which entity should own the rights?
4. Is the English word independently important enough for a separate filing?
5. Is EU-wide protection required now, or would selected national markets be sufficient?

### Option set

- minimum US word-mark filing;
- recommended US word plus device and EU staged filing;
- broader multi-class plan with deferred components;
- search-first route if naming flexibility remains.

The customer receives value before completing a jurisdictional form. The later Formal Intake is narrower because the major choices already have reasons.

## 21. Product and technical implications

Decision-ready intake requires:

- Business Context Snapshot and Need Brief;
- typed source facts, candidates, assumptions and Unknowns;
- progressive question engine;
- materiality and dependency rules;
- country, class, goods, applicant, mark and document modules;
- option-set versioning;
- explanation and trade-off records;
- customer confirmation events;
- formal-intake contracts;
- multi-dimensional readiness assessment;
- revalidation and material-change impact;
- professional Review routing;
- source and evidence provenance.

## 22. Commercial implications

Better intake can improve:

- time to useful proposal;
- customer understanding;
- quote acceptance;
- scope quality;
- later-stage fee transparency;
- document readiness;
- Provider instruction quality;
- reduction of rework and complaints.

The Product should not optimize conversion by hiding cost, uncertainty or future obligations.

A decision not to file, a staged plan or a request for more evidence may be the best commercial and professional Outcome.

## 23. The chapter lock

```text
Need before form
Candidate before fact
Explanation before apparent certainty
Decision readiness before Formal Intake
Formal Intake before preparation
Readiness before protected action
```

> MarkReg should ask fewer questions because it understands more of the decision structure. It should never ask fewer questions by pretending that ownership, scope, evidence, authority or professional judgment no longer matter.
