# Chapter 11 — Trademark Transactions Require Due Diligence, Commercial Separation and Official Recordal

A commercial match is not a completed trademark transfer.

Lite may discover a buyer, prepare a Trademark Marketing Pack, generate an Inquiry and qualify a Transaction Opportunity. MarkReg becomes relevant when the parties need governed verification, documents, professional work, payment milestones, recordal and evidence-backed completion.

```text
Qualified Transaction Opportunity
→ transaction scope and authority
→ due diligence
→ commercial terms and contract
→ closing conditions
→ assignment and delivery
→ jurisdiction-specific recordal
→ official update evidence
→ post-closing completion
```

Each stage has a different source, authority and effect.

## 1. The transaction enters MarkReg through a governed Handoff

A Lite-to-MarkReg transaction Handoff may include:

- originating Workplace;
- Buyer Relationship Owner;
- Seller Relationship Owner;
- source Listing and version;
- Resale Authorization;
- qualified buyer context;
- identified trademark rights;
- asking price and current commercial terms;
- known due-diligence findings;
- confidentiality and disclosure scope;
- commission and attribution rules;
- communication restrictions;
- proposed transaction mode;
- unresolved facts;
- intended MarkReg service scope.

The Handoff does not transfer the buyer, seller or customer relationship.

```text
Transaction Handoff
≠ Customer Relationship Transfer
≠ Seller Appointment
≠ Buyer Commitment
≠ Transfer Authority
```

MarkReg must perform its own intake and readiness review before accepting professional or operational responsibility.

## 2. Trademark, commercial asset and transaction package are different

The legal trademark right may be only one part of the commercial package.

Possible transaction assets include:

- registered or pending trademark rights;
- applications;
- common-law or unregistered rights where relevant;
- logos and brand files;
- domains;
- social accounts;
- websites;
- product designs;
- packaging;
- customer or distributor relationships;
- licences;
- marketing content;
- goodwill;
- inventory;
- business records.

MarkReg should identify which assets are:

```text
Included
Excluded
Subject to separate transfer
Not owned by seller
Licensed only
Unknown
Requires specialist review
```

```text
Trademark Transfer
≠ Business Transfer
≠ Domain Transfer
≠ Automatic Transfer of Goodwill or Other Assets
```

## 3. Transaction scope begins with an affected-right set

A brand name alone is not enough to define transaction scope.

The Affected-right Set should reconcile the commercial schedule against official and internal records and identify:

- matched rights;
- applications versus registrations;
- jurisdictions;
- classes and goods;
- current official owner;
- seller’s claimed authority;
- unmatched schedule entries;
- portfolio rights omitted from the schedule;
- duplicates;
- inactive, expired or disputed rights;
- pending assignments or recordals;
- included and excluded rights;
- review requirements.

```text
Same Brand Name
≠ Same Legal Right
```

Every right should retain its own transaction and official recordal state.

## 4. Parties and authority must be verified

The transaction may involve:

- seller or transferor;
- buyer or transferee;
- beneficial owner;
- official registered owner;
- customer or instructing party;
- director or signatory;
- broker or co-broker;
- Relationship Owner;
- Contracting Party;
- Payment Receiver;
- escrow or payment provider;
- legal adviser;
- local recordal Provider;
- tax or corporate adviser.

MarkReg should verify:

- legal identity;
- company registration;
- official ownership;
- authority to sell or buy;
- signatory authority;
- broker or intermediary authority;
- corporate approvals;
- conflicts;
- sanctions or compliance checks where applicable;
- contractual capacity.

```text
Seller Controls the Brand in Practice
≠ Seller Is Official Owner

Broker Has Listing Access
≠ Broker Can Bind Seller
```

## 5. Due diligence must precede commercial certainty

A governed due-diligence route is:

```text
Define scope
→ collect official and contractual evidence
→ validate parties and rights
→ classify issues
→ identify remediation
→ price and term impact
→ proceed, modify, pause or reject
```

Due diligence may cover:

- current status;
- owner and chain of title;
- mark representation;
- classes and goods;
- renewal and maintenance;
- use and non-use vulnerability;
- pending OA, opposition, appeal or cancellation;
- licences;
- security interests or encumbrances;
- coexistence or settlement agreements;
- restrictions on assignment;
- claims, disputes or enforcement history;
- certificates and official records;
- pending recordals;
- included digital and brand assets;
- seller authority;
- buyer’s intended use and expansion needs;
- recordal formalities;
- fees and timing.

```text
Document Uploaded
≠ Document Verified

Seller Statement
≠ Independent Finding

Due Diligence Complete
≠ Legal Clearance for Every Future Use
```

## 6. Due-diligence findings require categories, not one score

Findings may be classified as:

```text
Verified and satisfactory
Acceptable with disclosure
Remediable before signing
Remediable before closing
Requires price or term adjustment
Requires jurisdiction-specific opinion
Blocks recordal until resolved
Blocks transaction until resolved
Unknown because evidence is unavailable
Outside current scope
```

A single score can hide a decisive ownership gap or pending cancellation.

A buyer may accept a narrow goods scope but should not unknowingly accept an unresolved chain-of-title defect.

## 7. Chain of title must preserve every link

A Chain-of-title View may show:

```text
Original Applicant
→ Registered Owner
→ Name Change or Merger
→ Assignment
→ Current Official Owner
→ Contractual Seller
→ Proposed Buyer
```

Each link should include:

- parties;
- right and scope;
- execution date;
- effective date;
- document;
- source;
- signatory authority;
- official-record state;
- jurisdiction;
- unresolved gaps.

The Product must not fill a missing link with inference merely because the companies belong to the same group.

```text
Group Relationship
≠ Chain of Title
```

## 8. Contractual effect and official recordal are separate

Possible states include:

```text
Draft terms
Commercial terms agreed
Agreement signed
Conditions precedent outstanding
Effective between parties
Closing completed
Assignment document signed
Recordal package prepared
Recordal approved
Recordal filed
Office acknowledgement received
Official owner updated
Post-closing obligations pending
Transaction fully closed
```

```text
Signed Assignment
≠ Effective Transfer in Every Jurisdiction
≠ Official Recordal
≠ Updated Right Baseline
```

One country’s recordal does not update another country.

## 9. Transaction mode determines responsibility

Possible modes include:

### Direct owner sale

The seller and buyer contract directly. MarkReg may provide due diligence, document, coordination or recordal services.

### Referral

A Workplace introduces the opportunity but does not coordinate the full transaction.

### Co-brokerage

Buyer and seller relationships remain with separate Workplaces. Attribution, communication and commission rules must be explicit.

### White-label transaction support

The originating Workplace retains the customer relationship while MarkReg provides bounded professional or operational support.

### MarkReg-assisted transfer

The MarkReg-operated Workplace may act as Contracting Party or coordinator for defined services, subject to professional and legal boundaries.

### Platform-coordinated third-party transaction

MarkReg may orchestrate independent advisers, payment providers and local recordal Providers without becoming the legal seller, buyer or professional authority.

```text
Transaction Coordinator
≠ Seller
≠ Buyer
≠ Professional Representative
≠ Payment Custodian by Default
```

## 10. Commercial terms must be versioned

The commercial record may distinguish:

- owner net expectation;
- asking price;
- buyer indication;
- formal offer;
- counteroffer;
- permitted discount;
- deposit;
- balance;
- taxes;
- broker commission;
- co-broker split;
- due-diligence fee;
- document fee;
- recordal fee;
- official fees;
- Provider fees;
- refund or release conditions;
- currency and exchange basis.

```text
Displayed Asking Price
≠ Accepted Price

Accepted Price
≠ Signed Agreement

Signed Agreement
≠ Payment Settled

Payment Settled
≠ Official Recordal Completed
```

Material term changes should preserve prior versions and approvals.

## 11. Payment milestones must remain explicit

Possible milestones include:

```text
Deposit requested
Deposit paid
Deposit reconciled
Due-diligence fee paid
Closing payment initiated
Closing payment settled
Seller funds released
Commission payable
Commission paid
Official fees paid
Refund or adjustment pending
```

MarkReg may record instructions and evidence. This chapter does not authorize MarkOrbit to hold funds, operate escrow or become payment custodian.

```text
Payment Instruction
≠ Payment Custody

Payment Evidence
≠ Title Transfer
```

Where an external escrow or payment provider is used, its contractual role and evidence must be explicit.

## 12. The agreement cannot replace right-specific verification

The transaction agreement should identify:

- parties;
- affected rights;
- included assets;
- exclusions;
- purchase price;
- conditions precedent;
- warranties and representations;
- disclosure schedule;
- assignment documents;
- recordal obligations;
- cooperation duties;
- cost allocation;
- tax responsibility;
- confidentiality;
- non-circumvention where lawful and proportionate;
- dispute resolution;
- post-closing obligations.

A broad phrase such as “all trademarks related to the brand” may be commercially intended but is not enough for operational recordal.

## 13. Seller statements and professional findings remain separate

The Product should distinguish:

```text
Seller Statement
Official-source Fact
Professional Finding
Contractual Representation
Warranty
Condition Precedent
Buyer Risk Acceptance
```

For example, the seller may state that no dispute exists. Due diligence may find an active cancellation proceeding. The original statement, finding and contractual consequence should all remain visible.

## 14. Licence and ownership are different

A Licence Context should identify:

- licensed rights;
- territory;
- goods and services;
- exclusivity;
- term;
- sublicensing;
- quality control;
- authorized use;
- evidence permissions;
- recordal requirements;
- termination;
- confidentiality;
- effect of change of control or assignment.

```text
Licence
≠ Assignment
≠ Official Ownership
```

A licence may support use evidence or commercial continuity. It does not cure an ownership gap automatically.

## 15. Recordal strategy is jurisdiction-specific

Before recordal, MarkReg should determine:

- whether recordal is required, optional or recommended;
- timing relative to closing;
- current official owner;
- right-specific forms;
- original, notarized or legalized documents;
- translations;
- signatory requirements;
- fees;
- Provider requirements;
- office sequencing;
- interaction with renewal, OA or dispute;
- certificate reissue;
- official update evidence.

Possible routes include:

```text
Recordal before closing
Recordal filed after closing
Recordal and renewal in parallel
Recordal before renewal
Renewal under current official owner while recordal proceeds
Recordal blocked pending chain repair
```

No universal order applies to every jurisdiction.

## 16. Recordal uses exact packages and approvals

Each Recordal Package Candidate should identify:

- right and jurisdiction;
- current official owner;
- proposed owner;
- transaction and chain-of-title references;
- exact documents;
- translations and certifications;
- fees;
- Provider route;
- deadline;
- dependencies;
- package version;
- unresolved issues.

Approval should be right-, version-, cost- and route-specific.

```text
Agreement Approved
≠ Every Recordal Package Approved
```

## 17. Official update evidence closes only the relevant state

A Provider may return:

- submission receipt;
- filing reference;
- deficiency notice;
- acceptance notice;
- updated register extract;
- corrected certificate;
- partial acceptance;
- rejection;
- unknown state.

The Owning Service validates the official result before updating the Right Baseline.

```text
Provider Reports Recordal Filed
≠ Official Owner Updated
```

A transaction can be commercially closed while official recordal remains pending. The Product should display both states.

## 18. Partial completion is normal

A transaction may include rights in ten jurisdictions. Possible result:

- six official owner updates completed;
- two recordals pending;
- one deficiency response required;
- one right excluded after due diligence;
- domain transfer completed;
- social account transfer pending;
- final commission partly payable.

```text
Transaction Partially Complete
≠ Transaction Failed Entirely
≠ Every Right Transferred
```

Each item needs its own state and responsible actor.

## 19. Post-closing work remains part of the service plan

Post-closing obligations may include:

- official recordal;
- certificate reissue;
- domain and account transfer;
- delivery of brand files;
- removal of old access;
- licence termination or replacement;
- renewal;
- evidence preservation;
- customer and Provider notifications;
- Listing withdrawal;
- Resale Authorization termination;
- commission settlement;
- tax documents;
- transition support;
- buyer’s new applications.

The transaction should not be marked fully complete while material accepted obligations remain open.

## 20. Relationship and non-circumvention protection

Transaction work may expose buyer, seller, broker, customer and Provider information.

The Engagement should define:

- who owns each relationship;
- permitted direct contact;
- purpose of contact;
- customer and seller communication routes;
- attribution and commission;
- named-buyer protection;
- pre-existing relationships;
- customer choice;
- exceptions for professional necessity;
- duration and remedies;
- post-closing future-service rules.

```text
Access to Transaction Data
≠ Right to Bypass Relationship Owner
```

Protection should not create permanent customer captivity or prevent independently sourced relationships.

## 21. AI assistance and its limits

AI may:

- extract parties and dates;
- reconcile right schedules;
- detect owner mismatches;
- identify chain gaps;
- compare contracts and official records;
- organize due-diligence findings;
- draft transaction checklists;
- summarize open conditions;
- prepare candidate recordal data.

AI may not:

- decide whether title passed;
- infer missing chain links as fact;
- approve a transaction;
- accept an offer;
- release funds;
- convert a licence into ownership;
- decide professional legal sufficiency;
- declare recordal complete without official evidence;
- appoint Providers;
- waive customer or seller rights.

## 22. Failure modes

The transaction system fails when:

- a Listing is treated as sale authority;
- a buyer Inquiry becomes commitment;
- a brand name defines the affected-right set;
- seller control is treated as official ownership;
- due diligence becomes a document-upload checklist;
- one risk score hides a title defect;
- group-company relationships fill missing title links;
- contract signature becomes official recordal;
- buyer payment becomes proof of title;
- the platform’s Payment Receiver role becomes assumed escrow authority;
- one jurisdiction’s recordal updates all jurisdictions;
- a licence is presented as ownership;
- Provider email becomes official owner update;
- customer relationships move through the Handoff;
- post-closing obligations disappear after commercial closing.

## 25. The operating principle

```text
Verify the parties
Define every right and asset
Investigate before commitment
Separate commercial and official states
Version terms and payments
Use right-specific recordal Packages
Validate official updates
Keep post-closing work visible
```

> A trademark transaction is complete only when the parties, rights, authority, commercial terms, documents, payments, recordals and evidence have reached the states the parties actually agreed to—not when a marketplace label changes to sold.
