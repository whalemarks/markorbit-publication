# Chapter 06 — Filing Preparation Requires Exact Identity, Mark, Scope and Documents

A filing form gives the appearance of precision.

It contains applicant name, address, mark image, classes, goods and services, priority, representative details and signatures. Yet a form can be perfectly complete and professionally wrong.

The applicant may be the wrong entity. The address may be ordered incorrectly. The logo may differ from the intended mark. The goods may not describe the business. A Power of Attorney may be signed by the wrong person. A translation may be missing. A scan may be accepted for intake but not for filing. The customer may have approved a general plan without approving the exact Package.

MarkReg should therefore treat filing preparation as the construction of an exact, versioned and reviewable Filing Package Candidate.

```text
Accepted service scope
→ Formal Intake
→ Requirement Set
→ exact identity, mark and scope
→ Document and Evidence readiness
→ Filing Package Candidate
→ Professional Review
→ Filing Approval
```

## 1. Filing data is the result of prior decisions

A form usually assumes that the following decisions already exist:

- who should apply;
- which legal entity and address should be used;
- whether the word mark and device mark are separate filing units;
- which countries and routes are selected;
- which classes are required;
- which goods and services should be claimed;
- whether priority or use basis applies;
- which documents are required;
- who has authority to sign;
- which Provider will act where local qualification is required.

MarkReg begins before the form and preserves the reasoning that produced the final fields.

## 2. Filing Package Candidate is a governed artifact

A Filing Package Candidate should identify:

- originating Workplace;
- represented customer;
- applicant and authority context;
- jurisdiction and route;
- exact filing unit;
- mark representation and description;
- translation and transliteration;
- classes and goods/services version;
- priority and use basis;
- declarations and disclaimers;
- documents and signatures;
- Provider or connector route;
- fees and commercial conditions;
- source Jurisdiction Pack version;
- unresolved items;
- review state;
- expiry and revalidation triggers.

A Package Candidate remains proposed preparation. It is not a Filing Approval or official submission.

## 3. Customer, applicant, owner, payer and signatory are separate

The person paying for the service may not be the applicant. The client may be an agency acting for its customer. A parent company may instruct while a subsidiary applies. The recorded owner may differ from the beneficial business operator.

```text
Customer
≠ Applicant
≠ Owner
≠ Payer
≠ Signatory
≠ Instructor
```

MarkReg should make these identities visible where they affect authority, ownership, documents, priority, conflict, price or later recordal.

## 4. Applicant identity requires evidence

Applicant readiness may require:

- exact legal name;
- prior or translated names;
- company registration number;
- entity type;
- jurisdiction of incorporation;
- official or principal address;
- business address where relevant;
- signatory name and title;
- authority basis;
- ownership relationship to the brand;
- evidence of name or ownership changes.

Candidate matches from databases remain candidates until verified for the filing purpose.

```text
Similar company name
≠ same legal entity

Existing customer record
≠ current applicant authority
```

## 5. Address handling is substantive

Addresses may differ because of:

- local ordering conventions;
- transliteration;
- abbreviations;
- unit and building structure;
- registered office versus operating address;
- old and new addresses;
- database normalization;
- official form requirements.

MarkReg should preserve the source address and the filing representation rather than silently rewriting one into the other.

Where an office receipt presents the same components in a different order, the Product should support comparison and professional confirmation rather than immediately declaring an error.

## 6. Mark identity must be stable

The Package should distinguish:

- word mark;
- stylized word mark;
- device mark;
- composite mark;
- color claim;
- black-and-white representation;
- non-Latin characters;
- logo version;
- commercial display variation;
- candidate redesign.

```text
Commercial logo
≠ filed mark automatically

AI-refined image
≠ continuity established
```

A material visual change may require renewed search, customer confirmation, professional review or a separate filing unit.

## 7. Translation, transliteration and description are controlled content

For non-Latin or stylized marks, the Package may require:

- transliteration;
- English translation;
- literal and contextual meaning;
- mark description;
- character identification;
- color description;
- disclaimer language;
- consent or name statement.

AI may extract and draft these fields. The final version must be reviewed under the applicable jurisdictional requirements.

A previous office action may reveal a recurring requirement. That history can inform the new Package but should not be copied blindly if the mark or jurisdiction differs.

## 8. Goods and services begin with the real business

MarkReg should map:

```text
Business activity
→ primary goods and services
→ required coverage
→ recommended adjacent coverage
→ jurisdiction-acceptable wording
→ classes and fee units
```

It should not ask the customer to browse hundreds of Nice terms without context.

The Product may present:

- minimum plan;
- recommended plan;
- broader plan;
- cost-saving plan;
- future expansion plan.

Each plan should disclose legal coverage, commercial fit, cost and uncertainty.

## 9. Class number is not enough

A class may contain diverse goods. Two products in the same class may have little commercial relationship, while related services may appear in another class.

MarkReg should preserve:

- exact wording;
- source terminology;
- customer business meaning;
- jurisdiction adaptations;
- item-count constraints;
- fee consequences;
- proof-of-use implications where relevant;
- deleted or deferred items;
- reasons for material amendments.

## 10. Jurisdiction Packs operationalize difference

Jurisdictions vary in:

- acceptable goods wording;
- class and item limits;
- filing basis;
- priority documents;
- POA timing;
- original-document requirements;
- notarization or legalization;
- translation;
- applicant type;
- representative appointment;
- fees;
- online filing format.

The stable Product journey should use a versioned Jurisdiction Pack rather than hard-code one universal rule.

```text
Pack support for new filing
≠ support for every later lifecycle stage

Jurisdiction Pack
≠ official law
```

Each Pack needs source, effective date, Professional Owner, support state and change history.

## 11. Document requirements need typed states

A document should not have one generic uploaded status.

Possible states include:

```text
Not yet required
Required before filing
Permitted after filing
Draft prepared
Customer review required
Signed scan received
Original required
Original dispatched
Original received
Notarization required
Legalization required
Translation required
Provider confirmation pending
Validated for this purpose
Rejected or superseded
```

```text
Document uploaded
≠ document valid
≠ filing ready
```

## 12. Power of Attorney requires its own governance

A POA may vary by:

- applicant;
- jurisdiction;
- service;
- representative;
- signature method;
- corporate seal;
- notarization;
- legalization;
- original requirement;
- filing deadline;
- permitted late submission;
- reuse period.

MarkReg should record:

- template and version;
- represented applicant;
- signatory and authority;
- signature date;
- scan and original status;
- courier evidence;
- Provider confirmation;
- accepted purpose;
- expiry or supersession.

Past experience that a scan was accepted in some cases is useful evidence, not a universal rule.

## 13. Source conflict must remain visible

A requirement may differ across:

- official guidance;
- Provider quotation;
- Provider email;
- prior case experience;
- internal Knowledge;
- customer document;
- AI extraction.

When sources conflict, MarkReg should not choose silently. It should show:

- which source is authoritative or more current;
- what remains uncertain;
- who must confirm;
- which deadline is affected;
- whether the Package can proceed conditionally.

## 14. Commercial readiness does not equal filing readiness

A customer may accept a Quote and pay while key facts remain unresolved.

```text
Quote accepted
≠ Formal Intake sufficient

Payment received
≠ Package reviewed

Package reviewed
≠ Filing approved
```

MarkReg should display readiness by dimension:

- commercial;
- identity and authority;
- mark;
- scope;
- documentary;
- professional;
- Provider;
- execution.

## 15. Professional Review applies to an exact version

A valid Filing Package Review records:

- reviewer identity and qualification;
- Package version;
- jurisdiction and service;
- sources used;
- findings;
- required corrections;
- unresolved assumptions;
- recommendation;
- validity period.

A later material change can invalidate the Review.

```text
Reviewed once
≠ every later version reviewed
```

## 16. Filing Approval is a bounded authorization

Filing Approval should identify:

- approving actor;
- represented party;
- exact Package version;
- jurisdiction and filing unit;
- approved cost or fee basis where relevant;
- Provider or route;
- expiry;
- conditions;
- intended consequence.

It should not be inferred from:

- customer silence;
- Quote acceptance;
- payment;
- document upload;
- professional review alone;
- Provider readiness.

## 17. Material change and invalidation

Material changes include:

- applicant or owner;
- address;
- mark image or wording;
- translation;
- jurisdiction or route;
- classes or goods;
- priority;
- filing basis;
- document requirement;
- Provider;
- fee;
- deadline.

The Product should perform impact analysis across:

- search;
- recommendation;
- Quote;
- Formal Intake;
- Package Candidate;
- Review;
- Filing Approval;
- Provider instruction.

## 18. AI's role

AI may:

- extract entity data;
- compare addresses;
- detect mark text;
- propose translations;
- classify business descriptions;
- suggest goods candidates;
- identify missing fields;
- compare document versions;
- draft customer questions;
- detect contradictions.

AI may not:

- verify legal identity without evidence;
- decide applicant ownership;
- confirm signatory authority;
- publish a Jurisdiction Pack;
- approve goods wording;
- validate a POA by itself;
- grant Filing Approval;
- submit the filing autonomously.

## 19. Example: a three-character Chinese device mark

A customer wants to refile a previously abandoned US application for a stylized Chinese-character mark.

The prior office action identified two issues:

- required transliteration and translation;
- required mark description.

The new preparation flow should:

1. verify the exact new mark image;
2. confirm whether the characters are unchanged;
3. preserve the prior office-action source;
4. prepare the transliteration and translation as a candidate;
5. prepare the mark description;
6. obtain customer and professional confirmation;
7. verify applicant identity and goods;
8. assemble the new Package version;
9. record that the prior abandonment does not itself create a new filing instruction;
10. obtain Filing Approval.

## 20. Failure modes

Preparation fails when:

- a completed form is treated as a complete decision;
- customer and applicant are merged;
- database normalization overwrites source identity;
- a commercial logo silently replaces the filed mark;
- goods are selected only by class;
- a prior country's document rule becomes universal;
- uploaded files are assumed valid;
- scan and original status are merged;
- source conflicts are hidden;
- customer payment becomes Filing Approval;
- Review survives material change without revalidation;
- AI-generated translations are accepted without accountable review.

## 21. Product and technical implications

MarkReg requires:

- applicant and authority contexts;
- source-preserving identity normalization;
- filing-unit and mark asset versioning;
- structured goods decisions;
- Jurisdiction Pack versions;
- Requirement Sets;
- typed document states;
- POA and signature workflows;
- readiness assessments;
- Package Candidates;
- review and approval records;
- material-change impact analysis;
- stable Handoff references.

## 22. Commercial implications

Clear preparation reduces:

- avoidable rework;
- emergency document requests;
- wrong-applicant corrections;
- goods disputes;
- Provider clarification cost;
- filing delay;
- customer surprise.

Premium preparation and review may be chargeable, but ordinary factual transparency and authority warnings should not be withheld behind unnecessary upsells.

## 23. Operating principle

```text
Confirm the actor
Stabilize the mark
Translate the business into exact scope
Type every document requirement
Show every Unknown
Review the exact version
Approve the exact consequence
```

> A Filing Package is ready only when its identity, mark, scope, documents, authority and version are ready together.
