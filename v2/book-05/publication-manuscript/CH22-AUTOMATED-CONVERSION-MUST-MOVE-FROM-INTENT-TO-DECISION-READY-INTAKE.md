# Chapter 22 — Automated Conversion Must Move from Intent to Decision-ready Intake

A visitor reading a trademark article is not yet a customer. A person asking a question is not yet instructing a filing. A user selecting an option is not yet creating an Order.

Automated conversion is valuable only when it preserves those distinctions while reducing the effort required to reach a real decision.

```text
Content or Service Interaction
→ Intent Candidate
→ Progressive Clarification
→ Service Need Candidate
→ Explainable Options
→ Decision-ready Intake
→ Commercial and Professional Handoff
```

The objective is not to maximize form submissions. It is to help the right user reach the right service route with enough understanding to make an accountable choice.

## 1. Conversion begins with context

The user may arrive from:

- a country service page;
- a maintenance deadline article;
- an OA explanation;
- a fee calculator;
- a transaction page;
- a social post created in Lite;
- a partner Workplace page;
- a generated answer citing MarkReg;
- a direct service search.

The conversion flow should retain:

- source page and version;
- represented Workplace;
- jurisdiction and service topic;
- claims already shown;
- fee or deadline context;
- campaign or referral provenance;
- consent and contact state.

The user should not need to repeat information already provided by the governed source, but the system must not convert contextual inference into confirmed fact silently.

## 2. Intent is provisional

A visitor may be:

- researching generally;
- comparing providers;
- acting for a client;
- seeking urgent help;
- checking an existing Matter;
- considering a future filing;
- asking about an unrelated jurisdiction;
- looking for educational content only.

Possible states include:

```text
Informational Interest
Possible Service Need
Qualified Service Need
Existing-customer Obligation
Existing Matter Inquiry
Partner-owned Opportunity
Unsupported or Misrouted Intent
```

```text
Page View
≠ Service Need

Service Need
≠ Customer Instruction
```

## 3. Ask less by reusing authorized context

Question reduction should come from:

- the page topic;
- explicit user selections;
- authenticated Workplace context;
- permitted customer and portfolio data;
- official-record candidates;
- prior approved answers;
- bounded inference;
- progressive confirmation.

It should not come from assuming that:

- the visitor is the trademark owner;
- a company name is the applicant;
- the page jurisdiction is the intended jurisdiction;
- a registration number belongs to the visitor;
- public contact information creates permission;
- the lowest-price option is preferred.

## 4. Every field needs an epistemic state

Conversion data should distinguish:

```text
User-confirmed Fact
Authorized Reused Fact
Source-derived Candidate
AI Inference
Default Assumption
Professional Recommendation
Unknown
Not Applicable
Requires Professional Advice
```

The interface may feel simple while the underlying state remains precise.

## 5. Ask only questions that change the route

High-value questions include:

- which jurisdiction and right are involved;
- whether the user is the owner, applicant, agent or another party;
- whether a deadline or official notice exists;
- whether the mark is already used;
- whether the owner or address changed;
- whether an original document is available;
- whether speed, cost or protection breadth is the priority;
- whether the user wants information, quotation, review or execution.

Each question should explain:

- why it matters;
- which route it changes;
- what happens if it remains Unknown;
- whether professional review is required.

## 6. Dynamic questioning should be risk-sensitive

The system may stop early when a low-risk service route is clear. It should ask more when:

- applicant authority is uncertain;
- a legal deadline may be imminent;
- the user requests restoration;
- ownership changed;
- evidence sufficiency matters;
- multiple jurisdictions interact;
- a dispute or opposition exists;
- the requested service may be unavailable.

```text
Fewer Questions
≠ Fewer Material Facts
```

## 7. Recommendations should be option sets

A Decision-ready Intake should present comparable routes such as:

```text
Minimum Required Route
Recommended Route
Broader Strategic Route
Cost-saving Alternative
Urgent Protective Route
Professional Review Before Action
No Safe Route Yet
```

Each option should state:

- purpose;
- included and excluded work;
- required documents;
- known fees and assumptions;
- timing;
- risks;
- customer responsibilities;
- professional-review requirements;
- next decision.

The Product should not hide a strategically relevant route merely because another route creates higher margin.

## 8. Pricing must remain conditional and decomposed

Automated pricing may use:

- jurisdiction;
- class count;
- service phase;
- official fees;
- Provider route;
- document requirements;
- urgency;
- translation;
- review complexity;
- customer or partner pricing rules.

The output should distinguish:

```text
Indicative Estimate
Rule-based Quote Candidate
Reviewed Quotation
Customer-accepted Quotation
Invoice
Payment
```

```text
Calculator Result
≠ Binding Quote

Quote Accepted
≠ Filing Authorized
```

## 9. The Relationship Owner must remain visible

Automated conversion may operate for:

- a MarkReg-operated Workplace;
- an independent partner Workplace;
- a white-label service page;
- a co-delivery route;
- a bounded capability service.

The conversion record should preserve:

- Demand Originator;
- Relationship Owner;
- represented Workplace;
- referral source;
- service route;
- commercial attribution;
- permitted customer contact.

A centrally operated conversion engine does not automatically centralize the customer relationship.

## 10. Existing customers require a different route

A user entering an existing registration number or Matter reference may be asking about an accepted obligation.

The system should identify whether the situation is:

```text
Existing Matter Communication
Accepted Customer Obligation
New Service Opportunity
Former-customer Reactivation
Unknown Relationship — Review Required
```

An existing duty must not be treated as a fresh lead merely to improve conversion metrics.

## 11. Contact collection needs purpose and consent

The conversion flow should state:

- why contact information is requested;
- which Workplace will use it;
- whether the purpose is a requested response, service administration or marketing;
- what channels may be used;
- how the user can withdraw marketing consent;
- whether data will be shared with a Provider later and under what Handoff.

```text
Contact Submitted
≠ General Marketing Consent
≠ Provider Appointment
```

## 12. AI may interpret but not bind

AI may:

- classify intent;
- extract identifiers;
- propose missing questions;
- summarize the user's objective;
- generate option explanations;
- identify contradictions;
- draft a Quote Candidate;
- recommend escalation.

AI may not:

- fabricate official status;
- confirm legal availability;
- accept customer instruction;
- approve fees on behalf of a Workplace;
- appoint a Provider;
- file or abandon a Matter;
- turn silence into consent.

## 13. Decision-ready Intake is a distinct Product state

A Decision-ready Intake means the user has enough structured information to compare routes.

It may include:

- objective;
- represented parties;
- jurisdiction and trademark references;
- known facts and Unknowns;
- candidate service need;
- options;
- indicative or reviewed fees;
- material risks;
- required next decisions.

It does not mean:

```text
Decision-ready Intake
≠ Formal Intake Complete
≠ Order Created
≠ Matter Created
≠ Payment Received
≠ Execution Ready
```

## 14. Formalization requires explicit Handoff

After a user chooses a route, the system creates a Handoff candidate containing:

- selected option and version;
- customer-confirmed facts;
- unresolved Unknowns;
- represented authority;
- Relationship Owner;
- commercial terms;
- consent and contact rules;
- required documents;
- professional-review requirements;
- source-content lineage;
- next Owning Service.

The receiving service performs its own validation before creating Customer, Order, Matter, Payment or Assignment records.

## 15. Urgent conversion needs safe escalation

A user may arrive with an imminent deadline. The system should:

- identify the urgency candidate;
- avoid guaranteeing the deadline;
- request the official notice or registration data;
- route to qualified review;
- state what can and cannot be done before confirmation;
- record the communication and response window.

Urgency should not be used as a manipulative sales tactic.

```text
Urgent Language
≠ Verified Urgent Deadline
```

## 16. Failed and abandoned flows remain useful evidence

A user may stop because:

- the price is too high;
- the route is unclear;
- required documents are unavailable;
- the service is not viable;
- trust is insufficient;
- the questions are too burdensome;
- they only wanted information.

The Product should record structured, privacy-respecting reasons without treating every abandonment as a sales failure.

An honest “no viable route” can be a successful Product outcome.

## 17. Conversion metrics should measure quality

Useful metrics include:

- intent-classification accuracy;
- number of material questions;
- Decision-ready completion;
- route-change rate after professional review;
- Quote-to-Instruction conversion;
- low-quality inquiry rate;
- unsupported-service rate;
- duplicate-customer detection;
- refund and correction rate;
- customer comprehension;
- time to accountable decision;
- relationship-attribution accuracy.

```text
More Leads
≠ Better Conversion

Higher Conversion Rate
≠ Correct Service Matching
```

## 18. Experimentation boundaries

The Product may test:

- question order;
- layout;
- explanation length;
- option presentation;
- CTA language;
- use of examples;
- reminder timing where permitted.

It must not experiment with different material truths, legal risks, official deadlines or hidden fees across user groups.

```text
Conversion Experiment
≠ Permission to Experiment with Professional Truth
```

## 19. Product implications

MarkReg requires:

- source-context retention;
- typed intent states;
- epistemic field states;
- progressive-question logic;
- explainable option generation;
- conditional pricing;
- relationship and consent records;
- Decision-ready Intake;
- formalization Handoff;
- urgent escalation;
- conversion-quality analytics;
- experiment governance.

## 20. Commercial implications

Governed conversion can improve:

- qualified demand;
- customer confidence;
- partner white-label adoption;
- quote preparation cost;
- service selection;
- maintenance and recovery capture;
- cross-border route understanding.

The commercial objective should be qualified, informed conversion—not extracting commitment from a confused user.

## 21. Operating principle

```text
Retain context
Infer provisionally
Ask what changes the route
Explain options
Preserve relationship ownership
Formalize explicitly
Measure correct conversion
```

> Automated conversion is successful when the user reaches an informed, traceable decision with less friction—not when the platform turns every expression of interest into a sales record.
