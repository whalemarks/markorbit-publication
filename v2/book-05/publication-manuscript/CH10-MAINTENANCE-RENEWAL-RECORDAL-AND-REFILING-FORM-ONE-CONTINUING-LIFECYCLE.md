# Chapter 10 — Maintenance, Renewal, Recordal and Re-filing Form One Continuing Lifecycle

Post-registration work is often fragmented into isolated services:

- renewal;
- declaration of use;
- address change;
- owner change;
- representative change;
- restoration;
- re-filing;
- certificate correction;
- evidence collection.

To the customer, these may appear to be unrelated future tasks. Operationally, they are connected consequences of the same registered right.

MarkReg should treat them as one continuing-right lifecycle while preserving the independent authority, scope and evidence of each action.

```text
Right Baseline
→ obligation and vulnerability map
→ verified deadline and responsibility
→ customer and professional decision
→ service-specific Package
→ review and approval
→ governed execution
→ official acknowledgement or update
→ refreshed Right Baseline
```

## 1. Continuing lifecycle does not mean one status

A right may be:

- officially registered;
- within a use-declaration period;
- approaching renewal;
- affected by an owner mismatch;
- under cancellation challenge;
- weak in use evidence;
- waiting for an original document;
- subject to a licence;
- selected for non-renewal;
- being considered for re-filing.

These conditions can coexist.

```text
Registered
≠ Maintenance-compliant
≠ Renewal-ready
≠ Ownership-correct
≠ Evidence-sufficient
≠ Dispute-free
```

The Product should maintain distinct obligation and vulnerability records rather than one green or red portfolio status.

## 2. The Maintenance Obligation Set

Each obligation should be represented separately with:

- trademark right and jurisdiction;
- obligation type;
- triggering official event;
- sourced rule or Jurisdiction Pack version;
- opening, ordinary, late and restoration dates;
- required documents, declarations and evidence;
- affected classes and goods or services;
- customer decision requirements;
- professional review requirements;
- Provider or representative requirements;
- official and professional fees;
- responsible actors;
- current state;
- consequence of omission;
- uncertainty and supersession.

Possible obligations include:

- use declaration;
- declaration of incontestability where applicable;
- renewal;
- registration-stage fee;
- evidence preservation;
- representative or address-for-service maintenance;
- office-request response;
- ownership correction;
- certificate correction;
- recordal after merger or assignment;
- licence-related action;
- opposition, cancellation or restoration deadline.

A right can have several simultaneous obligations.

## 3. Deadlines require source hierarchy

A deadline may come from:

```text
Official stated date
Calculated date from official event
Provider-reported date
Professional interpretation
Internal safety date
Customer reminder date
Grace-period date
Restoration deadline
```

These dates should not be collapsed.

A Deadline Record should preserve:

- source;
- triggering event;
- calculation rule;
- jurisdiction;
- time zone;
- holiday treatment;
- ordinary and exceptional periods;
- verifier;
- confidence;
- correction history;
- responsible owner;
- escalation threshold.

```text
Reminder Date
≠ Official Deadline

Calendar Entry
≠ Deadline Verification
```

When official and Provider sources conflict, the Product should preserve both, apply a conservative internal target where appropriate and require professional verification.

## 4. Maintenance state belongs to the individual obligation

An obligation may be:

```text
Not Yet Open
Planned
Evidence Collection Active
Preparation Active
Waiting for Customer
Waiting for Provider
Ready for Review
Reviewed
Approved
Execution Requested
Filed
Provider Reports Filed
Officially Acknowledged
Officially Updated
Late
Grace Period
Restoration Required
Missed
Unknown — Reconciliation Required
Closed by Deliberate Decision
```

A generic status such as `Maintenance in progress` is not enough when one deadline is verified, one evidence issue is unresolved and one filing route remains unknown.

## 5. Use evidence is a continuing record, not a last-minute upload

Evidence may be needed for:

- declarations of use;
- maintenance filings;
- renewal in selected jurisdictions;
- opposition or cancellation defense;
- enforcement;
- licence control;
- transaction due diligence;
- portfolio decisions.

A Use-Evidence Coverage Record may map:

- mark version;
- goods and services;
- territory;
- period;
- owner, licensee or actual user;
- product or service context;
- sales materials;
- advertising;
- webpages and marketplace records;
- invoices and transactions;
- packaging and labels;
- source and custodian;
- confidentiality and use permission;
- professional inclusion assessment;
- gaps and next collection action.

```text
Evidence Exists
≠ Evidence Is Legally Sufficient

Concept Mockup
≠ Actual Use Evidence

Published Social Image
≠ Qualifying Use by Default
```

The Product should help collect and organize evidence early without allowing AI or automation to decide legal sufficiency.

## 6. Renewal begins from a current Right Baseline

Before preparing renewal, MarkReg should verify:

- official registration number;
- current owner;
- current representative;
- registered classes and goods;
- ordinary renewal window;
- grace or restoration route;
- active proceedings;
- pending recordals;
- declarations and evidence requirements;
- current official fees;
- Provider requirements;
- source freshness.

A spreadsheet row copied years earlier is not enough authority for a protected renewal action.

## 7. Renewal requires an exact Package

A Renewal Package Candidate should identify:

- exact right and baseline version;
- current owner basis;
- full or partial renewal scope;
- continuing and lapsing classes or items;
- ordinary, late or restoration route;
- documents and evidence;
- official, professional and Provider costs;
- tax, surcharge and currency;
- Provider or connector route;
- deadline;
- dependencies;
- package version;
- unresolved issues.

```text
Reminder Sent
≠ Renewal Instructed

Renewal Form Prepared
≠ Renewal Approved

Renewal Filed
≠ Renewed Status Verified
```

## 8. Full, partial and non-renewal decisions differ

Where the jurisdiction permits, a customer may:

- renew the full scope;
- renew selected classes;
- allow selected goods to lapse;
- defer a related new filing;
- choose not to renew;
- attempt restoration;
- re-file instead.

The decision surface should explain:

- continuing rights;
- lapsing scope;
- cost difference;
- irreversible consequences;
- restoration limits;
- portfolio gaps;
- use or evidence implications;
- related rights that remain independent.

A deliberate Non-Renewal Decision must be recorded with actor, authority, scope and consequence.

```text
Customer Silence
≠ Non-Renewal Decision

Non-payment
≠ Informed Abandonment Decision
```

## 9. Ownership and recordal can block or reshape renewal

Possible states include:

```text
Official owner confirmed
Name change not recorded
Address change pending
Merger evidence available
Assignment signed but not recorded
Instructor differs from owner
Ownership disputed
Chain-of-title incomplete
```

Depending on the jurisdiction and purpose, the professional route may require:

- recordal before renewal;
- renewal under the current official owner;
- combined filing;
- parallel actions;
- additional evidence;
- suspension until ownership is resolved.

The Product must not apply one universal shortcut.

## 10. Recordal begins with a typed change context

A change should identify:

- change type;
- legal or factual cause;
- current official value;
- customer or business claim;
- proposed value;
- effective date;
- source;
- affected rights and jurisdictions;
- signatory authority;
- supporting documents;
- Provider and fee requirements;
- interaction with renewal, dispute or transaction work;
- unresolved conflicts.

Name change, address change, merger, assignment, representative change and clerical correction are not one generic `update` action.

## 11. One business event may require many official actions

A corporate reorganization may affect:

- pending applications;
- registrations;
- oppositions;
- renewals;
- licences;
- local representatives;
- certificates;
- several countries.

MarkReg can group the business initiative, but each official action retains its own:

- right;
- jurisdiction;
- current official value;
- requirement set;
- package;
- approval;
- execution identity;
- official acknowledgement;
- result.

```text
One Corporate Event
≠ One Global Recordal
```

The initiative may therefore be partially complete.

## 12. Current, claimed, proposed and recorded values remain separate

The Product should preserve a diff:

```text
Current Official Value
→ Customer or Business Claim
→ Proposed Filed Value
→ Officially Recorded Value
```

For an address, this may involve:

- original-language address;
- English translation;
- jurisdictional formatting;
- office normalization;
- unit or postal-code corrections.

For an owner, it may involve:

- historical registered owner;
- successor by merger;
- contractual transferee;
- proposed recorded owner;
- current official owner.

Display normalization must not silently become a legal record.

## 13. Recordal requires version-specific approval

A Recordal Package Candidate should contain:

- change-context reference;
- current and proposed values;
- affected-right list;
- documents and authority;
- form or statement content;
- Jurisdiction Pack and source versions;
- fees;
- Provider route;
- deadline and sequence;
- dependencies;
- unresolved issues;
- exact version.

Approval should bind the exact rights, changes, documents, fees and route.

```text
CRM Data Edited
≠ Recordal Approved

Assignment Document Uploaded
≠ Ownership Update Approved
```

## 14. Restoration and re-filing require a real route assessment

A missed deadline or abandoned right may lead to:

```text
Late filing available
Restoration available
Petition or reinstatement available
Belated document filing available
Re-filing required
No viable recovery route
Professional advice required
```

The Product should verify:

- current official status;
- ordinary and exceptional deadlines;
- statutory or procedural recovery route;
- required explanation or evidence;
- additional fees;
- Provider capability;
- remaining rights or priority consequences;
- whether a new application is preferable;
- customer decision and urgency.

```text
Expired or Abandoned
≠ Automatically Restorable

Re-filing Possible
≠ Prior Rights or Priority Preserved
```

## 15. Recovery advice must not become false certainty

Provider advice may differ, especially where offices apply local practice inconsistently.

MarkReg should record:

- official rule or portal source;
- Provider opinion;
- factual assumptions;
- deadline calculation;
- required documents;
- estimated success and uncertainty;
- alternative routes;
- customer cost decision;
- professional recommendation.

The system should not state “can be restored” when the route is merely being investigated.

## 16. Re-filing must reuse context without copying stale truth

A re-filing journey may reuse:

- customer and applicant context;
- mark representation;
- goods and service history;
- translations;
- prior office issues;
- documents;
- search findings;
- Provider relationships.

Every reused item must be revalidated for the new filing purpose.

A prior OA may reveal required corrections, such as:

- translation;
- transliteration;
- mark description;
- applicant identity;
- goods wording;
- disclaimer;
- evidence basis.

```text
Reuse Prior Context
≠ Repeat Prior Error
```

## 17. Continuing-right services require explicit responsibility

For each obligation, the system should identify:

- Relationship Owner;
- Professional Owner;
- Deadline Owner;
- Evidence Owner;
- Document Owner;
- Customer Decision Owner;
- Finance Owner;
- Provider Coordinator;
- backup and escalation path.

A reminder system cannot be the accountable owner.

```text
Automated Reminder
≠ Accepted Monitoring Responsibility
```

A Workplace should not imply that it monitors all future deadlines unless the service scope, source coverage and responsibility are explicit.

## 18. Communication must distinguish obligation from opportunity

An existing accepted monitoring duty belongs in the obligation workflow.

A right identified from public data without an existing customer relationship may create a candidate service opportunity, subject to entity, relationship, consent and outreach governance.

```text
Accepted Customer Duty
≠ Sales Lead

Public Maintenance Signal
≠ Customer Instruction
```

This prevents organizations from treating missed internal obligations as marketing opportunities or using public deadlines to justify aggressive outreach.

## 19. Portfolio coordination without global authority

A Portfolio Continuity View may show:

- upcoming obligations;
- late or restoration risks;
- ownership inconsistencies;
- weak evidence;
- representative changes;
- duplicate rights;
- rights no longer commercially relevant;
- potential re-filing;
- dispute and transaction dependencies.

Every aggregate must resolve back to the individual right, source, owner, state and action.

```text
Portfolio View
≠ Global Official Record
≠ Automatic Filing Plan
```

## 20. AI assistance and its limits

AI may:

- extract official dates;
- calculate candidate windows;
- compare owners and addresses;
- classify evidence;
- draft reminders;
- identify likely recordal dependencies;
- compare prior and current requirements;
- prepare candidate renewal or re-filing checklists.

AI may not:

- establish the controlling deadline alone;
- decide evidence sufficiency;
- decide ownership;
- select rights to abandon;
- authorize partial renewal;
- declare restoration available conclusively;
- create a Recordal Approval;
- mark an official update complete without sourced evidence;
- infer customer instruction from silence.

## 21. Failure modes

The continuing lifecycle fails when:

- one renewal date represents every obligation;
- reminder dates are presented as official deadlines;
- use evidence is collected only at the last moment;
- payment is treated as renewal instruction;
- customer silence becomes non-renewal;
- assignment signature becomes official owner update;
- one corporate change is shown as globally recorded;
- CRM edits replace official recordal;
- re-filing repeats unresolved prior defects;
- a stale spreadsheet drives protected action;
- Provider advice becomes unquestioned law;
- portfolio signals become automatic filings;
- future monitoring is implied without responsibility;
- one challenged right makes the entire portfolio appear invalid.

## 22. Product and technical implications

MarkReg requires:

- Right Baselines;
- Maintenance Obligation Sets;
- Use-Evidence Coverage Records;
- sourced Deadline Records;
- Renewal Package Candidates;
- Non-Renewal Decisions;
- Recordal Contexts and Packages;
- restoration and re-filing route assessments;
- version-specific approvals;
- official acknowledgement and update evidence;
- responsibility and escalation;
- change-impact propagation;
- independent-right Portfolio views.

## 23. Commercial implications

Continuing-right services can support:

- lifecycle subscriptions;
- monitoring services;
- evidence planning and review;
- renewal;
- late and restoration work;
- owner and address recordals;
- re-filing;
- portfolio remediation;
- professional review;
- Provider fulfillment.

Pricing should separate ordinary work, late fees, official fees, Provider fees, recordal dependencies, evidence review and exceptional recovery work.

## 24. The operating principle

```text
Start from a current Right Baseline
Model each obligation separately
Verify deadlines and ownership
Collect evidence continuously
Require exact customer and professional decisions
Execute through service-specific Packages
Validate official outcomes
Refresh the baseline
```

> Maintenance, renewal, recordal and re-filing are not disconnected after-sales services. They are the continuing governance of a legal right whose facts, evidence, ownership and obligations change over time.
