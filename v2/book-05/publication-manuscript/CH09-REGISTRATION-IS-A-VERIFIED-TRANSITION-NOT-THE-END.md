# Chapter 09 — Registration Is a Verified Transition, Not the End

Many trademark services are designed around a single celebratory moment:

> The trademark is registered.

That moment matters. It is also frequently misunderstood.

A Provider may report registration before the official record is available. A certificate may arrive later, contain an error, or show a historical state that differs from the current register. The registered goods may be narrower than the approved filing scope. The recorded owner may differ from the customer’s expected company. A registration may already carry maintenance, use, recordal or dispute obligations.

MarkReg should therefore treat registration as a verified lifecycle transition rather than a closing label.

```text
Prosecution-stage result candidate
→ official-source verification
→ registered-scope comparison
→ owner and certificate reconciliation
→ Right Baseline
→ continuing obligations
→ customer delivery and portfolio continuity
```

## 1. Registration begins with sourced official evidence

The Product should not create a registered-right state merely because:

- a Provider says “approved” or “registered”;
- a government fee was paid;
- a certificate file was received;
- an internal spreadsheet says registered;
- an estimated registration date passed;
- an AI extraction classified a document as a certificate;
- a customer or colleague expects the result.

A registration outcome should preserve:

- jurisdiction and office;
- official application and registration identifiers;
- official event or register source;
- registration and effective dates;
- recorded owner;
- mark representation;
- registered classes and goods or services;
- limitations, disclaimers, exclusions and conditions;
- source retrieval time;
- confidence, conflict and correction status;
- certificate relationship;
- reviewer or validation record.

```text
Provider Report
≠ Official Registration

Internal Status
≠ Official Registration

Certificate Image
≠ Current Official Register
```

A Provider report can initiate verification. It cannot manufacture the official result.

## 2. The official record, certificate and Product projection are different artifacts

A mature Product keeps several representations separate.

### Official registration record

The office-controlled current or historical record is the primary source for official status, owner and scope, subject to jurisdictional source limitations.

### Certificate

The certificate is an office-issued representation of registration. It may be electronic, paper, color, black-and-white, corrected, reissued or delayed.

### Certificate file

The file received by email, portal, scan or courier is evidence that must be linked to its source and version. It may be incomplete, illegible or a copy.

### Registration Outcome Record

MarkReg may preserve a sourced historical record of what was verified at the registration transition.

### Right Baseline

The Right Baseline is the reviewed operational starting point for future maintenance, evidence, recordal, transaction and portfolio work.

### Product projection

The user-facing explanation derives from the underlying records and should preserve source, freshness and uncertainty.

```text
Official Record
≠ Certificate
≠ Certificate File
≠ Registration Outcome Record
≠ Right Baseline
≠ Product Projection
```

The distinctions are operationally important. A certificate may remain historically valid evidence while the register later reflects a name change, assignment, limitation or correction.

## 3. Registration requires a scope diff

The Product should compare at least three versions:

```text
Approved Filing Package
→ Officially Acknowledged Application
→ Officially Registered Scope
```

The comparison may reveal:

- deleted classes;
- deleted or narrowed goods;
- office-normalized descriptions;
- disclaimers;
- territorial or procedural limitations;
- owner normalization;
- mark-description changes;
- translation or transliteration changes;
- prosecution-stage amendments;
- different registration dates by class or right.

The customer should not receive a report claiming the original requested scope was registered when the final right is narrower.

### Example

The approved Package contains:

```text
Class 11
lighting apparatus;
electric coffee machines;
air fryers;
water purification apparatus
```

After examination, the registered record contains:

```text
Class 11
lighting apparatus;
electric coffee machines;
air fryers
```

MarkReg should show the removed item and the decision history that led to the amendment.

```text
Original Requested Scope
≠ Registered Scope
```

## 4. Owner verification is part of registration verification

The registered owner should be compared with:

- the approved applicant;
- the official filing acknowledgement;
- the current official record;
- the customer’s legal-entity record;
- the business operator or brand user;
- pending name changes, mergers or assignments;
- the instructor and signatory authority.

Possible states include:

```text
Owner matches approved applicant
Office normalized owner name
Minor address-format difference
Name change pending
Assignment signed but not recorded
Different group company recorded
Owner conflict requiring professional review
Source conflict unresolved
```

The Product must not silently replace the official owner with the company the customer expected to own the right.

```text
Expected Business Owner
≠ Official Registered Owner
```

A mismatch may affect maintenance, enforcement, licensing, transactions, use evidence and later recordal.

## 5. Certificate handling is a controlled lifecycle

Certificate delivery may pass through several states:

```text
Certificate not yet issued
Electronic certificate available
Provider copy received
Scan quality review required
Original paper certificate received by Provider
Original dispatched by courier
Original received by Workplace
Customer delivery pending
Customer delivery confirmed
Correction or reissue required
Archived with source and custody record
```

The system should record:

- digital or paper format;
- original, certified copy, ordinary copy or scan;
- color and page completeness;
- source sender;
- received date;
- courier tracking where relevant;
- custody holder;
- customer-delivery method;
- correction request;
- retention and access rules.

A black-and-white certificate may be authentic. A color certificate may still contain incorrect data. Visual appearance alone is not validation.

## 6. Certificate absence and registration absence are different

A certificate may be delayed while the official register already shows registration. Conversely, a certificate may exist while later events have changed the right.

```text
Certificate Not Received
≠ Registration Did Not Occur

Certificate Received
≠ Right Remains Unchanged
```

MarkReg should explain the situation accurately:

- “Registration is verified in the official record; the certificate is pending.”
- “The certificate copy has been received; current official status is being refreshed.”
- “The certificate differs from the current official owner record; both are retained and the difference is under review.”

## 7. The Right Baseline starts the continuing lifecycle

The Right Baseline should contain:

- jurisdiction and identifiers;
- official owner and representative;
- registered mark;
- registered classes and goods or services;
- registration, priority and term dates;
- official source and retrieval time;
- certificate state;
- limitations and disclaimers;
- active proceedings;
- known use obligations;
- maintenance and renewal obligations;
- current ownership or recordal issues;
- next verified deadline;
- evidence sources;
- unresolved conflicts.

A later official change creates a new baseline version. It does not rewrite the historical registration event.

```text
Registration Outcome v1
→ Right Baseline v1
→ Official Change
→ Right Baseline v2
```

## 8. Registration does not remove vulnerability

A registered trademark may remain vulnerable because of:

- non-use;
- incorrect ownership;
- incomplete recordal;
- pending opposition, appeal or cancellation;
- disclaimer or scope limitation;
- maintenance filing requirements;
- representative or address requirements;
- evidence weakness;
- licence expiry;
- unpaid later-stage fees;
- official-record error;
- inconsistent mark use;
- third-party challenge.

MarkReg should display registration and vulnerability separately.

```text
Registered
≠ Maintenance-compliant
≠ Secure from Challenge
≠ Correctly Owned
≠ Commercially Used
```

## 9. Registration may not apply to the entire initiative

A brand initiative can contain independent rights:

- US word application;
- US device application;
- EU word application;
- UK registration;
- China application;
- Madrid designation;
- separate classes or divisional rights.

One right may register while others remain under examination, opposition, refusal or planning.

A Portfolio view can summarize, but it cannot create one global status.

```text
One Right Registered
≠ Brand Registered Everywhere
```

## 10. Registration creates customer communication duties

The registration communication should explain:

- what is officially registered;
- the verified owner;
- the exact registered scope;
- differences from the filed or requested scope;
- certificate state;
- registration and term dates;
- current limitations or proceedings;
- next obligations;
- use-evidence recommendations;
- whether any owner, address or representative correction is required;
- what is included in the current service;
- what future services require separate instruction.

A celebratory message should not hide material limitations.

### Client-facing language

Prefer:

> Registration has been verified in the official record. The registered scope is narrower than the original application in one item, as shown in the attached comparison. The electronic certificate has been received. The next official renewal window is not yet open, but an evidence-maintenance plan is recommended.

Avoid:

> Everything is completed forever.

## 11. Customer delivery does not close every responsibility

Delivery may include:

- registration report;
- certificate copy;
- official-source link or extract;
- scope comparison;
- Right Baseline summary;
- maintenance calendar;
- evidence recommendations;
- invoice or balance statement;
- original-certificate custody information;
- future-service options.

The Engagement should define whether MarkReg or the Relationship Owner remains responsible for:

- certificate follow-up;
- original delivery;
- maintenance monitoring;
- use-evidence reminders;
- address or ownership updates;
- later official notices.

```text
Registration Report Delivered
≠ Every Continuing Obligation Accepted
```

## 12. Service and relationship modes remain visible

### Direct self-operated service

The MarkReg-operated Workplace may deliver the registration result and offer continuing monitoring directly.

### White-label delivery

The originating Workplace may communicate with the customer while MarkReg supplies verified result records and delivery materials.

### Co-delivery

One party may verify official status while another manages certificates, customer explanation or maintenance planning.

### Bounded verification service

A Workplace may purchase only registration-status or certificate verification without transferring the wider Matter or customer relationship.

```text
Registration Verification Access
≠ Customer Relationship Transfer
```

## 13. Commercial completion and professional completion differ

A service may have several completion dimensions:

```text
Professional work completed
Provider invoice received
Official registration verified
Certificate received
Customer delivery completed
Outstanding balance settled
Original certificate delivered
Continuing service accepted
```

The Product should not compress them into one `Completed` status.

An unpaid balance does not make the official registration false. A verified registration does not make the commercial account settled.

## 14. Corrected official events require impact analysis

The office may correct:

- owner name;
- registration number;
- goods wording;
- date;
- mark image;
- class;
- certificate content;
- status.

The system should:

1. preserve the prior source;
2. retrieve or receive the corrected source;
3. compare the records;
4. determine which Product projections, communications and baselines are affected;
5. correct downstream records;
6. notify responsible actors where material;
7. preserve the correction history.

```text
Corrected Official Record
≠ Permission to Delete Historical Evidence
```

## 15. AI assistance and its limits

AI may:

- extract certificate fields;
- compare filed and registered goods;
- detect possible owner mismatches;
- draft customer explanations;
- classify certificate files;
- identify possible maintenance obligations;
- flag source conflicts.

AI may not:

- authenticate a certificate conclusively;
- create registration;
- decide ownership;
- remove official limitations;
- declare a dispute closed;
- establish a controlling deadline alone;
- infer continued validity from an old certificate;
- authorize future filings or payments.

## 16. Failure modes

The Product fails when:

- Provider email becomes the registration source;
- certificate receipt becomes current-status verification;
- the filed scope is shown as the registered scope;
- one registered jurisdiction makes the whole portfolio appear registered;
- expected owner replaces official owner silently;
- certificate and current register conflicts are hidden;
- a customer receives a certificate without maintenance guidance;
- registration closes the Matter while unresolved recordal or evidence work remains;
- a Product projection is treated as Official Truth;
- AI authentication is presented as legal certainty;
- original-certificate custody is not recorded;
- future monitoring is implied without an accepted service scope.

## 17. Product and technical implications

MarkReg requires:

- Registration Outcome Records;
- official-source retrieval and snapshots;
- certificate file and custody records;
- filed-to-registered scope diff;
- owner and identity comparison;
- Right Baseline versioning;
- source-conflict handling;
- customer-delivery packages;
- correction propagation;
- portfolio-independent right states;
- links to maintenance, evidence, recordal and transaction workflows.

The legal and official records remain with their authoritative sources and applicable Owning Services. MarkReg owns the focused Product journey and its reviewed projections.

## 18. Commercial implications

Registration creates legitimate service opportunities:

- certificate verification and delivery;
- original-certificate handling;
- maintenance monitoring;
- evidence planning;
- renewal;
- owner and address recordal;
- portfolio review;
- licensing and transaction support.

These opportunities must remain explicit. A filing service should not claim ongoing monitoring unless the service has been accepted and responsibility assigned.

## 19. The operating principle

```text
Verify the official event
Compare the registered scope
Reconcile the owner and certificate
Create a current Right Baseline
Explain what remains vulnerable
Assign continuing responsibility explicitly
```

> Registration is not the end of trademark work. It is the point at which an application becomes a right whose scope, ownership, evidence, obligations and future changes must remain continuously verifiable.
