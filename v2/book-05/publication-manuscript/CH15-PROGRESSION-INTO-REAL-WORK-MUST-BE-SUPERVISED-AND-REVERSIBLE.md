# Chapter 15 — Progression into Real Work Must Be Supervised and Reversible

A professional production system needs a path from learning to real contribution.

Without that path, training remains disconnected from work. New professionals cannot build evidence, senior reviewers cannot develop successors and MarkReg cannot expand capacity safely.

The opposite failure is more dangerous: a platform treats course completion or simulation success as permission to work on live customer Matters.

MarkReg therefore needs a progression system in which each increase in independence is supported by evidence, supervision and bounded authorization.

```text
Simulation
→ Shadow Production
→ Supervised Live Production
→ Bounded Independent Production
→ Complex Production
→ Review Authority
```

The path is not automatic, irreversible or universal.

## 1. Progression is Capability-specific

A person does not progress once for all trademark work.

They may be:

- independent in official-status verification;
- supervised in goods normalization;
- training-only in use-evidence assessment;
- ineligible for office-action analysis;
- professionally qualified for one jurisdiction but not another;
- eligible to review data quality but not legal strategy.

Every progression decision should identify:

- Work Package type;
- jurisdiction;
- language;
- complexity;
- data sensitivity;
- permitted tools;
- review level;
- excluded activities;
- evidence basis;
- effective and expiry dates.

```text
Progressed in One Capability
≠ Independent in All Work
```

## 2. Simulation creates safe difficulty

Simulation uses synthetic, fictional or sufficiently de-identified facts.

It may reproduce:

- status verification;
- classification and goods decisions;
- evidence screening;
- deadline calculation;
- filing-package preparation;
- Provider-instruction review;
- customer communication;
- error recovery.

A simulation should state:

- learning objective;
- source and rule version;
- supplied facts;
- intentionally missing facts;
- allowed tools and AI;
- expected decisions;
- rubric;
- alternative valid routes;
- review and feedback method.

Production connectors, customer communication, payment and official submission remain disabled.

```text
Simulated Filing
≠ Official Filing
```

## 3. Shadow production compares without controlling the Outcome

A shadow participant works on the structure of a real or realistic Work Package, but their work does not control the customer Outcome.

Their Contribution may be compared with:

- the accepted production result;
- reviewer reasoning;
- official outcome;
- known corrections;
- alternative professional approaches.

Shadow production is valuable because it introduces real ambiguity without exposing the customer to the learner’s independent decision.

Where real Matter data is used, access, confidentiality and de-identification must still be governed.

```text
Shadow Work
≠ Live Responsibility
≠ Unrestricted Customer Data Access
```

## 4. Supervised live production creates real responsibility under a supervisor

In supervised production, the participant contributes to a real Work Package.

The Assignment must identify:

- participant;
- supervisor or Reviewer;
- Delivery Owner;
- scope;
- prohibited actions;
- data boundary;
- review requirement;
- correction responsibility;
- deadline;
- customer-facing identity rule;
- compensation;
- escalation route.

The supervisor must have time and authority to review the work. A nominal supervisor who cannot examine the Contribution does not create safe supervision.

```text
Supervisor Named
≠ Supervision Available
```

## 5. The customer Outcome remains protected

A supervised contributor may prepare:

- a status report;
- evidence inventory;
- candidate goods wording;
- draft customer questions;
- document checklist;
- filing instruction draft.

They may still be prohibited from:

- giving reserved advice;
- approving the Package;
- communicating directly with the customer;
- instructing a Provider;
- signing or filing;
- changing official state;
- accepting payment;
- deciding abandonment or settlement.

The accepted Outcome is controlled by the assigned Reviewer, Professional Authority and Delivery Owner.

## 6. Independent standard production is bounded

Independence should begin with repeatable, lower-risk Work Packages.

Possible examples include:

- verifying a supported official status;
- normalizing structured applicant data;
- checking required document presence;
- organizing evidence by goods and date;
- preparing a standard instruction sheet from approved inputs;
- validating receipt identifiers.

Independent status should state what remains outside scope.

A contributor may be independent in preparing a package but still require complete professional review before external use.

```text
Independent Production
≠ Independent Professional Approval
```

## 7. Complex production requires exception evidence

Progression to complex work should depend on evidence that the person can:

- identify conflicts and Unknowns;
- stop unsafe work;
- distinguish facts from inference;
- recognize stale sources;
- handle inconsistent documents;
- communicate material uncertainty;
- choose an escalation route;
- recover from an error;
- work across dependencies and deadlines.

High volume of routine work is not enough.

```text
Many Routine Tasks
≠ Complex-task Readiness
```

## 8. Review authority is a separate Capability

A strong producer is not automatically a strong Reviewer.

Review authority requires evidence of the ability to:

- understand the Work Package scope;
- apply an explicit rubric;
- distinguish material error from preference;
- identify source and version problems;
- calibrate review depth to risk;
- explain corrections;
- escalate to Professional Authority;
- preserve contributor attribution;
- detect weaknesses in the template or system itself.

Reviewer performance should also be reviewed and calibrated.

```text
Production Quality
≠ Review Capability
```

## 9. Progression evidence should be mixed

A progression decision may rely on:

- structured learning;
- simulation results;
- shadow comparisons;
- supervised live work;
- accepted Contributions;
- correction history;
- Reviewer feedback;
- later Matter Outcomes;
- recent practice;
- professional qualification where relevant.

No single evidence source should carry more meaning than it supports.

Passing a timed assessment may show decision speed under controlled conditions. It does not prove safe customer communication or exception handling.

## 10. Real work should be paid when it creates production value

Supervised work may create both learning and customer value.

The participant should not be denied compensation merely because they are progressing.

The terms should distinguish:

- simulation with no production value;
- shadow work used only for learning;
- supervised live production;
- partially accepted production;
- work requiring correction;
- new scope added after Assignment.

```text
Learning Value
≠ Substitute for Compensation
```

Compensation does not prove competence. It recognizes agreed work or service value.

## 11. Progression should be reversible

Authorization may need to move backward after:

- a serious quality failure;
- repeated material corrections;
- long inactivity;
- rule or template change;
- security or confidentiality incident;
- missed escalation;
- qualification lapse;
- work outside scope;
- reviewer calibration showing prior over-promotion.

Possible responses include:

```text
Additional Review
Scope Restriction
Refresher Learning
Reassessment
Return to Supervised Production
Temporary Suspension
Authorization Revocation
```

Regression should be evidence-based and appealable. It should not be used as informal punishment or commercial retaliation.

## 12. Reversibility protects both customer and professional

A reversible system benefits the contributor because it allows:

- safe re-entry after inactivity;
- targeted remediation;
- recovery from one category of weakness without losing all Capability status;
- correction of overbroad authorization;
- preservation of valid prior evidence.

One serious issue may justify restricting a narrow scope rather than erasing the person’s entire history.

## 13. Urgent customer work is not a training opportunity first

A real deadline may be too close for safe supervised progression.

The Delivery Plan should consider:

- time needed for review;
- likely revision;
- backup performer;
- professional availability;
- document and Provider lead time;
- consequence of delay.

When urgency exceeds the safe learning route, MarkReg should assign an experienced eligible performer. The case may later be converted into a simulation or de-identified learning case.

```text
Educational Value
≠ Permission to Endanger a Deadline
```

## 14. Customer-facing identity must be honest

A supervised participant may work behind the scenes. Where they communicate externally, the Engagement should define:

- represented Workplace;
- role;
- whether supervision is disclosed;
- who approves the message;
- whether the customer may rely on the communication;
- who holds Professional Authority.

White-label delivery does not permit fictitious qualifications or concealment of legally required professional identity.

## 15. Data access should expand only as necessary

Progression should not be measured by how much customer data a participant can see.

Access may expand from:

```text
Synthetic Case
→ De-identified Historical Case
→ Shadow Workspace
→ Minimum Live Data
→ Broader Matter Context Where Required
```

Each expansion requires purpose, security and Assignment justification.

A person may become highly capable while continuing to work through narrow, privacy-preserving Work Packages.

## 16. AI-assisted progression must be labeled

A participant may use AI for:

- source retrieval;
- comparison;
- structure;
- draft preparation;
- missing-field detection;
- explanation.

The evidence record should state:

- AI mode;
- model or tool where material;
- source restrictions;
- required human checks;
- whether the assessment was assisted;
- what the person independently decided.

```text
AI-assisted Success
≠ Unassisted Capability Evidence
```

AI use may be legitimate and expected. It must not hide the actual conditions under which the work was performed.

## 17. Failure attribution must be careful

A rejected Contribution may result from:

- contributor error;
- unclear Work Package scope;
- outdated Knowledge;
- defective template;
- missing source data;
- inconsistent Reviewer preference;
- late customer change;
- system failure;
- incorrect Assignment.

Progression decisions should not automatically blame the participant.

The review should identify both individual and system causes.

## 18. Progression metrics should resist gaming

Useful measures include:

- first-pass acceptance by task type;
- material correction rate;
- escalation quality;
- source and version discipline;
- timeliness;
- recovery quality;
- later Outcome correlation;
- Reviewer agreement;
- recency.

Unsafe measures include:

- raw task count;
- speed without difficulty context;
- revenue alone;
- customer rating without scope;
- course completion count;
- platform engagement time.

The system should not encourage contributors to accept easy tasks merely to inflate a score.

## 19. Product implications

MarkReg requires:

- simulation environments;
- shadow-production mode;
- supervised live Assignments;
- bounded independent authorization;
- progression criteria;
- reviewer-capability records;
- compensation handling;
- data-access progression;
- regression, remediation and appeal;
- AI-assistance labels;
- system-cause analysis;
- current evidence and expiry.

## 20. Commercial implications

A governed progression path creates:

- a reliable production pipeline;
- lower onboarding risk;
- better Reviewer leverage;
- professional development value;
- retention for contributors and staff;
- more predictable capacity;
- evidence for higher-value task routing.

It should not create a permanent low-paid trainee class whose work is always reviewed but never recognized as production.

## 21. Operating principle

```text
Practice safely
Shadow without controlling the Outcome
Supervise real work explicitly
Grant bounded independence from evidence
Separate production from review authority
Pay for production value
Regress when evidence requires
Preserve appeal and re-entry
```

> Progression is credible only when every increase in independence can be explained—and every unsafe expansion can be reversed before it becomes a customer failure.
