# Chapter 16 — Professional Contribution Networks Need Bounded Scope, Review and Relationship Protection

MarkReg needs distributed professional contribution, but it does not need an ungoverned freelance marketplace.

Many parts of trademark service can be decomposed into bounded work that does not require a participant to become the external Provider Organization for the Matter.

Examples include:

- official-status verification;
- applicant-data normalization;
- goods and services cleanup;
- search-result organization;
- evidence indexing;
- translation verification;
- factual content review;
- visual continuity review;
- document completeness checking;
- Provider Return validation support.

A Professional Contribution Network allows eligible people and Workplaces to perform such Contributions under governed Work Packages.

The central boundary is:

```text
Professional Contribution Network
≠ Open Gig Marketplace
≠ MGSN Provider Network
≠ Customer Directory
≠ Automatic Professional Appointment
```

## 1. Why a separate contribution network is necessary

Without a distinct model, MarkReg faces two bad choices.

The first is to keep every task internal, limiting scale and specialist access.

The second is to treat every external participant as a full Provider, creating unnecessary procurement, customer exposure and authority confusion.

A contribution network supports bounded production while preserving the difference between:

- internal staff;
- contracted individuals;
- partner Workplace members;
- expert reviewers;
- external professional Providers;
- MGSN Organizations.

## 2. Contribution begins with a Work Package

A contributor should never receive a vague request such as:

> Please help with this trademark case.

The Work Package must state:

- exact Outcome;
- source Matter;
- scope and exclusions;
- required Capability;
- authorization level;
- input versions;
- allowed data;
- allowed tools and AI;
- review standard;
- deadline;
- compensation;
- revision rules;
- confidentiality;
- relationship and communication restrictions;
- completion Evidence.

The participant contributes against this contract.

```text
Access to a Matter
≠ Permission to Perform Any Useful Work
```

## 3. Contributions are bounded artifacts

A Contribution may be:

- structured data;
- a verification report;
- an evidence map;
- a translation review;
- a candidate wording set;
- a factual quality report;
- an exception list;
- a visual review;
- a completed checklist;
- a recommendation for escalation.

A Contribution is not automatically:

- legal advice;
- customer instruction;
- Provider appointment;
- accepted production;
- official evidence;
- formal state.

```text
Contribution Submitted
≠ Contribution Accepted
≠ Customer Outcome
≠ External Protected Action
```

## 4. The network is not MGSN

MGSN routes formal professional and institutional services through accountable Organizations and Workplaces.

A Professional Contribution Network routes bounded production and review.

| Dimension | Professional Contribution Network | MGSN |
|---|---|---|
| primary unit | Work Package and Contribution | professional service need and Provider engagement |
| typical participant | eligible individual or Workplace contributor | qualified Provider Organization or professional Workplace |
| customer relationship | normally remains with originating Workplace | remains governed by Engagement and route |
| formal authority | generally absent unless separately established | may include local professional authority |
| output | Contribution for review | typed Provider Return or formal service result |
| appointment | Assignment | Provider selection, appointment and acceptance |
| commercial model | bounded work compensation | professional procurement and service fees |

The same person may participate in both systems under different roles, but the records and authority must remain separate.

```text
Contributor in One Assignment
≠ MGSN Provider for the Matter
```

## 5. Network membership does not create eligibility

A network participant may have a profile, Capability evidence and prior Contributions.

Eligibility still depends on:

- current production authorization;
- Work Package scope;
- jurisdiction and language;
- conflict status;
- data sensitivity;
- recency;
- capacity;
- quality history;
- Reviewer availability;
- Workplace restrictions;
- required professional qualification.

Membership is discovery context, not permission.

## 6. Assignment must be explicit and accepted

A contributor receives an offer and accepts an exact Work Package version.

The Assignment records:

- participant;
- represented Organization or Workplace where relevant;
- role;
- accepted scope;
- compensation;
- data boundary;
- start and expiry;
- Reviewer;
- communication permissions;
- prohibited actions;
- escalation route.

The platform should not auto-assign customer work merely because the person is online or highly ranked.

## 7. Minimum-necessary data is a network rule

Distributed work increases privacy and relationship risk.

A contributor should receive only the information necessary to produce the Outcome.

Examples:

- a status verifier may need the jurisdiction and application number, not the customer’s complete portfolio;
- a translator may need the relevant text and context, not pricing history;
- a visual reviewer may need the filed mark and candidate design, not customer bank details;
- an evidence screener may need selected files and goods scope, not unrelated correspondence.

Access should expire after completion, cancellation or review unless continued retention is justified.

## 8. Relationship ownership must be explicit

A contributor may see customer or partner information while working.

That access does not create:

- customer ownership;
- direct marketing permission;
- authority to quote additional services;
- authority to contact the trademark owner;
- entitlement to future Matters;
- permission to bypass the originating Workplace.

```text
Customer Data Access
≠ Customer Relationship
```

The Assignment should identify the Relationship Owner and permitted communication route.

## 9. Non-circumvention must be proportionate

Relationship protection is legitimate when a participant gains access only through the Assignment.

However, restrictions should be:

- linked to the actual customer or opportunity;
- time-bounded;
- clear before acceptance;
- compatible with customer choice;
- limited to misuse of confidential access;
- not a blanket prohibition on independent professional activity.

The network should prevent opportunistic bypass without creating unreasonable control over a professional’s entire market.

## 10. Direct communication requires specific permission

Possible communication modes include:

```text
No Customer Contact
Draft for Relationship Owner
Platform-mediated Communication
Supervised Direct Communication
Authorized Direct Professional Communication
Emergency Contact under Defined Rule
```

The mode depends on:

- service relationship;
- customer expectation;
- white-label rules;
- professional disclosure requirements;
- urgency;
- language;
- authority;
- privacy and consent.

A contributor who drafts a message does not automatically become its sender.

## 11. White-label contribution must preserve truth

In white-label delivery, a contributor may work behind the partner Workplace’s customer interface.

White-label does not authorize:

- fictitious professional identity;
- false claims about who reviewed the work;
- concealment of required local counsel;
- misleading fee or Provider representation;
- hidden customer transfer.

The internal record should preserve actual contribution, review and authority even where the external presentation is simplified.

## 12. Review protects both customer and contributor

Every Contribution should have defined review behavior.

Possible review modes include:

- deterministic validation;
- sampled operational review;
- complete specialist review;
- professional review;
- dual review;
- customer confirmation;
- official-source verification.

The Reviewer should state whether the Contribution is:

```text
Accepted
Accepted with Limitation
Revision Required
Partially Accepted
Escalated
Rejected with Reason
Cancelled because Source or Scope Failed
```

A vague rejection such as “not good enough” is not sufficient for quality evidence or compensation disputes.

## 13. Revision is governed by scope

Revision may be required because:

- the contributor missed the stated requirement;
- evidence was incomplete;
- the customer changed instructions;
- the source changed;
- the Reviewer introduced a new preference;
- the Work Package expanded;
- an official event changed the context.

The system should distinguish correction from amendment.

```text
Contributor Error Correction
≠ New Unpaid Scope
```

Material scope expansion should create a revised offer or new Work Package.

## 14. Compensation and Capability evidence are separate

Accepted paid work creates at least two records:

```text
Compensation Record
+ Capability Evidence Record
```

The Compensation Record concerns:

- agreed price;
- premium or adjustment;
- payment trigger;
- payment state;
- tax or withholding context;
- dispute.

The Capability Evidence Record concerns:

- task type and difficulty;
- assistance mode;
- independence;
- review result;
- corrections;
- timing;
- later Outcome relevance.

Payment does not prove competence. Rejected work does not always justify non-payment if the failure arose from source or scope defects.

## 15. Lowest-price bidding is not the default model

The network should not optimize by asking contributors to underbid one another for professional production.

Pricing may consider:

- task complexity;
- expected effort;
- scarcity;
- jurisdiction and language;
- urgency;
- data sensitivity;
- review responsibility;
- qualification;
- revision burden;
- consequence of error.

Transparent fixed or bounded prices are generally more compatible with quality and progression than hidden auctions.

## 16. Contributor rights and reuse must be explicit

The Assignment should define:

- ownership or licence of the Contribution;
- confidentiality;
- whether templates may be retained;
- whether general know-how remains usable;
- whether the work may enter a portfolio;
- whether de-identified use is permitted;
- whether the Contribution may become a Knowledge candidate;
- whether AI training use is permitted.

```text
Contribution Accepted
≠ Public Reuse Authorized
≠ Shared Learning Authorized
≠ AI Training Consent
```

Customer and partner content should not enter platform learning silently.

## 17. Conflict rules apply before and during work

Potential conflict may arise from:

- prior work for an adverse party;
- access to competing buyer or seller information;
- personal or organizational interest;
- reviewer conflict;
- reuse of confidential strategy;
- simultaneous work for multiple parties in a dispute.

Conflict checks should match the task and professional rules.

A new conflict discovered during work may require:

- immediate pause;
- access restriction;
- reassignment;
- review of exposed data;
- customer or Professional Authority notification;
- preservation of compensation for completed safe work.

## 18. Network quality must preserve context

Useful quality signals include:

- task-specific acceptance;
- source discipline;
- correction patterns;
- timeliness;
- escalation quality;
- difficulty;
- Reviewer consistency;
- recurrence of errors;
- recent practice.

The network should avoid one universal public score.

A participant who performs difficult exception work may have more revisions than someone who completes only routine tasks.

## 19. Complaints and disputes need an independent route

Disputes may involve:

- task scope;
- acceptance criteria;
- Reviewer inconsistency;
- payment;
- attribution;
- customer contact;
- confidentiality;
- intellectual property;
- deauthorization;
- alleged circumvention.

The system should preserve evidence and avoid allowing the direct commercial beneficiary to be the only decision maker in every dispute.

## 20. Portability and exit

A contributor should be able to leave the network while preserving:

- earned compensation;
- appropriate Capability evidence;
- appeal rights;
- a record of accepted work;
- lawful use of general know-how.

On exit, MarkReg should:

- revoke access;
- reassign active Work Packages;
- preserve customer records;
- handle confidential local copies;
- resolve payment;
- retain necessary audit evidence;
- stop future routing.

Exit does not grant permission to retain customer data or solicit customers obtained only through the network.

## 21. Customer choice and emergency protection

Relationship protection should not override the customer’s lawful right to choose a professional or protect an urgent deadline.

Where the customer directly requests contact or an emergency requires a qualified professional to act, the Engagement should govern:

- verification of the request;
- notification of the Relationship Owner;
- scope of direct contact;
- fee and responsibility changes;
- records and evidence;
- future relationship handling.

The system should prevent secret bypass while allowing transparent, authorized change.

## 22. AI may support routing and review but not relationship appropriation

AI may:

- identify candidate contributors;
- compare Capability and scope;
- detect missing review;
- summarize Contributions;
- flag possible conflicts;
- identify quality patterns;
- draft feedback.

AI cannot:

- assign customer ownership;
- create consent;
- authorize direct contact;
- decide professional conflict alone;
- suppress contributor rights;
- convert private Contributions into shared learning;
- appoint a Provider.

## 23. Product implications

MarkReg requires:

- network participant records;
- Work Package and Assignment contracts;
- eligibility and authorization gates;
- data-minimization controls;
- communication modes;
- relationship and non-circumvention terms;
- review and revision workflows;
- compensation and Capability evidence;
- rights and reuse controls;
- conflict handling;
- complaints and appeals;
- exit and reassignment;
- separation from MGSN.

## 24. Commercial implications

A governed contribution network can provide:

- elastic production capacity;
- specialist access;
- lower coordination cost;
- professional development;
- agency overflow support;
- better review utilization;
- stronger service margins;
- evidence-backed progression.

Its value should come from trusted decomposition and review—not from hiding labor, transferring customer relationships or replacing professional Providers with cheaper unqualified workers.

## 25. Operating principle

```text
Route bounded Work Packages
Verify current eligibility
Assign explicitly
Minimize data
Protect the relationship
Review against stated criteria
Pay fairly
Preserve contributor and customer rights
Keep the Contribution Network distinct from MGSN
```

> A Professional Contribution Network should let many people create value inside one accountable service without allowing access, visibility or production skill to become customer ownership or professional authority.
