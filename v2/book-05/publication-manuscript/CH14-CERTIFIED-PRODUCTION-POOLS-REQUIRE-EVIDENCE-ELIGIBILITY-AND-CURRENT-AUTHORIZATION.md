# Chapter 14 — Certified Production Pools Require Evidence, Eligibility and Current Authorization

A production pool can increase capacity only if it increases trust faster than it increases operational risk.

The unsafe version is easy to build:

- users complete a course;
- the platform awards a badge;
- the badge unlocks customer work;
- tasks are routed by self-declared skill and low price;
- errors appear only after customer delivery.

That model confuses learning, certification, capability, eligibility, Assignment and professional authority.

MarkReg needs a Certified Production Pool, but certification must be one input into a stricter production-access system.

```text
Learning or assessment evidence
→ scoped Capability
→ current production authorization
→ task-specific eligibility
→ explicit Assignment
→ minimum-necessary access
→ Contribution
→ review and accepted Outcome
```

## 1. The pool is not an open task marketplace

A Certified Production Pool is a governed set of people who may become eligible for defined Work Packages.

It is not:

- a public bidding board;
- a list of anyone who completed a course;
- a substitute for employment or procurement contracts;
- a directory of legal professionals;
- automatic access to customer data;
- automatic permission to perform formal actions;
- a ranking system based on volume alone.

```text
Pool Membership
≠ Task Eligibility
≠ Assignment
≠ Production Access
```

## 2. Certification is only bounded evidence

A certification should identify:

- issuer;
- Capability and scope;
- assessment conditions;
- tools or AI assistance allowed;
- evidence basis;
- issue date;
- expiry or refresh date;
- suspension and revocation conditions;
- verification method;
- limitations.

For example:

```text
Certified for routine US post-registration
status verification and evidence organization
under reviewed source and template versions
```

is materially different from:

```text
US trademark specialist
```

The second statement is too broad to support production routing.

```text
Platform Certification
≠ Government Licence
≠ Professional Qualification
≠ Customer Representation Authority
```

## 3. Production access requires additional controls

Before entering the pool for live work, a participant may need:

- verified identity;
- accepted employment, contractor or partner status;
- confidentiality and data-protection commitments;
- conflict policy;
- security requirements;
- current Capability evidence;
- supervised-practice evidence;
- quality and correction history;
- jurisdiction and language scope;
- capacity declaration;
- tax and payment information where applicable;
- acknowledgement of customer and relationship boundaries.

The exact requirements should follow the type and consequence of work.

A contributor verifying public official records does not require the same control level as a person screening confidential use evidence or reviewing a response draft.

## 4. Production authorization is separate from Capability

Capability answers:

> Can this person perform the work within a defined scope?

Production authorization answers:

> Is this person currently permitted to perform this category of MarkReg work under these conditions?

Possible production-authorization states may include:

```text
Training Only
Shadow Work Permitted
Supervised Production
Independent Standard Production
Advanced Production
Review Authority
Suspended
Expired
Revoked
```

The exact state names remain a Product decision, but the distinction is required.

A person may have strong Capability evidence and still lack current production authorization because:

- confidentiality terms expired;
- identity verification is incomplete;
- a conflict is unresolved;
- security requirements changed;
- recent quality evidence is insufficient;
- supervision is unavailable;
- professional qualification lapsed;
- the person has no current commercial relationship with the Workplace.

## 5. Authorization is scoped and perishable

Production authorization should specify:

- Work Package types;
- jurisdiction;
- language;
- complexity;
- permitted data classification;
- permitted AI mode;
- required review depth;
- excluded activities;
- effective and expiry dates;
- issuing Workplace or authority;
- renewal conditions.

```text
Authorized Once
≠ Authorized Forever

Authorized for One Work Type
≠ Authorized for Every Matter
```

Authorization may need renewal after:

- a major rule change;
- long inactivity;
- repeated material corrections;
- a security incident;
- new data sensitivity;
- a changed production template;
- a changed professional requirement.

## 6. Eligibility is calculated for each Work Package

Eligibility depends on the current task, not only the person’s profile.

A routing check may consider:

- required Capability ID and level;
- production-authorization scope;
- jurisdiction;
- language;
- complexity;
- professional qualification where required;
- data sensitivity;
- conflict status;
- recent quality evidence;
- rework history;
- current capacity;
- deadline fit;
- time zone;
- reviewer availability;
- permitted tools;
- Workplace and customer restrictions.

```text
Certified
≠ Eligible for This Work Package
```

A participant may be eligible for routine evidence organization but ineligible for legal sufficiency review. They may be eligible in English but not for source-language translation. They may be capable but unavailable before the deadline.

## 7. Routing recommendation does not create Assignment

The system may rank eligible candidates by:

- task fit;
- quality evidence;
- recency;
- capacity;
- expected completion time;
- review burden;
- continuity with prior work;
- cost;
- relationship and confidentiality constraints.

The ranking should be explainable and commercially transparent.

A human or authorized allocation process still decides whether to offer the Assignment.

```text
Highest-ranked Candidate
≠ Assigned Performer
```

The platform’s financial interest in a contributor, internal team or Provider route should not silently override safety or Workplace choice.

## 8. Task offer must precede acceptance

An eligible contributor should receive a clear task offer stating:

- Work Package purpose;
- required Outcome;
- scope and exclusions;
- source and input versions;
- risk level;
- expected effort;
- deadline;
- review standard;
- revision rules;
- compensation;
- payment condition;
- access restrictions;
- AI-use policy;
- intellectual-property and confidentiality terms;
- cancellation and dispute rules.

A task should not be presented as a small verification job and later expand into substantive professional analysis without amendment.

## 9. Assignment and access are distinct events

A contributor may accept the commercial offer before the system grants data access.

The system should then verify:

- Assignment state;
- current authorization;
- conflict status;
- required confidentiality;
- data scope;
- reviewer assignment;
- expiry;
- security controls.

Only then should the scoped workspace open.

```text
Offer Accepted
≠ Data Access Granted
```

Access should expose only the minimum information necessary for the Work Package.

## 10. Review is part of eligibility design

Some Work Packages should not be routed unless an eligible Reviewer is available.

A supervised evidence-screening task may require:

- a qualified evidence Reviewer;
- complete review before use;
- no external action permission;
- defined correction feedback;
- escalation for uncertain goods or use relationships.

The contributor’s eligibility therefore depends partly on the surrounding review capacity.

```text
Performer Eligible Alone
≠ Delivery Route Ready
```

## 11. Current quality evidence matters more than lifetime volume

The pool should consider:

- accepted Outcomes;
- material corrections;
- repeated error patterns;
- timeliness;
- source discipline;
- uncertainty disclosure;
- communication quality;
- escalation behavior;
- reviewer calibration;
- recent practice.

One thousand low-risk tasks do not prove readiness for a high-consequence task.

A recent serious issue may matter more than a historical average.

The quality record should preserve task difficulty and review context. It should not reduce every contributor to one public score.

## 12. Suspension and revocation need due process

Production authorization may be suspended because of:

- unresolved security or confidentiality concern;
- identity issue;
- conflict violation;
- serious quality failure;
- repeated missed deadlines;
- falsified evidence;
- qualification lapse;
- policy violation;
- customer or partner complaint requiring investigation.

Possible actions include:

```text
Warning
Additional Review Required
Scope Restricted
Supervised-only Status
Temporary Suspension
Authorization Expired
Authorization Revoked
```

The record should state:

- reason;
- evidence;
- decision maker;
- affected scope;
- effective date;
- appeal route;
- remediation conditions;
- impact on active Assignments.

A suspension should not silently erase historical Contributions or earned compensation.

## 13. Appeals and correction protect legitimacy

A contributor should be able to challenge:

- identity mismatch;
- misattributed error;
- incorrect task difficulty;
- inconsistent review;
- missing evidence;
- unfair rejection;
- an authorization decision based on stale data.

The appeal process should preserve the original decision and create a review record.

An appeal is not an automatic reversal, but a pool without correction rights will lose reliable professionals and accumulate hidden reviewer errors.

## 14. Professional qualification remains an external boundary

Some Work Packages require a legal, regulated or officially recognized qualification.

The pool must store qualification evidence and scope, but it cannot create that qualification.

Professional qualification may affect eligibility for:

- formal legal advice;
- office representation;
- signature;
- filing;
- opposition or cancellation strategy;
- regulated declarations;
- funds custody where legally restricted.

```text
MarkReg Production Authorization
≠ Professional Qualification
```

A qualified professional still requires Assignment and Matter authority. A contributor with excellent production evidence still cannot perform a reserved action without qualification.

## 15. No pay-to-enter or pay-to-rank shortcut

A participant may pay for learning or assessment access. Payment must not purchase:

- certification result;
- production authorization;
- eligibility;
- higher routing rank;
- exemption from review;
- immunity from suspension.

Likewise, a contributor should not be required to perform substantial unpaid customer work merely to remain visible in the pool.

```text
Course Purchase
≠ Production Access

Unpaid Customer Production
≠ Legitimate Assessment
```

## 16. Pool visibility is purpose-limited

Internal operations may see detailed Capability, quality and authorization records.

A partner Workplace may see only what is needed to approve a production route.

Customers may need to know:

- whether the work is supervised;
- which professional holds authority;
- whether a Provider is involved;
- which organization remains responsible.

They do not automatically need access to private contributor performance records.

The pool should not become a public leaderboard or a source of unrestricted contributor contact details.

## 17. AI can assist the pool but cannot govern it alone

AI may:

- match Work Package requirements to evidence;
- identify stale authorization;
- suggest candidates;
- summarize quality history;
- detect recurring errors;
- propose review depth;
- flag conflicts or missing data.

AI cannot:

- invent Capability evidence;
- grant professional qualification;
- make irreversible suspension decisions alone;
- bypass task acceptance;
- authorize external protected action;
- suppress negative evidence because a route is commercially preferred.

## 18. Product implications

MarkReg requires:

- certification and Capability records;
- production-authorization records;
- task-specific eligibility rules;
- identity and qualification verification;
- conflict and security checks;
- offer and Assignment workflows;
- access gating;
- reviewer-capacity checks;
- quality and correction evidence;
- expiry, suspension and revocation;
- appeal and remediation;
- purpose-limited pool views.

## 19. Commercial implications

A governed pool can support:

- scalable routine production;
- agency overflow;
- supervised professional development;
- higher utilization of specialist reviewers;
- transparent contributor compensation;
- more reliable service margins;
- reduced rework and recovery cost.

The commercial value comes from trusted eligibility and review—not from turning professional work into lowest-price bidding.

## 20. Operating principle

```text
Certification supplies evidence
Authorization permits a bounded production role
Eligibility is checked for the current Work Package
Assignment requires acceptance
Access follows Assignment
Review follows risk
Authorization remains current, reversible and appealable
```

> A Certified Production Pool is valuable only when the platform can explain why a person is eligible for this task now—and what still prevents them from acting beyond it.
