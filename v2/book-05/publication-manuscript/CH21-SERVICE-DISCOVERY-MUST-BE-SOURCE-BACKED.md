# Chapter 21 — Service Discovery Must Be Source-backed

A customer often meets MarkReg before they understand that they have a trademark service need.

They may search for a deadline, a filing requirement, a country cost, a Power of Attorney rule, a registration problem or a transfer question. The first Product responsibility is therefore not to sell immediately. It is to help the user discover the right service through trustworthy, current and explainable information.

```text
Question or business intent
→ source-backed service knowledge
→ discoverable public surface
→ user understanding
→ candidate service need
```

Service discovery is part of the MarkReg lifecycle, but public publishing remains a separate Product responsibility.

## 1. Discovery starts before formal Intake

Traditional service systems begin when a customer fills in a form or sends an email. That is too late.

Before formal Intake, the user may need to understand:

- whether registration is required;
- which jurisdiction matters;
- whether a filing is still recoverable;
- whether an original document is required;
- what a maintenance deadline means;
- whether transfer and recordal are separate;
- why a quoted price contains several stages;
- which facts require professional review.

A discoverable service system should answer enough for the user to select a sensible next route without pretending that a public page has completed the professional analysis.

## 2. MarkReg supplies service meaning; Sites supplies public delivery

The division is:

```text
MarkReg
= service definitions, decision logic, requirements, risks and conversion routes

Sites
= page publication, URL structure, indexing, schema, rendering and performance

Lite
= content creation, campaigns, social distribution and daily marketing operations

Knowledge / Brain
= sources, provenance, freshness, contradiction and correction context
```

MarkReg should not become a general-purpose CMS. Sites should not invent legal or service meaning merely because it owns the page.

## 3. The discoverable unit should be a governed professional object

A public service page should be generated from governed objects such as:

- Jurisdiction Pack;
- Service Catalog item;
- Requirement Set;
- Deadline Rule;
- Fee Basis;
- Document Requirement;
- Provider Constraint;
- Common Exception;
- Recovery Route;
- Evidence Standard;
- Decision Option.

A useful assembly path is:

```text
Jurisdiction Pack
+ Service Catalog
+ Requirement Set
+ Fee Basis
+ Common Exceptions
+ Source and Freshness
→ Service Knowledge Candidate
→ Review
→ Publishable Projection
```

This structure is stronger than copying a paragraph into dozens of country pages.

## 4. Page families should reflect real user decisions

MarkReg may support:

- country service pages;
- filing requirement pages;
- fee explanation pages;
- document and POA pages;
- deadline and maintenance pages;
- recovery and re-filing pages;
- class and goods guidance;
- transaction and recordal pages;
- question-and-answer pages;
- de-identified case pages;
- service-comparison pages;
- problem-diagnosis pages.

Each family should have an explicit purpose and conversion route.

A page titled “US Trademark Registration” and a page titled “Can an abandoned US application be restored?” should not share the same generic Intake.

## 5. SEO is not keyword multiplication

SEO should connect a genuine search intent to a governed answer.

A page should state:

- the question being answered;
- jurisdiction and service scope;
- direct answer;
- conditions;
- required facts and documents;
- official and professional fee components where available;
- expected route and timing;
- important exceptions;
- source date;
- next safe action.

```text
More indexed pages
≠ more useful discovery

Keyword match
≠ service fit
```

Large-scale pages without material distinctions create duplication, stale claims and low-trust conversion.

## 6. GEO requires answer-ready structure

Generative systems tend to prefer content that is explicit, attributable and structurally clear.

A MarkReg answer block may follow:

```text
Direct Answer
→ Applicable Conditions
→ Required Documents
→ Fee Components
→ Timing
→ Important Exceptions
→ Sources and Freshness
→ Next Decision
```

For example, a POA answer should distinguish:

- whether a scan may be used initially;
- whether an original is later mandatory;
- whether notarization or legalization applies;
- whether the rule is official or Provider practice;
- what happens if the original is late;
- what remains to be confirmed for the specific Matter.

```text
AI engine citation
≠ professional approval
≠ universal Matter-specific answer
```

## 7. Claims need types

Public claims should retain the same discipline as other MarkReg decisions.

Possible claim types include:

```text
Official Rule
Official Fee
Official Deadline
Provider-reported Practice
Professional Interpretation
MarkReg Service Policy
Typical Market Range
Illustrative Example
Unverified Candidate
```

A Provider's operational preference must not be presented as a legal requirement. A typical fee must not be displayed as an official fee.

## 8. Freshness is part of truth

Every consequential public claim should record:

- source;
- jurisdiction;
- effective date;
- last verified date;
- reviewer;
- confidence;
- known exceptions;
- expiry or recheck trigger;
- dependent pages and calculators.

Possible states include:

```text
Current
Recheck Due
Source Changed
Conflicted
Withdrawn
Superseded
Unknown — Publication Restricted
```

A page can be linguistically correct and still be operationally dangerous because its fee, form or deadline is stale.

## 9. Contradictions must remain visible

Trademark services frequently contain disagreement between:

- official text;
- official portal behavior;
- local Provider practice;
- recent case experience;
- historical guidance.

The Product should not average these into a clean answer.

It should state:

- what each source says;
- which source has formal authority;
- which practice may affect execution;
- what needs current Provider confirmation;
- what safe route follows from the uncertainty.

```text
Conflicting Sources
≠ Permission to Publish the Most Convenient Claim
```

## 10. Public examples require legal and privacy control

Cases may be useful for discovery, but they should not expose:

- private customer identity;
- confidential instructions;
- unpublished evidence;
- sensitive prices;
- unverified allegations;
- protected communications.

A case should be:

- authorized for publication;
- de-identified or synthetic where necessary;
- clear about jurisdiction and date;
- explicit about what is illustrative;
- linked to current service logic rather than treated as permanent law.

## 11. Fees require decomposition

A public price should distinguish, where relevant:

- official filing fee;
- publication fee;
- registration fee;
- local Provider fee;
- MarkReg or Workplace service fee;
- translation or document fee;
- additional-class fee;
- optional review;
- possible exception cost;
- tax, remittance or courier costs.

```text
Starting Price
≠ Complete Lifecycle Cost

Official Fee
≠ Total Customer Price
```

The page should also state when a fee is indicative, current, confirmed, promotional or subject to Matter-specific review.

## 12. Discovery should preserve commercial neutrality

A MarkReg-operated Workplace may offer its own service route. Other Workplaces may use MarkReg in white-label or co-delivery mode.

The public surface should therefore preserve:

- represented Workplace;
- Relationship Owner;
- applicable service route;
- commercial interest;
- availability of alternative governed routes where relevant.

Preferred placement cannot silently convert into a false claim that only one Provider or route is viable.

## 13. Search visibility must not outrank professional safety

The following should not be optimized away:

- material exceptions;
- uncertainty;
- required professional review;
- extra official phases;
- original-document requirements;
- realistic timing;
- recovery limitations;
- customer responsibilities.

```text
Higher Click-through Rate
≠ Better Professional Content
```

## 14. Publication gates

Before publication, a consequential service page should pass:

- source and freshness gate;
- professional-fact review;
- fee and deadline review;
- jurisdiction-scope review;
- privacy and rights review;
- brand and editorial review;
- SEO and GEO structure review;
- conversion-route review;
- correction and withdrawal readiness.

The page itself remains a Projection. The underlying rules and formal service states stay with their authoritative sources and Owning Services.

## 15. Correction propagation

When a source changes, the correction path is:

```text
Source Change or Error Report
→ Affected Claim Identification
→ Page and Answer-block Impact
→ Calculator and Intake Impact
→ Temporary Warning or Withdrawal
→ Review
→ Corrected Projection
→ Downstream Notification where required
```

The Product should track derivative content across:

- service pages;
- FAQ blocks;
- social posts;
- generated answers;
- fee calculators;
- Intake recommendations;
- quotations not yet accepted.

## 16. Discovery metrics should measure qualified understanding

Useful metrics include:

- source freshness coverage;
- answer completeness;
- user comprehension;
- qualified service-intent rate;
- correct-route selection;
- low-quality inquiry rate;
- correction frequency;
- time to update after source change;
- conversion into Decision-ready Intake;
- later Matter mismatch and refund rate.

Traffic and ranking remain useful, but they are not the final quality measure.

## 17. Product implications

MarkReg requires:

- governed service-knowledge objects;
- claim types;
- source and freshness records;
- page-family schemas;
- Publishable Projection workflows;
- review gates;
- dependency and correction graphs;
- fee decomposition;
- service-specific conversion routes;
- Sites and Lite integration;
- public-content audit views.

## 18. Commercial implications

Source-backed discovery can create:

- lower customer-acquisition cost;
- higher-quality Intake;
- more accurate service matching;
- stronger white-label distribution;
- recurring maintenance demand;
- defensible GEO visibility;
- reduced correction and complaint cost.

Its commercial value depends on trust. Publishing more pages than the organization can keep current creates a liability rather than an asset.

## 19. Operating principle

```text
Govern the fact
Type the claim
Show the condition
Preserve the source
Publish through Sites
Convert through MarkReg
Correct every derivative
```

> Service discovery becomes part of professional delivery when the answer is traceable, current and connected to a safe next decision—not merely optimized to attract a click.
