# Chapter 05 — Search Is a Decision Product, Not a Result Dump

Trademark search is often sold as a list of records.

A user enters a name, selects a country and receives dozens or hundreds of results. The screen looks complete because it contains data. Yet the user still does not know:

- whether the proposed mark should be searched at all;
- which mark version was searched;
- whether the selected goods were represented correctly;
- which databases and dates were covered;
- which results matter;
- which risks remain unseen;
- whether the recommended next step is to proceed, narrow, redesign, defer or investigate further.

A result dump transfers interpretation back to the customer. MarkReg should instead treat search as a decision product.

```text
Business objective
→ search question
→ scoped search plan
→ sourced findings
→ relevance and risk interpretation
→ comparable options
→ accountable decision
→ change propagation
```

## 1. Search begins with a decision, not a query box

The first question is not:

> Which string should the system search?

It is:

> Which decision will this search support?

Possible decisions include:

- whether to adopt a new mark;
- whether to file now or redesign first;
- whether to protect a word mark, device mark or both;
- whether a proposed goods scope is defensible;
- whether a selected country should remain in the filing plan;
- whether an acquired trademark is useful to the buyer;
- whether additional professional review is justified;
- whether a previously searched plan remains current after material change.

The search plan should be proportionate to the decision, market importance, timing, budget and consequence.

## 2. Search modes answer different questions

MarkReg may support several search modes where source coverage and Product support permit.

### Exact or knockout search

Used to identify obvious identical and near-identical records quickly.

It is useful for early elimination. It is not a conclusion that the mark is safe when no exact result appears.

### Similarity search

May consider:

- spelling;
- phonetics;
- transliteration;
- translation;
- semantic association;
- visual construction;
- word structure;
- device elements;
- series-mark patterns.

The method and limitations must remain visible.

### Goods- and class-aware search

Search should use the actual goods and services decision, not class number alone.

```text
Same class
≠ same commercial field

Different class
≠ no relevant conflict
```

### Owner and portfolio search

May reveal families of marks, related entities, repeated naming patterns and broader enforcement context.

It does not establish ownership identity merely from similar names.

### Market or common-law search

Where supported, MarkReg may examine use signals beyond the official register. Coverage, reliability and legal significance vary by jurisdiction.

### Professional opinion

Professional review converts sourced evidence into a bounded opinion and proposed action. It does not guarantee registration, use or freedom from dispute.

## 3. The Search Record

Each search request, result and interpretation should retain:

- originating Workplace and Product Session;
- represented customer or applicant context;
- decision purpose;
- exact mark or filing-unit version;
- jurisdiction;
- classes and goods/services version;
- databases and sources;
- search modes and methods;
- query assumptions;
- search date and source freshness;
- raw or normalized result references;
- AI or deterministic processing used;
- reviewer identity and scope;
- material findings;
- residual uncertainty;
- recommended actions;
- expiry and re-search triggers.

A search result without its scope and date should not be reused as current evidence.

## 4. Facts, candidates and opinions must remain separate

A search product may produce several layers:

```text
Official source record
System-retrieved candidate
AI similarity inference
Analyst relevance assessment
Professional risk opinion
Customer decision
```

These layers support one another but are not interchangeable.

For example:

```text
A prior mark exists in the official register
= source fact

The prior mark is phonetically similar
= system or analyst finding

The prior mark creates a material refusal risk
= professional opinion

The customer chooses to proceed
= commercial or strategic decision

The filing is approved
= separate bounded approval
```

## 5. Search scope must follow the filing plan

A search should specify:

- word, device and composite versions;
- language and transliteration variants;
- jurisdiction and filing route;
- primary goods and services;
- adjacent or related goods;
- intended commercial use;
- applicant or owner context where relevant;
- exclusions;
- time and budget constraints.

A search performed for one logo version should not silently cover a substantially redesigned logo. A search based on “clothing” should not be reused after the plan shifts to cosmetics and retail services.

## 6. Search recommendation should be explainable

MarkReg may recommend different levels of search:

```text
No search recommended for this limited decision
Basic knockout search
Expanded similarity search
Selected-market professional review
Multi-jurisdiction search program
Additional market-use investigation
```

The recommendation should explain:

- why search is or is not proportionate;
- what risk the search can reduce;
- what it cannot eliminate;
- which countries and goods are covered;
- how the decision may change after findings;
- estimated cost and time;
- whether professional review is included.

Paid search should not be recommended merely because it increases revenue.

## 7. Risk should not collapse into one score

A useful assessment may show:

- identity or similarity basis;
- goods and market relationship;
- jurisdictional relevance;
- official status and freshness;
- owner or portfolio context;
- source quality;
- uncertainty;
- likely procedural or commercial consequence;
- available response routes.

A summary such as low, moderate or high may assist reading. It should not replace the explanation.

A candidate may be:

```text
High visual similarity
+ weak goods relationship
+ uncertain status
+ meaningful commercial concern
```

One number cannot communicate that structure safely.

## 8. Negative findings are not guarantees

The absence of a relevant result may reflect:

- incomplete source coverage;
- source delay;
- unsearched language variants;
- unregistered use;
- database normalization failure;
- goods-scope misunderstanding;
- image-search limitations;
- later-filed or newly published rights.

```text
No material result found
≠ mark available
≠ registration guaranteed
≠ use cleared
```

The user-facing report should state the remaining uncertainty in plain language.

## 9. Search should produce action options

A search report becomes useful when it leads to concrete choices.

Possible routes include:

- proceed with the current plan;
- proceed with recorded residual risk;
- narrow goods or services;
- change the mark;
- file word and device marks separately;
- defer a jurisdiction;
- investigate a specific prior right;
- seek consent or coexistence advice;
- conduct a broader market search;
- reject the current candidate.

Each route should state consequences for cost, speed, coverage and later filing preparation.

## 10. Customer risk acceptance is not filing approval

A customer may understand a risk and still choose to proceed.

The record should preserve:

- the finding;
- the explanation;
- alternatives offered;
- professional recommendation;
- customer decision;
- exact affected mark, goods and jurisdiction versions.

```text
Customer accepts risk
≠ Professional Review waived automatically
≠ Filing Package approved
≠ Provider appointed
```

## 11. Search and commercial independence

Search results may influence whether the customer purchases filing, redesign or further review services. This creates an incentive conflict.

MarkReg should disclose where relevant:

- whether the search provider also sells filing services;
- whether the recommended route creates additional platform revenue;
- whether a paid result receives preferential visibility;
- whether the system or reviewer has a related-party interest.

The recommendation should not inflate risk to sell more work or suppress risk to improve filing conversion.

## 12. Search versioning and material change

A change may invalidate all or part of the search.

Material changes include:

- different mark wording;
- redesigned device;
- changed transliteration;
- new jurisdiction;
- added class;
- changed goods or services;
- changed business model;
- corrected applicant or owner context;
- new official source information;
- long delay before filing.

The system should record:

```text
prior search version
→ change detected
→ impact assessment
→ still usable / targeted refresh / full re-search
→ revised recommendation
→ affected Quote, Review and Filing Package
```

Historical findings remain part of the decision record.

## 13. Search should connect to filing preparation

Accepted search outcomes should flow into later work through stable references:

- selected mark version;
- selected jurisdictions;
- goods and class versions;
- known risks;
- limitations or proposed amendments;
- customer decisions;
- required professional conditions;
- search expiry.

The filing team should not reconstruct these decisions from a PDF attachment and email thread.

## 14. AI's role

AI may assist with:

- query expansion;
- phonetic and semantic candidate generation;
- translation and transliteration variants;
- grouping and deduplication;
- owner and portfolio pattern detection;
- goods relationship analysis;
- result summarization;
- change-impact identification;
- drafting explanations.

AI may not:

- create official records;
- present inferred similarity as verified conflict;
- declare legal clearance;
- hide source limitations;
- issue professional advice without accountable review;
- convert a confidence score into a filing decision;
- mutate formal Matter state.

## 15. Example: one name, three possible routes

A customer proposes a short English mark for household appliances in the United States and European Union.

Search produces:

- one identical inactive US record with uncertain current significance;
- one phonetically similar active EU mark for related retail services;
- several low-relevance records in unrelated goods.

MarkReg may present:

### Route A — proceed unchanged

- fastest;
- preserves brand preference;
- requires professional acceptance of the identified risk;
- may face examination or opposition cost.

### Route B — narrow and refine

- modifies selected goods;
- reduces one area of overlap;
- requires updated filing and marketing plan.

### Route C — adopt a new mark

- delays filing;
- creates new search and design work;
- may reduce long-term conflict exposure.

The Product should not convert these trade-offs into one “availability percentage.”

## 16. Failure modes

Search architecture fails when:

- search starts without a decision purpose;
- class number substitutes for goods analysis;
- data freshness is hidden;
- AI candidates are displayed as official facts;
- a negative search is marketed as clearance;
- one score replaces explanation;
- old searches remain active after material changes;
- the customer accepts risk without seeing alternatives;
- paid search recommendations are commercially biased;
- raw results are detached from source and version;
- search findings do not reach filing preparation.

## 17. Product and technical implications

MarkReg requires:

- source-aware search connectors;
- exact input versioning;
- query and scope records;
- result provenance;
- candidate and official-record separation;
- goods-aware relevance;
- review routing;
- structured findings;
- risk explanations;
- customer decision records;
- re-search and expiry rules;
- downstream impact analysis;
- stable links to Filing Package preparation.

## 18. Commercial implications

MarkReg may earn through:

- basic search services;
- expanded professional search;
- jurisdiction bundles;
- premium review;
- redesign and refiling support;
- portfolio analysis.

The revenue model should reward decision quality and evidence, not result volume or fear.

## 19. Operating principle

```text
Search the proposed decision
Preserve the exact scope
Show the source
Separate candidate from fact
Explain the risk
Offer alternatives
Record the decision
Refresh after material change
```

> Search is valuable when it changes a decision responsibly—not when it merely produces more records.
