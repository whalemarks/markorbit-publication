# Chapter 24 — Revenue Must Follow Accountable Outcomes and Explicit Roles

MarkReg can support several revenue models, but every charge must correspond to a visible role, a defined Outcome and an accountable service boundary.

A customer should not be asked to pay for an unexplained bundle called “trademark service” when the bundle actually contains official fees, local professional work, MarkReg production, partner margin, optional review, future phases and contingent risk.

```text
Revenue
→ role
→ scope
→ promised Outcome
→ completion evidence
→ financial responsibility
```

## 1. Revenue follows accountable value

MarkReg may support:

- direct self-operated service margin;
- white-label production fees;
- co-delivery orchestration fees;
- bounded capability-fulfillment fees;
- managed MGSN fulfillment;
- lifecycle monitoring plans;
- maintenance and renewal services;
- transaction and transfer services;
- premium review;
- Growth & Conversion services;
- enterprise operating packages.

These are not interchangeable labels. Each model changes who contracts, who invoices, who delivers, who carries recovery responsibility and who may communicate with the customer.

```text
Revenue Recipient
≠ Relationship Owner
≠ Delivery Owner
≠ Professional Authority
```

## 2. A charge needs a service basis

Every customer-facing charge should identify:

- charge category;
- service or external cost basis;
- included scope;
- excluded scope;
- payer;
- payment receiver;
- contracting party;
- Delivery Owner;
- expected Outcome;
- completion evidence;
- refund or cancellation rule;
- expiry or quote-validity period.

A general “processing fee” without a defined basis creates mistrust and makes later disputes difficult to resolve.

## 3. Quote architecture must be decomposed

A Quote may include:

```text
Official Filing Fee
Official Publication Fee
Official Registration Fee
Local Professional Fee
MarkReg Service Fee
Partner or Workplace Service Fee
Translation / Notarization / Legalization
Courier / Remittance / Tax
Optional Search or Review
Future Mandatory Phase
Contingent Refusal, Opposition or Recovery Work
```

The customer should understand what is:

- fixed;
- estimated;
- optional;
- contingent;
- pass-through;
- refundable;
- non-refundable after action;
- subject to exchange-rate or official-fee change.

```text
Quoted Total
≠ Every Future Lifecycle Cost Included
```

## 4. Outcome definition precedes price comparison

Two prices cannot be compared safely unless the promised Outcome is the same.

One provider may quote only filing preparation. Another may include official filing, publication monitoring, registration fee and certificate delivery.

```text
Lower Price
≠ Lower Cost for the Same Outcome
```

MarkReg should compare:

- included lifecycle phases;
- official fees;
- professional responsibility;
- evidence returned;
- customer communication;
- correction obligations;
- deadline protection;
- post-filing monitoring;
- exclusions.

## 5. Commercial models must remain explicit

### Direct self-operated service

The MarkReg-operated Workplace may contract, collect payment, own the customer relationship and accept delivery responsibility.

### White-label production

The partner Workplace normally remains customer-facing Contracting Party and Relationship Owner. MarkReg charges the partner for bounded production or managed fulfillment.

### Co-delivery

The commercial split must identify which party earns for:

- customer acquisition;
- advice or solution design;
- production;
- professional review;
- local filing;
- coordination;
- recovery;
- customer delivery.

### Bounded capability fulfillment

A Workplace may purchase one Work Package without buying the full lifecycle.

### MGSN fulfillment

Professional Provider economics remain separately governed and are not disguised as Contributor compensation.

## 6. Platform interest must be disclosed

MarkReg may recommend a route from which the platform or an affiliated Workplace earns revenue.

That interest should be disclosed when it could affect the recommendation.

```text
Preferred Commercial Route
≠ Hidden Mandatory Route
```

A Workplace retains the right to use its own eligible internal or external route where the Engagement and law permit.

## 7. Revenue cannot create authority

Payment may confirm a commercial commitment. It does not create:

- Filing Approval;
- Provider appointment;
- Professional Authority;
- customer identity verification;
- document validity;
- External Protected Action permission.

```text
Payment Received
≠ Execution Ready
```

The Product must preserve commercial, professional and execution readiness as separate planes.

## 8. Growth and conversion revenue needs clean attribution

SEO, GEO and automated conversion may contribute to a service opportunity. Revenue attribution should preserve:

- originating page or campaign;
- Lite content contribution;
- Sites publication surface;
- partner referral;
- Relationship Owner;
- conversion assistance;
- professional fulfillment;
- post-conversion delivery.

A page view should not be treated as a sale, and platform attribution should not erase a partner relationship.

```text
Conversion Source
≠ Customer Owner
```

## 9. Monitoring revenue must correspond to a real maintained capability

A recurring lifecycle plan may fund:

- official-status checks;
- deadline verification;
- obligation monitoring;
- evidence reminders;
- owner and address reconciliation;
- escalation;
- customer reporting.

It should not charge indefinitely for a static reminder that is never revalidated.

```text
Recurring Fee
→ Recurring Maintained Capability
```

## 10. Revenue must account for recovery

The real economic cost of delivery includes:

```text
Direct Production Cost
+ Review Cost
+ Rework Risk
+ Delay Risk
+ Customer Communication
+ Provider Failure
+ Refund and Correction Exposure
```

A low-cost route that repeatedly creates recovery work may be economically inferior to a higher direct-price route.

## 11. Refund and cancellation rules follow stage and responsibility

Refundability may differ before and after:

- professional review;
- Provider appointment;
- official fee payment;
- submission;
- document preparation;
- courier dispatch;
- transaction closing.

The rule should identify whether failure arose from:

- customer withdrawal;
- missing customer action;
- Provider failure;
- MarkReg error;
- official rejection;
- uncontrollable external change;
- duplicated or invalid payment.

A “no refund under any circumstance” clause is not a substitute for accountable service design.

## 12. Margin analytics must remain contextual

MarkReg may measure:

- gross revenue;
- official and Provider costs;
- Contributor compensation;
- review cost;
- customer-support burden;
- correction cost;
- refund exposure;
- expected lifecycle value;
- retention;
- relationship impact.

Margin should not be optimized by silently reducing review, choosing an ineligible Provider or concealing later mandatory phases.

## 13. Product implications

MarkReg requires:

- typed charge categories;
- Quote versioning;
- role and revenue attribution;
- Outcome-based scope;
- fixed, estimated, optional and contingent labels;
- quote validity and amendment;
- commercial-interest disclosure;
- refund and cancellation logic;
- cost and recovery analytics;
- links between conversion source, Order, Matter and delivery.

## 14. Operating principle

```text
Explain the role
Define the Outcome
Separate the costs
Disclose the interest
Version the Quote
Do not sell authority
Measure recovery, not only gross margin
```

> Revenue becomes trustworthy when the customer can see who is charging, what is being delivered, what remains uncertain and what evidence will prove completion.
