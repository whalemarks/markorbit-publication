# Book 05 — Chapter and Thesis Map

## Book thesis

MarkReg is the governed international trademark growth and full-lifecycle service product of MarkOrbit.

It connects service discovery, decision-ready Intake, commercial commitment, professional production, external execution, continuing rights, transactions, evidence, recovery, economics and learning without collapsing Product, Workplace, customer relationship, professional authority or official state.

## Part I — Lifecycle and customer relationship

### CH01 — One Accountable Lifecycle

A coherent customer journey requires one accountable lifecycle view, not one authority, database or status.

### CH02 — Product, Service and Operating Model

MarkReg Product, Product Installation, Service Catalog, Operating Model and MarkReg-operated Workplace are distinct layers.

### CH03 — Customer Relationship Modes

Direct service, white-label delivery, co-delivery and bounded fulfillment require explicit Relationship Owner, Contracting Party, Delivery Owner, payment and professional roles.

### CH04 — Intake and Decision Readiness

MarkReg should ask fewer high-value questions, preserve facts, inferences and Unknowns, and separate Decision-ready Intake from Formal Intake and Execution Readiness.

## Part II — Trademark lifecycle services

### CH05 — Search Is a Decision Product

Search should produce scoped, sourced and explainable decision support rather than an unqualified result list or clearance claim.

### CH06 — Filing Preparation

Filing preparation requires exact applicant identity, mark version, goods scope, authority and document readiness.

### CH07 — External Filing

Provider discovery, eligibility, selection, appointment, acceptance, submission and official acknowledgement remain distinct.

### CH08 — Prosecution and Response

Official events, issue decomposition, deadlines, customer decisions, professional strategy, Package review and protected submission must follow risk and authority.

### CH09 — Registration as Verified Transition

Registration requires official verification, registered-scope comparison, certificate reconciliation and creation of a Right Baseline.

### CH10 — Continuing Rights

Maintenance, renewal, declarations, recordals, ownership changes, evidence, recovery and re-filing form one continuing lifecycle.

### CH11 — Trademark Transactions

Transaction Opportunity, due diligence, commercial terms, closing, assignment, asset delivery and official recordal remain separate.

## Part III — Production architecture

### CH12 — Order, Matter and Work Package

Commercial commitment, governed professional context and bounded production units must remain distinct.

### CH13 — One Delivery Model

Internal teams, systems, AI, Contributors, Reviewers, partners and Providers can participate in one Delivery Plan while preserving role and authority differences.

### CH14 — Certified Production Pools

Certification does not create production access; Capability, authorization, eligibility, Assignment and scoped data access remain separate.

### CH15 — Progression into Real Work

Simulation, shadow work, supervised production, bounded independence and review authority form an evidence-backed and reversible progression.

### CH16 — Professional Contribution Networks

Bounded professional Contributions require scope, review, compensation, confidentiality and customer-relationship protection and remain distinct from MGSN.

## Part IV — Quality and responsibility

### CH17 — Risk-based Review

Review depth follows consequence, reversibility, authority, source quality, uncertainty, value and deadline.

### CH18 — Customer Communication

A coherent customer experience must preserve represented identity, purpose, source, approvals, Relationship Owner and real responsibility.

### CH19 — Evidence and Audit

Files, Evidence, facts, Decisions, approvals, Provider Returns and official state require traceable provenance and versioning.

### CH20 — Failure and Recovery

Provider silence, rejected documents, Unknown submission, payment failure, stale public content and routing errors are core governed Product paths.

## Part V — Growth, discovery and conversion

### CH21 — Source-backed Service Discovery

MarkReg supplies governed service meaning and professional objects for SEO and GEO while Sites, Lite and Knowledge retain their respective publishing, distribution and provenance roles.

### CH22 — Automated Conversion

Conversion should move from provisional intent through progressive clarification and explainable options to Decision-ready Intake and formal Handoff.

### CH23 — Content Quality Testing

Professional truth, usefulness, SEO, GEO, conversion quality and operational safety require separate measurement and governance.

## Part VI — Economics

### CH24 — Accountable Revenue

Every charge should identify the role, scope, promised Outcome, evidence and financial responsibility.

### CH25 — Contributor Compensation

The bounded Work Package is the economic unit for Contributor production; real production cannot be disguised as unpaid learning.

### CH26 — Provider and Funds Governance

Official fees, Provider fees, service fees, funds checkpoints, remittance, custody and reconciliation remain distinct.

## Part VII — Learning and investment

### CH27 — Case Distillation

Operational records require rights, privacy, provenance, synthesis and review before becoming learning, Knowledge, public cases or AI-training data.

### CH28 — Outcome-based Capability and Routing

Validated Outcomes should improve scoped Capability, Reviewer, Provider and process decisions without creating universal rank or authority.

### CH29 — Market Signals and Investment

Demand, conversion, failure, economics and supply-readiness signals should guide hypotheses and bounded pilots without becoming automatic truth.
