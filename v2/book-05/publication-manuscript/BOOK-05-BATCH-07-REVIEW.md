# Book 05 v2 Publication Reconstruction — Batch 07 Review

## Scope reviewed

- CH24 — Revenue Must Follow Accountable Outcomes and Explicit Roles
- CH25 — Contributor Compensation Must Reward Bounded Production Value
- CH26 — Provider Economics, Official Fees and Funds Checkpoints Must Remain Distinct

## Structural verdict

Batch 07 establishes MarkReg's economic architecture without selecting final prices, commission percentages, payment providers or custody models.

The governing chain is:

```text
Defined role and scope
→ Quote and approval
→ commercial commitment
→ funds checkpoint
→ governed production or Provider service
→ evidence-backed Outcome
→ reconciliation
```

## CH24 review — revenue architecture

The chapter links every charge to a role, scope, promised Outcome, evidence and financial responsibility.

It separates:

- official fees;
- Provider professional fees;
- MarkReg service fees;
- partner or Workplace fees;
- Contributor compensation;
- translation, legalization, courier, tax and remittance;
- future mandatory phases;
- contingent refusal, opposition and recovery work.

It preserves direct, white-label, co-delivery, bounded fulfillment and MGSN commercial models without collapsing Relationship Owner, Contracting Party, Payment Receiver, Delivery Owner and Professional Authority.

The following distinction is accepted:

```text
Revenue Recipient
≠ Relationship Owner
≠ Delivery Owner
≠ Professional Authority
```

## CH25 review — Contributor compensation

The chapter makes the bounded Work Package the economic unit for Contributor production.

It requires:

- a clear offer before work;
- visible compensation and payment triggers;
- typed premiums and adjustments;
- revision classification;
- reasoned rejection;
- reviewer compensation;
- cancellation and partial-work treatment;
- rights and reuse terms;
- disputes and appeal;
- separation of compensation from Capability evidence.

It explicitly prevents training, certification or exposure from replacing payment for real production value.

```text
Learning Opportunity
≠ Free Customer Production
```

## CH26 review — Provider and official-fee economics

The chapter separates Provider procurement, official fees, funds checkpoints, remittance and reconciliation.

It correctly distinguishes:

```text
Customer Paid
≠ Provider Paid
≠ Official Fee Paid
≠ Filing Completed
```

Provider estimates remain distinct from verified official fees and final procurement cost. Fee changes propagate to public content, calculators, Quotes, Orders and pending execution routes.

The chapter also preserves the difference between Provider Organizations and Contributors, and does not infer payment custody from financial orchestration.

## Accepted distinctions

```text
Revenue ≠ Authority
Quoted Total ≠ All Future Lifecycle Cost
Lower Price ≠ Same Outcome at Lower Cost
Payment Received ≠ Execution Ready
Compensation Record ≠ Capability Evidence
Revision Requested ≠ Contributor Fault Proven
Payment ≠ Unrestricted Reuse Right
Provider ≠ Contributor with More Permissions

Professional Completion ≠ Financial Reconciliation
```

## Failure-mode check

The batch directly addresses:

- opaque all-inclusive pricing;
- low filing prices hiding later mandatory phases;
- platform self-preference without disclosure;
- payment being mistaken for execution authority;
- real production disguised as training;
- unlimited unpaid revision;
- unexplained Contributor rejection;
- unpaid reviewer dependency;
- lowest-price Provider routing;
- Provider invoices being treated as official-fee authority;
- one paid flag hiding multiple funds stages;
- fee changes not reaching pages, Quotes and Matters;
- professional completion without financial reconciliation;
- payment orchestration silently becoming custody.

No blocking contradiction with Books 04, 06 or 07 was identified.
