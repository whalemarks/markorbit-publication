## CH17 review — risk-based review

CH17 establishes review as a governed decision architecture rather than a generic approval flag.

It correctly begins with upstream quality design:

- source quality;
- Work Package clarity;
- suitable Assignment;
- deterministic validation;
- version control;
- proportional review;
- accountable approval.

Review depth is based on separate dimensions:

- legal and professional consequence;
- reversibility;
- source quality;
- uncertainty;
- financial exposure;
- customer impact;
- novelty and complexity;
- deadline and recovery risk;
- performer history;
- external authority.

The chapter introduces a practical review spectrum without turning it into a universal legal classification.

The following distinctions are explicit:

```text
Review
≠ Approval
≠ Customer Decision
≠ Professional Authority
≠ External Execution

Version 1 Reviewed
≠ Materially Changed Version 2 Reviewed
```

The chapter also extends quality governance to SEO, GEO, public professional content and conversion routes.

## CH18 review — customer communication

CH18 establishes communication as formal delivery work.

It preserves a coherent customer experience while retaining:

- Relationship Owner;
- Contracting Party;
- Payment Receiver;
- Delivery Owner;
- Professional Authority;
- represented sender identity.

Communication purposes are typed, and drafting, approval, sending, receipt and interpretation remain separate.

The chapter correctly preserves:

```text
One Customer Experience
≠ One Hidden Actor

Positive Reply
≠ Customer Instruction

Customer Silence
≠ Filing Approval or Abandonment Instruction

Task Access
≠ Customer Relationship
```

White-label and co-delivery remain valid, but fictitious professional identity and silent customer transfer are not permitted.

Operational Matter communication is also kept distinct from marketing and cross-sell communication.

## CH19 review — evidence and audit

CH19 establishes a full evidence chain from source to validated Outcome.

It separates:

```text
File
≠ Evidence Accepted
≠ Fact Verified
≠ Decision Authorized
```

The chapter defines:

- typed Evidence records;
- purpose-scoped acceptance;
- evidence-quality dimensions;
- Decision Records;
- exact-version approvals;
- Evidence Contracts for Provider and external actions;
- customer, delivery, professional, commercial and content audit views;
- corrected and superseded evidence;
- auditable Unknowns;
- AI provenance;
- public-claim and conversion-event lineage;
- retention and purpose-limited access.

The chapter correctly avoids claiming that cryptographic integrity alone establishes legal truth.

## CH20 review — failure and recovery

CH20 treats failure and recovery as ordinary Product paths.

It establishes:

```text
Failure Signal
→ Containment
→ Verification
→ Responsibility
→ Communication
→ Recovery Options
→ Approval and Action
→ Evidence-backed Resolution
→ Learning
```

It covers:

- source and status failure;
- identity and authority failure;
- document and evidence failure;
- deadline failure;
- execution ambiguity;
- commercial and payment failure;
- communication failure;
- SEO, GEO, content and conversion failure.

The chapter preserves Unknown external effect and explicitly prevents unsafe retry.

It also governs Provider silence, rejected documents, missed action, amendments, duplicate instructions, correction propagation, incident severity, Recovery Owner authority, evidence-backed closure and fallback routes.
