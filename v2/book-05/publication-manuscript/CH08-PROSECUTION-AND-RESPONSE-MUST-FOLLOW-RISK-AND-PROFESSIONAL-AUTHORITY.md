# Chapter 08 — Prosecution and Response Must Follow Risk and Professional Authority

Filing creates a procedural position, not a finished right.

The office may issue a formalities notice, identification requirement, translation request, disclaimer, substantive refusal, prior-right objection, evidence request or other action. A third party may oppose. A deadline may be unclear. The customer may need to choose between amendment, argument, evidence, negotiation, appeal, abandonment or refiling.

A generic case-management system can record that an office action arrived. MarkReg must do more: preserve the source, classify consequence, identify the decision owner, prepare bounded response options and govern the route to an authorized professional submission.

```text
Official or Provider event
→ source validation
→ issue decomposition
→ risk and deadline classification
→ responsibility and authority assignment
→ response options
→ customer and professional decisions
→ Response Package
→ review and approval
→ authorized submission
→ official acknowledgement and continuing state
```

## 1. Prosecution begins with event authority

An email from a Provider, a portal alert, an official notice and an internal reminder do not carry the same authority.

MarkReg should distinguish:

```text
Official notice
Official register event
Provider report
Provider interpretation
Connector delivery record
Internal reminder
AI-classified event candidate
```

Before a consequential response route is created, the Product should preserve:

- jurisdiction;
- application or right identifier;
- official or external source;
- source date and retrieval time;
- notice version;
- language and translation status;
- deadline stated or derived;
- confidence and conflict;
- linked Filing Package and official acknowledgement;
- professional interpretation status.

## 2. Office actions are not one task type

MarkReg should decompose the notice into issues.

Possible issue classes include:

- formal correction;
- applicant or address issue;
- mark-description issue;
- translation or transliteration;
- disclaimer;
- goods and services amendment;
- filing-basis issue;
- signature or authority issue;
- specimen or use-evidence issue;
- distinctiveness refusal;
- descriptiveness refusal;
- prior-right conflict;
- geographic or deceptiveness issue;
- procedural deficiency;
- evidence request;
- opposition or cancellation;
- appeal or review;
- locally qualified strategy requirement.

A single notice may contain several issue classes with different deadlines, authorities and response routes.

## 3. Triage should follow consequence

A triage model may consider:

- whether the issue is curable;
- whether rights may be lost;
- deadline certainty;
- customer decision required;
- professional qualification required;
- evidence burden;
- cost;
- Provider dependency;
- strategic impact;
- possibility of partial response;
- appeal or refiling alternatives.

Possible triage outcomes include:

```text
Routine correction candidate
Customer fact confirmation required
Professional review required
Evidence collection required
Local counsel strategy required
Settlement or negotiation route
Refile or abandon option
Emergency escalation
Unknown — source reconciliation required
```

## 4. Deadline governance is its own discipline

A deadline may come from:

- the official notice;
- statutory rule;
- official portal;
- Provider calculation;
- internal calendar;
- extension or grace-period rule;
- court or tribunal order.

The Deadline Record should include:

- source;
- jurisdiction and procedure;
- event triggering the deadline;
- calculation rule;
- time zone;
- ordinary and extended deadline;
- extension conditions;
- confidence;
- Professional Owner;
- current responsible party;
- revalidation trigger.

```text
Calendar reminder
≠ verified deadline
```

Conflicting deadline sources should create an exception, not a silent choice.

## 5. Customer communication should explain the decision

A useful office-action report should tell the customer:

- what the office said;
- which parts are formal facts;
- what the Provider or professional concludes;
- what options exist;
- what each option costs;
- what evidence is needed;
- which scope may be lost or changed;
- what deadline applies;
- what happens if no action is taken;
- which questions require customer instruction.

A long legal notice forwarded without explanation is not a professional product outcome.

## 6. Customer fact, commercial decision and legal strategy differ

The customer may confirm:

- identity;
- address;
- actual goods;
- use dates;
- translation meaning;
- willingness to narrow scope;
- budget;
- business priority;
- settlement preference;
- whether to continue.

The professional may determine:

- legal interpretation;
- argument structure;
- acceptable amendment;
- evidence sufficiency;
- procedural route;
- appeal viability;
- filing strategy.

```text
Customer chooses a business objective
≠ customer provides legal opinion

Professional recommends a route
≠ customer authorized cost and action
```

## 7. Response options should preserve trade-offs

A Response Option may include:

- comply with a formal request;
- amend goods or description;
- provide translation or disclaimer;
- submit evidence;
- argue against refusal;
- request extension;
- divide or partially abandon;
- negotiate coexistence;
- oppose or defend;
- appeal;
- refile;
- abandon intentionally.

For each option, MarkReg should show:

- expected objective;
- scope consequence;
- evidence and document requirements;
- professional route;
- estimated fees;
- deadline;
- uncertainty;
- later-stage implications.

## 8. AI may prepare, but professional authority controls strategy

AI may:

- extract issues;
- translate and summarize notices;
- compare the notice with filed records;
- identify missing evidence;
- draft customer questions;
- organize authorities supplied by professionals;
- compare response versions;
- detect unsupported claims;
- prepare a structured draft.

AI may not:

- establish the official deadline by confidence alone;
- issue final legal strategy;
- decide evidence sufficiency;
- accept a disclaimer or deletion;
- submit a response;
- infer customer abandonment from silence;
- override a locally qualified professional.

## 9. The Response Package is versioned

A Response Package may contain:

- exact notice and issue references;
- application and Filing Package context;
- response position;
- amendments;
- argument;
- declarations;
- exhibits and evidence;
- translations;
- customer confirmations;
- professional review;
- filing instructions;
- approved fees;
- submission route;
- deadline;
- unresolved risks.

A response approved for one version cannot automatically authorize a materially revised version.

## 10. Evidence requires provenance and purpose

Evidence may include:

- product photographs;
- webpages;
- invoices;
- sales records;
- advertising;
- declarations;
- corporate records;
- consent documents;
- translations;
- prior registrations;
- market reports.

For each item, MarkReg should preserve:

- source;
- date;
- creator or custodian;
- jurisdiction and issue purpose;
- authenticity or validation state;
- confidentiality;
- permitted use;
- reviewer finding;
- final submitted version.

```text
Evidence uploaded
≠ evidence sufficient
≠ evidence submitted
```

## 11. Goods amendments need change propagation

An office action may require narrowing or clarifying goods. A change can affect:

- customer expectations;
- search relevance;
- legal coverage;
- pricing;
- future use evidence;
- related jurisdictions;
- portfolio strategy;
- Marketing Packs and Listings.

MarkReg should preserve the prior version, reason, professional recommendation, customer approval and downstream impact.

## 12. Extensions are not default safety nets

An extension may require:

- eligibility;
- filing before a specific date;
- official fee;
- customer approval;
- Provider action;
- formal evidence.

The existence of a possible extension should not reduce urgency until the route is verified and approved.

```text
Extension may be available
≠ deadline safely extended
```

## 13. Opposition and adversarial matters need a stronger mode

Adversarial work may introduce:

- opposing party and counsel;
- tribunal or court;
- pleadings;
- evidence disclosure;
- settlement privilege;
- costs exposure;
- conflicts;
- litigation hold;
- higher professional qualification;
- strategic confidentiality.

MarkReg may coordinate the Product journey, but it should not represent adversarial work as an ordinary response checklist.

A separate Engagement, Matter context, Provider appointment and professional strategy may be required.

## 14. Publication and opposition windows remain open states

Publication is not registration.

The Product should distinguish:

```text
Accepted for publication
Published
Opposition window open
Opposition filed
Opposition resolved
Registration fee due
Registration issued
```

A Provider's optimistic forecast should not close the opposition or registration state before official evidence exists.

## 15. Appeals, cancellation and restoration are distinct routes

A failed response may lead to:

- administrative review;
- appeal;
- petition;
- cancellation defense;
- restoration;
- refiling;
- abandonment;
- negotiated resolution.

Each route has its own:

- authority;
- deadline;
- evidence;
- fee;
- professional qualification;
- customer decision;
- expected outcome.

The system should not label every rescue route as “appeal.”

## 16. Silence and non-response require explicit decision rules

Customer silence may create serious risk. MarkReg should:

- preserve outreach attempts;
- show the verified deadline;
- escalate according to Engagement and Policy;
- distinguish inability to reach the customer from intentional abandonment;
- record whether the Workplace has authority to take any protective step;
- prevent unauthorized expenditure or filing.

```text
Customer silent
≠ customer instructed abandonment
≠ professional authorized to respond
```

## 17. Submission follows the same protected-action chain

A response should progress through:

```text
Response Package prepared
→ Professional Review
→ customer and commercial decisions
→ response approval
→ Provider instruction
→ Provider Acceptance
→ submission
→ evidence Return
→ official acknowledgement
```

Provider changes, extra fees or new evidence requirements may trigger reapproval.

## 18. Official response outcome remains sourced

After submission, possible states include:

- Provider reports filed;
- official receipt pending;
- officially acknowledged;
- accepted;
- partially accepted;
- further action issued;
- refused;
- abandoned;
- appealed;
- unknown.

A customer-facing update should state the source and remaining next action.

## 19. Failure and recovery

Common failures include:

- notice attached to the wrong application;
- mistranslated deadline;
- incomplete issue extraction;
- Provider silence;
- customer decision delayed;
- missing original or evidence;
- fee increase;
- filing rejected;
- duplicate response;
- official portal unavailable;
- response filed but receipt missing.

Each exception should record:

- failure source;
- affected rights and deadline;
- current evidence;
- responsible actor;
- recovery options;
- fee consequence;
- customer communication;
- safe retry decision.

## 20. Example: translation and mark-description requirements

An application receives an office action requiring:

- transliteration and translation of non-Latin characters;
- a description stating that the mark consists of three stylized Chinese characters.

A governed process should:

1. validate the official notice;
2. separate the two issues;
3. confirm the exact characters and mark image;
4. prepare translation and description candidates;
5. obtain professional review;
6. explain whether the scope or identity changes;
7. obtain required customer confirmation and response approval;
8. instruct the Provider with the exact Response Package;
9. obtain filing evidence;
10. validate official acknowledgement.

If the application was previously abandoned, refiling is a new service route, not a silent continuation of the old Matter.

## 21. Failure modes

Prosecution governance fails when:

- Provider email becomes official truth;
- one notice becomes one undifferentiated task;
- deadline sources are hidden;
- customer facts and legal strategy are merged;
- AI drafting becomes final advice;
- evidence upload becomes evidence sufficiency;
- goods changes do not propagate;
- possible extensions reduce urgency without confirmation;
- adversarial matters are treated as routine checklists;
- customer silence becomes consent or abandonment;
- response approval survives material change;
- submission is declared complete without official evidence.

## 22. Product and technical implications

MarkReg requires:

- sourced event ingestion;
- issue decomposition;
- Deadline Records;
- risk-based triage;
- role and responsibility assignment;
- customer decision requests;
- Response Options;
- evidence provenance;
- Response Packages;
- professional review and approval;
- Provider Handoffs and Returns;
- official acknowledgement validation;
- exception and recovery workflows;
- change propagation into portfolio and continuing-right state.

## 23. Commercial implications

Prosecution revenue may include:

- notice reporting;
- routine formal response;
- substantive professional response;
- evidence preparation;
- local counsel;
- extension;
- opposition or appeal;
- refiling.

Pricing should distinguish the original service scope from later-stage work and disclose official fees, Provider costs and uncertainty. The Product should not use an intentionally low filing price while hiding predictable prosecution economics.

## 24. Operating principle

```text
Validate the event
Separate the issues
Verify the deadline
Assign responsibility
Show the options
Preserve professional authority
Approve the exact response
Submit through a governed route
Validate the official result
```

> Prosecution becomes manageable when every notice is converted into sourced issues, bounded decisions and evidence-backed action without allowing speed or automation to replace professional authority.
