# Chapter 13 — Internal Teams, Systems, Contributors and Providers Need One Delivery Model

A full-lifecycle trademark service cannot be delivered by one kind of performer.

One Matter may require:

- deterministic status checks;
- AI-assisted information extraction;
- internal customer coordination;
- contributor data verification;
- expert review;
- local professional filing;
- courier handling;
- official-source verification;
- final customer delivery.

The common response is to build a separate operating process for each source of work. Employees use an internal checklist. Freelancers receive messages. AI runs through prompts. External lawyers receive email instructions. Partners use their own spreadsheets. The customer sees one service, but the production system underneath is fragmented.

MarkReg needs one Delivery Model without pretending that all performers have the same legal, commercial or professional status.

```text
One governed Delivery Model
≠ one employment relationship
≠ one authority level
≠ one data-access model
≠ one commercial contract
```

## 1. The common unit is the Work Package

The common denominator is not the person or system performing the work. It is the Work Package defined in Chapter 12.

A Work Package states:

- the source Matter and Order context;
- the required Outcome;
- the input and version;
- scope and exclusions;
- required Capability;
- risk tier;
- allowed tools and AI mode;
- required authority;
- data-access boundary;
- deadline and dependencies;
- review and acceptance criteria;
- completion Evidence;
- compensation or procurement terms where applicable;
- failure and escalation route.

The same Work Package type may be fulfilled through different production lanes, but the contract does not disappear.

For example, `Official Status Verification` may be performed by:

- deterministic retrieval from a supported official connector;
- an internal operations specialist;
- a certified contributor;
- an external professional where interpretation is required;
- a partner Workplace using its own approved source.

The accepted Outcome remains a sourced, dated, scoped verification—not a screenshot or a casual statement that the case “looks active.”

## 2. Five production lanes

MarkReg should distinguish at least five production lanes.

### Internal team lane

Work performed by employees or directly managed staff of the Delivery Workplace.

This lane may support:

- customer communication;
- case coordination;
- document collection;
- routine preparation;
- review;
- recovery;
- delivery assembly.

Internal employment does not automatically grant professional or protected-action authority.

### Deterministic system lane

Work performed by rules, connectors, scripts, validation engines or reproducible transformations.

Examples include:

- schema validation;
- field comparison;
- deadline candidate calculation;
- document presence checks;
- duplicate detection;
- file conversion;
- official-source retrieval where supported.

A deterministic result may be highly reliable within its scope. It cannot decide facts or legal conclusions outside that scope.

### AI-assisted production lane

AI may:

- extract data;
- compare versions;
- retrieve Knowledge;
- cluster issues;
- draft customer questions;
- summarize official notices;
- propose goods wording;
- identify possible missing evidence;
- prepare a candidate report.

The Work Package must state the permitted AI mode, prohibited data disclosure, human verification requirement and responsibility for the final Contribution.

```text
AI-generated Output
≠ Accepted Contribution
≠ Professional Decision
```

### Contributor lane

A contracted individual or bounded contributor may perform defined production or review work without becoming the external professional Provider for the Matter.

Examples include:

- status checking;
- data cleanup;
- evidence screening;
- goods normalization;
- translation verification;
- content factual review;
- visual review;
- search-result organization.

Contributor work requires explicit eligibility, Assignment, minimum-necessary access, review and compensation rules.

### Professional Provider lane

A qualified external professional or Organization may perform work requiring local representation, regulated authority, institutional responsibility or jurisdiction-specific professional judgment.

Examples include:

- signing or filing before an office;
- formal legal opinion;
- office-action response;
- opposition or cancellation work;
- regulated certification;
- custody or handling that requires a professional or institutional role.

A Provider is not merely a contributor with broader permissions. Provider eligibility, appointment, instruction, acceptance and Return remain governed stages.

## 3. Partner Workplace delivery is not invisible outsourcing

A partner Workplace may deliver part or all of a service through:

- white-label production;
- co-delivery;
- bounded capability fulfillment;
- its own local professional network;
- a MarkReg Product Installation.

The partner retains its identity and business sovereignty. The Delivery Model should record:

- originating Workplace;
- Relationship Owner;
- Contracting Party;
- Delivery Owner;
- Professional Authority;
- data controller or responsible party where applicable;
- customer-facing communication mode;
- evidence-return obligations;
- commercial and recovery responsibility.

```text
Shared Work Package Contract
≠ Workplace Merger
≠ Customer Relationship Transfer
```

## 4. One Delivery Plan assembles the lanes

A Matter should produce a versioned Delivery Plan.

The plan identifies:

- required Work Packages;
- lane proposed for each package;
- dependencies;
- critical path;
- customer actions;
- Provider lead times;
- review capacity;
- data-access requirements;
- payment and procurement checkpoints;
- fallback routes;
- final Outcome assembly.

Example:

```text
US Section 8 Matter

WP01 status verification          → deterministic + internal validation
WP02 owner reconciliation         → internal specialist
WP03 goods-use questionnaire      → customer + AI-assisted preparation
WP04 specimen screening           → certified contributor
WP05 evidence sufficiency review  → qualified reviewer
WP06 filing Package preparation   → internal production
WP07 customer approval            → Relationship Owner
WP08 attorney filing              → appointed Provider
WP09 official receipt validation  → internal + official source
WP10 customer delivery            → Delivery Owner
```

The plan may change if:

- the customer changes scope;
- a Provider declines;
- the deadline changes;
- evidence proves insufficient;
- a contributor becomes unavailable;
- a conflict appears;
- an official system is unavailable;
- the required professional route changes.

Material changes create a new plan version and may invalidate prior Assignment or approval.

## 5. Delivery Owner remains accountable

Distributed production does not distribute final accountability into nothing.

The Delivery Owner is responsible for ensuring that:

- all required packages exist;
- dependencies are understood;
- Assignments are accepted;
- review occurs at the required depth;
- customer decisions are obtained;
- protected actions remain gated;
- Returns are validated;
- exceptions are owned;
- the assembled customer Outcome is coherent.

The Delivery Owner does not need to perform every Work Package. It must know who or what is responsible for each one and what Evidence demonstrates completion.

```text
Many Performers
≠ No Accountable Delivery Owner
```

## 6. Responsibility and authority are typed

A production participant may have one or more roles:

- preparer;
- contributor;
- coordinator;
- reviewer;
- approver;
- professional representative;
- Provider manager;
- deadline owner;
- evidence owner;
- recovery owner.

The system must not infer authority from job title, system access or seniority.

An operations manager may coordinate a foreign filing but lack authority to sign the local submission. A qualified attorney may possess professional authority but have no Assignment for the Matter. An AI system may identify a deadline candidate but cannot be accountable for the deadline.

```text
Role
≠ Permission
≠ Assignment
≠ Professional Authority
```

## 7. Technical similarity does not erase relationship differences

Two people may perform the same status-verification Work Package under different legal and commercial relationships:

- employee;
- independent contractor;
- partner Workplace member;
- external professional Provider;
- customer representative.

The Work Package may be technically similar, but the system must preserve differences in:

- employment or contractor status;
- tax and payment treatment;
- confidentiality;
- intellectual-property rights;
- professional responsibility;
- data-controller roles;
- insurance;
- supervision;
- dispute resolution;
- customer communication.

MarkReg should not use one generic `worker` record to flatten these distinctions.

## 8. Systems are participants, not fictitious humans

Deterministic systems and AI should be represented honestly.

The record should show:

- system identity and version;
- function performed;
- source and input versions;
- confidence or validation result where relevant;
- human verification requirement;
- known limitations;
- failure state;
- responsible owner for operation and review.

The interface should not imply that an AI system is a licensed attorney, account manager or accountable reviewer.

```text
System Performed a Step
≠ System Holds Professional Responsibility
```

## 9. Contribution and Return remain distinct

A contributor submits a Contribution against an Assignment.

A Provider returns evidence or service results under an appointment and instruction.

Both must be validated, but they are not identical.

### Contribution

Usually a bounded production artifact such as:

- data verification;
- screening report;
- translated draft;
- structured analysis;
- review result.

### Provider Return

Usually an institutional or professional result such as:

- acceptance of appointment;
- filed document;
- official receipt;
- professional opinion;
- certificate;
- formal status report.

```text
Contribution
≠ Provider Return

Provider Return
≠ Official Truth
```

The originating Owning Service or authorized professional must validate the Return before it changes formal state.

## 10. Review is part of the Delivery Model, not an afterthought

Every Work Package should define whether review is:

- not required because the result is fully deterministic and validated;
- sampled;
- complete;
- specialist;
- professional;
- dual or panel review;
- customer confirmation;
- official-source verification.

Review depth follows:

- consequence;
- reversibility;
- source quality;
- contributor history;
- complexity;
- jurisdiction;
- time pressure;
- customer value;
- professional-responsibility requirements.

A high-volume production system cannot compensate for weak review merely by collecting more data.

## 11. Outcome assembly protects the customer from fragmentation

The customer should not receive an unfiltered package of internal fragments.

MarkReg should assemble accepted Contributions and validated Returns into a customer Outcome that explains:

- what was requested;
- what was completed;
- which official or professional evidence supports it;
- current state;
- material differences from the approved plan;
- unresolved risk or Unknown;
- fees and payment state where relevant;
- next action;
- future lifecycle obligations.

A coherent customer experience does not require hiding who held professional authority or who remains responsible.

## 12. Failure and reassignment

A production lane may fail because:

- the performer withdraws;
- the system is unavailable;
- a conflict appears;
- source access fails;
- the Contribution is rejected;
- review capacity is unavailable;
- the Provider does not accept;
- the deadline becomes unsafe;
- customer data is incomplete;
- the task exceeds the assigned scope.

The recovery path should:

1. preserve work and Evidence already produced;
2. revoke unnecessary access;
3. identify whether the Work Package remains valid;
4. assess deadline and duplicate risk;
5. select an eligible fallback lane;
6. issue or amend the Assignment;
7. notify the responsible owner;
8. preserve compensation and dispute rights;
9. update the Delivery Plan;
10. revalidate the final Outcome.

Reassignment does not erase the original performer’s history or convert a failed route into success.

## 13. Customer and relationship protection

Participants receive access for a defined production purpose.

That access does not permit them to:

- solicit the customer;
- redirect the Matter;
- reuse customer contact data;
- promote unrelated services;
- disclose confidential facts;
- bypass the Relationship Owner;
- claim ownership of future work.

At the same time, relationship protection should not prevent lawful customer choice, required professional disclosure or emergency direct communication that has been authorized by the Engagement.

## 14. Product implications

MarkReg requires:

- Work Package templates;
- production-lane types;
- Delivery Plans;
- Assignment contracts;
- system-participant records;
- capability and authority gates;
- data-scope controls;
- review policies;
- Contribution and Return validation;
- dependency and version management;
- Outcome assembly;
- failure and reassignment workflows;
- customer and relationship controls.

## 15. Commercial implications

One Delivery Model allows MarkReg to compare quality, cost, capacity and recovery across production lanes without treating them as interchangeable commodities.

It supports:

- internal operating efficiency;
- agency overflow support;
- paid contributor work;
- professional Provider procurement;
- co-delivery;
- white-label fulfillment;
- automation investment;
- transparent service margins.

The Product should not route work solely to the lowest-cost lane. The appropriate route depends on risk, authority, evidence quality, time and customer responsibility.

## 16. Operating principle

```text
Define one Work Package contract
Identify the production lane
Preserve relationship and authority
Assign explicitly
Limit access
Review according to risk
Validate Contributions and Returns
Assemble one accountable Outcome
```

> MarkReg can unify delivery without pretending that an employee, an AI system, a bounded contributor and a licensed local Provider are the same kind of participant.
