## 1. Decision

MarkReg is not limited to professional delivery after a customer has already decided to purchase a trademark service.

It also includes a bounded Growth & Conversion Layer that connects source-backed public service knowledge to decision-ready customer intake.

```text
Question, service need or commercial intent
→ source-backed discovery
→ governed professional content
→ intent understanding
→ progressive clarification
→ explainable service options
→ decision-ready Intake
→ commercial and professional Handoff
→ full-lifecycle delivery
→ continuing-right responsibility
→ corrected knowledge and growth signal
```

The amendment adds three publication chapters covering:

1. source-backed SEO, GEO and service discovery;
2. automated conversion from intent to decision-ready Intake;
3. content-quality testing across truth, usefulness, visibility and conversion.

## 2. Why the amendment is necessary

The prior Book 05 architecture began primarily after a customer had formed a recognizable trademark-service need.

In practice, many customers begin with a question:

- how to register a trademark in a country;
- which classes or goods to choose;
- whether an original POA is required;
- when a declaration or renewal is due;
- whether an abandoned mark can be restored;
- whether a registered trademark can be purchased and used;
- how official fees, professional fees and later-stage costs differ.

A full service Product should be able to transform those questions into governed understanding and an appropriate next route without forcing the customer to understand the professional workflow first.

Without this layer, MarkReg is a strong fulfillment system but an incomplete commercial Product.

## 3. Product boundary

The new layer is not authority for all public content and marketing activity.

```text
MarkReg
= trademark-service objects, decision logic, conversion routes and lifecycle outcomes

Sites
= public pages, publishing, indexing, schema, technical SEO and performance

Lite
= content creation, campaigns, distribution, social operations and daily growth work

Knowledge / Brain
= source, provenance, freshness, relationship and correction context

Core
= shared semantics for Need, Opportunity, Customer, Decision, Order, Matter and Evidence
```

MarkReg may supply a source-backed service page object, question-answer structure, conversion route and Service Intake entry point.

Sites may render and publish it.

Lite may adapt and distribute it through content channels.

Knowledge supplies the sourced facts and freshness controls.

The products remain distinct.

```text
MarkReg Growth Layer
≠ General CMS
≠ General CRM
≠ Advertising Platform
≠ Autonomous Outreach Agent
≠ Replacement for Lite, Sites or Knowledge
```

## 4. SEO scope

MarkReg SEO begins with professional objects rather than keyword volume.

Potential governed source objects include:

- Jurisdiction Pack;
- Service Catalog Item;
- Lifecycle Obligation;
- Requirement Set;
- Fee Basis;
- Document Requirement;
- Deadline Rule;
- Common Exception;
- Recovery Route;
- de-identified Case;
- Service Comparison;
- Customer Question.

A source-backed service page should identify:

- question or service intent;
- jurisdiction and scope;
- direct answer;
- material conditions;
- documents;
- fee components;
- timing basis;
- exceptions and Unknowns;
- professional-review requirement;
- sources and freshness;
- next governed action.

```text
SEO Page Published
≠ Legal Rule Verified

High Ranking
≠ Professional Accuracy
```

## 5. GEO scope

GEO means optimization for generative and answer engines without sacrificing professional truth.

A useful answer structure is:

```text
Question
→ direct answer
→ conditions
→ required documents
→ fee components
→ expected timing
→ exceptions
→ risks and Unknowns
→ source and date
→ next step
```

The Product should make facts machine-readable and human-understandable while preserving:

- source authority;
- jurisdiction;
- effective date;
- exceptions;
- fact, inference and recommendation distinctions;
- correction and supersession.

```text
AI Engine Citation
≠ Matter-specific Legal Conclusion
```

## 6. Automated conversion scope

A content or service visitor may enter a governed conversion flow:

```text
Content or Service-page Interaction
→ Intent Candidate
→ Relationship and Consent Check
→ High-value Clarification
→ Candidate Service Need
→ Option Set
→ Route and Fee Comparison
→ User Decision
→ Decision-ready Intake
→ Formal Commercial Handoff
```

The process should ask fewer questions by using authorized context, structured extraction, bounded inference and progressive clarification.

It must preserve:

```text
Content Interest
≠ Customer Instruction

AI Recommendation
≠ Professional Approval

Quote Viewed
≠ Order Created

Form Completed
≠ Protected Action Authorized
```

## 7. Content quality model

Content quality must be evaluated across separate dimensions.

### Factual and professional quality

- source accuracy;
- rule and fee freshness;
- jurisdiction and scope;
- material exceptions;
- fact, inference and recommendation separation;
- professional-review boundaries.

### SEO quality

- intent fit;
- page structure;
- discoverability;
- schema and internal links;
- duplication and index state;
- technical performance through Sites.

### GEO quality

- direct answer quality;
- structured facts;
- source accessibility;
- exception coverage;
- citation readiness;
- correction and supersession behavior.

### Conversion quality

- qualified service Need creation;
- Decision-ready Intake completion;
- Quote and Order progression;
- wrong-route and low-quality lead rate;
- professional correction cost;
- customer understanding and abandonment reasons.

```text
High Traffic
≠ High-quality Content

High Conversion
≠ Correct Conversion

AI Citation
≠ Professional Accuracy
```

## 8. Experiment boundary

A/B and multivariate testing may optimize:

- titles and summaries;
- page sequence;
- visual hierarchy;
- calls to action;
- question order;
- option presentation;
- long versus short explanation;
- content format.

It must not silently test different versions of:

- official fees;
- legal deadlines;
- document requirements;
- professional conclusions;
- success probability;
- material risk disclosure;
- customer authorization language;
- undisclosed preferential routing.

```text
Conversion Experiment
≠ Permission to Experiment with Legal Truth
```

## 9. Chapter architecture impact

The earlier v2 master contained 25 chapters.

The publication reconstruction had already expanded that structure by separating:

- registration verification; and
- maintenance, renewal, recordal and re-filing.

That created a 26-chapter equivalent before the new growth scope.

Adding three growth chapters produces the controlling publication structure:

```text
29 chapters
```

The earlier provisional estimate of 28 chapters is therefore corrected and superseded.

The new chapter placement is after quality and responsibility and before economics:

```text
CH21 — Service Discovery Must Be Source-backed
CH22 — Automated Conversion Must Move from Intent to Decision-ready Intake
CH23 — Content Quality Testing Must Separate Truth, Usefulness and Conversion
```

Economics moves to CH24–CH26. Learning and platform value moves to CH27–CH29.
