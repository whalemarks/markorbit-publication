# Part I — Product Promise and Service Modes

## Chapter 01 — One accountable lifecycle

International trademark work is fragmented across customer decisions, official databases, local rules, documents, payments, agents, lawyers, deadlines and evidence. Customers experience that fragmentation as uncertainty.

MarkReg's first promise is not that every step becomes automatic. It is that the lifecycle becomes visible, explainable and accountable.

```text
customer objective
→ decision-ready plan
→ accepted service scope
→ governed production
→ qualified external action where required
→ verified Return
→ customer delivery
→ continuing lifecycle memory
```

## Chapter 02 — MarkReg is product, service and operating model

MarkReg is simultaneously:

- a Workplace-scoped Product Installation;
- a service catalog;
- a lifecycle orchestration model;
- a quality and evidence system;
- a self-operated MarkReg Workplace;
- a white-label or co-delivery capability;
- a source of demand, cases and capability evidence.

These roles must not be collapsed. Product access does not automatically create a service contract. A service contract does not automatically transfer the customer relationship. Platform operation does not automatically create legal authority.

## Chapter 03 — Direct self-operated service

In direct mode, a MarkReg-operated Workplace may hold the customer relationship, contract, payment and delivery responsibility. Local professional authority may still belong to an MGSN or approved external provider.

The self-operated Workplace must remain isolated from partner Workplaces. Platform administration does not grant unrestricted access to partner customers, pricing or matters.

## Chapter 04 — White-label delivery

In white-label mode, the originating Workplace normally remains Relationship Owner and customer-facing contracting party. MarkReg supplies defined production and delivery capabilities behind that relationship.

The Engagement must state:

- which party communicates with the customer;
- whether MarkReg branding is visible;
- who approves recommendations;
- who receives payment;
- who bears final delivery responsibility;
- which professional authority acts externally;
- what data MarkReg may retain or reuse.

## Chapter 05 — Co-delivery

Co-delivery allows the originating Workplace and MarkReg to divide responsibilities openly.

For example:

```text
Originating Workplace
→ customer context, relationship and commercial approval

MarkReg
→ solution design, production orchestration and quality control

MGSN provider
→ local professional filing or response
```

The customer should see one coherent service, even when responsibility is distributed.

## Chapter 06 — Bounded capability fulfillment

A Workplace may use only one MarkReg capability without purchasing a complete lifecycle service.

Examples include:

- status and deadline verification;
- search review;
- filing-instruction preparation;
- evidence screening;
- certificate validation;
- transaction due diligence;
- provider instruction quality review.

The bounded service must identify its limits so the recipient does not mistake one work product for complete matter responsibility.

## Chapter 07 — Relationship ownership is explicit

Every MarkReg Engagement records at least:

```text
Demand Originator
Relationship Owner
Contracting Party
Payment Receiver
Delivery Owner
Professional Authority
```

The roles may be held by different participants. Their separation is not administrative overhead; it is the basis for trust between MarkReg and partner Workplaces.

## Chapter 08 — The customer should not manage the supply chain

Customers should not need to coordinate the Data Refinery, AI, certified contributors, MarkReg staff and local counsel.

MarkReg presents a coherent service layer while preserving internal attribution and accountability. Unified delivery must not erase who performed, reviewed or formally authorized each step.

## Chapter 09 — MarkReg is not the whole ecosystem

MarkReg should be the flagship proving ground for MarkOrbit capabilities, but it must not become the only route through which every Workplace can operate.

A Workplace may:

- perform work internally;
- use Lite and individual capabilities;
- engage MarkReg;
- engage MGSN;
- use a self-managed external route;
- combine these under a governed Engagement.

MarkReg grows by being reliable and reusable, not by forcing all customer relationships into one operator.
