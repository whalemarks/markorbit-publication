# Part IV — Certified Production and Professional Authority

## Chapter 34 — Scaling through governed production

As MarkReg grows, the limiting resource will not be AI alone. It will be verified human judgment, review capacity, customer communication and qualified local action.

The solution is a governed production network, not anonymous outsourcing.

```text
Capability Certification
+ Production Authorization
+ scoped Assignment
+ review
+ quality evidence
= eligible production participation
```

## Chapter 35 — Certification is not production access

A learner may demonstrate knowledge in simulation without being ready to access real customer data.

Production access additionally requires:

- verified identity;
- confidentiality agreement;
- conflict policy;
- appropriate contracting or employment status;
- supervised practice;
- quality history;
- current capability evidence;
- available capacity;
- jurisdiction and language fit.

## Chapter 36 — Production authorization levels

A practical production ladder is:

```text
P0 training only
P1 supervised production
P2 independent standard work
P3 advanced production or review
P4 delivery lead
```

P1 users receive minimum data, cannot perform external protected actions and require complete review. Higher levels expand only after accepted evidence.

## Chapter 37 — Simulation to real work

The progression path is:

```text
simulation
→ shadow task
→ supervised live task
→ independent standard task
→ complex task
→ review authority
```

A shadow task uses real structure but does not control the actual customer Outcome. It compares the learner's work with the accepted production result.

## Chapter 38 — Routing criteria

Work Packages should be routed by more than a self-declared title.

Matching considers:

- Capability ID and level;
- production authorization;
- jurisdiction;
- professional qualification where required;
- language;
- recency;
- quality and rework history;
- capacity and deadline;
- conflict status;
- permitted data scope;
- reviewer availability.

## Chapter 39 — Task offer

A contributor receives a clear offer before accepting:

- required capability;
- production level;
- estimated effort;
- deadline;
- compensation;
- urgency or complexity premium;
- expected artifacts;
- revision obligations;
- access restrictions;
- review and payment conditions.

The system should not encourage bidding against other contributors for the lowest price.

## Chapter 40 — Scoped task workspace

Accepted work occurs in a scoped workspace containing only necessary context.

The contributor should not automatically see:

- the full customer portfolio;
- private pricing and margin;
- unrelated communications;
- other contributors' private records;
- reusable customer contact information.

Access expires after completion, review or cancellation.

## Chapter 41 — AI within production tasks

Each Work Package defines an allowed human–AI mode.

AI may:

- retrieve sources;
- structure information;
- compare versions;
- identify missing fields;
- draft questions or reports;
- perform deterministic checks through tools.

The contributor remains accountable for the responsibilities assigned to the human role. Undisclosed AI use outside policy may invalidate the Contribution.

## Chapter 42 — Reviewers are also governed

Review authority requires its own capability and evidence.

A reviewer must know:

- what was within the contributor's scope;
- what risk the review covers;
- which sources and versions apply;
- when to escalate to Professional Authority;
- how to distinguish correction from preference;
- how the review affects contributor evidence.

Reviewer quality should be measured and calibrated.

## Chapter 43 — Professional Authority

Some actions require a legally or professionally qualified person or Organization.

Professional Authority may be required for:

- reserved legal advice;
- signature;
- representation before an office;
- formal filing;
- opposition or litigation strategy;
- regulated certification;
- custody of client funds in certain structures.

MarkOrbit certification cannot replace that authority.

## Chapter 44 — MGSN provider eligibility

An MGSN provider is not merely a contributor with more permissions.

The provider operates through an accountable Organization and Workplace, with verified supply, professional qualifications, procurement terms, capacity and Return obligations.

MGSN work follows:

```text
need projection
→ route recommendation
→ provider allocation and acceptance
→ instruction
→ execution
→ typed Return
→ originating validation
```

## Chapter 45 — Internal staff, contributors and providers

The same task type may be performed through different labor and service relationships.

MarkReg must distinguish:

- employee or internal team work;
- contracted individual contribution;
- contracted institutional service;
- MGSN professional fulfillment;
- customer or partner Workplace work.

Technical similarity does not erase legal, tax, confidentiality or responsibility differences.

## Chapter 46 — Compensation and evidence

Accepted paid work creates two separate records:

```text
Compensation Record
Capability Evidence Record
```

Payment reflects agreed labor or service. Capability evidence reflects quality, independence, review result and later Outcome. Payment alone does not prove competence, and a useful training task may create evidence without being customer-billable.
