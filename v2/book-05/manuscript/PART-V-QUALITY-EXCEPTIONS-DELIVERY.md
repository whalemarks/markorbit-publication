# Part V — Quality, Exceptions and Customer Delivery

## Chapter 47 — Quality is designed before review

Quality does not begin when a senior person checks the final file.

It begins with:

- accurate source data;
- explicit capability requirements;
- complete Work Package inputs;
- version locks;
- deterministic checks;
- appropriate assignment;
- proportional review;
- traceable approval.

Final review cannot reliably repair a badly structured production system.

## Chapter 48 — Risk-based review

MarkReg uses risk tiers to determine human attention.

```text
R0 deterministic and reversible
R1 standardized with sampling
R2 professional review required
R3 senior or cross-disciplinary review
R4 qualified or expert final authority
```

Risk depends on legal consequence, deadline, financial value, reversibility, uncertainty, customer impact, contributor history and jurisdiction.

## Chapter 49 — Quality gates

A Matter may contain gates such as:

- intake completeness;
- applicant identity verified;
- search decision approved;
- goods version locked;
- document readiness;
- payment readiness;
- provider instruction accepted;
- pre-filing approval;
- receipt verification;
- customer delivery completeness.

A gate is a decision state with evidence, not a decorative checklist.

## Chapter 50 — Deadline governance

Deadlines should preserve:

- official source;
- event date;
- calculation rule;
- jurisdiction and timezone;
- ordinary and late periods;
- confidence;
- reviewer;
- customer dependency;
- next escalation.

A reminder is not proof that the deadline is correct. High-impact deadlines require source-qualified validation.

## Chapter 51 — Customer communication as formal work

Customer communication is part of professional delivery.

MarkReg should record:

- what question was asked;
- why the information matters;
- what alternatives exist;
- what deadline applies;
- what risk follows from no response;
- who approved the outgoing message;
- what the customer replied;
- how the reply changed the Matter.

AI may draft communication, but controlled customer channels and represented Workplace identity remain explicit.

## Chapter 52 — Contributors do not acquire customer rights

A contributor may see a limited task, draft a question or review evidence. That participation does not create an independent right to contact, solicit, retain or reuse customer information.

Customer communication occurs only where the Assignment and Engagement authorize it.

## Chapter 53 — Provider silence

Provider non-response is a governed exception.

The system should track:

```text
instruction sent
acknowledgement due
reminder threshold
escalation threshold
replacement eligibility
deadline exposure
funds status
customer communication need
```

Silence must not remain hidden inside one employee's inbox.

## Chapter 54 — Official-system ambiguity

An online status may be delayed, incomplete or inconsistent with a receipt.

MarkReg distinguishes:

- official filing evidence;
- portal status;
- provider statement;
- system inference;
- unresolved discrepancy.

The product should report uncertainty rather than manufacture a clean state.

## Chapter 55 — Rejected documents

When a POA, evidence item, translation or specimen is rejected, MarkReg records:

- rejecting authority;
- stated reason;
- affected filing or deadline;
- correction options;
- customer action;
- additional cost;
- responsibility;
- prevention lesson.

The corrected document does not erase the rejection history.

## Chapter 56 — Payment failure

Payment status and professional action must remain distinct.

A Matter may be professionally ready but commercially blocked, or paid but not professionally ready.

Payment failure handling includes:

- official fee deadline impact;
- provider prepayment requirement;
- customer notification;
- suspension rule;
- refundability;
- currency and remittance evidence;
- restart condition.

## Chapter 57 — Missed action and recovery

Where an action may have been missed, MarkReg should not immediately assume abandonment or promise recovery.

The recovery path is:

```text
verify official status
→ preserve evidence
→ identify ordinary, late and restoration options
→ obtain qualified opinion where required
→ present customer choices and costs
→ record approval
→ execute and monitor
```

## Chapter 58 — Duplicate and conflicting instructions

The same customer request may arrive through email, Lite, staff notes and provider correspondence.

MarkReg uses idempotency, versioning and authority rules to prevent duplicate filings, double payment or conflicting instructions.

## Chapter 59 — Customer-facing delivery

A delivery package should include what the customer needs to understand and retain:

- completed action;
- official reference;
- date and current status;
- accepted documents;
- costs paid;
- unresolved issues;
- next deadline;
- recommended next action;
- responsible contact;
- downloadable evidence.

Internal production complexity should not be dumped onto the customer.

## Chapter 60 — Certificate and receipt validation

A certificate or filing receipt should be checked for:

- trademark representation;
- owner name and address;
- number;
- classes and goods;
- dates;
- color or design treatment;
- document completeness;
- official authenticity indicators;
- consistency with the Matter.

Receiving a document is not the same as accepting it as correct.

## Chapter 61 — Closure is a governed state

An Order closes only when its completion definition is met.

The Matter may remain active for future lifecycle monitoring.

Closure records:

- accepted Outcomes;
- outstanding limitations;
- final commercial status;
- documents delivered;
- future events created;
- evidence completeness;
- candidate learning artifacts.

## Chapter 62 — Quality evidence improves routing

Accepted and failed Outcomes improve future decisions about:

- which Work Package needs more review;
- which contributor is suitable;
- which provider is reliable;
- which checklist is incomplete;
- which rule changed;
- which customer explanation is ineffective.

Quality data must remain contextual. A provider's strength in one jurisdiction or action does not prove universal capability.
