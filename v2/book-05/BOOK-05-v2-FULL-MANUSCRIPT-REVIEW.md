# Book 05 v2 Full Manuscript Review

## Decision

`READY FOR OWNER REVIEW — NOT YET ACCEPTED AS PUBLICATION AUTHORITY`

## Scope completed

The Book 05 v2 master synopsis has been expanded into a six-part working manuscript with seventy-seven numbered chapters and a closing thesis.

Coverage includes:

- direct, white-label, co-delivery and bounded capability service modes;
- customer decision readiness and minimal-question guidance;
- country, class, goods, applicant, mark and document preparation;
- search, filing, prosecution, registration, maintenance and transaction lifecycles;
- Matter and Order separation;
- Order decomposition into governed Work Packages;
- Assignment, Contribution, Review and Outcome assembly;
- certification, production authorization and professional qualification separation;
- supervised production and contributor routing;
- MGSN provider eligibility and typed Return;
- risk-based review, deadlines, customer communication and exception handling;
- quote, contributor and provider economics;
- case distillation, capability feedback and staged 2026–2036 evolution.

## Semantic locks preserved

- MarkReg does not automatically acquire partner customer relationships.
- Matter remains distinct from Order.
- Work Package remains distinct from Assignment and Contribution.
- certification does not grant legal qualification or automatic production access.
- formal professional action remains with Professional Authority.
- MGSN remains distinct from internal production contribution.
- provider Return is validated before becoming formal truth.
- the active Canon and frozen Book 05 baseline remain unchanged.

## Product judgments

1. MarkReg should be built around complete lifecycle Outcomes rather than filing transactions.
2. The first production pilot should remain United States post-registration maintenance because it exercises data, knowledge, customer guidance, evidence, contributor work, professional filing and future monitoring.
3. White-label and co-delivery are essential for ecosystem adoption; direct self-operated service alone would create partner distrust.
4. Distributed production must begin with explicit Work Packages and supervised access, not a generic task marketplace.
5. Formal provider service and bounded contributor work require separate commercial and authority models.

## Review questions

1. Is MarkReg's flagship role strong enough without becoming the default commercial owner of the ecosystem?
2. Are the four service modes sufficiently distinct for product and contract implementation?
3. Does the Work Package model create enough standardization without oversimplifying country differences?
4. Should the seventy-seven short chapters be consolidated later into approximately thirty-five publication chapters?
5. After merge, should the next full manuscript be Book 03 Execution, followed by Book 07 MGSN and then Book 02 semantic reconciliation?
