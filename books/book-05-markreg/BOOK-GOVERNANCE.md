Book 05 governs the dedicated MarkReg publication. It defines the Product constitution, lifecycle journeys, decision surfaces, handoffs, review boundaries, and conformance requirements of the flagship international trademark Product.

## 3. Constitutional Responsibility Lock

```text
Core defines shared meaning.
Execution governs coordinated work.
Workplace provides authorized organization context.
Products compose focused user journeys.
MGSN connects independent organizational Orbits.
Owning Services change and record formal business facts.
Humans remain professionally accountable.
AI assists under explicit governance.
```

MarkReg is a Product within this model.

## 4. Product Boundary

MarkReg may:

- understand user need;
- assemble authorized context;
- prepare explainable recommendations;
- compare options;
- guide intake;
- prepare quotations and filing packages;
- expose readiness and missing information;
- request Human Review and approval;
- hand work into governed Execution;
- display sourced official outcomes;
- maintain lifecycle continuity;
- coordinate Product handoffs.

MarkReg may not, by Product authority alone:

- redefine Core Objects or formal statuses;
- create ungoverned Workflows;
- approve professional judgment;
- submit, file, pay, instruct, appoint, or publish;
- mutate official or formal business facts;
- treat AI output as final professional advice;
- treat a provider recommendation as appointment;
- convert a Product state into official truth.

## 5. Full-Lifecycle Boundary

Full lifecycle means continuity from need recognition through filing, examination, registration, maintenance, recordals, portfolio learning, and relevant adversarial journeys.

It does not mean one universal Workflow, one Product-owned state machine, or automatic implementation of every jurisdiction and service.

## 6. Recommendation Boundary

Country, route, class, goods/services, search, risk, provider, deadline, and response outputs remain explainable recommendations or candidates until the applicable Human Review, approval, formalization, or external outcome occurs.

```text
Recommendation ≠ Decision
Candidate ≠ Canonical
Readiness ≠ Approval
Approval ≠ Execution
Submission ≠ Official Receipt
Official Receipt ≠ Registration
```

## 7. Human and AI Boundary

AI may ask minimal questions, assemble context, rank alternatives, explain assumptions, draft, compare, and prepare next steps.

AI may not become the applicant, representative, reviewer, approver, signatory, payer, provider selector, filing authority, or official source.

Material professional outputs require accountable human review.

## 8. Network Boundary

MarkReg may request provider discovery and comparison through MGSN or governed Routing. Private relationships are considered first. Human Selection, appointment preparation, required approval, governed instruction, provider acceptance, and external delivery remain distinct.

## 9. Jurisdiction-Pack Boundary

Jurisdiction packs may provide versioned Product configuration, source references, questions, document requirements, fee logic, validation rules, and Workflow entry points.

A jurisdiction pack is not law, legal advice, an official office, or formal authority by itself.

## 10. Chapter Map Control

B05-TOC-V0.1 is a candidate map until owner acceptance.

After acceptance, chapter additions, removals, renumbering, or structural movement require explicit Book 05 change control.

## 11. Upstream Findings

Book 05 findings must be classified as:

- Product-specific decision;
- upstream editorial correction;
- upstream Change Proposal.

Book 05 must not directly rewrite Books 01–04. Only a blocking contradiction may pause drafting.

## 12. Non-Authorization

The manuscript does not authorize unrestricted implementation, production deployment, legal reliance, external communication, filing, payment, provider instruction, official recordal, or autonomous professional action.
