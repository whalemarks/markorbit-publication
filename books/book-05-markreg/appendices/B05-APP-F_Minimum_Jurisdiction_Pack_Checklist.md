# Appendix F — Minimum Jurisdiction Pack Checklist

**Status:** Controlled Scaffold — content completion pending PF-05  
**Primary sources:** B05-SPEC-0004, CH09–CH21, CH30–CH45  
**Reader purpose:** help Product, Knowledge and professional teams determine whether a jurisdiction/service combination is genuinely supported.

## F.1 Support Rule

A country name, fee table or provider listing does not constitute a supported jurisdiction.

```text
Supported jurisdiction/service
= scoped Pack
+ controlled sources and rules
+ professional ownership
+ tested Product behavior
+ maintenance and change process
```

## F.2 Pack Identity

A Pack should declare:

- Pack ID and version;
- jurisdiction and official authority;
- service family;
- lifecycle stages covered;
- applicant types;
- filing or action routes;
- language scope;
- effective period;
- exclusions and known gaps;
- professional owner and reviewer;
- release and next-review dates.

## F.3 Source Checklist

- [ ] current legislation, regulation or treaty basis identified where material;
- [ ] official office rules and practice guidance identified;
- [ ] current official fee source captured;
- [ ] current forms and official filing requirements captured;
- [ ] official deadlines and window rules sourced;
- [ ] source language and translation limitations recorded;
- [ ] retrieval date, effective date and version identity recorded;
- [ ] superseded source retained;
- [ ] provider advice distinguished from official source;
- [ ] unresolved source conflict visible.

## F.10 Product Behavior Checklist

- [ ] minimum user questions defined;
- [ ] reusable facts and source conditions defined;
- [ ] recommendation and alternative behavior defined;
- [ ] warnings, blockers and overrides defined;
- [ ] user-facing explanations defined;
- [ ] professional Review surface defined;
- [ ] approval and authority requirements defined;
- [ ] provider and Execution route defined;
- [ ] failure, retry and reconciliation behavior defined;
- [ ] official evidence and status mapping defined;
- [ ] future obligations and lifecycle continuation defined;
- [ ] priority conformance scenarios pass.

## F.11 Rule and AI Governance Checklist

- [ ] Source Records exist for material rules;
- [ ] Rule Records include source, scope, effective date and status;
- [ ] Pack release and approval are recorded;
- [ ] change-detection routes are defined;
- [ ] fee and form changes trigger impact assessment;
- [ ] affected open artifacts can be identified;
- [ ] organization overlays remain separate;
- [ ] provider-specific advice is dated and scoped;
- [ ] AI tasks receive the correct Pack and source context;
- [ ] material AI outputs retain source and Human Review status;
- [ ] unknown or disputed rules block protected action where required.

## F.12 Pack Readiness Decision

A Pack may be classified as:

| State | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| research only | sources collected but Product behavior is not approved |
| guided information | explanations permitted; no protected action may rely on it |
| preparation capable | controlled recommendations and packages may be prepared for Review |
| execution capable | approved route, evidence, failure and acknowledgement behavior exist |
| lifecycle capable | post-filing, maintenance and later-stage continuity are supported |
| suspended | material rule or operational uncertainty prevents current use |

The declared state must be service- and stage-specific.
