# B05-SPEC-0002 — MarkReg Reference Journeys

## Status

- **Status:** Controlled Draft
- **Applies to:** CH08–CH22 and later lifecycle chapters
- **Purpose:** Provide canonical end-to-end examples for Product conformance and editorial continuity

## 1. Reference Journey A — Multi-Jurisdiction New Filing

### Case identity

**Applicant:** Northstar Kitchenware (Shenzhen) Co., Ltd.  
**Brand:** `EMBERLOOP` word mark plus a flame-loop device  
**Business:** smart outdoor cooking equipment and a companion mobile application  
**Current markets:** China  
**Planned markets:** United States, European Union, United Kingdom, Japan, and Australia  
**Timing:** crowdfunding launch in four months  
**Budget:** limited initial budget, but willing to prioritize core markets  
**Risk posture:** prefers search before filing in core markets

### CH08 — Need understanding

The Product asks only for product categories, launch markets, launch date, current use, applicant structure, mark forms, and budget priority.

It reuses the organization profile and does not ask again for known company registration data.

**Need Brief v1:** protect the word and core device before launch, prioritize the United States and European Union, avoid unnecessary filings, and preserve an expansion route.

### CH09–CH10 — Jurisdiction and route options

MarkReg presents:

- Option A: US + EU first, UK later;
- Option B: US + EU + UK first;
- Option C: Madrid route after confirming a suitable basic filing;
- deferred markets: Japan and Australia until launch traction is clearer.

Assumptions, local-representation requirements, timing uncertainty, route dependencies, and estimated later costs remain visible.

### CH11 — Filing units

The Product identifies:

- word mark filing unit;
- composite/device filing unit;
- possible simplified device variant;
- no automatic assumption that one filing protects every visual variation.

The professional recommends the word mark as mandatory and the device as strongly recommended in core markets.

### CH12 — Applicant and authority

The user first selects the operating subsidiary, but portfolio context shows that the parent company owns other marks.

MarkReg raises applicant ambiguity and asks a targeted ownership question. The professional records the selected applicant and the reason. This change invalidates the first document requirement preview.

### CH13–CH14 — Classes and goods/services

Candidate classes include cooking equipment, downloadable software, retail services, and possibly connected-device services.

MarkReg shows:

- primary class candidates;
- related classes;
- items supported by current business evidence;
- optional future items;
- local wording and item-count constraints;
- fee impact of adding classes.

The user chooses a narrower launch scope and preserves a future expansion note.

### CH15 — Search and risk

The Product recommends knockout searches in all core markets and expanded review in the United States and European Union.

Search results do not become a guarantee. The professional records a moderate risk around a similar mark for grills and recommends narrowing one goods description.

### CH16–CH17 — Proposal, options, and pricing

The proposal compares:

- word-only core filing;
- word + device core filing;
- broader five-market launch package.

The Quote separates official fees, professional fees, provider pass-through costs, tax treatment, currency, validity period, expected later-stage fees, and exclusions.

### CH18 — Acceptance and instruction

The client accepts the word + device option for US, EU, and UK. The acceptance references Quote v3 exactly. Payment is required as a deposit before filing preparation begins.

### CH19–CH20 — Formal intake and documents

Formal Intake requests only service-specific facts not already confirmed. The Requirement Set identifies:

- applicant evidence;
- mark files;
- priority information;
- signature authority;
- POA requirements by jurisdiction;
- whether scans or originals are acceptable;
- translation or certification conditions.

A UK document is complete, while one provider-specific US declaration remains pending.

### CH21 — Readiness

The Product shows separate readiness dimensions:

| Dimension | Result |
| --- | --- |
| Commercial | Ready after deposit receipt |
| Intake | Ready |
| Documents | Conditional |
| Professional | Ready after goods revision |
| Filing approval | Not yet granted |
| Execution | Not ready |

The client can see why the journey is not filing-ready and what action is required next.

### CH22 — Handoff

After payment, document completion, professional review, and client confirmation, MarkReg creates a Handoff Envelope referencing:

- accepted Quote v3;
- Commercial Instruction v1;
- Formal Intake v4;
- Requirement Set v2;
- Readiness Assessment v5;
- unresolved non-blocking warning;
- responsible professional;
- expected formal Order, Matter, and Execution references.

The Product does not itself create official truth or claim that filing has occurred.

## 2. Reference Journey B — Multi-Country Renewal with Ownership Change

### Case identity

**Portfolio:** six registrations for `RIVERKITE`  
**Current owner in official records:** Riverkite Trading Limited  
**Current business owner:** Riverkite Brands Pte. Ltd. after group restructuring  
**Upcoming deadlines:** three renewals within 120 days  
**Additional issue:** two jurisdictions require local recordal before or with renewal

### Journey contrast

This journey begins from official and portfolio records rather than a new-filing Need Brief.

MarkReg must:

1. verify the official registration identifiers and source dates;
2. distinguish portfolio projection from current official owner data;
3. identify chain-of-title uncertainty;
4. determine whether recordal precedes renewal, can be combined, or must follow;
5. separate renewal fees from assignment/recordal fees;
6. identify evidence, signatures, notarization, legalization, and originals;
7. present deadline confidence and source authority;
8. prevent a single “renew all” action when ownership prerequisites differ;
9. create separate readiness and approval decisions per jurisdiction;
10. continue each right into its next maintenance cycle after verified acknowledgement.

### Product outcome

The client receives one portfolio-level explanation but distinct jurisdiction actions. A late ownership correction in one jurisdiction does not silently alter the already approved renewal package for another jurisdiction.

## 3. Editorial Use Rule

CH08–CH22 should reference Journey A where a concrete example is needed. Parts V–VI should extend Journey A or use Journey B instead of inventing unrelated examples for every chapter.

Every future example must either:

- advance one of these reference journeys; or
- state why a new case is necessary to test a materially different Product behavior.
