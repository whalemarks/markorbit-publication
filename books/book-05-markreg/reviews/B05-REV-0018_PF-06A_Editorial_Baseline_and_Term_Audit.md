## 4. Editorial Standard Review

### 4.1 Voice and normative language

B05-PUB-0001 v0.2 establishes a direct, precise and professionally neutral voice.

It distinguishes:

- `must` for constitutional, safety and conformance requirements;
- `must not` for prohibited behavior;
- `should` for recommended behavior;
- `may` for permission;
- `can` for factual capability.

**Result:** PASS.

### 4.2 Controlled capitalization

The standard now distinguishes defined concepts from ordinary-language uses, including Product, Workplace, Core, Execution, Review, Decision, Communication, Document, Evidence, Knowledge, Capability, Order, Matter, Task, Jurisdiction Pack and Human actions.

The standard avoids the opposite failure of capitalizing ordinary words merely for emphasis.

**Result:** PASS.

### 4.3 Stable artifact names

The accepted artifact inventory covers:

- recommendation and commercial records;
- Intake and filing records;
- post-filing and portfolio records;
- Product and governance records.

The suffix rule for Record, Snapshot, View, Context, Set, Baseline, Envelope, Candidate and Package is explicit.

**Result:** PASS.

### 4.4 Authority and state language

The standard requires the editor to identify:

- actor;
- object;
- authority;
- exact version;
- state domain;
- evidence source;
- external effect.

It prohibits ambiguous uses of `filed`, `registered`, `renewed`, `transferred`, `accepted` and `completed` where the source or state is unclear.

**Result:** PASS.

### 4.5 AI language

AI remains limited to extraction, comparison, drafting, proposal, classification and uncertainty detection. Review, Decision, Approval, instruction and professional responsibility remain Human-accountable.

**Result:** PASS.

### 4.6 Commercial language

The standard reflects B05-SPEC-0004 v0.2 and keeps separate:

- official fee;
- mandatory third-party cost;
- professional fee;
- provider pass-through;
- internal provider cost;
- tax;
- currency adjustment;
- discount;
- credit;
- margin.

**Result:** PASS.

### 4.7 Jurisdiction-support language

Support claims must identify jurisdiction, service family, lifecycle stage, Pack Version and support state. The accepted support-state vocabulary is fixed.

**Result:** PASS.

### 4.8 Compression rules

B05-PUB-0001 v0.2 preserves the first full constitutional explanation and permits later cross-reference where the same boundary has already been established.

It specifically prohibits compression of:

- source hierarchy;
- exact-version Approval;
- official versus provider Evidence;
- unknown-state and duplicate-safety controls;
- participant authority;
- case final-state locks;
- Pack support scope;
- zero-tolerance requirements.

**Result:** PASS.

### 4.9 Chapter transitions

The editorial standard requires every chapter to identify:

1. the controlled output now available;
2. the material question still unresolved;
3. the reason for the next chapter.

**Result:** PASS.

### 4.10 Prohibited semantic drift

The standard expressly prohibits silent changes to:

- Core objects;
- Human authority;
- Product, formal, Execution and official-state boundaries;
- provider/official evidence distinctions;
- MGSN model;
- Jurisdiction Pack support claims;
- controlled IDs;
- scenario behavior;
- reference-journey final states;
- implementation or protected-action authority.

**Result:** PASS.
