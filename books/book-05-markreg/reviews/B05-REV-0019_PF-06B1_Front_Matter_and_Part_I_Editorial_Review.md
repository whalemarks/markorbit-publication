## 3. Editorial Method

PF-06B1 did not apply isolated synonym replacement.

The chapters were restructured around distinct editorial responsibilities:

```text
CH00 — why the book exists and how the Product is bounded
CH01 — chapter map and reader routes
CH02 — why a dedicated Product is necessary
CH03 — mission, value and success measures
CH04 — architecture responsibility allocation
CH05 — participant, role, authority and accountability
CH06 — durable Product principles and non-goals
CH07 — lifecycle and state-plane model
```

Repeated constitutional statements were retained once in the chapter where they are most authoritative, then referenced through transitions rather than restated in every chapter.

## 6. Chapter-Level Findings

### CH00 — Preface

Accepted changes:

- reduced repeated summaries of the seven Parts;
- clarified the central Product question;
- integrated AI, state and authority boundaries into one opening framework;
- stated the reader outcomes more directly;
- added a clear Handoff to CH01.

The Preface does not redefine any controlled record or architecture boundary.

### CH01 — Table of Contents

Accepted changes:

- preserved all CH00–CH47 titles and the Back Matter sequence;
- added Product/architecture, professional, commercial/operations and implementation/review reading routes;
- clarified that role-specific reading routes do not override Part I dependencies;
- preserved Appendix A–G outside chapter numbering.

B05-TOC-V0.1 remains unchanged and owner accepted.

### CH02 — Why MarkReg Exists

Accepted changes:

- replaced repeated tool-by-tool criticism with a concise partial-solution matrix;
- retained the form, questionnaire, case system, AI, provider and connector distinctions;
- consolidated the Decision-network and Jurisdiction Pack argument;
- made lifecycle continuity and sourced status part of the core reason for existence;
- added a direct Handoff to the mission chapter.

### CH03 — Product Mission and Value Proposition

Accepted changes:

- retained the mission and Product promise;
- consolidated participant value into client, professional, organization, provider and ecosystem sections;
- preserved the five Product value tests;
- linked metrics to quality guardrails;
- recognized honest stops as valid Product outcomes;
- removed repeated mission-boundary wording already controlled by CH04 and CH06.

### CH04 — Architecture Position

Accepted changes:

- replaced many repeated boundary sections with one responsibility chain and matrix;
- distinguished live reference, snapshot, Product Projection, candidate and cache;
- clarified Product-local state and formal-state boundaries;
- consolidated Execution, MGSN, Owning Service, provider, connector, office and AI responsibilities;
- established one Handoff contract.

Books 02–04 remain authoritative for their respective domains.

### CH05 — Participants and Responsibility

Accepted changes:

- distinguished user, actor and participant;
- separated client, instructor, applicant, owner, signatory, payer and acting organization;
- separated role, Permission, responsibility, Review, Decision and approval;
- added bounded delegation and role-switch requirements;
- preserved provider stages and Communication authority;
- incorporated the PF-04 participant and visibility contract without duplicating Appendix C.

### CH06 — Principles and Non-Goals

Accepted changes:

- converted the constitutional lock into eighteen operational principles;
- preserved Need Before Form, Ask Less, Candidate Before Canonical, Recommendation Before Decision and Product Loop First;
- added explicit support-claim and unknown-state principles from PF-04/PF-05;
- consolidated non-goals;
- added a ten-question Product-expansion test.

No new constitutional rule was introduced beyond the accepted Book 05 specifications and prior chapters.

### CH07 — Lifecycle and State Distinctions

Accepted changes:

- established a canonical lifecycle table;
- separated Product, recommendation, commercial, formal, Review/approval, Execution, provider, official and continuing-right state planes;
- preserved critical inequality rules;
- clarified material change, supersession, Handoff, Return and reconciliation;
- made unknown state first class;
- preserved independent jurisdiction and Portfolio states;
- aligned client-facing state language with Appendix B.
