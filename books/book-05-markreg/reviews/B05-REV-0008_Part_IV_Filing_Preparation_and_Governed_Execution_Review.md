## 4. Chapter Results

### CH23 — Filing Package as a Governed Artifact

**Result:** PASS

The chapter defines the Filing Package Candidate as a versioned, source-linked, purpose-bound Product artifact. It distinguishes source files, formal Documents, rendered forms, payloads and the governed package. Material-change and structured-diff rules are explicit.

### CH24 — Professional Review and Filing Approval

**Result:** PASS

The chapter separates factual confirmation, Professional Review, commercial or internal approval and Filing Approval. Approval remains package-version, route and authority specific. Conditional approval and invalidation rules are observable.

### CH25 — Provider Discovery, Private Partners and Routing

**Result:** PASS

The chapter begins with Capability Need, preserves private organization relationships, and separates eligibility, conflict, availability, commercial evidence, recommendation and Human Selection. It does not create an open marketplace or universal provider ranking.

### CH26 — Provider Appointment, Instruction and Acceptance

**Result:** PASS

Selection, appointment, governed instruction, provider receipt and provider acceptance remain distinct. Data disclosure is purpose-limited. Provider-proposed changes return to the appropriate Product and professional gates.

### CH27 — Direct Connector, Provider Filing and Owning Service Boundaries

**Result:** PASS

The chapter preserves:

```text
MarkReg Product preparation
≠ Book 03 Execution
≠ connector authority
≠ provider professional action
≠ Owning Service formal records
≠ official-office authority
```

Manual filing remains governed. Route substitution does not bypass renewed authority and duplicate checks.

### CH28 — Submission States, Acknowledgement and Official Evidence

**Result:** PASS

The state model prevents `sent`, delivery, provider report and technical success from being represented as official acknowledgement. Evidence hierarchy, official identifiers, filing dates, fees and unknown state are sourced and versioned.

### CH29 — Failure, Retry, Duplicate Safety, Communication and Audit

**Result:** PASS

The chapter defines partial failure, stable idempotency identity, retry classes, reconciliation, remediation, route substitution, honest Communication and append-oriented audit continuity. Unknown external effect blocks optimistic retry.
