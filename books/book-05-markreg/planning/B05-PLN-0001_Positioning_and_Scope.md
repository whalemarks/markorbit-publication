
## 2. Positioning

MarkReg is the flagship international trademark Product within MarkOrbit.

It composes a focused user journey across:

- business need;
- jurisdiction and filing-route strategy;
- mark and applicant context;
- classification and goods/services guidance;
- search and risk support where applicable;
- proposal, quote, intake, and commercial confirmation;
- filing-package preparation;
- Human Review and approval;
- provider collaboration or direct connector handoff;
- official acknowledgement and examination;
- office actions and adversarial matters;
- registration, maintenance, renewal, changes, and assignment;
- portfolio and organizational learning.

## 3. Core Product Problem

International trademark work is difficult because users must make interconnected professional, commercial, procedural, documentary, and jurisdiction-specific decisions before they understand the consequences.

Traditional products usually expose one of three incomplete experiences:

```text
Form Portal
→ asks users to know the answers before the Product helps

Case System
→ starts after the need and commercial decision are already formed

AI Chat
→ explains or drafts without owning a governed Product journey
```

MarkReg should instead reduce professional complexity while preserving professional responsibility.

## 4. Primary Product Loop

```text
Need
→ Understand
→ Recommend
→ Compare
→ Decide
→ Intake
→ Validate
→ Quote and Confirm
→ Formalize
→ Prepare
→ Review
→ Execute
→ Acknowledge
→ Examine
→ Respond
→ Register or Other Outcome
→ Maintain
→ Learn
```

This is a Product loop, not one universal Workflow.

## 5. Primary Users

MarkReg may serve:

- trademark professionals;
- law firms and agencies;
- internal brand and legal teams;
- service coordinators;
- authorized clients and applicants;
- external providers participating under purpose-limited access.

Different users may see different surfaces. The Product constitution remains the same.

## 6. User Experience Principle

MarkReg should ask fewer questions and perform more governed interpretation.

The preferred interaction is:

```text
Small amount of user input
→ context assembly
→ explainable recommendation
→ clear alternatives
→ visible assumptions and missing information
→ user choice
→ progressive next step
```

This does not authorize silent decisions. The Product must distinguish:

- required information;
- inferred context;
- recommendation;
- user selection;
- professional review;
- approval;
- formal outcome.

## 7. Scope

### In Scope

- international trademark application and lifecycle journeys;
- direct national, regional, and international filing routes;
- word, device, and other supported mark representations;
- applicant, priority, ownership, and authority context;
- jurisdiction, class, goods/services, and search guidance;
- proposals, quotations, intake, and filing preparation;
- examination, office actions, publication, opposition, and relevant further proceedings;
- registration, renewal, maintenance, changes, and assignment;
- provider collaboration and Product handoffs;
- AI-assisted understanding, recommendation, preparation, and explanation;
- Product metrics and lifecycle learning.

### Out of Scope

- becoming the organization Workplace;
- redefining Core or Execution;
- all intellectual property domains;
- autonomous legal advice or filing;
- open bidding marketplace behavior;
- universal jurisdiction truth without source and version;
- production APIs, schemas, repository topology, or deployment design;
- guaranteed clearance, registration, deadline, or outcome.

## 8. Product Value Tests

MarkReg should measurably:

- reduce missed or incorrect work;
- improve professional judgment;
- accelerate service conversion;
- improve delivery quality;
- accumulate organizational capability.

## 9. Success Condition for the Publication

Book 05 succeeds when a reader can explain:

- what MarkReg is and is not;
- how its Product loop creates value;
- where each decision and state belongs;
- how AI and humans cooperate;
- how work enters governed Execution;
- how formal outcomes return;
- how lifecycle continuity survives Products, providers, and jurisdiction differences;
- how implementation can proceed without authority drift.
