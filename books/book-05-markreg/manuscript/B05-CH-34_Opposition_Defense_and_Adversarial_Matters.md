# B05-CH-34 — Opposition, Defense and Adversarial Matters

**Status:** Complete Draft 1  
**Chapter Map:** B05-TOC-V0.1 — Owner Accepted  
**Part:** Part V — Examination, Publication and Disputes

## Chapter Purpose

A verified opposition or comparable challenge creates an adversarial Product journey with separate parties, scope, deadlines, Evidence, costs and authority.

CH34 answers:

> What is challenged, which options and Evidence are available, who may decide or negotiate, and what procedural effect has actually occurred?

```text
verified challenge
→ MR-C05 Adversarial Context
→ positions and Evidence needs
→ MR-D07 Adversarial or Settlement Decision
→ MR-A24 Adversarial Package Candidate
→ governed filing or Communication
→ sourced procedural outcome
```

MarkReg organizes the journey. It does not decide the dispute or become a general litigation system.

## 1. User Question and Primary Action

**User question:** What is challenged, what are our options and what authority is required now?

**Primary action:** Approve the bounded defense, limitation, withdrawal, negotiation or other procedural path.

The surface must show challenged and unaffected scope separately.

## 2. Adversarial Context

`MR-C05 Adversarial Context` references:

- official proceeding and source;
- claimant, respondent and representatives;
- challenged application, registration and scope;
- grounds, requested relief and procedural stage;
- deadlines and hearing dates;
- conflict, confidentiality and privilege controls;
- positions, Evidence and pleadings;
- provider, commercial and responsibility records;
- settlement authority and Communications;
- Decisions, filings and official outcomes.

```text
Adversarial Context
≠ formal Matter
≠ official case file
≠ privileged legal conclusion
≠ procedural outcome
```

## 3. Party, Representation and Conflict Gate

The Product distinguishes right holder, challenger, client, payer, instructor, settlement authority, professional, local provider, witness and tribunal.

Before sensitive disclosure or instruction, verify:

- official parties and affiliates;
- current representation and provider conflict;
- engagement scope and confidentiality;
- authority to instruct, sign and settle;
- deadline-protection needs;
- local representation requirements.

For `MR-SCN-19` and `MR-SCN-22`, conflict or excess access stops the affected provider path without disclosing unrelated information.

## 4. Positions, Options and Strategy

The professional analysis should map each sourced ground to affected scope, facts, Evidence needs and requested relief.

Available paths may include:

- contest all or selected grounds;
- limit or amend selected goods/services;
- challenge standing, entitlement or procedure;
- submit argument and Evidence;
- negotiate consent, coexistence or limitation;
- request suspension or cooling-off where available;
- withdraw selected scope or the proceeding;
- proceed to hearing or decision;
- reserve review or appeal;
- take no action with recorded consequence.

`MR-D07 Adversarial or Settlement Decision` records exact authority, scope, limits, expiry and actor.

## 5. Evidence and Package Governance

`MR-A23 Evidence Plan` maps each item to a proposition, ground, source, custodian, date, territory, confidentiality, translation, exhibit version and professional inclusion Decision.

Each pleading or procedural action becomes an exact-version `MR-A24 Adversarial Package Candidate` under CH32 and Part IV controls.

A witness statement may be organized with AI assistance, but testimony, personal knowledge and signatures remain Human and source controlled.

## 6. Settlement and Coexistence

The Product distinguishes:

```text
exploratory contact
approved negotiation boundary
proposal and counterproposal
agreed commercial terms
signed agreement
procedural withdrawal or limitation filing
official closure
```

A signed agreement is not official closure. Discussions do not suspend a deadline without verified procedural effect.

No provider may accept a term outside the client-approved range.

## 7. Cost, Communication and State

Adversarial cost stages should separate initial assessment, pleading, Evidence, negotiation, hearing, provider work, translations, official fees and appeal contingency.

Communications preserve purpose, recipients, privilege, source, approvals and delivery evidence.

Useful Product states include `proceeding verified`, `defense instructed`, `pleading filed`, `Evidence stage`, `negotiation active`, `decision pending`, `settlement signed` and `official closure pending`.

These states do not replace the formal Matter or official proceeding.

## 8. `EMBERLOOP` — `EL-27`

The verified EU opposition challenges selected cooking-equipment goods.

The client authorizes a bounded strategy:

1. file the required defense to preserve rights;
2. contest the opposition for core smart-cooking equipment;
3. explore a narrow limitation for selected peripheral goods;
4. prohibit broader territory or channel restrictions without new authority;
5. cap the initial defense stage and require a further Decision before Evidence-heavy work.

MarkReg records Adversarial Context EU and the exact `MR-D07` negotiation authority.

```text
Opposition proceeding active
Defense instructed
Negotiation active within approved boundary
Official settlement or closure not established
```

No later settlement, withdrawal or decision is invented.

## 9. Claimant-Side Boundary

The same Product may support a right holder considering an opposition, but only after standing, earlier-right evidence, proportionality, deadline, professional recommendation and filing authority are established.

A similarity signal does not authorize a challenge.

## 10. Controlled Scenarios

- `MR-SCN-19` — provider conflict or unavailability;
- `MR-SCN-20` — provider proposes a material change;
- `MR-SCN-22` — provider over-access;
- `MR-SCN-26` — corrected proceeding event;
- `MR-SCN-28` — client silence is not settlement or abandonment authority;
- `MR-SCN-33` — one challenged right does not change unrelated rights.

## 11. AI Assistance

AI may extract parties, grounds, deadlines and relief; organize chronology, issue and Evidence matrices; compare settlement terms to approved limits; and draft decision briefs.

AI may not decide the case, invent testimony, waive privilege, choose strategy, accept settlement, surrender scope, sign pleadings, communicate outside approved authority or claim official closure without evidence.

## 13. Handoff to CH35

CH34 leaves the EU opposition active with defense and bounded negotiation underway.
