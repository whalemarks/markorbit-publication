# B05-CH-34 — Opposition, Defense and Adversarial Matters

**Status:** Part V Draft  
**Chapter Map:** B05-TOC-V0.1 — Owner Accepted  
**Part:** Part V — Examination, Publication and Disputes

## Chapter Purpose

A verified opposition or comparable challenge changes the Product journey from examination management to an adversarial proceeding.

CH34 answers:

> How does MarkReg organize parties, grounds, pleadings, evidence, settlement, deadlines and professional responsibility without becoming a general litigation system or deciding the dispute itself?

```text
Verified adversarial event
→ Adversarial Context
→ conflict and representation checks
→ Position and Issue Set
→ defense, settlement or procedural options
→ client and professional decisions
→ governed pleadings, evidence and Communications
→ verified procedural outcome
```

The Product must support both claimant and respondent journeys while preserving the independence of each organization and professional.

---

## 1. User Question and Primary Action

**User question:** What is being challenged, what are our options, what decisions and evidence are needed, and who is responsible?

**Primary action:** Review the adversarial strategy and authorize the selected defense, settlement, limitation, withdrawal or other procedural path.

A formal opposition should not appear as one generic red warning. The Product should show affected scope, grounds, deadlines, decisions and next actions.

---

## 2. Adversarial Context

An Adversarial Context is a Product-local working context referencing the formal Matter and official proceeding.

It should contain:

- proceeding identifier and source;
- tribunal or office;
- claimant, respondent and other parties as officially recorded;
- representatives and appointment status;
- challenged application, registration, goods, services or rights;
- grounds and source pleadings;
- procedural stage;
- deadlines and hearing dates;
- positions and requested relief;
- conflict and confidentiality controls;
- evidence registers;
- settlement and consent discussions;
- commercial scope and provider costs;
- decisions, approvals, filings and official outcomes.

```text
Adversarial Context
≠ formal Matter
≠ official case file
≠ privileged legal conclusion
≠ general litigation platform
```

---

## 3. Party and Role Model

The Product should distinguish:

```text
Applicant or right holder
Opposer or challenger
Respondent
Intervener or interested party
Client
Payer
Instructing party
Responsible professional
Local counsel or provider
Witness or declarant
Tribunal or office
Settlement authority
```

One person or organization may hold several roles, but the roles and authorities should remain explicit.

A payer or business contact is not automatically authorized to settle or surrender rights.

---

## 4. Conflict and Representation Gate

Before disclosing sensitive information or accepting defense work, the Product should verify:

- party identities and affiliates;
- challenged right and proceeding;
- existing representation;
- provider conflict result;
- engagement scope;
- confidentiality and privilege requirements;
- authority to accept instructions;
- local representation conditions;
- deadline-protection needs;
- whether preliminary review is permitted before full engagement.

A recommended provider is not appointed until CH25–CH26 controls are satisfied.

---

## 5. Grounds and Position Set

The Product should create a versioned Position and Issue Set containing:

- ground identifier;
- claimant allegation or official characterization;
- source pleading section;
- affected goods, services or right;
- factual assertions;
- cited rights and official identifiers;
- requested relief;
- respondent position;
- undisputed and disputed facts;
- evidence needed;
- procedural options;
- professional interpretation;
- residual uncertainty.

Ground labels should be jurisdiction-specific and source-backed. MarkReg should not force every proceeding into a universal legal taxonomy.

---

## 6. Response Options

Available paths may include:

```text
Contest all grounds
Contest selected grounds
Limit or amend goods/services
Seek proof or procedural clarification
Challenge standing, entitlement or admissibility
Submit evidence and argument
Negotiate consent, coexistence or limitation
Request suspension or cooling-off where available
Withdraw selected scope
Withdraw the application or proceeding
Accept an adverse outcome
Proceed to hearing or decision
Reserve appeal or review
```

The Product should explain prerequisites, costs, deadlines and scope consequences.

It must not present settlement as automatically better than defense or defense as automatically better than limitation.

---

## 7. Strategy Artifact

The Adversarial Strategy should identify:

- objectives and protected business interests;
- grounds contested or accepted;
- proposed amendments or limitations;
- argument plan;
- evidence plan;
- procedural motions or requests;
- settlement authority and boundaries;
- cost and provider assumptions;
- decision and filing schedule;
- escalation and appeal path;
- communications plan;
- responsible actors;
- version and supersession.

```text
Adversarial Strategy
≠ pleading
≠ settlement authority
≠ filed response
≠ tribunal outcome
```

## 8. Pleadings and Procedural Packages

Each pleading or procedural action should be treated as a versioned package under CH32 and Part IV controls.

Examples include:

- notice of defense or answer;
- statement of grounds;
- counterstatement;
- evidence submission;
- request for proof;
- amendment or limitation;
- extension or suspension request;
- hearing request;
- written submissions;
- settlement filing;
- withdrawal;
- appeal notice.

Every package should reference the proceeding, source event, strategy, affected scope, review and Filing Approval.

---

## 9. Evidence in Adversarial Matters

Evidence governance should preserve:

- proposition and ground supported;
- witness or custodian;
- source and chain of custody;
- dates and territory;
- confidentiality and privilege;
- translation and certification;
- exhibit version;
- disclosure status;
- objections or limitations;
- professional inclusion decision;
- tribunal acceptance or rejection where known.

The Product should support evidence batches without turning every file into an undifferentiated upload list.

---

## 10. Witnesses and Declarations

Where declarations or witness evidence are used, the Product should identify:

- witness identity and role;
- facts within personal knowledge;
- source materials reviewed;
- draft and final statement versions;
- translator or interpreter;
- signature and certification requirements;
- conflicts or inconsistencies;
- confidentiality;
- review and filing status;
- hearing or cross-examination availability where relevant.

AI may help organize a statement. It must not invent testimony or place words in a witness’s mouth.

---

## 11. Settlement and Coexistence

Settlement work should distinguish:

- exploratory contact;
- without-prejudice or protected negotiation where applicable;
- settlement proposal;
- counterproposal;
- approved negotiation boundary;
- agreed commercial terms;
- final written agreement;
- procedural withdrawal or limitation;
- official acknowledgement;
- future monitoring and breach handling.

A settlement agreement and an official procedural outcome are separate artifacts.

The Product should not display a dispute as closed until required signatures and official procedural effects are verified.

---

## 12. Settlement Authority

The Decision Record should show who may authorize:

- opening negotiations;
- offering or accepting limitations;
- agreeing market, channel or territory restrictions;
- accepting cost allocation;
- withdrawing the application or opposition;
- consenting to registration;
- signing the agreement;
- filing procedural withdrawals;
- communicating final settlement.

Material settlement authority should be specific, time-bounded and versioned.

---

## 13. Cost and Budget Control

Adversarial work may include:

- initial assessment;
- pleading preparation;
- evidence review and collection;
- discovery or disclosure work where applicable;
- hearing preparation;
- local counsel;
- translations and certifications;
- settlement negotiation;
- official fees;
- appeal contingency;
- cost awards or exposure.

MarkReg should separate estimates, caps, fixed stages and contingent stages under CH17 controls.

A budget ceiling should trigger review before new work exceeds the approved stage.

## 16. `EMBERLOOP` European Union Opposition

The verified opposition challenges selected cooking-equipment goods based on an earlier right reported in the official opposition record.

MarkReg opens Adversarial Context EU v1 and requests a distinct formal opposition Matter because the proceeding has separate parties, deadlines, costs and evidence.

### Initial actions

- verify the opponent and cited-right identifiers;
- complete conflict checks for the current EU provider;
- confirm representation and defense deadline;
- map challenged and unaffected goods;
- preserve the filed application and opposition documents;
- issue a client decision brief;
- obtain a staged defense Quote.

### Strategy options

The professional presents:

- Option A — defend the challenged goods and preserve the full application scope;
- Option B — defend core goods while offering a targeted limitation;
- Option C — explore coexistence while preserving the defense deadline;
- Option D — withdraw the challenged goods and preserve unaffected scope;
- Option E — take no action and accept the sourced procedural consequence.

The client selects a combined strategy:

1. file the required defense to preserve rights;
2. contest the opposition for core smart-cooking equipment;
3. authorize a narrow negotiation range for selected peripheral goods;
4. prohibit any territory-wide or channel restriction without new approval;
5. cap the first defense stage and require a new decision before evidence-heavy work.

MarkReg records Adversarial Strategy EU v2 and the exact negotiation authority.

The Product does not label the case settled merely because discussions begin.

## 17. Claimant-Side Journey

MarkReg may also support a right holder considering an opposition.

The claimant-side journey should preserve:

- monitored application and source;
- standing and earlier-right verification;
- affected goods and territories;
- deadline and extension options;
- business objective;
- proportionality and risk;
- warning or negotiation options;
- evidence and use requirements;
- professional recommendation;
- decision authority;
- filing package and official acknowledgement.

The Product should not encourage challenges solely because a similarity signal exists.

## 18. Conformance Scenarios

### MR-SCN-34A — Conflict discovered after initial review

**Given** a provider begins preliminary review and then identifies a conflict.  
**When** the conflict is confirmed.  
**Then** MarkReg stops further disclosure and instruction, preserves the conflict event, limits access and reopens private-first provider routing without transferring privileged analysis improperly.

### MR-SCN-34B — Settlement proposal exceeds authority

**Given** opposing counsel proposes a restriction outside the client-approved negotiation range.  
**When** the provider receives the proposal.  
**Then** MarkReg records it as a proposal, blocks acceptance, shows the variance and requests new client authority.  
**Authority boundary:** provider receipt is not client acceptance.

### MR-SCN-34C — Only selected goods are challenged

**Given** an opposition affects selected goods only.  
**When** the proceeding is summarized.  
**Then** MarkReg shows challenged and unaffected scope separately and avoids labelling the entire application invalid or refused.

### MR-SCN-34D — Signed settlement but no official closure

**Given** the parties sign a settlement requiring withdrawal of the opposition.  
**When** the agreement is uploaded.  
**Then** the Product records `settlement signed`, keeps the official proceeding open, prepares the withdrawal filing and waits for sourced procedural closure.

## 19. AI Assistance

AI may:

- extract parties, grounds, deadlines and requested relief;
- compare pleadings and positions;
- organize issue and evidence matrices;
- prepare chronology and factual summaries;
- identify unsupported assertions or inconsistencies;
- draft decision briefs and negotiation comparisons;
- track authority boundaries and budget stages;
- compare settlement terms to approved limits.

AI must not:

- decide legal strategy;
- invent evidence or testimony;
- contact an opposing party autonomously;
- waive rights or deadlines;
- accept settlement terms;
- expose privileged information;
- act as accountable counsel;
- represent a dispute outcome as certain.

---

## 20. Failure Modes to Reject

```text
Formal opposition treated as ordinary task
Payer assumed to have settlement authority
Provider conflict ignored after discovery
Challenged goods not separated from unaffected scope
Negotiation assumed to suspend deadline
Settlement proposal shown as agreement
Signed agreement shown as official closure
Evidence files lack proposition and provenance
AI drafts testimony as fact
General litigation features absorb unrelated disputes
```

## 21. Minimum Adversarial Matter Lock

```text
Adversarial work begins from
a verified proceeding and
an explicit formal Matter boundary.

Parties, grounds, scope,
deadlines, positions, evidence,
settlement authority and costs
remain versioned and reviewable.

Pleading, settlement agreement,
procedural filing and official outcome
remain distinct.

AI may organize and compare.
Accountable humans advise,
decide, negotiate, sign and file.
```

## 22. Handoff to Appeals, Reviews and Cancellation

An adversarial proceeding may end in settlement, withdrawal, acceptance, partial refusal, dismissal or another decision.

CH35 defines how MarkReg opens a controlled remedy or challenge journey after a sourced decision, and how appeal, review, cancellation and invalidation remain distinct from ordinary prosecution and general litigation.
