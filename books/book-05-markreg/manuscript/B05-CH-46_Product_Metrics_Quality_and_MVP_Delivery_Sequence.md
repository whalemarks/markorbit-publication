# B05-CH-46 — Product Metrics, Quality and MVP Delivery Sequence

**Status:** Complete Draft 1  
**Chapter Map:** B05-TOC-V0.1 — Owner Accepted  
**Part:** Part VII — Product Experience and Evolution

## Chapter Purpose

MarkReg must prove that it reduces complexity without hiding defects, authority gaps or external-state uncertainty.

```text
Product question
→ MR-G08 Metric Definition
→ MR-G09 Evaluation Record
→ MR-C11 Pilot Context
→ MR-D13 Pilot or Release Decision
```

`EL-39` tests a bounded Product increment. It does not convert a manuscript, prototype or pilot into production authority.

## 1. User Question and Primary Action

**User question:** Is this Product increment clearer, safer and more useful, and what constraint should be addressed next?

**Primary action:** Review evidence and make a bounded Pilot or Release Decision.

Metrics support a Decision. They do not decorate a dashboard or justify a predetermined launch.

## 2. Measurement Principles

MarkReg measurement must:

- separate Product quality from legal outcomes;
- preserve jurisdiction, service, profile and participant context;
- distinguish speed from unsafe shortcutting;
- measure rework, correction and blocked unsafe actions;
- include negative, abandoned and conflicting-source cases;
- retain Human override and disagreement;
- expose source and data-quality limits;
- connect every material metric to an owner and Decision.

One global success score is insufficient.

## 3. MR-G08 Metric Definition

A Metric Definition records:

- purpose and accountable owner;
- source events and population;
- calculation and segmentation;
- exclusions and data-quality limits;
- expected direction and guardrails;
- review cadence;
- Decision supported;
- version and supersession.

Useful metric layers include:

| Layer | Core question |
| --- | --- |
| user understanding | Does the user understand the recommended option and consequence? |
| journey progression | Can the user reach the next valid state without hidden blockers? |
| professional quality | Are artifacts sourced, reviewable and appropriate? |
| governance safety | Are Decision, approval, Execution and official truth separated? |
| operational reliability | Are Handoffs, submissions, acknowledgements and recovery safe? |
| commercial clarity | Are scope, fee layers and later-stage costs understood? |
| lifecycle continuity | Do rights, deadlines and obligations remain connected? |
| learning quality | Does reviewed feedback improve Product behavior without contaminating truth? |

## 4. Quality and Safety Indicators

Quality is reviewed by correctness, completeness, traceability, clarity, scope integrity, authority integrity, freshness, consistency, recoverability and privacy.

Zero-tolerance indicators include:

- external action without active exact-version approval;
- approval applied to a superseded artifact;
- duplicate legal or financial effect;
- unsafe retry while external state is unknown;
- unsourced official status or deadline;
- confidential Evidence disclosed outside purpose;
- administrator, coordinator, client or AI acting as an eligible professional;
- provider access beyond the accepted engagement;
- stale Rule used for protected action.

A blocked unsafe action is a safety success, not a throughput defect.

## 5. MR-G09 Evaluation Record

An Evaluation Record identifies:

- Product, model, Pack and scenario versions;
- evaluation set and expected behavior;
- actual result and evidence;
- reviewer and date;
- failure class and severity;
- limitation and follow-up;
- release disposition.

Evaluation sets should include standard, edge, stale-source, conflicting-source, missing-information, fee-change, deadline-correction, provider-disagreement, multi-jurisdiction, abandonment and recovery cases.

Task-specific AI evaluation includes source attribution, unsupported-claim rate, scope accuracy, confidentiality, escalation appropriateness, Rule-version adherence and professional disposition.

A general language benchmark does not establish legal Product fitness.

## 6. Product-Loop Validation

A valid loop preserves:

```text
need
→ Product interaction
→ controlled artifact
→ accountable Human Decision
→ governed Handoff or action
→ sourced result
→ reviewed learning
→ Product, Pack, policy or training change
```

Telemetry without artifact versions, Decision context and sourced outcomes does not complete the loop.

Learning is classified before reuse as Product UX, missing question, Pack update, organization policy, provider evidence, professional training, client Communication, operational reliability, commercial model, Core or architecture finding.

## 7. MVP Thesis

A first MarkReg release should be a narrow end-to-end vertical slice:

> A user describes a business need with fewer material questions, receives explainable options, confirms commercial scope, completes service-specific Intake and hands a reviewable Package to accountable professionals without losing source, version or authority.

A full-lifecycle constitution does not require a full-lifecycle first release.

## 8. Bounded MVP Sequence

### MVP 0 — Guided Decision and Handoff

```text
Need Brief
→ Recommendation and Option Sets
→ Proposal and transparent Quote
→ exact-version acceptance
→ Formal Intake and Requirement Set
→ Readiness Assessment
→ Handoff Envelope
```

MVP 0 may stop before external filing.

### MVP 1 — Filing Preparation and Governed Action

Adds Package Candidate, diff, Professional Review, client confirmation, Filing Approval, provider or route selection, Provider Acceptance, Execution Evidence, official acknowledgement and unknown-state reconciliation.

A governed manual or provider route may be safer than a premature direct connector.

### MVP 2 — Selected Post-Filing Continuity

Adds controlled official events, deadlines, examination response, Communication, registration baseline and selected maintenance obligations. It must not claim universal monitoring.

### MVP 3 — Portfolio and Network Depth

Adds broader Pack coverage, renewals, recordals, use Evidence, monitoring signals, provider capability evidence, embedded experience and organization-specific learning where governance is proven.

## 9. MR-C11 Pilot Context

A Pilot Context declares:

- Product edition and Conformance Profile;
- organizations, users and roles;
- services, jurisdictions and Pack states;
- provider, connector or manual routes;
- data and Evidence boundaries;
- evaluation cases and success criteria;
- zero-tolerance stop conditions;
- fallback, rollback and disable path;
- incident and professional owners;
- start, end and review dates.

The pilot scope must not exceed Pack support, participant permissions or operational ownership.

## 10. MR-D13 Pilot or Release Decision

The Decision records:

- exact Product and Pilot Context versions;
- evaluation evidence;
- passed and failed scenarios;
- accepted limitations and exclusions;
- unresolved findings;
- authorized next increment;
- rollback or stop conditions;
- publication, implementation, production and protected-action status separately.

A pilot approval authorizes only the declared pilot scope. It is not general production or filing authority.

## 11. Controlled Scenario

`MR-SCN-38` tests a faster journey that creates more late blockers or Package defects. The Product must reject a speed-only success claim and investigate the quality loss.

Zero-tolerance scenarios from `B05-SPEC-0003` remain release and pilot stop conditions for every profile that consumes them.

## 12. Reference-Journey Evaluation

For `EMBERLOOP`, metrics assess question burden, professional modification, Package rework, unknown-state recovery and whether users understand independent UK, US and EU states.

For `RIVERKITE`, metrics assess verified Right Baselines, chain-of-title gaps, independent renewal readiness, use-Evidence coverage, provider and fee variance, duplicate prevention and documented non-renewal choices.

Neither journey is scored by registration volume alone.

## 13. Minimum Metrics and Pilot Lock

```text
Speed ≠ quality.
Activity ≠ outcome.
Legal result ≠ Product quality alone.
Pilot ≠ production.
Evaluation pass ≠ external-action authority.
Feature presence ≠ lifecycle Conformance.
```
