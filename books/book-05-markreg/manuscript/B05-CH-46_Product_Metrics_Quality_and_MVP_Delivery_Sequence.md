# B05-CH-46 — Product Metrics, Quality and MVP Delivery Sequence

**Status:** Part VII Draft  
**Chapter Map:** B05-TOC-V0.1 — Owner Accepted  
**Part:** Part VII — Product Experience and Evolution

## Chapter Purpose

CH45 defines governed rules and AI assistance.

CH46 answers:

> How does MarkReg prove that it reduces complexity and improves professional work without optimizing for misleading conversion, automation or legal-outcome metrics, and how should a safe MVP be delivered?

```text
Product mission
→ measurable user and professional outcomes
→ quality and safety indicators
→ controlled Product-loop validation
→ narrow vertical MVP
→ progressive lifecycle depth
```

A full-lifecycle Product vision does not require a full-lifecycle first release.

---

## 1. User Question and Primary Action

**User question:** Is MarkReg making the journey clearer, safer and more effective, and what should be built next?

**Primary action:** Review evidence, identify the limiting constraint, and approve the next bounded Product increment.

Metrics must support decisions, not decorate dashboards.

---

## 2. Measurement Principles

MarkReg measurement follows these principles:

1. measure controllable Product quality separately from legal outcomes;
2. preserve jurisdiction, service and user-segment context;
3. distinguish speed from unsafe shortcutting;
4. measure rework and correction, not only completion;
5. record Human override and disagreement;
6. measure missing and stale source conditions;
7. include negative and failure outcomes;
8. avoid one global success score;
9. use reviewed samples, not only telemetry;
10. connect each metric to a Product decision.

---

## 3. Metric Layers

A balanced MarkReg metric model includes:

| Layer | Question |
| --- | --- |
| user understanding | Does the user know what is needed and why? |
| journey progression | Can the user reach the next valid state? |
| professional quality | Is the work reviewable, sourced and appropriate? |
| governance safety | Are authority, approval and official truth preserved? |
| operational reliability | Do handoffs, submissions and acknowledgements work safely? |
| commercial clarity | Are price, scope and later-stage costs understood? |
| lifecycle continuity | Do rights, deadlines and obligations remain connected? |
| learning quality | Does feedback improve Product behavior without contaminating truth? |

No layer alone proves Product success.

---

## 4. User Understanding Metrics

Useful measures include:

- percentage of users who can identify the recommended option and its reason;
- number of clarification loops before a material decision;
- percentage of questions answered from reusable verified context;
- abandonment by step and stated reason;
- time spent on unclear fields;
- rate of conflicting client answers;
- decision reversal after professional explanation;
- client acknowledgment of exclusions and uncertainty;
- comprehension test results for key consequences.

A shorter journey is not better if users misunderstand scope or cost.

---

## 5. Need and Recommendation Metrics

Part II may be evaluated through:

- time to first usable Need Brief;
- proportion of required information collected without full-form completion;
- recommendation coverage by source-backed jurisdiction rules;
- rate of professional modification;
- modification reason;
- rate of unsupported or stale recommendation candidates;
- option-set diversity where meaningful alternatives exist;
- unnecessary jurisdiction or class reduction after Review;
- decision latency after recommendation;
- client-selected option versus recommended option.

Professional modification is not automatically an AI or Product failure.

It may identify context the Product should learn to request.

---

## 6. Commercial and Intake Metrics

Parts III metrics may include:

- Proposal-to-Quote conversion by service and client segment;
- Quote expiry before decision;
- fee variance after acceptance;
- later-stage fee disclosure completeness;
- discount approval exceptions;
- payment-related delay;
- Formal Intake rework;
- reused fact acceptance and rejection;
- document request cycles;
- average blockers per Readiness Assessment;
- percentage of blockers discovered after Handoff.

The Product should reduce late surprises, not merely increase acceptance.

---

## 7. Filing Preparation and Execution Metrics

Part IV measures include:

- package diff frequency after client confirmation;
- material changes after Professional Review;
- approval expiry rate;
- provider decline or conflict rate;
- provider-proposed change rate;
- instruction acceptance time;
- submission acknowledgement time;
- unknown-state frequency;
- reconciliation time;
- duplicate-prevention interventions;
- duplicate legal or payment effects;
- package-to-official-receipt mismatch;
- correction filing rate.

A blocked unsafe retry is a safety success, not a throughput failure.

---

## 8. Post-Filing Metrics

Parts V–VI measures include:

- official-event ingestion latency;
- stale status detection;
- corrected-notice handling time;
- deadline verification coverage;
- issues mapped to affected scope;
- client decision latency;
- response-package rework;
- missed official deadlines;
- unsupported outcome Communications;
- publication-window verification coverage;
- maintenance obligation completeness;
- renewal package readiness time;
- chain-of-title gap rate;
- use-evidence coverage;
- portfolio action completion by priority.

Legal success rates require careful interpretation because Product quality does not control office decisions, opponent behavior or client evidence.

---

## 9. Quality Dimensions

Every material Product artifact may be reviewed across dimensions such as:

| Dimension | Example question |
| --- | --- |
| correctness | Does the artifact match verified facts and applicable rules? |
| completeness | Are required fields, sources and decisions present? |
| traceability | Can each material value be traced to source and version? |
| clarity | Can the intended participant understand the consequence? |
| scope integrity | Is the artifact limited to the approved purpose? |
| authority integrity | Are Review, approval and execution boundaries preserved? |
| freshness | Are time-sensitive sources and facts current enough? |
| consistency | Do linked artifacts and views agree? |
| recoverability | Can failure or correction be handled without losing history? |
| privacy | Is information limited to permitted participants and purpose? |

Quality should be reported by dimension, not collapsed prematurely.

---

## 10. Safety Indicators

Critical safety indicators include:

- external action without active approval;
- approval applied to the wrong version;
- provider instructed before acceptance;
- client silence treated as decision;
- unsourced official status;
- stale rule used for protected action;
- duplicate filing or payment;
- missed official deadline;
- confidential evidence exposed outside purpose;
- provider or administrator over-permission;
- AI output presented as professional conclusion without required Review;
- historical record rewritten after rule change.

Some indicators should have zero-tolerance release gates.

---

## 11. Human Override Metrics

MarkReg should record when eligible professionals:

- accept a recommendation;
- modify it;
- reject it;
- add an exception;
- escalate uncertainty;
- approve despite a warning;
- block despite Product readiness.

Override analysis should include:

- reason category;
- affected rule or fact;
- jurisdiction and service;
- Product version;
- later outcome;
- whether Product or Pack improvement is warranted.

The goal is not to minimize professional override to zero.

The goal is to make override informed, visible and useful for improvement.

---

## 12. AI Evaluation

AI-assisted features require task-specific evaluation.

Evaluation dimensions may include:

- source attribution;
- unsupported claim rate;
- missing-question detection;
- scope-mapping accuracy;
- explanation clarity;
- amendment-diff accuracy;
- evidence classification;
- rule-version adherence;
- confidentiality compliance;
- escalation appropriateness;
- professional acceptance with or without modification.

A general language benchmark does not establish legal Product fitness.

---

## 13. Evaluation Sets

Controlled evaluation sets should include:

- synthetic but realistic cases;
- de-identified historical cases where permitted;
- edge cases;
- conflicting-source cases;
- missing-information cases;
- stale-rule cases;
- provider disagreement;
- fee changes;
- deadline corrections;
- multi-jurisdiction divergence;
- unsuccessful and abandoned journeys;
- adversarial and cancellation cases;
- maintenance and chain-of-title cases.

Expected behavior should be reviewed by eligible professionals.

Evaluation data must not silently become current jurisdiction truth.

---

## 14. Product-Loop Validation

A Product loop should test:

```text
Need
→ Product interaction
→ controlled artifact
→ Human decision
→ governed action or handoff
→ observed result
→ reviewed learning
→ Product or Pack improvement
```

A loop is valid only if:

- the entry need is known;
- the Product action is observable;
- the artifact version is retained;
- accountable decisions are recorded;
- external results are sourced;
- learning is reviewed before reuse.

Telemetry without decision context does not complete the loop.

---

## 15. Learning Categories

Observed learning should be classified as:

- Product UX improvement;
- missing question;
- rule or source update;
- organization policy improvement;
- provider capability evidence;
- professional training need;
- client communication improvement;
- operational reliability defect;
- commercial-model issue;
- architecture or Core finding.

Each category has a different owner and change path.

MarkReg must not absorb every learning into Product code.

---

## 16. MVP Definition

The MarkReg MVP should be a narrow, end-to-end vertical slice that proves the Product thesis:

> A user can describe a business need with fewer questions, receive explainable options, confirm a commercial scope, complete a service-specific intake, and hand a reviewable package to accountable professionals without losing source, version or authority.

The MVP does not need to automate every office, jurisdiction or lifecycle stage.

---

## 17. MVP Service Scope

A practical initial scope should limit:

- service family;
- jurisdiction set;
- applicant types;
- filing routes;
- supported languages;
- provider routes;
- document types;
- official integration depth;
- post-filing stages.

A recommended first service family is standard new trademark filing for a controlled set of jurisdictions with frequent organization demand and reliable professional coverage.

The selection should be evidence-based and recorded, not hard-coded into the constitutional specification.

---

## 18. MVP 0 — Guided Decision and Handoff

MVP 0 may include:

```text
Need Brief
→ jurisdiction, route, class and goods/services candidates
→ Option Set and Proposal
→ transparent price basis and Quote
→ exact-version acceptance
→ Formal Intake and Requirement Set
→ Readiness Assessment
→ Handoff Envelope
```

MVP 0 may stop before external filing.

Success proves:

- reduced question burden;
- explainable recommendation;
- commercial clarity;
- reusable verified context;
- governed handoff to professionals.

---

## 19. MVP 1 — Filing Package and Approval

MVP 1 adds:

- Filing Package Candidate;
- package diff;
- client confirmation;
- Professional Review;
- Filing Approval;
- private-first provider routing;
- provider instruction and acceptance;
- manual or provider execution evidence;
- official acknowledgement and unknown-state handling.

Direct connectors are optional.

A governed manual route may be the safer first implementation.

---

## 20. MVP 2 — Post-Filing Continuity

MVP 2 adds selected:

- Official Event Snapshots;
- examination triage;
- deadline governance;
- response package preparation;
- outcome Communication;
- registration and Right Baseline;
- maintenance obligations.

It should not claim universal office monitoring until source coverage and responsibility are proven.

---

## 21. MVP 3 — Portfolio and Network Depth

Later increments may add:

- broader jurisdiction packs;
- portfolio continuity views;
- renewal and recordal packages;
- use-evidence management;
- monitoring signals;
- provider capability evidence;
- advanced embedded experiences;
- approved direct connectors;
- organization-specific learning and distillation.

Expansion should follow validated Product constraints rather than feature count.

---

## 22. Delivery Gates

Each increment should pass gates for:

1. scope definition;
2. architecture conformance;
3. jurisdiction-pack readiness;
4. professional workflow readiness;
5. role and permission design;
6. data and evidence handling;
7. failure and recovery behavior;
8. conformance scenarios;
9. evaluation results;
10. operational ownership;
11. pilot acceptance;
12. rollback or disable capability.

A feature that cannot be safely disabled is not ready for high-risk pilot use.

---

## 23. Pilot Design

A pilot should define:

- participating organizations;
- eligible users and professionals;
- client-consent basis;
- service and jurisdiction scope;
- supported providers;
- manual fallback;
- official-source responsibilities;
- issue escalation;
- evaluation sample;
- stop conditions;
- incident and correction process.

Pilot data should not be generalized beyond its context without review.

---

## 24. Stop Conditions

A pilot or feature should pause when:

- a protected action occurs without authority;
- duplicate legal or financial effect occurs;
- official status is materially misrepresented;
- a deadline responsibility is unclear;
- confidential evidence is disclosed improperly;
- rule freshness cannot be maintained;
- eligible professional coverage is unavailable;
- recovery or audit is insufficient;
- users systematically misunderstand a material consequence.

Growth targets must not override stop conditions.

---

## 25. Metric Governance

Each material metric should have:

- definition;
- purpose;
- owner;
- source;
- calculation;
- segmentation;
- quality checks;
- review frequency;
- known limitation;
- target or guardrail;
- decision it informs.

Metric definitions must be versioned when changed.

Historical reports should retain the definition used at the time.

---

## 26. Anti-Metrics

MarkReg should not optimize blindly for:

- maximum number of filed applications;
- maximum classes per application;
- maximum client acceptance;
- minimum professional intervention;
- highest AI-generated text volume;
- shortest completion time regardless of accuracy;
- provider price alone;
- registration success without case and jurisdiction context;
- reminders sent;
- dashboard activity.

These may create revenue or activity while reducing professional quality.

---

## 27. EMBERLOOP Metrics

The `EMBERLOOP` journey may be evaluated through:

- questions avoided through reusable context;
- professional changes to jurisdiction and class recommendations;
- Quote revisions caused by fee changes;
- package changes after client confirmation;
- provider acceptance time;
- unknown EU acknowledgement resolution time;
- US response rework;
- client understanding of independent country outcomes;
- UK maintenance obligation completeness.

No single `global registration success` metric represents the journey.

---

## 28. RIVERKITE Metrics

The `RIVERKITE` portfolio may be evaluated through:

- rights with verified Right Baselines;
- maintenance obligations with sourced dates;
- chain-of-title gaps resolved;
- evidence coverage by goods and period;
- renewals completed without duplicate fees;
- cancellation-defense deadlines met;
- licenses reviewed before expiry;
- non-renewal decisions documented;
- portfolio recommendations accepted or rejected with reason.

## 29. Conformance Scenarios

### Scenario A — faster but less accurate

**Given** a new question flow reduces completion time  
**But** increases post-Handoff blockers  
**When** the Product team reviews performance  
**Then** the flow is not considered an improvement without resolving the quality loss.

### Scenario B — high professional override

**Given** professionals frequently modify one class recommendation  
**When** reasons show a missing business-context question  
**Then** the Product improves intake or rule mapping  
**And** does not penalize professionals for overriding.

### Scenario C — successful blocked retry

**Given** a connector timeout creates duplicate risk  
**When** MarkReg blocks retry and reconciles successfully  
**Then** the event counts as a safety intervention  
**Not** merely a failed submission.

### Scenario D — MVP scope request

**Given** a stakeholder requests opposition automation in MVP 0  
**When** the approved vertical slice stops at governed Handoff  
**Then** the request is deferred unless scope and gates are formally changed.

### Scenario E — misleading registration metric

**Given** one jurisdiction has lower registration rates due to office practice  
**When** Product quality is compared  
**Then** results are segmented and adjusted for context  
**And** the jurisdiction is not labeled a Product failure automatically.

---

## 30. AI Assistance

AI may assist with:

- metric anomaly detection;
- qualitative feedback clustering;
- override-reason classification;
- evaluation-case generation;
- conformance-test drafting;
- pilot summary;
- identifying possible rule or UX improvements.

AI may not:

- change metric definitions silently;
- label a professional override as error without review;
- infer legal success causation;
- expand MVP scope;
- waive release gates;
- suppress negative outcomes;
- approve pilot continuation after a stop condition.

---

## 31. Failure Modes

MarkReg fails this chapter if:

- legal outcome is treated as the only Product metric;
- speed improvements hide rework;
- automation rate becomes a quality target;
- AI is evaluated only with generic benchmarks;
- pilots lack stop conditions;
- MVP attempts all jurisdictions and lifecycle stages;
- Product learning overwrites source truth;
- metric definitions change without versioning;
- negative cases are excluded from evaluation;
- expansion occurs before a vertical Product loop is proven.

---

## 32. Minimum Product Lock

A conforming MarkReg edition must provide:

1. layered user, quality, safety, operational and lifecycle metrics;
2. metric definitions and ownership;
3. task-specific AI evaluation;
4. controlled evaluation sets including failures and edge cases;
5. reviewed Human override learning;
6. a narrow vertical MVP scope;
7. progressive delivery stages;
8. release, pilot and stop gates;
9. manual fallback and disable capability;
10. evidence-based expansion decisions.
