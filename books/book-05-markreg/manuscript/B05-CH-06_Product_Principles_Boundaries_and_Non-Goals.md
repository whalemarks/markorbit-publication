# B05-CH-06 — Product Principles, Boundaries and Non-Goals

**Status:** Foundation Draft  
**Chapter Map:** B05-TOC-V0.1 — Candidate  
**Part:** Part I — Product Constitution and Lifecycle Position

## Chapter Purpose

A flagship Product attracts expansion.

Every client request, Workflow, provider need, data source, AI feature, and adjacent legal service may appear suitable for inclusion in MarkReg.

Without durable principles, the Product can become:

- a generic case system;
- an organization Workplace;
- an all-intellectual-property platform;
- a provider marketplace;
- an AI legal practice;
- an official-status database;
- a universal Workflow engine.

This chapter establishes the principles that should survive every MarkReg edition, jurisdiction pack, implementation, and roadmap change.

The central proposition is:

```text
Conforming MarkReg
=
Need Before Form
+ Ask Less, Explain More
+ Candidate Before Canonical
+ Recommendation Before Decision
+ Product Loop First
+ Shared Reference Before Copied Truth
+ Source and Version Visibility
+ Human Judgment at Consequential Boundaries
+ Prepared Action Before Protected Action
+ Official-Source Authority
+ Private First
+ Honest Stops
+ Full-Lifecycle Continuity
+ Independent Product Evolution
```

---

## 1. Need Before Form

MarkReg should begin by understanding what the user is trying to achieve.

The first interaction should not assume that the user already knows:

- country;
- route;
- applicant;
- filing unit;
- class;
- goods/services wording;
- document set;
- provider;
- cost;
- timing.

Forms remain necessary later.

They should represent understood and selected context rather than replace the understanding process.

---

## 2. Ask Less, Explain More

The Product should avoid unnecessary questions.

It may use authorized context, known records, inference, and structured options.

It must still expose:

- which facts were reused;
- which facts were inferred;
- which assumptions remain;
- which questions materially affect the recommendation;
- which information must be verified before action.

```text
Fewer questions
+ visible reasoning
+ correction path
≠ hidden assumptions
```

---

## 3. Progressive Disclosure

Not every field belongs at the beginning.

MarkReg should request information when it becomes relevant.

Examples:

- detailed signatory information after the applicant and document route are known;
- provider-specific documents after a provider route is selected;
- evidence questions after an office action or adversarial matter exists;
- renewal owner verification before a renewal package is approved.

Progressive disclosure reduces burden without lowering sufficiency.

---

## 4. Candidate Before Canonical

AI, rules, prior records, providers, and users may produce candidate information.

Examples include:

- applicant candidate;
- jurisdiction candidate;
- class candidate;
- goods/services candidate;
- provider candidate;
- deadline candidate;
- status interpretation;
- Knowledge candidate.

A candidate becomes formal only through the applicable validation and Owning Service process.

```text
Candidate
≠ accepted fact
≠ approved decision
≠ official outcome
```

---

## 5. Recommendation Before Decision

MarkReg may recommend:

- jurisdiction;
- route;
- bundle;
- filing unit;
- class;
- goods/services wording;
- search;
- provider;
- response option;
- maintenance action.

The Product should explain:

- why;
- based on which facts;
- under which source and version;
- with which alternatives;
- with which uncertainty;
- which facts would change the result.

Recommendation supports decision.

It does not impersonate decision.

---

## 6. Product Loop First

MarkReg should be designed around a repeatable user-value loop:

```text
Need
→ Understand
→ Recommend
→ Compare
→ Decide
→ Intake
→ Validate
→ Prepare
→ Review
→ Execute
→ Receive Outcome
→ Maintain
→ Learn
```

Shared services and platform components should be extracted after repeated Product evidence.

The Product should not begin by building a universal platform for every hypothetical service.

---

## 7. Full Lifecycle, Not Filing Endpoint

MarkReg begins before formal intake and continues beyond filing.

It should preserve continuity across:

- strategy;
- filing;
- examination;
- publication;
- opposition;
- registration;
- maintenance;
- renewal;
- changes;
- assignment;
- cancellation;
- portfolio learning.

A Product version may implement stages progressively.

The architecture must not make later stages impossible or disconnected.

---

## 8. Full Lifecycle Does Not Mean One Universal Workflow

International trademark journeys vary by:

- jurisdiction;
- route;
- service type;
- mark type;
- applicant;
- class;
- provider;
- deadline;
- office procedure;
- outcome.

MarkReg provides stable Product meaning while composing different Workflow Contracts, Guides, jurisdiction packs, and providers.

```text
Shared lifecycle
≠ identical procedure
```

---

## 9. Shared Reference Before Copied Truth

Formal objects and authoritative external outcomes should be referenced through stable contracts.

Product-local copies may support display or history.

They should remain identified as:

- snapshot;
- cache;
- projection;
- candidate;
- derivative.

The Product must not create isolated copies of Customer, Matter, Task, Document, or official status and then treat those copies as canonical.

---

## 10. Source and Version Visibility

Material recommendations and outcomes should preserve:

- source;
- jurisdiction;
- checked or effective date;
- rule version;
- Product version;
- source document where applicable;
- assumptions;
- confidence;
- superseded state.

This is especially important for:

- official fees;
- document requirements;
- classification practice;
- opposition periods;
- maintenance obligations;
- deadlines;
- official status.

---

## 11. Professional Judgment at Consequential Boundaries

Automation may increase with low-consequence, reversible preparation.

Human judgment should remain explicit where consequence rises.

Consequential boundaries include:

- applicant identity;
- filing strategy;
- specification;
- search interpretation;
- filing readiness;
- office action response;
- evidence sufficiency;
- provider appointment;
- filing approval;
- payment approval;
- assignment or ownership recordal.

The Product should not remove review merely to improve completion rate.

---

## 12. Prepared Action Before Protected Action

Before a protected action, MarkReg should prepare an inspectable package containing:

- intent;
- actor and organization;
- target;
- inputs;
- version;
- provenance;
- expected consequence;
- Permission and Policy requirements;
- review and approval requirements;
- downstream service;
- expiry;
- idempotency;
- missing conditions.

The protected action remains governed by Execution and the relevant service.

---

## 13. Official-Source Authority

Official facts should be attributed to an authoritative source.

MarkReg may interpret or normalize them.

It should not display an unsourced Product state as official truth.

```text
Product projection
+ no source
≠ official status
```

When the source is unavailable or contradictory, the Product should show uncertainty rather than invent resolution.

---

## 14. Private First

Client data, organization Knowledge, pricing, provider relationships, and Matter context remain private by default.

Provider discovery begins with private relationships.

Shared learning begins as a candidate and requires purpose, review, and governance.

MarkReg should not use private work for broad AI training or public ranking by default.

---

## 15. Purpose Limitation

Every participant and AI Agent should receive context for a defined purpose.

The Product should avoid:

- full portfolio exposure for one filing;
- internal margin exposure to a client;
- unrelated Matter exposure to a provider;
- hidden reuse of client evidence;
- indefinite AI context retention.

Purpose limitation should shape both interface and data flow.

---

## 16. Explainability Before Confidence Theatre

A recommendation should be understandable.

A numeric confidence score without rationale is insufficient.

The Product should expose:

- material factors;
- source quality;
- unresolved facts;
- alternative outcomes;
- sensitivity to changed assumptions;
- review requirement.

The goal is not to reveal internal chain-of-thought.

The goal is to provide an auditable professional rationale.

---

## 17. Honest Stops

MarkReg should be able to stop or pause a journey because:

- the need is unclear;
- identity is unresolved;
- documents are unavailable;
- the deadline is unverifiable;
- professional risk is too high;
- no provider is eligible;
- review rejects the work;
- approval is withheld;
- the external outcome is unknown.

The Product should not fabricate completion to preserve a funnel.

---

## 18. Safe Degradation

When a dependency is unavailable, MarkReg should degrade honestly.

It may allow:

- local preparation;
- draft editing;
- cached comparison;
- information collection;
- offline checklist use.

It must not claim:

- current Permission;
- current official status;
- current provider availability;
- verified deadline;
- successful submission;

without current evidence.

---

## 19. Reversibility Before Irreversibility

Early Product actions should be easy to correct.

Examples:

- revise recommendation;
- change jurisdiction option;
- update applicant candidate;
- edit goods/services candidate;
- replace document draft.

As the journey approaches external filing, payment, provider instruction, or formal recordal, the Product should increase:

- validation;
- review;
- confirmation;
- trace;
- idempotency;
- consequence visibility.

---

## 20. Version-Specific Review

Review and approval apply to a defined version.

Material edits after approval may require new review.

Examples include changes to:

- applicant;
- mark image;
- class;
- goods/services;
- priority;
- provider;
- fee;
- recipient;
- filing route;
- evidence;
- response argument.

The Product should not attach an old approval badge to a materially changed package.

---

## 21. Deterministic Representation for Exact Facts

AI may draft and explain.

Exact factual outputs should use deterministic rendering where precision matters.

Examples:

- applicant name;
- filing number;
- class list;
- fee breakdown;
- deadline;
- document checklist;
- goods/services approved version;
- provider instruction scope.

A fluent paraphrase should not alter exact formal content.

---

## 22. Human Choice Before Provider Routing Action

MarkReg may rank candidates.

The organization selects.

The appointment is prepared and approved where required.

Execution governs instruction.

The provider accepts independently.

The Product should not optimize for automatic assignment at the expense of trust and responsibility.

---

## 23. Independent Product Evolution

MarkReg may:

- release independently;
- support standalone or embedded use;
- use different models or Skills;
- add jurisdiction packs;
- change navigation;
- replace implementation;
- run controlled experiments.

It must preserve:

- shared semantics;
- authority boundaries;
- formal references;
- review;
- official-source rules;
- lifecycle continuity.

---

## 24. Jurisdiction Packs, Not Jurisdiction Forks

Country differences should be represented through versioned configuration and sourced Knowledge where possible.

A jurisdiction pack may define:

- questions;
- validation;
- documents;
- fees;
- local terminology;
- filing route;
- provider need;
- Workflow entry;
- maintenance signals.

It should not become a separate incompatible Product architecture for every country.

---

## 25. Progressive Complexity

A solo practitioner, small agency, enterprise team, and external provider may need different surfaces.

MarkReg may hide complexity not required for a current user.

It should not remove governance merely because the user interface is simple.

```text
Simple experience
≠ simplified authority
```

---

## 26. Metrics Must Reflect Value and Safety

Useful metrics may include:

- time to useful recommendation;
- missing-information rate;
- package rework;
- review rejection;
- acknowledgement success;
- duplicate prevention;
- deadline-source completeness;
- lifecycle task coverage;
- client surprise rate.

Vanity metrics such as clicks, AI messages, or automatic completion should not dominate Product decisions.

---

## 27. Non-Goal: MarkReg Is Not the Workplace

MarkReg should not own:

- the whole organization identity;
- all clients;
- all private Knowledge;
- all Products;
- all people administration;
- all organization memory.

It consumes authorized Workplace context.

---

## 28. Non-Goal: MarkReg Is Not Core

MarkReg should not redefine:

- Customer;
- Trademark;
- Matter;
- Task;
- Document;
- Evidence;
- Routing;
- Capability;
- Permission;
- Policy;
- Event.

Product convenience does not create semantic authority.

---

## 29. Non-Goal: MarkReg Is Not Execution

MarkReg may request and visualize governed work.

It should not create a parallel Workflow, Task, Review, approval, retry, or audit system.

---

## 30. Non-Goal: MarkReg Is Not an Official Office

MarkReg may retrieve and explain office outcomes.

It cannot create an official filing number, registration, refusal, or deadline merely by changing Product state.

---

## 31. Non-Goal: MarkReg Is Not an Open Marketplace

Provider discovery is private-first and evidence-based.

The Product should not expose every client need to public bidding or rank providers primarily by payment or price.

---

## 32. Non-Goal: MarkReg Is Not an Autonomous Professional Practice

AI may assist.

MarkReg may not become:

- lawyer;
- representative;
- reviewer;
- approver;
- signatory;
- provider selector;
- filing authority.

Professional responsibility remains human and organizational.

---

## 33. Non-Goal: MarkReg Is Not All Intellectual Property

Trademark work may connect to:

- designs;
- copyright;
- patents;
- domain names;
- customs;
- enforcement;
- corporate transactions.

Those connections do not automatically enter MarkReg scope.

Cross-domain work may use other Products, Capabilities, or Handoffs.

---

## 34. Non-Goal: MarkReg Is Not a Generic CRM or Case System

The Product may display clients, opportunities, Orders, Matters, and communications.

Its primary problem remains the international trademark Product journey.

It should not become the default home for every business and operational process.

---

## 35. Non-Goal: MarkReg Is Not a Hidden Training Pool

Private client work, documents, evidence, and professional decisions must not be silently reused for broad model training.

Learning requires:

- purpose;
- authority;
- privacy control;
- provenance;
- candidate status;
- validation.

---

## 36. Conformance Test

A proposed feature should answer:

1. Which user problem does it solve?
2. Which MarkReg Product loop does it improve?
3. Which Product state does MarkReg own?
4. Which shared object or service does it reference?
5. Which source and version apply?
6. Which Human Review is required?
7. Which protected action is prepared?
8. Which Owning Service or external authority acts?
9. Which outcome returns?
10. Which failure or uncertainty remains visible?

A feature that cannot answer these questions is not ready for architecture acceptance.

---

## 37. Minimum Product Lock

```text
Need before form.

Ask less, explain more.

Candidate before canonical.

Recommendation before decision.

Prepared Action before protected action.

Human judgment at consequential boundaries.

Official outcome only with source.

Private first.

Full lifecycle without one universal Workflow.

Independent Product without architectural sovereignty.

Honest stop before fabricated completion.
```

---

## 38. Chapter Conclusion

MarkReg should feel intelligent, complete, and easy to use.

It should not achieve that experience by hiding authority, uncertainty, or responsibility.

The Product principles in this chapter are constraints on every future feature.

They are also the reason the Product can grow safely.
