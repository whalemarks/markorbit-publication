# B05-CH-06 — Product Principles, Boundaries and Non-Goals

**Status:** Complete Draft 1  
**Chapter Map:** B05-TOC-V0.1 — Owner Accepted  
**Part:** Part I — Product Constitution and Lifecycle Position

## Chapter Purpose

A flagship Product attracts expansion. Every client request, Workflow, provider need, data source, AI feature and adjacent legal service may appear suitable for inclusion in MarkReg.

Without durable principles, the Product can drift into a generic case system, second Workplace, open marketplace, autonomous legal practice, official-status database or all-IP platform.

This chapter defines the principles that must survive every MarkReg edition, Jurisdiction Pack, interface and implementation.

## 1. Constitutional Product Lock

```text
Conforming MarkReg
=
Need Before Form
+ Ask Less, Explain More
+ Candidate Before Canonical
+ Recommendation Before Decision
+ Product Loop First
+ Shared Reference Before Copied Truth
+ Source and Version Visibility
+ Human Judgment at Consequential Boundaries
+ Prepared Action Before Protected Action
+ Official-Source Authority
+ Private First
+ Full-Lifecycle Continuity
```

These principles are not optional design preferences. They define whether an implementation still represents the MarkReg Product described by this book.

## 2. Need Before Form

The Product begins with the user’s business objective, intended protection, timing, risk and constraints rather than a jurisdictional filing form.

A form remains useful when the relevant strategy and scope have become sufficiently clear.

```text
Need understanding
→ recommendation
→ Decision
→ Formal Intake
→ filing data
```

Beginning with a form transfers professional complexity to the user and hides the Decisions that created the final fields.

## 3. Ask Less, Explain More

MarkReg should ask only questions that materially affect the recommendation, readiness or next action.

It may reduce questions by:

- reusing authorized context;
- deriving a candidate from verified records;
- delaying a question until it becomes relevant;
- presenting bounded options;
- explaining a default assumption;
- using AI Assistance to extract or compare information.

The Product must show which answers are facts, inferred candidates, assumptions or unresolved issues.

`Ask Less` never means collect insufficient facts or hide uncertainty.

## 4. Candidate Before Canonical

New information should enter the Product as a candidate until its source, scope and acceptance are clear.

This applies to:

- applicant and ownership data;
- mark and specification versions;
- jurisdiction Rules and fees;
- provider Capability Evidence;
- deadlines;
- official events;
- organization Knowledge;
- AI-generated content.

A candidate may be useful before it becomes canonical. It must remain visibly provisional.

```text
Observed or generated
→ candidate
→ source and scope Review
→ accepted for a defined purpose
→ formalized where authorized
```

## 5. Recommendation Before Decision

MarkReg may recommend jurisdictions, routes, filing units, classes, goods or services, search scope, providers and service options.

A recommendation should contain rationale, assumptions, alternatives, uncertainty and material commercial factors.

It does not become a Decision until an accountable actor accepts, modifies or rejects it within a defined authority scope.

```text
Recommendation ≠ Decision
Ranking ≠ Human Selection
AI suggestion ≠ Professional Review
```

## 6. Product Loop First

Features should support a coherent Product loop rather than accumulate as disconnected tools.

```text
Need
→ understand
→ recommend
→ decide
→ intake
→ prepare
→ Review
→ approve
→ execute
→ reconcile
→ continue
```

A classification assistant, fee calculator, Document uploader or status feed is valuable only when its inputs, outputs and consequences connect to the wider journey.

The Product loop may branch and skip stages. It must preserve the lineage between them.

## 7. Shared Reference Before Copied Truth

When a formal shared object or authoritative source exists, MarkReg should prefer a stable reference over an uncontrolled Product copy.

A copy may support display, historical comparison, offline preparation or performance. It must retain its representation type and source.

```text
reference
snapshot
Product Projection
candidate
cache
```

These terms are not interchangeable.

A copied official status is not Official Truth merely because it is stored inside the Product.

## 8. Source and Version Visibility

Every consequential Artifact, Rule, fee, deadline, official event and outcome should preserve:

- source;
- retrieval or effective time;
- jurisdiction and service scope;
- version;
- status and confidence;
- owner or Professional Owner;
- supersession behavior;
- affected downstream records.

The Product should make material changes visible rather than silently rewriting accepted history.

A new version may invalidate a recommendation, Quote, Review, approval or prepared action.

## 9. Human Judgment at Consequential Boundaries

AI Assistance and Product logic may support routine analysis, but consequential boundaries require accountable Human Judgment where defined by Policy, law, engagement or Product governance.

Examples include:

- applicant and ownership resolution;
- material scope and risk Decisions;
- Professional Review;
- Filing Approval;
- settlement or abandonment;
- provider selection and appointment;
- Rule publication;
- release and Conformance claims.

A Human action must identify the actor, authority, subject, version, basis and consequence.

Human involvement should be meaningful, not a decorative click added after an automated Decision.

## 10. Prepared Action Before Protected Action

MarkReg prepares the context and Artifact required for the next action.

Execution governs whether and how an approved protected action proceeds.

```text
prepared
≠ approved

approved
≠ executed

executed
≠ officially acknowledged
```

A Product button may request an action. It must not bypass Permission, Review, approval, idempotency, retry or audit controls.

## 11. Official-Source Authority

Official offices remain authoritative for official procedural events and records.

MarkReg may retrieve, normalize, translate, explain and project official information. It must preserve the source and distinguish:

- official record or notice;
- technical delivery Evidence;
- Provider Report;
- professional interpretation;
- Product Projection;
- internal reminder or estimate.

```text
Provider Report ≠ Official Truth
Product Projection ≠ official record
Reminder ≠ verified deadline
```

## 12. Private First

Client data, organization Knowledge, provider relationships, pricing, internal cost, margin, Review notes and work product remain within their authorized boundaries.

Private First means:

- use the organization’s own relationships before public discovery where Policy requires;
- expose information only for a defined purpose;
- separate client-visible price from internal cost and margin;
- prevent unrelated provider access;
- keep personal or Lite-local context from entering organization Knowledge automatically;
- treat shared learning as a reviewed, purpose-limited candidate.

Private First does not mean isolated forever. It means sharing requires an explicit governed path.

## 13. Full-Lifecycle Continuity

MarkReg begins before formal filing and continues after submission or registration.

The Product should preserve continuity across:

- need and strategy;
- commercial instruction;
- preparation and filing;
- examination and disputes;
- registration and maintenance;
- renewal and recordal;
- transactions and Portfolio action.

Continuity means stable references, source history, Decisions, Documents, Evidence, responsibility and next actions remain usable across stages.

It does not mean one Product owns every Workflow or official state.

## 14. Independent Jurisdiction and Right States

A multi-jurisdiction initiative is not one global application or right.

Each jurisdiction and right retains its own:

- filing unit and route;
- applicant and owner;
- Package and approval;
- provider and cost;
- official identifier;
- procedural status;
- deadlines and obligations;
- Evidence and outcome.

Country bundles and Portfolio views may simplify comparison. They must not hide independent consequences.

## 15. Explainability Before Apparent Certainty

MarkReg should explain why a recommendation, risk, price or next action exists.

A useful explanation identifies:

- source facts;
- applicable Rule or reasoning;
- assumptions;
- alternatives;
- uncertainty;
- factors that would change the result;
- responsible Human Decision.

A precise-looking score without these elements may create false confidence.

## 16. Safe Failure and Honest Unknowns

The Product should represent failure, conflict, expiry, missing Evidence and unknown external state explicitly.

When the outcome is unknown, MarkReg should reconcile before retrying an External Protected Action.

```text
unknown submission state
→ investigate Evidence
→ reconcile provider and official sources
→ decide whether retry is safe
```

A journey that stops honestly is better than a journey that invents completion.

## 17. Conformance Claims Must Be Bounded

An implementation may support only part of MarkReg.

Conformance must be declared through a defined Profile and supported by evidence for the relevant scenarios and Jurisdiction Pack modules.

```text
Feature exists
≠ Profile passed

Profile passed
≠ every jurisdiction supported

Pack support
≠ production authority
```

A support claim must identify jurisdiction, service, stage, Pack Version, support state and evidence.

## 18. Non-Goals

MarkReg is not intended to become:

### A Second Workplace

It does not recreate the organization, private Knowledge, general CRM, permissions, people or provider relationships as Product-owned substitutes.

### A Second Core

It does not invent competing canonical definitions for shared objects.

### A Second Execution System

It does not own generic Task, Review, approval, retry, Event or protected-action lifecycle.

### An Open Provider Marketplace

It does not convert professional relationships into public bidding, automatic ranking or platform-controlled inventory.

### An Autonomous Legal Practice

AI does not become the professional, representative, reviewer, approver or filing authority.

### An Official Source

The Product does not manufacture official status, deadlines or outcomes.

### An All-IP Platform

Adjacent patent, design, copyright, domain, customs, enforcement or general legal work should connect through appropriate Products and Capabilities rather than silently expanding MarkReg.

### A Jurisdictional Legal Manual

Jurisdiction Packs operationalize sourced, versioned Product behavior. They do not replace official sources or professional interpretation.

### A Final Implementation Specification

Book 05 defines Product constitution, journeys and controlled contracts. Implementation architecture, APIs and production permissions remain separate work.

## 19. Product-Expansion Test

Before adding a feature, ask:

1. Which MarkReg user problem does it solve?
2. Which Product-loop stage does it support?
3. Is the state Product-local, shared or externally authoritative?
4. Which actor remains responsible?
5. Does an existing Core, Execution, MGSN or Owning Service contract already own the responsibility?
6. Which source, version and change controls apply?
7. Which Conformance Scenario tests the feature?
8. Does the feature expand jurisdiction or service support claims?
9. What private information does it use or expose?
10. Can the feature fail safely?

A feature that cannot answer these questions should not enter the Product roadmap as an assumed MarkReg responsibility.

## Chapter Lock

```text
MarkReg starts from need, not form.
It asks less without hiding assumptions.
It produces candidates before canonical records.
It recommends before Humans decide.
It prepares before Execution acts.
It references shared and official truth rather than copying authority.
It preserves privacy, version and lifecycle continuity.
It remains a focused Product.
```
