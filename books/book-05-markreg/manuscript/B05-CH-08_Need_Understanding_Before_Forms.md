# B05-CH-08 — Need Understanding Before Forms

**Status:** Complete Draft 1  
**Chapter Map:** B05-TOC-V0.1 — Owner Accepted  
**Part:** Part II — Need, Strategy and Recommendation

## Chapter Purpose

MarkReg begins with the business problem, not a filing form.

This chapter defines how ordinary-language requests, authorized context and unresolved facts become a versioned **Need Brief**. That brief supports later jurisdiction, route, filing-unit, classification, goods/services, search and commercial decisions without pretending that those decisions already exist.

The canonical record rules are defined in B05-SPEC-0001. The controlled example is `EL-01` in B05-SPEC-0002.

## 1. Product Question

> What problem is the user trying to solve, and which missing facts would materially change the next recommendation?

A request such as “register this logo overseas” does not establish:

- the applicant;
- the filing unit;
- the jurisdictions or route;
- the classes or goods/services;
- the search scope;
- the timing strategy;
- the budget allocation;
- filing authority.

The Product must understand enough to prepare recommendations while avoiding a long questionnaire that transfers the full professional burden to the user.

## 2. Inputs and Source Status

MarkReg may use:

1. authorized organization, client, brand and portfolio context;
2. user-provided business facts;
3. uploaded brand assets;
4. market, timing, budget and risk preferences;
5. prior applications, registrations and business plans;
6. professional corrections or clarifications.

Each material fact must remain distinguishable as:

- source-backed;
- user-confirmed;
- inferred candidate;
- professionally corrected;
- conflicting;
- stale;
- unresolved.

Known facts should be reused rather than re-asked. Reuse does not remove the user’s ability to inspect, correct or reject them.

## 3. Ask the Smallest Material Question Set

The first interaction should normally establish:

| Area | Material question |
| --- | --- |
| objective | What business outcome should protection support? |
| markets | Where is the business active or preparing to launch? |
| offering | What products, software, services or activities are involved? |
| timing | Is there a launch, exhibition, funding, filing or priority deadline? |
| mark use | Which mark forms are already used or planned? |
| ownership | Is there a known applicant or group ownership policy? |
| risk | Is speed, search depth or cost the main constraint? |
| budget | Which markets or filing units have priority if scope must be reduced? |

A follow-up question is justified when its answer changes a material recommendation, exposes a professional-risk issue or affects a later protected action.

## 4. Controlled Output

The chapter produces:

```text
MR-A01 — Business Context Snapshot
→ MR-A02 — Need Brief
```

A conforming Need Brief records:

- stable identity and version;
- business objective and intended protection;
- brand and offering summary;
- current and planned markets;
- relevant timing;
- applicant and authority candidates;
- known mark forms;
- budget and risk posture;
- assumptions and unresolved questions;
- source references;
- confirmation and supersession state.

The Need Brief is intentionally smaller than Formal Intake. It may contain visible assumptions. It is not a filing instruction, Quote, Order or Matter.

## 5. User Surface

The user should see:

- the interpreted need in plain language;
- facts reused from authorized context;
- assumptions and source status;
- conflicts or missing facts;
- the likely effect of a material edit;
- one primary action: **Confirm or edit the Need Brief**.

The surface must explain that confirmation permits recommendation work to begin. It does not authorize payment, provider appointment or filing.

## 6. Reference Journey — `EL-01 EMBERLOOP`

Northstar Kitchenware plans a crowdfunding launch for smart outdoor cooking equipment and a companion application. The initial budget requires core-market prioritization.

MarkReg prepares:

> **Need Brief v1:** Protect the `EMBERLOOP` word mark and core flame-loop device before launch; treat the United States and European Union as core markets; preserve the United Kingdom, Japan and Australia as staged expansion candidates.

The operating subsidiary is an applicant candidate, but parent-company portfolio ownership creates an unresolved identity issue for CH12. No filing or official outcome is implied.

## 7. Controlled Scenarios

### `MR-SCN-13` — Material business change

A changed market, product, mark form, timing assumption or ownership fact creates a new Need Brief version and identifies affected downstream records. Prior acceptance does not silently apply to new facts.

### `MR-SCN-01` — Applicant evidence conflict

Where applicant evidence conflicts, both sources remain visible. The Product asks the smallest material question and does not infer the legal owner.

Minimum retained evidence includes source records, questions, answers, assumptions, changes, actor and confirmed version.

## 8. Change and Supersession

A material change may affect:

- jurisdiction and route recommendations;
- filing units;
- classes and goods/services;
- search scope;
- Proposal and Quote;
- Formal Intake and requirements;
- package readiness.

The Product creates a new version and records impact. It must not rewrite accepted commercial scope or formal records silently.

## 9. Product Measures

Useful measures include:

- questions asked before the first useful Need Brief;
- percentage of authorized facts reused;
- user correction rate;
- time to confirmation;
- downstream revisions caused by missed need facts;
- abandonment caused by premature form burden.

The objective is not the fewest possible questions. It is the smallest question set that supports a safe and explainable next Decision.

## 10. Handoff to CH09

A confirmed Need Brief permits preparation of jurisdiction and route candidates within the Recommendation Set.

CH09 asks where protection matters, which routes are available and which dependencies remain unresolved. Recommendation remains separate from client Decision, professional judgment and filing authority.
