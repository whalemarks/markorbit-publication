# B05-CH-10 — Country Bundles and Portfolio Strategy

**Status:** Complete Draft 1  
**Chapter Map:** B05-TOC-V0.1 — Owner Accepted  
**Part:** Part II — Need, Strategy and Recommendation

## Chapter Purpose

Users often think in commercial regions such as “Europe,” “Southeast Asia” or “our core export markets.” Trademark rights, fees, documents, providers, deadlines and outcomes remain jurisdiction-specific.

This chapter defines how MarkReg presents country combinations as understandable **Option Sets** without treating a bundle as one right, one price or one lifecycle state.

The controlled example is `EL-03` in B05-SPEC-0002.

## 1. Product Question

> Which combination of markets best fits the current objective, budget, timing and expansion plan?

A useful bundle reduces comparison burden while preserving every material independent consequence.

## 2. Bundle Types

MarkReg may present:

| Bundle type | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| Core-market | Protect the jurisdictions most important to current revenue or launch. |
| Launch | Align protection with a defined market-entry sequence. |
| Defensive | Address manufacturing, sourcing, copying or distributor risk. |
| Regional comparison | Compare a regional route with selected national alternatives. |
| Budget tier | Show minimum, recommended and broader protection scenarios. |
| Expansion | Preserve a staged roadmap with explicit reconsideration triggers. |
| Portfolio repair | Address gaps, inconsistent ownership, missed maintenance or duplicate rights. |

A bundle is a Product comparison device, not a formal legal object.

## 3. Controlled Output

A bundle is recorded in `MR-A04 Option Set` and must state:

- included and excluded jurisdictions;
- route per jurisdiction;
- included filing units;
- class and goods/services assumptions;
- search assumptions;
- provider and representation dependencies;
- estimated official fees, client price and later-stage cost boundaries;
- timing and uncertainty;
- document requirements known at the current stage;
- independent risks and deadlines;
- deferred items and reconsideration triggers;
- Pack Version and source status;
- version, selection and supersession history.

Every jurisdiction must remain inspectable independently.

## 4. Portfolio Strategy Logic

Bundle composition should consider:

1. current business activity;
2. planned launch order;
3. manufacturing and sourcing locations;
4. sales channels, distributors and licensees;
5. existing rights and coverage gaps;
6. mark variants and local-language needs;
7. timing and priority opportunities;
8. search risk;
9. available budget;
10. future maintenance and provider burden.

The Product may rank scenarios. It must disclose the factors and assumptions that shape the ranking.

## 5. User Surface

The user should see two to four meaningful scenarios rather than dozens of checkboxes.

Each scenario should show:

- a plain-language name and purpose;
- included jurisdictions and routes;
- total estimate with per-jurisdiction detail;
- filing units and main scope assumptions;
- excluded or deferred items;
- trade-offs against other scenarios;
- the effect of customization;
- one primary action: **Choose or customize a bundle**.

Selection creates a new Option Set version. It does not create one global right, merge independent jurisdiction states or authorize filing.

## 6. Reference Journey — `EL-03 EMBERLOOP`

MarkReg compares three scenarios:

### Core launch

- United States and European Union;
- word mark prioritized;
- device scope reduced or deferred;
- United Kingdom, Japan and Australia deferred.

### Recommended core protection

- United States, European Union and United Kingdom;
- word and flame-loop device candidates;
- Japan and Australia deferred with launch or distributor triggers.

### Broader five-market launch

- United States, European Union, United Kingdom, Japan and Australia;
- selected word and device filings;
- greater immediate cost, document and provider burden.

Northstar selects the recommended core-protection scenario. The Option Set retains the other scenarios and all independent market consequences.

## 7. Controlled Scenarios

### `MR-SCN-03` — Country-bundle comparison

A request for “Europe” must preserve EU, UK and relevant national scope, fees, representation and post-filing consequences. The bundle is not one right.

### `MR-SCN-14` — Non-recommended client choice

When the client selects a narrower or higher-risk scenario, MarkReg preserves the professional recommendation and records the informed client Decision and consequences.

Minimum evidence includes jurisdictions considered, route assumptions, Pack versions, price basis, professional rationale, client selection and superseded Option Set versions.

## 8. Change Propagation

Customizing a bundle may require regeneration of:

- jurisdiction and route recommendations;
- provider needs;
- filing-unit combinations;
- classification and goods/services candidates;
- search scope;
- Proposal and Quote;
- document requirements;
- readiness.

A changed bundle creates a new Option Set. It must not silently modify accepted commercial scope or an approved Package.

## 9. Deferred Does Not Mean Forgotten

Each deferred market should carry a trigger, such as:

- market launch;
- distributor appointment;
- manufacturing start;
- funding milestone;
- competitor signal;
- priority deadline;
- budget release;
- renewal or recordal event.

The trigger creates a future-action candidate, not an automatic filing.

## 10. Handoff to CH11

The selected Option Set identifies where protection may be sought.

CH11 determines what representation may require protection in each market: word, device, composite, local-language form, color claim or another versioned filing-unit candidate.
