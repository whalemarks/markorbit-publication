## Chapter Purpose

Users often think in commercial regions such as “Europe,” “Southeast Asia,” or “our core export markets.” Trademark rights, fees, documents, providers, deadlines, and outcomes remain jurisdiction-specific.

This chapter defines how MarkReg creates understandable country bundles without pretending that a bundle is one legal right or one indivisible service.

## 1. Product Question

> Which combination of markets best fits the user’s objective, budget, timing, and expansion plan?

A useful bundle reduces comparison burden while preserving independent consequences.

## 2. Bundle Types

MarkReg may offer:

| Bundle type | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| Core-market bundle | Protect the jurisdictions most important to current revenue or launch. |
| Launch bundle | Align protection with a defined launch sequence. |
| Defensive bundle | Address manufacturing, sourcing, imitation, or distributor risk. |
| Regional comparison bundle | Compare a regional route with selected national alternatives. |
| Budget-tier bundle | Present minimum, recommended, and broader protection scenarios. |
| Expansion bundle | Preserve a staged roadmap with explicit reconsideration triggers. |
| Portfolio-repair bundle | Address gaps, inconsistent ownership, missed maintenance, or duplicate rights. |

A bundle is an `Option Set` composition device under `MR-A04`.

## 3. Bundle Contract

Each bundle must state:

- included jurisdictions;
- filing route per jurisdiction;
- included filing units;
- class and goods/services assumptions;
- search assumptions;
- provider and representation dependencies;
- estimated cost and timing;
- included and excluded stages;
- document requirements known at the current stage;
- independent risks and deadlines;
- later expansion trigger;
- versioned sources and jurisdiction-pack references.

The Product must allow the user to open each jurisdiction and inspect its independent consequences.

## 4. Portfolio Strategy Logic

Bundle composition should consider:

1. current business activity;
2. planned launch order;
3. manufacturing and sourcing locations;
4. sales channels and distributors;
5. existing rights and coverage gaps;
6. mark variants and local-language needs;
7. timing and priority opportunities;
8. search risk;
9. available budget;
10. future maintenance burden.

The Product may rank scenarios, but the ranking must expose its assumptions.

## 5. User Surface

The user should see two to four meaningful scenarios rather than dozens of country checkboxes.

A conforming surface provides:

- a plain-language bundle name;
- purpose and intended user;
- included jurisdictions and routes;
- total estimate plus per-jurisdiction detail;
- what is deferred or excluded;
- trade-offs against other scenarios;
- the consequence of customization;
- one primary action: **Choose or customize a bundle**.

The selection creates a new Option Set version. It does not merge the jurisdictions into one formal object.

## 6. Reference Journey A — EMBERLOOP

For EMBERLOOP, MarkReg compares:

### Essential launch

- United States word mark;
- European Union word mark;
- device deferred;
- United Kingdom, Japan, and Australia deferred.

### Recommended core protection

- United States word and device;
- European Union word and device;
- United Kingdom word and device;
- Japan and Australia deferred.

### Broader five-market launch

- United States, European Union, United Kingdom, Japan, and Australia;
- word and selected device filings;
- higher immediate cost and document burden.

The user chooses the recommended core-protection scenario and can inspect independent costs and dependencies for each market.

## 7. Conformance Scenario

### MR-SCN-03 — Country-bundle comparison

**Given** the user requests protection in “Europe” without specifying countries.  
**When** MarkReg prepares portfolio scenarios.  
**Then** it compares European Union, United Kingdom, and selected national routes without hiding separate scope, fees, representation, timing, and post-regional consequences.  
**Authority boundary:** a bundle is a comparison device, not one legal right.  
**Evidence retained:** jurisdictions considered, assumptions, fee-source versions, alternatives, and selected Option Set.

## 8. Change Propagation

Customizing a bundle may require regeneration of:

- route recommendations;
- provider needs;
- filing-unit combinations;
- classification and goods/services variants;
- search scope;
- Proposal and Quote;
- document requirements;
- readiness.

A changed bundle creates a new Option Set. It must not silently alter an accepted Quote or instructed scope.

## 9. Portfolio Continuity

A deferred jurisdiction should carry a reconsideration trigger, such as:

- market launch;
- distributor appointment;
- manufacturing start;
- funding milestone;
- competitor signal;
- priority deadline;
- budget release;
- renewal or recordal event.

This prevents “deferred” from becoming forgotten.

## 10. Handoff to CH11

The selected bundle identifies where protection may be sought.

CH11 determines what representation or filing unit should be protected in each selected jurisdiction: word, device, composite, local-language form, color claim, or another versioned candidate.
