**Status:** Part VII Draft

## Chapter Purpose

CH43–CH46 define Product surfaces, participation, jurisdiction intelligence, AI, metrics and delivery.

CH47 answers:

> What does it mean for an edition or implementation to conform to Book 05, how may MarkReg evolve, and what remains outside the authority of this publication?

```text
Books 01–04 Portfolio Baseline
→ Book 05 Product constitution
→ controlled lifecycle contracts
→ conformance profile
→ bounded implementation roadmap
→ reviewed evolution without architecture drift
```

The conclusion is not an authorization to build or act. It is a controlled statement of what must remain true when future work proceeds.

---

## 1. MarkReg Defined

MarkReg is the focused full-lifecycle international trademark Product that helps clients, professionals and organizations move from a complex business need to understandable options, governed professional work, sourced official outcomes and continuing portfolio decisions.

It provides:

- need understanding before forms;
- explainable jurisdiction, route, class and scope recommendations;
- commercial transparency and service-specific intake;
- governed filing and response packages;
- Human Review and version-specific approval;
- private-first provider collaboration;
- safe Execution handoffs and official acknowledgement states;
- examination, publication, dispute and remedy journeys;
- registration, maintenance, renewal, recordal and chain-of-title continuity;
- portfolio views and learning;
- standalone, embedded and cross-Product experience;
- source-backed jurisdiction intelligence and bounded AI assistance.

---

## 2. MarkReg Is Not

MarkReg is not:

- the whole MarkOrbit Operating System;
- the Frozen Core Specification;
- the Execution System;
- a professional organization’s Workplace;
- a public provider marketplace;
- an official trademark office;
- an Owning Service for every formal business fact;
- a universal legal database;
- an autonomous lawyer or trademark agent;
- a guarantee of registration or legal outcome;
- an unrestricted implementation specification;
- authorization for production deployment or external protected action.

These boundaries are constitutional, not optional product positioning.

---

## 3. Upstream Conformance

A conforming MarkReg edition must preserve the Books 01–04 Portfolio Baseline.

### 3.1 Book 01

MarkReg must preserve:

- the industry and Operating System mission;
- organization independence;
- professional responsibility;
- explainable and governed collaboration;
- safe connection across independent participants.

### 3.2 Book 02

MarkReg must consume rather than silently redefine shared Core semantics.

Where implementation requires a missing or changed Core contract, the issue must become an upstream finding or Change Proposal.

### 3.3 Book 03

MarkReg must hand approved actions into governed Execution.

It may define Product readiness, packages and requested actions. It may not create a parallel Execution authority, bypass idempotency, or treat technical success as official effect.

### 3.4 Book 04

MarkReg must remain a Product within the Workplace and Product Architecture.

It may appear standalone or embedded, but organization identity, permission, responsibility, private Knowledge and operating autonomy remain governed by the appropriate Workplace context.

---

## 4. Downstream Relationships

Future publications and specifications may extend MarkReg without absorbing it.

### 4.1 MarkOrbit Lite

Lite may provide:

- personal or lightweight Workplace context;
- local database and case library;
- personal Knowledge and reusable context;
- a lightweight MarkReg entry surface.

Lite does not change MarkReg’s professional and external-action boundaries.

### 4.2 Mark Global Service Network

MGSN may provide:

- provider identity and capability evidence;
- trust and relationship signals;
- private-first discovery and routing support;
- accepted service connections.

MGSN does not automatically select or appoint providers for MarkReg.

### 4.3 Horizontal capabilities

Crawl, Distillery, Brain, Intelligence, Artifact and other horizontal capabilities may support source ingestion, Knowledge, analysis, rendering and learning.

They remain capabilities or systems with their own contracts. They do not become the MarkReg Product constitution.

## 5. Conformance Profiles

Not every implementation must launch the entire lifecycle at once.

A conforming implementation should declare a profile.

| Profile | Minimum scope |
| --- | --- |
| Foundation | Product constitution, roles, state distinctions and authority boundaries |
| Guided Decision | Need Brief through Proposal and explainable options |
| Commercial Intake | Quote, acceptance, Intake, Requirement Set, Readiness and Handoff |
| Filing Preparation | Filing Package, Review, client confirmation and Filing Approval |
| Governed Filing | accepted provider or connector route, submission and acknowledgement |
| Post-Filing | official events, deadlines, response and outcome Communication |
| Portfolio Continuity | registration, maintenance, renewal, recordal and portfolio views |
| Full-Lifecycle | all prior profiles plus cross-stage continuity and Product evolution controls |

A profile must state exclusions clearly.

A partial profile may be conforming if it does not claim unsupported stages.

---

## 6. Conformance Statement

An edition or implementation should publish a conformance statement containing:

- Product and version;
- supported profile;
- supported jurisdictions and services;
- relevant Jurisdiction Pack versions;
- supported participants and surfaces;
- Owning Services and Execution dependencies;
- provider and connector routes;
- excluded lifecycle stages;
- known limitations;
- required Human roles;
- evaluation and validation status;
- production and external-action authority status.

Marketing language must not exceed the conformance statement.

---

## 7. Mandatory Constitutional Rules

The reusable MarkReg rules remain mandatory:

1. recommendation is not decision;
2. Product readiness is not approval;
3. approval is version- and purpose-specific;
4. approval is not Execution;
5. provider recommendation is not appointment;
6. sent is not officially acknowledged;
7. Product projection is not official truth without source;
8. AI does not replace accountable Human Judgment.

A future edition may add rules. It may not silently weaken these rules.

---

A conforming full filing journey should preserve the lineage:

→ Proposal
→ Quote
→ Commercial Instruction
→ Formal Intake
→ Requirement Set

Alternative services may use different subsets, but equivalent stages must remain distinct where their meaning differs.

---

## 9. Required State Integrity

MarkReg must preserve distinctions among:

- Product-local candidate state;
- formal Order and Matter state;
- Execution state;
- provider-reported state;
- official status;
- professional interpretation;
- client-facing projection;
- outcome and continuing obligation.

One convenient `status` field must not replace these dimensions.

---

## 10. Required Source Integrity

A material recommendation, rule, deadline, fee, official event or outcome should be traceable to:

- source;
- source type;
- checked or retrieved time;
- effective date where relevant;
- jurisdiction and service scope;
- applicable Pack or rule version;
- professional verification status;
- supersession history.

Unknown and conflicting source conditions must remain visible.

---

## 11. Required Human Authority

Conforming operation requires eligible Humans for roles such as:

- professional judgment;
- Review;
- client decision;
- internal approval;
- Filing Approval;
- provider selection and appointment;
- settlement authority;
- deadline responsibility;
- official-outcome verification;
- rule and Pack approval.

The same person may hold several roles where permitted, but the actions remain separately recorded.

---

## 12. Required Failure and Correction Behavior

A conforming Product must support:

- stale data detection;
- version conflict;
- missing information;
- approval expiry;
- provider decline and change proposal;
- technical failure and unknown state;
- reconciliation before unsafe retry;
- duplicate prevention;
- corrected official source;
- corrected client Communication;
- chain-of-title gap;
- missed or disputed rule update;
- rollback or disable capability for unsafe features.

Success-path demos alone do not establish conformance.

---

## 13. Upstream Finding Control

Implementation or publication may reveal an upstream issue.

An Upstream Finding should record:

- affected book or architecture authority;
- observed gap;
- evidence;
- why Book 05 cannot resolve it locally;
- affected Product behavior;
- temporary bounded handling;
- proposed owner;
- decision and status.

MarkReg may document the finding. It may not silently amend a frozen or owner-accepted upstream baseline.

---

## 14. Product Change Categories

Future MarkReg changes should be classified as:

| Category | Examples | Typical control |
| --- | --- | --- |
| editorial | clarity, examples, terminology | editorial review |
| Product behavior | questions, options, warnings, surfaces | Product change review and scenarios |
| jurisdiction rule | fee, form, deadline, document rule | Pack change workflow |
| organization overlay | review policy, provider preference | Workplace policy control |
| Core semantic | shared object or contract | Book 02 Change Proposal |
| Execution behavior | protected action, idempotency, retry | Book 03 governance |
| architecture | Product or Workplace authority | architecture decision |
| implementation | code, infrastructure, connector | separate implementation task and repository |

Classification prevents local convenience from changing constitutional meaning.

## 16. Roadmap Horizons

### Horizon 1 — Product-loop MVP

Prove a guided filing decision and governed Handoff for a controlled service and jurisdiction set.

### Horizon 2 — Filing preparation and execution continuity

Add package preparation, approval, provider collaboration, acknowledgement and safe recovery.

### Horizon 3 — Post-filing professional journeys

Add official events, deadlines, examination responses and selected dispute workflows.

### Horizon 4 — Registration and portfolio continuity

Add Right Baseline, maintenance, renewal, recordal, chain of title and portfolio action planning.

### Horizon 5 — Embedded and networked ecosystem

Deepen Lite, Workplace and MGSN integration through typed Handoffs, private relationships and controlled learning.

### Horizon 6 — Broader jurisdiction intelligence

Expand source-backed Packs and approved connectors only where maintenance and professional ownership are sustainable.

The roadmap should advance by validated constraint, not by feature count.

## 21. Conformance Scenarios

### Scenario A — partial implementation claim

**Given** an implementation supports Guided Decision and Commercial Intake only  
**When** it publishes a conformance statement  
**Then** it may claim those profiles  
**But** may not claim full-lifecycle operation.

### Scenario B — local Core redefinition

**Given** implementation needs a shared object not defined upstream  
**When** the team is tempted to add a conflicting local meaning  
**Then** MarkReg records an Upstream Finding  
**And** does not silently amend Book 02.

### Scenario C — production inference

**Given** Book 05 reaches a Release Candidate  
**When** a stakeholder asks whether external filing is authorized  
**Then** the answer remains no unless a separate implementation and authority gate has passed.

### Scenario D — marketing overclaim

**Given** an MVP supports three jurisdictions and new filings  
**When** marketing describes it as complete global trademark automation  
**Then** the claim conflicts with the conformance statement and must be corrected.

### Scenario E — future rule weakening

**Given** a future edition proposes automatic Filing Approval for low-risk cases  
**When** the change removes accountable Human authority  
**Then** it is non-conforming unless the upstream architecture and constitutional rules are formally changed.

## 24. Conclusion

MarkReg should make international trademark work feel simpler without pretending the underlying rights, jurisdictions, participants and responsibilities are simple.

Its value comes from preserving important distinctions while reducing unnecessary burden:

```text
fewer questions
without missing material facts

clearer recommendations
without hiding uncertainty

faster preparation
without bypassing Review

connected providers
without creating a public marketplace

AI assistance
without replacing professional responsibility

one portfolio experience
without inventing one global right
```

The long-term direction is not fully autonomous filing.

It is a professional Product that makes complex trademark journeys understandable, governable, reusable and continuously improvable while keeping authority where it belongs.
