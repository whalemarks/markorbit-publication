# B05-CH-47 — Conformance, Roadmap and Conclusion

**Status:** Complete Draft 1  
**Chapter Map:** B05-TOC-V0.1 — Owner Accepted  
**Part:** Part VII — Product Experience and Evolution

## Chapter Purpose

Book 05 defines the MarkReg Product constitution and full-lifecycle contracts. It does not authorize implementation, production or an External Protected Action.

```text
Books 01–04 Portfolio Baseline
→ Book 05 Product constitution
→ controlled records and scenarios
→ MR-G10 Conformance Statement
→ MR-D13 Pilot or Release Decision
→ separate implementation and operating authority
```

`EL-40` states what a future edition or implementation may claim and what must remain true as MarkReg evolves.

## 1. MarkReg Defined

MarkReg is the focused international trademark Product that helps clients, professionals and organizations move from a complex business need to explainable options, governed professional work, sourced official outcomes and continuing portfolio Decisions.

It provides:

- need understanding before forms;
- jurisdiction, route, filing-unit, class and scope recommendations;
- commercial transparency and service-specific Intake;
- versioned Packages, Human Review and accountable approval;
- provider and governed Execution handoffs;
- sourced examination, publication, dispute and remedy journeys;
- registration, maintenance, renewal, recordal and title continuity;
- independent-right portfolio views;
- standalone, embedded and cross-Product experience;
- Pack-bound AI assistance and controlled learning.

## 2. Constitutional Boundary

MarkReg is not the whole MarkOrbit Operating System, Frozen Core, Execution System, professional Workplace, public provider marketplace, official office, universal legal database or autonomous professional actor.

It does not guarantee registration or legal outcome. It does not own every formal business fact. It does not grant production, filing, payment, settlement or other protected authority.

## 3. Upstream Conformance

A conforming MarkReg edition preserves:

- **Book 01:** organization independence, professional responsibility and governed collaboration;
- **Book 02:** shared Core semantics rather than local redefinition;
- **Book 03:** governed Execution, idempotency, evidence and safe recovery;
- **Book 04:** Product and Workplace boundaries, identity, permission and responsibility.

A missing or conflicting upstream contract becomes an Upstream Finding or Change Proposal. Book 05 may not silently amend an owner-accepted or frozen baseline.

## 4. Downstream and Horizontal Relationships

Lite may provide personal or lightweight Workplace context and a local case library. MGSN may provide private-first provider identity and capability evidence. Crawl, Distillery, Brain, Intelligence, Artifact and other capabilities may support source ingestion, Knowledge, analysis, rendering or learning.

These systems keep their own contracts. They do not become the MarkReg constitution or automatically acquire MarkReg authority.

## 5. Conformance Profiles

| Profile | Minimum declared scope |
| --- | --- |
| Foundation | Product constitution, roles, state and authority boundaries |
| Guided Decision | Need Brief through explainable Recommendation, Option Set and Proposal |
| Commercial Intake | Quote, Client Acceptance, Intake, Requirements, Readiness and Handoff |
| Filing Preparation | Package Candidate, Review, client confirmation and Filing Approval |
| Governed Filing | accepted provider or execution route, submission evidence and acknowledgement |
| Post-Filing | official events, deadlines, response, dispute and Communication controls |
| Portfolio Continuity | registration, maintenance, renewal, recordal, title and portfolio views |
| Full-Lifecycle | all prior profiles plus cross-stage continuity and Product evolution controls |

A partial profile may conform when its exclusions are explicit. It may not market itself as Full-Lifecycle.

A Profile claim cannot exceed:

- implemented controlled records and surfaces;
- passed scenario evidence;
- active Pack support states;
- available Human roles and Owning Services;
- provider, connector and operational capability;
- production and protected-action authority actually granted.

## 6. MR-G10 Conformance Statement

A Conformance Statement identifies:

- Product edition and implementation version;
- supported Profile;
- jurisdictions, services and lifecycle stages;
- Pack versions and module support states;
- participants and surfaces;
- Owning Service and Execution dependencies;
- provider, connector and manual routes;
- excluded stages and known limitations;
- required Human roles;
- scenario and evaluation evidence;
- unresolved findings;
- publication, implementation, production and External Protected Action status.

Marketing language must not exceed this statement.

## 7. Mandatory Constitutional Rules

A conforming edition preserves `MR-CR-01–MR-CR-08`:

```text
Recommendation ≠ Decision
Readiness ≠ approval
Approval ≠ Execution
Submission ≠ official acknowledgement
Product projection ≠ Official Truth
AI assistance ≠ accountable Human Review
Product-local record ≠ formal shared object
Material record → source, version, responsibility and supersession
```

A later edition may add controls. It may not silently weaken these rules.

## 8. Required Record and State Integrity

A conforming implementation preserves the material lineage needed for its Profile and distinguishes:

- Product-local candidate and Decision state;
- formal Order, Matter, Document, Review, Approval and Communication state;
- Book 03 Execution state;
- provider report and Provider Acceptance;
- official status and official evidence;
- professional interpretation;
- client-facing projection;
- continuing obligation and portfolio action.

One convenient `status` field cannot replace these dimensions.

## 9. Required Source, Human and Failure Controls

Material recommendations, Rules, deadlines, fees and official outcomes remain traceable to source, retrieval or checked time, effective date where relevant, jurisdiction and service scope, Pack version, professional verification and supersession history.

Eligible Humans remain accountable for professional judgment, client Decisions, Review, approval, provider selection, settlement authority, deadline responsibility, official verification and Pack release.

A conforming Product supports stale-data detection, version conflict, approval expiry, provider change, unknown-state reconciliation, duplicate prevention, corrected sources and Communications, title gaps, Rule change and rollback or disable behavior.

## 10. Controlled Scenarios

- `MR-SCN-39` — a partial implementation cannot claim Full-Lifecycle Conformance;
- `MR-SCN-40` — publication or Release Candidate status cannot be treated as production or protected-action authority;
- `MR-SCN-41` — a local implementation cannot redefine Book 02 Core semantics.

Success-path demonstrations alone do not establish Conformance.

## 12. Publication, Implementation and Operating Gates

```text
Complete Draft or Release Candidate
≠ final publication
≠ implementation approval
≠ software release
≠ production readiness
≠ External Protected Action authority
```

`MR-D13 Pilot or Release Decision` records only the exact edition, Profile, scope, evidence, limitations and authority granted.

Implementation requires separate requirements, data and API contracts, permission model, UI specifications, connector contracts, security and privacy design, evaluation plans, rollout controls and implementation pull requests citing the Book 05 baseline.

## 13. Roadmap Horizons

1. **Guided Product loop:** Need, options, commercial clarity and governed Handoff.
2. **Filing preparation:** Package, Review, approval, provider collaboration and acknowledgement.
3. **Post-filing journeys:** official events, deadlines, responses and selected disputes.
4. **Portfolio continuity:** Right Baseline, maintenance, renewal, recordal, title and portfolio action.
5. **Embedded ecosystem:** Lite, Workplace and MGSN through typed Handoffs and private relationships.
6. **Broader jurisdiction intelligence:** sustainable source-backed Packs and approved execution routes.

Expansion follows validated constraints, not feature count.

## 14. Reference-Journey Final Positions

```text
Japan and Australia — future-action candidates only
```

One portfolio view explains these states without inventing one global outcome.

### RIVERKITE

```text
six independent registration histories
four ordinary renewal workflows
one ownership-linked renewal workflow
one cancellation-defense right
open use-Evidence and licence actions
```

Contractual and official ownership remain distinct until sourced recordals close the gaps.

## 15. Book 05 Conclusion

MarkReg is valuable only if it makes a difficult trademark journey understandable without making it deceptively simple.

The Product may reduce questions, reuse verified context, assist professionals, connect providers and improve continuity. It must still preserve source authority, independent jurisdiction outcomes, accountable Human Decisions, exact-version approvals, governed Execution, official evidence and continuing obligations.

The conclusion of Book 05 is therefore a control statement:

```text
Make the journey easier to understand.
Do not make authority, uncertainty or official truth disappear.
```
