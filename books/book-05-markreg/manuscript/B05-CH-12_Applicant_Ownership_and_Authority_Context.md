# B05-CH-12 — Applicant, Ownership and Authority Context

**Status:** Part II Draft  
**Chapter Map:** B05-TOC-V0.1  
**Part:** Part II — Need, Strategy and Recommendation

## Chapter Purpose

CH11 defined the filing unit as the proposed mark representation that may need separate evaluation, pricing, Review, and filing.

The next question is not merely:

```text
What name should appear in the applicant field?
```

It is:

> Which legal person should own the proposed right, what is the ownership and route rationale, who has authority to instruct and sign, and which facts must be verified before the Product may proceed?

The client, applicant, payer, user, signatory, brand operator, parent company, IP holding company, and current owner may be different persons.

The central proposition is:

```text
Applicant Context
=
Verified Legal Identity
+ Ownership Rationale
+ Group and Relationship Context
+ Existing-Right Alignment
+ Route and Priority Eligibility
+ Authority to Instruct
+ Authority to Sign
+ Representative Authority
+ Source and Version
+ Unresolved Change or Conflict
+ Human Review
```

The Product output is a versioned **Applicant and Authority Context Candidate**.

```text
Client
≠ Applicant
≠ Current Owner
≠ Payer
≠ Signatory
≠ Representative
≠ Authorized Instructor
```

---

## 1. Applicant Is a Legal Role in a Specific Filing

The applicant is the person identified as seeking the right in a particular application.

The Product should not choose the applicant merely from:

- the logged-in user;
- the billing contact;
- the client record;
- the company website;
- the payer;
- the prior Matter;
- the organization that created the journey.

Each may be relevant.

None is automatically the applicant.

---

## 2. The Client Relationship and Applicant Identity Must Remain Distinct

A professional organization may serve:

- a parent company;
- a subsidiary;
- a founder;
- an IP holding company;
- a distributor;
- a licensee;
- a joint venture.

The client may instruct work for another legal person.

MarkReg should preserve:

```text
Client relationship holder
Legal applicant
Beneficiary or business owner
Payer
Instruction source
```

This supports both professional responsibility and accurate formalization.

---

## 3. Applicant Identity Requires Exact Legal Information

Relevant identity fields may include:

- legal name;
- previous legal name;
- legal form;
- jurisdiction of formation;
- registration number where relevant;
- registered address;
- principal business address;
- nationality or domicile where relevant;
- language;
- group affiliation;
- status;
- source document;
- effective date.

The Product should distinguish user-entered text from verified legal identity.

---

## 4. Display Name Is Not Legal Name

A company may use:

- English trading name;
- local-language name;
- abbreviated name;
- former name;
- brand name;
- marketplace seller name;
- registered legal name.

MarkReg should not replace the legal applicant with the most visible commercial name.

The exact legal name may affect filing, priority, assignment, and later recordals.

---

## 5. Entity Type May Affect the Journey

Relevant entity types may include:

- corporation;
- limited company;
- partnership;
- individual;
- nonprofit;
- public body;
- association;
- trust or other locally recognized structure.

The Product should use jurisdiction-specific Knowledge where entity classification affects:

- application data;
- documents;
- signatory;
- route eligibility;
- fees;
- representation;
- ownership.

It should not invent a universal entity taxonomy that overrides official requirements.

---

## 6. Ownership Rationale Should Be Explicit

Possible rationales include:

- operating company owns and uses the brand;
- parent company centralizes IP ownership;
- IP holding company licenses use;
- founder owns before incorporation;
- joint venture owns the local brand;
- acquirer will own after completion;
- distributor owns under a disputed or temporary arrangement;
- current rights remain with a predecessor.

MarkReg should record why the proposed applicant is being considered.

A preferred name without rationale may hide future chain-of-title risk.

---

## 7. Group Structure Does Not Automatically Transfer Rights

Companies in one corporate group remain separate legal persons.

The Product should not infer:

```text
Same group
therefore
same applicant or owner
```

Relevant group context may support a recommendation.

Formal ownership still depends on the actual legal person and applicable documents.

---

## 8. Existing Rights Must Be Aligned with the Proposed Applicant

The Product should compare:

- current owner of existing rights;
- proposed applicant;
- priority claimant;
- owner of the basic application or registration;
- owner of related local rights;
- pending assignment;
- group relationship;
- licence arrangement.

Misalignment may affect:

- route eligibility;
- priority;
- maintenance;
- enforcement;
- evidence;
- portfolio coherence.

The Product should show the mismatch before the filing strategy is finalized.

---

## 9. Pending Corporate Changes Must Be Visible

Applicant context may be affected by:

- incorporation;
- merger;
- acquisition;
- name change;
- address change;
- conversion;
- dissolution;
- assignment;
- restructuring;
- change of representative.

MarkReg should identify whether the proposed filing should occur:

- before the change;
- after the change;
- with a later recordal;
- after professional review.

It should not silently assume the change is complete.

---

## 10. Priority Claimant Must Be Evaluated Separately

A proposed applicant may wish to claim priority from an earlier application.

The Product should compare:

- earlier applicant;
- proposed applicant;
- relationship;
- transfer of priority rights where applicable;
- filing date;
- mark identity;
- scope;
- supporting documents.

The existence of a related earlier filing does not automatically create a valid priority claim.

---

## 11. Route Eligibility May Depend on Applicant Context

International or regional routes may depend on factors such as:

- nationality;
- domicile;
- establishment;
- existing application or registration;
- ownership alignment;
- membership in the relevant system.

MarkReg may prepare an eligibility candidate.

It should preserve the source and checked date.

Final acceptance remains external.

---

## 12. Applicant and Filing Unit Must Be Evaluated Together

The same applicant may not be appropriate for every filing unit.

Examples:

- parent owns the house mark;
- subsidiary owns the product line;
- joint venture owns the local-language mark;
- founder owns a legacy logo;
- acquirer will own a future version.

MarkReg should not apply one applicant to every unit merely for operational convenience.

---

## 13. Applicant and Goods/Services Scope Must Be Coherent

The proposed applicant should have a plausible relationship to:

- current use;
- intended use;
- licensing;
- manufacturing;
- sales;
- group structure;
- evidence;
- maintenance duties.

The Product should not make a definitive legal-use determination automatically.

It should flag incoherence for professional Review.

---

## 14. Authority to Instruct Is Not the Same as Applicant Identity

A person may instruct because they are:

- company director;
- legal team member;
- authorized employee;
- external counsel;
- agent;
- founder;
- group representative.

The Product should record:

- who gave the instruction;
- for which organization;
- under what role;
- with which evidence or relationship;
- which actions the authority covers.

A user account alone is not evidence of authority.

---

## 15. Authority to Sign Is a Separate Question

The signatory may be:

- director;
- officer;
- authorized employee;
- legal representative;
- external attorney;
- other permitted person.

The Product should distinguish:

```text
Authority to provide information
Authority to instruct
Authority to approve cost
Authority to sign
Authority to file
```

One person may hold several authorities.

They should not be assumed.

---

## 16. Power of Attorney Does Not Create Unlimited Authority

A POA may be:

- general;
- specific;
- jurisdiction-specific;
- service-specific;
- time-limited;
- revocable;
- subject to formalities;
- pending signature;
- superseded.

MarkReg should preserve:

- principal;
- representative;
- scope;
- effective date;
- expiry;
- signature status;
- notarization or legalization status;
- language;
- source version.

The presence of a POA file does not prove it is valid for the current filing.

---

## 17. Representative Authority Must Be Purpose-Bound

A local representative may be authorized for:

- one application;
- one jurisdiction;
- one service;
- one portfolio;
- prosecution;
- opposition;
- maintenance;
- recordal.

Provider participation does not automatically create representative authority.

Appointment, instruction, acceptance, and formal representation remain separate.

---

## 18. Payer and Applicant May Differ

Payment may come from:

- applicant;
- parent;
- agency;
- distributor;
- group company;
- third-party service purchaser.

The Product should preserve payer context for commercial workflow.

It should not rewrite applicant identity to match the payer.

---

## 19. Ownership Dispute Requires an Honest Stop

Warning signs include:

- two parties claim ownership;
- former employee or founder controls the mark;
- distributor filed first;
- assignment is unsigned;
- corporate records conflict;
- brand use and registration ownership diverge;
- divorce, inheritance, insolvency, or acquisition affects ownership;
- client instruction conflicts with official records.

MarkReg should stop automatic recommendation and escalate.

It should not resolve an ownership dispute through interface defaults.

---

## 20. Reusable Applicant Profiles Require Current Verification

A saved applicant profile may reduce repeated entry.

The Product should show:

- last verified date;
- source;
- legal name;
- address;
- entity status;
- known changes;
- unresolved warnings.

Reuse should trigger confirmation when the information is material to the current filing.

```text
Reusable
≠ timeless
```

---

## 21. Source Hierarchy Matters

Possible sources include:

- official corporate registry;
- formation document;
- certificate;
- existing official trademark record;
- executed agreement;
- signed client instruction;
- verified organization record;
- provider report;
- website;
- user statement;
- AI inference.

The Product should identify the source and authority level.

A website footer is not equivalent to an official registry.

---

## 22. Applicant Data May Be Sensitive

Applicant context may contain:

- personal address;
- identity document;
- ownership structure;
- acquisition plan;
- dispute;
- signature;
- corporate records;
- tax information;
- private agreement.

Access should remain purpose-limited.

The Product should not expose the complete context to a provider or network participant unless required and authorized.

---

## 23. Cross-Orbit Sharing Must Be Minimal

When MarkReg requests provider support, it may initially share:

- jurisdiction;
- service;
- applicant type;
- basic eligibility question;
- conflict-check identifiers where authorized.

It should not automatically share:

- full portfolio;
- private transaction;
- internal ownership rationale;
- unrelated documents;
- client budget;
- all group entities.

Information sharing should increase only with purpose and authority.

---

## 24. Applicant Context Is Versioned

Material changes include:

- legal name;
- entity type;
- address;
- ownership;
- group structure;
- signatory;
- authority;
- representative;
- assignment status;
- route eligibility.

A filing package reviewed against version A should not silently use version B.

The Product should identify affected recommendations and approvals.

---

## 25. Human Review Is Required at Consequential Boundaries

Professional Review may be required when:

- applicant differs from current owner;
- priority is claimed;
- route eligibility depends on establishment or domicile;
- group ownership is complex;
- a pending transaction exists;
- signatory authority is unclear;
- POA validity is uncertain;
- official and client records conflict;
- ownership is disputed;
- maintenance or use consequences may be material.

MarkReg may assemble the context.

It does not make the final professional judgment automatically.

---

A useful candidate may contain:

```text

Exact legal identity
Entity type
Formation jurisdiction
Addresses

Ownership rationale
Group relationship

Priority alignment

Authority to sign
Representative and POA context
Pending changes
Contradictions
Sensitivity
Required Review
Version
```

This is Product preparation.

Formal applicant, representative, Order, Matter, and filing facts remain service-owned.

---

## 27. Approval Must Be Version- and Purpose-Specific

An applicant approval should identify:

- exact legal person;
- filing unit;
- jurisdictions;
- route;
- purpose;
- version;
- unresolved limitations.

Approval for a quote does not automatically approve the applicant for filing.

Approval for one jurisdiction does not automatically approve all jurisdictions.

---

## 28. Failure Modes to Reject

```text
Logged-in user treated as applicant
Client treated as applicant automatically
Payer treated as applicant
Trading name treated as legal name
Group relationship treated as ownership transfer
Saved applicant reused without verification
POA file treated as universally valid
Provider access treated as representative appointment
Signatory inferred from email sender
Priority assumed because an earlier filing exists
Pending assignment treated as completed
Ownership dispute resolved by Product default
Applicant data exposed beyond purpose
```

---

## 29. Minimum Applicant and Authority Lock

```text
Applicant is a specific legal person
in a specific filing context.

Client, owner, payer, signatory,
representative, and instructor remain distinct.

Legal identity is sourced and verified.

Ownership rationale remains visible.

Existing rights, priority, route,
and filing unit are aligned.

Authority to instruct, approve, sign,
and file remains purpose-specific.

Pending change and conflict remain visible.

The Product prepares context.

Human professionals review consequential questions.

Formal services record the final business facts.
```

---

## 30. Transition to Classification Recommendation

Once MarkReg understands:

- the business need;
- jurisdictions and routes;
- filing units;
- proposed applicant and authority;

it may analyze the commercial scope that the filing should cover.

CH13 begins with class recommendation.

The next question is:

```text
Which classes are relevant to the actual business,
which are primary or adjacent,
and what uncertainty remains before item-level drafting?
```
