# B05-CH-11 — Mark Identity, Filing Units and Variants

**Status:** Part II Draft  
**Chapter Map:** B05-TOC-V0.1  
**Part:** Part II — Need, Strategy and Recommendation

## Chapter Purpose

CH08–CH10 established the Need Brief, jurisdiction strategy, and portfolio scenarios.

The next Product question is:

> What exactly is the organization trying to protect, and how many distinct filing units does that objective require?

A brand is not automatically one mark.

A logo file is not automatically the complete filing subject.

A family of marks is not automatically one application.

The central proposition is:

```text
Filing Unit Candidate
=
Brand Context
+ Exact Mark Representation
+ Mark Type
+ Version
+ Wording and Visual Elements
+ Language, Transliteration, and Translation
+ Color and Other Claims
+ Jurisdiction Context
+ Existing Rights
+ Intended Use
+ Applicant Context
+ Professional Review
```

The output is a versioned **Mark Filing Unit Candidate**.

```text
Brand
≠ Mark
≠ Filing Unit
≠ Application
≠ Registration
```

---

## 1. Brand Is the Commercial Identity; Mark Is the Filing Subject

A brand may include:

- company name;
- product name;
- word mark;
- logo;
- packaging;
- slogan;
- color;
- mascot;
- sound;
- local-language name;
- abbreviation;
- product shape;
- domain name;
- social handle.

A trademark filing protects a defined mark representation within a defined legal and procedural context.

MarkReg should preserve the relationship between the broader Brand and the specific filing unit.

---

## 2. One Brand May Produce Several Filing Units

Examples:

```text
Brand:
ORBITA

Possible filing units:
- ORBITA word mark
- ORBITA stylized logo
- Chinese-character version
- ORBITA + device composite
- slogan
- product-shape mark
```

The Product should not assume that all units are required.

It should explain:

- why a unit may be useful;
- which jurisdiction or use supports it;
- whether the unit already exists;
- whether a separate application is likely;
- which facts remain uncertain.

---

## 3. Filing Unit Is a Product Decision Concept

A filing unit means:

> a proposed mark representation that may need to be evaluated, priced, reviewed, and filed separately or through a jurisdiction-specific mechanism.

It is useful for Product planning.

It is not automatically a new Core Object.

The formal Trademark, application, Document, or other record remains governed by the relevant Owning Service.

---

## 4. The Exact Representation Must Be Preserved

For each candidate, MarkReg should preserve:

- exact wording;
- exact image;
- file hash or version reference;
- dimensions and format where relevant;
- claimed colors;
- disclaimed elements where applicable;
- description;
- language;
- transliteration;
- translation;
- date received;
- source;
- actor who supplied it.

A thumbnail alone is insufficient for high-consequence review.

---

## 5. Word Marks and Device Marks Serve Different Strategies

A word mark may support protection of the wording across changing presentation.

A device or stylized mark may support protection of a particular visual form.

A composite mark may combine both.

The Product should explain that these are different filing units and may create different:

- scope;
- evidence;
- examination;
- use;
- maintenance;
- enforcement;
- cost.

The Product should not state that one is always better.

---

## 6. Composite Marks Must Be Decomposed for Understanding

A composite mark may contain:

- word element;
- device;
- slogan;
- color;
- non-Latin characters;
- descriptive wording;
- common symbols.

MarkReg may identify the components to support:

- search;
- description;
- transliteration;
- disclaimer analysis;
- separate filing recommendations;
- similarity review.

Component extraction is analysis.

It does not change the exact mark representation.

---

## 7. Image Extraction Is Not Mark Identity

AI or image tools may detect:

- text;
- shapes;
- color;
- layout;
- likely logo elements;
- duplicate images.

The extracted text may be wrong.

The Product should preserve:

```text
Original image
AI-extracted text
User-confirmed wording
Professional interpretation
```

These states must remain distinct.

---

## 8. Version Control Is Essential

A mark may change during:

- design refinement;
- localization;
- rebranding;
- packaging change;
- client correction;
- provider preparation;
- image conversion;
- color adjustment.

Every material change may affect:

- search;
- filing scope;
- priority;
- approval;
- use evidence;
- provider instruction;
- official submission.

The Product should never overwrite the reviewed mark with a later file silently.

---

## 9. Material and Non-Material Changes Must Be Distinguished

Examples of potentially material change:

- changed wording;
- added or removed device;
- changed character;
- new slogan;
- changed composition;
- changed claimed color;
- different transliteration.

Examples of potentially non-material technical change:

- higher-resolution copy;
- background transparency;
- file-format conversion;
- crop correction that does not alter the mark.

Whether a change is legally material may require professional review.

The Product may flag the difference.

It should not decide conclusively without authority.

---

## 10. Color Strategy Must Be Explicit

The Product should identify:

- color version;
- black-and-white version;
- grayscale version;
- claimed color;
- unclaimed color;
- jurisdiction-specific practice;
- actual use;
- design consistency.

A color image is not automatically a formal color claim.

The recommendation should preserve the source of the decision.

---

## 11. Local-Language Versions May Be Separate Marks

A brand may use:

- original language;
- translated name;
- transliteration;
- phonetic approximation;
- local nickname;
- character mark;
- bilingual composite.

The Product should ask:

- Is the local version used or planned?
- Was it selected by the organization?
- Is the translation literal or adapted?
- Could consumers create an unofficial name?
- Is the wording already filed?
- Is separate protection useful?
- Does the jurisdiction require or prefer specific representation?

A machine translation should not become the official filing mark automatically.

---

## 12. Transliteration and Translation Must Remain Distinct

```text
Transliteration
→ represents sound across scripts

Translation
→ represents meaning across languages
```

A mark may require both.

The Product should preserve:

- original text;
- target script;
- transliteration standard;
- translation;
- source;
- reviewer;
- ambiguity.

An unreviewed transliteration may create a different commercial identity.

---

## 13. Series and Family Marks Require Explicit Strategy

An organization may use:

- numbered variants;
- product-line variants;
- prefix or suffix families;
- shared logo system;
- seasonal versions;
- co-branded marks.

MarkReg should identify:

- common element;
- changing element;
- exact variants;
- intended use;
- existing rights;
- filing priority;
- cost consequence.

It should not assume that a family relationship creates one filing.

---

## 14. Slogans and Secondary Elements Need Independent Review

A slogan may be:

- core brand element;
- temporary campaign;
- descriptive wording;
- separate mark;
- composite component.

The Product should avoid recommending a separate filing merely because text appears in an image.

It should ask whether the slogan has independent commercial significance and expected duration.

---

## 15. Product Shapes, Packaging, Sound, and Other Non-Traditional Marks Need Specialized Paths

Non-traditional marks may require:

- specific representation;
- description;
- technical file;
- distinctiveness analysis;
- evidence;
- jurisdiction-specific eligibility;
- specialized search;
- professional Review.

MarkReg may identify the candidate.

It should route unsupported types to specialized guidance rather than force them into a word-or-logo form.

---

## 16. Intended Use Helps Determine the Filing Unit

Relevant use may include:

- standalone word use;
- logo-only use;
- word and logo together;
- packaging use;
- app icon;
- storefront;
- product shape;
- advertising slogan;
- local-language use.

The Product should compare intended use with the proposed filing unit.

A mark never intended for use may create maintenance or evidence risk in some contexts.

---

## 17. Existing Rights Should Be Matched by Exact Unit

A portfolio may contain rights for:

- old logo;
- current word mark;
- different owner;
- different language;
- different color;
- composite only;
- limited goods/services;
- expired or cancelled unit.

MarkReg should not show “already protected” solely because the brand name appears in the portfolio.

It should identify which filing unit the existing right actually covers.

---

## 18. Filing Unit and Applicant Context Are Linked

The same brand may be owned or used by:

- parent company;
- operating company;
- IP holding company;
- licensee;
- joint venture;
- founder;
- distributor.

The Product should not finalize the filing-unit recommendation without considering who should own and use it.

CH12 defines applicant and authority context.

---

## 19. Filing Unit and Priority Context Are Linked

A proposed unit may claim priority only if the applicable requirements are satisfied.

MarkReg should compare:

- earlier mark;
- proposed mark;
- applicant;
- filing date;
- scope;
- route;
- source documents.

Brand similarity is not automatically sufficient.

The Product should flag the need for professional assessment.

---

## 20. Filing Unit and Search Strategy Are Linked

Search may differ for:

- word mark;
- device mark;
- transliteration;
- translation;
- composite mark;
- local-language mark;
- slogan;
- non-traditional mark.

The Product should not reuse one search result across materially different units without explanation.

CH15 defines search modes and risk assessment.

---

## 21. Filing Unit and Classification Are Linked

Different units may cover:

- identical business scope;
- different product lines;
- different services;
- different markets;
- different licensing programs.

The Product should allow shared class recommendations while preserving unit-specific differences.

One class set should not be copied blindly to every mark variant.

---

## 22. Multiple Filing Units Should Be Compared Transparently

A comparison may show:

| Filing unit | Strategic role | Existing right | Search need | Estimated extra cost | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Word mark | core name | none | high | medium | high |
| Current logo | visual identity | old version only | medium | medium | high |
| Local-language mark | local market | none | high | medium | medium |
| Slogan | campaign | none | low | medium | low |

The dimensions should be adapted to the organization.

---

## 23. The Product May Recommend Consolidation

A filing unit may be unnecessary when:

- the element is temporary;
- the difference is purely technical;
- an existing right is sufficient;
- the business will not use the variant;
- the unit creates low value relative to cost;
- the mark is not final;
- ownership is unresolved.

A no-filing recommendation is valid.

---

## 24. The Product May Recommend Separation

Separate filing units may be useful when:

- the word and logo have independent value;
- local-language use is material;
- several logos are materially different;
- a slogan has independent recognition;
- a mark type requires distinct representation;
- different owners or product lines are involved;
- a route or jurisdiction requires separate treatment.

The recommendation should explain the consequence and cost.

---

## 25. User Selection Is Not Final Mark Approval

The user may select:

- one filing unit;
- several units;
- a preferred logo;
- a local-language version;
- a color strategy.

Selection does not prove:

- exact file correctness;
- legal ownership;
- priority alignment;
- registrability;
- professional approval;
- filing readiness.

The reviewed version must remain identifiable.

---

## 26. Review Must Apply to the Exact Version

A professional Review should identify:

- filing-unit identifier;
- exact image or wording;
- version;
- description;
- translation and transliteration;
- claimed features;
- intended jurisdictions;
- intended scope;
- applicant context;
- review purpose.

Approval of version A does not approve version B.

---

## 27. Mark Filing Unit Candidate Structure

A useful candidate may contain:

```text
Brand reference
Filing-unit identifier
Mark type
Exact representation reference
Wording
Device description
Language
Transliteration
Translation
Color information
Version
Source
Intended use
Jurisdiction relevance
Existing-right references
Applicant assumption
Priority context
Search requirement
Class/scope assumption
Recommendation rationale
Uncertainty
Required Review
```

This remains Product-owned planning state until formalized through the appropriate service.

---

## 28. Mark Asset Storage and Formal Document Status Must Remain Distinct

An uploaded logo may be:

- source Asset;
- Product input;
- draft Artifact;
- attachment;
- formal Document;
- official filing representation;
- official certificate image.

The Product should reference the correct status.

File existence does not create formal Document or official status automatically.

---

## 29. Failure Modes to Reject

```text
One brand treated as one automatic application
Thumbnail treated as exact filing representation
AI-extracted text treated as confirmed wording
Later image silently overwrites reviewed image
Color image treated as formal color claim automatically
Translation and transliteration merged
Local-language name invented by the Product
All variants copied into every jurisdiction
Existing right matched only by similar brand name
User selection shown as final filing approval
One search result reused across materially different marks
Temporary slogan recommended as permanent filing without context
Unsupported non-traditional mark forced into a word-mark form
```

---

## 30. Minimum Filing-Unit Lock

```text
Brand is the commercial context.

Mark is the defined sign.

Filing unit is the Product planning unit.

Application and registration are formal or official records.

Every filing-unit candidate preserves
the exact representation, version,
language, source, intended use,
jurisdiction context, and review need.

Variants remain explicit.

AI extraction remains separate from confirmation.

Material change triggers re-evaluation.

No selected image or wording becomes
a filing instruction by itself.
```

---

## 31. Transition to Applicant, Ownership and Authority Context

Once MarkReg has identified what may be protected, it must determine who may own, instruct, sign for, pay for, and authorize the proposed work.

CH12 defines the applicant and authority context.

The next question is:

```text
Which legal person should be the applicant,
what is the ownership rationale,
and who has authority to act?
```
