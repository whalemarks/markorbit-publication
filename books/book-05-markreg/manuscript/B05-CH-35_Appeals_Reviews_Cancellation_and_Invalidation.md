# B05-CH-35 — Appeals, Reviews, Cancellation and Invalidation

**Status:** Part V Draft  
**Chapter Map:** B05-TOC-V0.1 — Owner Accepted  
**Part:** Part V — Examination, Publication and Disputes

## Chapter Purpose

A sourced decision may create a new right to appeal, request review, seek correction, defend cancellation or pursue invalidation.

CH35 answers:

> When an adverse or challengeable outcome occurs, how does MarkReg identify available remedies, preserve the decision record, obtain authority, and open a governed new proceeding without treating every disagreement as one generic appeal?

```text
Verified decision or challenge
→ Remedy Context
→ eligibility, standing and deadline verification
→ Remedy Option Set
→ professional recommendation
→ authorized decision
→ governed package and filing
→ sourced remedy outcome
```

Appeal, administrative review, judicial review, correction, cancellation, revocation and invalidation are different proceedings with different authorities and consequences.

---

## 1. User Question and Primary Action

**User question:** What decision or challenge exists, what remedies are actually available, what will each path cost or preserve, and by when must we decide?

**Primary action:** Approve a sourced Remedy Strategy, choose not to proceed, or authorize urgent preservation action under policy.

The Product should show the decision in plain language while retaining the official source and professional analysis.

---

## 2. Remedy Context

A Remedy Context is a Product-local working context referencing:

- the official decision, challenge or petition;
- formal Matter and proceeding identifiers;
- affected application, registration or scope;
- parties and representatives;
- source date and service or notification evidence;
- available procedural routes;
- eligibility, standing and authority;
- deadlines and extensions;
- record below and new evidence rules;
- costs, security, provider and forum requirements;
- client and professional decisions;
- remedy packages, filings and outcomes.

```text
Remedy Context
≠ formal appeal or cancellation Matter
≠ official case file
≠ guarantee that a remedy is available
≠ general litigation workspace
```

A distinct formal Matter should be requested where the remedy or challenge has separate parties, scope, responsibility, fees or procedural identity.

---

## 3. Proceeding Types

The Product may need to distinguish:

| Proceeding | Typical trigger |
| --- | --- |
| correction or reconsideration | clerical, procedural or reviewable office decision |
| administrative review | decision review within an office or tribunal structure |
| appeal | appealable adverse decision to a designated body |
| judicial review | challenge to legality or procedure before a court or equivalent body |
| cancellation or revocation | challenge to an existing registration under sourced grounds |
| invalidation or nullity | challenge to validity from the outset or under applicable grounds |
| non-use challenge | challenge based on use or maintenance requirements |
| restoration or revival | missed deadline or abandoned state where a sourced remedy may exist |
| record correction | ownership, bibliographic or official-record correction |

The actual label, forum and remedy must come from the jurisdiction pack and professional review.

---

## 4. Decision Snapshot

Before options are presented, MarkReg should create a Decision Snapshot containing:

- decision identifier and source;
- issuing authority;
- decision date and service date;
- affected right and scope;
- findings and ordered consequence;
- granted and refused relief;
- reasons and cited sources;
- costs or fee consequences;
- remedy information stated in the decision;
- official status and retrieval time;
- correction or supersession status;
- professional interpretation status.

The Product must distinguish the text of the decision from the professional view of its correctness or appealability.

---

## 5. Eligibility and Standing

A remedy analysis should verify:

- who may file or defend;
- current owner or applicant identity;
- chain of title;
- party status in the prior proceeding;
- representative and signatory authority;
- territorial or procedural standing;
- whether fees, security or local counsel are required;
- whether the right or proceeding remains live;
- whether prior settlement, waiver or conduct affects the route;
- whether the deadline is still open.

A client’s commercial interest does not automatically establish procedural standing.

---

## 6. Remedy Option Set

The Product may present:

```text
Accept the decision
Seek correction or clarification
Request reconsideration or review
Appeal all findings
Appeal selected findings or scope
Pursue settlement while preserving remedy rights
Refile or pursue a different route
Limit or abandon affected scope
Defend a cancellation or invalidation action
Counterclaim or bring a separate challenge where available
Take no action with recorded consequence
```

Each option should show:

- source-backed availability;
- decision deadline;
- affected scope;
- record and evidence rules;
- standard or grounds for review where professionally stated;
- cost and provider assumptions;
- interim effect or stay status;
- risk and possible outcomes;
- downstream portfolio effect;
- authority required.

---

## 7. Record and New Evidence

The Product should identify whether the proceeding relies on:

- the existing record only;
- limited new evidence;
- a new evidentiary record;
- declarations or witness evidence;
- certified official documents;
- use evidence;
- ownership evidence;
- translations;
- expert or survey evidence;
- procedural affidavits or explanations.

The allowed scope should be source-backed and professionally reviewed.

Evidence that would have been useful earlier is not automatically admissible later.

---

## 8. Remedy Strategy

The Remedy Strategy should contain:

- target outcome;
- challenged findings or grounds;
- scope to preserve, surrender or attack;
- procedural route and forum;
- argument and record plan;
- new evidence plan where permitted;
- interim measures or deadline-preservation action;
- settlement or refile alternative;
- provider and responsible professional;
- staged budget;
- decision, filing and hearing schedule;
- residual risk;
- fallback if the remedy fails;
- approval and supersession rules.

```text
Remedy Strategy
≠ remedy filing
≠ stay or suspension
≠ accepted appeal
≠ reversal
```

---

## 9. Urgent Preservation

A remedy deadline may require action before full strategy or commercial approval is complete.

Under an authorized policy, the Product may support:

- preliminary notice of appeal;
- extension or time-preservation request;
- provider conflict and availability check;
- formal Matter request;
- urgent client decision request;
- limited filing intended only to preserve a right.

The action must disclose:

- exact purpose;
- authority basis;
- cost exposure;
- scope preserved;
- further steps required;
- consequence if the client does not continue.

Urgent preservation must not silently commit the client to full proceedings.

---

## 10. Cancellation and Invalidation Defense

A challenge to an existing registration should preserve:

- current official owner and right data;
- challenged goods/services and territories;
- grounds and source petition;
- use and maintenance history;
- assignments, licences and group use;
- prior proceedings and settlements;
- standing and representative authority;
- response deadline;
- evidence burden;
- partial-defense or limitation options;
- settlement and coexistence context;
- maintenance obligations running in parallel.

The Product should not treat a challenged registration as cancelled until a sourced official outcome exists.

---

## 11. Claimant-Side Cancellation or Invalidation

For a client considering a challenge, MarkReg should require:

- verified target right and owner;
- business objective;
- source-backed grounds;
- standing or entitlement analysis;
- evidence and investigation plan;
- proportionality and retaliation risk;
- alternative negotiation or opposition route;
- cost and provider scope;
- Human Review and decision authority;
- filing package and official acknowledgement.

A search result or commercial conflict alone does not justify autonomous challenge initiation.

---

## 12. Ownership and Chain-of-Title Risk

Appeal and cancellation matters frequently expose ownership inconsistencies.

The Product should compare:

- official owner;
- current business owner;
- prior assignments;
- recordal status;
- applicant or registrant name in pleadings;
- signatory and instructor authority;
- right to rely on use by affiliates or licensees where professionally relevant.

If ownership affects standing or evidence, the Product should open a targeted resolution and may require recordal work without assuming that later recordal cures every past issue.

---

## 13. Parallel Proceedings

A right may face parallel:

- appeal and settlement;
- cancellation and infringement action;
- opposition and national proceedings;
- recordal and renewal;
- non-use challenge and proof-of-use request;
- administrative and judicial remedies;
- several country proceedings based on related marks.

MarkReg should link the contexts but preserve separate:

- formal Matters;
- deadlines;
- parties and providers;
- confidentiality;
- evidence-use permissions;
- budgets;
- decisions;
- official outcomes.

A decision in one proceeding should not silently determine another.

---

## 15. `RIVERKITE` Contrasting Journey

The `RIVERKITE` portfolio contains registrations undergoing renewal and ownership regularization.

A third party files a cancellation request against one registration, alleging that the right should not remain registered for selected goods. This example is necessary because cancellation of an existing right requires different ownership, use, evidence and maintenance controls from the `EMBERLOOP` filing journey.

MarkReg creates:

- Official Event Snapshot RK-CAN v1;
- Decision/Challenge Snapshot and Remedy Context;
- a distinct cancellation-defense Matter request;
- challenged and unaffected goods mapping;
- response deadline and evidence-collection schedule;
- current official-owner versus business-owner conflict;
- provider conflict and capability checks;
- staged defense Quote.

### Ownership issue

The official register still shows Riverkite Trading Limited, while the business group states that Riverkite Brands Pte. Ltd. owns the portfolio after restructuring.

The Product blocks any unsupported statement that the new entity is already the official owner. It requests:

- chain-of-title Documents;
- assignment and recordal status;
- instructor authority;
- professional analysis of who should defend and sign;
- whether recordal should proceed before, with or separately from the defense.

### Evidence issue

The client provides group sales and distributor records. MarkReg maps each item to:

- relevant goods;
- territory;
- date range;
- seller and brand owner;
- licence or group relationship;
- source and custodian;
- confidentiality;
- professional inclusion decision.

The Product does not determine that the evidence is legally sufficient.

### Strategy

The accountable professional recommends:

1. preserve the response deadline;
2. resolve instructor and signatory authority;
3. defend core goods supported by sourced evidence;
4. consider a limitation for unsupported peripheral goods;
5. coordinate recordal without assuming it retroactively resolves all standing questions;
6. retain renewal work as a separate but linked Matter.

The client approves Remedy Strategy RK v2 and a first-stage evidence budget.

## 16. Appeal Example from an Adversarial Decision

If the `EMBERLOOP` EU opposition later produces a partial adverse decision, MarkReg should:

- create a Decision Snapshot;
- separate refused and unaffected goods;
- verify appeal and review routes;
- preserve settlement and refile alternatives;
- identify the decision and appeal deadlines;
- open a new Remedy Option Set;
- require new provider capability and commercial review if appeal work was not included;
- avoid displaying the appeal as filed until governed acknowledgement exists.

This chapter defines the controlled behavior without predicting that such a decision will occur.

## 17. Conformance Scenarios

### MR-SCN-35A — Appeal information in decision is incomplete

**Given** an official decision states an adverse outcome but does not clearly state the remedy deadline.  
**When** MarkReg opens the Remedy Context.  
**Then** it preserves the decision, marks remedy timing incomplete, requests sourced jurisdiction verification and prevents an unverified date from becoming the controlling deadline.

### MR-SCN-35B — Business owner differs from official owner

**Given** the business group claims ownership but the official register names another entity.  
**When** cancellation defense instructions are requested.  
**Then** MarkReg shows the conflict, limits unsupported authority, requests chain-of-title review and blocks final signatory or standing conclusions until professionally resolved.

### MR-SCN-35C — Preservation filing only

**Given** an appeal deadline is imminent and the client authorizes only a right-preserving filing.  
**When** the filing is prepared.  
**Then** the Product labels the limited scope, records cost and follow-up obligations, prevents it from being shown as full appeal authorization and communicates the next decision deadline.

### MR-SCN-35D — One registration challenged in a portfolio

**Given** one `RIVERKITE` registration is challenged.  
**When** the portfolio view updates.  
**Then** MarkReg links the challenge to that registration and related evidence while preserving the independent status of the other five registrations.

## 18. AI Assistance

AI may:

- extract findings, relief and remedy language;
- compare decisions to pleadings and prior strategy;
- organize grounds and record references;
- identify candidate ownership or evidence gaps;
- prepare remedy comparisons and timelines;
- classify evidence and build chronologies;
- draft client decision briefs;
- flag inconsistent positions across related proceedings.

AI must not:

- establish standing or appealability;
- invent a remedy route or deadline;
- decide whether evidence is sufficient;
- authorize a challenge;
- abandon or limit rights;
- file a proceeding autonomously;
- promise reversal;
- act as counsel or tribunal.

---

## 19. Failure Modes to Reject

```text
Every adverse event labelled appealable
Decision summary replaces official source
Business ownership treated as recorded ownership
Preservation action shown as full proceeding authority
Cancellation petition shown as cancelled registration
Parallel matters collapsed into one deadline and budget
New evidence assumed admissible
Settlement discussion shown as stay
Appeal filing shown as accepted without acknowledgement
AI predicts reversal as fact
```

## 20. Minimum Remedy and Challenge Lock

```text
Every remedy begins from
a sourced decision or challenge.

Proceeding type, standing,
scope, record, deadline,
authority, cost and provider
must be verified separately.

Appeal, review, correction,
cancellation, invalidation,
restoration and recordal
remain distinct.

A challenge does not equal loss.
A filing does not equal acceptance.
A remedy does not equal reversal.

AI may organize and compare.
Accountable humans advise,
authorize, sign and proceed.
```
