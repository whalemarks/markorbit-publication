# B05-CH-38 — Maintenance Calendar and Post-Registration Duties

**Status:** Part VI Draft  
**Chapter Map:** B05-TOC-V0.1 — Owner Accepted  
**Part:** Part VI — Registration and Portfolio Continuity

## Chapter Purpose

A registered trademark remains operational only when jurisdiction-specific duties, evidence, use conditions, representative requirements and deadlines are understood and governed.

The central progression is:

```text
Right Baseline
→ Maintenance Obligation Set
→ Verified Deadline Records
→ Responsibility Assignment
→ Evidence and Document Preparation
→ Approval and Governed Action
→ Official Acknowledgement
```

```text
Calendar Reminder
≠ Official Deadline
≠ Completed Obligation
≠ Continued Validity
```

---

## 1. User Question and Primary Action

**User question:** What must be done to preserve this right, when, by whom, and with what evidence?

**Primary action:** Review and accept the Maintenance Obligation Set, assign owners and approve the next required preparation step.

---

## 2. Maintenance Is Broader Than Renewal

Post-registration duties may include:

- use declarations;
- specimens or evidence of use;
- maintenance affidavits;
- renewal;
- registration-stage fees;
- local representative maintenance;
- address-for-service requirements;
- classification updates;
- publication or certificate fees;
- recordal of ownership or name changes;
- monitoring pending proceedings;
- responding to office requests;
- proof of control or licence quality requirements.

The Product should not reduce all post-registration work to one renewal date.

---

## 3. Maintenance Obligation Set

Each obligation should preserve:

- right and jurisdiction;
- obligation type;
- legal or procedural basis;
- triggering event;
- official due date or window;
- opening date;
- grace period;
- extension possibility;
- source and source version;
- checked date;
- affected scope;
- required evidence or documents;
- responsible organization and person;
- professional reviewer;
- commercial condition;
- provider requirement;
- current state;
- consequence of omission;
- uncertainty or conflict.

A generic event title such as `maintenance due` is not sufficient.

---

## 4. Deadline Sources and Calculation

A Maintenance Deadline Record should distinguish:

```text
Official Date
Calculated Date
Provider-Reported Date
Internal Target Date
Reminder Date
Grace-Period End
```

The calculation should preserve:

- source event;
- calculation rule;
- time zone;
- inclusive or exclusive day treatment;
- weekend and holiday rule where relevant;
- extension basis;
- verifier;
- verification time;
- confidence;
- superseded result.

Calculated dates should not be presented as official dates unless the office has confirmed them.

---

## 5. Maintenance Windows

Some obligations are windows rather than single dates.

The Product should show:

- earliest filing date;
- ordinary due date;
- late or grace period;
- surcharge consequences;
- restoration possibility;
- evidence period;
- risk of filing too early or too late.

The user should understand whether action is available, recommended, late, blocked or already missed.

---

## 6. Use Obligations and Evidence Readiness

Where use affects maintenance or vulnerability, the Product should track:

- relevant goods or services;
- jurisdiction and territory;
- use period;
- owner or authorized user;
- form of use;
- mark version used;
- sales or service evidence;
- advertising and market evidence;
- invoices, labels, packaging, screenshots or photographs;
- source and date;
- confidentiality;
- professional sufficiency review.

Evidence collection should be continuous where practical, not started only days before a deadline.

---

## 7. Non-Use Vulnerability Is Separate from Filing Duty

A right may have no immediate filing obligation but may already be vulnerable to non-use challenge.

The Product should distinguish:

```text
Maintenance Filing Due
Use Evidence Recommended
Non-Use Grace Period Running
Non-Use Vulnerability Active
Challenge Pending
```

A green renewal status should not hide a red use vulnerability.

---

## 8. Responsibility and Escalation

Every material obligation should identify:

- client decision owner;
- professional owner;
- evidence owner;
- document owner;
- finance owner;
- provider manager;
- deadline owner;
- escalation path;
- backup owner.

Sending a reminder does not transfer responsibility.

Escalation should begin early enough to allow evidence correction, owner verification, provider appointment and payment.

---

## 9. Commercial and Provider Conditions

Maintenance preparation may depend on:

- updated official fees;
- provider quotation;
- local representative status;
- document legalization;
- evidence review;
- ownership correction;
- advance payment;
- client credit terms;
- currency and tax.

The Product should show which condition blocks filing and which may be resolved later.

## 12. Reference Journey — EMBERLOOP

The UK Right Baseline produces a Maintenance Obligation Set containing:

- future renewal window;
- non-use vulnerability milestone;
- representative and address verification;
- recommended annual evidence review.

No immediate official filing is due. MarkReg therefore shows:

```text
Next official filing: not yet open
Evidence collection: active recommendation
Owner verification: complete
Representative verification: complete
```

The EU application remains in opposition and does not receive registration-maintenance obligations. Its proceeding deadlines remain in Part V.

## 13. Reference Journey — RIVERKITE

Six existing registrations are reviewed.

The Product identifies:

- four ordinary renewals approaching;
- one right requiring ownership recordal before renewal;
- one right under cancellation challenge;
- incomplete use evidence for two classes;
- provider quotes that exclude late fees.

Each right receives an independent Maintenance Obligation Set. The cancellation challenge does not stop unrelated renewals unless a professional decision says otherwise.

## 14. Conformance Scenarios

### Scenario A — Reminder date mistaken for official due date

**Given** an internal reminder is six months before the official deadline  
**When** the user opens the calendar  
**Then** the Product labels both dates and does not present the reminder as the official deadline.

### Scenario B — Renewal not due but use vulnerability active

**Given** renewal is years away  
**When** the non-use grace period has ended  
**Then** the Product shows no renewal filing due but displays active use vulnerability and recommends evidence review.

### Scenario C — Ownership mismatch before maintenance filing

**Given** the official owner differs from the instructing entity  
**When** maintenance preparation begins  
**Then** the Product creates an ownership blocker or professional-review condition rather than copying the instructing entity into the filing.

### Scenario D — Late window

**Given** the ordinary due date has passed but a grace period remains  
**When** the Product recalculates readiness  
**Then** it shows late status, surcharge and verified grace-period end, and requires renewed commercial approval.

## 15. AI Assistance Boundary

AI may:

- extract maintenance rules from approved jurisdiction packs;
- calculate candidate dates;
- identify missing evidence;
- classify documents;
- summarize consequences;
- draft reminders and preparation checklists.

AI may not:

- establish the official deadline without source governance;
- decide evidence sufficiency alone;
- declare continued validity;
- waive a missed obligation;
- assume use;
- file or pay without governed approval.

---

## 16. Minimum Maintenance Lock

```text
Maintenance is obligation-specific.

Official dates, calculated dates,
internal targets and reminders remain distinct.

Renewal readiness does not erase
use, ownership, dispute or evidence risk.

Every obligation has a source,
owner, consequence, state and next action.
```

---

## 17. Handoff to Renewal Preparation

The output is a versioned Maintenance Obligation Set with verified Deadline Records and assigned responsibilities.

CH39 defines how a renewal candidate becomes a reviewed, approved, filed and officially acknowledged renewal action.
