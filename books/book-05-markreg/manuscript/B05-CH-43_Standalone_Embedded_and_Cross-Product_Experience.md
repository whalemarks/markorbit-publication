# B05-CH-43 — Standalone, Embedded and Cross-Product Experience

**Status:** Complete Draft 1  
**Chapter Map:** B05-TOC-V0.1 — Owner Accepted  
**Part:** Part VII — Product Experience and Evolution

## Chapter Purpose

MarkReg may appear directly, inside Lite, inside a professional Workplace or through another Product. These are different entry surfaces for one governed Product journey, not separate lifecycle implementations.

```text
One Product journey
→ purpose-limited entry
→ MR-A12 Handoff Envelope
→ MR-C10 Product Session
→ controlled artifacts and Decisions
→ MR-A30 Return Envelope
```

A surface may change presentation. It may not create duplicate authority, hidden state or a second version of official truth.

## 1. User Question and Primary Action

**User question:** Which context am I acting in, what may I do here, and where will the result return?

**Primary action:** Continue, pause or return the governed journey through the declared route.

Every material surface must show:

- personal or organization context;
- active role and purpose;
- client, brand, portfolio or Matter reference;
- Product journey and current step;
- visible artifact versions and source freshness;
- action rights and blockers;
- formal owner of each business fact;
- return destination.

## 2. Controlled Experience Modes

| Mode | Context supplied | MarkReg responsibility | Return behavior |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| standalone | authenticated personal or organization context | full Product journey and Product-local records | remains in MarkReg or opens a governed external context |
| Lite embedded | local, personal or lightweight team context | Product journey without silently formalizing local facts | returns approved facts and candidates to Lite |
| Workplace embedded | organization, client, role, policy and formal references | Product-specific artifacts, Decisions and views | returns typed results to the Workplace |
| cross-Product | purpose-limited context from another Product | requested MarkReg capability or bounded journey | returns the requested result or next-action candidate |

The mode does not alter constitutional rules, professional responsibility or External Protected Action authority.

## 3. MR-A12 Handoff Envelope

`EL-36` begins with a typed `MR-A12 Handoff Envelope` containing, at minimum:

- stable handoff and correlation identity;
- source and destination Product or Workplace;
- purpose and requested MarkReg journey;
- initiating actor, role and organization;
- client, portfolio and formal-object references;
- inherited facts, artifacts and source versions;
- permission and confidentiality scope;
- expected return type;
- expiry and revalidation conditions;
- idempotency or duplicate-prevention identity.

A Handoff Envelope is not Client Acceptance, Commercial Instruction, Filing Approval, provider appointment or Execution authority.

## 4. Context Acceptance

MarkReg classifies inherited context before use:

```text
accepted for display
accepted for recommendation
client confirmation required
Professional Review required
official-source verification required
stale
conflicting
outside purpose
prohibited from reuse
```

A conflict opens a resolution step. It does not overwrite one source with another.

Local Lite facts, private notes and organization records retain their original visibility and provenance. Transfer requires an explicit purpose and permitted field set.

## 5. MR-C10 Product Session

`MR-C10 Product Session` records the current interaction, including:

- actor, role and represented organization;
- entry mode and active purpose;
- current journey step;
- visible artifact and Pack versions;
- blockers, warnings and unresolved questions;
- unsaved or local-only changes;
- last accountable Decision;
- pause reason and resume eligibility;
- return route.

The Product Session is not the formal Matter, Order or Execution state.

On resume, MarkReg rechecks:

- permission and organization context;
- official status and deadlines;
- Pack and Rule versions;
- artifact supersession;
- approval validity;
- calling-context freshness.

## 6. MR-A30 Return Envelope

A valid return point creates `MR-A30 Return Envelope`, which may contain:

- a Recommendation Set or Option Set;
- Proposal, Quote or acceptance references;
- Package, Review or Approval references;
- official evidence, Outcome Snapshot or Right Baseline references;
- blockers and unresolved questions;
- service-specific next-action candidates;
- source, version and permission metadata.

The receiving surface must preserve candidate, Decision, formal-object, Execution and official-state distinctions.

A Return Envelope is idempotent for its purpose. Reconsuming the same return must not create duplicate Orders, Matters, payments or protected actions.

## 7. Deep Links and Navigation

A governed deep link identifies destination, purpose, expected organization, requested view, expiry and one-time or reusable status.

The destination rechecks identity, organization, role and permission. A URL alone carries neither authority nor sensitive facts.

## 8. Cross-Surface State Integrity

One canonical Product version may be rendered in several surfaces. MarkReg must prevent:

- parallel drafts presented as one accepted version;
- Lite data overwriting a Workplace-approved artifact;
- stale approval displayed as active;
- client access to internal margin or provider notes;
- provider access beyond the accepted engagement;
- a Return Envelope silently creating a formal object;
- private notes crossing organization boundaries.

Conflicting edits require explicit merge, supersession or rejection.

## 9. Controlled Scenarios

Part VII uses the formal scenario registry rather than chapter-local scenarios:

- `MR-SCN-25` — a Return Envelope cannot be consumed twice to create duplicate formal effects;
- `MR-SCN-34` — personal Lite context is not automatically organization truth or transferable;
- `MR-SCN-35` — a deep link opened in the wrong organization is blocked;
- `MR-SCN-10` — stale official status cannot remain current merely because the surface was cached.

## 10. Reference Journeys

### EMBERLOOP

The Workplace opens one MarkReg session containing independent UK, US and EU references. A client link exposes reviewed scope, cost and decisions but not internal margin or provider comparison. A newer EU opposition event invalidates a stale portfolio summary before return.

The Return Envelope preserves:

- UK Right Baseline and maintenance reference;
- US examination next-action candidate;
- EU adversarial-status reference;
- no unsupported global status.

### RIVERKITE

A professional begins in Lite and later hands approved facts, sources and candidate artifacts into the organization Workplace. Personal notes remain local. The Workplace resolves organization roles, formal Matters and commercial policy before protected work proceeds.

## 11. Minimum Experience Lock

```text
Surface ≠ authority.
Session ≠ Matter state.
Handoff ≠ approval.
Return ≠ formal-object creation.
Deep link ≠ permission.
Local context ≠ organization truth.
```
