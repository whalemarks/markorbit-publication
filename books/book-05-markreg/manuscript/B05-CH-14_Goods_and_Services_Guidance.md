# B05-CH-14 — Goods and Services Guidance

**Status:** Complete Draft 1  
**Chapter Map:** B05-TOC-V0.1 — Owner Accepted  
**Part:** Part II — Need, Strategy and Recommendation

## Chapter Purpose

A class number does not define the commercial scope of protection. Goods and services wording must connect real business activity to jurisdiction-specific rules, fees, search strategy, evidence, amendment limits and future portfolio value.

This chapter defines how MarkReg prepares explainable, versioned wording candidates without copying an unrelated catalogue or narrowing the filing beyond the realistic plan.

The controlled example is `EL-07` in B05-SPEC-0002.

## 1. Product Question

> Which goods and services should be claimed now, which should be optional or deferred, and how should the same business concept be expressed in each jurisdiction?

Business language, controlled concept and local filing wording are related but distinct.

## 2. Required Context

Guidance may use:

- selected class candidates;
- current and planned products, software, services and channels;
- filing-unit candidates;
- jurisdictions and routes;
- search findings;
- use and evidence context;
- budget and item-count preference;
- existing portfolio wording;
- Jurisdiction Pack vocabulary, rule, fee unit, amendment constraint and support state.

A local vocabulary list or provider suggestion must retain its source status. It does not become a universal rule.

## 3. Scope Categories

| Category | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Core | Directly supports the principal current or launch offering. |
| Recommended | Materially supports the planned business or protection strategy. |
| Optional | Adds coverage but may be removed for budget or focus. |
| Future | Retained as a later expansion candidate with a trigger. |
| Unsupported | Not justified by current facts or evidence. |
| Local alternative | Jurisdiction-specific expression of the same business concept. |
| Professional Review required | Scope, acceptability, evidence or risk cannot be resolved safely without accountable judgment. |

## 4. Controlled Output

Goods/services candidates remain within `MR-A03 Recommendation Set` and `MR-A04 Option Set`. Each records:

- stable business-concept identifier;
- class and jurisdiction;
- source business language;
- proposed filing wording;
- local vocabulary or Rule Record source;
- scope category;
- filing-unit relationship;
- item-count and official-fee effect;
- client-price and provider-cost effect where known;
- search effect;
- acceptability confidence;
- evidence or use note;
- amendment or deletion constraint;
- user selection and Professional Review state;
- version and supersession history.

The stable business concept should survive even where local wording differs.

## 5. Product Behavior

MarkReg should:

1. translate business descriptions into controlled concepts;
2. distinguish current, planned, optional and unsupported scope;
3. map concepts to jurisdiction-specific wording;
4. show item limits and price effects before selection;
5. identify wording that is overbroad, narrow, duplicative, conflicting or unclear;
6. connect search findings and evidence risk to affected items;
7. preserve local variants under one traceable concept;
8. request Professional Review for material wording or scope judgment;
9. explain exclusions and deferred concepts.

AI may draft, compare and flag candidates. It may not present unreviewed wording as accepted local practice or final professional advice.

## 6. User Surface

The user should see:

- plain-language business concept;
- proposed filing wording;
- core, recommended, optional or future category;
- jurisdiction-specific variation;
- item-count and price effect;
- search, evidence or amendment warning;
- why the choice matters;
- one primary action: **Choose scope or request revision**.

The surface should allow focused and broader options to be compared without requiring the user to edit long legal-style lists manually.

## 7. Reference Journey — `EL-07 EMBERLOOP`

For EMBERLOOP, MarkReg connects the selected equipment and software classes to:

- core outdoor-cooking equipment concepts;
- connected-control and downloadable-application concepts;
- optional retail activity;
- future platform or service concepts.

A search finding creates moderate risk around one grill-related expression. The professional recommends narrower wording for that item while preserving the broader connected-device strategy.

Northstar selects the focused launch scope. Deferred concepts remain future-action candidates, not current protection.

## 8. Controlled Scenarios

### `MR-SCN-09` — Professional override

When a controlled rule warns that wording is unacceptable but an eligible professional identifies a sourced local exception, the override must be scoped, versioned and reviewable. Only authorized Human Review may override the warning.

### `MR-SCN-13` — Material scope change

A material goods/services change creates a new recommendation version and identifies affected search, Quote, Intake, Package and readiness records.

Where item count changes price, the Product shows the active Pack Version, calculation and need for Quote revision. It does not silently reprice accepted scope.

## 9. Version and Change Propagation

A goods/services version identifies the exact:

- class selection;
- Jurisdiction Pack version;
- business facts;
- wording source;
- user selection;
- Professional Review;
- downstream Proposal, Quote, Intake or Package that consumed it.

A wording change may affect:

- search and risk assessment;
- item or class fees;
- client price and provider cost;
- Proposal and Quote;
- declarations or evidence;
- Formal Intake;
- readiness;
- Filing Package Candidate.

Updating a Pack or local vocabulary must not rewrite an accepted or approved version silently.

## 10. Handoff to CH15

CH14 produces versioned goods/services candidates and a selected scope.

CH15 determines whether search is proportionate, how search scope is defined and how residual risk changes the Recommendation Set before commercial options are prepared.
