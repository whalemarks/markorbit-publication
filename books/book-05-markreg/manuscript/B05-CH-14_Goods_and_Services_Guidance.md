# B05-CH-14 — Goods and Services Guidance

**Status:** Revised Draft — Productized  
**Chapter Map:** B05-TOC-V0.1 — Owner Accepted  
**Part:** Part II — Need, Strategy and Recommendation  
**Revision basis:** B05-REVISION-PACK-001

## Chapter Purpose

A class number does not define the commercial scope of protection. The goods and services wording must connect real business activities to jurisdiction-specific filing rules, fees, search strategy, evidence, amendment limits, and future portfolio value.

This chapter defines how MarkReg turns selected class concepts into explainable, versioned wording candidates.

## 1. Product Question

> Which goods and services should be claimed now, which should be optional or deferred, and how should they be expressed in each jurisdiction?

The Product must avoid two opposite failures:

- copying an excessively broad catalogue unrelated to the business;
- narrowing the wording so much that the filing no longer supports the user’s realistic plan.

## 2. Required Inputs

Goods/services guidance may consume:

- selected class candidates;
- confirmed products, software, services, sales channels, and planned activities;
- filing-unit candidates;
- jurisdictions and routes;
- search findings;
- use and evidence context;
- budget and item-count preference;
- existing portfolio wording;
- jurisdiction-pack vocabulary, local rules, fee units, and amendment constraints.

## 3. Scope Categories

Each wording concept should be identified as:

| Category | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Core | Directly supports the principal current or launch offering. |
| Recommended | Materially supports the planned business or practical protection strategy. |
| Optional | Adds coverage but may be removed for budget or focus. |
| Future | Retained as a later expansion candidate with a trigger. |
| Unsupported | Not justified by current facts or evidence. |
| Local alternative | A jurisdiction-specific expression of an equivalent business concept. |
| Professional review required | Scope, acceptability, evidence, or risk cannot be safely resolved automatically. |

## 4. Wording Candidate Contract

Each candidate records:

- stable concept identifier;
- class and jurisdiction;
- business-language source;
- proposed wording;
- local vocabulary or rule source;
- scope category;
- filing-unit relationship;
- item-count and fee effect;
- search effect;
- acceptability confidence;
- evidence or use note where relevant;
- amendment or deletion constraint;
- user selection;
- professional review status;
- version and supersession history.

The Product should preserve a stable business concept even when local wording differs.

## 5. Product Behavior

MarkReg should:

1. translate business descriptions into candidate concepts;
2. distinguish current, planned, optional, and unsupported scope;
3. map concepts to jurisdiction-specific wording;
4. show item limits and fee effects before selection;
5. identify overbroad, narrow, duplicate, conflicting, or unclear wording;
6. connect search results and risk findings to affected items;
7. preserve local variants under one traceable concept;
8. request professional review for material wording or scope judgment;
9. explain what is excluded or deferred.

## 6. User Surface

The user sees:

- plain-language business concept;
- proposed filing wording;
- core, recommended, optional, or future status;
- jurisdiction-specific variation;
- item and fee effect;
- risk or evidence warning;
- why a wording choice matters;
- one primary action: **Choose scope or request revision**.

The user should be able to compare a focused and broader scope without manually editing legal-style lists.

## 7. Reference Journey A — EMBERLOOP

For EMBERLOOP, MarkReg links the selected equipment and software classes to:

- core outdoor cooking equipment concepts;
- connected-control and downloadable application concepts;
- optional retail activity;
- future platform or service concepts.

A search finding creates moderate risk around one grill-related expression. The professional recommends narrowing that item while preserving the broader connected-device strategy.

The user chooses the focused launch wording and retains deferred concepts for later expansion.

## 8. Conformance Scenarios

### MR-CH14-SCN-01 — Item-count and fee consequence

**Given** a jurisdiction charges or restricts based on item count.  
**When** the user adds optional goods.  
**Then** MarkReg shows the changed item count, fee effect, local rule source, and any need to revise the Quote before confirming the change.  
**Authority boundary:** the Product applies the versioned rule but does not silently change accepted pricing.  
**Evidence retained:** pack version, selected items, calculation, user decision, and affected artifacts.

### MR-SCN-09 — Professional override

**Given** an automated rule warns that a proposed item is unacceptable.  
**When** an authorized professional identifies a sourced local exception.  
**Then** MarkReg permits a reasoned override limited to the affected item, jurisdiction, and version.  
**Authority boundary:** only authorized Human Review can override the rule.  
**Evidence retained:** original warning, source, reviewer, reason, scope, and expiry condition.

## 9. Version and Local Variation

A goods/services version must identify the exact:

- class selection;
- jurisdiction-pack version;
- business facts;
- wording source;
- user selection;
- professional decision;
- downstream Proposal, Quote, Intake, or Filing Package that consumed it.

Updating a local vocabulary or rule does not silently mutate an accepted or approved version.

## 10. Change Propagation

A wording change may affect:

- search and risk assessment;
- item or class fees;
- provider review cost;
- Proposal and Quote;
- declarations or evidence;
- Formal Intake;
- readiness;
- Filing Package Candidate.

The Product must identify and revalidate the affected chain.

## 11. Handoff to CH15

CH14 produces versioned goods/services candidates and selected scope.

CH15 determines whether search is appropriate, which search mode is proportionate, how risk findings affect the Recommendation Set, and what uncertainty the user must understand before commercial options are prepared.
