# B03-PLN-0008 Review Gate Model

## Purpose

Define review gate language for Book 03 planning while preserving Book 02 authority and keeping Book 03 in planning phase.

## Definitions

- Review Gate: an execution stop point where work cannot proceed until required review conditions are satisfied.
- Human Review: review performed by an authorized human reviewer who accepts responsibility for the review decision. AI output is not Human Review.
- Approval Boundary: the line between preparation assistance and authorized approval for a protected action.
- Reviewer Role: a human role permitted by policy to inspect evidence, request changes, approve, reject, or defer.
- Review Evidence: records, inputs, drafts, validation results, reviewer notes, and decision artifacts used to support review.
- Review Outcome: an auditable result such as approved, rejected, changes requested, deferred, or escalated.

## Protected Action Rule

Protected actions require Human Review and permission/policy gates before execution. AI-generated text, recommendations, summaries, or confidence scores may support preparation but never satisfy Human Review.

## AI/Agent Boundary

AI and agents may organize context, draft proposed materials, identify missing evidence, and suggest review questions. They may not approve, override reviewer decisions, bypass gates, or perform protected actions.

## Planning Use

Chapter drafts must identify where review gates occur, what evidence is available, which human reviewer role is required, and what outcome is recorded. They must not create new Book 02 review contracts.
