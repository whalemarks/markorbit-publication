This glossary explains how recurring terms are used in Book 06. It does not replace the Specifications or create new controlled meanings.

## A

### <a id="b06-term-active-organization"></a>Active Organization

The Organization context under which the current Lite Session operates. It controls which roles, policies, customer/trademark projections, memories and destinations may be used. The same person may have different authorized context in different Organizations.

**Not:** a personal preference or a universal account context.  
**Primary reading:** CH03, CH07, CH08; `ML-S01`.

### <a id="b06-term-ai-assistance"></a>AI Assistance

Use of an AI capability to help retrieve, organize, compare, draft, transform or evaluate information. AI Assistance remains subject to source, privacy, rights, Review and action controls.

**Not:** source authority, professional approval, identity, consent or authority to act.  
**Primary reading:** CH03, CH06, CH17–CH21, CH33.

### <a id="b06-term-artifact"></a>Artifact

A purpose-bound, audience-specific and versioned business result prepared through Lite. An Artifact may have one or more representations such as text, PDF, image or video.

**Not:** raw Content, a generic file, a formal Document or Evidence.  
**Primary reading:** CH17–CH21; `ML-W02–W03`.

### <a id="b06-term-artifact-version"></a>Artifact Version

An immutable snapshot of an Artifact used for Review, rendering, confirmation, delivery, publication and audit. A substantive later edit creates a new version.

**Not:** a mutable current document.  
**Primary reading:** CH18–CH21; `ML-W03`.

### <a id="b06-term-attention-item"></a>Attention Item

A Product-local presentation of something the user should inspect, decide or continue. It references a source observation, Candidate, returned result or unresolved state.

**Not:** an active Task, authoritative deadline or formal assignment.  
**Primary reading:** CH07, CH11, CH26–CH29; `ML-S03`.

### <a id="b06-term-authorized-context"></a>Authorized Context

The minimum purpose-limited set of user, Organization, Customer, Trademark, Matter, source, memory and destination information that may be used for a specific Product journey.

**Not:** everything technically available to the user or device.  
**Primary reading:** CH08, CH12–CH17, CH22, CH29.

## C

### <a id="b06-term-candidate"></a>Candidate

A Product-local proposal that something may deserve inspection, preparation, qualification, reuse or formalization. Candidate families include service-value, reactivation, prospect, work-product, preference, memory, case lesson, reusable Asset and Knowledge contribution candidates.

**Not:** formal business truth, approval, Customer, Opportunity or instruction.  
**Primary reading:** CH09–CH10; `ML-O03–O06`, `ML-M03–M08`.

### <a id="b06-term-capability-need"></a>Capability Need

A bounded description of professional capability required from MGSN, including jurisdiction, service, timing, qualifications and disclosure constraints.

**Not:** provider selection, recommendation, appointment, assignment or acceptance.  
**Primary reading:** CH27; `ML-H04`, `ML-HC-02`.

### <a id="b06-term-commercial-plan"></a>Commercial Plan

A versioned promise about included capabilities, limits, support, price, quotas and entitlement windows for a Product Edition.

**Not:** Product identity, permission, professional authority or proof of value.  
**Primary reading:** CH32–CH33; `ML-E05–E06`.

### <a id="b06-term-communication"></a>Communication

A governed message action with exact sender, recipients, channel, purpose, content version, attachments and send consequence. The Communication Service owns formal send state.

**Not:** a prepared draft or general relationship history.  
**Primary reading:** CH15, CH20, CH28; `ML-H06`, `ML-HC-04`.

### <a id="b06-term-constitutional-change"></a>Constitutional Change

A change to Lite’s enduring Product identity, target users, four loops, architectural role, non-goals or authority boundaries.

**Not:** a UI improvement, new renderer, plan change or ordinary Product Increment.  
**Primary reading:** CH33; Change Class E.

### <a id="b06-term-contactability"></a>Contactability

Evidence and limits concerning whether a person or Organization can be contacted through a particular channel for a particular purpose.

**Not:** purchase intent, current accuracy or permission for every channel.  
**Primary reading:** CH13–CH15; `ML-SCN-07–10`.

### <a id="b06-term-continuation-state"></a>Continuation State

A Product-local record preserving unfinished context, prior decisions, missing information and the safe next step across sessions or destination Returns.

**Not:** destination-owned lifecycle state.  
**Primary reading:** CH11, CH26, CH29; `ML-S05`.

### <a id="b06-term-correction-record"></a>Correction Record

A sourced record identifying what was wrong or stale, which versions and outputs are affected, and what must be corrected, re-reviewed, replaced or retired.

**Not:** silent overwrite of history.  
**Primary reading:** CH21–CH25, CH29; `ML-M02`.

### <a id="b06-term-customer"></a>Customer

A formal relationship object owned by the relevant Core or Owning Service and consumed by Lite through authorized projections.

**Not:** a Prospect Candidate, contact detail, public organization record or inferred buyer.  
**Primary reading:** CH04, CH08, CH12–CH16.

## D

### <a id="b06-term-decision"></a>Decision

An explicit Human or destination disposition with defined authority and consequence. Depending on context, a Decision may accept, reject, defer, limit, confirm, approve or require more information.

**Not:** a Recommendation, ranking or generated option.  
**Primary reading:** CH10–CH11, CH18, CH28.

### <a id="b06-term-delivery"></a>Delivery

Transfer of an exact Artifact Version or representation to a defined recipient or destination under access, validity and evidence rules.

**Not:** preparation of a Delivery Package.  
**Primary reading:** CH20; `ML-W09`, `ML-HC-07`.

### <a id="b06-term-development-package"></a>Development Package

A Product-local package joining a customer/prospect Candidate with its source, relevance, work product, outreach draft, contact limits, qualification criteria and next-step options.

**Not:** proof of purchase intent or a formal Opportunity.  
**Primary reading:** CH15; `ML-W06`.

### <a id="b06-term-document"></a>Document

A formal or controlled business record such as received correspondence, filing material, certificate, agreement or official form, owned by the relevant Document/Owning Service.

**Not:** an Artifact merely because both may be represented as files.  
**Primary reading:** CH04, CH18, CH24–CH25.

## E

### <a id="b06-term-entitlement"></a>Entitlement

A time-bound, plan-specific right to receive or use a defined capability, quantity, format or support level under stated conditions.

**Not:** authority to access restricted data, skip Review or act externally.  
**Primary reading:** CH32; `ML-E06`.

### <a id="b06-term-evidence"></a>Evidence

Material used to support a claim, action or decision with authenticity, provenance, purpose and retention meaning controlled by the relevant Owning Service.

**Not:** a reusable Artifact, public case example or generic training asset.  
**Primary reading:** CH18, CH22, CH24–CH25.

### <a id="b06-term-external-protected-action"></a>External Protected Action

A consequential external operation such as filing, payment, formal submission, legal representation or rights change that requires stronger authority, approval, Execution and audit controls.

**Not:** a Lite Prepared Action or ordinary user confirmation.  
**Primary reading:** CH04, CH06, CH28–CH31.

## F

### <a id="b06-term-formal-business-truth"></a>Formal Business Truth

Authoritative business or lifecycle state owned by the relevant service, such as formal Customer, Opportunity, Task, Matter, Communication send result or MarkReg Product Session state.

**Not:** a Lite Candidate, projection, recommendation or Handoff request.  
**Primary reading:** CH04, CH09–CH11, CH16, CH26–CH29.

### <a id="b06-term-fulfillment-observation"></a>Fulfillment Observation

Evidence of whether a promised entitlement was scheduled, prepared, available, used, fulfilled, failed, replaced, credited or expired.

**Not:** a generation job count or file existence alone.  
**Primary reading:** CH32; `ML-E06`.

## H

### <a id="b06-term-handoff"></a>Handoff

A versioned, purpose-limited transfer request from Lite to an Owning Service or governed destination. It preserves origin, exact subject/version, sources, disclosure scope, authority context, intended consequence and return address.

**Not:** destination acceptance, completion or authority transfer.  
**Primary reading:** CH11, CH16, CH26–CH29; `ML-H01`, `ML-H03–H08`.

### <a id="b06-term-human-review"></a>Human Review

A bounded professional act in which an accountable Human evaluates an exact version, source set, claims, audience, purpose and requested decision.

**Not:** reading, editing, user confirmation or automated checking.  
**Primary reading:** CH18, CH20, CH22–CH25, CH28; `ML-W04`, `ML-H05`.

### <a id="b06-term-hybrid-minimization"></a>Hybrid Minimization

Use of the smallest appropriate combination of local and shared processing so a purpose can be achieved without unnecessary synchronization or disclosure.

**Not:** automatic upload of all readable local data.  
**Primary reading:** CH08, CH23, CH25, CH29.

## K

### <a id="b06-term-knowledge"></a>Knowledge

Governed professional propositions, explanations and references accepted through Knowledge governance for a defined scope.

**Not:** personal memory, Organization operating practice, one successful case or AI-generated text.  
**Primary reading:** CH17, CH22–CH25.

### <a id="b06-term-knowledge-contribution-candidate"></a>Knowledge Contribution Candidate

A sourced, scoped proposition submitted for consideration by Knowledge governance, including contradictions, conditions, exclusions and review history.

**Not:** accepted canonical Knowledge.  
**Primary reading:** CH24–CH25; `ML-M08`, `ML-HC-08`.

## L

### <a id="b06-term-lite-session"></a>Lite Session

The Product-local operating context that identifies active user, Organization, Product mode and expiry for a bounded period of work.

**Not:** universal identity or permanent authorization.  
**Primary reading:** CH07–CH08; `ML-S01`.

### <a id="b06-term-local-only"></a>Local Only

A classification or processing boundary indicating that source information remains within an authorized local environment unless a specific later disclosure is approved.

**Not:** permission for synchronization, remote AI processing or Organization sharing.  
**Primary reading:** CH08, CH23, CH25, CH29; `ML-SCN-24`.

## M

### <a id="b06-term-markreg"></a>MarkReg

The MarkOrbit Product that owns the formal trademark Product Session and lifecycle described by Book 05. Lite may prepare launch/continuation context and present returned results.

**Not:** a lifecycle owned or duplicated by Lite.  
**Primary reading:** CH04, CH16, CH26; `ML-H03`, `ML-HC-01`.

### <a id="b06-term-matter"></a>Matter

A formal professional work/case object owned by the relevant service and carrying authority, lifecycle, responsibility and restricted context.

**Not:** a Lite Session, Candidate, Artifact or generic folder.  
**Primary reading:** CH04, CH08, CH22, CH26–CH29.

### <a id="b06-term-mgsn"></a>MGSN

The Mark Global Service Network boundary that owns provider identity, capability, Trust, Routing and cross-Organization collaboration depth.

**Not:** a public directory or provider score maintained by Lite.  
**Primary reading:** CH04, CH27; `ML-H04`, `ML-HC-02`.

## O

### <a id="b06-term-observation"></a>Observation

A normalized, source-linked fact or event captured for possible Product use, retaining source version, freshness, classification and uncertainty.

**Not:** canonical truth merely because it was extracted.  
**Primary reading:** CH08–CH09; `ML-O01`.

### <a id="b06-term-opportunity"></a>Opportunity

A formal commercial service possibility owned by the Opportunity Service, including owner, stage, forecast and lifecycle.

**Not:** a Service-Value Candidate, Reactivation Candidate, Prospect Candidate or Qualification Result.  
**Primary reading:** CH04, CH10, CH16, CH28; `ML-H07`, `ML-HC-05`.

### <a id="b06-term-organization"></a>Organization

The formal operating entity that provides roles, policies, shared data, responsibility and ownership context. Lite is individual-first but Organization-centered.

**Not:** an inferred company name from a prospect source.  
**Primary reading:** CH03–CH05, CH07–CH08, CH23.

### <a id="b06-term-organization-memory"></a>Organization Memory

Reviewed, sourced and scoped shared operating memory for an Organization, with an owner and correction/retirement path.

**Not:** one user’s habit, private preference or canonical Knowledge.  
**Primary reading:** CH23–CH25; `ML-M05`.

### <a id="b06-term-owning-service"></a>Owning Service

The service responsible for authoritative formal state, policy, lifecycle and mutation of a given business object.

**Not:** whichever Product currently displays the information.  
**Primary reading:** CH04, CH11, CH26–CH29.

## P

### <a id="b06-term-personal-memory"></a>Personal Memory

A narrowly scoped continuity record accepted for one user, such as a preference, private checklist or personal working convention.

**Not:** Organization policy, identity, signature or authority to act.  
**Primary reading:** CH23–CH25; `ML-M04`.

### <a id="b06-term-prepared-action"></a>Prepared Action

An exact-purpose Product-local action package stating subject, destination, consequence, sources, version, required checks, Review and confirmation.

**Not:** execution, completed communication, formal Task or protected action.  
**Primary reading:** CH11, CH20, CH28; `ML-W05`.

### <a id="b06-term-product"></a>Product

A coherent MarkOrbit user-facing system with defined users, value promise, journeys, records, boundaries, evidence and responsibility. In Book 06, Lite is the AI business operating system for independent trademark professionals and small agencies.

**Not:** a feature bundle, model, plan, database or interface alone.  
**Primary reading:** CH02–CH06, CH33.

### <a id="b06-term-product-baseline"></a>Product Baseline

The accepted semantic baseline in `B06-SPEC-0001–0004`, including Product-local record families, journeys, conformance scenarios, Handoff contracts, MVP acceptance criteria and historical reconciliation.

**Not:** an implementation schema.  
**Primary reading:** Specifications; CH30–CH33.

### <a id="b06-term-product-edition"></a>Product Edition

A bounded configuration of Lite for a population or operating context, such as Basic, Portfolio, Team, Local/Private or Professional.

**Not:** a separate Product constitution or broader authority.  
**Primary reading:** CH32–CH33.

### <a id="b06-term-product-increment"></a>Product Increment

A bounded capability or journey improvement mapped to accepted Product records, ownership boundaries, scenarios, evidence and risks.

**Not:** a silent Product Baseline or constitutional change.  
**Primary reading:** CH33.

### <a id="b06-term-prospect-candidate"></a>Prospect Candidate

A source-linked candidate person or Organization that may merit professional inspection and development under defined freshness, duplication, contactability and relevance rules.

**Not:** a Customer, Qualified Lead, buying intent or formal Opportunity.  
**Primary reading:** CH14–CH15; `ML-O05`, `ML-J03`.

### <a id="b06-term-publish-package"></a>Publish Package

A prepared package for an exact Artifact Version, audience, account, media, rights, disclaimer, timing, response route and evidence expectation.

**Not:** publication itself.  
**Primary reading:** CH20–CH21; `ML-W09`, `ML-HC-07`.

## Q

### <a id="b06-term-qualification-result"></a>Qualification Result

A Product-local, evidence-based assessment of whether a real service need exists, what remains unknown and which formal destination is appropriate.

**Not:** a formal Opportunity, customer instruction or MarkReg acceptance.  
**Primary reading:** CH10, CH15–CH16; `ML-O08`.

## R

### <a id="b06-term-recommendation"></a>Recommendation

A sourced and explainable presentation of relevance, options, trade-offs, uncertainty and proposed next steps.

**Not:** Decision, approval, instruction or automatic action.  
**Primary reading:** CH10; `ML-O07`.

### <a id="b06-term-render"></a>Render

Transformation of an exact Artifact Version into a usable representation such as PDF, image, web page, audio or video, with deterministic-field and format validation.

**Not:** professional Review or permission to publish.  
**Primary reading:** CH19; `ML-W07–W08`.

### <a id="b06-term-return"></a>Return

A sourced presentation of a destination’s typed result, including acceptance, more information required, rejection, block, failure, unknown outcome or formal reference.

**Not:** Lite-owned formal state.  
**Primary reading:** CH11, CH16, CH26–CH29; `ML-H02`.

### <a id="b06-term-reusable-asset"></a>Reusable Asset

A reviewed, versioned and rights-scoped template, component, example or pattern that may contribute to new purpose-bound work products.

**Not:** unrestricted public content or canonical Knowledge.  
**Primary reading:** CH21, CH24–CH25; `ML-M07`.

### <a id="b06-term-review"></a>Review

See [Human Review](#b06-term-human-review). In Book 06, capitalized Review refers to the controlled professional act, not generic reading or editing.

## S

### <a id="b06-term-service-value-candidate"></a>Service-Value Candidate

A source-linked explanation that an authorized customer/trademark context may contain a timely professional service need and customer value.

**Not:** formal Opportunity, mandatory service or revenue entitlement.  
**Primary reading:** CH09–CH12; `ML-O03`, `ML-J01`.

### <a id="b06-term-signal"></a>Signal

A Product-local connection between an Observation and authorized Customer/Trademark context that may justify further explanation.

**Not:** service need or purchase intent by itself.  
**Primary reading:** CH09; `ML-O02`.

### <a id="b06-term-source"></a>Source

The origin supporting an Observation, claim, Candidate, work product, memory or Handoff, including identity, version/time, freshness, authority, classification and permitted purpose.

**Not:** automatically authoritative merely because it is traceable.  
**Primary reading:** CH08–CH09, CH17, CH22–CH25.

### <a id="b06-term-structured-content"></a>Structured Content

Purpose-bound factual and narrative material assembled for an Artifact, including audience, claims, source references, uncertainties, deterministic fields and required Review.

**Not:** the finished Artifact.  
**Primary reading:** CH17; `ML-W01`.

## T

### <a id="b06-term-task"></a>Task

A formal unit of assigned work owned by the Task/Execution service, with responsible actor, lifecycle and completion meaning.

**Not:** Today item, reminder, Candidate or Prepared Action.  
**Primary reading:** CH04, CH07, CH11, CH28.

### <a id="b06-term-today"></a>Today

Lite’s daily business cockpit: a sourced, time-bound projection of relevant opportunities, work products, decisions, Returns and continuation needs.

**Not:** Lite’s complete Product identity, a formal Task list or deadline authority.  
**Primary reading:** CH07–CH11; `ML-S02–S03`.

### <a id="b06-term-trademark"></a>Trademark

A formal trademark identity/status object owned by the relevant Core, MarkReg or other Owning Service and consumed through authorized projections.

**Not:** a universal Lite `TrademarkAsset` record or an Artifact.  
**Primary reading:** CH04, CH08–CH09, CH12, CH26.

## U

### <a id="b06-term-unknown-external-outcome"></a>Unknown External Outcome

A typed state used when Lite cannot determine whether a consequential external action completed.

**Not:** completed, failed, rejected or safe to retry.  
**Primary reading:** CH15, CH20–CH21, CH28–CH31; `ML-SCN-18`.

### <a id="b06-term-user-confirmation"></a>User Confirmation

The user’s explicit decision that an exact action should occur to an exact destination, recipient or audience with a visible consequence.

**Not:** professional Human Review, destination acceptance or protected-action authority.  
**Primary reading:** CH11, CH15, CH20, CH28.

## W

### <a id="b06-term-workflow"></a>Workflow

A formal, governed sequence of work owned by the appropriate Task/Execution or other Owning Service.

**Not:** a Lite journey diagram, Today sequence or Product-local Continuation State.  
**Primary reading:** CH04, CH11, CH28.
