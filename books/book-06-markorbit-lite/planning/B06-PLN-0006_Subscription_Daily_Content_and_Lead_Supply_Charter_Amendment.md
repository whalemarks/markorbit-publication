## 2. Owner clarification

The prior Charter wording made `Today` sound like one abstract recommendation.

The actual intended Product is a low-cost subscription tool with a concrete recurring delivery promise:

```text
Monthly subscription
→ RMB 99 per active subscription month

Daily content entitlement
→ at least one publishable content unit per calendar day

Supported daily unit types
→ illustrated post
OR short video
OR Moments / private-domain copy

Growth supply
→ intention-customer candidates and development support
```

This amendment treats the Chinese statement “每天提供至少一篇图文、一个视频或朋友圈文案” as:

> At least one usable content outcome each day, with the format selected from illustrated post, short video or Moments copy.

It does not mean that all three formats must be supplied every day unless a later commercial plan explicitly promises that higher entitlement.

## 3. Corrected Product-market definition

The canonical architecture identity remains:

> **MarkOrbit Lite is a lightweight Workplace Product for trademark professionals.**

The concrete subscription Product promise becomes:

> **For RMB 99 per month, Lite gives a trademark professional a daily supply of usable content and an ongoing supply of intention-customer candidates, then helps the professional publish, develop, follow up and move qualified needs into governed service journeys.**

A concise market expression is:

> **每天有内容，每天有客户可开发。**

An English expression is:

> **Daily content and qualified growth opportunities for trademark professionals.**

The promise is operational, not merely descriptive.

```text
Today-centered
≠ one recommendation card

AI-assisted
≠ prompt-only writing

Daily content
≠ topic suggestion

Video delivered
≠ video brief prepared

Lead candidate
≠ guaranteed customer

Candidate customer
≠ formal Opportunity
```

## 4. Subscription contract baseline

### 4.1 Commercial baseline

```text
Initial reference price: RMB 99 / month
Billing model: recurring subscription
Primary buyer: independent trademark professional or small agency user
Primary value period: daily and weekly use, not occasional project use
```

The price is a Product-market baseline for Book 06 planning. It is not a permanent universal price and does not replace future edition, promotion, channel, tax or regional pricing decisions.

### 4.2 Daily content entitlement

An active subscription should receive at least one `Daily Content Unit` for each calendar day covered by the subscription.

A Daily Content Unit must be usable without requiring the user to begin from a blank page.

Eligible types include:

1. **Illustrated Post**
   - complete title;
   - complete body copy;
   - one or more usable graphics or a ready graphic layout;
   - platform or audience guidance;
   - source and rights notes where required.

2. **Short Video**
   - finished usable video representation, normally MP4;
   - title, caption and cover where the supported profile promises them;
   - accurate trademark names, figures, dates and CTA through deterministic rendering;
   - source, Asset and rights trace.

3. **Moments / Private-Domain Copy**
   - complete publishable wording;
   - optional matching image or recommendation card;
   - intended audience and CTA;
   - no cross-client confidential information.

The following do not independently satisfy the daily entitlement:

- a topic only;
- a headline only;
- raw AI notes;
- an unreviewed generic paragraph;
- a `videoBrief` without a delivered video when the unit type is declared as video;
- a scene plan without a delivered representation;
- a Publish Package that contains no usable content.

### 4.3 Content-type rotation

The RMB 99 plan may rotate content types rather than letting every subscriber demand a full video every day.

A conforming monthly plan may use an editorial mix such as:

```text
illustrated posts
+ Moments copy
+ template-rendered short videos
= at least one Daily Content Unit per day
```

The exact monthly mix, video quota and premium-credit model are commercial and implementation decisions to be specified later.

The system must disclose the promised mix before purchase and must not call a script or storyboard a delivered video.

### 4.4 Personalization levels

Daily content may be produced at several levels:

```text
Level 0 — shared industry content
Level 1 — jurisdiction / service / audience variation
Level 2 — organization identity, CTA and visual template
Level 3 — client, case or trademark-specific content under authorization
```

The base RMB 99 plan may rely mainly on Levels 0–2 to remain economically viable.

Level 3 requires stronger source, client, Matter, confidentiality and professional-review controls and may require a higher tier or explicit user action.

## 5. Daily Growth Desk

`Today` is retained as the home experience, but its practical meaning is revised.

The recommended surface name at Product level is **Daily Growth Desk**.

It should answer four concrete questions:

```text
What can I publish today?
Who may be worth developing or following up today?
What should I do next?
What happened to the content and outreach I already used?
```

The minimum daily surface contains:

### 5.1 Today's Content

- at least one entitled Daily Content Unit;
- format and target channel;
- source and freshness;
- preview and editable fields;
- download, copy or Publish preparation;
- use, skip, save and feedback actions.

### 5.2 Customer Opportunities

- intention-customer candidates;
- reason for inclusion;
- available contact or relationship context;
- relevant trademark/service signal;
- confidence and limitations;
- suggested development angle;
- qualification and follow-up preparation.

### 5.3 Action Queue

- publish or distribute the selected content;
- contact or follow up a selected candidate;
- prepare a case-supported explanation;
- request formal Opportunity creation;
- launch or continue a MarkReg journey;
- dismiss, suppress, correct or defer.

### 5.4 Results

- content copied, downloaded, delivered or published;
- user-reported versus platform-confirmed result;
- candidate contacted or not contacted;
- response received;
- formal Opportunity returned;
- MarkReg result returned;
- feedback, correction and suppression.

Today therefore remains the Product shell, but **daily supply is the product promise**.

## 6. Two primary supply loops

Book 06 should no longer describe one generic recommendation loop as the only core loop.

### 6.1 Daily Content Supply Loop

```text
editorial calendar / trademark data / governed Knowledge / approved cases
→ content opportunity
→ Content brief
→ Content and Artifact preparation
→ Render and optional Edit
→ Daily Content Unit
→ user preview and adjustment
→ copy / download / Publish Package
→ final user confirmation or manual publication
→ publication/use result
→ performance and preference feedback
```

The Product must produce the usable Artifact before asking the user what to do with an abstract recommendation.

### 6.2 Intention-Customer Supply Loop

```text
authorized public, organization, client or trademark observation
→ commercial or professional signal
→ intention-customer candidate
→ reason, source and confidence
→ user qualification
→ development angle and follow-up package
→ final recipient confirmation
→ Communication or manual outreach
→ response / no response / invalid candidate
→ formal Opportunity request where appropriate
→ service or MarkReg Handoff
→ outcome and learning
```

### 6.3 Combined growth loop

The two loops may converge:

```text
Daily Content Unit
+ intention-customer candidate
+ case / service context
→ targeted development action
→ client response
→ qualified service need
→ governed Opportunity / MarkReg / service journey
```

Content is both a public/private-domain growth asset and a supporting resource for direct development.

## 7. Intention-customer candidate definition

An `Intention-Customer Candidate` is a Product-local growth candidate, not a guaranteed lead and not a formal Opportunity.

It should preserve where available:

- candidate identity or candidate organization;
- lawful and authorized source;
- contactability status;
- relevant trademark, brand, jurisdiction or service signal;
- reason for recommendation;
- estimated relevance and confidence;
- freshness;
- duplicate state;
- client/relationship ownership context;
- permitted use and outreach channel;
- suggested service and development angle;
- opt-out, suppression or correction state;
- user disposition;
- later response and formalization result.

The Product must distinguish:

```text
publicly discoverable organization
≠ permission to contact by every channel

contact detail found
≠ current contact detail

signal detected
≠ purchase intent proven

intention-customer candidate
≠ existing Customer

candidate accepted by user
≠ formal Opportunity created

formal Opportunity
≠ order or revenue
```

## 8. Lead-supply promise and quantity

The Charter accepts intention-customer supply as a second core Product output.

It does not yet promise a fixed number of new candidates every calendar day because candidate availability and lawful quality depend on:

- supported jurisdictions and services;
- data-source coverage;
- freshness;
- duplication and suppression;
- user geography and customer focus;
- permitted contact data;
- confidence threshold;
- prior relationship ownership.

The later Product baseline must decide:

- minimum weekly or monthly quantity;
- qualification levels;
- replacement treatment for invalid candidates;
- duplicate rules;
- exclusive versus non-exclusive candidates;
- contact-data scope;
- supported acquisition sources;
- compliance and opt-out rules.

The Product should prefer fewer explainable, contactable and relevant candidates over a large unverified list.

## 9. Artifact, Render, Edit and Publish interpretation

The owner-supplied Artifact/Render/Edit/Publish proposal is accepted as important supporting architecture input, subject to Books 01–05.

```text
governed data and Knowledge
→ Content / Artifact preparation
→ Render
→ optional Edit
→ Publish or Delivery preparation
→ user-confirmed distribution
→ use and conversion result
```

### 9.1 Artifact

Daily deliverables should be governed business Artifacts, not raw model outputs.

Relevant Product-specific Artifact forms may include:

- Daily Content Unit;
- Graphic Post;
- Moments Copy;
- Video Project;
- rendered Short Video;
- client development card;
- case-supported explanation;
- recommendation page;
- Publish Package;
- follow-up package.

Book 06 must continue to preserve:

```text
Content ≠ Artifact
Artifact ≠ Document
Artifact ≠ file
Artifact ready ≠ reviewed
Artifact reviewed ≠ published
```

### 9.2 Render

Render converts structured content, data, templates and Assets into a usable representation such as PDF, PNG, SVG, MP3 or MP4.

For the daily subscription promise:

- graphic output should use deterministic templates for exact Chinese text and trademark data;
- video output should use deterministic text, logo, fee, date and CTA rendering;
- AI-generated images or B-roll may supplement the output but must not control exact professional facts;
- a render failure means the promised video or image unit has not been delivered.

### 9.3 Edit

Edit improves or transforms existing media, such as:

- removing pauses;
- captions;
- trimming;
- logo and CTA application;
- technical correction;
- derivative short clips.

Edit is not the first requirement for every daily unit and may remain a later capability.

### 9.4 Publish

Initial Publish modes should remain:

```text
copy text
+ download media
+ open destination platform
+ user checks final account and audience
+ user confirms publication
+ result or URL recorded
```

A local publish helper may later fill permitted fields while credentials remain local.

The RMB 99 content promise does not authorize cloud-side account takeover or silent publication.

## 10. Revised Product promise

Lite promises the subscriber:

1. a dependable daily supply of at least one usable content outcome;
2. content formats suitable for actual public or private-domain output;
3. an ongoing supply of explainable intention-customer candidates;
4. practical development angles and follow-up preparation;
5. case, data and Knowledge support where authorized;
6. copy, download, Render, Delivery and Publish preparation;
7. user control over final claims, recipient, account and external action;
8. formal Handoff into Opportunity, Communication, MarkReg, MGSN, Review or Execution where required;
9. returned result, correction and learning without false formal state.

## 12. Revised MVP 0

The prior `Attention-to-Prepared-Value Loop` is too abstract as the first commercial validation.

The revised MVP 0 is:

> **30-Day Daily Content and Customer Development Loop**

Minimum scope:

```text
one active RMB 99 reference subscription
→ 30 calendar-day entitlement window
→ at least one Daily Content Unit per covered day
→ illustrated post / Moments copy / selected template video rotation
→ copy, download and manual Publish support
→ usage feedback

PLUS

supported intention-customer candidate feed
→ source and recommendation reason
→ user qualification
→ one follow-up package
→ user-confirmed outreach or Communication handoff
→ response / invalid / no-response outcome
→ formal Opportunity or MarkReg handoff where appropriate
```

The MVP must test both supply promises:

- **Can Lite deliver useful content consistently enough to justify daily use?**
- **Can Lite surface sufficiently relevant candidates to create real development actions?**

The MVP may stop before automated external send, autonomous publication, formal Opportunity mutation or any External Protected Action.

## 13. Revised reference journey

The first reference journey becomes the **Daily Content-to-Customer Development Journey**.

```text
subscriber profile and content calendar
→ today's usable content Artifact
→ user copies, downloads or prepares publication
→ content is published or used in private-domain outreach
→ Lite supplies an intention-customer candidate
→ user inspects source, reason and relevance
→ Lite prepares a development angle and follow-up package
→ user confirms recipient, claims and channel
→ Communication/manual outreach
→ response and qualification
→ formal Opportunity request or MarkReg Handoff
→ returned result

```

Existing-client value remains an important journey variant, but it is no longer the only first-loop definition.

## 14. Economic and quality guardrails

A RMB 99 monthly price creates strict operating requirements.

### 14.1 What should be standardized

- shared industry and jurisdiction content pools;
- reusable editorial calendars;
- controlled templates;
- deterministic graphic/video rendering;
- organization identity variables;
- format rotation;
- batch production;
- reusable case and Knowledge assets;
- self-service preview and minor edits.

### 14.2 What should not be promised without a higher tier

- one fully bespoke expert article every day;
- one bespoke rendered video every day for every subscriber;
- unlimited manual professional review;
- unlimited client-specific case analysis;
- guaranteed lead conversion;
- exclusive high-intent leads without a separate commercial model;
- unrestricted multi-platform publishing;
- unlimited cloud rendering or digital-human generation.

### 14.3 Cost truth

The Product must measure:

- content-generation cost per active subscriber;
- render cost by format;
- human review cost;
- storage and bandwidth;
- candidate acquisition and validation cost;
- invalid candidate replacement cost;
- support cost;
- gross margin and cohort retention.

Low price does not justify low source quality, fake leads or content that cannot be used.

## 15. Revised metrics

### Daily content metrics

- daily entitlement fulfillment rate;
- percentage of units actually usable without regeneration;
- copy/download/publish preparation rate;
- format mix;
- video render success;
- user edit rate;
- content repetition and fatigue;
- source, rights and factual defect rate;
- 7-day and 30-day active use;
- subscription renewal and cancellation reason.

### Intention-customer metrics

- candidates supplied;
- valid identity/contactability rate;
- user acceptance and rejection reason;
- duplicate and stale rate;
- contact attempt rate;
- response rate;
- qualified need rate;
- formal Opportunity request rate;
- MarkReg/service Handoff rate;
- complaint, opt-out and compliance defects;
- cost per valid candidate and per qualified outcome.

### Combined metrics

- content-assisted outreach rate;
- content-to-response rate;
- case-supported follow-up rate;
- subscriber time saved;
- monthly user-perceived value relative to RMB 99;
- gross margin without quality dilution.

## 17. Effect on prior Charter wording

This amendment supersedes the following interpretations where they conflict:

- `Today` as one abstract recommendation;
- one signal card as the primary commercial promise;
- the Existing Client Value Journey as the sole first-loop definition;
- MVP 0 as only one signal-to-follow-up package;
- content generation as an optional supporting feature;
- a video brief or script as sufficient video delivery.

It preserves:

- Lite as a focused Product rather than the whole Workplace;
- candidate-before-canonical behavior;
- Human control over external action;
- formal Opportunity ownership outside Lite;
- Communication, Publish, MarkReg and MGSN Handoffs;
- Artifact, Render, Edit and Publish distinctions;
- privacy, source, rights and professional-review boundaries.
