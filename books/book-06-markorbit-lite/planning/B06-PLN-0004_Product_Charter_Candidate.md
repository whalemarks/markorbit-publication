## 4. Canonical Product identity

> **MarkOrbit Lite is the AI business operating system for independent trademark professionals and small trademark agencies.**

In MarkOrbit architecture terms, it remains:

> **a lightweight Workplace Product for trademark professionals, individual-first but organization-centered.**

The two descriptions serve different purposes:

```text
User-facing identity
AI business operating system

Architectural identity
lightweight Workplace Product
```

Lite is designed to help the user:

- identify business and professional value hidden in fragmented data;
- decide what deserves attention;
- prepare trustworthy work products and customer actions;
- move consequential work to the correct governed Product or service;
- preserve results, corrections, cases and reusable capability;
- improve with continued use without taking over professional responsibility.

## 5. Why Lite exists in the MarkOrbit portfolio

MarkOrbit already defines:

```text
Core
→ shared meaning and formal object ownership

Execution
→ governed work, review and protected action

Workplace
→ full organization operating context

MarkReg
→ full-lifecycle trademark Product journeys

MGSN
→ service capability, Trust and cross-organization network depth
```

The missing Product is a lightweight, affordable and frequently used operating surface through which an individual professional or small team can:

```text
observe
→ understand
→ prepare
→ act through the correct boundary
→ receive outcomes
→ retain useful capability
```

Lite therefore becomes the low-friction daily operating layer and ecosystem entry point, not a second Core, Workplace, MarkReg or MGSN.

## 6. Primary users

### 6.1 Independent trademark professional

This user often owns the complete relationship between business development and professional delivery.

Typical conditions include:

- customer, trademark and case information spread across chat, email, files, spreadsheets and official sources;
- limited time for systematic follow-up and content production;
- dependence on personal memory and repeated manual preparation;
- difficulty turning prior cases into reusable capability safely;
- need to cooperate with foreign agents and specialist providers;
- need for a lightweight Product rather than enterprise administration.

### 6.2 Small trademark agency or team

This team needs:

- shared but bounded customer, case and template context;
- coordination without a heavy Workplace rollout;
- clear personal, organization, customer and Matter scopes;
- reusable operating memory;
- consistent client-facing work products;
- governed entry into MarkReg and MGSN.

### 6.3 Larger Workplace participant using Lite mode

A larger Organization may expose Lite as:

- a personal business cockpit;
- a mobile preparation surface;
- a local/private companion;
- a focused growth or professional-work profile;
- an embedded entry into MarkReg, MGSN, Review or Execution.

Lite mode does not replace the parent Workplace or expand the participant's authority.

## 7. Primary Product problem

Trademark professionals do not mainly lack AI text generation.

They face fragmentation between:

- customers and trademark portfolios;
- official events and service opportunities;
- prospects and trustworthy qualification;
- cases and reusable experience;
- professional Knowledge and client-facing explanation;
- drafts and usable business work products;
- personal memory and organization capability;
- recommendations and formal action;
- local/private materials and shared systems;
- completed work and future business development.

The resulting failures include:

- missed service opportunities in existing customer portfolios;
- inconsistent customer follow-up;
- repeated preparation from a blank page;
- valuable cases that remain unusable or are reused unsafely;
- generic content disconnected from actual services and customer needs;
- unqualified lead lists with little commercial value;
- loss of context when moving into MarkReg or external provider work;
- knowledge that disappears when one person forgets or leaves;
- unsafe shortcuts from AI output to external action.

The Product problem is:

> How can a trademark professional turn fragmented customers, trademarks, cases, Knowledge and market signals into timely business actions and trustworthy professional work products, move qualified needs into governed service delivery, and accumulate reusable capability over time?

## 8. Product promise

Lite promises to help the user:

1. **see** what is commercially and professionally important now;
2. **understand** the customer, trademark, case, source and service context;
3. **choose** whether to follow up, prepare, defer, reject, correct or hand off;
4. **produce** a useful work product rather than stop at a suggestion or raw draft;
5. **develop** customers and service opportunities with relevant reasons and materials;
6. **handoff** formal work to MarkReg, MGSN, Review, Execution or an Owning Service;
7. **receive** typed results without inventing formal or external outcomes;
8. **retain** corrections, outcomes, cases, templates and preferences within allowed scope;
9. **improve** future work through governed personal and organization continuity.

## 9. Four core Product loops

### 9.1 Customer and Service Growth Loop

Purpose:

- find relevant value in existing customers, historical customers and authorized prospects;
- connect customer context to a real trademark or professional service need;
- prepare the explanation and follow-up;
- qualify the response;
- move genuine needs into formal Opportunity or MarkReg journeys.

Preferred priority:

```text
existing customer portfolio opportunity
→ historical customer reactivation
→ user-owned prospect development
→ platform-supplied prospect candidates
```

Canonical flow:

```text
authorized customer / trademark / market observation
→ service-value candidate
→ source and relevance explanation
→ customer or prospect qualification
→ development angle and work product
→ final user confirmation
→ outreach or Communication Handoff
→ response and need qualification
→ formal Opportunity / MarkReg / service Handoff
→ returned outcome
```

A candidate is not a formal Opportunity, Order or revenue event.

### 9.2 Professional Work Product Loop

Purpose:

- convert trusted data, Knowledge, cases and user intent into usable professional or commercial results;
- preserve audience, purpose, source and version;
- support delivery, sharing or publication without confusing preparation with external effect.

Canonical flow:

```text
customer / trademark / case / Knowledge context
→ structured Content and business intent
→ Artifact candidate
→ review and correction
→ Render and optional Edit
→ usable work product
→ Delivery / Publish / Communication preparation
→ user-confirmed or governed operation
→ result and feedback
```

Work products may include:

- customer analysis and service recommendation;
- trademark layout proposal;
- quotation or fee explanation;
- case explanation;
- client confirmation package;
- recommendation page;
- report, PDF or graphic;
- public or private-domain content;
- short video;
- follow-up package;
- MarkReg or MGSN Handoff package.

Daily content is one useful work-product supply pattern. It is not the constitutional identity of Lite.

### 9.3 Professional Memory and Business Asset Loop

Purpose:

- preserve decisions, corrections, outcomes and useful cases;
- separate personal memory, organization memory, reusable Assets and governed Knowledge;
- make future work more relevant and less repetitive;
- create durable value that a generic AI tool cannot reproduce from one prompt.

Canonical flow:

```text
practice and outcome
→ sourced observation or correction
→ personal / organization memory candidate
→ reviewed case lesson or reusable Asset candidate
→ approved reuse within scope
→ future recommendation or work product
→ later outcome and correction
```

Required distinctions:

```text
personal note ≠ organization truth
case experience ≠ canonical Knowledge
successful result ≠ universal rule
reusable Asset ≠ public material
Evidence ≠ permission to publish
AI memory ≠ unrestricted data pool
```

### 9.4 MarkOrbit Ecosystem Handoff Loop

Purpose:

- let Lite discover and prepare value without duplicating formal lifecycle ownership;
- preserve context when moving into specialist Products and services;
- return results to the user's daily operating context.

Canonical flow:

```text
Lite discovers and prepares
→ typed Handoff Envelope
→ destination Product or Owning Service revalidates
→ governed work and formal action
→ typed Return Envelope
→ Lite presents outcome and next value
```

Destinations include:

- MarkReg;
- MGSN;
- Human Review;
- Book 03 Execution;
- Communication Service;
- Opportunity Service;
- Task Service;
- another authorized Product or Workplace surface.

## 10. Today as the daily business cockpit

`Today` is not the Product identity and is not limited to one recommendation card.

It is the daily operating cockpit through which the four loops become actionable.

A conforming Today surface may contain:

```text
Customer and Service Opportunities
+ Professional Work Products
+ Decisions and Prepared Actions
+ MarkReg / MGSN / service outcomes
+ Follow-up responses
+ personal and organization continuity
```

Today should answer:

1. Which customer or trademark deserves attention?
2. Why does it matter?
3. What work product or action is ready?
4. What must I decide or confirm?
5. Which formal Product or service should receive the work?
6. What happened to previous actions?

Today is not a universal Task list and does not own deadline truth.

## 14. Artifact, Render, Edit and Publish

The following chain is an important Product production capability:

```text
governed data and Knowledge
→ structured Content and intent
→ Artifact
→ Render
→ optional Edit
→ Delivery / Publish / Communication preparation
→ user-confirmed or governed operation
→ result
```

Required distinctions:

```text
Content ≠ Artifact
Artifact ≠ Document
Artifact ≠ Evidence
Artifact ≠ file
Render complete ≠ professionally approved
Publish Package ready ≠ published
user-reported outcome ≠ platform-confirmed outcome
```

Tools such as document renderers, graphic renderers, video frameworks, TTS providers, editing Skills and local publishing helpers are implementation candidates. They must not define Product purpose.

## 15. Data, privacy and AI constitution

Lite must preserve:

- Public Source;
- Shared Governed;
- Organization Private;
- Client Restricted;
- Matter Restricted;
- Personal Private;
- Local Device / Local Vault;
- Trusted Network Exchange.

Every material candidate should preserve where applicable:

- source and version;
- controller and active Organization;
- customer, Matter and subject scope;
- purpose;
- classification;
- freshness;
- confidence and limitations;
- allowed destination;
- transformation history;
- expiry and review requirement.

AI may:

- summarize, classify, extract and compare;
- identify gaps and candidates;
- rank and explain;
- prepare work products and Handoffs;
- support authorized local/private retrieval;
- learn from explicit corrections and sourced outcomes within scope.

AI may not silently:

- become the professional identity;
- sign, approve or publish;
- choose final external recipients;
- create formal state;
- appoint a provider;
- treat case outcomes as universal law;
- pool unrestricted personal or organization memory;
- retry unknown external actions.

## 17. Reference journeys

The Product baseline should include at least four reference journeys.

### J1 — Existing Customer Portfolio Opportunity

```text
customer and trademark portfolio
→ service-value observation
→ explanation and relevant case / Knowledge
→ customer work product and follow-up package
→ user confirmation and contact
→ qualified response
→ MarkReg or formal Opportunity
→ returned outcome and next opportunity
```

This is the recommended first primary journey because it provides high relevance, stronger data authority and direct MarkReg continuity.

### J2 — Historical Customer Reactivation

```text
inactive relationship and authorized history
→ relevant current trigger
→ reactivation candidate
→ updated work product and message
→ user-confirmed contact
→ response and qualification
→ formal service journey
```

### J3 — Prospect Candidate Development

```text
authorized public or user-owned source
→ Prospect Candidate
→ source, relevance and contactability review
→ development package
→ user confirmation
→ response and qualification
→ formal Customer / Opportunity request where appropriate
```

### J4 — Case-to-Work-Product

```text
authorized case context
→ reviewed lesson or approved case Asset
→ audience-specific Artifact
→ Render and review
→ Delivery / Publish / customer use
→ result and correction
```

## 18. MVP strategy

### MVP 0 — Customer Opportunity-to-Governed-Service Loop

Goal:

> Prove that Lite can identify one relevant service opportunity from authorized customer and trademark context, prepare a trustworthy work product and follow-up, support user-confirmed contact, and move a qualified need into MarkReg or another governed service journey.

Minimum scope:

- one Organization context;
- a small authorized customer and trademark set;
- one supported opportunity type;
- one explainable candidate;
- one case or Knowledge-supported work product;
- one follow-up package;
- final user confirmation;
- manual or Communication-Service Handoff;
- one MarkReg Launch and Return path;
- response, no response, rejection, correction and failure states;
- explicit personal, organization, client and Matter boundaries;
- outcome and reuse feedback.

MVP 0 may include daily content and prospect supply as supporting experiments, but success is not defined by content volume or lead volume alone.

### Later increments

- broader customer and trademark signals;
- historical customer reactivation;
- platform-supplied prospect candidates;
- richer work products and Render formats;
- Case Center depth;
- small-team Review and responsibility;
- MGSN candidate and collaboration surfaces;
- commercial plan and entitlement automation;
- policy-bounded external automation after evidence.

## 19. Success model

Lite should be evaluated across:

| Layer | Core question |
| --- | --- |
| recurring use | Does Lite become part of the user's weekly operating rhythm? |
| opportunity quality | Are surfaced needs relevant, timely and explainable? |
| work-product value | Does the user actually use the prepared result? |
| customer action | Does the user contact or follow up with confidence? |
| qualified response | Does the action produce a meaningful response or need? |
| Handoff quality | Can MarkReg or another destination accept the context without avoidable re-entry? |
| capability accumulation | Do cases, corrections and outcomes improve future work? |
| safety and privacy | Are restricted data and consequential actions controlled? |
| commercial sustainability | Do plans create retention and sustainable gross margin? |

Candidate metrics include:

- weekly active professional rate;
- accepted and rejected opportunity-candidate rate;
- time from signal to usable work product;
- work-product use and modification rate;
- customer contact and response rate;
- qualified service-need rate;
- MarkReg Handoff acceptance and rework;
- repeated-use and retention rate;
- case/Asset reuse under approved scope;
- source, privacy and rights defects;
- unsafe-action block count;
- cost to produce and support each commercial plan.

## 20. Non-goals

Lite is not:

- a generic AI chat application;
- a daily content factory detached from customer and service context;
- a lead-list marketplace;
- a universal CRM;
- a universal case-management system;
- a smaller clone of the full Workplace;
- a second Core or Execution system;
- a lightweight clone of MarkReg;
- a second MGSN;
- an autonomous sales agent;
- an autonomous lawyer or trademark professional;
- a global unrestricted data pool;
- an automatic publisher;
- a shortcut around Permission, Policy, Human Review or Owning Services.

## 21. Zero-tolerance conditions

A pilot or release must block the affected capability if Lite:

- exposes one customer or Matter's restricted information to another;
- presents a candidate as a formal Opportunity, Task, Communication, Document, Evidence or official result;
- invents or hides source, status, fee, deadline or case outcome;
- sends, publishes or performs external action without required confirmation or authority;
- promotes private experience into shared Knowledge without review;
- appoints a provider automatically;
- retries an unknown external result unsafely;
- treats local access as synchronization or professional authority;
- lets revenue ranking override client interest or professional risk;
- uses content count, lead count or activity volume as proof of customer value.
