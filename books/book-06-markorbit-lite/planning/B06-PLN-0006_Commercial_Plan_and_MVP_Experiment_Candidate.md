## 2. Purpose

This record preserves the valuable commercial ideas around:

- an RMB 99 monthly entry plan;
- recurring content supply;
- customer/prospect candidate supply;
- Artifact, Render, Edit and Publish support;
- low-friction MarkReg conversion;
- 30-day Product-market testing.

```text

```

## 3. Candidate entry plan

### Working name

```text
Lite Basic / Growth Starter
```

### Reference price

```text
RMB 99 / month
```

The price is a planning hypothesis, not a permanent or universal price.

It may later vary by:

- edition;
- billing channel;
- promotion;
- tax;
- region;
- organization size;
- work-product quotas;
- Render or TTS cost;
- support and Review level.

## 4. Candidate target user

The plan is intended primarily for:

- independent trademark professionals;
- early-stage or small trademark agencies;
- professionals who need a low-risk first step into MarkOrbit;
- users not yet ready to connect a complete customer and trademark portfolio;
- users seeking recurring growth support and practical work products.

It is not intended to represent the full value of Lite for mature agencies or larger Workplaces.

## 5. Candidate plan value

A conforming entry plan may combine:

1. recurring professional content or work-product supply;
2. customer and service opportunity prompts;
3. a bounded number of Prospect Candidates;
4. basic customer-development packages;
5. selected Artifact and Render quotas;
6. manual or local-helper Publish support;
7. MarkReg service entry and Handoff;
8. limited personal preferences and continuity;
9. upgrade paths into portfolio-connected or team editions.

## 6. Daily content hypothesis

A daily-content entitlement is retained as a strong acquisition and habit hypothesis.

Possible formulation:

```text
at least one usable content unit per covered day
selected from:
- illustrated post
- Moments / private-domain copy
- template-rendered short video
```

A usable content unit must not require the user to begin from a blank page.

The following do not count by themselves:

- topic;
- headline;
- raw AI notes;
- generic paragraph;
- script without the promised representation;
- scene plan;
- video brief;
- empty Publish Package.

If a plan promises a video, the delivered entitlement must include a usable video representation.

## 7. Content economics

The RMB 99 hypothesis is viable only if the base plan relies mainly on:

- shared editorial calendars;
- jurisdiction/service/audience variants;
- deterministic templates;
- batch generation;
- controlled organization branding;
- format rotation;
- limited high-cost video and TTS use;
- limited manual expert Review;
- measurable storage, Render and support cost.

The base plan should not imply:

- one bespoke expert article per subscriber per day;
- one bespoke video per subscriber per day;
- unlimited revisions;
- unlimited AI or Render consumption;
- unlimited human legal or professional Review.

## 8. Customer and prospect supply hypothesis

The plan may provide multiple value levels rather than label every record an “intention customer.”

### Level A — Existing Customer Opportunity

An authorized customer or portfolio observation indicates a relevant service need.

This is the preferred high-value supply because context, relationship and ownership are clearer.

### Level B — Historical Customer Reactivation

A prior relationship has a current, relevant and permitted reactivation reason.

### Level C — User-Owned Prospect Candidate

The user imports or owns the prospect source and Lite helps qualify and prepare development.

### Level D — Platform-Supplied Prospect Candidate

The platform identifies a potentially relevant organization through a lawful or authorized source.

Required distinctions:

```text
Prospect Candidate
≠ Qualified Lead

Service signal
≠ proven purchase intent

contact detail found
≠ contact permitted by every channel

candidate selected
≠ formal Opportunity
```

## 9. Candidate quantity and quality

The first commercial plan should not lock a large daily quantity before validation.

A later entitlement specification must define:

- supported source classes;
- minimum weekly or monthly quantity;
- freshness threshold;
- duplicate and suppression rules;
- contactability standard;
- service relevance threshold;
- invalid-candidate replacement;
- exclusive or non-exclusive status;
- outreach and opt-out rules;
- user feedback and correction;
- plan-specific quota and carryover.

The Product should prefer fewer explainable and relevant candidates over a large unverified list.

## 12. 30-day experiment

### Experiment name

```text
30-Day Lite Growth Starter Experiment
```

### Goal

> Determine whether a low-price plan can create recurring use and lead users into the deeper Lite business loops without reducing Lite to content production or lead supply.

### Required experiment tracks

#### Track 1 — Existing customer opportunity

```text
small authorized customer/trademark set
→ one relevant service-value candidate
→ work product and follow-up
→ user-confirmed contact
→ response and qualification
→ MarkReg or formal service Handoff
```

This is the primary outcome track.

#### Track 2 — Recurring content supply

```text
editorial plan
→ usable content/work product
→ copy, download or Publish preparation
→ actual use and feedback
```

This tests habit, acquisition and professional visibility.

#### Track 3 — Prospect candidate supply

```text
authorized source
→ Prospect Candidate
→ user qualification
→ development package
→ contact / no contact
→ response / invalid / suppression
```

This tests whether new-customer supply can become a scalable capability.

### Economics

## 14. Pass and fail criteria

The experiment must not pass merely because it:

- generates 30 files;
- fills a daily calendar;
- supplies many organization names;
- records high click volume;
- creates unqualified Opportunity records;
- counts user-reported publication as platform-confirmed publication.

A meaningful pass requires evidence that users:

- find relevant customer/service value;
- use the prepared work product;
- take a customer action;
- receive a meaningful response or learn from rejection;
- enter a governed service journey where appropriate;
- return to Lite because context and capability accumulate;
- perceive value above the plan price;
- can be served with sustainable cost and acceptable risk.

## 15. Guardrails

The plan must not:

- expose private customer or Matter data;
- treat public data as unrestricted outreach permission;
- supply stale or fabricated prospects;
- count raw drafts as completed work products;
- promise video while delivering only a script;
- publish or send externally without required user confirmation;
- create formal Opportunity, Task, Communication, Order or revenue state inside Lite;
- use low price as justification for unsafe or misleading output.
