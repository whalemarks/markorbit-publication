## 5. Manuscript rule

Every chapter must:

- project the accepted Charter and Specifications;
- preserve Product/formal-state distinctions;
- avoid silent upstream change;
- avoid implementation-specific commitments;
- keep commercial experiments subordinate to Product identity;
- connect its argument to the accepted chapter role;
- avoid pre-writing later Parts beyond necessary transitions.

A manuscript chapter may explain a controlled record but may not change its meaning.

## 9. MVP and conformance rule

MVP 0 remains:

```text

```

The pass rule remains:

```text
ML-AC-01–ML-AC-11: mandatory
ML-AC-12: Product/commercial evidence required
commercial thresholds: experiment-specific
```

All BLOCKING scenarios must pass. MAJOR findings must be closed before a baseline or release. No positive commercial metric offsets a zero-tolerance violation.

## 10. Commercial-plan rule

```text
Product Constitution
→ Product Edition
→ Commercial Plan
→ Entitlement Window
→ Fulfillment Observation
```

Price, content cadence, Prospect Candidate quantity, Render quota, support and Review level may change independently of the Product Charter.

Commercial-plan changes may not grant broader data access, professional authority, provider appointment, protected-action authority or Review bypass.

## 11. Evolution rule

Changes must be classified as:

```text
Class A — Editorial clarification
Class B — Implementation change
Class C — Product Increment
Class D — Product Baseline change
Class E — Constitutional change
```

Class D requires Product Baseline versioning and owner acceptance.

Class E requires explicit Product Charter revision, cross-Book review and owner acceptance.

Historical models, prototypes and successful implementations remain evidence until mapped into the accepted authority chain.

## 12. Whole-book review rule

After Wave 7 merge, a separate whole-book review must examine:

- CH00–CH33 continuity;
- controlled-term consistency;
- duplicate or conflicting explanations;
- chapter metadata;
- complete controlled-range coverage;
- scenario and Handoff coverage;
- cross-Book boundaries;
- reader apparatus needs;
- open findings;
- structural and editorial readiness.

The whole book becomes Complete Draft 1 only after that review is accepted and findings are closed.
