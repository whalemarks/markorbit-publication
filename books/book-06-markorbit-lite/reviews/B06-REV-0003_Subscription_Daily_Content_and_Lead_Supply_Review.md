## 2. Review question

> Does the clarified RMB 99 subscription model, daily content entitlement and intention-customer supply create a concrete and viable Lite Product promise without violating Books 01–05, misrepresenting content delivery, collapsing Product-local candidates into formal Opportunities or authorizing unsafe external action?

## 3. Owner clarification reviewed

The Review accepts that the previous “one Today recommendation” wording was too abstract.

The intended commercial Product is:

```text
RMB 99 monthly subscription
→ at least one usable content unit per calendar day
→ illustrated post OR short video OR Moments copy
→ ongoing intention-customer candidates
→ development and governed service Handoff support
```

## 4. Product clarity result

```text
Intention-customer supply as core promise: PASS
Today retained only as recommendation card: REJECTED
Today as Daily Growth Desk: PASS
Topic/brief counted as delivered content: REJECTED

```

The revised promise is substantially clearer and more commercially testable than an abstract attention recommendation.

## 6. Daily content delivery review

A Daily Content Unit must be a usable outcome.

### Accepted

- complete illustrated post;
- complete Moments/private-domain copy;
- finished template-rendered short video;
- disclosed format rotation;
- content calendar and reusable templates;
- organization identity and CTA variables;
- copy, download and manual Publish preparation;
- source and rights trace.

### Rejected

- a topic only;
- a headline only;
- unreviewed generic filler;
- script-only delivery presented as video;
- duplicated content used only to satisfy a daily count;
- incorrect trademark names, logos, dates or claims;
- AI-image text treated as precise business output.

**Result:** PASS WITH QUALITY CONTROL.

## 7. RMB 99 economic review

The price can be plausible only if the base tier relies on:

- shared editorial planning;
- reusable templates;
- batch generation;
- deterministic rendering;
- controlled personalization;
- format rotation;
- self-service review and minor editing;
- limited manual professional intervention;
- measured video and cloud-render cost.

The price is not plausibly compatible with:

- one bespoke expert article per subscriber per day;
- one bespoke video per subscriber per day;
- unlimited human review;
- unlimited client-specific case analysis;
- unlimited digital-human or premium TTS use;
- exclusive high-intent leads without separate economics.

The Charter correctly treats RMB 99 as an initial reference price rather than a permanent universal price.

**Result:** PASS WITH COST AND MARGIN EVIDENCE REQUIRED.

## 8. Intention-customer review

The Product may provide candidate organizations or contacts when it preserves:

- lawful or authorized source;
- recommendation reason;
- service relevance;
- freshness;
- contactability;
- permitted-use context;
- confidence and limitations;
- duplicate and suppression state;
- user disposition;
- later response and formalization result.

The Product must not imply:

```text
candidate supplied
= customer interested

contact found
= contact permitted

user accepted candidate
= formal Opportunity

formal Opportunity
= order

order
= revenue collected
```

A fixed candidate quantity is appropriately deferred until the source and quality model is validated.

**Result:** PASS WITH QUALITY AND COMPLIANCE CONTROL.

## 9. Artifact / Render / Edit / Publish review

The owner-supplied proposal is consistent with Book 04 when treated as supporting capabilities rather than new unrestricted authority.

```text
Content preparation
→ Artifact
→ Render
→ optional Edit
→ Publish Package
→ final user confirmation
→ Delivery / Publish evidence
```

Important accepted distinctions:

```text
Artifact ≠ file
Render ≠ approval
Edit ≠ professional review
Publish Package ≠ published
user-reported publish ≠ platform-confirmed publish
```

**Result:** PASS.

## 10. Revised MVP review

The `30-Day Daily Content and Customer Development Loop` is a stronger commercial MVP than one abstract signal card.

It tests:

- subscription entitlement;
- daily production reliability;
- content use;
- format economics;
- intention-customer relevance;
- follow-up preparation;
- response and qualification;
- formal Opportunity or MarkReg Handoff;
- retention and perceived value.

The MVP remains bounded because it may stop before automated send, autonomous Publish, formal Opportunity mutation or protected professional action.

**Result:** PASS.

## 12. Risks requiring explicit validation

1. **Content fatigue** — daily output becomes repetitive or generic.
2. **False fulfillment** — drafts and briefs are counted as usable outcomes.
3. **Video economics** — rendering, TTS, storage or support exceeds the price model.
4. **Lead dilution** — quantity replaces relevance and contactability.
5. **Compliance** — public data is treated as unrestricted outreach permission.
6. **Cross-client leakage** — case or customer context contaminates another output.
7. **Rights defects** — logos, images, voice, video or stock Assets lack usable rights.
8. **Fake conversion metrics** — candidate activity is presented as revenue success.
9. **Publishing risk** — local or manual publication is displayed as confirmed platform success.
10. **Renewal failure** — users do not perceive enough monthly value despite daily volume.
