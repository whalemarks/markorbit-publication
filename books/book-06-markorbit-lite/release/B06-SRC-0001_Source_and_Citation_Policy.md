# B06-SRC-0001 — Source and Citation Policy

## 1. Identity

```text
Record: B06-SRC-0001
Book: Book 06 — MarkOrbit Lite
Record type: Publication source and citation policy
Status: Release Candidate validation input
Product meaning authority: NO
Implementation authority: NO
```

## 2. Purpose

Book 06 is primarily a Product architecture publication. Its central claims are governed by the MarkOrbit publication authority chain rather than by current external websites, vendors or market commentary.

This policy distinguishes:

1. internal architecture authority;
2. illustrative professional-practice explanation;
3. jurisdiction-specific legal or procedural claims;
4. temporally sensitive commercial, provider or technology claims;
5. commercial hypotheses and Product experiments.

## 3. Internal architecture authority

The following source classes may support Book 06 Product meaning:

```text
MarkOrbit Architecture Canon
→ Books 01–04 Portfolio Baseline
→ Book 05 MarkReg RC1 contracts
→ Book 06 Product Charter v0.3
→ Book 06 Product Baseline v0.1 / B06-SPEC-0001–0004
→ B06-TOC-V0.1
→ accepted chapter prose
```

Reader Apparatus and release-validation records explain, map and test these sources. They do not outrank them.

## 4. External factual claim rule

An external factual claim requires an attributable source when it is material and a reasonable reader may treat it as current truth.

Examples include:

- a current law, official fee, procedure or deadline;
- a named authority's current practice;
- a current provider capability or limitation;
- a current technology or platform feature;
- current market size, adoption, pricing or performance;
- a dated operational incident represented as generally applicable.

A source note should identify, where applicable:

```text
publisher or authority
publication or page title
date or access date
jurisdiction / edition / version
scope and limitations
```

## 5. Jurisdiction-specific legal and procedural claims

Book 06 is not a jurisdictional trademark practice manual.

A legal or procedural example must therefore be either:

- expressed as a generic illustration without implying jurisdictional law; or
- supported by an official or primary source and dated; and
- framed so that it cannot be mistaken for client-specific legal advice.

The manuscript must not convert a single office practice, case result or agent experience into a universal rule.

## 6. Illustrative professional-practice statements

Statements such as the following are architectural or illustrative when used to explain Product behavior:

- professionals work across fragmented customer, trademark, document and communication contexts;
- stale or conflicting sources should reduce confidence;
- a customer response is stronger evidence than an email open;
- a rendered file is not automatically professionally approved;
- repeated personal behavior does not automatically become Organization policy.

These statements do not require external citation when they are presented as design rationale rather than measured universal facts.

## 7. Commercial hypotheses

The following remain explicitly classified as commercial experiments or examples:

```text
RMB 99 monthly entry plan
recurring or daily content supply
Prospect Candidate quantities
replacement and exclusivity rules
Render or Artifact quotas
support or Review levels
Handoff service levels
```

They must not be cited or described as permanent Product constitution, prevailing market prices or guaranteed unit economics.

A commercial experiment should retain:

- date and edition;
- target user and region;
- entitlement definition;
- fulfillment measurement;
- cost and margin assumptions;
- cancellation, replacement and service-credit treatment;
- evidence required for continuation or change.

## 8. Provider and technology neutrality

Named AI models, renderers, TTS providers, data sources, CRMs, workflow tools or infrastructure services may appear only as implementation examples or test dependencies.

They do not define Product identity or formal authority.

A current provider claim must be verified against the relevant provider's primary documentation at the time it is relied upon.

## 9. Source inventory classes

The RC validation tool inventories:

- external URLs;
- domains;
- four-digit year markers;
- currency and price markers;
- unresolved editorial markers;
- internal links and anchors.

The inventory is evidence for review, not an automatic legal or factual conclusion.

## 10. Citation placement

Citations should be placed as close as practical to the material claim.

Long endnotes detached from the relevant proposition should be avoided where they make scope unclear.

Internal architecture references may use stable repository record IDs, for example:

```text
B06-SPEC-0001
B06-PLN-0004 v0.3
B06-REV-0013
ML-HC-01
```

External references should not use controlled IDs reserved for MarkOrbit publication records.

## 11. Correction and retirement

If a cited external fact changes or is found to be inaccurate:

```text
identify affected claim
→ identify affected chapter / appendix / render
→ correct or qualify source text
→ create a new publication version
→ rerun source and rendered validation
→ retire superseded release artifacts
```

Historical release artifacts remain auditable but must not be represented as current.

## 12. RC acceptance condition

Release Candidate review must confirm either:

1. material external claims have appropriate sources and scope; or
2. the publication contains no material external factual claims requiring citation beyond the internal architecture authority chain.

The automated inventory supports, but does not replace, this Human editorial decision.
