# B06-CH-22 — From Case Context to Case Lesson

## Chapter Role

This chapter explains how MarkOrbit Lite may help a professional learn from actual case work without exposing restricted facts, confusing one outcome with a universal rule, or turning client Evidence into reusable public content.

The central distinction is:

```text
case experience
≠ Case Lesson
≠ Reusable Asset
≠ canonical Knowledge
```

A case becomes useful to future work only through controlled interpretation.

---

## 1. Why case experience is difficult to reuse

Trademark professionals learn continuously from:

- filing strategies;
- examination objections;
- oppositions and cancellations;
- classification decisions;
- applicant and ownership structures;
- client communications;
- local-agent coordination;
- evidence preparation;
- settlement discussions;
- deadline and procedural failures;
- unexpected authority practice;
- successful and unsuccessful commercial approaches.

Yet much of this learning remains trapped in:

- email threads;
- private notes;
- local folders;
- one person's memory;
- final documents without explanation;
- chat histories;
- isolated corrections;
- unstructured stories.

The problem is not simply storage.

The problem is that a case contains several different kinds of material:

```text
client facts
+ Matter-restricted facts
+ formal records
+ Evidence
+ professional decisions
+ communications
+ outcome observations
+ interpretation
+ later corrections
```

These elements do not all have the same owner, permission, reliability or reuse scope.

## 2. Case context is not a single object

In Lite, `authorized case context` means a purpose-limited projection of material that the active user and Organization may use for the current task.

It may include:

- a Customer or Contact reference;
- a Trademark or Matter reference;
- jurisdiction and procedure;
- relevant dates and status;
- selected communications;
- approved Document or Evidence references;
- the professional question being examined;
- the action that was taken;
- a destination result;
- later corrections or changes.

It does not mean that Lite owns the case.

```text
case context available to Lite
≠ Lite owns Matter truth
≠ Lite owns Evidence
≠ Lite may publish the facts
```

The relevant Owning Services continue to control formal case records, Documents, Evidence and official status.

## 3. The purpose must come first

Before extracting a lesson, the user or reviewer must identify the purpose.

Examples:

- improve future intake questions;
- prepare a private personal note;
- create an internal team checklist;
- produce an anonymized training example;
- propose a reusable client explanation;
- identify a Knowledge contribution candidate;
- correct a previously used template;
- document why a prior approach failed.

Different purposes permit different source use and require different Review.

```text
useful for personal continuity
≠ suitable for organization sharing
≠ suitable for client-facing reuse
≠ suitable for public publication
```

## 4. ML-M06 — Case Lesson Candidate

A Case Lesson Candidate is a bounded interpretation derived from authorized case context.

It should answer:

- what happened;
- which facts materially affected the outcome;
- what professional decision was made;
- what alternative options existed;
- what result was observed;
- what remains uncertain;
- which later correction affects the interpretation;
- where the lesson might apply;
- where it must not be generalized.

A Case Lesson Candidate should preserve:

```text
source references
+ jurisdiction
+ procedure
+ time period
+ factual pattern
+ professional question
+ decision and rationale
+ outcome type
+ confidence
+ limitations
+ data classification
+ allowed audience
+ review status
```

It is a candidate because interpretation is still subject to Human Review.

## 5. Outcome does not prove causation

One of the greatest risks in case learning is false causation.

For example:

```text
argument submitted
→ application accepted
```

This sequence does not automatically prove:

```text
the argument caused acceptance
```

Other explanations may exist:

- the examiner relied on another point;
- the cited right changed status;
- the office applied a procedural rule;
- the scope was amended;
- evidence not captured by Lite influenced the result;
- the official record is incomplete;
- the legal environment later changed.

Therefore, a Case Lesson Candidate should distinguish:

```text
observed sequence
from
supported causal explanation
```

## 6. Successful case does not create a universal rule

A successful response in one Matter may depend on:

- specific wording;
- particular goods or services;
- the identity of cited rights;
- timing;
- office practice at that date;
- evidence quality;
- procedural posture;
- the client's commercial position;
- negotiation behavior;
- reviewer judgment.

The safe conclusion may be:

> This approach was useful under these conditions and should be considered, not automatically repeated.

The unsafe conclusion is:

> This is the correct response for all similar cases.

This boundary implements `ML-SCN-20`:

```text
successful case
→ Case Lesson / Asset candidate
≠ universal rule
```

## 7. Failure can produce more valuable lessons

Case learning must not select only successful outcomes.

A failed or blocked case may expose:

- missing intake information;
- poor source freshness;
- an incorrect assumption;
- an authority boundary;
- an avoidable deadline problem;
- weak evidence;
- a communication failure;
- an unsuitable provider;
- an incomplete Handoff;
- a flawed review process;
- an external outcome that remained unknown.

A mature Lite should support lessons such as:

```text
Do not proceed until applicant ownership is verified.
```

or:

```text
This source is insufficient for asserting current official status.
```

A blocked unsafe action may be a successful Product outcome.

## 8. Restricted facts must remain restricted

Cases often contain:

- client identities;
- unreleased trademarks;
- commercial plans;
- settlement terms;
- legal advice;
- pricing;
- local-agent information;
- signatures and personal details;
- Evidence;
- confidential correspondence.

`ML-SCN-19` requires:

```text
Client/Matter Restricted facts
→ excluded from the lesson
or
→ use blocked
```

The Product should not assume that replacing a company name with “Client A” is sufficient.

Identity may remain inferable through:

- exact dates;
- rare marks;
- jurisdictions;
- product combinations;
- quoted language;
- transaction details;
- distinctive procedural history.

## 9. Anonymization is not permission

Anonymization is one control, not the authority to reuse.

```text
anonymized
≠ authorized for organization sharing
≠ authorized for client-facing reuse
≠ authorized for publication
```

A lesson may require:

- client consent;
- Matter-owner approval;
- removal of unnecessary facts;
- broader factual abstraction;
- delay until the Matter is no longer sensitive;
- legal or professional Review;
- complete rejection of reuse.

## 10. Evidence is not reusable content

Evidence may support a case, but it does not automatically become an Asset.

```text
Evidence
≠ Case Lesson
≠ training example
≠ marketing content
```

Evidence may have:

- a specific legal purpose;
- limited disclosure authority;
- personal data;
- third-party rights;
- authenticity requirements;
- chain-of-custody meaning;
- restrictions imposed by the client or authority.

Lite may reference Evidence under authorized context, but it must not silently copy it into a reusable content library.

## 11. Human Review and scope decision

A Case Lesson Candidate should be reviewed for:

- factual accuracy;
- source completeness;
- causal overstatement;
- legal or procedural currency;
- client and Matter restrictions;
- rights and confidentiality;
- intended audience;
- permitted reuse;
- expiry or review date;
- contradictions with accepted Knowledge.

The reviewer may decide:

```text
accept as personal lesson
accept as organization-memory candidate
accept as reusable-Asset candidate
submit as Knowledge contribution candidate
require abstraction
require correction
limit audience
defer
reject
retire
```

The decision is purpose-specific.

## 12. Multiple lessons may come from one case

One Matter may generate different lessons for different audiences.

Example:

```text
Case
├── personal lesson: questions to ask earlier
├── organization lesson: intake checklist change
├── reusable Asset: client explanation template
├── Knowledge candidate: jurisdiction-specific procedural note
└── public Artifact candidate: generalized educational example
```

Each branch must have its own:

- purpose;
- audience;
- source set;
- level of abstraction;
- Review;
- rights decision;
- version;
- retirement behavior.

## 13. Corrections must propagate

A later development may invalidate an earlier lesson.

Examples:

- official practice changes;
- the case is appealed;
- the registration is later cancelled;
- the client corrects a material fact;
- the original source was mistranslated;
- a cited rule was superseded;
- the apparent success was temporary.

The correction chain should be visible:

```text
Correction Record
→ affected Case Lesson Candidate
→ affected Memory Candidate
→ affected Reusable Asset
→ affected Knowledge Contribution
→ affected Artifacts and external uses
→ correction, replacement or retirement
```

A lesson that cannot be corrected safely should not be promoted broadly.

## 14. Case lesson lifecycle

A practical lifecycle is:

```text
authorized case context
→ lesson proposed
→ source and restriction check
→ Human Review
→ accepted with scope / revisions required / rejected
→ personal, organization, Asset or Knowledge path
→ future use observed
→ feedback and correction
→ review, expiry or retirement
```

Repeated use alone does not promote the lesson.

## 15. Example: an opposition response

Assume a professional handled an opposition where settlement discussions were delayed.

The raw case context may include:

- client name;
- mark;
- opponent;
- correspondence;
- extension request;
- negotiation dates;
- strategy;
- cost;
- confidential settlement position.

Possible controlled outputs:

### Personal lesson

> Request a dated negotiation chronology before seeking a limited extension.

### Organization-memory candidate

> Add “state of negotiations and delay chronology” to the opposition extension intake checklist.

### Reusable Asset candidate

> A client-facing checklist explaining what information is needed to support a shorter extension request.

### Knowledge contribution candidate

> A reviewed procedural note on the authority's discretion and the types of explanation that may support an extension position.

The original correspondence does not automatically become part of any reusable output.

## 16. What Lite should display

A Case Lesson Candidate should make visible:

- source Matter and access scope;
- lesson statement;
- supporting and contradicting facts;
- confidence;
- jurisdiction and time scope;
- restrictions;
- intended audience;
- Review state;
- permitted next destinations;
- review/expiry date;
- related corrections;
- affected Assets or Artifacts.

The Product should avoid a single button labelled “Save to Knowledge.”

Such a button hides several materially different decisions.

## 17. Chapter conclusion

Lite can create long-term professional value only when practical experience becomes reusable capability without losing its boundaries.

The correct chain is:

```text
case experience
→ authorized context
→ bounded interpretation
→ Case Lesson Candidate
→ Human Review and scope decision
→ controlled promotion or rejection
```

The next chapter explains how lessons, preferences and continuity differ across personal and Organization memory.
