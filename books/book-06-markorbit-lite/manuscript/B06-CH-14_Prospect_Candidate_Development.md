# B06-CH-14 — Prospect Candidate Development

**Status:** Complete Draft 1  
**Chapter Map:** B06-TOC-V0.1 — Owner Accepted  
**Part:** Part III — Customer and Service Growth

## Chapter Purpose

This chapter explains how MarkOrbit Lite may help a professional identify and develop a new prospect from authorized public or user-owned sources without pretending that a discoverable company, a contact detail or a business signal is already a customer, a qualified lead or a formal Opportunity.

The central proposition is:

> Prospect development begins with explainable relevance and disciplined qualification—not with relabeling a company list as “intent customers.”

This chapter projects `ML-J03 — Prospect Candidate Development`.

## 1. Prospect Supply Is Useful but Easy to Misrepresent

A platform can often find:

- companies in a target industry;
- new brands or product launches;
- marketplace sellers;
- exhibitors and event participants;
- recently formed entities;
- expanding businesses;
- public trademark filings;
- public contact details;
- social accounts and websites;
- publicly announced investment or market expansion.

These signals may help a trademark professional identify possible service relevance.

They do not prove purchase intent.

```text
company discovered
≠ customer

contact found
≠ contact permitted

business signal
≠ trademark need verified

trademark need possible
≠ willingness to buy
```

## 2. A Prospect Candidate Is a Product-Local Record

`ML-O05 Prospect Candidate` is a Product-local candidate built from an authorized source.

It should preserve:

- source and retrieval time;
- subject identity and matching confidence;
- industry or business context;
- relevant jurisdiction or market;
- observed trigger;
- possible service relevance;
- contact source and freshness;
- duplicate and relationship checks;
- limitations and uncertainty;
- allowed purpose and destination;
- suppression or exclusion state.

It is not a new Core Customer record.

It is not a formal Opportunity.

It is not a promise that the person or organization wants service.

## 3. Source Authorization Comes Before Scoring

A source may be public, user-owned, licensed, partner-provided or commercially supplied.

Before using it, Lite must determine:

- whether the source may be processed for this purpose;
- whether contact details may be used;
- whether the data is current;
- whether source restrictions must be preserved;
- whether the user or Organization may act on the data;
- whether platform-wide reuse is prohibited;
- whether local or private data may leave its zone.

A high-scoring prospect from an unauthorized source remains unusable.

```text
high relevance
+ unauthorized source
→ blocked candidate
```

## 4. Identity Resolution Must Be Visible

Company and brand names may be ambiguous.

A candidate may incorrectly join:

- two entities with similar names;
- a brand with an unrelated corporate owner;
- a distributor with a manufacturer;
- a marketplace seller with a rights owner;
- a foreign subsidiary with a parent company;
- an abandoned website with an active business;
- an old contact with a current organization.

Lite should show identity confidence and evidence rather than presenting a guessed entity match as fact.

Where identity remains uncertain, the candidate should be marked incomplete or require verification.

## 5. Duplicate and Existing-Relationship Checks Are Mandatory

Before presenting a “new” prospect, Lite should check whether the subject already appears as:

- an active Customer;
- a historical Customer;
- an existing Contact;
- an Organization-owned prospect;
- a suppressed entity;
- an active Opportunity;
- an open Matter;
- a partner or provider;
- a prospect assigned to another responsible participant.

Under `ML-SCN-11`, duplicates should be deduplicated or shown as a shared identity with provenance.

A duplicate is not merely a data-quality problem. It may create:

- conflicting outreach;
- relationship ownership disputes;
- repeated contact;
- commission or attribution disputes;
- customer trust damage.

## 6. Service Relevance Requires a Professional Reason

A Prospect Candidate should explain why a trademark-related service may be relevant.

Examples include:

- a visible brand launch with no known protection in a target market;
- an expanding seller entering a new jurisdiction;
- inconsistent public brand ownership information;
- a product category suggesting possible class coverage questions;
- a business event that may require portfolio review;
- a public trademark event creating a bounded service question.

The explanation must state what is observed and what is inferred.

```text
observed fact
→ professional question
→ possible service
→ uncertainty and verification need
```

The Product must not fabricate urgency or imply legal risk without adequate basis.

## 7. Contactability Is Not One Boolean Field

A candidate may have:

- a public business email;
- a general website form;
- a named employee email;
- a social account;
- a telephone number;
- a marketplace message channel;
- a contact supplied by the user;
- no verified contact route.

Contactability depends on:

- source;
- freshness;
- business role;
- purpose;
- channel;
- jurisdictional and platform restrictions;
- opt-out and suppression;
- relationship ownership.

Under `ML-SCN-10`, unknown channel permission must remain unknown and require a final Human decision.

## 8. Platform-Supplied Prospects Need Commercial Quality Rules

Where a subscription or commercial plan supplies Prospect Candidates, the experiment should define:

- candidate quantity;
- source categories;
- freshness window;
- duplicate rules;
- invalidity definition;
- replacement policy;
- exclusivity or non-exclusivity;
- geographic and industry scope;
- contact-field expectations;
- suppression handling;
- user feedback and correction process.

These rules belong to `ML-E05 Commercial Experiment` and `ML-E06 Entitlement / Fulfillment Observation`.

They do not change the constitutional meaning of Prospect Candidate.

A low-cost plan should not preserve a quantity promise by lowering the definition of validity.

## 9. Qualification Before Development Package

The user should be able to inspect:

- source quality;
- subject identity;
- existing-relationship status;
- service relevance;
- contactability;
- likely responsible person;
- uncertainty;
- suppression and permission limits.

The user may then:

- accept for preparation;
- defer;
- reject as irrelevant;
- mark invalid;
- mark duplicate;
- suppress;
- correct identity;
- request more information.

This Product-local qualification still does not create a formal Opportunity.

## 10. Development Should Be Specific, Not Generic

A useful `ML-W06 Development Package` may contain:

- a concise prospect profile;
- the observed business trigger;
- the possible trademark-service relevance;
- a verification question;
- a selected professional work product;
- a short outreach draft;
- alternative channels;
- required Human checks;
- follow-up options;
- a correction and opt-out path.

The package should not rely on false familiarity or imply access to private facts.

Bad outreach says:

> We know you urgently need trademark registration.

A better approach explains a visible, bounded observation and asks a relevant verification question.

## 11. The Prospect Development Journey

```text
authorized source
→ Source Observation
→ Prospect Candidate
→ identity and duplicate inspection
→ freshness and contactability review
→ service relevance explanation
→ User Disposition
→ Qualification Result
→ Development Package
→ exact recipient/channel confirmation
→ outreach
→ response / no response / invalid / opt-out / unknown
→ formal Customer or Opportunity request only when justified
```

Every stage may stop safely.

## 12. Response Does Not Retroactively Validate the Candidate

A response may:

- confirm relevance;
- correct the company or contact identity;
- indicate no interest;
- request no further contact;
- show that another provider handles the matter;
- ask for more information;
- reveal a different service need;
- indicate that the contact is not responsible.

The response should update the candidate and qualification result.

It should not erase the source and reasoning that produced the original candidate.

## 13. Formalization Requires a Real Business Relationship or Need

A formal Customer or Opportunity request may be appropriate when:

- the identity is sufficiently verified;
- there is a legitimate relationship or explicit interaction;
- a service need is confirmed or credibly qualified;
- an owner can be proposed;
- the source and outreach evidence are preserved;
- the destination can revalidate the request.

```text
candidate accepted by user
≠ formal Customer

message answered
≠ qualified Opportunity
```

The Opportunity Service or relevant Owning Service decides whether formal state is created.

## 14. Safe Stops

The Product should stop or suppress when:

- the source is unauthorized;
- identity confidence is too low;
- the prospect is a duplicate or existing restricted relationship;
- contact information is stale or invalid;
- the proposed channel is inappropriate;
- the service relevance is weak or fabricated;
- opt-out applies;
- the user marks the candidate invalid;
- outreach would reveal restricted data;
- the destination rejects formalization.

## 15. Quality Measures

Useful measures include:

- identity verification rate;
- duplicate detection rate;
- invalid candidate rate;
- source freshness;
- user acceptance-for-preparation rate;
- correction and suppression rate;
- meaningful response rate;
- qualified need rate;
- formalization acceptance rate;
- customer acquisition cost and service outcome;
- replacement fulfillment for commercial plans.

The following are insufficient:

- number of companies scraped;
- number of contact fields found;
- number of messages generated;
- number of Opportunities created without destination acceptance;
- open rate without meaningful business result.

## 16. Why Prospect Development Is Not the First Journey

New prospects can expand the user’s market, but they begin with less relationship context, more identity uncertainty and greater contact-permission risk than existing-customer journeys.

Therefore they should follow, not replace:

```text
existing portfolio value
→ historical reactivation
→ user-owned prospect development
→ platform-supplied prospect experiments
```

This order does not make prospect development unimportant. It makes its quality standard explicit.

## Chapter Conclusion

MarkOrbit Lite can make prospect development more useful by connecting authorized sources, visible business triggers, identity checks, professional relevance, Human qualification and bounded outreach preparation.

The Product must never turn a scraped or purchased company list into fictional “intent customers.” A Prospect Candidate is valuable precisely because its uncertainty, source and limitations remain visible until a real relationship and service need justify formalization.
