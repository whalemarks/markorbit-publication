## 17.1 A professional work product begins before writing

A trademark professional rarely needs “some text.”

They need something that performs a defined business function:

- explain why a customer should consider a new filing;
- compare jurisdictions or service routes;
- summarize an examination result;
- prepare a quotation or proposal;
- support a customer-development conversation;
- convert a case lesson into an internal training asset;
- publish a trustworthy article, image post or video;
- prepare the next governed action.

The first question is therefore not:

> What should the AI write?

It is:

> What result must this work product achieve, for whom, using which authorized facts and under which responsibility boundary?

This distinction separates professional production from generic generation.

## 17.2 Purpose is the first control

Every work-product journey should begin with a purpose statement.

A useful purpose statement identifies:

- the business or professional problem;
- the intended audience;
- the decision or action the product should support;
- the allowed source scope;
- the expected form;
- the professional and legal risk;
- the required reviewer;
- the intended destination.

For example:

```text
Purpose:
Help an existing customer understand why its new product line may require
additional trademark coverage in the United States.

Audience:
Customer brand manager.

Allowed sources:
Authorized customer portfolio, product information, current MarkReg data,
approved jurisdiction Knowledge and approved case references.

Expected result:
A concise customer-facing recommendation with options, limitations and a
clear invitation to confirm next steps.

Not authorized:
Final legal opinion, filing instruction or external submission.
```

The same source material may produce different work products when the purpose changes.

```text
one case
→ internal lesson
→ customer explanation
→ public article
→ short video
→ quotation support
```

These are not one interchangeable output. Each has a different audience, disclosure boundary, claim strength, review requirement and destination.

## 17.3 `ML-O06` — Work-Product Candidate

A `Work-Product Candidate` records that a defined result may be useful.

It may arise from:

- a Service-Value Candidate;
- a customer question;
- a MarkReg Return;
- a case lesson;
- a content calendar;
- a recurring professional duty;
- a user-created request;
- a commercial entitlement.

It should answer:

1. Why is a work product needed?
2. Who is it for?
3. What decision or action should it support?
4. What authorized context is available?
5. What is missing?
6. What format is appropriate?
7. What review is required?
8. Where may it go?

A Work-Product Candidate is not yet an Artifact.

```text
need identified
≠ content assembled
≠ professional result prepared
≠ reviewed
≠ ready to use
```

## 17.4 Source assembly is a controlled act

Lite may assemble information from multiple authorized sources, including:

- Customer and Trademark projections;
- Matter-restricted documents or facts;
- MarkReg results;
- approved organizational Knowledge;
- personal or local sources permitted for the purpose;
- approved reusable Assets;
- public sources;
- user-supplied facts;
- current price or service catalog references.

Every important claim should remain traceable to one or more sources.

The Product should preserve:

- source identity;
- source type;
- version or retrieval time;
- freshness;
- data classification;
- controller;
- permitted purpose;
- confidence;
- known contradiction;
- transformation history.

Combining sources does not erase their restrictions.

```text
Public source
+ Client Restricted source
→ result remains constrained by the Client Restricted contribution
```

A local file does not become Organization Knowledge because it was retrieved. A past case does not become a public example because it was summarized. A MarkReg return does not become a legal conclusion because an AI paraphrased it.

## 17.5 Knowledge supports judgment; it does not replace current facts

Knowledge can provide:

- definitions;
- jurisdiction patterns;
- standard questions;
- approved service descriptions;
- known risks;
- reusable explanations;
- reviewed templates;
- prior lessons.

Current customer and trademark context provides:

- actual owner and applicant details;
- current status;
- present goods and services;
- relevant deadlines;
- current business objectives;
- current instructions;
- current response or decision.

The two must not be confused.

```text
Knowledge says what is generally relevant.
Current context says what may be true here.
Human judgment decides what should be said or done.
```

Where current facts conflict with general Knowledge, the work product must expose the conflict or stop.

## 17.6 `ML-W01` — Structured Content

Structured Content is purpose-bound factual and narrative material prepared for an Artifact.

It is more than raw text, but less than a finished business result.

A Structured Content record may contain:

- purpose;
- audience;
- title and summary;
- key facts;
- claims and claim strength;
- source references;
- uncertainties;
- recommended structure;
- jurisdiction or service sections;
- options and trade-offs;
- call to action;
- deterministic fields;
- visual or media instructions;
- prohibited disclosures;
- required disclaimers;
- review questions.

Example:

```text
Structured Content
├── customer and trademark context
├── observed portfolio gap
├── business relevance
├── jurisdiction explanation
├── option A / option B
├── limitations and missing facts
├── recommended next confirmation
├── source references
└── review questions
```

Structured Content may be reused across formats only when the purpose and disclosure scope remain compatible.

## 17.7 Content is not an Artifact

The distinction is fundamental:

```text
Content
= words, facts, media instructions and structure

Artifact
= a versioned business result prepared for a defined purpose and audience
```

An AI-generated paragraph is Content.

A reviewed customer recommendation, with exact customer, trademark, jurisdiction, sources, options, limitations and version, may become an Artifact.

A video script is Content.

A rendered, reviewed and audience-specific video package may become an Artifact.

A table of prices is Content.

A customer quotation with controlled prices, terms, validity and responsibility is an Artifact.

This is why `ML-SCN-13` is blocking:

```text
AI generates raw text
→ it remains Content or draft
→ it must not be represented as an approved Artifact
```

## 17.8 Deterministic and generative content must be separated

Some information may be generated or summarized:

- explanatory prose;
- alternative wording;
- headings;
- narrative transitions;
- suggested calls to action.

Other information should be deterministic or explicitly verified:

- customer name;
- trademark;
- serial or registration number;
- jurisdiction;
- status;
- dates and deadlines;
- quoted price;
- recipient;
- file version;
- service scope;
- official reference.

The Product should identify which fields are deterministic and which are generated.

A polished sentence cannot compensate for a wrong filing number or stale deadline.

## 17.9 Missing information is part of the content model

Professional work often begins before every fact is available.

Lite should not hide gaps. It should record:

- missing applicant details;
- uncertain ownership;
- unverified contact identity;
- unavailable goods/services list;
- conflicting status;
- unknown jurisdiction instruction;
- missing consent;
- unconfirmed price;
- incomplete source rights.

The output may then be classified through `ML-W10 Readiness Result` as:

```text
incomplete
stale
unsupported
blocked
review_required
ready_for_draft
```

A useful Product can prepare questions instead of pretending to complete the answer.

## 17.10 Format follows purpose

The same professional purpose may be expressed through:

- customer email;
- instant-message draft;
- PDF recommendation;
- quotation;
- one-page service proposal;
- image post;
- short video;
- internal checklist;
- MarkReg intake support;
- Publish Package.

Format selection should consider:

- audience;
- complexity;
- channel;
- urgency;
- evidence requirements;
- reuse potential;
- accessibility;
- review burden;
- rendering cost;
- commercial entitlement.

The Product should not choose video merely because video is available, or choose a long report merely because more words appear more professional.

## 17.11 Daily content is one production mode

A daily or recurring content entitlement can use the same model:

```text
content calendar or market signal
→ Work-Product Candidate
→ authorized source set
→ Structured Content
→ Artifact Draft
→ review / render / publish preparation
```

The content may be:

- an image post;
- a short video;
- a social post;
- a customer education note;
- a service explanation;
- a case-based insight.

But daily frequency does not relax purpose, source, rights or quality requirements.

```text
scheduled today
≠ factually current
≠ authorized to publish
≠ professionally useful
```

## 17.12 A practical structured-content checklist

Before an Artifact Draft is created, Lite should be able to answer:

```text
Purpose known?                         YES / NO
Audience known?                        YES / NO
Authorized source scope known?         YES / NO
Current customer/trademark facts?      YES / NO / N/A
Source freshness adequate?             YES / NO
Claims linked to sources?              YES / NO
Missing information visible?           YES / NO
Data restrictions preserved?           YES / NO
Deterministic fields identified?        YES / NO
Review requirement identified?         YES / NO
Destination and format known?           YES / NO
```

A `NO` does not always prevent drafting, but it must affect readiness and downstream action.

## 17.13 Chapter conclusion

Professional work-product production begins with purpose, not prompting.

```text
Purpose
→ Authorized Context
→ Source Assembly
→ Knowledge and Current Facts
→ Work-Product Candidate
→ Structured Content
→ Readiness for Artifact Draft
```

Structured Content gives Lite a disciplined bridge between fragmented information and a professional business result.

It keeps sources visible, separates deterministic facts from generated language, exposes missing information and ensures that the next stage—Artifact drafting—starts from a defined purpose rather than an empty page.
