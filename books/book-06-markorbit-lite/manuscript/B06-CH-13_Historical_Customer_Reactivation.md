# B06-CH-13 — Historical Customer Reactivation

**Status:** Complete Draft 1  
**Chapter Map:** B06-TOC-V0.1 — Owner Accepted  
**Part:** Part III — Customer and Service Growth

## Chapter Purpose

This chapter explains how MarkOrbit Lite may help a professional reconnect with an inactive or historical customer when a current, relevant reason exists.

The central proposition is:

> Historical relationship value should be reactivated through a current professional trigger, verified contactability and respectful Human judgment—not by treating every old contact as permanent marketing permission.

This chapter projects `ML-J02 — Historical Customer Reactivation`.

## 1. Historical Customers Are Not a Dormant Mailing List

A professional may have worked with a customer years ago on:

- one filing;
- one renewal;
- one opposition;
- one assignment;
- one country expansion;
- a consultation that did not proceed;
- a matter transferred to another provider;
- a brand that later changed owner or strategy.

The historical relationship may still contain value, but it does not automatically justify a new approach.

```text
past relationship
≠ current permission

old contact record
≠ current contactability

past service
≠ current service need
```

Lite should reactivate a relationship only when an authorized historical context is connected to a current, explainable professional trigger.

## 2. A Reactivation Candidate Requires Two Sides

A valid `ML-O04 Reactivation Candidate` requires both:

### Historical relationship context

- who the customer was;
- which Organization served the customer;
- what service or Matter existed;
- who owned the relationship;
- what contact channels were used;
- what restrictions, dissatisfaction or opt-out history exists;
- whether the relationship data remains permitted for the current purpose.

### Current trigger

- a trademark maintenance event;
- a public business expansion;
- a returned official status;
- a previously deferred service becoming relevant;
- a brand or ownership change;
- a new product or jurisdiction;
- a documented portfolio gap;
- a current customer request affecting an older Matter.

Neither side is sufficient alone.

```text
historical relationship
+ current trigger
→ possible reactivation value
```

## 3. Contact Freshness Is a Professional Data Question

Historical records often contain:

- former employees;
- changed email addresses;
- closed entities;
- transferred brands;
- outdated telephone numbers;
- channels no longer used for business;
- duplicate contacts;
- relationship owners who have left the Organization.

Lite must present contact freshness and source.

A contact record may be:

- verified recently;
- historically valid but unverified now;
- contradicted by another source;
- invalid;
- suppressed;
- subject to opt-out;
- missing a lawful or appropriate contact purpose.

Under `ML-SCN-07`, stale contactability must be visible and verified before consequential outreach.

## 4. Past Permission Is Purpose- and Channel-Specific

A customer may have communicated by email for a specific Matter without consenting to unrelated marketing through every available channel.

The Product must distinguish:

- service communication;
- relationship follow-up;
- professional update;
- direct marketing;
- platform notification;
- public social engagement;
- personal messaging.

```text
email used in prior Matter
≠ permission for every future campaign

phone number available
≠ permission for messaging-app outreach
```

The professional remains responsible for deciding whether the current purpose and channel are appropriate.

## 5. Opt-Out and Suppression Override Growth Ranking

An opt-out, complaint, relationship restriction or explicit suppression must override commercial value.

Under `ML-SCN-08`:

```text
historical customer opted out
→ Reactivation Candidate suppressed
→ no outreach package prepared
```

The Product should not hide suppressed contacts, regenerate them from another source or treat a new public signal as permission to bypass an earlier restriction.

Suppression may be scoped by:

- person;
- customer;
- channel;
- purpose;
- Organization;
- time period;
- service type.

## 6. The Trigger Must Be Relevant and Current

A reactivation message should not begin with a generic statement such as “We have not spoken for a while.”

It should begin from an explainable current reason, for example:

- a known registration is approaching a maintenance stage;
- the customer has entered a new market;
- an earlier application result affects a current option;
- a previously discussed protection gap remains relevant;
- a business change may require ownership confirmation;
- a new product appears outside the known portfolio.

The trigger still requires verification.

A public announcement, machine-detected status or inferred business move may justify inspection, not immediate outreach.

## 7. Reactivation Should Add Value Before Asking for Work

The Development Package should help the professional provide useful context, not merely ask whether the customer has new business.

It may include:

- a concise explanation of the current trigger;
- the historical relationship reference;
- a portfolio or status summary;
- a limited risk or opportunity explanation;
- a proposed verification question;
- a relevant work product;
- a clear, low-pressure next step;
- a correction path if the facts or responsible contact have changed.

```text
relevant trigger
+ useful explanation
+ respectful next step
→ credible reactivation
```

## 8. Relationship Ownership Must Remain Visible

A historical customer may belong to:

- a specific Organization;
- a former partner;
- an account owner;
- a team or office;
- a referring agency;
- a restricted commercial arrangement.

Lite must not assume that the current user may contact every historical record available in the broader platform.

The candidate should preserve:

- originating Organization;
- relationship owner;
- referral or channel restrictions;
- current responsible participant;
- allowed use;
- conflict or duplication concerns.

Historical private data must not be transformed into platform-wide prospect supply.

## 9. The Reactivation Journey

```text
authorized historical relationship
+ current professional trigger
→ Reactivation Candidate
→ source and freshness inspection
→ contactability and permission review
→ Recommendation Presentation
→ User Disposition
→ Development Package
→ recipient, channel and exact-version confirmation
→ Communication Handoff or confirmed manual action
→ response / no response / invalid / opt-out / correction
→ Qualification Result
→ formal service Handoff where justified
```

Each state remains explicit.

## 10. Response States Matter

A historical customer may:

- welcome the update;
- confirm a service need;
- ask for more detail;
- state that the brand or company has changed;
- identify a new contact;
- state that another provider is responsible;
- request no further contact;
- reject the service;
- not respond;
- produce an unknown delivery result.

These outcomes should affect future relationship handling.

```text
no response
≠ consent to repeat frequently

negative response
≠ permission to try another channel

new contact supplied
≠ automatic relationship transfer without validation
```

## 11. Reactivation Feedback Should Improve Future Work

Useful feedback may include:

- corrected ownership;
- corrected contact details;
- changed brand plans;
- service already completed elsewhere;
- timing preference;
- channel preference;
- opt-out;
- relationship owner correction;
- reason the trigger was irrelevant.

This feedback may create `ML-M01 Feedback Record`, `ML-M02 Correction Record` or a scoped preference candidate.

It must not automatically become shared Organization truth without the required governance.

## 12. Safe Stops

The Product should stop when:

- the historical relationship cannot be authorized;
- the contact is invalid or stale and cannot be verified;
- opt-out or suppression applies;
- the current trigger lacks credible relevance;
- the customer relationship belongs to another Organization or restricted channel;
- the message would reveal restricted Matter information;
- the professional decides that contact would not serve the customer;
- the destination rejects formalization.

## 13. What Reactivation Success Means

Useful measures include:

- percentage of candidates with a verified current trigger;
- contact verification rate;
- suppression and correction rate;
- meaningful response rate;
- qualified service need rate;
- successful formal Handoff rate;
- relationship data improvement;
- reduction in repetitive or inappropriate contact.

The following do not prove success:

- number of historical contacts imported;
- number of messages sent;
- open rate without meaningful response;
- repeated contact after silence;
- Opportunities created without qualification.

## 14. Why Reactivation Is Second in the Growth Order

Historical customers may be more relevant than unknown prospects because some relationship and service context already exists.

However, the data is often older and permission is more uncertain than in an active portfolio.

Therefore the preferred order remains:

```text
active existing customer portfolio
→ historical customer reactivation
→ user-owned prospect development
→ platform-supplied Prospect Candidates
```

This order reflects context quality and relationship responsibility, not only volume.

## Chapter Conclusion

Historical customer reactivation can create meaningful service value when Lite connects a current professional trigger with an authorized past relationship, verifies contactability and supports respectful Human judgment.

The Product must not convert old records into a permanent sales list. It should help the professional re-establish relevance, provide useful context, respect opt-out and relationship ownership, and move only a genuinely qualified need into formal service.
