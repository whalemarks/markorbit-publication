# B06-CH-16 — From Qualified Need to Opportunity and MarkReg

**Status:** Complete Draft 1  
**Chapter Map:** B06-TOC-V0.1 — Owner Accepted  
**Part:** Part III — Customer and Service Growth

## Chapter Purpose

This chapter explains how MarkOrbit Lite moves from a Product-local Qualification Result to a formal Opportunity request, a MarkReg Launch or another governed service destination without allowing Lite to create formal business truth by itself.

The central proposition is:

> A real customer response may justify formal service work, but formalization begins only when the destination revalidates identity, need, authority, scope and ownership and returns an accepted formal reference.

## 1. Qualification Is a Boundary, Not a Label

A customer or prospect interaction may produce useful evidence, but the Product must determine whether it supports a real service need.

`ML-O08 Qualification Result` should state:

- who expressed the need;
- relationship to the customer or prospect;
- which facts were confirmed;
- which service question exists;
- urgency and timing;
- intended jurisdiction or procedure;
- expected scope;
- missing information;
- professional or conflict concerns;
- source and strength of evidence;
- recommended destination;
- reasons not to formalize yet.

Qualification remains Product-local.

```text
qualified by Lite
≠ accepted by Opportunity Service
≠ accepted by MarkReg
```

## 2. Activity Is Not Need

The following may indicate engagement without establishing a qualified need:

- message opened;
- link clicked;
- content downloaded;
- social interaction;
- generic positive reply;
- request to keep information on file;
- informal discussion without authority;
- internal user belief that the customer is interested.

Stronger evidence may include:

- explicit request for advice;
- confirmed portfolio fact requiring analysis;
- request for quotation or service scope;
- instruction to prepare options;
- confirmed filing, maintenance or dispute objective;
- authorized request to proceed;
- identified decision maker and timing.

The Product should preserve evidence strength rather than forcing a binary answer.

## 3. Qualification Must Protect Customer Interest

A qualified need should not be created merely because a service can be sold.

The professional should consider:

- whether the service addresses a real objective or risk;
- whether another action is more appropriate;
- whether the service would be premature;
- whether the cost is proportionate;
- whether the customer already has suitable representation;
- whether conflict, independence or authority issues exist;
- whether the proposed scope is understandable;
- whether the customer has supplied or approved the necessary context.

A correct outcome may be:

```text
not qualified
needs verification
not now
refer elsewhere
no service required
```

## 4. Formal Opportunity and MarkReg Serve Different Purposes

A formal Opportunity may be appropriate when the organization needs to manage a commercial service possibility, ownership, forecast, follow-up and conversion.

MarkReg may be appropriate when the customer need is sufficiently defined to enter or continue a trademark Product journey.

The paths may occur:

- sequentially;
- in parallel under policy;
- through another formal service first;
- not at all if the need remains unqualified.

```text
commercial opportunity management
≠ trademark lifecycle management
```

Lite should prepare the correct request, not collapse both into a single Product-local record.

## 5. Opportunity Formalization Request

`ML-H07 Opportunity Formalization Request` and `ML-HC-05` should include:

- customer or prospect reference;
- identity confidence;
- qualified need summary;
- service relevance;
- response and supporting evidence;
- proposed owner;
- source Organization and relationship context;
- commercial and timing information where appropriate;
- restrictions and missing information;
- originating Lite records;
- return address.

Opportunity Service should revalidate:

- whether a formal Opportunity already exists;
- Customer/Contact relationship;
- ownership and assignment;
- duplicate and conflict state;
- stage eligibility;
- required evidence;
- Organization policy.

It may return:

- formal Opportunity reference;
- more information required;
- duplicate reference;
- rejection;
- blocked result.

## 6. MarkReg Handoff

`ML-H03 MarkReg Handoff` and `ML-HC-01` should include the minimum authorized package needed to launch or continue the Product journey:

- customer and applicant context;
- brand and trademark references;
- expressed business outcome;
- jurisdiction or procedure candidate;
- service need and source;
- selected customer work-product version;
- known assumptions;
- missing information;
- authority context;
- deadlines or urgency with source;
- Review or confirmation references;
- return address.

Lite must not pretend that this package is already Formal Intake, an approved filing instruction or an official submission.

## 7. MarkReg Revalidates the Journey

MarkReg owns the trademark Product Session and lifecycle meaning defined by Book 05.

It should revalidate:

- applicant and customer identity;
- mark and filing-unit context;
- jurisdiction and route;
- goods and services scope;
- authority and representation;
- required Documents;
- provider needs;
- cost and timing assumptions;
- Review and approval conditions;
- official and deadline sources.

The destination may request more information, reject the launch or return an existing Product Session reference.

```text
Lite Handoff
≠ MarkReg acceptance

MarkReg Product Session
≠ formal Matter state
```

## 8. Do Not Recreate MarkReg Inside Lite

Lite may show:

- that a MarkReg journey was launched;
- the returned Product Session reference;
- information requested by MarkReg;
- a simplified status projection;
- next user attention required;
- later results that create new customer value.

Lite should not reproduce the full MarkReg lifecycle, jurisdiction logic, filing packages, provider appointment, official execution or post-registration management.

The boundary keeps Lite lightweight and allows formal Product depth to remain coherent.

## 9. Return Envelope Preserves Continuity

`ML-H02 Return Envelope Presentation` may present:

- request accepted;
- more information required;
- formal reference returned;
- rejected;
- blocked;
- failed;
- completed by destination;
- unknown external outcome.

The Return should preserve:

- destination;
- original Handoff reference;
- returned record reference;
- source and time;
- typed status;
- user-facing explanation;
- permitted next actions.

Lite does not rewrite the returned result into a stronger status.

## 10. More Information Required Is a Product State

A destination may need:

- applicant confirmation;
- current owner details;
- mark representation;
- jurisdiction plan;
- goods and services information;
- priority details;
- deadline evidence;
- budget or provider constraints;
- conflict clarification;
- customer authority.

Lite may use Continuation State to help the user gather and return the missing information.

```text
more information required
≠ rejected
≠ accepted
```

The Product should preserve what is missing and which destination requested it.

## 11. Rejection and Blocking Need Explanations

A request may be rejected or blocked because:

- identity cannot be established;
- a duplicate formal record exists;
- the user lacks authority;
- conflict or policy prevents action;
- the service is unsupported;
- mandatory information is absent;
- the proposed owner is invalid;
- the destination is unavailable;
- the need belongs to another Product or provider.

Lite should explain the reason and safe alternatives where available.

It should not silently create a local substitute for the rejected formal record.

## 12. Customer Communication After Formalization

Formalization does not end the customer relationship.

Lite may help prepare:

- acknowledgement that the request entered formal review;
- a request for missing information;
- a scope or quotation Artifact;
- an explanation of next steps;
- a meeting brief;
- a later returned-result explanation.

These remain separate work products and Communication actions with their own Review and confirmation.

## 13. Returned Results Create New Value Candidates

A MarkReg or Opportunity result may create another relevant action:

- customer information required;
- service scope changed;
- provider capability needed;
- quotation updated;
- official event creates a decision;
- Matter outcome suggests portfolio review;
- declined option becomes relevant later;
- corrected customer data affects other marks.

```text
formal result
→ sourced Return presentation
→ new Observation or Candidate
→ next governed journey
```

This is how the growth and ecosystem loops connect without transferring formal ownership to Lite.

## 14. Feedback and Capability Accumulation

The formalization journey may produce:

- corrected qualification criteria;
- better source interpretation;
- clearer missing-information patterns;
- effective work-product structures;
- customer timing preferences;
- destination rejection reasons;
- reusable handoff patterns.

These may become feedback, correction, memory or Asset candidates with scope and Review.

Repeated acceptance does not automatically create policy or canonical Knowledge.

## 15. End-to-End Growth Journey

```text
authorized portfolio / historical relationship / prospect source
→ relevant Candidate
→ Recommendation and User Disposition
→ Development Package
→ Human-reviewed and user-confirmed contact
→ typed response
→ Qualification Result
→ Opportunity Formalization Request and/or MarkReg Handoff
→ destination revalidation
→ accepted formal reference / more information / rejected / blocked
→ Return Envelope Presentation
→ customer continuation and next value
→ scoped feedback and capability candidate
```

The journey succeeds because each boundary remains visible.

## 16. Measures That Matter

Useful measures include:

- percentage of responses producing a defensible Qualification Result;
- formalization acceptance rate;
- duplicate and rejection reasons;
- information-completeness rate;
- MarkReg Launch acceptance;
- time from qualified need to destination response;
- customer continuation;
- formal service outcome;
- corrections and reusable capability;
- value generated relative to customer cost and risk.

Insufficient measures include:

- Opportunities created by Lite without destination acceptance;
- number of MarkReg buttons clicked;
- theoretical pipeline value;
- messages sent before qualification;
- revenue claims without formal result.

## 17. Safe Stops

The Product should stop or defer when:

- the respondent lacks authority;
- the need remains ambiguous;
- customer identity is unresolved;
- conflict or professional risk requires review;
- the proposed service is not in the customer’s interest;
- mandatory information is missing;
- destination ownership is unclear;
- the destination rejects or blocks the request;
- external outcome is unknown.

## Chapter Conclusion

MarkOrbit Lite completes its customer-growth role when it converts evidence of a real need into a precise, source-linked request for the correct formal destination.

Lite does not create the Opportunity, the Matter, the MarkReg lifecycle or the official result. It qualifies, prepares, hands off, presents the returned state and helps the professional continue the relationship.

That separation allows growth to remain commercially useful, professionally responsible and architecturally coherent.
