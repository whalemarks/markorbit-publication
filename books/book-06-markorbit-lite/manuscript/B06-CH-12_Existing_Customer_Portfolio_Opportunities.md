## Chapter Purpose

This chapter explains why the first and most defensible growth source for MarkOrbit Lite is usually not a purchased lead list, a broad outbound campaign or a generic content calendar. It is the authorized portfolio of customers, brands, trademarks and prior service relationships that the professional already serves.

The central proposition is:

> The strongest Lite growth journey begins by finding timely, relevant and professionally justified value inside an existing customer relationship, then helping the professional explain and deliver that value without turning service discovery into indiscriminate selling.

This chapter projects `ML-J01 — Existing Customer Portfolio Opportunity`.

## 1. Existing Relationships Contain Unseen Service Value

A trademark professional may already hold authorized context about:

- customers and decision makers;
- brands and business territories;
- registered and pending trademarks;
- filing dates, renewal windows and maintenance history;
- classes, goods and services;
- jurisdictions and route choices;
- past searches, objections and disputes;
- prior instructions and declined options;
- business expansion discussed in earlier communications;
- known gaps, assumptions and unresolved questions.

Yet this context is often distributed across email, spreadsheets, documents, official databases, local folders and personal memory.

The professional may know each fact separately without seeing the current service implication created by their combination.

```text
known customer
+ known trademark portfolio
+ current change or approaching event
+ relevant professional service
→ possible customer value
```

The opportunity is not created by a sales script. It becomes visible when existing facts are connected to a current professional reason.

## 2. Portfolio Growth Is Not Automatic Cross-Selling

An existing customer does not authorize every possible offer.

The Product must distinguish:

```text
service exists
≠ service is relevant

service is relevant
≠ service is necessary now

service may help
≠ customer should be pressured to buy
```

A valid `ML-O03 Service-Value Candidate` requires an explainable relationship between:

1. an authorized customer or trademark context;
2. a current observation or change;
3. a professional need, risk, option or efficiency;
4. a bounded service that may address it;
5. the interests and constraints of the customer.

Revenue potential alone cannot create or rank the candidate above customer interest and professional risk.

## 3. Common Portfolio Opportunity Patterns

Examples may include:

- a renewal or maintenance event approaching;
- a registered mark not covering a newly launched product line;
- expansion into a jurisdiction not represented in the portfolio;
- ownership information that may require confirmation or recordal;
- a pending application whose status creates a new decision;
- a prior refusal or limitation that affects current plans;
- inconsistent marks, applicants or goods across countries;
- a brand asset used in commerce but not reflected in protection;
- a customer request that implies a broader portfolio question;
- a returned MarkReg result that creates a follow-up service need.

These are patterns, not automatic conclusions.

Each instance still requires current sources, customer scope, jurisdictional context and Human professional judgment.

## 4. The Journey Begins with Authorized Projection

Lite should not copy the full Customer, Trademark or Matter into a separate truth store merely to produce a recommendation.

The journey begins with authorized projections and references:

```text
Customer reference
+ Trademark / Brand reference
+ permitted relationship and Matter context
→ ML-O01 Source Observation
→ ML-O02 Customer / Trademark Signal
```

The projection must preserve:

- source service;
- source version or retrieval time;
- active Organization;
- user access;
- subject identity;
- data classification;
- freshness;
- missing or conflicting information;
- permitted purpose and destinations.

If these conditions are not satisfied, the journey must stop or request verification.

## 5. From Signal to Service-Value Candidate

A signal becomes a Service-Value Candidate only after Lite can explain:

- what changed or what is approaching;
- which customer, brand or trademark is affected;
- why the observation may matter;
- what professional question should be considered;
- which service may be relevant;
- what is still uncertain;
- what evidence or review is needed;
- what happens if no action is taken;
- what happens if action is taken unnecessarily.

A useful candidate is therefore more than an alert.

```text
Signal
+ professional interpretation
+ customer context
+ timing
+ uncertainty
+ possible next action
→ Service-Value Candidate
```

## 6. Explainability Is Part of Customer Value

The professional should be able to inspect the candidate before acting.

A strong presentation should answer:

1. What is the source?
2. How current is it?
3. Which customer and trademark does it concern?
4. Why is it relevant now?
5. What professional value might the service provide?
6. What assumptions remain?
7. What customer facts are missing?
8. What safe next steps are available?

The explanation must not conceal uncertainty in order to increase conversion.

When the source is stale or conflicting, the Product should show the limitation or block confident preparation under `ML-SCN-02`.

## 7. User Disposition Precedes Preparation

The professional may:

- inspect;
- accept for preparation;
- defer until a better time;
- reject as irrelevant;
- suppress recurring noise;
- correct the customer or trademark relationship;
- request more information;
- route the candidate to another responsible participant.

These choices are meaningful Product states.

```text
candidate displayed
≠ candidate accepted

accepted for preparation
≠ customer contact approved
```

The system should learn from explicit correction without automatically converting one decision into a universal rule.

## 8. Customer Work Products Make the Opportunity Understandable

A raw alert rarely gives the customer enough information to decide.

Lite should prepare a bounded professional work product such as:

- a portfolio gap summary;
- a renewal or maintenance explanation;
- a jurisdiction expansion note;
- a mark or class comparison;
- a service option summary;
- a short client-facing report;
- a proposed next-step checklist;
- a fee and scope explanation sourced from the proper catalog;
- a meeting brief or follow-up package.

The output follows the controlled work-product chain:

```text
purpose and authorized sources
→ ML-W01 Structured Content
→ ML-W02 Artifact Draft
→ ML-W03 Artifact Version
→ Review where required
→ ML-W06 Development Package
```

The work product must not overstate legal certainty, customer urgency or expected outcome.

## 9. Contact Is a Consequential Action

Before customer contact, the professional should see:

- exact recipient;
- relationship and contact source;
- selected channel;
- exact work-product version;
- message purpose;
- relevant claims and assumptions;
- attachments;
- expected consequence;
- Review requirement;
- confirmation state.

The customer action may then proceed through a governed Communication Handoff or a confirmed manual action.

```text
prepared customer package
≠ sent Communication
```

Human confirmation remains required for consequential external contact.

## 10. Response Is Not Yet a Qualified Need

Possible responses include:

- asks for more information;
- confirms the underlying business change;
- requests a quotation;
- asks to proceed;
- states that the service is not needed;
- corrects the underlying facts;
- indicates that another provider is responsible;
- does not respond;
- cannot be delivered;
- has an unknown external outcome.

These states must remain distinct.

```text
response
≠ qualified need

no response
≠ rejection

message delivered
≠ customer interest
```

Qualification is handled later through `ML-O08` and Chapter 16.

## 11. The Existing Portfolio Journey

The complete journey is:

```text
authorized Customer / Trademark projection
→ Source Observation
→ Customer / Trademark Signal
→ Service-Value Candidate
→ Recommendation Presentation
→ User Disposition
→ Structured Content
→ Artifact Draft and Version
→ Development Package
→ Prepared Action
→ final user confirmation
→ customer contact
→ typed response
→ Qualification Result
→ Opportunity and/or MarkReg Handoff
→ returned formal reference or result
→ feedback, correction and next value
```

Each arrow represents a meaning boundary, not a screen transition.

## 12. Safe Stops Are Part of Product Quality

The journey must safely stop when:

- access to the customer or Matter is absent;
- trademark identity is ambiguous;
- the source is stale or conflicting;
- there is no current professional relevance;
- the proposed service conflicts with customer interest;
- contact purpose or channel is not appropriate;
- required Review is missing;
- the user rejects or suppresses the candidate;
- the destination refuses formalization.

A blocked unsafe action is a correct Product outcome.

## 13. What This Journey Should Measure

Useful measures include:

- percentage of candidates inspected;
- correction and suppression rate;
- percentage accepted for preparation;
- work products meaningfully used or edited;
- customer actions confirmed;
- typed response distribution;
- qualified needs;
- accepted MarkReg or Opportunity Handoffs;
- returned outcomes;
- reusable corrections or capability candidates.

The following are insufficient by themselves:

- number of alerts;
- number of messages generated;
- number of messages sent;
- theoretical service value;
- number of formal Opportunities created without qualification.

## 14. Why This Is the Primary Growth Journey

Existing customer portfolio growth is the preferred first journey because it can combine:

- authorized context;
- an established relationship;
- relevant trademark facts;
- lower discovery ambiguity;
- more defensible professional value;
- clear connection to MarkReg;
- meaningful feedback and capability accumulation.

It is not always the largest source of volume. It is usually the strongest starting point for proving that Lite creates business value without degrading professional responsibility.

## Chapter Conclusion

MarkOrbit Lite should begin growth by helping professionals see and explain relevant value inside the relationships and trademark portfolios they already serve.

The Product does not manufacture demand. It connects authorized context, current observations and professional judgment, prepares a trustworthy customer work product, supports Human-confirmed contact and hands a genuinely qualified need to the correct formal service.

That is the foundation for growth that is useful to the customer, defensible for the professional and sustainable for MarkOrbit.
