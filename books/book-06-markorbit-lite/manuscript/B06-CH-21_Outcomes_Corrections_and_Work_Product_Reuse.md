# B06-CH-21 — Outcomes, Corrections and Work-Product Reuse

**Status:** Complete Draft 1  
**Chapter Map:** B06-TOC-V0.1 — Owner Accepted  
**Part:** Part IV — Professional Work Products  
**Primary controls:** ML-H02, ML-M01, ML-M02, ML-M07, ML-E02, ML-E03

## Chapter Purpose

This chapter explains how outcomes, feedback and corrections close the work-product loop and how bounded reuse can create compounding capability without turning one result into a universal rule.

The central proposition is:

> A work product proves its value through controlled use, observable outcomes, responsible correction and safe reuse—not when a file is generated.

## 21.1 The work-product loop does not end at delivery

A professional Artifact has continuing value only when the Product can understand what happened after it was used.

Possible outcomes include:

- customer understood and requested the next step;
- customer corrected a fact;
- customer rejected the recommendation;
- no response was received;
- the Communication failed;
- a quotation was accepted;
- a report was reused internally;
- a public post generated an enquiry;
- a case example was challenged;
- an Artifact was withdrawn;
- the external result remains unknown.

These outcomes determine whether Lite should continue, correct, retire, reuse or learn.

## 21.2 Outcome evidence has different strengths

Outcome evidence may come from:

- destination service Return;
- Communication or Delivery evidence;
- MarkReg or Opportunity reference;
- user report;
- customer response;
- reviewer correction;
- publication analytics;
- internal reuse record.

The Product should preserve the evidence class.

```text
platform-confirmed publication
≠ user-reported publication

customer reply
≠ inferred customer understanding

page view
≠ service interest
```

## 21.3 Typed Outcome Observation

`ML-E02 Outcome Observation` may record:

- source of the outcome;
- Artifact Version;
- representation/package used;
- audience or recipient;
- action and time;
- result class;
- evidence strength;
- customer or business consequence;
- required next action;
- uncertainty;
- whether a correction is needed;
- whether reuse evidence exists.

Useful result classes include:

```text
used_internally
sent
published
viewed
responded
qualified_need
rejected
opted_out
invalid_contact
failed
unknown
corrected
superseded
retired
reused
```

These states must not be collapsed into a single “successful” metric.

## 21.4 Activity is not value

The Product should distinguish:

```text
Artifact generated
→ production activity

Artifact delivered
→ external operation

Artifact understood or used
→ utility evidence

customer responds with relevant need
→ growth evidence

formal Handoff accepted
→ governed progression

result reused with reduced effort and preserved quality
→ capability accumulation
```

A high volume of generated or published content may have little professional or business value.

## 21.5 `ML-M01` — Feedback Record

Feedback may concern:

- factual accuracy;
- clarity;
- tone;
- relevance;
- visual quality;
- missing information;
- customer response;
- channel suitability;
- professional reasoning;
- privacy or rights;
- commercial usefulness.

A Feedback Record should identify:

- who provided it;
- which Artifact Version it concerns;
- context and purpose;
- whether it is fact, preference or opinion;
- supporting evidence;
- scope;
- required disposition.

Feedback does not automatically become a Product rule.

## 21.6 `ML-M02` — Correction Record

A correction is stronger than general feedback.

It should identify:

- incorrect or outdated statement;
- affected Artifact Version;
- corrected fact or reasoning;
- correction source;
- authority and confidence;
- affected representations;
- affected deliveries/publications;
- whether Review is invalidated;
- required notification or takedown;
- retirement and replacement treatment.

Corrections may originate from:

- customer;
- reviewer;
- MarkReg Return;
- updated official source;
- internal audit;
- service provider;
- user discovery.

## 21.7 Correction propagation

A material correction should trigger a dependency review.

```text
Correction Record
→ affected Structured Content
→ affected Artifact Versions
→ affected Render Results
→ affected Packages
→ affected external uses
→ replacement / notification / retirement
```

The Product should not overwrite history. It should preserve what was used and mark it corrected or retired.

## 21.8 Retirement is a governed state

An Artifact or representation may be retired because:

- facts became stale;
- law or procedure changed;
- price validity ended;
- rights expired;
- client consent changed;
- a material error was found;
- branding changed;
- a better approved version replaced it;
- its original purpose ended.

Retirement means the Product should not recommend the item for new use.

It does not mean the historical record disappears.

## 21.9 Reuse is not copy-and-paste

A useful Artifact may support future work, but reuse must preserve purpose and scope.

Possible reuse modes include:

- same Artifact Version reused for the same authorized audience;
- template extracted as a Reusable Asset Candidate;
- Structured Content adapted to another format;
- case lesson reused under a new purpose;
- approved explanation reused for another customer after fact substitution and Review;
- design system reused without reusing restricted facts.

```text
previously approved
≠ approved for every future customer, jurisdiction or channel
```

## 21.10 `ML-E03` — Reuse Evidence

Reuse Evidence should identify:

- source Artifact or Asset;
- new purpose;
- new audience;
- reused components;
- changed components;
- source and rights continuity;
- Review performed;
- effort or time saved;
- quality result;
- resulting Artifact Version;
- outcome.

Reuse becomes valuable Product evidence when it reduces repeated effort without weakening professional quality or data boundaries.

## 21.11 From Artifact to Reusable Asset Candidate

A strong work product may produce a `ML-M07 Reusable Asset Candidate` such as:

- template;
- structure;
- approved explanation;
- checklist;
- visual system;
- standard question set;
- audience-specific pattern;
- approved case example.

Promotion requires:

- provenance;
- rights;
- scope;
- audience;
- de-identification where appropriate;
- review;
- expiry and retirement rules.

Customer-specific details should not silently become part of the reusable Asset.

## 21.12 Learning from outcomes without overfitting

Lite may observe patterns such as:

- customers respond better to concise option comparisons;
- a particular audience prefers image summaries;
- a certain CTA produces more qualified questions;
- a report type often requires the same missing information;
- one renderer repeatedly introduces deterministic-field errors.

These observations may become evaluation or preference candidates.

They should not automatically become universal rules.

```text
one successful outcome
≠ general best practice

repeated correlation
≠ proven causal rule
```

## 21.13 Negative outcomes are valuable evidence

A rejected recommendation, opt-out, failed delivery or blocked publication may reveal:

- poor timing;
- incorrect relationship assumptions;
- weak relevance;
- data-quality problems;
- wrong audience;
- unsuitable channel;
- excessive complexity;
- rights or privacy risk;
- missing professional Review.

The Product should preserve these outcomes instead of removing them to improve vanity metrics.

## 21.14 Public corrections and takedowns

When a public Artifact is materially wrong or no longer authorized, the Product should support:

- correction decision;
- affected URL/account identification;
- takedown or replacement package;
- public correction where appropriate;
- destination evidence;
- customer/client notification;
- retirement of source versions;
- prevention of future reuse.

An unknown takedown result remains unknown until evidence returns.

## 21.15 Outcome attribution must be cautious

A customer may respond after seeing several touchpoints:

- a public article;
- a direct message;
- a prior meeting;
- an existing relationship;
- a recommendation report.

Lite may record contribution or sequence, but should avoid false single-source attribution.

```text
response followed publication
≠ publication alone caused response
```

## 21.16 Work-product performance dimensions

A meaningful evaluation can include:

- production time;
- review burden;
- correction rate;
- deterministic-field error rate;
- use/download/send rate;
- customer response quality;
- qualified-need rate;
- destination Handoff rate;
- reuse rate;
- retirement rate;
- safety/privacy findings;
- cost per usable Artifact.

No single metric should control professional quality.

## 21.17 The complete work-product loop

```text
Purpose
→ Structured Content
→ Artifact Draft
→ immutable Version
→ Review
→ Render / Edit
→ Readiness
→ Delivery / Communication / Publish Package
→ confirmation and operation
→ typed Outcome
→ Feedback / Correction
→ Reuse / Asset Candidate / Retirement
```

The loop returns to future production with better context, not with automatic authority.

## Chapter Conclusion

The value of a professional work product is not proven when a file is generated. It is proven through controlled use, observable outcomes, responsible correction and bounded reuse.

Lite should preserve what happened, how confidently it is known, what must be corrected and which parts may safely compound into future capability.

This closes the Professional Work Products loop and prepares the next Part: how cases, preferences, memory and reusable business Assets can accumulate without turning private experience into uncontrolled shared truth.
