# B06-CH-24 — Reusable Assets and Knowledge Contribution

## Chapter Role

This chapter explains how MarkOrbit Lite may turn reviewed lessons, templates and work-product patterns into reusable business capability while preserving the distinction between an internal reusable Asset and governed canonical Knowledge.

The central distinctions are:

```text
useful example
≠ Reusable Asset

Reusable Asset
≠ canonical Knowledge

Knowledge Contribution Candidate
≠ accepted Knowledge
```

---

## 1. Why reusable capability matters

A professional practice becomes stronger when good work can be reused safely.

Examples include:

- intake checklists;
- quotation structures;
- client explanation patterns;
- issue-spotting guides;
- reviewed email frameworks;
- jurisdiction-specific filing questions;
- evidence preparation checklists;
- case-study structures;
- service opportunity explanations;
- internal review prompts;
- Artifact templates;
- common Handoff packages.

Without controlled reuse, each professional repeatedly recreates the same work.

With uncontrolled reuse, the Organization spreads:

- stale law;
- client-specific facts;
- wrong assumptions;
- unlicensed material;
- confidential information;
- outdated prices;
- unsupported claims;
- templates approved for another purpose.

The Product must make reuse easier without making it careless.

## 2. ML-M07 — Reusable Asset Candidate

A Reusable Asset Candidate is a proposed template, example, case-derived resource or work-product component intended for controlled future use.

It may originate from:

- an accepted Case Lesson Candidate;
- a successful Artifact Version;
- a corrected template;
- an Organization Memory Candidate;
- a recurring work-product structure;
- an approved communication pattern;
- a provider or jurisdiction checklist;
- a reviewed public source;
- a manually created internal resource.

A candidate should identify:

```text
purpose
+ intended users
+ intended audience
+ source and provenance
+ rights holder
+ permitted transformations
+ required variables
+ prohibited uses
+ review status
+ jurisdiction/time scope
+ expiry/review date
+ owner
```

## 3. Asset types

Reusable Assets may include:

### Structure Assets

- report outline;
- quotation layout;
- intake questionnaire;
- client-update structure;
- case-summary format.

### Content Assets

- reviewed explanation block;
- approved disclaimer;
- frequently used client instruction;
- internal checklist item;
- reusable CTA.

### Case Assets

- generalized case example;
- reviewed lesson pattern;
- anonymized chronology;
- decision-tree example.

### Production Assets

- presentation template;
- visual layout;
- video scene structure;
- Render configuration;
- approved brand component.

### Handoff Assets

- MarkReg intake preparation checklist;
- Review Package structure;
- Communication preparation checklist;
- MGSN Capability Need template.

Asset type does not determine permission. Each Asset still requires scope and rights.

## 4. Reusable does not mean universal

An Asset may be reusable only for:

- one Organization;
- one service;
- one jurisdiction;
- one language;
- one client segment;
- one channel;
- one time period;
- one type of work product.

```text
reusable
≠ unrestricted
≠ universally correct
≠ public
```

The Product should avoid a single undifferentiated “template library.”

## 5. Variables and deterministic fields

A safe Asset distinguishes reusable structure from matter-specific fields.

For example:

```text
Reusable explanation structure
+ variables:
  client name
  mark
  jurisdiction
  service scope
  fee
  deadline
  contact
```

Deterministic fields should not be embedded as forgotten example values.

A common failure is:

```text
old client name or fee remains in copied template
```

The Asset should define:

- required variables;
- source of each variable;
- validation rules;
- fallback behavior;
- whether Human Review is required after substitution.

## 6. Rights and permission

An Asset may contain or derive from:

- client material;
- third-party text;
- screenshots;
- images;
- official forms;
- provider content;
- licensed templates;
- AI-generated material;
- employee work product.

The Product should preserve:

- rights holder;
- license or permission;
- allowed audience;
- allowed modification;
- attribution requirements;
- expiry;
- geographic limits;
- publication restrictions.

```text
technically copyable
≠ legally or professionally reusable
```

## 7. Internal approval is not public permission

A Reusable Asset may be approved for:

- personal use;
- internal team use;
- client-specific reuse;
- client-facing use under Review;
- public educational publication.

These are separate scopes.

```text
approved internal Asset
≠ approved public content
```

This implements the control in `ML-J04`.

## 8. Reuse should create a new Artifact Version

When an Asset is applied to a new purpose, it should contribute to a new work product.

```text
Reusable Asset
+ new authorized context
+ new variables
+ current sources
→ Structured Content
→ new Artifact Draft
→ new Artifact Version
```

The reused Asset remains a source, not the final output.

This preserves:

- context;
- version lineage;
- Review;
- correction behavior;
- outcome evaluation.

## 9. ML-M08 — Knowledge Contribution Candidate

A Knowledge Contribution Candidate proposes that a reviewed statement, rule, pattern or procedural explanation be considered by Knowledge governance.

It should include:

- proposition;
- supporting sources;
- source dates and versions;
- jurisdiction and procedure;
- confidence;
- contradictions;
- applicable conditions;
- exclusions;
- review history;
- proposed audience;
- suggested review/expiry date;
- relationship to existing Knowledge.

It is a contribution request, not accepted Knowledge.

## 10. Asset versus Knowledge

A practical distinction is:

| Question | Reusable Asset | Governed Knowledge |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Primary purpose | help produce or perform work | support professional understanding and reasoning |
| Typical form | template, example, checklist, component | proposition, rule, explanation, structured reference |
| Scope | often personal or Organization-specific | governed for a defined broader scope |
| Authority | approved use within scope | accepted by Knowledge governance |
| Change | Asset owner and Review | Knowledge governance and source review |
| Output role | source for a new Artifact | source for reasoning and Structured Content |

The same case may produce both, but through separate decisions.

## 11. Knowledge governance owns acceptance

Lite may prepare `ML-HC-08 Knowledge Governance Handoff` containing:

- contribution candidate;
- provenance;
- sources;
- scope;
- contradiction analysis;
- affected existing Knowledge;
- review requirement;
- proposed owner;
- return address.

Knowledge governance may return:

```text
accepted with Knowledge reference
accepted with limited scope
more information required
revisions required
rejected
superseded by existing Knowledge
blocked by rights or confidentiality
```

Lite must present the returned result without pretending that submission created canonical truth.

## 12. Contradiction is valuable information

A contribution should not hide conflict.

Sources may disagree because of:

- jurisdiction;
- time period;
- office practice;
- procedural posture;
- translation;
- interpretation;
- factual conditions;
- later amendment.

A mature Knowledge contribution may state:

> Source A supports X under condition Y, while Source B indicates Z after date D.

Flattening disagreement into a single confident rule is unsafe.

## 13. Source freshness

Knowledge-like material can become stale.

Examples:

- fees;
- forms;
- deadlines;
- office practice;
- accepted goods descriptions;
- filing channels;
- signature requirements;
- local-agent rules;
- procedural extensions.

A candidate should define freshness expectations:

```text
stable principle
slow-changing procedure
frequently changing fee/status
one-time case observation
```

The review cycle should match the change rate.

## 14. AI-generated contributions

AI may assist with:

- summarization;
- structure;
- source comparison;
- identifying contradictions;
- drafting a candidate;
- proposing tags and scope.

AI output alone is not a valid source.

```text
AI statement
≠ Evidence
≠ source
≠ accepted Knowledge
```

The contribution must point to authorized, reviewable sources.

## 15. Reusable Asset quality controls

Before approval, an Asset should be checked for:

- purpose clarity;
- source integrity;
- rights;
- client/Matter restrictions;
- variable completeness;
- stale values;
- factual and professional accuracy;
- jurisdiction/time scope;
- required Review;
- audience;
- transformation limits;
- retirement triggers.

Possible decisions:

```text
approved for personal use
approved for Organization internal use
approved for client-facing use with Review
approved for public use
revisions required
blocked
rejected
retired
```

## 16. Reuse Evidence

`ML-E03 Reuse Evidence` records that an Asset or lesson contributed to later work.

It may capture:

- Asset reference and version;
- new Artifact reference;
- purpose and audience;
- user modifications;
- Review result;
- time saved;
- correction required;
- outcome type;
- whether the Asset remained suitable.

Reuse Evidence is not only a success metric.

It may show that an Asset should be narrowed or retired.

## 17. Example: quotation explanation

A team repeatedly explains that official fees and professional fees are separate.

Possible chain:

```text
reviewed client explanations
→ recurring structure identified
→ Reusable Asset Candidate
→ Organization Review
→ approved internal/client-facing explanation block
→ new quotation Artifact uses current fees and client scope
→ user confirms
→ customer response observed
```

The reusable Asset contains the explanation structure, not a permanent fee amount.

## 18. Example: refusal-response lesson

A case suggests that an argument was effective under a specific factual pattern.

Possible outputs:

### Reusable Asset

A checklist of factual questions to examine before preparing a response.

### Knowledge Contribution Candidate

A bounded proposition describing the relevant legal/procedural factor with sources and limitations.

### Not permitted

Copying the client's Evidence bundle into a general library.

## 19. Asset discovery and recommendation

Lite may recommend an Asset when:

- purpose matches;
- audience matches;
- jurisdiction/time scope matches;
- rights permit use;
- the Asset is current;
- required variables are available;
- no correction or retirement blocks it.

The recommendation should show why the Asset matches and what still requires Review.

```text
Asset suggested
≠ Asset automatically inserted
≠ resulting Artifact approved
```

## 20. Asset ownership

Every Asset should have a responsible owner for:

- Review;
- rights;
- corrections;
- scope;
- expiry;
- retirement;
- replacement.

An unowned Asset tends to become stale but remain available.

Ownership may belong to:

- an individual;
- an Organization function;
- a Product team;
- a Knowledge governance role;
- another Owning Service.

## 21. Promotion and demotion

Capability can move in both directions.

```text
Case Lesson Candidate
→ Reusable Asset Candidate
→ approved Asset
→ broader Knowledge Contribution Candidate
```

But also:

```text
accepted Asset
→ narrowed scope
→ internal-only
→ superseded
→ retired
```

Knowledge acceptance does not eliminate the need to maintain the Asset version used in actual work.

## 22. Chapter conclusion

Reusable capability emerges through controlled transformation:

```text
reviewed lesson or work pattern
→ Reusable Asset Candidate
→ rights, scope and Review
→ approved Asset
→ new purpose-bound Artifact use
→ Reuse Evidence and correction
```

Broader professional understanding follows a separate path:

```text
bounded proposition and sources
→ Knowledge Contribution Candidate
→ Knowledge Governance Handoff
→ accepted, limited, revised or rejected Knowledge result
```

The next chapter explains how provenance, rights, correction and retirement keep this accumulated capability trustworthy over time.
