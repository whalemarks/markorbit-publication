# B06-CH-06 — Principles, Boundaries and Non-Goals

**Status:** Complete Draft 1  
**Chapter Map:** B06-TOC-V0.1 — Owner Accepted  
**Part:** Part I — Product Constitution and Place in MarkOrbit

## Chapter Purpose

This chapter defines the constitutional principles and non-goals that constrain every MarkOrbit Lite edition, interface, AI capability, commercial plan and implementation.

The central proposition is:

> Lite should become easier, more useful and more capable without becoming a shadow formal system, an autonomous professional actor or an uncontrolled pool of customer, case and Organization data.

## 1. Why Product Principles Matter

A Product can drift without changing its name.

A daily cockpit can become a noisy notification feed. A growth capability can become a lead marketplace. A professional work-product system can become a content factory. A memory capability can become unrestricted surveillance. A helpful AI can become an implied decision-maker. A lightweight Product can gradually reproduce the whole Workplace, Core, MarkReg or MGSN.

Feature-by-feature reasoning may fail to detect this drift because each individual addition appears useful.

Product principles provide a higher test:

1. Does the capability serve the accepted Product identity?
2. Does it preserve source, scope and Human responsibility?
3. Does it remain within Lite ownership?
4. Does it hand formal work to the correct destination?
5. Does it improve long-term capability rather than only activity volume?
6. Can it fail safely?

## 2. Principle One — Daily Value Must Be Actionable

Lite should reduce the distance between attention and useful professional action.

A Today item should not merely state that something is important. It should help the user understand:

- the affected Customer, Trademark, relationship or work product;
- the source and observation time;
- why the item may matter;
- uncertainty or missing information;
- the available options;
- what can be prepared;
- what the user must decide;
- which formal destination may be required.

```text
notification alone
≠ Product value

recommendation alone
≠ completed operating loop
```

The Product should avoid presenting more items than the user can meaningfully inspect and act upon.

## 3. Principle Two — Candidate State Must Remain Distinct from Formal Truth

Lite operates before, around and after formal work. This requires candidate state.

Candidate state includes:

- observations;
- service-value candidates;
- reactivation candidates;
- Prospect Candidates;
- work-product candidates;
- recommendation presentations;
- qualification results;
- Prepared Actions.

These records allow exploration and preparation without prematurely changing formal business truth.

Required distinctions include:

```text
Attention Item ≠ active Task
Service-Value Candidate ≠ formal Opportunity
Prospect Candidate ≠ Customer or Qualified Lead
Qualification Result ≠ accepted Order
Prepared Action ≠ execution
```

The transition into formal state must occur through the relevant Owning Service and a typed Return.

## 4. Principle Three — Source and Scope Travel with the Work

Professional usefulness depends on knowing where information came from and where it may be used.

Material Lite records should preserve, where applicable:

- source identity;
- source version or observation time;
- active Organization;
- Customer, Trademark and Matter scope;
- data classification;
- controller;
- purpose;
- freshness;
- confidence and limitations;
- allowed destination;
- transformation history;
- expiry and Review requirement.

A copied fact without provenance may become more dangerous as it is reused.

```text
available to the user
≠ available for every purpose

public source
≠ unrestricted outreach permission

anonymized material
≠ approved public material
```

## 5. Principle Four — Human Judgment Remains Visible

AI can reduce cognitive and preparation burden. It should not hide where Human judgment is still required.

The Product must expose:

- assumptions;
- uncertainty;
- alternatives;
- unresolved facts;
- affected version;
- requested decision;
- consequence of proceeding.

Human actions must remain distinct:

```text
user inspection
user disposition
factual confirmation
professional Review
client confirmation
Organization approval
execution authorization
```

One click may represent several of these actions only when the acting person genuinely holds all relevant roles and the consequence is clearly disclosed.

## 6. Principle Five — Preparation and External Action Are Separate

Lite should make consequential work easy to prepare and difficult to perform accidentally.

The default sequence is:

```text
prepare
→ preview exact version, subject, recipient/audience and consequence
→ Human Review where required
→ final user confirmation
→ governed or manual operation
→ typed result
```

This applies to:

- customer Communications;
- public content;
- provider instructions;
- Opportunity creation requests;
- MarkReg Launch;
- Delivery and Publish;
- Tasks and protected actions.

Unknown external outcomes must remain unknown.

```text
request accepted technically
≠ action completed

no error returned
≠ official acknowledgement

unknown result
≠ safe to retry blindly
```

## 7. Principle Six — Client Interest and Professional Risk Precede Revenue Ranking

Lite may help the user grow. Growth remains part of professional service.

The Product must not rank opportunities solely by expected revenue.

Factors may include:

- client benefit;
- urgency;
- rights risk;
- cost and proportionality;
- evidence quality;
- relationship context;
- professional confidence;
- likely service fit;
- commercial value.

A conforming Product may recommend:

- not proceeding;
- obtaining more information;
- deferring;
- using a narrower service;
- referring the matter;
- warning that the issue requires specialist Review.

A revenue-maximizing recommendation that harms client interest is non-conforming.

## 8. Principle Seven — Work Products Must Be More Than Generated Content

AI generation can create useful raw material. Lite must preserve the work-product lifecycle.

```text
Structured Content
→ Artifact Draft
→ Artifact Version
→ Review Package
→ Render / Edit
→ Delivery, Communication or Publish Package
→ operation and result
```

Required distinctions:

```text
Content ≠ Artifact
Artifact ≠ Document
Artifact ≠ Evidence
Artifact ≠ file
Render complete ≠ approved
Publish Package ready ≠ published
```

A high-volume content entitlement does not justify lowering source, version, rights or Review controls.

## 9. Principle Eight — Learning Must Be Bounded and Reversible

Lite should improve through use, but every learned item must have a scope.

Possible destinations include:

- personal preference;
- Personal Memory;
- Organization Memory;
- Case Lesson;
- reusable Asset;
- Knowledge contribution.

No automatic promotion chain should exist.

```text
observed repeatedly
≠ accepted preference

used by one person
≠ Organization standard

successful once
≠ general rule

reviewed internally
≠ approved for public use
```

Corrections, contradictions, expiry and retirement must be possible.

## 10. Principle Nine — Local and Private Context Remain Governed

A local-first or hybrid design can improve privacy, control and usability.

Local storage is not an exemption from governance.

Lite must distinguish:

- readable locally;
- indexed locally;
- used for a current purpose;
- synchronized to an Organization service;
- shared with a destination Product;
- promoted into memory or Knowledge.

```text
local access
≠ synchronization
≠ Organization truth
≠ permission to share
≠ professional authority
```

The minimum necessary context should be transferred for each Handoff.

## 11. Principle Ten — Deeper Products and Services Revalidate

Lite can prepare high-quality packages, but destination systems retain authority.

MarkReg revalidates trademark-service context. MGSN revalidates Capability Need and network conditions. Opportunity Service decides whether formal Opportunity state is created. Communication Service owns send state. Review determines approval. Execution governs coordinated work.

A destination must be able to return:

- accepted;
- more information required;
- rejected;
- blocked;
- failed;
- unknown external outcome;
- formal record reference;
- completed result.

The user should see that returned decision without Lite rewriting it into a more favorable state.

## 12. Principle Eleven — Product and Commercial Plan Remain Separate

Commercial plans may package capabilities differently.

A plan may define:

- price;
- billing period;
- trial;
- content frequency;
- Prospect Candidate quantity;
- Artifact or Render quotas;
- support level;
- team seats;
- storage or local capability;
- included MarkReg entry services.

These are commercial decisions.

```text
Product Charter
≠ edition
≠ plan
≠ entitlement
≠ quota
≠ experiment
```

The RMB 99 monthly candidate may be useful, but it cannot determine what Lite permanently is.

## 13. Principle Twelve — Product Success Is Outcome and Capability, Not Activity Alone

Activity metrics are useful operational evidence. They are insufficient as the definition of success.

```text
content generated
≠ content used

prospects delivered
≠ prospects relevant

messages sent
≠ meaningful responses

responses received
≠ qualified service needs

Handoffs created
≠ Handoffs accepted
```

Product success requires evidence across:

- recurring use;
- candidate relevance;
- work-product usefulness;
- Human action;
- qualified response;
- Handoff quality;
- returned outcome;
- capability accumulation;
- safety and privacy;
- commercial sustainability.

## 14. Non-Goal — Generic AI Chat

Lite may provide chat, but it is not defined by open-ended conversation.

A generic chat interface cannot by itself guarantee:

- active Organization;
- source and version;
- stable Product state;
- formal-object references;
- Review;
- Handoff and Return;
- memory promotion rules;
- external-action controls.

Chat is an interaction mode inside the Product.

## 15. Non-Goal — Content Factory

Lite may produce recurring articles, graphics, videos and social posts.

It must not become a factory optimized only for volume.

Content should participate in:

- professional education;
- customer development;
- case and Knowledge reuse;
- Organization visibility;
- work-product production;
- measured outcomes.

A content plan is one commercial expression, not the constitutional Product.

## 16. Non-Goal — Lead-List Marketplace

Lite may supply Prospect Candidates under a plan or experiment.

It must not represent a list as verified intention.

It must preserve:

- source;
- freshness;
- duplicate treatment;
- contactability limitations;
- channel restrictions;
- suppression and opt-out;
- service relevance;
- invalid-candidate evidence.

The Product must not depend on endless low-quality list volume.

## 17. Non-Goal — Universal CRM

Lite supports customer-development journeys but does not own the universal Customer or Opportunity lifecycle.

It may:

- present authorized Customer references;
- discover candidates;
- prepare follow-up;
- record Product-local qualification;
- request formal Customer or Opportunity creation.

It does not replace the formal services responsible for those objects.

## 18. Non-Goal — Universal Case-Management System

Lite may surface returned Tasks, Matters, Communications and Product results.

It does not own the full work-management lifecycle.

Case-management depth belongs to Workplace, Execution, MarkReg and other relevant Products and services.

## 19. Non-Goal — Reduced Workplace

Lite is architecturally a lightweight Workplace Product, but it is not a compressed copy of every Workplace module.

It should not reproduce all:

- Organization administration;
- finance;
- human resources;
- universal work management;
- every Product surface;
- every network function;
- every reporting need.

It selects the minimum context and capabilities needed for its focused operating journeys.

## 20. Non-Goal — Reduced MarkReg

Lite may identify a trademark-service need and prepare a MarkReg Handoff.

It must not rebuild:

- jurisdiction recommendation;
- full classification and goods/services journey;
- applicant and authority lifecycle;
- formal intake;
- filing package and approval;
- examination and dispute workflows;
- registration and maintenance lifecycle.

These belong to MarkReg.

## 21. Non-Goal — Second MGSN

Lite may prepare Capability Need and display minimized provider candidates.

It must not own:

- network eligibility;
- universal Trust;
- Routing depth;
- provider appointment;
- cross-Organization collaboration lifecycle;
- public marketplace bidding.

These belong to MGSN and related governed services.

## 22. Non-Goal — Autonomous Professional or Sales Agent

Lite is not authorized to act as the lawyer, trademark agent, salesperson or Organization owner.

It must not autonomously:

- give final professional advice;
- approve work products;
- contact customers;
- publish claims;
- appoint providers;
- accept quotations;
- create formal service commitments;
- perform official or protected actions.

Policy-bounded automation may be considered later only after the relevant authority, evidence and rollback controls exist.

## 23. Zero-Tolerance Conditions

The affected capability or pilot must be blocked when Lite:

- exposes one Customer or Matter’s restricted information to another;
- presents a candidate as formal Opportunity, Task, Communication, Document, Evidence or official result;
- invents or hides source, status, fee, deadline or case outcome;
- sends, publishes or performs external action without required authority;
- promotes private experience into shared Knowledge without Review;
- appoints a provider automatically;
- retries an unknown external result unsafely;
- treats local access as synchronization or authority;
- lets revenue ranking override client interest or professional risk;
- uses content count, lead count or activity volume as proof of customer value.

These are constitutional failures, not minor implementation defects.

## 24. A Test for Every Future Capability

Before adding a capability, the Product team should ask:

1. Which of the four loops does it strengthen?
2. Which user and purpose does it serve?
3. Which sources and data classifications does it use?
4. Which Product-local records does it create?
5. Which formal objects remain externally owned?
6. What Human decision or Review is required?
7. What Handoff and Return occur?
8. How can the capability fail safely?
9. What evidence proves real value?
10. Can the capability change or be removed without redefining Lite?

A capability that cannot answer these questions is not ready to enter the Product baseline.

## 25. The Constitutional Locks

The constitutional boundaries can be summarized as:

```text
Lite ≠ generic AI chat
Lite ≠ content factory
Lite ≠ lead-list marketplace
Lite ≠ universal CRM
Lite ≠ universal case-management system
Lite ≠ reduced Workplace
Lite ≠ reduced MarkReg
Lite ≠ second MGSN
Lite ≠ autonomous professional actor
```

And:

```text
candidate ≠ formal truth
preparation ≠ execution
visibility ≠ permission
local access ≠ synchronization
Handoff ≠ approval
Return ≠ Lite-owned truth
repeated use ≠ canonical Knowledge
activity volume ≠ customer value
```

These principles complete the Product constitution.

Part II will show how the constitution becomes a usable daily operating model through Today, authorized context, candidates, user disposition, Prepared Actions and safe Handoffs.
