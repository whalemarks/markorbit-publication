# Part I — Product Constitution and Place in MarkOrbit

Part I establishes the durable meaning of Lite before discussing surfaces, features or commercial plans.

It explains why Lite exists, what “AI business operating system” means, where Lite sits in the wider architecture, which users and value loops it serves, and which constitutional boundaries every edition must preserve.

## B06-CH-02 — Why MarkOrbit Lite Exists

Explains the missing low-friction daily operating layer between fragmented professional practice and the deeper MarkOrbit systems.

It addresses missed service value, repeated preparation, lost context, weak opportunity-to-service continuity and the inadequacy of a single recommendation card.

## B06-CH-03 — The AI Business Operating System for Trademark Professionals

Defines the user-facing identity of Lite and explains why it is an operating system for professional practice rather than a generic AI assistant.

It introduces the individual-first but Organization-centered model and the compounding value produced by use, correction and outcomes.

## B06-CH-04 — Position Between Workplace, Core, Execution, MarkReg and MGSN

Allocates responsibility among Lite and the surrounding MarkOrbit architecture.

It preserves the constitutional rule:

```text
Lite owns Product experience, candidates, preparation and continuity.
Owning Services own formal business truth.
```

## B06-CH-05 — Users, Value Promise and the Four Product Loops

Defines primary users, secondary modes, Product promise and the four enduring loops:

1. Customer and Service Growth;
2. Professional Work Products;
3. Professional Memory and Business Assets;
4. MarkOrbit Ecosystem Handoff.

## B06-CH-06 — Principles, Boundaries and Non-Goals

Defines the constitutional locks and rejects drift into generic chat, content factory, lead marketplace, universal CRM, reduced Workplace, reduced MarkReg, second MGSN or autonomous professional practice.

# Part III — Customer and Service Growth

Part III explains growth as relevant professional value, not activity volume or indiscriminate outreach.

The preferred growth sequence is:

```text
existing customer portfolio
→ historical customer reactivation
→ user-owned prospect
→ platform-supplied Prospect Candidate
```
