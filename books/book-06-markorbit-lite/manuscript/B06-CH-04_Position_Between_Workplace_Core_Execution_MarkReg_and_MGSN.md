# B06-CH-04 — Position Between Workplace, Core, Execution, MarkReg and MGSN

**Status:** Complete Draft 1  
**Chapter Map:** B06-TOC-V0.1 — Owner Accepted  
**Part:** Part I — Product Constitution and Place in MarkOrbit

## Chapter Purpose

This chapter places MarkOrbit Lite within the wider MarkOrbit architecture and allocates responsibility among Lite, Workplace, Core, Execution, MarkReg, MGSN and Owning Services.

The central rule is:

> Lite owns Product experience, candidate state, preparation and continuity. Owning Services own formal business truth.

## 1. A Focused Product Needs a Stable Surrounding Architecture

Lite can remain lightweight only because it does not attempt to own every responsibility required by professional practice.

Its daily journeys depend on surrounding capabilities:

- shared identity, Organization, Customer, Trademark and Opportunity meaning;
- permission and policy;
- governed Tasks, Workflows, Reviews and Communications;
- full trademark-service journeys;
- provider capability and network relationships;
- formal Documents, Evidence, Orders, Matters and official results.

If Lite duplicates these responsibilities, it stops being lightweight. It also creates conflicting truth.

The correct relationship is composition, not absorption.

```text
shared meaning
+ authorized Organization context
+ governed work
+ focused Lite journey
+ destination Product/service
→ coherent professional operation
```

## 2. The Responsibility Chain

The accepted architecture assigns distinct responsibilities.

```text
Core defines shared meaning.
Execution governs coordinated work.
Workplace provides authorized Organization context.
Products compose focused user journeys.
MGSN connects independent organizational Orbits.
Owning Services change formal business facts.
Humans remain professionally accountable.
AI assists under explicit governance.
```

Lite is one Product in this chain.

It is not placed above the other layers. It uses them to create a coherent user journey.

## 3. Core Defines Shared Meaning

Core provides stable semantics for shared business objects and services.

Depending on the accepted Core baseline, these include meanings such as:

- User;
- Organization;
- role and permission context;
- Customer and Contact;
- Trademark;
- Opportunity;
- Order and Matter;
- Task and Communication references;
- Document and Evidence;
- Review and AI Output;
- formal ownership and lifecycle references.

Lite consumes these meanings. It must not create a parallel interpretation merely because a lighter interface would be convenient.

For example:

```text
ML-O05 Prospect Candidate
≠ Customer

ML-O03 Service-Value Candidate
≠ formal Opportunity

ML-S03 Attention Item
≠ active Task

ML-W02 Artifact Draft
≠ Document or Evidence
```

These distinctions allow Lite to operate before formalization without corrupting shared truth.

## 4. Owning Services Change Formal Business Facts

Core semantics alone do not imply that every Product may mutate every object.

Formal state belongs to the relevant Owning Service.

Examples include:

- Customer Service creates or updates formal Customer records;
- Opportunity Service creates and manages formal Opportunities;
- Task Service owns active Task state;
- Communication Service owns send state and evidence;
- Document and Evidence services preserve their formal records;
- Order and Matter services own their lifecycles;
- MarkReg owns its Product lifecycle;
- MGSN and related services own Trust, Routing and collaboration state.

Lite may:

- discover a candidate;
- collect missing context;
- prepare an exact-purpose request;
- send a typed Handoff;
- present the returned result.

Lite may not silently create the destination’s formal state.

## 5. Workplace Provides Organization Context

Book 04 defines Workplace as the operating shell of an independent Organization Orbit.

Workplace provides context such as:

- acting Organization;
- people, roles and permissions;
- Customer relationships;
- private Knowledge and AI context;
- Organization preferences and business rules;
- operating surfaces;
- local and synchronized data boundaries;
- embedded Products and cross-Product continuity.

Lite is architecturally a lightweight Workplace Product. This does not mean it recreates all Workplace responsibilities.

The distinction is:

```text
Workplace architecture
→ defines the full Organization operating environment

Lite
→ composes a focused daily operating Product within that environment
```

An independent professional may experience Lite as the primary surface. A larger Organization may use it as a lightweight entry, mobile cockpit or companion. In both cases, Organization context remains authoritative outside the Product-local projection.

## 6. Execution Governs Coordinated Work

Book 03 defines how approved intentions become governed work.

Execution includes concerns such as:

- Task and Workflow;
- dependencies;
- assignments;
- Review and approval gates;
- Communications;
- protected actions;
- retries and failure handling;
- audit and result reconciliation.

Lite may prepare work, but preparation is not execution.

```text
candidate
→ recommendation
→ work product
→ Prepared Action
→ Handoff
→ governed execution
```

Lite should expose enough information for the user to understand the consequence of an action. When the action requires coordinated work, formal responsibility or external effect, the correct destination must govern it.

### User confirmation is not Execution authority

A user may confirm:

- the selected recipient;
- the chosen Artifact version;
- the intended purpose;
- the desire to continue.

That confirmation does not automatically establish:

- professional approval;
- organizational authorization;
- technical send permission;
- official filing authority;
- provider appointment;
- execution success.

## 7. MarkReg Owns the Full Trademark Product Lifecycle

MarkReg is the dedicated Product for full-lifecycle international trademark work.

Its scope includes:

- need understanding;
- jurisdiction and route strategy;
- applicant and ownership context;
- classification and goods/services guidance;
- search and risk;
- proposal, pricing and intake;
- filing preparation;
- professional Review and approval;
- provider and connector routes;
- examination, disputes and responses;
- registration, maintenance and portfolio continuity.

Lite must not recreate this lifecycle in a simplified form.

Lite’s MarkReg relationship is:

```text
Lite discovers or receives a possible service need
→ prepares source-linked context and work product
→ user qualifies the need
→ ML-H03 MarkReg Handoff
→ MarkReg revalidates and creates/continues Product Session
→ MarkReg returns typed result
→ Lite presents the result and possible next value
```

A deep link is not a Handoff. A prefilled form is not destination acceptance. A successful client conversation is not a MarkReg Product Session.

## 8. MGSN Owns Network Depth

MGSN connects independent professional organizations through Capability, Trust, Routing and governed collaboration.

Lite may need an external provider or capability, but it does not own the network lifecycle.

Lite may prepare:

- jurisdiction;
- required service;
- deadline or urgency;
- language;
- disclosure scope;
- commercial constraints;
- private relationship preference;
- required evidence of capability.

This becomes a Capability Need Handoff.

MGSN or the relevant network service may return a minimized candidate set or indicate that more information is required.

Lite must not:

- convert a provider directory into verified eligibility;
- expose unrestricted provider pricing;
- appoint a provider automatically;
- create one universal Trust score;
- absorb cross-Organization collaboration state;
- treat a returned candidate as engagement acceptance.

Book 07 will define the network in greater depth. Book 06 defines only the Lite-side gateway and continuity.

## 9. Lite Owns Product-Local Experience and State

Lite requires controlled Product-local records to provide continuity.

### Session and attention

- `ML-S01` Lite Session;
- `ML-S02` Today Snapshot;
- `ML-S03` Attention Item;
- `ML-S04` User Disposition;
- `ML-S05` Continuation State.

### Observations and candidates

- source observations;
- customer/trademark signals;
- service-value candidates;
- reactivation candidates;
- prospect candidates;
- work-product candidates;
- recommendation presentations;
- qualification results.

### Work products and action preparation

- Structured Content;
- Artifact drafts and versions;
- Review Packages;
- Prepared Actions;
- Development Packages;
- Render requests and results;
- Delivery or Publish Package candidates;
- readiness results.

### Memory and continuity

- feedback and corrections;
- preference and memory candidates;
- case lessons;
- reusable Asset and Knowledge contribution candidates;
- Handoff and Return presentations;
- evaluation and commercial experiment records.

This state supports Product operation. It does not become a shadow Core.

## 10. Projection Is Not Ownership

Lite may display formal information from another service.

A Today item may show:

- a MarkReg status;
- an active Task summary;
- a Communication result;
- an Opportunity stage;
- a provider response;
- a Document requirement.

The presentation must retain:

- source service;
- source record reference;
- source time or version;
- active Organization and scope;
- whether the information is current, stale or unresolved.

```text
visible in Lite
≠ owned by Lite

editable surface
≠ mutation authority

cached locally
≠ canonical truth
```

## 11. Handoff Is the Constitutional Connection

Lite connects to the surrounding architecture through typed Handoffs.

The common Handoff Envelope includes, as applicable:

- origin Product;
- active Organization and user;
- subject and purpose;
- Customer, Trademark and Matter references;
- selected work-product version;
- sources and freshness;
- uncertainty and missing information;
- intended consequence;
- required Review or confirmation;
- allowed destination;
- return address.

The destination may return:

```text
accepted
more information required
rejected
blocked
failed
unknown external outcome
formal record reference returned
completed by destination
```

The Return remains a sourced destination result.

## 12. AI Operates Inside Every Boundary

AI may assist across Lite, Workplace, MarkReg, MGSN and Execution. Its authority does not expand because it crosses Product boundaries.

Within Lite, AI may:

- assemble authorized context;
- identify and rank candidates;
- explain relevance;
- prepare work products;
- detect missing information;
- prepare Handoff Envelopes;
- analyze explicit feedback.

The Product must still preserve:

- acting identity;
- data scope;
- source and version;
- uncertainty;
- Human Review;
- destination revalidation;
- formal result authority.

An AI Agent is not an Owning Service.

## 13. Example: Existing Customer Portfolio Opportunity

Consider an existing client with a registered mark in one market and evidence of expansion into another.

### Lite

Lite may:

- surface the authorized observation;
- connect it to the Customer and Trademark references;
- explain the possible service value;
- prepare a portfolio note or client-facing report;
- prepare follow-up language;
- record user disposition;
- send a Communication or MarkReg Handoff after confirmation.

### Communication Service

The Communication Service owns the actual send request, send result and evidence.

### Opportunity Service

If the client response confirms a qualified need, the Opportunity Service decides whether to create a formal Opportunity and returns its reference.

### MarkReg

MarkReg revalidates the need, applicant, mark, jurisdiction and authority context and owns the full service journey.

### Lite again

Lite presents the returned result and may identify a future follow-up or reusable lesson.

No layer needs to pretend it owns the entire chain.

## 14. Boundary Failures

The architecture fails when Lite:

- creates a Customer from a public company name without formalization;
- labels a candidate as an Opportunity before Opportunity Service acceptance;
- marks a draft message as sent;
- marks a rendered file as professionally approved;
- copies a Document into Artifact state and loses its formal meaning;
- treats a MarkReg deep link as a Product Session;
- treats a provider candidate as appointed;
- treats a local file as synchronized Organization truth;
- creates a completed result from an unknown external outcome.

These are not minor UI defects. They are authority errors.

## 15. The Position of Lite

Lite sits at the point where daily professional attention becomes usable work and then enters the deeper MarkOrbit system.

```text
Organization context and shared meaning
→ Lite daily Product journey
→ Human judgment and preparation
→ governed destination
→ formal business truth
→ returned result and bounded continuity
```

Its power comes from connection.

Its safety comes from not absorbing the connected systems.

The next chapter defines the users, value promise and four Product loops that operate within this position.
