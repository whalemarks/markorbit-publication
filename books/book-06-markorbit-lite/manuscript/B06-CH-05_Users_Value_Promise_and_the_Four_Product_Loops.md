# B06-CH-05 — Users, Value Promise and the Four Product Loops

**Status:** Complete Draft 1  
**Chapter Map:** B06-TOC-V0.1 — Owner Accepted  
**Part:** Part I — Product Constitution and Place in MarkOrbit

## Chapter Purpose

This chapter defines the primary users of MarkOrbit Lite, the value promise made to them, and the four enduring Product loops through which that promise is delivered.

The central proposition is:

> Lite creates value when it helps a professional identify relevant work, produce a trustworthy result, move the work into a governed destination and retain capability that improves future practice.

## 1. Primary Users

The primary users are:

- independent trademark professionals;
- small trademark agencies;
- small teams in which one person may combine professional, commercial and operating responsibilities.

These users may differ in legal qualification, jurisdiction, service scope and business model. Their common problem is not simply lack of software. It is the difficulty of coordinating professional judgment, client development, work-product preparation and service continuity with limited operating capacity.

A typical primary user may:

- manage a portfolio of existing clients;
- perform filing, renewal, recordal, response or dispute work;
- collaborate with foreign associates;
- prepare quotations and client explanations;
- maintain personal or team knowledge;
- produce educational or marketing content;
- develop new business;
- work across email, messaging, local files, spreadsheets and public databases;
- need deeper MarkReg or MGSN capability without operating the full MarkOrbit Workplace.

Lite should reduce operating friction without reducing professional responsibility.

## 2. Secondary Modes

Lite may also operate in secondary modes.

### Lightweight entry for a larger Workplace

A user in a larger Organization may use Lite as a focused daily entry into customer opportunities, prepared work products and returned Product results.

The larger Workplace remains responsible for Organization-wide operating context, permissions and broader surfaces.

### Mobile business cockpit

Lite may present selected Today items, approvals, work-product previews and returned outcomes on a mobile surface.

Mobile convenience does not reduce source, version or confirmation requirements.

### Local/private companion

Lite may help the user work with authorized local files, local retrieval and private context.

Local access does not imply synchronization, Organization sharing or professional authority.

### Product-entry channel

Lite may be the first MarkOrbit Product experienced by a small agency. A qualified need may later enter MarkReg, MGSN or a fuller Workplace.

This commercial role does not make Lite the owner of those destination lifecycles.

## 3. The User Is Not One Abstract Persona

A useful Product cannot assume that every user is simultaneously:

- the Organization owner;
- the responsible professional;
- the salesperson;
- the reviewer;
- the sender;
- the payer;
- the final approver.

An independent practitioner may hold several roles. A small agency may separate them.

Lite therefore needs to preserve:

- active Organization;
- acting user;
- professional responsibility;
- permission and policy;
- Customer and Matter scope;
- Review responsibility;
- external-action authority.

The Product may simplify the surface for a single user, but it must not collapse the meanings.

## 4. The Value Promise

The user-facing promise can be stated simply:

> Lite helps you understand what deserves attention, prepare useful professional work, develop relevant customer value, move qualified work into the right MarkOrbit service and retain what you learn.

The promise is not:

- “AI will run your practice for you”;
- “every recommendation will generate revenue”;
- “every prospect is an interested customer”;
- “every generated output is ready to use”;
- “every action can be automated.”

The Product promise includes four forms of value.

### Timely value

The right customer, trademark, case or returned result becomes visible at a useful time.

### Explainable value

The user can see why the item matters, which source supports it, what is uncertain and what consequence may follow.

### Usable value

The Product prepares a work product, Development Package, Prepared Action or Handoff that the user can inspect and use.

### Compounding value

Corrections, outcomes and approved reusable Assets improve future work within the correct scope.

## 5. Loop One — Customer and Service Growth

The Customer and Service Growth Loop begins with authorized context and ends with a qualified need entering a governed service journey.

```text
authorized customer, trademark or market context
→ service-value candidate
→ explanation and qualification
→ customer-development work product
→ user-confirmed action
→ response
→ qualified service need
→ formal Opportunity / MarkReg / service Handoff
→ returned outcome
```

### Growth is not prospect volume

A large list of companies may create activity without value.

The preferred order is:

```text
existing customer portfolio
→ historical customer reactivation
→ user-owned prospect
→ platform-supplied Prospect Candidate
```

This order reflects increasing uncertainty and decreasing relationship authority.

### Existing customer value comes first

Existing customer and trademark context is usually more relevant because:

- the relationship is known;
- the professional may already have authority to use the data;
- the portfolio is visible;
- service history provides context;
- client needs can be explained more precisely;
- MarkReg continuity is stronger.

### Growth remains bounded by client interest

The highest-value commercial action may be to advise against unnecessary work, defer an action or explain uncertainty.

```text
revenue opportunity
≠ client benefit

client response
≠ qualified need

qualified need
≠ accepted Order
```

The Growth Loop must preserve professional risk and client interest over revenue ranking.

## 6. Loop Two — Professional Work Products

The Professional Work-Product Loop turns trusted context into a usable result.

```text
purpose and authorized context
→ Structured Content
→ Artifact Draft
→ Artifact Version
→ Review
→ Render and optional Edit
→ usable work product
→ Delivery / Communication / Publish preparation
→ result and feedback
```

### Work products are broader than content

Lite may prepare:

- customer analysis;
- trademark portfolio review;
- service recommendation;
- quotation or proposal;
- case explanation;
- professional report;
- graphic or presentation;
- article or social post;
- short video;
- customer follow-up package;
- MarkReg or MGSN Handoff.

Daily content is one supply mode. It is not the complete Product.

### A work product has purpose and audience

The same source material may support different outputs.

A case may become:

- an internal lesson;
- a client explanation;
- a training example;
- a public article;
- a short video;
- a reusable template.

Each output requires its own purpose, audience, source scope, version, rights and Review.

### Generation is not completion

```text
AI text
≠ Structured Content

Structured Content
≠ Artifact

Artifact Draft
≠ approved Artifact Version

Render Result
≠ professionally approved result

Publish Package
≠ published
```

The Product must make these transitions visible.

## 7. Loop Three — Professional Memory and Business Assets

The Professional Memory and Business Asset Loop creates long-term value.

```text
practice and outcome
→ sourced feedback or correction
→ preference, memory or case candidate
→ Human Review
→ accepted memory / reusable Asset / Knowledge contribution
→ future use
→ later outcome and correction
```

### Personal continuity

Lite may learn that a user prefers:

- concise client messages;
- particular report structures;
- certain service priorities;
- specific working-language conventions;
- a preferred order of review.

These observations remain candidates until accepted and scoped.

### Organization capability

A small agency may approve:

- quotation templates;
- client-explanation patterns;
- internal checklists;
- reviewed case lessons;
- service-development packages;
- reusable graphics and documents.

Organization approval is not inferred from one person’s repeated use.

### Knowledge contribution

Some experience may be suitable for contribution to shared governed Knowledge.

The Knowledge destination decides whether the contribution is accepted, rejected or requires revision.

```text
case experience
≠ canonical Knowledge

personal memory
≠ Organization truth

reusable Asset
≠ public material
```

## 8. Loop Four — MarkOrbit Ecosystem Handoff

The Ecosystem Handoff Loop connects Lite to deeper capabilities.

```text
Lite discovers and prepares
→ typed Handoff Envelope
→ destination revalidation
→ governed work and formal result
→ typed Return
→ Lite presents outcome and next value
```

Destinations include:

- MarkReg;
- MGSN;
- Human Review;
- Communication Service;
- Opportunity Service;
- Task and Execution;
- Delivery and Publish;
- Knowledge Governance.

### Handoff avoids duplicate entry

A strong Handoff carries enough context to reduce unnecessary re-entry:

- subject and purpose;
- active Organization and user;
- Customer, Trademark and Matter references;
- source and version;
- selected work product;
- missing information;
- intended consequence;
- required confirmation or Review;
- return address.

### Handoff does not grant acceptance

The destination may reject the package, request more information or apply a different formal classification.

```text
complete-looking Envelope
≠ destination acceptance

Handoff
≠ approval

Return
≠ Lite-owned formal truth
```

## 9. The Loops Must Reinforce One Another

The Product becomes stronger when the four loops connect.

### Example: portfolio expansion

1. Lite detects a possible jurisdiction gap in an existing customer portfolio.
2. It explains the source and uncertainty.
3. It prepares a client-facing portfolio note.
4. The user confirms the contact.
5. The customer responds with a real expansion need.
6. Lite sends a MarkReg Handoff.
7. MarkReg returns a Product Session reference.
8. The outcome later becomes a bounded case lesson and reusable report structure.

This one journey uses all four loops.

### Example: public educational content

1. A reviewed case lesson is approved for a defined public audience.
2. Lite creates Structured Content and an Artifact Version.
3. The user reviews the claims and rights.
4. The Artifact is rendered and published through a governed operation.
5. A customer responds.
6. The response becomes a qualification candidate.
7. A formal service journey begins if appropriate.
8. The actual response corrects future content strategy.

Content is valuable because it participates in the larger operating system.

## 10. Value Metrics Must Follow the Loops

A Product that measures only output volume will optimize the wrong behavior.

Useful measures include:

### Growth

- accepted and rejected service-value candidates;
- customer contact rate;
- meaningful response rate;
- qualified service-need rate;
- MarkReg or service Handoff acceptance;
- rework caused by missing context.

### Work products

- time to usable work product;
- user modification rate;
- actual use, delivery or publication;
- Review defects;
- source and rights defects;
- corrected or retired versions.

### Memory and Assets

- accepted corrections;
- approved reusable Assets;
- reuse within allowed scope;
- contradiction and retirement events;
- improvement in future preparation.

### Ecosystem continuity

- Handoff acceptance;
- more-information requests;
- duplicate entry avoided;
- Return completeness;
- unknown or failed external outcomes;
- unsafe-action blocks.

### Commercial sustainability

- recurring use;
- retention;
- cost to fulfill a plan;
- support burden;
- gross-margin evidence;
- conversion into appropriate deeper services.

No single metric should become the Product constitution.

## 11. What the User Should Experience

The user should not need to think in terms of every underlying service and controlled record.

The experience should feel like:

```text
I can see what matters.
I understand why it matters.
Useful work is already prepared.
I know what I must decide.
The right system receives the work.
I can see what happened.
The Product becomes more useful over time.
```

The architecture remains visible where it affects trust, permission, Review, consequence or formal status.

## 12. The Value Promise Is Conditional

Lite cannot promise that:

- every candidate is correct;
- every client will respond;
- every service will convert;
- every generated work product will be accepted;
- every provider will be available;
- every external result will be immediate;
- every commercial experiment will succeed.

It can promise a disciplined operating process:

- source-linked candidates;
- visible uncertainty;
- user control;
- professional work-product preparation;
- governed Handoffs;
- typed outcomes;
- bounded learning.

This is a more durable promise than unlimited generation or guaranteed leads.

## 13. Product Success

Lite succeeds when it becomes part of the user’s weekly operating rhythm and improves the quality of professional action.

The desired result is:

```text
less missed value
+ less repeated preparation
+ better client explanation
+ stronger service continuity
+ more reusable capability
+ preserved professional responsibility
```

The four loops provide the structure for that result.

The next chapter establishes the principles, boundaries and non-goals that prevent these loops from drifting into an unsafe or incoherent Product.
