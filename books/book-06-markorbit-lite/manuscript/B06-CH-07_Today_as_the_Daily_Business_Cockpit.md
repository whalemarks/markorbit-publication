# B06-CH-07 — Today as the Daily Business Cockpit

**Status:** Complete Draft 1  
**Chapter Map:** B06-TOC-V0.1 — Owner Accepted  
**Part:** Part II — The Daily Operating Model

## Chapter Purpose

This chapter defines `Today` as the daily business cockpit of MarkOrbit Lite.

The central proposition is:

> Today is a sourced, time-bound Product projection that helps a professional understand what deserves attention, what has already been prepared, what requires a decision, and what has returned from a formal destination.

Today is not:

- a universal Task list;
- the official source of deadlines;
- a replacement for Customer, Matter, Opportunity or Communication records;
- an AI-generated priority list without provenance;
- the complete identity of MarkOrbit Lite.

Today is where the four Product loops become visible in one daily operating surface.

## 1. A Professional Day Is Not Organized by One Object Type

An independent trademark professional may begin the day with several unrelated-looking concerns:

- a client registration is approaching a maintenance period;
- an inactive client has launched a new brand;
- a trademark status source has changed;
- a customer-facing report is ready for review;
- a draft follow-up has not yet been sent;
- MarkReg has returned a request for missing information;
- a previous customer has replied;
- a reusable case lesson requires confirmation;
- a local file is available but not authorized for synchronization.

These items do not all belong to one shared object type.

Some are:

- observations;
- Product-local candidates;
- projections of formal records;
- Prepared Actions;
- returned results;
- unfinished Product context;
- review or confirmation requests.

A single formal Task model cannot accurately represent all of them.

```text
what deserves attention today
≠ everything that exists
≠ every formal Task
≠ every deadline
≠ every recommendation
```

Today therefore organizes attention, not universal business truth.

## 2. Today Is a Projection, Not a New System of Record

The accepted Product Baseline defines:

```text
ML-S01 — Lite Session
ML-S02 — Today Snapshot
ML-S03 — Attention Item
ML-S04 — User Disposition
ML-S05 — Continuation State
```

A Today Snapshot is a Product-local projection set.

It may assemble views of:

- a Customer or Trademark record owned elsewhere;
- an Observation generated from a source;
- a Service-Value Candidate owned by Lite;
- a returned MarkReg result;
- an Artifact Version waiting for review;
- a Communication draft waiting for recipient confirmation;
- a Continuation State from an unfinished Lite journey.

The surface may present these items together, but it must preserve their different authority.

```text
one surface
≠ one owner
≠ one lifecycle
≠ one permission model
```

Today does not convert external records into Lite records merely by displaying them.

## 3. The Today Snapshot Is Time-Bound

A daily cockpit becomes unsafe when it hides when its information was assembled.

A Today Snapshot should retain:

- snapshot generation time;
- active user;
- active Organization;
- Product mode;
- source retrieval time;
- source freshness;
- destination return time;
- expiry or suppression state;
- known missing information.

A user may open Today at 08:00 and return at 17:00. During that period:

- a customer may reply;
- an official status may change;
- a destination may reject a Handoff;
- a deadline source may be corrected;
- an Artifact may receive a new version;
- another team member may complete a review.

The Product must not pretend that the earlier snapshot remains current.

```text
Today Snapshot at 08:00
≠ live universal truth at 17:00
```

A conforming experience may refresh, show stale indicators, or require revalidation before a consequential action.

## 4. Attention Items Need a Reason to Exist

An Attention Item is not merely a card with a title.

It should answer:

1. What is the subject?
2. Why is this appearing now?
3. Which source or returned result supports it?
4. Which customer, trademark, Matter or Product journey is involved?
5. What is uncertain or missing?
6. What can the user do next?
7. What happens if the user does nothing?
8. Which destination owns the formal consequence?

A weak item may say:

> Renewal opportunity detected.

A useful item should explain:

- which registration is involved;
- what source date supports the observation;
- whether the obligation is confirmed or only possible;
- which customer relationship is in scope;
- why the issue matters commercially or professionally;
- whether preparation can begin safely;
- what must be reviewed before external contact.

The difference is not visual design. It is Product integrity.

## 5. Today Must Combine the Four Product Loops

Today is valuable because it does not reduce the day to one narrow category.

### 5.1 Customer and Service Growth

Today may show:

- a timely portfolio opportunity;
- a historical customer reactivation candidate;
- a Prospect Candidate with a specific service signal;
- a customer response requiring qualification;
- a previously deferred opportunity whose source remains current.

### 5.2 Professional Work Products

Today may show:

- a customer report ready for review;
- an Artifact Version requiring correction;
- a Render Result that failed;
- a Delivery Package ready for confirmation;
- a work product blocked by missing information.

### 5.3 Professional Memory and Business Assets

Today may show:

- a correction that should be preserved;
- a Case Lesson Candidate awaiting review;
- a Preference Candidate requiring explicit acceptance;
- an approved Asset whose source has become stale;
- a reusable template affected by a legal change.

### 5.4 MarkOrbit Ecosystem Handoff

Today may show:

- a MarkReg request for more information;
- an MGSN capability result;
- a Human Review decision;
- a Communication failure;
- a formal Opportunity reference returned by the owning service;
- an unknown external outcome that requires reconciliation.

The cockpit therefore represents a daily cross-section of Product value.

## 6. Today Is Not the Product Constitution

It is tempting to define Lite by its home screen.

This would be a mistake.

Today is an operating surface. Lite also includes:

- authorized context assembly;
- candidate creation and explanation;
- qualification;
- work-product preparation;
- version and review handling;
- Handoff and Return continuity;
- feedback and correction;
- memory and reusable capability.

A Product could technically present Today in another interface while preserving the same semantics.

Similarly, a dashboard called “Today” does not conform merely because it displays cards.

```text
Today label
≠ Today semantics
```

The Product is defined by the controlled journey and authority model, not by one screen name.

## 7. Today Item Is Not an Active Task

This distinction is constitutional.

An active Task typically has:

- a formal owner;
- an assigned participant;
- a lifecycle;
- due dates;
- dependencies;
- completion state;
- audit and escalation rules.

An Attention Item may instead be:

- a possible opportunity;
- a returned result;
- a blocked draft;
- a source warning;
- an unanswered customer response;
- an optional reusable-asset review.

Turning every Attention Item into a Task would create noise and duplicate formal work.

```text
Attention Item
→ may become Prepared Action
→ may generate Task / Execution Handoff
→ destination decides whether a formal Task exists
```

Lite may prepare the request. It does not silently create the formal Task lifecycle.

## 8. Today Is Not the Official Deadline Source

Trademark work contains deadlines with serious consequences.

Today may display a deadline projection or a source-backed urgency indicator. However:

- the official or owning source retains authority;
- the displayed time must retain source and freshness;
- conflicting sources must remain visible;
- a calculated date may require professional verification;
- a Today ranking does not create or modify the deadline.

A red badge is not legal authority.

```text
urgent presentation
≠ verified deadline
≠ formal responsibility assignment
```

When a deadline must govern work, the relevant formal service, Task, Workflow or Matter context remains responsible.

## 9. Ranking Must Serve Professional Value

Today needs ordering because attention is limited.

Possible ranking factors include:

- time sensitivity;
- client impact;
- professional risk;
- source confidence;
- readiness;
- relationship relevance;
- work already invested;
- destination response;
- expected customer value;
- user preference.

Revenue potential may be one factor. It must not override:

- client interest;
- professional responsibility;
- legal or factual uncertainty;
- opt-out or suppression;
- privacy constraints;
- permission limitations.

```text
highest expected revenue
≠ highest professional priority
```

A blocked unsafe action may be more important than a promising sales opportunity.

## 10. The User Needs Meaningful Dispositions

`ML-S04 User Disposition` gives the user more options than “Done” or “Ignore.”

A user may:

- inspect;
- accept for preparation;
- defer;
- dismiss;
- suppress;
- correct;
- route;
- request more information.

These choices mean different things.

### Dismiss

The item is not useful in the current context, but a future independently supported candidate may still appear.

### Suppress

The user or policy indicates that similar items should not appear within a defined scope.

### Defer

The item remains relevant but should return at a specific time or condition.

### Correct

The user identifies a factual, contextual or Product error that should affect the current item and possibly a scoped learning candidate.

### Accept for preparation

The Product may prepare a work product or action package. It does not authorize external execution.

The Product should not flatten all of these into one hidden engagement score.

## 11. Continuation Preserves Work Without Inventing State

A professional may begin preparing a client explanation, stop, and return later.

`ML-S05 Continuation State` preserves:

- current Product journey;
- selected candidate;
- exact work-product version;
- unresolved questions;
- required Review;
- intended destination;
- last user action;
- expiry or freshness requirements.

It must not claim:

- that a Communication was sent;
- that an Opportunity was created;
- that MarkReg accepted the Handoff;
- that a Task was assigned;
- that a customer agreed.

Continuation is Product continuity, not formal state continuity.

## 12. Returned Results Must Re-enter Today With Their Source

One of Lite’s defining advantages is that a journey can continue after leaving Lite.

For example:

```text
Lite prepares MarkReg Handoff
→ MarkReg requests more information
→ Return Envelope reaches Lite
→ Today shows the request
→ user continues preparation
```

Today must display:

- destination;
- returned status;
- source time;
- formal reference where available;
- missing information;
- next permitted actions;
- whether revalidation is required.

A Return is not a Lite-created truth. Today presents it as a sourced destination result.

## 13. Unknown and Failed Outcomes Need Visibility

A professional operating cockpit becomes dangerous if it hides uncertainty.

Today should preserve states such as:

```text
stale
incomplete
unsupported
blocked_by_permission
blocked_by_policy
review_required
rejected
failed
unknown_external_outcome
expired
suppressed
retired
```

An unknown external outcome may require:

- checking the destination;
- asking the user;
- reconciling a connector response;
- reviewing a Communication record;
- waiting for an official source.

It must not be converted to “completed” merely to keep the interface clean.

## 14. A Useful Daily Cockpit Reduces Cognitive Switching

The value of Today is not that it stores everything.

Its value is that it reduces repeated reconstruction:

- the user does not need to search several tools merely to understand context;
- the user does not need to remember why a customer should be contacted;
- the user does not need to find the latest work-product version manually;
- the user can see which action is safe to prepare;
- returned results remain connected to the originating purpose.

This does not eliminate professional judgment. It concentrates judgment where it matters.

## 15. Minimum Conforming Today Experience

A minimum conforming experience should demonstrate:

1. an active Lite Session with user and Organization context;
2. a time-bound Today Snapshot;
3. Attention Items with source and reason;
4. at least one projection of external formal state;
5. at least one Product-local candidate;
6. meaningful User Dispositions;
7. a Continuation State;
8. visible stale, blocked or unknown handling;
9. no automatic conversion of Attention Items into formal Tasks;
10. no claim that Today is the official deadline source.

A visually polished feed that cannot demonstrate these distinctions is not conforming.

## Chapter Conclusion

Today is the daily business cockpit of MarkOrbit Lite because it assembles the most relevant Product-local candidates, work products, decisions, returned results and unfinished context into one sourced, time-bound view.

It remains useful only while preserving the authority of every underlying record.

```text
Today organizes attention.
It does not replace formal work.
```
