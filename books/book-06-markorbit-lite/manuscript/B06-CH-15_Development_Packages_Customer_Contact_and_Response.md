# B06-CH-15 — Development Packages, Customer Contact and Response

**Status:** Complete Draft 1 — Owner Accepted on Wave 3 Merge  
**Chapter Map:** B06-TOC-V0.1 — Owner Accepted  
**Part:** Part III — Customer and Service Growth

## Chapter Purpose

This chapter explains how MarkOrbit Lite converts a qualified customer, reactivation or prospect candidate into a useful Development Package, prepares consequential contact safely and records the customer’s response without confusing activity with business value.

The central proposition is:

> Growth becomes operational when the professional can inspect a relevant explanation, a trustworthy work product and an exact outreach action before contact—and when the resulting response is preserved as typed evidence rather than flattened into “interested” or “not interested.”

## 1. A Candidate Is Not Yet Ready for Contact

A candidate may explain why a service could matter, but contact still requires preparation.

The professional needs to know:

- which person or organization is being contacted;
- why the contact is relevant now;
- which facts are observed and which are inferred;
- what service or verification question is proposed;
- which work product supports the discussion;
- what source and relationship restrictions apply;
- which channel is appropriate;
- what Review and confirmation are required;
- what should happen after different responses.

A candidate without this package leaves the professional to reconstruct the reasoning manually and increases the risk of generic, inaccurate or inappropriate outreach.

## 2. `ML-W06 Development Package`

A Development Package is a Product-local work package that joins the candidate with the material needed for a Human to decide and act.

It may contain:

- candidate identity and type;
- customer, historical relationship or prospect reference;
- source and freshness;
- trigger and service relevance;
- assumptions and missing information;
- contactability and channel limits;
- selected Artifact version;
- outreach draft;
- verification questions;
- alternatives and safe stops;
- expected consequence;
- follow-up options;
- qualification criteria;
- destination candidates if a need is confirmed.

It does not prove purchase intent.

## 3. One Package Must Serve One Purpose

The same candidate may support different purposes:

- request factual verification;
- explain a maintenance issue;
- present an optional portfolio review;
- ask whether a new market is planned;
- provide a case-supported educational note;
- invite a short consultation;
- respond to an inbound question.

Each purpose may require a different:

- audience;
- tone;
- work-product version;
- disclosure scope;
- channel;
- Review level;
- next step.

```text
one candidate
≠ one universal message
```

A Development Package must state its purpose so that the user can judge whether the content and channel fit.

## 4. The Work Product Carries Professional Value

Outreach should not be only a request for attention.

The package may include:

- a concise portfolio gap summary;
- a status or maintenance explanation;
- a country or class comparison;
- a short case example with approved scope;
- a visual or written educational Artifact;
- a quotation or service-scope candidate;
- a checklist of information needed;
- a short action plan.

The Artifact remains governed by its own source, version, Review and rights requirements.

```text
AI-generated text
≠ approved customer work product
```

## 5. Outreach Drafting Must Preserve Truth and Uncertainty

A useful draft should distinguish:

- facts known from authorized sources;
- public observations;
- professional interpretation;
- uncertainty;
- questions for the customer;
- service options;
- claims requiring Review.

The draft should not:

- manufacture urgency;
- imply a prior relationship that does not exist;
- claim that a service is mandatory without basis;
- disclose restricted customer or Matter information;
- promise results;
- pretend that a public signal proves business intent;
- conceal that the message was prepared with AI Assistance.

## 6. Recipient and Channel Require Final Inspection

Before external contact, the user should inspect:

- exact recipient;
- role and organization;
- source of the contact detail;
- freshness;
- relationship owner;
- channel;
- purpose;
- opt-out or suppression state;
- selected message and Artifact version;
- attachments;
- expected consequence.

A general email address, named contact, social account and messaging-app number are not interchangeable.

```text
contact detail available
≠ chosen recipient

chosen recipient
≠ permitted channel
```

## 7. Human Review and User Confirmation Are Different

A professional claim, legal explanation, sensitive quotation or externally reusable case may require Human Review.

The user must also confirm the exact external action.

```text
Human Review
→ is the content professionally acceptable?

User confirmation
→ should this exact version be sent to this exact recipient through this channel now?
```

One does not replace the other.

## 8. Communication Handoff

Where the action proceeds through Communication Service, `ML-H06 Communication Handoff` and `ML-HC-04` should include:

- exact draft version;
- recipient or recipient set;
- channel;
- purpose;
- attachments;
- approval and confirmation references;
- send consequence;
- return address for the result.

The destination revalidates permissions and returns:

- accepted;
- blocked;
- failed;
- unknown send outcome;
- other typed result.

```text
Handoff accepted
≠ message delivered

message delivered
≠ message read

message read
≠ customer need
```

## 9. Confirmed Manual Contact Is Still a Governed Path

Not every contact must be executed by a connector.

The Product may support a confirmed manual path:

```text
Development Package ready
→ user previews exact action
→ user confirms manual operation
→ user performs call, email or platform action
→ user reports result
```

A user-reported outcome must remain distinguishable from platform-confirmed evidence.

The Product should not claim technical confirmation it does not possess.

## 10. Typed Response States

Customer or prospect responses may include:

- confirmed interest;
- asks for more information;
- confirms underlying facts but not a service need;
- requests quotation;
- requests a meeting;
- asks to proceed;
- corrects the company, mark or contact;
- states another provider is responsible;
- declines the service;
- opts out;
- invalid contact;
- no response within the observation window;
- delivery failed;
- unknown external outcome.

These states should not be collapsed into one conversion field.

## 11. Response Evidence Has Different Strengths

Possible evidence includes:

- provider-confirmed delivery;
- Communication Service status;
- email reply;
- meeting record;
- user-entered call note;
- customer-uploaded information;
- formal instruction;
- payment or Order reference.

The Product should preserve evidence source and strength.

```text
user reported “customer interested”
≠ verified instruction
```

This does not make user-entered notes useless. It keeps their authority clear.

## 12. Follow-Up Must Be State-Aware

A follow-up may be appropriate when:

- more information was requested;
- a quotation was requested;
- timing was deferred;
- the customer asked for a later reminder;
- the destination needs missing information;
- a meeting requires preparation.

Follow-up should stop or change when:

- opt-out applies;
- contact is invalid;
- the customer declined;
- the relationship owner changed;
- the service is no longer relevant;
- the previous external outcome is unknown and duplicate contact could occur.

```text
no response
≠ unlimited follow-up permission
```

## 13. Response Creates Qualification Input

The response becomes input to `ML-O08 Qualification Result`.

Qualification should consider:

- identity;
- authority of the respondent;
- confirmed business facts;
- service relevance;
- urgency and timing;
- requested scope;
- budget or commercial constraints where appropriate;
- missing information;
- professional and conflict considerations;
- destination suitability.

The response itself does not automatically create a formal Opportunity.

## 14. Corrections Improve the Product

Customer contact may reveal that:

- the trademark is owned by another entity;
- the public status was misread;
- the contact has changed;
- the service was already completed;
- the customer has entered or left a market;
- the proposed service is irrelevant;
- the work product used an outdated assumption.

These should produce scoped feedback or correction records and may retire affected candidate or Artifact versions.

They must not silently rewrite formal source records.

## 15. Failure and Unknown Outcomes

The Product must preserve:

- blocked by permission;
- blocked by policy;
- Review required;
- send failed;
- delivery failed;
- recipient invalid;
- unknown external outcome;
- response window expired;
- suppressed;
- retired.

An unknown outcome is especially important.

If a connector times out after a consequential send request, Lite must not automatically retry without determining whether a duplicate message may already have been sent.

## 16. Measures That Matter

Useful measures include:

- Development Packages inspected;
- meaningful edits;
- work-product usage;
- confirmed customer actions;
- response-state distribution;
- correction rate;
- opt-out and invalid-contact rate;
- qualified need rate;
- formal Handoff acceptance;
- time from candidate to qualified need;
- customer value and service outcome.

Insufficient measures include:

- number of drafts generated;
- number of sends;
- open rate alone;
- “interested” labels without evidence;
- repeated follow-up count.

## Chapter Conclusion

The Development Package is where Lite turns a candidate into an understandable, reviewable and actionable customer-development unit.

It combines source, relevance, work product, outreach language, contact constraints and qualification criteria. Human Review protects professional content; user confirmation protects the exact external action; typed response states preserve what actually happened.

This is how customer development becomes a governed Product journey rather than a message-generation feature.
