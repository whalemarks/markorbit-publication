## Planning Locks

### Platform resource lock

MGSN provider supply, procurement, routing interfaces, funds controls, network data and fulfillment systems are platform-owned or platform-governed resources.

### Hub topology lock

Demand-side and provider-side participants connect through MGSN.

MGSN does not establish an independent participant-to-participant network graph.

### Private-space lock

Demand-side and provider-side participants retain independent private spaces.

Private space does not mean user ownership of MGSN supply.

### External-route lock

A user may retain an External Self-Managed Route without importing the provider into MGSN.

The route receives no implied MGSN procurement, funds, replacement or fulfillment guarantee.

Manual status and Document uploads preserve source and confidence.

### Matching lock

MGSN may automatically evaluate and preselect the Recommended Best Route.

Automatic matching does not equal provider appointment.

The user retains final confirmation unless a later accepted managed-service mandate expressly grants standing allocation authority.

### Preferred-provider lock

A user-preferred provider remains subject to platform admission, qualification, conflict, availability, procurement, service and risk controls.

### Formal-state lock

MGSN must not silently duplicate:

- Customer;
- Opportunity;
- Order;
- Matter;
- Task;
- Workflow;
- Review;
- Approval;
- Communication send state;
- Payment authority;
- Execution;
- official outcome.

## Change Governance

A future Charter Candidate must request review before changing any planning lock.

A Book 02 or architecture Change Proposal may be required if Book 07 later proposes to:

- own all user customers;
- mutate formal business facts outside an Owning Service;
- erase provider professional responsibility;
- create peer-to-peer participant authority;
- equate recommendation with appointment;
- treat projection as official truth;
- authorize autonomous protected action.
