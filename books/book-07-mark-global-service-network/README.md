## Working Product Interpretation

Mark Global Service Network, or MGSN, is the platform-owned and platform-governed global professional service network through which demand-side users and service providers connect to the platform.

MGSN gives users capabilities they do not independently possess.

Its working value proposition is:

> MGSN gives professional users governed access to global service capability, purchasing leverage, controlled funds, reliable fulfillment and continuously improving provider coverage through one stable platform interface.

## Network Topology Candidate

```text
Demand-side Workplace / Lite
            ↓
          MGSN
            ↑
Provider Workplace / Provider Interface
```

All demand-side and provider-side participants connect to MGSN.

MGSN does not support an independent peer-to-peer network among participants.

A permitted direct conversation may occur inside an MGSN-governed collaboration, but it does not create an unrestricted lateral network relationship or bypass right.

## Meaning of Private Space

`Private space` means the participant's own:

```text
Organization Workplace
or
MarkOrbit Lite personal / small-team space
```

The user's private space may contain:

- customers and customer contacts;
- leads, opportunities and internal relationships;
- internal prices and sales strategy;
- documents, knowledge and memory;
- preferences and approvals;
- service history;
- MGSN requests, results and organization-specific notes.

Private space does not mean a user-owned provider network, unrestricted provider contacts, provider export or ownership of MGSN supply, procurement terms, Trust signals or routing infrastructure.

## Critical Balance

MGSN is neither an unrestricted public marketplace nor a passive directory.

The platform must retain control over:

- network entry and provider admission;
- provider supply and network resources;
- capability classification and verification;
- procurement strategy and negotiated supply terms;
- routing interfaces and disclosure stages;
- funds custody, release and settlement controls;
- fulfillment monitoring, exceptions and suspension;
- network data, aggregate evidence and platform Trust signals;
- service continuity and system-wide risk controls.

Users retain bounded authority inside their private space and over defined business decisions, including:

- customer relationship and customer communication, unless a specific managed-service model says otherwise;
- expression of service need and constraints;
- authorization of customer-context projection into MGSN;
- review of permitted options or platform recommendations;
- required approvals and instructions;
- acceptance of customer-facing commercial terms;
- review of outcomes and correction requests.

Bounded user authority does not mean ownership of MGSN resources, unrestricted provider access, direct extraction of platform supply, peer-to-peer networking or bypass of platform procurement, funds and fulfillment controls.

## Non-Goals

Book 07 must not reduce MGSN to:

```text
provider directory
open bidding hall
free public marketplace
lowest-price procurement board
user-owned partner database
peer-to-peer provider network
uncontrolled direct-contact network
automatic provider appointment engine
unregulated escrow or payment wallet
single universal provider score
```
