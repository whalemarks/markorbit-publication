# B07-CH-00 — Preface: Global Capability Without Building Every Foreign Relationship Yourself

International trademark work appears simple from a distance: identify a country, find a local agent, send instructions, pay fees and wait for a result. In practice, every jurisdiction introduces its own professional rules, documentary requirements, fee structures, response times, language conventions and operational risks. A professional who serves clients globally cannot realistically build, maintain and supervise every foreign relationship alone.

The traditional solution is a private spreadsheet of agents, email threads, informal recommendations and personal memory. That approach can work at small scale, but it is fragile. Prices become stale. Contacts change firms. Qualifications are rarely rechecked. Instructions are inconsistent. Evidence is scattered. Payment and delivery are separated. When a provider delays or disappears, the demand-side professional must reconstruct the matter manually.

MGSN begins from a different premise:

> Global capability should be available through a governed network, not rebuilt case by case by every participant.

MGSN is MarkOrbit's platform-owned managed network for global professional supply, procurement, routing, funds control, fulfillment and Trust. It allows an Originating Workplace to request capability it does not possess while preserving its own customer relationship, Matter context, pricing, Knowledge and accountable business responsibility.

The network does not replace the professional or convert foreign legal work into an anonymous commodity. It creates a controlled operating structure in which capability can be qualified, compared, recommended, accepted, funded, fulfilled, corrected and returned.

The practical promise is:

```text
local professional responsibility
+ platform-managed global supply
+ governed commercial and fulfillment controls
= expandable global service capability
```

A participant does not need to claim that it owns offices or relationships everywhere. It needs a reliable way to access appropriate capability, understand the selected route, protect the commercial and evidentiary chain, and return validated results to the Workplace where the customer and Matter are managed.

This book follows that chain from beginning to end. It explains:

- the network constitution and participant roles;
- supply formation and provider admission;
- procurement, cost layers and service offers;
- Capability Need, routing and bounded user choice;
- funds controls, fulfillment and typed Return;
- Trust, relationship provenance and governance;
- an operator-assisted MVP and the global capability flywheel.

MGSN is a managed network rather than a free directory, open bidding hall or user-owned provider graph. It also remains distinct from Core, Execution, Workplace, MarkReg, Lite, Finance and official authority. Those boundaries are stated once in the Product Constitution and then applied throughout the book.

The long-term outcome is not that every participant becomes a multinational law firm. It is that each participant can operate from its own Workplace, retain its business sovereignty and gain progressively stronger global capability through a network whose supply, evidence, routing and performance improve over time.
