# B07-CH-07 — Provider Network Profiles and Capability Claims

## A Provider Is Not Merely a Contact

A professional service network cannot be built from address books alone. A name, email address or law-firm website may help someone make contact, but it does not explain what the provider can lawfully, reliably and currently deliver through MGSN.

MGSN therefore does not treat every known foreign associate as an immediately usable provider. It separates the stable Organization from the network-specific representation of that Organization.

```text
Organization
≠ MGSN Provider Network Profile
≠ Capability Claim
≠ verified Capability
≠ current availability
≠ active Network Service Engagement
```

The Organization remains the stable participant identity. The Provider Network Profile is the platform-governed representation of that Organization for supply-side participation.

## Provider Network Profile

The Provider Network Profile records the minimum network context needed to evaluate and operate supply. It may include:

- Organization identity and legal form;
- relevant office locations;
- professional and regulatory status;
- approved contacts and operating roles;
- supported languages;
- permitted communication and disclosure scope;
- conflict-check process;
- funds and settlement prerequisites;
- current participation status;
- restrictions, suspensions or review flags.

The profile is not a public marketing page and is not a substitute for the provider’s own Workplace records. MGSN governs the network representation; the provider retains its private internal operations, staff information, methods and professional responsibility.

## Capability Is Granular

A provider should not be described only as “a US agent” or “an EU lawyer.” Such descriptions are too broad for reliable routing.

A Capability Claim should be specific enough to answer:

```text
Who can perform
what service
in which jurisdiction
for which matter type
under which qualifications
with which scope and limitations?
```

Typical dimensions include:

- jurisdiction;
- filing office or authority;
- service family;
- procedural stage;
- applicant or owner type;
- goods and services complexity;
- language capability;
- urgency;
- signing or local-representation requirements;
- exclusions and referral dependencies.

A provider may be qualified for trademark filing but not opposition litigation. It may handle ordinary office-action responses but not hearings. It may serve corporate applicants but not certain regulated sectors. Capability must therefore be represented as a set of bounded claims rather than one universal provider label.

## Claim Is Not Proof

A Capability Claim is an assertion submitted by or for the provider. It may originate from:

- provider self-declaration;
- platform recruitment;
- historical successful work;
- demand-side introduction;
- professional directory or official register;
- operator research.

But the source of a claim does not automatically establish its truth or current usability.

```text
Capability Claim
≠ Capability Evidence
≠ Verification
≠ Qualification
```

The separation matters because networks degrade when unverified statements are promoted into routing facts.

## Contextual Supply Role

An Organization does not become permanently classified as only a provider. It assumes a Supply role when it offers an admitted Capability through MGSN.

The same Organization may simultaneously request services in another jurisdiction. Its Provider Network Profile therefore coexists with its demand-side Workplace context, while permissions, data access, conflict review and financial records remain separated.

## Profile Visibility

MGSN may disclose different profile layers at different stages.

Before route confirmation, the user may see only the information necessary to compare the service route: qualification status, experience, service fit, expected timing, price and whether the provider is an existing relationship.

After confirmation, more identity information may be disclosed where needed for legal responsibility, conflict review or professional selection. Direct contact details remain governed by the managed-service route and are not automatically exportable.

## Correction and Accountability

Provider Profiles and Capability Claims must be correctable. Providers should be able to challenge outdated or inaccurate information, but corrections must preserve evidence and audit history.

A corrected profile does not erase prior network decisions. It creates a governed new version and explains why the previous representation changed.

## Product Rule

MGSN does not route work to a generic contact. It routes through an admitted provider representation and a bounded Capability context.

```text
Stable Organization
+ governed Provider Network Profile
+ specific Capability Claim
+ accepted Evidence and review
= supply candidate, not automatic appointment
```
