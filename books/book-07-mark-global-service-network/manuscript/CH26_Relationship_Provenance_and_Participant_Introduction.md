# B07-CH-26 — Relationship Provenance and Participant Introduction

## 1. Why relationship provenance matters

Global professional networks often grow through prior cooperation, referrals and introductions. Those relationships matter operationally, but they must not become invisible claims of ownership.

MGSN therefore records **Relationship Provenance**: who introduced whom, through which context, under what authority and for what bounded purpose.

```text
Introduction
→ provenance record
→ admission and qualification
→ bounded attribution
→ possible preference or incentive
```

## 2. What provenance records

A Relationship Provenance record may include:

- introducing Organization or Workplace;
- introduced participant;
- direction of introduction;
- date and source;
- prior cooperation context;
- service and jurisdiction context;
- consent and disclosure status;
- attribution period;
- private preference flags;
- incentive eligibility;
- restrictions or conflicts;
- superseding or expiry conditions.

It should not expose unrelated customers, private correspondence or full commercial history.

## 3. Bidirectional introduction

MGSN supports both directions:

```text
Demand participant introduces provider
Provider introduces demand participant
```

In either direction, introduction does not bypass:

- identity checks;
- participation terms;
- capability evidence;
- qualification;
- conflict review;
- service-package admission;
- route-specific controls.

```text
Introduced
≠ Verified
≠ Qualified
≠ Activated
```

## 4. Attribution period

Attribution should be bounded by time, scope and policy.

A valid attribution period may support:

- referral recognition;
- route explanation;
- relationship-preserved preference;
- bounded incentive;
- anti-poaching review;
- historical continuity.

It must not create:

- permanent commissions;
- permanent customer ownership;
- permanent provider ownership;
- indefinite exclusivity;
- control over unrelated future work.

## 5. Private preference

An Originating Workplace may record a private preference for a provider based on prior cooperation.

That preference can influence R3 routing only after the provider passes current hard gates.

```text
Private Preference
≠ current Qualification
≠ current Availability
≠ automatic allocation
```

The preference should remain private unless disclosure is required for routing, conflict or related-party review.

## 6. Anti-poaching without permanent ownership

MGSN may protect legitimate originating relationships through:

- provenance visibility;
- restricted direct-contact disclosure;
- bounded non-circumvention terms;
- operator review of suspicious rerouting;
- incentive rules;
- sanctions for undisclosed rebrokering or diversion.

But the platform must not convert protection into perpetual ownership of independent parties.

The appropriate rule is:

> No undisclosed or ungoverned rebrokering, diversion or circumvention.

It is not:

> A participant permanently owns every introduced counterparty.

## 7. Consent and transparency

Introduction should be consent-aware.

The introduced party should understand:

- who introduced them;
- what data is being shared;
- what MGSN participation means;
- whether attribution or incentive applies;
- whether contact details remain restricted;
- how to correct or contest provenance.

Hidden attribution undermines Trust and can create unfair routing effects.

## 8. Conflicting provenance claims

Two parties may claim the same relationship. The platform should preserve competing evidence rather than overwriting one claim.

Review may consider:

- earliest reliable evidence;
- scope of prior cooperation;
- consent;
- current relationship;
- service and jurisdiction specificity;
- applicable attribution policy;
- whether claims concern different contexts.

The outcome may be accepted, rejected, shared, narrowed, expired or disputed.

## 9. Portability and exit

When a Workplace exits MGSN, private relationship records should remain portable to the extent permitted by law and policy.

Network-level attribution, incentives, incidents and governance records may remain with the platform where necessary for audit, settlement, Trust and dispute handling.

```text
Workplace portability
≠ deletion of all network provenance
```

## 10. Product rule

Relationship Provenance preserves continuity and bounded recognition without turning introductions into permanent ownership, unrestricted data access or automatic routing authority.
