# B07-CH-24 — Incidents, Corrections, Escalation and Replacement

## 1. Failure must be governable

A managed network cannot treat every problem as an informal message between two participants.

MGSN needs a typed exception path for:

- delay;
- missing evidence;
- wrong scope;
- incorrect filing or work product;
- unavailability after acceptance;
- conflict discovered late;
- communication failure;
- unauthorized substitution;
- funds disagreement;
- confidentiality or conduct concern;
- provider failure or withdrawal.

## 2. Network Incident

A Network Incident records a material deviation from the accepted engagement or expected network behavior.

It should identify:

- engagement and milestone;
- incident type;
- reporter and source;
- time discovered;
- affected scope;
- deadline impact;
- customer or rights impact;
- available evidence;
- immediate containment action;
- responsible review owner;
- current severity and status.

An incident is not automatically proof of provider fault. It is a governed request for assessment.

## 3. Severity and urgency

Severity should consider:

- risk of missed legal deadline;
- irreversibility;
- customer harm;
- official-fee loss;
- confidentiality impact;
- repeat behavior;
- availability of correction;
- availability of replacement;
- uncertainty in the evidence.

Urgency and fault are separate. A high-urgency incident may still require investigation before responsibility is assigned.

## 4. Correction Request

A Correction Request defines what must be remedied, by whom and by when.

It should include:

- identified defect or gap;
- required correction outcome;
- supporting evidence;
- deadline;
- review and acceptance method;
- effect on milestones and funds;
- escalation consequence if not corrected.

Correction must be proportionate. Minor formatting issues should not be treated the same as a missed filing deadline.

## 5. Escalation

Escalation may move through several levels:

```text
participant clarification
→ operator intervention
→ specialist or legal review
→ commercial reconciliation
→ governance review
→ dispute or appeal
```

The escalation route should reflect severity, jurisdiction and contractual responsibility.

The Originating Workplace should receive enough information to manage its customer relationship without being forced into direct unmanaged negotiation with the provider.

## 6. Replacement Decision

Replacement is appropriate when continued execution by the current provider is unsafe, impossible or commercially unreasonable.

A Replacement Decision should consider:

- deadline and remaining time;
- transferability of work and documents;
- conflict and confidentiality;
- available qualified alternatives;
- additional official or professional fees;
- responsibility for extra cost;
- customer approval requirements;
- effect on evidence and continuity;
- whether the prior provider remains involved in correction or handover.

```text
Replacement
≠ silent substitution
```

If the provider identity, scope, cost or risk materially changes, the Originating Workplace must receive a new controlled decision point unless emergency policy lawfully permits otherwise.

## 7. Continuity package

A replacement handoff should preserve:

- accepted scope and latest instructions;
- all relevant documents;
- deadlines;
- completed work;
- unresolved defects;
- payment and cost assumptions;
- evidence already received;
- communications needed for continuity;
- restrictions on access and onward use.

The new provider should not need the Originating Workplace to reconstruct the entire matter from memory.

## 8. Cost responsibility

Additional cost can arise from:

- provider error;
- platform routing or coordination error;
- customer or Workplace scope change;
- authority action;
- unavoidable external event;
- emergency replacement.

The platform must not decide responsibility solely from who paid first or who has more bargaining power.

The outcome may include:

- provider-funded correction;
- platform service credit;
- additional customer approval;
- shared cost;
- refund;
- reserve use;
- dispute.

The authoritative financial result belongs to the relevant Finance service.

## 9. Restrictions and suspension

Serious or repeated incidents may trigger:

- route-level restriction;
- capability restriction;
- temporary suspension;
- admission review;
- portfolio removal;
- network retirement.

These governance effects require evidence, proportionality and appeal rights. A single disputed incident should not automatically erase all unrelated capability.

## 10. Product boundary

This chapter defines incident, correction, escalation and replacement semantics. It does not authorize automatic sanctions, automatic provider removal, legal liability findings, payment movement or external remedial action.
