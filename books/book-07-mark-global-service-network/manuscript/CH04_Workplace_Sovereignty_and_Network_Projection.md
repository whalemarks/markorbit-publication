# B07-CH-04 — Workplace Sovereignty and Network Projection

MGSN can only work correctly when the platform network and participant business spaces are clearly separated.

## Workplace as business-sovereignty boundary

A Workplace is the Organization-scoped boundary for customer relationships, business responsibility, permissions, Product operation, private Knowledge and authorized projections. The Originating Workplace controls why a service is needed, which customer and Matter it serves, what may be disclosed, what price is offered and who may approve the route.

This sovereignty does not permit arbitrary mutation of formal records. Core defines shared semantics, and an Owning Service remains responsible for formal-state change.

## Five authority dimensions

Every cross-network decision should identify:

```text
business sovereignty
semantic authority
formal-state authority
physical custody
legal / source authority
```

For example, the platform may physically store a provider certificate, MGSN may govern its verification projection, the provider may hold the underlying legal qualification, and Core may define the meaning of Evidence. These are different authorities.

## Product Installation and connection

A Workplace connects to MGSN through a Workplace-scoped Product Installation or Gateway. The connection determines:

- enabled demand and supply roles;
- permission scope;
- data-access scope;
- projection scope;
- accepted terms;
- audit context;
- current connection status.

```text
MGSN Connection / Gateway ≠ MGSN Network
```

The Workplace owns or controls its connection configuration and private preferences. The platform owns or governs the shared provider network, procurement, routing, funds controls, fulfillment and platform Trust.

## Projection, Handoff and Return

A Projection is a purpose-limited view of source information. A Handoff is an authorized transmission for another responsibility. A Return is a typed response carrying source-qualified result or evidence back to the originating context.

```text
Projection ≠ source record transfer
Handoff sent ≠ destination accepted
Return received ≠ formal-state mutation
```

The Originating Workplace should disclose only the minimum necessary information for qualification, conflict, routing and fulfillment. Provider-side internal methods, unrelated customers and private costs should not flow back merely because the provider performed the work.

## Portable sovereignty

A participant may retain and export its own customers, internal records, approved documents, private preferences and relationship history subject to law and contract. Portability does not include platform provider pools, confidential procurement terms, routing weights, cross-participant Trust evidence or network-operating intelligence.

The balance is deliberate:

> Participants control their business; the platform controls the shared managed network; formal truth remains with the appropriate Owning Service.
