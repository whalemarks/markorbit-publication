# B07-CH-08 — Evidence, Verification, Qualification and Availability

## Four Different Questions

A reliable network must answer four different questions before a provider can be considered usable:

```text
What does the provider claim?
What evidence supports the claim?
Has the platform verified the evidence?
Is the provider qualified and available for this need now?
```

These questions must not be collapsed into one status.

## Evidence

Capability Evidence supports a Capability Claim. Depending on the service, it may include:

- professional licences;
- official registry records;
- firm registrations;
- powers or appointments recognized by the relevant authority;
- prior filing or prosecution evidence;
- sample reports or work products;
- references;
- insurance information;
- sanctions, tax or banking documents;
- historical MGSN fulfillment evidence.

Evidence has a source, date, scope and freshness condition. A licence issued years ago may still be valid, expired, suspended or irrelevant to the claimed service. A successful prior matter may demonstrate experience but not present authority.

## Verification

Verification is the platform’s governed assessment of evidence. It should record:

- what was checked;
- against which source;
- by whom or by which controlled process;
- on what date;
- with what result;
- what uncertainty remains;
- when re-verification is required.

Verification does not mean that the platform guarantees every future professional decision. It means the platform has performed the specified check and can explain its result.

```text
Evidence received
≠ evidence verified

Evidence verified
≠ provider appointed
```

## Qualification

Qualification answers whether a provider is eligible to provide a particular service through MGSN under current platform rules.

Qualification can depend on more than professional credentials. It may also require:

- accepted network terms;
- approved settlement arrangements;
- conflict and confidentiality procedures;
- service-package compatibility;
- response-time commitments;
- data-protection controls;
- sanctions and jurisdiction checks;
- acceptable performance history;
- no active restriction or suspension.

Qualification is therefore service-specific and may be conditional.

A provider may be:

```text
qualified for ordinary filing
conditionally qualified for urgent filing
not qualified for contentious proceedings
suspended for a specific service
```

## Availability

Qualification says the provider may perform the service. Availability says whether the provider can take this work now.

Availability should account for:

- workload and capacity;
- jurisdiction deadlines;
- holidays and office closures;
- conflicts;
- staffing;
- urgency;
- language or document requirements;
- temporary operational incidents.

Availability must be time-scoped. A declaration made last month should not be treated as a permanent routing fact.

## Freshness and Expiry

Every important evidence and status item should have a freshness rule.

```text
verified once
≠ valid forever
```

MGSN may require periodic renewal, event-driven review or re-verification before a high-risk engagement. Expired evidence should not silently remain active.

Possible statuses include:

- current;
- expiring;
- expired;
- under review;
- disputed;
- replaced;
- not required for this Capability.

## Negative and Unknown Results

The system must support safe negative and unknown outcomes.

```text
not verified
≠ false

unknown
≠ approved
```

Where evidence is incomplete, MGSN should request correction, narrow the Capability, require operator review or exclude the provider from the current candidate set. It should not fill the gap with assumptions.

## Correction and Appeal

Providers must be able to submit new evidence and challenge inaccurate findings. Corrections should preserve the earlier result, the new evidence and the reason for change.

For material restrictions or suspensions, the provider should have a governed review or appeal path, subject to urgent protective action where risk requires immediate restriction.

## Product Rule

The supply chain is cumulative:

```text
Capability Claim
→ Evidence
→ Verification
→ Qualification
→ Current Availability
→ eligible supply candidate
```

Failure at one stage does not necessarily condemn the Organization permanently. It determines whether a specific Capability can be used under current conditions.
