# B07-CH-09 — Multi-Provider Supply Portfolios

## One Provider Is Not a Network

A service network becomes fragile when one country, service or procedural stage depends on one provider. Even a trusted long-term associate may become unavailable because of conflict, workload, illness, sanctions, pricing changes, staffing problems or professional restrictions.

MGSN therefore manages supply as a portfolio rather than as a single preferred contact.

```text
Jurisdiction + Service + Matter Context
→ multiple qualified supply routes
```

The purpose is not to create an auction. It is to build resilience, specialization and commercially useful alternatives while retaining platform control.

## Supply Portfolio Entry

A Supply Portfolio Entry connects an admitted provider Capability to a defined service context. It may record:

- jurisdiction and office;
- service type and procedural stage;
- eligible applicant or matter types;
- qualification and availability requirements;
- service-package compatibility;
- procurement terms;
- capacity expectations;
- risk and restriction conditions;
- internal portfolio role;
- effective and review dates.

The entry is network-governed. It does not transfer ownership of the provider’s Organization or internal operations to the platform.

## Internal Portfolio Roles

A provider may hold different internal roles for different services:

```text
Recommended Primary
Qualified Alternative
Specialist
Urgent-Capacity Route
Relationship-Preserved Route
Backup Route
Pilot / Testing Route
Restricted / Suspended Route
```

These roles are contextual and changeable. They are not permanent rankings and need not all be shown to users.

A provider may be the primary route for ordinary filings, a specialist for complex classification work and unavailable for litigation. Another may be more expensive but better for urgent deadlines. A former primary provider may move to backup while performance is reviewed.

## Why Multiple Providers Matter

A portfolio supports:

- continuity when the primary provider cannot act;
- conflict substitution;
- urgent capacity;
- specialist matching;
- negotiation leverage;
- service comparison;
- regional or language coverage;
- controlled testing of new supply;
- replacement after performance failure.

It also reduces the risk that historical relationships become permanent monopolies.

## Relationship-Preserved Routes

When a participant introduces an existing provider, MGSN may preserve that relationship as a preferred candidate if the provider is admitted and qualified.

The system may identify the route as the participant’s existing provider and consider relationship continuity in recommendation. But the relationship does not override:

- qualification;
- conflict;
- availability;
- service fit;
- unacceptable price or risk;
- restriction or suspension;
- customer interest.

```text
relationship preference
≠ routing monopoly
```

## Portfolio Governance

Portfolio changes should be governed by evidence rather than informal impressions. Inputs may include:

- verification status;
- response reliability;
- fulfillment evidence;
- correction frequency;
- deadline performance;
- dispute history;
- capacity;
- procurement stability;
- service-specific Trust.

A change in portfolio role should be explainable internally and auditable. Sensitive procurement information and complete ranking logic remain platform-controlled.

## No Public Leaderboard

MGSN does not require a universal public provider ranking. A single score would hide important differences between services, jurisdictions and risk levels.

The user may see enough information to understand a Recommended Route and meaningful alternatives, but the full supply portfolio, internal classifications and procurement economics do not become a public directory.

## Safe Portfolio Gaps

A portfolio may have no qualified provider for a need. That is a valid and safer result than silently using an unverified contact.

```text
no eligible supply
→ operator review, narrowed service, external route or safe decline
```

Network coverage must be represented honestly.

## Product Rule

MGSN supply is built from qualified, service-specific portfolio entries rather than one permanent provider per country.

```text
multiple admitted routes
+ contextual portfolio roles
+ evidence-based governance
= resilient managed supply
```
