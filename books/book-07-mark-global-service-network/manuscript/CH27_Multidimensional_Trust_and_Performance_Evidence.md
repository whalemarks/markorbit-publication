# B07-CH-27 — Multidimensional Trust and Performance Evidence

## 1. Trust is not a universal star rating

A provider can perform well in one jurisdiction and poorly in another, excel at filing but not contentious work, or deliver quickly while producing weak evidence.

MGSN therefore treats Trust as multidimensional, contextual and source-aware.

```text
Performance Evidence
→ contextual assessment
→ Trust dimensions
→ route and governance inputs
```

## 2. Performance Evidence

Performance Evidence may arise from:

- verified milestone timing;
- completeness and quality of returned evidence;
- correction frequency;
- deadline compliance;
- communication responsiveness;
- commercial accuracy;
- dispute outcomes;
- replacement events;
- official-source confirmation;
- Originating Workplace review;
- operator review.

Raw observations should not be converted automatically into Trust judgments without context.

## 3. Context dimensions

A Trust assessment should be scoped by factors such as:

- jurisdiction;
- service type;
- procedural stage;
- complexity;
- urgency;
- language;
- provider team or office;
- evidence source;
- time period;
- sample size.

A provider's record in routine renewals should not automatically establish suitability for complex opposition work.

## 4. Trust dimensions

Possible dimensions include:

- qualification confidence;
- professional fit;
- deadline reliability;
- evidence quality;
- communication quality;
- commercial accuracy;
- correction responsiveness;
- fulfillment continuity;
- conflict transparency;
- governance compliance.

No single composite score should erase the underlying dimensions.

## 5. Source and confidence

Every Trust assessment should identify:

- supporting evidence;
- assessment method;
- source quality;
- time window;
- confidence;
- reviewer or automated contribution;
- correction and appeal status.

```text
Trust assertion without provenance
= weak network evidence
```

## 6. Private versus network-visible Trust

Some observations belong only to the Originating Workplace, while others support platform network governance.

Private preferences and subjective relationship notes should not automatically become network-wide penalties or endorsements.

Network-visible Trust should rely on governed evidence and purpose-limited disclosure.

## 7. Recency and decay

Trust is time-sensitive.

Old performance may remain relevant but should not outweigh current qualification, availability or recent evidence indefinitely.

The model should support:

- freshness windows;
- recency weighting;
- re-verification;
- expiry;
- rehabilitation after correction;
- separation of historical and current status.

## 8. Corrections and appeals

Providers must be able to:

- view material adverse assessments where policy permits;
- identify supporting evidence;
- submit correction evidence;
- explain external causes;
- contest inaccurate attribution;
- request review.

Trust without correction rights becomes opaque punishment rather than governance.

## 9. Trust in routing

Trust is an input after hard eligibility gates.

```text
Qualification / conflict / availability gates
→ eligible candidates
→ Trust and performance comparison
→ recommendation
```

A high Trust assessment cannot override a current conflict, missing qualification or lack of capacity.

Likewise, a lower Trust dimension does not always exclude a provider if the issue is irrelevant to the present service.

## 10. No public leaderboard by default

MGSN is not designed as an open public review marketplace.

Publishing a universal score can:

- expose confidential engagements;
- reward volume over quality;
- enable gaming;
- ignore service complexity;
- create unfair reputational harm;
- distort routing.

Trust should primarily support controlled network decisions and explainable governance.

## 11. Product rule

MGSN Trust must be service-specific, jurisdiction-specific, source-aware, time-scoped, correctable and subordinate to current eligibility and professional responsibility.
