# B07-CH-10 — Service Package Admission and Standardization

## Capability Must Become an Operable Offer

Knowing that a provider can perform a service is not enough. MGSN also needs a controlled description of what will be delivered, under which assumptions, at what stage and with which evidence.

A Service Package Admission converts an admitted Capability into a routable and fulfillable network offer.

```text
admitted Capability
+ defined scope
+ controlled assumptions
+ deliverables and evidence
+ procurement terms
= admitted Service Package
```

## Why Standardization Matters

International trademark work is often described with deceptively simple labels such as “filing,” “renewal” or “office-action response.” In practice, the scope may vary widely.

A filing package may or may not include:

- classification review;
- specification drafting;
- preliminary search;
- local-agent appointment;
- translation;
- official fees;
- priority claim;
- power-of-attorney review;
- filing report;
- publication monitoring;
- registration-stage fees;
- certificate delivery.

Without controlled package definitions, two prices may appear comparable while covering different work.

## Minimum Package Definition

An admitted package should state:

- jurisdiction and authority;
- service and procedural stage;
- included professional actions;
- excluded actions;
- required inputs;
- official fees and variable disbursements;
- expected deliverables;
- expected Evidence;
- timing assumptions;
- validity period;
- urgency handling;
- dependency and exception rules;
- correction and change-control path.

The package should also identify which items are fixed, estimated, conditional or externally determined.

## Versioning

Service packages change when official fees, law, practice, provider terms or platform operating rules change.

A package therefore needs version identity and effective dates.

```text
same service name
≠ same package version
```

A matter should retain the version accepted for that engagement unless a governed change is approved. Updating the current package must not silently rewrite prior commitments.

## Inclusions and Exclusions

Exclusions are not secondary fine print. They define the boundary of responsibility.

Examples include:

- substantive refusal responses;
- hearings or appeals;
- legalization or notarization;
- courier costs;
- excessive item or class fees;
- third-party searches;
- translations beyond stated limits;
- unexpected office requirements.

A package that hides likely exclusions is not standardized; it is merely simplified marketing.

## Provider-Specific and Platform-Managed Layers

The provider may define professional delivery conditions. MGSN may add platform-managed layers such as:

- standardized intake;
- document completeness review;
- managed communication;
- milestone monitoring;
- funds coordination;
- exception handling;
- replacement support;
- governed Return.

These layers must remain distinguishable so that users understand what the provider performs and what the platform manages.

## Package Admission Is Not Permanent

A package may be admitted, conditional, under review, suspended or retired.

Reasons for review may include:

- fee changes;
- repeated scope disputes;
- provider capacity problems;
- changed legal requirements;
- missing Evidence;
- poor fulfillment performance;
- sanctions or payment restrictions.

Package admission should therefore have review dates and event-driven triggers.

## Standardization Without False Uniformity

MGSN should standardize the contract and evidence structure without pretending every jurisdiction works identically.

The correct model is:

```text
common package grammar
+ jurisdiction-specific rules
+ provider-specific admitted terms
```

The platform can compare and route packages because they share controlled fields, not because the underlying legal services are identical.

## Product Rule

A Capability becomes commercially and operationally usable only after its service package is admitted.

```text
Capability says what can be done.
Service Package says what this route will actually deliver.
```
