# B07-CH-01 — How to Read This Book

Book 07 is a Product and network-governance publication. It explains what MGSN must mean and how its controlled responsibilities fit the wider MarkOrbit system. It is not a database design, API reference, payment licence, production runbook or provider contract.

## Authority chain

Read every chapter through this order:

```text
Active Architecture Canon vNext.1
→ Book 02 Frozen Core Specification
→ Book 03 Execution System
→ Book 04 Workplace and Product Architecture
→ Book 05 MarkReg
→ Book 06 MarkOrbit Lite
→ Book 07 Charter and Controlled Product Baseline
```

A later Product book may specialize an accepted concept, but it may not silently redefine Core semantics, Owning Service authority, Workplace sovereignty or Execution controls.

## Five authority dimensions

The word ownership is too imprecise unless the dimension is named. This book separates:

1. **Business sovereignty** — who controls the customer relationship and operating context;
2. **Semantic authority** — who defines the formal meaning of records and states;
3. **Formal-state authority** — which Owning Service may create or modify official business facts;
4. **Physical custody** — which infrastructure stores or processes data;
5. **Legal or source authority** — who holds the underlying legal, professional, evidentiary or official right.

These dimensions frequently belong to different actors. CH04 applies them to Workplace sovereignty and network projection.

## Controlled language

Several distinctions recur throughout the book:

```text
Organization ≠ Provider Network Profile
Capability Claim ≠ Evidence ≠ Verification
Recommendation ≠ Appointment
User confirmation ≠ Provider Acceptance
Projection ≠ Handoff ≠ Return
Return received ≠ formal-state mutation
Funds projection ≠ Finance ledger
MGSN Connection ≠ MGSN Network
```

Capitalized terms carry controlled meaning. Product-local records such as `MG-R06 Routing Recommendation` support MGSN responsibilities; they do not automatically become Core objects, database tables or implementation services.

## Product versus implementation

The publication may require a control without claiming that the control is already legally or technically implemented. For example, MGSN requires typed funds allocation and release conditions, but this does not establish escrow status, client-money authority or automatic release power.

Likewise, a routing recommendation may be computed, but ordinary MVP services still require user disposition, platform allocation and Provider Acceptance. CH32 consolidates the implementation prerequisites and legal gates.

## Reading path

The book can be read in four layers:

```text
Constitution and participation: CH02–CH06
Supply and commercial formation: CH07–CH15
Routing, fulfillment and governance: CH16–CH29
MVP, conformance and evolution: CH30–CH33
```

More specifically:

- CH02–CH06 establish the Product Constitution, hub topology, Workplace boundary, contextual roles and participation lifecycle.
- CH07–CH11 explain provider profiles, Capability, Evidence, qualification, supply portfolios, Service Packages and admission.
- CH12–CH15 cover aggregated demand, procurement, cost layers, offers and routing integrity.
- CH16–CH20 convert customer need into a governed route and preserve bounded human choice.
- CH21–CH25 cover engagement, funds, milestones, incidents and typed Return.
- CH26–CH29 cover relationships, Trust, provider evolution, disputes and governance.
- CH30–CH33 define the operator-assisted MVP, conformance baseline, implementation prerequisites and capability flywheel.

The governing reading rule is simple:

> Treat every recommendation, projection and observation according to its authority and evidence—not according to how convenient it would be to treat it as final truth.

## Reading forward

CH02 states the MGSN Product Constitution. Later chapters should be read as applications of that Constitution rather than as independent feature proposals.
