# B07-CH-13 — Official Fees, Disbursements and Cost Layers

## Why one total price is not enough

A global trademark service price is usually made from several economically and legally different components. Treating them as one undifferentiated number makes comparison unreliable, hides change risk and creates disputes when an official fee, translation cost or local disbursement changes.

MGSN therefore separates cost layers before presenting a customer-facing offer.

```text
provider procurement cost
+ official fees and required disbursements
+ platform service, support and risk cost
+ demand-side margin where permitted
= demand-side offer
```

The formula is conceptual. The exact legal, tax and accounting treatment depends on the jurisdiction, contracting entities and payment route.

## Official fees

Official fees are amounts charged by an office, registry, court or other competent authority. Their treatment must record at least:

- authority and jurisdiction;
- fee event;
- class, item, page or claim basis;
- currency;
- effective date;
- known exemptions or surcharges;
- source and freshness;
- whether the amount is fixed, estimated or pending confirmation.

An official fee is not automatically immutable. Offices may amend schedules, exchange rates may move, and the final charge may depend on facts not known at quote time.

## Disbursements

Disbursements may include:

- translation or transliteration;
- notarization, legalization or certification;
- courier or original-document handling;
- publication charges;
- bank and remittance charges;
- local tax or mandatory levy;
- search, recordal or registry-copy fees;
- hearing, evidence or attendance expenses.

A disbursement should not be presented as an official fee merely because it is unavoidable. The distinction matters for explanation, margin analysis, tax treatment and later reconciliation.

## Provider procurement cost

Provider procurement cost is the amount or formula negotiated with the Execution Provider Workplace. It may include professional work, local administration, evidence preparation, reporting and agreed corrections.

This layer remains separate from:

- the provider's internal cost structure;
- the customer's final price;
- MGSN's platform margin;
- the Originating Workplace's private margin.

MGSN may govern the procurement term without exposing it to every demand participant.

## Platform service, support and risk cost

The platform may charge for capabilities that are not contained in the provider's professional fee, including:

- supply formation and verification;
- procurement and package normalization;
- matching and recommendation;
- funds and settlement coordination;
- milestone tracking;
- exception management;
- replacement capacity;
- evidence continuity;
- operator support;
- network governance and Trust maintenance.

This is not a hidden official fee. It is a platform service and risk layer and should be treated as such in internal controls and customer-facing disclosures where required.

## Demand-side margin

The Originating Workplace may add its own service or commercial margin where permitted by law, contract and product configuration. That margin belongs to the Originating Workplace's private business context.

```text
MGSN procurement economics
≠ Originating Workplace private margin
```

MGSN should not require a Partner to expose its internal margin to the provider or to unrelated network participants.

## Estimates, variables and contingencies

Some amounts cannot be final at quotation time. The Product should distinguish:

- fixed amount;
- estimate;
- capped estimate;
- pass-through at actual cost;
- conditional surcharge;
- excluded future event;
- amount requiring renewed approval.

A contingency must be explained by its trigger, not hidden inside a vague total.

## Currency and exchange-rate handling

Cross-border services may involve several currencies. The commercial record should identify:

- source currency;
- offer currency;
- exchange-rate source or method;
- rate timestamp or validity window;
- who bears currency movement;
- rounding method;
- bank or conversion charges;
- re-quotation trigger.

The Product Baseline does not establish a regulated foreign-exchange service. It requires the commercial assumptions to be typed and reviewable.

## Taxes and withholding

Taxes, withholding and invoice requirements may differ between the provider, platform, Originating Workplace and end customer. They cannot be reduced to one universal rule.

MGSN may record tax assumptions and required evidence, but final tax treatment requires appropriate legal and accounting authority.

## Reconciliation

After payment or completion, estimated and actual amounts may differ. Commercial Reconciliation should show:

- original assumption;
- actual charge;
- supporting evidence;
- approved variance;
- refund, additional collection or credit;
- responsible party;
- link to the appropriate Finance record.

MGSN controls the service-level explanation and projection. It does not silently duplicate the Finance ledger.

## Control statement

Transparent cost layers make procurement comparable and offers defensible. They also protect the system from two opposite errors: presenting every cost as an official fee, and treating every difference between procurement cost and customer price as unexplained margin.
