# B07-CH-15 — Commercial Neutrality and Routing Integrity

## Profit is permitted; distortion is not

MGSN is a commercial platform. It may earn from procurement advantage, managed-service packaging, support, risk control and network operations. Commercial sustainability is necessary if the platform is expected to recruit providers, maintain alternatives, manage exceptions and improve global coverage.

But commercial value cannot become unchecked routing power.

```text
commercial optimization
≠ professional override
```

The platform may optimize among eligible routes. It may not make an ineligible route eligible because it is more profitable.

## The routing integrity hierarchy

Before commercial preference is considered, a route should satisfy higher-order controls:

1. legal and platform admission;
2. Capability and service fit;
3. current qualification;
4. conflict clearance;
5. availability and capacity;
6. deadline and urgency feasibility;
7. unacceptable-risk checks;
8. continuity and evidence requirements.

Only after these gates may the platform compare price, procurement efficiency, provider relationship, platform margin and portfolio strategy.

## Lowest price is not neutrality

A lowest-price rule can create the appearance of objectivity while producing poor outcomes. It may favor providers with incomplete scope, stale fees, weak evidence duties or unrealistic timing.

Price remains an important dimension, but it must be compared on a like-for-like basis:

- same service scope;
- same official-fee assumptions;
- same evidence and reporting duties;
- same urgency level;
- same qualification and conflict status;
- comparable correction and replacement terms.

## Paid placement and hidden preference

MGSN is not an advertising marketplace. A provider should not become Recommended Primary merely because it paid for placement, offered a rebate unrelated to the matter or accepted a commercially favorable but professionally unsuitable term.

Any strategic or commercial preference used in routing should remain subordinate to eligibility and recorded in a reviewable internal basis.

## Explainable recommendation

A recommendation should be explainable in user-relevant terms, such as:

- suitable Capability and qualification;
- expected completion time;
- stable package scope;
- relationship continuity;
- urgent capacity;
- specialist fit;
- price or value advantage;
- lower operational risk.

The explanation need not reveal confidential procurement cost, full ranking weights or the entire provider pool. It should reveal enough for the user to understand why the route is appropriate and what material trade-offs exist.

## Bounded alternatives

Where useful, the Product may present one Recommended Route and up to two materially differentiated alternatives. Alternatives should not be cosmetic duplicates.

A meaningful alternative may provide:

- lower customer-facing price;
- faster service;
- stronger specialist fit;
- preserved historical relationship;
- different service depth;
- stronger continuity or backup value.

The user may confirm, reject, request rematch, request an eligible preferred provider or choose the External Self-Managed Route.

## Recommendation, selection and appointment

Commercial neutrality depends on keeping stages separate:

```text
recommendation
≠ user instruction
≠ provider allocation
≠ provider acceptance
≠ completed engagement
```

No commercial model should collapse these stages into silent automatic appointment.

## Self-dealing and related parties

A dual-role Organization or platform affiliate may appear on both demand and supply sides. Related-party participation requires additional controls:

- relationship disclosure;
- separate permissions and ledgers;
- conflict review;
- prohibition on self-routing without approval;
- independent comparison where practical;
- Trust protection against artificial transactions;
- auditable operator decision.

A related party is not automatically prohibited, but it cannot receive invisible preferential treatment.

## Customer interest and platform resilience

Routing integrity does not mean that the platform must ignore its own resilience. It may distribute work to preserve capacity, test a pilot route or reduce concentration risk, provided that the route remains eligible and the user's material interests are not degraded without disclosure and control.

## Review and correction

A participant should be able to challenge a recommendation or report suspected distortion. Review may examine:

- stale qualification;
- undisclosed conflict;
- inaccurate price comparison;
- unexplained provider substitution;
- related-party influence;
- misuse of relationship provenance;
- margin overriding customer interest.

Correction may lead to rematch, revised offer, restriction, operator review or governance escalation.

## Control statement

MGSN can be both commercially sustainable and professionally credible. The dividing line is routing integrity: profit may shape how the platform operates among safe and suitable choices, but it may not redefine what is safe, suitable or authorized.
