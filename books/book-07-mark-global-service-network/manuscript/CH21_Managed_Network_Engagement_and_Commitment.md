# B07-CH-21 — Managed Network Engagement and Commitment

## 1. The engagement boundary

A recommended route does not become a managed engagement merely because the user prefers it.

MGSN requires a controlled transition:

```text
Accepted Service Offer
→ confirmed route and scope
→ Provider Allocation
→ Provider Acceptance
→ Managed Network Engagement
→ instruction readiness
```

Each step answers a different question.

- the Service Offer defines the demand-side commercial proposition;
- route confirmation records the user's choice;
- Provider Allocation identifies the intended execution route;
- Provider Acceptance records whether the provider will actually take responsibility;
- Managed Network Engagement binds the accepted parties, scope and controls into one network-governed engagement.

## 2. Engagement is not automatic execution

A Managed Network Engagement is a controlled operating envelope. It does not itself prove that work has started, that money may be released or that an external filing has occurred.

```text
Engagement created
≠ provider instructed
≠ provider started
≠ official action completed
```

Before instruction, the platform may still need to verify:

- identity and authority;
- conflict clearance;
- document completeness;
- deadlines and urgency;
- offer validity;
- funds requirements;
- required approvals;
- sanctions or restricted-party checks;
- jurisdiction-specific prerequisites.

## 3. Core engagement contents

A governed engagement should identify at least:

- Originating Workplace;
- Execution Provider Workplace;
- route type;
- accepted Service Offer version;
- Service Package version;
- scope, exclusions and assumptions;
- user disposition;
- provider acceptance;
- commercial and official-fee assumptions;
- expected milestones and evidence;
- Handoff and Return expectations;
- escalation path;
- correction and replacement rules;
- legal, regulatory and policy restrictions.

The engagement may reference Customer, Order, Matter, Task and financial records, but it does not replace their Owning Services.

## 4. Provider commitment

Provider Acceptance must be explicit enough to establish:

- accepted scope;
- accepted timing assumptions;
- named responsible provider organization;
- current availability;
- applicable Service Package;
- known exceptions;
- required preconditions;
- acknowledgement of Return and evidence duties.

Silence, prior cooperation or an old quotation is not sufficient.

```text
Historical relationship
≠ current acceptance

Prior availability
≠ current capacity
```

## 5. Instruction preparation

Once commitment exists, MGSN can prepare a controlled instruction package. The package should use progressive disclosure: only the information required for the accepted task is shared.

The instruction package may include:

- typed need and jurisdiction;
- relevant party identity;
- approved documents;
- deadlines;
- accepted goods or services scope;
- filing or response instructions;
- evidence expectations;
- communication and escalation route;
- restrictions on reuse or onward disclosure.

It must not expose unrelated Workplace customers, internal margin or private operating history.

## 6. Responsibility allocation

The Originating Workplace remains responsible for its customer relationship and business context.

The Execution Provider Workplace remains responsible for professional execution, professional judgment and provider-side evidence.

MGSN remains responsible for network governance, controlled routing, engagement coordination and governed Return.

The Owning Service remains responsible for formal business state.

```text
Managed coordination
≠ transfer of all professional responsibility to the platform
```

## 7. Material changes after commitment

After Provider Acceptance, a material change to scope, deadline, provider, price basis or required evidence must not be silently absorbed.

The controlled options are:

- amendment and re-acceptance;
- additional approval;
- correction request;
- replacement;
- cancellation;
- refund or reconciliation;
- dispute escalation.

The engagement must preserve which version was accepted, by whom and under what assumptions.

## 8. Product boundary

This chapter defines the product meaning of a Managed Network Engagement and Provider Commitment.

It does not authorize:

- automatic provider appointment;
- automatic external instruction;
- payment custody;
- funds release;
- legal representation;
- production deployment;
- External Protected Action.
