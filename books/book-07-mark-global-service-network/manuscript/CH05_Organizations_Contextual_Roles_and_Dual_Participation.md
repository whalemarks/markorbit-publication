# B07-CH-05 — Organizations, Contextual Roles and Dual Participation

MGSN does not divide the world into permanently fixed demand-side and provider-side institutions. The stable participant is the Organization. Demand and Supply are contextual roles attached to a specific capability and engagement.

## Stable identity, contextual role

A Chinese trademark agency may provide China filing capability while requesting United States or European Union capability. A foreign law firm may supply prosecution services in its home jurisdiction while requesting China filing or translation support. The same Organization can therefore act in both directions at the same time.

```text
Organization Participant
├── Demand Role
└── Supply Role
```

The Supply Role exists only when a relevant Capability has been admitted and remains qualified, available and unrestricted. The Demand Role exists when a Workplace projects a valid Capability Need.

## Why one identity matters

Separate unrelated accounts would fragment Trust, permissions, financial reconciliation and relationship history. A stable Organization identity permits one participant to maintain a coherent profile while still separating each operational context.

The Product experience may expose two modes:

```text
Organization Space
├── Request Services
└── Provide Services
```

The modes share identity but not unrestricted data access.

## Required separation

Dual participation requires distinct controls for:

- permissions;
- customer visibility;
- demand-side payables;
- provider-side receivables;
- settlement;
- conflict checks;
- related-party disclosure;
- pricing and procurement visibility;
- audit context.

A provider role must not automatically see the demand-side customer record. A demand role must not expose unrelated provider operations. Receivables and payables must not be automatically netted merely because the same Organization appears on both sides.

## Self-dealing and affiliated routes

MGSN must detect when an Organization, affiliate or related party could influence both demand and supply in the same engagement. Such cases require disclosure and operator review. The platform must prevent:

- self-routing without approval;
- hidden affiliated-provider selection;
- artificial performance generation;
- Trust manipulation;
- circular incentives;
- unauthorized financial offset.

## Relationship is not role ownership

An Organization that introduces another participant does not own that participant’s future role. A prior customer-provider relationship may affect preference and attribution, but qualification, conflict, availability, price, risk and customer interest remain controlling.

## Constitutional result

The contextual-role model allows the network to grow organically. Every participant may contribute capability where it is strong and request capability where it is limited. MGSN therefore expands global capacity without forcing participants into a permanent platform-side category.
