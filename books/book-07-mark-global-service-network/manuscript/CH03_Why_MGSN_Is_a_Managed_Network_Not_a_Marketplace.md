# B07-CH-03 — Why MGSN Is a Managed Network, Not a Marketplace

A marketplace primarily helps two sides discover each other and transact. MGSN has a different responsibility. It must build and govern dependable global service capability across jurisdictions, qualifications, prices, professional risks, deadlines, evidence and funds.

## Central hub topology

All MGSN-managed participation connects through the platform hub:

```text
Originating Workplace → MGSN ← Execution Provider Workplace
```

The platform decides which supply is admitted, how Capability is represented, which procurement terms are valid, which routes are eligible, what information is disclosed, how funds and milestones are controlled, and when correction or replacement is required.

This central model is necessary because international professional work cannot be safely reduced to public listings and star ratings. A provider suitable for routine filing may be unsuitable for opposition, litigation, urgent restoration or a conflict-sensitive matter. Price is only one routing dimension.

## Why not open bidding

Open bidding creates incentives to simplify scope, understate risk and compete on headline price. MGSN instead uses qualified supply portfolios and controlled procurement. The demand-side user may see one Recommended Route and, where useful, up to two materially differentiated alternatives. The user does not need unrestricted access to the entire provider pool.

## Closed interfaces

Closed does not mean opaque or arbitrary. It means access is purpose-bound:

- provider identity is disclosed when professionally or legally necessary;
- route reasons and constraints are explainable;
- provider procurement cost and routing weights remain platform-confidential;
- communication can be enabled for the engagement without creating an exportable network edge;
- evidence and decisions remain auditable.

## No horizontal network ownership

A completed collaboration does not give either participant ownership of the other participant inside MGSN. Prior relationships may be recorded through Relationship Provenance, but they do not create permanent routing monopolies or bypass rights.

```text
collaboration ≠ unrestricted bilateral network
introduction ≠ ownership transfer
provider visibility ≠ provider-pool export
```

## Managed network value

MGSN earns its place by doing more than discovery:

- aggregating demand;
- negotiating and versioning procurement terms;
- verifying Capability and availability;
- constructing resilient multi-provider portfolios;
- controlling recommendation, acceptance and commitment;
- coordinating funds and fulfillment checkpoints;
- preserving evidence;
- correcting failure and replacing supply;
- learning from governed outcomes.

The Product succeeds when participants choose MGSN because it materially improves execution and management—not because it merely lists more names.
