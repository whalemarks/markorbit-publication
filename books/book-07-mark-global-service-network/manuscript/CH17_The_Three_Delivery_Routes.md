# B07-CH-17 — The Three Delivery Routes

## One need, three governance models

MGSN does not force every international service into the same operating model. The Product supports three constitutional routes:

```text
R1 — External Self-Managed Route
R2 — MGSN Recommended Managed Route
R3 — MGSN Managed Preferred-Provider Route
```

The routes differ in who selects the provider, which controls apply, what the platform governs and what guarantees are available.

## R1 — External Self-Managed Route

R1 applies when the Originating Workplace uses a provider or external route outside MGSN management.

The Workplace may retain:

- its own provider relationship;
- direct commercial terms;
- direct communications;
- its own payment arrangements;
- its own evidence collection process.

MGSN may still support continuity through reminders, expected Evidence definitions and later source-qualified manual uploads.

But R1 does not imply:

- platform provider qualification;
- platform procurement terms;
- platform funds control;
- platform fulfillment monitoring;
- platform replacement commitment;
- automated official-state return;
- platform responsibility for unknown outcomes.

```text
R1 continuity support
≠ MGSN-managed fulfillment guarantee
```

## R2 — MGSN Recommended Managed Route

R2 is the default managed route.

MGSN interprets the Capability Need, applies eligibility and risk gates, forms a Candidate Route Set and presents:

```text
one Recommended Route
+ up to two materially differentiated alternatives where useful
```

The user may confirm, reject, request rematching or move to R1.

After user confirmation, MGSN prepares provider allocation. The provider must still accept the instruction.

```text
Recommendation
≠ appointment

User confirmation
≠ Provider Acceptance
```

R2 may include:

- platform procurement terms;
- controlled Service Offers;
- provider allocation;
- funds and release checkpoints;
- fulfillment monitoring;
- correction and replacement processes;
- typed Return.

## R3 — MGSN Managed Preferred-Provider Route

R3 applies when the Originating Workplace requests a particular provider, often because of prior cooperation or a business preference.

The preference is respected but not absolute.

The provider must still satisfy:

- MGSN participation and admission;
- relevant Capability and Qualification;
- current Evidence and Availability;
- conflict requirements;
- Service Package compatibility;
- procurement and commercial controls;
- restrictions and risk gates.

```text
Preferred Provider
≠ automatically eligible provider
≠ guaranteed allocation
```

If the preferred provider fails a gate, MGSN may:

- explain the incompatibility;
- present qualified alternatives;
- request operator review;
- allow the Workplace to proceed externally through R1 where appropriate.

## Route selection is matter-specific

A Workplace may use different routes for different services.

For example:

- routine filings may use R2;
- a long-standing foreign associate may be requested through R3;
- a highly specialized litigation matter may remain R1;
- an urgent replacement may move from R3 to R2.

The route belongs to the specific Capability Need or engagement, not permanently to the Organization.

## Route changes

A route may change when:

- the user changes preference;
- a provider declines;
- a conflict appears;
- availability changes;
- the need changes materially;
- a Service Offer expires;
- fulfillment fails;
- the user elects to self-manage externally.

A route change must preserve the record of:

- the prior route;
- the reason for change;
- user disposition;
- affected commercial terms;
- projected data already disclosed;
- required Evidence or Return continuity.

## Responsibility matrix

| Responsibility | R1 | R2 | R3 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Provider chosen by | Workplace | MGSN recommendation plus user confirmation | Workplace preference subject to MGSN gates |
| Provider admission | outside MGSN | required | required |
| Platform procurement | not implied | normally applies | normally applies |
| Funds controls | not implied | managed controls | managed controls |
| Fulfillment monitoring | limited continuity support | managed | managed |
| Replacement | not guaranteed | governed | governed |
| Typed Return | manual/external evidence bridge | required | required |

## No hidden route conversion

The Product must not silently convert:

- R1 into an MGSN-managed engagement;
- R2 into a paid-placement route;
- R3 into automatic appointment;
- a provider substitution into an unchanged confirmed route.

Material route changes require disclosure and, where necessary, renewed user confirmation.

## Product consequence

The three-route model gives users freedom without weakening governance.

R1 preserves external autonomy. R2 provides the strongest default network experience. R3 preserves legitimate relationships while keeping platform qualification, procurement and fulfillment controls intact.
