# B07-CH-18 — Eligibility, Conflict and Availability Gates

## Recommendation begins with exclusion

A safe routing system does not begin by ranking every provider. It begins by removing routes that should not be considered.

The first stage is a set of hard and contextual gates:

```text
Eligibility
→ Qualification
→ Conflict
→ Availability
→ Restriction and Risk
→ Candidate eligibility
```

Only routes that survive the gates may enter a Candidate Route Set.

## Eligibility

Eligibility asks whether a provider route may legally, operationally and contractually perform the requested service.

It may depend on:

- jurisdiction;
- service type;
- applicant or owner status;
- local representation requirements;
- professional licensing;
- sanctions or geographic restrictions;
- language capability;
- accepted Service Package scope;
- document and signature requirements;
- permitted payment and settlement route;
- platform participation status.

Eligibility is specific to the current Capability Need.

```text
Provider admitted to MGSN
≠ eligible for every service
```

## Qualification

Qualification asks whether the provider has current, verified authority and demonstrated capability for the service.

A broad Provider Network Profile is not enough. The relevant Capability Claim must be supported by current Evidence and an active Qualification Status.

Qualification may be limited by:

- jurisdiction;
- service category;
- contentious versus non-contentious work;
- complexity level;
- professional role;
- volume or urgency;
- expiry date;
- supervision requirements.

## Conflict

Conflict checking is not a cosmetic confirmation after selection. It is a routing gate.

The exact process varies by jurisdiction and professional rules, but the Product must support:

- minimum identifiers required for conflict checking;
- purpose-limited disclosure;
- provider confirmation or refusal;
- time-stamped result;
- limits on reuse of disclosed information;
- renewed checks when parties or scope change.

A provider should not receive the complete Matter merely to perform an initial conflict check.

```text
Conflict-check disclosure
≠ full instruction disclosure
```

Conflict outcomes may include:

- clear;
- conflict identified;
- possible conflict requiring clarification;
- unable to check with current information;
- provider declines without detailed disclosure.

## Availability

Qualification describes what a provider can do. Availability describes whether it can do this work now.

Availability may consider:

- current capacity;
- response time;
- deadline feasibility;
- holiday or office closure;
- language staffing;
- specialist availability;
- workload limits;
- emergency handling capability;
- temporary suspension.

```text
Qualified
≠ currently available
```

Availability is time-sensitive. Old declarations cannot be treated as permanent.

## Restrictions and risk controls

A provider may be otherwise qualified and available but restricted because of:

- unresolved incidents;
- service-specific quality concerns;
- sanctions or payment constraints;
- expired documentation;
- repeated deadline failures;
- commercial dispute;
- pending governance review;
- limited pilot status;
- prohibited related-party routing.

Restrictions may apply to a whole participant, one jurisdiction, one service type or one operating condition.

## Hard gates and soft factors

The Product must distinguish hard exclusion gates from later recommendation factors.

Hard gates include matters such as:

- lack of legal eligibility;
- missing mandatory Qualification;
- confirmed conflict;
- inability to meet a critical deadline;
- active suspension;
- prohibited payment route.

Soft factors may include:

- relative speed;
- price;
- prior relationship;
- preferred communication style;
- performance history;
- workload balancing.

A soft factor cannot override a failed hard gate.

## Safe no-candidate outcomes

Sometimes no route passes all required gates.

The correct result is:

```text
No eligible candidate
```

Possible next actions include:

- request more information;
- expand the search to another admitted provider;
- ask an operator to review the need;
- revise timing or scope;
- use R1 with explicit external-route limits;
- decline to provide a managed route.

The Product must not fill the gap with an unverified contact merely to preserve conversion.

```text
No candidate
≠ permission to bypass governance
```

## Gate evidence and audit

Each material gate result should preserve:

- the gate type;
- input version;
- source;
- evaluator or operator;
- outcome;
- timestamp;
- expiry or reconsideration condition;
- explanation suitable for the permitted audience.

Not every internal detail must be exposed to the user, especially conflict-sensitive or provider-confidential information. But the route decision must remain auditable.

## Rechecking gates

Gates must be re-evaluated when:

- the Capability Need changes;
- a deadline changes;
- the provider changes;
- the prior result expires;
- parties or adverse interests change;
- a restriction is added;
- the provider's availability changes;
- the Service Offer is revised materially.

## Product consequence

Eligibility, conflict and availability gates convert the provider pool into a safe candidate domain.

They ensure that recommendation begins only after the Product has protected legal feasibility, professional integrity, deadline safety and network governance.
