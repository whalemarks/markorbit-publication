## Constitutional Balance

The review rejects the earlier overcorrection that treated all network relationships and resources as user-owned.

The correct balance is:

```text
Platform owns and governs MGSN network resources.

User Organization retains its own customer relationship,
internal business assets and professional decisions.

Provider retains independent professional identity,
qualification and responsibility.

Platform controls network admission, procurement,
routing interfaces, funds and fulfillment where the
MGSN service model applies.
```

## Product Findings

### Finding P-01 — Global capability is platform-enabled

Users are not assumed to possess comprehensive global service capacity independently.

MGSN extends their operating capability.

### Finding P-02 — Procurement is a core Product function

Unified negotiation, service packaging and demand aggregation are central to attracting strong providers and demand-side users.

### Finding P-03 — Funds control is a risk function

Funds custody, staged release, official-fee separation, settlement and reconciliation are not merely monetization features.

They protect against non-performance, misuse, incomplete delivery and cross-border financial risk.

### Finding P-04 — Closed operation is strategically justified

A relatively closed network can protect:

- provider quality;
- procurement terms;
- customer confidentiality;
- platform economics;
- interface stability;
- funds control;
- traceability;
- replacement and suspension mechanisms.

### Finding P-05 — User autonomy is bounded

The user needs meaningful control over service need, customer context, commercial acceptance and required approval.

The user does not require unrestricted ownership of provider supply or bypass rights.

### Finding P-06 — Provider attractiveness must be designed

Platform control is sustainable only if providers receive qualified demand, repeat volume, reliable payment, standardized instructions and fair dispute procedures.

## Experience Findings

### Finding X-01 — Service completion before provider browsing

The main Product experience should begin with service need and global capability coverage.

### Finding X-02 — Controlled disclosure is a deliberate design mechanism

Provider identity, cost, contact and case information may be disclosed progressively.

### Finding X-03 — Funds and milestones must be visible

The user should understand:

- what has been paid;
- what is held;
- what may be released;
- which evidence is pending;
- whether a dispute or replacement is active.

### Finding X-04 — Platform control must still be explainable

Closed operation must not become unexplained allocation or arbitrary withholding.

Appropriate users need visibility into service scope, qualifications, commercial terms, platform responsibility and dispute mechanisms.

### Finding X-05 — Visual identity should express governed global capability

MGSN should visually emphasize controlled global routes, verified nodes, protected funds and fulfillment continuity rather than auctions or public seller listings.

## Architecture Reconciliation

The platform-owned model can conform with the Orbit architecture when these distinctions remain explicit:

```text
platform-owned network resource
≠ platform ownership of every user customer

platform-controlled provider relationship
≠ elimination of provider professional independence

platform procurement
≠ hidden professional conflict

funds control
≠ unregulated custody assumption

closed interface
≠ unexplained or unauditable decision

user autonomy
≠ unrestricted provider extraction or bypass
```

No upstream Change Proposal is required at this stage because the existing Canon permits shared network responsibility and capability Routing.

A future Charter must trigger cross-book review if it proposes that the platform universally owns user customers, formal Matters, professional decisions or official outcomes.
