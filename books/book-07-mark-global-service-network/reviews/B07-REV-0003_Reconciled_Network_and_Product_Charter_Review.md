## Review Question

> Does the Book 07 Charter, after Workplace sovereignty reconciliation, define a coherent platform-owned MGSN Product and network without taking business sovereignty from participant Workplaces, weakening Core or Owning Service authority, or creating an uncontrolled participant-to-participant network?

## 1. Authority Chain

```text
Active Canon vNext.1
→ Books 01–04 accepted publication baseline
→ Book 02 frozen Core semantics
→ Book 03 Execution
→ Book 05 MarkReg boundaries
→ Book 06 Lite boundaries
→ Book 07 accepted Pre-Writing Audit
→ reconciled Charter baseline
```

Result: **PASS**.

The Charter no longer depends on the earlier ambiguous statement that MGSN directly connects independent Workplaces as a private participant-owned network.

## 2. Workplace Business Sovereignty

The reconciled Charter preserves:

- Originating Workplace Customer and Contact context;
- originating commercial relationship;
- internal customer pricing and margin;
- private Documents, Evidence and Knowledge;
- professional decisions and approvals;
- external self-managed route history;
- returned result and continuity context.

It does not transfer those assets to MGSN merely because the Workplace uses platform capability.

Result: **PASS**.

## 3. MGSN Platform Authority

The reconciled Charter confirms that MGSN owns or governs:

- provider recruitment and admission;
- provider supply portfolios;
- procurement and package terms;
- matching and routing;
- platform Trust and performance evidence;
- controlled funds and settlement processes;
- fulfillment, correction and replacement;
- suspension and retirement;
- network interfaces and operating data.

Result: **PASS**.

```text
Workplace sovereignty
≠ Workplace ownership of MGSN supply

Platform MGSN control
≠ platform ownership of Partner customers
```

## 4. MGSN Connection and Network

The required distinction is now explicit:

```text
MGSN Connection / Gateway
= Workplace-scoped participation and projection interface

MGSN Network
= platform-owned and platform-governed managed network
```

Result: **PASS**.

## 5. Originating and Execution Provider Workplaces

The canonical managed route is:

```text
Originating Workplace
→ authorized demand projection
→ MGSN Connection / Gateway
→ platform-owned MGSN Network
→ controlled allocation / instruction
→ Execution Provider Workplace
→ governed Return
→ Originating Workplace
```

The Originating Workplace retains originating business sovereignty. The Execution Provider Workplace retains provider-side operations and professional responsibility. Owning Services retain formal-state authority.

Result: **PASS**.

## 6. Participant Roles

The stable participant is an Organization. Demand and supply are contextual roles.

The same Organization may request one Capability and provide another through separate role, permission, financial and conflict contexts.

This does not create an unrestricted peer-to-peer participant network.

Result: **PASS**.

## 7. Delivery Routes

The three-route constitution remains valid:

```text
R1 — External Self-Managed Route
R2 — MGSN Recommended Managed Route
R3 — MGSN Managed Preferred-Provider Route
```

R1 remains outside MGSN fulfillment guarantees and resumes continuity through source-qualified manual Evidence.

R2 is the default managed route.

R3 permits a preferred provider only after platform admission and eligibility checks.

Result: **PASS**.

## 8. Matching and Choice

The Product may present:

```text
one Recommended Route
+ up to two materially differentiated alternatives where useful
+ rematch / preferred-provider request
+ External Self-Managed Route option
```

The complete provider pool, procurement cost and routing model are not user-facing constitutional requirements.

```text
Automatic matching
≠ automatic appointment

User confirmation
≠ Provider Acceptance
```

Result: **PASS**.

## 9. Commercial and Procurement Model

The platform wholesale procurement plus managed-service model remains coherent.

MGSN may aggregate demand, negotiate provider terms, standardize packages and produce customer-facing offers while separating:

- provider procurement cost;
- official fees and disbursements;
- platform service, support and risk cost;
- demand-side margin where permitted.

Commercial optimization may not override professional fit, conflict, qualification, deadlines or unacceptable risk.

Result: **PASS**.

## 10. Funds and Fulfillment

The Charter correctly establishes controlled funds and milestone governance as Product requirements while deferring the final legal custody, payment, accounting, tax, foreign-exchange and client-money model.

Result: **PASS WITH IMPLEMENTATION LEGAL GATE**.

The Charter does not claim universal escrow or trust-account status.

## 11. Provider and Trust Model

The Charter preserves:

```text
Organization
≠ MGSN Provider Profile
≠ Capability Claim
≠ Evidence
≠ verification
≠ contextual Provider role
≠ Network Service Engagement
```

Trust remains service-specific, multidimensional, source-aware, time-scoped and correctable.

Result: **PASS**.

## 12. Bidirectional Introduction and Relationship Provenance

Demand participants may introduce existing providers. Providers may introduce existing demand participants.

Introduction creates provenance and possible bounded incentives, not automatic admission, permanent ownership or unrestricted routing rights.

Pre-existing external business may remain outside MGSN. MGSN-originated or MGSN-managed work remains subject to platform controls.

Result: **PASS**.

## 13. Product Installation and Projection

Demand-side and provider-side access must occur through explicit Workplace Product Installations, MGSN Connections or accepted interfaces.

Client-facing or provider-facing views are projections and do not silently transfer business sovereignty or formal-state authority.

Result: **PASS**.

## 14. Book 02 and Cross-Book Boundary

`MO-B02-CPA-001` concludes that the active Canon clarification does not itself require a Book 02 semantic Change Proposal.

The Charter does not alter frozen Core object identity, cardinality, formal states, transitions, Owning Services or Event/Handoff contracts.

Result: **PASS**.

- existing proven foreign associates and selected verified providers;
- filing-through-registration packages where stable;
- selected high-volume post-filing services;
- human operation for admission, complex procurement, funds review, replacement, dispute and suspension.

Result: **PASS**.
