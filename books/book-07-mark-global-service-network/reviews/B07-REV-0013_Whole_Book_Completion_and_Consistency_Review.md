## Architecture and product consistency

### PASS — Workplace and authority boundaries

The manuscript consistently preserves:

- Workplace business sovereignty;
- Core semantic authority;
- Owning Service formal-state authority;
- MGSN platform-owned network authority;
- physical custody as distinct from business ownership;
- legal/source authority as context-specific.

### PASS — Managed-network identity

MGSN remains a central, platform-governed network rather than an open marketplace, provider directory or peer-to-peer network.

### PASS — Participant model

Organization remains the stable participant while Demand and Supply remain contextual roles. Dual-role permissions, conflicts, payables and receivables remain separated.

### PASS — Supply and procurement

Capability Claim, Evidence, Verification, Qualification, Availability and Trust remain distinct. Multi-provider portfolios do not become public bidding or permanent ranking.

### PASS — Routing and human choice

R1, R2 and R3 remain distinct. Recommendation, user disposition, allocation and Provider Acceptance remain separate decisions. No Candidate remains a safe result.

### PASS — Funds, fulfillment and Return

MGSN funds projections do not replace the Finance ledger or imply a universal escrow license. Fulfillment Evidence and observations do not silently mutate Matter or official truth. Return remains typed and downstream validated.

### PASS — Trust and governance

Trust remains multidimensional, scoped, source-aware, time-aware, correctable and appealable. Platform governance does not claim judicial or regulatory authority.

## Editorial findings

### EF-01 — Repeated constitutional disclaimers

Necessary boundary statements recur across multiple waves. They are consistent, but some can be shortened or cross-referenced during hardening without weakening safeguards.

### EF-02 — Cross-chapter navigation

Several chapters would benefit from explicit “see also” references to the earlier constitutional chapter and later implementation-boundary chapter.

### EF-03 — Controlled-ID discoverability

The manuscript explains the controlled concepts, but readers need a consolidated index for MG-N, MG-C, MG-P, MG-R, MG-F, MG-T and MG-E records.

### EF-04 — Journey and scenario discoverability

CH31 explains the journeys and scenarios, but publication apparatus should provide a direct MG-J and MG-SCN index.

### EF-05 — Terminology apparatus

A glossary is required for terms such as Workplace, Product Installation, Projection, Handoff, Return, Originating Workplace, Execution Provider Workplace, Managed Network Engagement and Owning Service.

### EF-06 — Implementation-boundary consolidation

CH32 is correct, but the publication should include a concise, reader-facing “This book does not authorize” notice outside the main chapter flow.

### EF-07 — Style normalization

Capitalization, hyphenation, code-block labels, list punctuation and repeated formula formatting need a single editorial pass.

### EF-08 — Publication apparatus

Book 07 requires appendices and indexes comparable in function to earlier books, adapted to a Product Book rather than copied mechanically.
