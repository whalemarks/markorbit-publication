## Key Conformance Findings

### Network ownership

The Charter correctly establishes MGSN supply, procurement, matching, funds rules, fulfillment and network evidence as platform-owned or platform-governed.

It does not incorrectly transfer demand-side customers, internal data, pricing strategy or private Knowledge to the platform.

### Topology

The Charter preserves:

```text
Demand-side participant ↔ MGSN
Provider participant ↔ MGSN
```

It does not create an unrestricted participant-to-participant network.

### Route integrity

```text
External Self-Managed Route
≠ MGSN-managed route

Automatic matching
≠ autonomous appointment

User confirmation
≠ Provider Acceptance

Same provider identity
≠ same commercial and responsibility route
```

### Formal ownership

MGSN owns the network collaboration projection but does not silently duplicate:

- Customer;
- Opportunity;
- Order;
- Matter;
- Task;
- Workflow;
- Review and Approval;
- Communication send state;
- official outcome;
- protected Execution.

### Commercial integrity

The Charter supports platform wholesale procurement, managed transactions and optional subscription/entitlement without making MGSN a free utility.

It permits platform margin and commercial optimization but prohibits commercial ranking from bypassing mandatory eligibility, conflict and capability controls.

### Funds integrity

The Charter requires platform-controlled funds rules but does not falsely claim that an unregulated entity may legally hold funds.

Implementation requires a legally suitable platform entity or regulated partner and separate specialist review.

### Provider integrity

The provider remains an independent Organization and professional actor while the MGSN network profile, admission, commercial status, Trust and routing projection remain platform-governed.

### MVP integrity

MVP 0 is appropriately bounded to selected repeatable trademark filing-through-registration services, selected supply, and operator-assisted management.

It does not claim complete global lifecycle coverage at launch.

## Zero-Tolerance Review

The Charter explicitly rejects:

- fabricated qualifications or outcomes;
- silent provider appointment;
- uncontrolled funds release;
- concealed conflict or suspension;
- procurement-price leakage;
- external work represented as MGSN guaranteed;
- unauthorized participant-data disclosure;
- unknown external outcome represented as completed;
- lowest-price-only allocation overriding professional gates.

## Deferred Decisions Review

The following are correctly deferred to later controlled or implementation work:

- legal entity and payment partner;
- tax, invoicing and revenue recognition;
- schema and APIs;
- matching weights;
- service-specific milestones;
- disclosure schedule;
- contract language;
- production cohort and pricing.

The deferrals do not prevent Charter acceptance.
