## Review Scope

This review reconciles:

- `B07-PLN-0001–0009` — accepted Pre-Writing Audit baseline;
- `B07-PLN-0010` — contextual participant roles, multi-provider supply and bidirectional introduction;
- `B07-PLN-0011` — Product and Experience Decision Lock Candidate;
- `B07-PLN-0012` — current remaining Charter questions.

The review evaluates Product identity, platform control, participant experience, supply strategy, network growth, relationship protection, financial separation and publication boundaries.

## 1. Participant Model Review

### Finding

Earlier planning language often used `demand side` and `provider side` as if they were permanent participant categories.

### Resolution

The stable participant is the Organization. Demand and supply are contextual roles.

```text
Organization
→ may request Capability
→ may provide admitted Capability
→ may do both concurrently
```

### Result

```text
PASS
```

This model better reflects the international trademark industry, where an agency or law firm commonly provides local capability while procuring foreign capability.

## 2. Hub Topology Review

### Finding

Dual-role participation could be misread as permission for a peer-to-peer participant network.

### Resolution

All MGSN-managed demand, supply, commercial, funds and fulfillment activity remains hub-mediated.

Permitted case communication does not create network ownership, unrestricted contact portability or platform bypass rights.

### Result

```text
PASS
```

## 3. Multi-Provider Supply Review

### Finding

A single provider per country or service would create pricing, conflict, availability, performance and continuity risk.

### Resolution

MGSN maintains dynamic multi-provider portfolios for material jurisdiction-and-service combinations.

The portfolio supports recommendation, alternatives, specialist routing, urgent capacity and replacement without becoming an open bidding market.

### Result

```text
PASS
```

## 4. User Choice Review

### Finding

Two undesirable extremes existed:

- one opaque platform route with no meaningful user agency;
- an unrestricted provider directory that weakens platform control.

### Resolution

The default interaction is:

```text
1 Recommended Route
+ up to 2 materially differentiated alternatives
```

Routine standardized services may show one package. High-risk or specialist services may show fewer options and require operator review.

### Result

```text
PASS
```

This preserves bounded user choice while maintaining platform curation and interface control.

## 5. Provider Disclosure Review

### Finding

Showing too little provider information weakens professional confidence. Showing direct contacts and full provider data too early enables bypass and exposes platform procurement assets.

### Resolution

Use staged disclosure:

1. package, qualification state, relevant evidence, timing, price and recommendation explanation;
2. provider identity and responsible professional when required for informed selection, conflict and instruction;
3. direct contact only within a controlled collaboration stage.

### Result

```text
PASS
```

## 6. Matching Priority Review

### Finding

Platform pricing and margin could distort matching if not constitutionally ordered.

### Resolution

Apply matching in the following order:

```text
eligibility
→ capability and professional fit
→ conflict and availability
→ risk and continuity
→ historical relationship preference
→ quality and fulfillment evidence
→ timing
→ user-facing price and procurement efficiency
→ platform margin and supply allocation
```

### Result

```text
PASS
```

Commercial benefit cannot override professional eligibility or material customer interest.

## 7. Bidirectional Introduction Review

### Finding

MGSN needs a practical mechanism for participants to migrate existing relationships into platform-managed operation.

### Resolution

Support:

- demand participant introduces provider;
- provider introduces demand Organization.

Every invited party joins independently and completes the applicable admission process.

### Result

```text
PASS
```

This creates network growth without creating participant-owned subnetworks.

## 8. Incentive Review

### Finding

No incentive would make participant-led network growth weak. Lifetime commissions or permanent routing rights would create unfair ownership and routing distortion.

### Resolution

Use bounded, event-based incentives after real platform value is created:

- service credits;
- transaction-fee discounts;
- one-time provider settlement bonuses;
- relationship attribution;
- no reward for registration alone;
- no default lifetime commission.

### Result

```text
PASS
```

Exact values remain Commercial Plan decisions.

## 9. Relationship Protection Review

### Finding

Participants will not migrate established relationships if the platform immediately disregards or captures them. Permanent exclusivity would, however, reduce route quality and network efficiency.

### Resolution

Adopt a default 12-month Relationship Provenance attribution period, renewable through successful managed cooperation.

The historical provider remains a preferred candidate when eligible, but not a permanent monopoly.

### Result

```text
PASS
```

## 10. Pre-Existing Business and Non-Circumvention Review

### Finding

The platform must distinguish genuine pre-existing business from MGSN-originated business.

### Resolution

Participants may continue genuine pre-existing external business.

MGSN-originated demand, platform-negotiated commercial terms, managed Matter context, funds protection and platform-created introductions remain inside the managed route for the applicable service and protection period.

### Result

```text
PASS
```

## 11. Wider Network Access Review

### Resolution

After independent admission:

- provider-introduced demand Organizations may access the wider MGSN according to Product eligibility;
- demand-introduced providers may serve other eligible demand participants.

The introducer does not own future network activity.

### Result

```text
PASS
```

## 12. Dual-Role Finance and Permission Review

### Finding

One Organization acting in both roles could create self-dealing, data leakage, improper netting or Trust manipulation.

### Resolution

Require separate:

- demand and supply permissions;
- payable and receivable ledgers;
- official-fee funds;
- settlement eligibility;
- conflict context;
- related-party audit flags.

No automatic netting or self-routing is allowed.

### Result

```text
PASS
```

## 13. Experience Architecture Review

### Resolution

Use one Organization identity with visibly separated modes:

```text
Request Services
Provide Services
```

The active role must be unmistakable through navigation, page title, financial context, action language and permission boundaries.

Avoid a mixed marketplace dashboard.

### Result

```text
PASS
```
