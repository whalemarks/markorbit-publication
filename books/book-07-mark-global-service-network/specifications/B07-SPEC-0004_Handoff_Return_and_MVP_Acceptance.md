# B07-SPEC-0004 — MGSN Handoff, Return and MVP Acceptance Baseline

## Handoff Contracts

### MG-HC-01 — Workplace to MGSN Capability Need

Source: Originating Workplace or authorized Product Installation.

Minimum envelope:

- source Workplace and Product Installation;
- authorized Capability Need projection;
- jurisdiction and service scope;
- timing and urgency;
- disclosure stage;
- route constraints and preferences;
- authority and expiry;
- customer or Matter references only where authorized.

MGSN must not infer unrestricted access to source records.

### MG-HC-02 — MGSN Candidate Route Presentation

Return to Originating Workplace:

- Recommended Route;
- up to two materially distinct alternatives where useful;
- user-facing price and validity;
- scope, timing, assumptions and exclusions;
- fit reasons and unresolved constraints;
- provider identity disclosure level;
- required user disposition.

Recommendation is not appointment.

### MG-HC-03 — User Route Disposition

Source: authorized user in Originating Workplace.

Allowed outcomes:

- confirm recommendation;
- confirm alternative;
- request rematch;
- request preferred provider;
- choose R1;
- defer or cancel.

Disposition does not equal Provider Acceptance.

### MG-HC-04 — MGSN Provider Allocation and Instruction Preparation

Source: MGSN.

Target: Execution Provider Workplace or Provider Interface.

Must include:

- admitted service package;
- authorized work scope;
- necessary projected context;
- timing and milestones;
- commercial and funds prerequisites;
- communication and Evidence requirements;
- acceptance deadline;
- replacement and escalation terms.

External instruction remains governed by Execution and protected-action authority.

### MG-HC-05 — Provider Acceptance or Refusal

Provider returns:

- accepted / conditionally accepted / refused;
- responsible professional or team where required;
- conflict and availability status;
- timing commitment;
- exceptions or required clarification;
- evidence of acceptance authority.

Acceptance does not equal completion.

### MG-HC-06 — Funds and Release Coordination

MGSN exchanges purpose-specific funds requirements, receipt projections, release conditions and settlement instructions with the authorized Finance boundary.

MGSN does not become the Finance ledger or legal funds holder by publication implication.

### MG-HC-07 — Fulfillment Milestone and Evidence

Provider returns milestone state and Evidence references.

MGSN may validate completeness, timeliness and service-package conformity.

A fulfillment observation does not automatically mutate Order, Matter or official lifecycle truth.

### MG-HC-08 — Completion Return

MGSN returns:

- service and engagement identity;
- provider and responsibility context;
- completion or partial-completion state;
- Evidence and Document references;
- official/source status and uncertainty;
- cost and settlement projection;
- exceptions, next actions and deadlines;
- provenance and validation state.

The receiving Owning Service decides whether and how to mutate formal state.

### MG-HC-09 — Incident, Correction and Replacement

MGSN coordinates:

- incident record;
- affected scope and deadlines;
- provider response;
- correction decision;
- replacement decision;
- funds consequence;
- continuity package;
- participant notification;
- audit and provenance.

### MG-HC-10 — External Route Evidence Bridge

For R1:

- MGSN or Product records visibility limitation;
- expected Evidence schedule;
- user-provided source and provenance;
- verification state;
- unresolved gaps;
- safe continuity decision.

No platform guarantee is implied.

## MVP 0 Acceptance Criteria

### MG-AC-01 — Workplace-Scoped Participation

Every demand or supply action is attributable to an authorized Organization, Workplace and Product/connection context.

### MG-AC-02 — Platform-Owned Supply Control

Only admitted supply can enter managed route evaluation. Provider contacts or historical spreadsheets alone are insufficient.

### MG-AC-03 — Capability and Evidence Separation

The MVP distinguishes Capability Claim, Evidence, verification, qualification and availability.

### MG-AC-04 — Three Route Support

The MVP supports R1, R2 and R3 with explicit responsibility and guarantee differences.

### MG-AC-05 — Explainable Recommendation

The MVP produces one Recommended Route with fit reasons, constraints, price, validity and unresolved conditions.

### MG-AC-06 — Bounded Alternatives

The MVP can show up to two materially different alternatives where useful without exposing the full provider pool or procurement costs.

### MG-AC-07 — User Final Confirmation

Ordinary MVP managed routes require authorized user confirmation before allocation.

### MG-AC-08 — Provider Acceptance

A route is not active until the provider accepts or an operator records an authorized acceptance result.

### MG-AC-09 — Funds Checkpoints

The MVP represents funds requirement, receipt/authorization, purpose allocation, release condition and settlement projection without claiming legal custody or replacing Finance truth.

### MG-AC-10 — Fulfillment Milestones

The MVP tracks expected milestones, observations, Evidence, exceptions and overdue conditions.

### MG-AC-11 — Typed Return

Completion and source Evidence return through a typed envelope and require downstream validation before formal-state mutation.

### MG-AC-12 — Incident and Replacement

The MVP supports delay, correction, provider refusal, replacement and continuity without losing Evidence or funds history.

### MG-AC-13 — Trust and Relationship Provenance

The MVP records multidimensional performance Evidence and relationship provenance without universal public scoring or permanent exclusivity.

### MG-AC-14 — Dual-Role Separation

A dual-role Organization has separate permissions, payable/receivable contexts and conflict controls.

### MG-AC-15 — No-Candidate Safety

No eligible candidate is represented as a valid result. The Product escalates to operator sourcing, deferred service or R1 rather than inventing supply.

### MG-AC-16 — Protected-Action Boundary

No filing, funds release, provider instruction, external communication or official act occurs merely because the Product Baseline is accepted.

## Evaluation Baseline

MVP evaluation should measure:

- Capability Need coverage;
- time to eligible route;
- percentage resolved through admitted managed supply;
- recommendation explanation completeness;
- user confirmation and rematch behavior;
- provider acceptance;
- milestone timeliness;
- exception and replacement recovery;
- repeat provider use by service scope;
- procurement improvement;
- privacy, authority and funds-control findings.

Raw provider count, page views, bidding count and transaction volume are insufficient proof of Product quality.
