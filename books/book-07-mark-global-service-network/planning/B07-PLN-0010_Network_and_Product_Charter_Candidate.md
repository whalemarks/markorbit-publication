## 4. Canonical Network and Product Identity

### 4.1 User-facing identity

> **MGSN is the platform-managed global professional service network that enables trademark professionals and agencies to deliver services worldwide through one governed commercial and fulfillment interface.**

### 4.2 Architectural identity

> **MGSN is the platform-owned network responsibility that organizes provider supply, Capability, procurement, matching, funds protection, fulfillment, Trust and service continuity across independent participant spaces.**

### 4.3 Commercial identity

> **MGSN is a managed global service procurement and fulfillment network, not a free provider directory or open bidding marketplace.**

The three descriptions serve different purposes:

```text
User promise
→ global capability through one trusted platform

Architecture
→ platform-owned network responsibility and controlled interfaces

Commercial model
→ managed procurement, service offer, funds and fulfillment
```

## 5. Why MGSN Exists

Most independent trademark professionals and small or medium agencies do not possess reliable direct service capacity in every jurisdiction.

Their international capability is often limited by:

- incomplete foreign-agent coverage;
- fragmented provider contacts;
- inconsistent quotations and service scopes;
- one-off negotiating power;
- unknown provider qualification or availability;
- advance-payment and non-performance risk;
- slow provider response;
- weak replacement options;
- lost documents and status gaps;
- manual reconciliation of official and professional fees;
- individual memory rather than institutional network intelligence.

MGSN exists to convert fragmented global supply into governed platform capability:

```text
platform provider coverage
+ standardized service scope
+ aggregated professional demand
+ negotiated procurement
+ eligibility and matching
+ controlled funds
+ milestone evidence
+ replacement and exception handling
+ typed Return
+ provider and network learning
```

The user does not need to build and operate the complete global network independently.

The platform continuously builds, negotiates, governs and improves that capability on the user's behalf.

## 6. Primary Participants

Primary demand-side users include:

- Chinese trademark agencies handling international work;
- independent international trademark professionals;
- small agencies using Lite or a lightweight Workplace;
- larger agencies using a Workplace or MarkReg;
- internal platform operators acting for an authorized demand-side Organization during early MVP stages.

Their core job is:

### 6.2 Provider Organization

Primary providers include:

- existing foreign trademark agents and law firms;
- strategic high-volume filing providers;
- specialist opposition, cancellation, review and enforcement counsel;
- regional or jurisdiction-specific service partners;
- later, verified adjacent brand-service providers.

Providers connect to MGSN as governed supply.

They retain professional identity and responsibility while accepting platform admission, procurement, service, evidence, settlement and performance rules.

The Network Operator is responsible for:

- Capability and qualification review;

### 6.4 End customer

The end customer may interact through the demand-side professional or through a later managed-service Product edition.

The Charter does not assume that every end customer directly accesses MGSN or sees the provider pool.

## 7. Primary Product Problem

The demand-side user needs to serve a jurisdiction or service need beyond the user's own reliable direct coverage.

The difficult problem is not simply finding a name in a directory.

It is coordinating:

- service scope;
- qualification;
- conflict;
- availability;
- provider cost;
- official fees;
- customer-facing price;
- instruction quality;
- payment safety;
- milestone evidence;
- deadlines;
- correction;
- replacement;
- result continuity.

The Product problem is:

> How can MGSN give each participating professional dependable global service capacity, select and manage the best available route, protect funds and fulfillment, preserve sufficient user control, and improve future service quality through governed evidence?

## 8. Product Promise

MGSN promises to help the demand-side user:

1. **cover** jurisdictions and services beyond direct internal capability;
2. **understand** available service scope, price, time and platform responsibility;
3. **receive** a platform-recommended best managed route;
4. **confirm, reject or adjust** the route before ordinary allocation;
5. **use a preferred provider** when that provider satisfies platform gates;
6. **retain an external route** when the user accepts self-management consequences;
7. **protect funds** through typed collection, allocation, release and settlement controls;
8. **track fulfillment** through platform milestones and evidence;
9. **recover** through correction, escalation or replacement;
10. **return** authoritative or source-qualified results to MarkReg, Workplace or Lite;
11. **expand** effective global capability as the platform network improves.

MGSN promises providers:

1. qualified and structured demand;
2. lower customer-acquisition cost;
3. repeat volume;
4. standardized service packages and instructions;
5. reliable platform-managed settlement;
6. reduced collection risk;
7. transparent milestone and dispute procedures;
8. capability visibility through verified delivery;
9. fair correction, restriction and appeal mechanisms.

## 10. Seven Core Network and Product Loops

### 10.1 Supply Formation Loop

Purpose:

- fill jurisdiction and service coverage gaps;
- admit reliable providers;
- keep Capability, qualification and availability current;
- create sustainable provider participation.

```text
coverage need
→ provider recruitment / application / referral
→ identity and qualification Evidence
→ Capability and jurisdiction review
→ commercial negotiation
→ service-package agreement
→ platform admission
→ active supply
→ monitoring, correction or suspension
```

### 10.2 Procurement and Service Offer Loop

Purpose:

- aggregate demand;
- negotiate stronger provider cost and conditions;
- standardize scope;
- produce a reliable platform offer.

```text
demand history and forecast
→ package and scope standardization
→ provider procurement
→ official-fee and disbursement model
→ platform service and risk cost
→ demand-side price / recommended resale model
→ offer validity and change control
```

### 10.3 Need and Route Loop

Purpose:

- determine whether the user will use an external route or MGSN;
- type the required Capability and constraints;
- preserve responsibility visibility.

```text
Customer / Matter service need
→ route choice
→ External Self-Managed Route
  or MGSN Managed Route
→ Capability and service typing
→ eligibility and disclosure gates
```

### 10.4 Matching and Confirmation Loop

Purpose:

- identify the best eligible managed route;
- support user confirmation without exposing the full provider pool or confidential procurement logic.

```text
MGSN Capability Need
→ eligible supply set
→ qualification / conflict / availability
→ price / time / performance / risk evaluation
→ Recommended Best Route
→ user confirm / reject / adjust
→ platform allocation
→ Provider Acceptance
```

### 10.5 Funds and Commitment Loop

Purpose:

- reduce non-performance and financial risk;
- protect official fees and provider settlement;
- connect release to evidence.

```text
accepted offer
→ funds request
→ funds received or authorized
→ purpose allocation
→ provider commitment
→ official-fee or milestone release
→ delivery Evidence
→ acceptance / correction / dispute
→ provider settlement
→ reconciliation
```

### 10.6 Fulfillment and Continuity Loop

Purpose:

- preserve instruction, milestone, deadline and Document continuity;
- recover from provider delay or failure.

```text
MGSN instruction package
→ Provider Acceptance
→ work and milestone Evidence
→ status Return
→ exception / correction / escalation
→ replacement where required
→ completion
→ downstream and demand-side Return
```

### 10.7 Outcome and Network Evolution Loop

Purpose:

- turn real delivery evidence into better future supply, procurement and matching.

```text
delivery and outcome Evidence
→ platform review
→ capability / performance / Trust update
→ procurement and routing consequence
→ reward / restriction / suspension / retirement
→ improved future service route
```

## 11. Delivery Route Constitution

### 11.1 R1 — External Self-Managed Route

The user may continue using an external provider without importing that provider into MGSN.

```text
user chooses external provider
→ user negotiates and pays directly
→ user manages instruction and follow-up
→ platform visibility is limited
→ user uploads Documents and status
→ Product resumes from source-qualified Evidence
```

MGSN does not imply:

- provider verification;
- procurement advantage;
- funds protection;
- provider settlement control;
- milestone monitoring;
- replacement guarantee;
- fulfillment guarantee;
- official status certainty.

The Product should make the process black hole visible and help the user bridge it through reminders, expected Evidence, manual upload and source labels.

### 11.2 R2 — MGSN Recommended Managed Route

R2 is the default managed experience when the user lacks a suitable external route or chooses platform capability.

The platform may automatically rank and preselect the Recommended Best Route.

```text
Automatic matching
≠ provider appointment

Recommended Best Route
≠ final instruction

User confirmation
≠ Provider Acceptance
```

Ordinary MVP service requires user confirmation before platform allocation.

### 11.3 R3 — MGSN Managed Preferred-Provider Route

A user may request a known provider.

The preference remains subject to:

- platform admission;
- service and jurisdiction Capability;
- qualification;
- conflict;
- availability;
- deadline;
- procurement agreement;
- service package;
- performance and incident status;
- data and professional restrictions.

A provider used outside MGSN and the same provider used through MGSN represent two different routes.

### 11.4 Standing Managed Allocation

Standing authorization for platform allocation is not the default Charter rule.

A later controlled plan may permit it only for:

- standardized service packages;
- clear budget and service limits;
- explicit user mandate;
- revocation and audit;
- no unresolved conflict or high-risk professional choice;
- service-specific policy.

## 12. Matching and User Choice Constitution

### 12.1 Default presentation

The preferred Product experience presents:

```text
one Recommended Best Route
+ the main reasons and constraints
+ a request-rematch or preference path where permitted
+ an External Self-Managed Route option
```

MGSN is not constitutionally required to expose the complete provider pool or a public ranked list.

### 12.2 Matching dimensions

The platform may consider:

- Capability and jurisdiction fit;
- qualification Evidence;
- conflict;
- availability;
- response time;
- expected completion time;
- provider cost;
- total platform cost;
- official-fee treatment;
- service quality;
- delivery reliability;
- correction and dispute history;
- capacity;
- language;
- data handling;
- sanctions and policy constraints;
- replacement continuity;
- network supply strategy.

Price is material but does not override eligibility, professional fit or unacceptable risk.

### 12.3 Explainability

The demand-side user should receive enough explanation to make a meaningful route decision, including where appropriate:

- service scope;
- expected timeline;
- price and validity;
- main fit reasons;
- known limitations;
- platform responsibility;
- funds and replacement protection;
- information still pending.

Confidential provider cost, internal routing weights and restricted network evidence need not be disclosed.

### 12.4 User authority

The user may:

- accept the Recommended Best Route;
- reject it;
- request a rematch;
- state a service, price, language or provider constraint;
- request a preferred provider;
- select the External Self-Managed Route;
- cancel before the applicable commitment point.

The user does not receive unrestricted authority over provider admission, procurement, provider cost, platform Trust, funds release, settlement or suspension.

## 14. Commercial and Procurement Constitution

### 14.1 Primary commercial model

The initial Charter direction is:

> **A platform-managed wholesale and managed-service network, with the applicable commercial model typed by service package and demand-side Product edition.**

For agency and professional users, the platform may provide:

- a platform wholesale price;
- recommended resale price;
- configurable customer-facing price within policy;
- fixed package where standardization is important;
- operator-assisted quotation for complex services.

For direct end-customer editions, MGSN may support a complete managed-service price.

### 14.2 Pricing layers

```text
Provider procurement cost
+ official fees and third-party disbursements
+ platform service, support and risk cost
+ demand-side margin where permitted
= customer-facing offer
```

These layers must not be collapsed silently.

### 14.3 Procurement control

The platform controls:

- provider cost negotiation;
- scope inclusions and exclusions;
- volume tiers;
- urgency conditions;
- official-fee handling;
- currency and tax assumptions;
- correction and replacement obligations;
- settlement timing;
- offer validity.

### 14.4 Price advantage

MGSN aims to achieve lower or more stable pricing than fragmented one-off procurement through:

- aggregated demand;
- repeat volume;
- negotiated terms;
- standard packages;
- provider payment reliability;
- reduced acquisition cost;
- lower failure and replacement cost.

This is a strategic target, not a universal lowest-price guarantee.

### 14.5 Routing neutrality

Commercial margin may inform network sustainability but must not silently override:

- eligibility;
- conflict;
- qualification;
- professional fit;
- deadline feasibility;
- customer interest;
- unacceptable financial or delivery risk.

Paid placement may not masquerade as professional fit.

## 15. Funds and Financial-Risk Constitution

### 15.1 Purpose

Funds control exists to reduce:

- advance-payment loss;
- provider non-performance;
- official-fee misuse;
- unauthorized fee change;
- incomplete delivery;
- cross-border reconciliation failure;
- provider collection risk;
- service abandonment;
- refund and invoice disputes.

### 15.2 Constitutional lifecycle

```text
offer accepted
→ funds requested
→ funds received or authorized
→ purpose separated
→ provider commitment
→ governed release
→ Evidence and reconciliation
→ settlement / refund / dispute
→ closure
```

### 15.3 Release principles

The future controlled baseline may support service-specific release, but must preserve:

- official-fee and professional-fee distinction;
- Evidence before consequential release where available;
- partial and milestone release;
- hold during dispute;
- refund and unused-balance treatment;
- traceable reconciliation;
- no provider unilateral withdrawal.

### 15.4 Legal model

The Charter does not claim that MGSN is universally an escrow service, trust account, money transmitter or regulated payment institution.

Each Product edition and jurisdiction must type:

- contracting entity;
- platform role;
- funds holder;
- payment provider;
- client-money obligations;
- tax and invoicing;
- foreign exchange;
- sanctions and AML/KYC;
- revenue recognition;
- insolvency treatment.

Implementation requires specialist legal, finance and accounting review.

## 24. MVP 0 Strategy

### 24.1 Primary MVP

> Prove that MGSN can receive one international trademark service need, produce one commercially and professionally defensible Recommended Best Route, obtain user confirmation, manage provider acceptance, funds and fulfillment, and return sourced completion evidence with lower procurement and operational risk than fragmented self-managed buying.

### 24.2 Initial demand-side user

Primary:

- Chinese trademark agencies handling international applications;
- independent international trademark professionals;
- Lite, Workplace or MarkReg users with authorized customer work.

### 24.3 Initial provider cohort

Primary:

- existing platform foreign associates;
- proven high-volume filing providers;
- selected verified law firms for initial jurisdictions.

### 24.4 Initial service scope

Start with:

- international trademark filing;
- filing through registration where a stable package is available;
- selected high-volume post-filing and registration services;
- operator-assisted exceptions for complex matters.

Complex oppositions, cancellations, litigation and enforcement may enter through an operator-assisted specialist route before full self-service.

### 24.5 Operating model

MVP 0 is operator-assisted.

Automation may support:

- need typing;
- provider eligibility;
- price and scope comparison;
- recommendation preparation;
- reminders and exception detection.

Human operators retain responsibility for:

- provider admission;
- procurement exceptions;
- complex matching;
- funds and release review;
- provider replacement;
- disputes and suspension.

### 24.6 Minimum scope

- one demand-side Organization context;
- a bounded initial jurisdiction set;
- existing admitted provider supply;
- typed service packages;

| response | Do providers acknowledge and accept reliably? |
| funds safety | Are funds allocated, released and reconciled without avoidable loss? |
| fulfillment | Are milestones and Documents delivered on time and with evidence? |
| continuity | Can MarkReg, Workplace or Lite resume without unnecessary re-entry? |
| recovery | Can delay, conflict and non-performance be corrected or replaced? |
| provider value | Do strong providers receive qualified demand and reliable settlement? |
| user capability | Does the user serve more jurisdictions and customers confidently? |
| economics | Does the network create sustainable gross margin after support and risk? |
| governance | Are privacy, authority, bypass and professional-risk incidents controlled? |

Candidate metrics include:

- priority jurisdiction and service coverage;
- eligible-route rate;
- time to Recommended Best Route;
- recommendation acceptance and rematch rate;
- procurement advantage by comparable scope;
- quote validity and price-change rate;
- Provider Acceptance and response time;
- milestone and Evidence completeness;
- on-time completion;
- replacement and refund rate;
- official-fee reconciliation defects;
- repeat demand and provider retention;
- external-route missing-Evidence rate;
- time to restore external-route continuity;
- platform margin after support, failure and financial-risk cost;
- unauthorized disclosure, bypass or funds incidents.

Raw provider count, public traffic, bidding count or transaction volume alone do not prove the Product.

## 27. Non-Goals

MGSN is not:

- a free global provider directory;
- an open bidding marketplace;
- a lowest-price auction;
- a public job board;
- a user-owned provider network;
- an unrestricted provider-contact export tool;
- a peer-to-peer participant network;
- a universal CRM for all external relationships;
- a public one-score provider ranking;
- an autonomous provider appointment engine;
- an unregulated escrow wallet;
- a replacement for Core, Execution, Workplace or MarkReg;
- the owner of every Customer, Order, Matter, Task or official outcome;
- a shortcut around professional responsibility, conflict, Permission, Review or Execution.

## 28. Zero-Tolerance Conditions

A pilot or release must block the affected capability if MGSN:

- exposes one customer's restricted context to another participant without authority;
- represents a provider Claim as verified Evidence;
- presents an ineligible or conflicted provider as available;
- automatically appoints a provider under the ordinary route without required user confirmation;
- releases funds without the required authority or evidence;
- hides material service scope, fee or responsibility changes;
- treats provider self-report or user external report as official truth;
- represents an External Self-Managed Route as platform guaranteed;
- lets margin or paid placement override eligibility or professional risk;
- creates unrestricted participant-to-participant network access;
- loses audit continuity during replacement or refund;
- retries unknown external actions unsafely;
- uses provider count or transaction volume as proof of service quality;
- denies provider correction or appeal where governance requires it.
