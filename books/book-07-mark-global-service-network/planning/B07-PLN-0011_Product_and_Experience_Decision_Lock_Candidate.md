# B07-PLN-0011 — Product and Experience Decision Lock Candidate

## Status

```text
Product Manager and Design Director Review Complete
Candidate for Charter Incorporation
```

## Purpose

This record resolves the principal Product and experience questions raised after the Pre-Writing Audit and unifies `B07-PLN-0002`, `B07-PLN-0003`, `B07-PLN-0006` and `B07-PLN-0010` into one stable Charter direction.

It is a decision-lock candidate, not the final Network and Product Charter.

## 1. Stable Participant Model

The stable network participant is an Organization.

Demand and supply are contextual roles, not mutually exclusive account identities.

```text
Organization Participant
├── Demand Role
│   └── requests a Capability through MGSN
└── Supply Role
    └── provides an admitted Capability through MGSN
```

An Organization may hold both roles simultaneously across different jurisdictions, services and Matters.

Product consequences:

- one Organization identity;
- separate demand-side and supply-side workspaces or modes;
- separate permissions and responsibilities;
- separate payable and receivable ledgers;
- separate conflict and audit contexts;
- no automatic netting or self-dealing.

## 2. Central Hub Remains Non-Negotiable

Contextual roles do not create participant-to-participant network ownership.

```text
Participant private space
→ MGSN-controlled projection
→ MGSN-managed route
→ counterparty-permitted projection
→ MGSN-governed result and Return
```

Participants may communicate where professional delivery requires it, but communication remains purpose-bound and auditable.

No participant gains unrestricted access to the platform provider pool, customer pool, direct contacts, procurement terms or routing infrastructure.

## 3. Supply Portfolio Decision

MGSN should maintain multiple qualified providers for material jurisdiction-and-service combinations.

The platform should avoid permanent single-provider dependency except where regulation, scarcity or commercial reality makes alternatives unavailable.

Internal portfolio roles may include:

```text
Recommended Primary
Qualified Alternative
Specialist
Urgent Capacity
Relationship-Preserved
Backup
Pilot / Testing
Restricted / Suspended
```

Portfolio classification is dynamic and context-specific. It is not a public permanent ranking.

## 4. Default User Option Model

The default user experience is:

```text
1 Recommended Managed Route
+ up to 2 materially differentiated alternatives
```

Alternatives should be shown only when they provide a meaningful trade-off, such as:

- lower total price;
- faster expected handling;
- stronger specialist fit;
- preservation of the user's admitted historical provider;
- different service depth or risk profile.

Routine or highly standardized services may show only one managed package when provider choice has no meaningful user value.

High-risk, urgent or specialist services may show fewer options and require operator review.

The Product must not show a long provider directory or create an auction experience.

## 5. Provider Disclosure Decision

Disclosure should be staged.

### Before route confirmation

Show:

- service package;
- jurisdiction and service capability;
- verified qualification state;
- relevant experience and performance indicators;
- expected timing;
- user-facing price;
- key inclusions, exclusions and risks;
- why the route is recommended;
- whether the route preserves an admitted historical relationship.

Provider Organization identity may be shown where it materially supports professional choice, legal responsibility or historical relationship preservation.

### After route confirmation and before instruction

Show the provider identity and responsible professional information required for:

- conflict review;
- professional responsibility;
- authorization;
- regulated disclosure;
- informed user confirmation.

### Direct contact

Direct contact information remains masked or platform-mediated unless a controlled collaboration stage requires disclosure.

Procurement cost, internal margin, full provider-pool data and routing weights remain platform-private.

## 6. Matching Priority Decision

Matching should apply gates and optimization in this order:

```text
1. legal and policy eligibility
2. capability and professional fit
3. conflict and current availability
4. service risk and continuity
5. historical relationship preference
6. quality and fulfillment evidence
7. timing
8. user-facing price and procurement efficiency
9. platform margin and supply allocation
```

Platform margin must never override eligibility, professional fit or material customer interest.

Lowest price alone must never determine allocation.

## 7. Bidirectional Introduction Decision

Both directions are supported:

```text
Demand participant → introduces provider
Provider participant → introduces demand Organization
```

The invited party independently joins MGSN and completes the applicable admission, verification and commercial process.

Introduction creates Relationship Provenance and attribution. It does not create automatic admission, activation, ownership or permanent exclusivity.

## 8. Introduction Incentive Decision

The initial incentive model should use bounded platform benefits rather than lifetime commissions.

Candidate default:

### Demand participant introduces a provider

After the provider is admitted and completes the first successful MGSN-managed service:

- one-time service credit or transaction-fee discount;
- Relationship-Preserved preference where the provider remains eligible;
- visible introduction attribution inside the participant's private space.

### Provider introduces a demand Organization

After the invited Organization completes its first paid MGSN-managed service:

- one-time settlement bonus, platform service credit or fee reduction;
- introduction attribution;
- no permanent ownership of the invited Organization's future demand.

No incentive is earned for registration alone.

No lifetime commission, perpetual routing right or hidden override of matching is accepted as the default model.

Exact monetary value remains a Commercial Plan decision.

## 9. Relationship Protection Decision

Pre-existing relationships should be respected without becoming permanent monopolies.

### Relationship provenance period

Use a default 12-month attribution period from verified introduction or migration into MGSN, renewable through active successful managed collaboration.

During the attribution period:

- the historical provider is included as a Relationship-Preserved candidate when eligible;
- the introducing party receives agreed attribution or bounded incentive;
- MGSN does not erase the historical relationship;
- better or safer routes may still be recommended.

Relationship protection does not override:

- qualification;
- conflict;
- availability;
- service scope;
- suspension;
- material price or quality disadvantage;
- user choice;
- customer interest.

## 10. Pre-Existing External Relationship and Non-Circumvention

Participants may continue genuine pre-existing business outside MGSN.

The platform should not claim ownership of business that existed independently before platform participation.

However:

```text
MGSN-originated demand
MGSN-negotiated commercial terms
MGSN-generated route or introduction
MGSN-managed Matter context
MGSN funds or fulfillment protection
```

must remain inside the managed platform route for the applicable service and protection period.

Pre-existing scope should be declared and evidenced when non-circumvention treatment matters.

Using a pre-existing relationship outside MGSN means the user accepts External Self-Managed Route consequences.

## 11. Access After Introduction

### Provider-introduced demand Organization

After independent onboarding, the invited Organization may access the wider MGSN capability network according to its Product plan, permissions and commercial eligibility.

The introducing provider does not own or control the invited Organization's future global demand.

### Demand-introduced provider

After full platform admission, the provider may serve other eligible demand participants.

The introducing demand Organization does not own the provider's MGSN supply profile or future platform work.

Relationship provenance remains visible and may influence preference only within governed limits.

## 12. Dual-Role Financial and Permission Separation

A dual-role Organization must have logically separate contexts for:

```text
Demand-side payment obligation
Supply-side settlement entitlement
Official-fee funds
Platform credit
Refund and adjustment
Provider deduction
Commission or introduction incentive
```

Rules:

- no automatic offset between payable and receivable balances;
- no self-allocation to the same Organization or controlled affiliate without disclosure and operator approval;
- no use of supply-side access to inspect demand-side customer data;
- no manipulation of Trust through related-party work;
- related-party transactions require explicit audit flags;
- conflict and professional independence remain mandatory.

## 13. Product Experience Structure

A dual-role Organization should use one Organization identity with two controlled operating modes:

```text
Organization Space
├── Request Services
│   ├── Needs and Routes
│   ├── Offers and Payments
│   ├── Managed Matters
│   └── Returns and Exceptions
└── Provide Services
    ├── Capabilities and Coverage
    ├── Invitations and Availability
    ├── Assigned Work
    ├── Delivery Evidence
    └── Settlement and Performance
```

The interface should make the active role unmistakable through navigation, labels, permissions and financial context.

Do not use one mixed dashboard that combines purchasing and supplying actions without role context.

## 14. Network Growth Principle

MGSN should grow by converting fragmented bilateral cooperation into managed network participation because it provides superior operational value:

- standardized intake;
- better document quality;
- lower procurement cost;
- protected funds;
- milestone visibility;
- structured status Return;
- reconciliation;
- replacement and dispute governance;
- broader global capability.

The platform's network effect is:

```text
More managed demand
→ stronger procurement and provider economics
→ more qualified supply
→ broader and more resilient capability
→ better user results
→ more managed demand
```

## 15. Decisions Recommended for Charter Lock

```text
CD-01 — Organization is the stable participant; demand and supply are contextual roles.
CD-02 — All MGSN-managed relationships remain hub-mediated.
CD-03 — MGSN maintains multi-provider supply portfolios.
CD-04 — Default presentation is one recommendation plus up to two meaningful alternatives.
CD-05 — Provider disclosure is staged; direct contacts and procurement data remain controlled.
CD-06 — Matching gates professional and risk factors before price and margin.
CD-07 — Bidirectional introduction is supported through independent onboarding.
CD-08 — Introduction incentives are bounded and event-based, not perpetual commissions.
CD-09 — Relationship provenance receives a default 12-month attribution period without monopoly.
CD-10 — Genuine pre-existing business may remain external; MGSN-originated business remains managed.
CD-11 — Introduced participants may access the wider network after independent admission.
CD-12 — Dual-role finance, permission, conflict and audit contexts remain separated.
```

## 16. Remaining Charter Questions

The Charter Candidate still needs to decide:

- MGSN's legal role by service model;
- formal ownership of allocation, instruction, milestone and closure records;
- legal funds holder and release model;
- initial service and jurisdiction scope;
- initial participant cohort;
- operator-assisted versus self-service boundaries;
- exact Commercial Plan values for credits, discounts and bonuses;
- enforceable non-circumvention language by jurisdiction;
- exact identity-disclosure rules for regulated service categories.
