## 2. Purpose

This matrix isolates the recommended Owner decisions required to make the MGSN Charter operationally coherent without prematurely accepting a Product Baseline, schema, API, payment model or manuscript.

## 3. Recommended Decisions

### OD-01 — Network and Product identity

```text
MGSN is the platform-owned and platform-governed
managed global professional service network.
```

Decision: **ACCEPT RECOMMENDED**

### OD-02 — User-facing value proposition

```text
MGSN enables a locally limited professional or agency
to deliver services globally through one governed platform interface.
```

Decision: **ACCEPT RECOMMENDED**

### OD-03 — Network topology

```text
Demand-side participant → MGSN ← Provider participant
```

MGSN does not create an independent participant-to-participant network graph.

Decision: **ACCEPT RECOMMENDED**

### OD-04 — Platform resource ownership

The platform owns or governs:

- provider supply;
- admission and verification;
- procurement terms;
- matching and routing intelligence;
- platform Trust and performance evidence;
- funds and fulfillment controls;
- network interfaces and operating rules.

Decision: **ACCEPT RECOMMENDED**

### OD-05 — Participant private spaces

Demand-side and provider-side participants retain independent private spaces.

Use of MGSN does not transfer all customer, provider, private Knowledge or internal business assets to the other participant.

Decision: **ACCEPT RECOMMENDED**

### OD-06 — Provider model

```text
Core Organization identity
+ MGSN Provider Profile
+ service-specific Provider role
```

A provider is not only a contact row and is not a platform-independent universal seller object.

Decision: **ACCEPT RECOMMENDED**

### OD-07 — Primary demand-side user

Initial priority:

1. Chinese trademark agencies handling international work;
2. independent international trademark professionals;
3. Lite, Workplace and MarkReg users with authorized customer service needs.

Decision: **ACCEPT RECOMMENDED**

### OD-08 — Initial provider cohort

Initial priority:

1. existing platform foreign associates;
2. proven high-volume filing providers;
3. selected verified law firms and specialist counsel.

Decision: **ACCEPT RECOMMENDED**

### OD-09 — Initial service scope

Initial priority:

- international trademark filing;
- filing through registration where a stable package exists;
- selected high-volume post-filing and registration services;
- operator-assisted specialist matters.

Decision: **ACCEPT RECOMMENDED**

### OD-10 — Three-route model

```text
R1 — External Self-Managed Route
R2 — MGSN Recommended Managed Route
R3 — MGSN Managed Preferred-Provider Route
```

Decision: **ACCEPT RECOMMENDED**

### OD-11 — External Self-Managed Route

A user may keep an external provider outside MGSN without importing that provider into Lite or Workplace.

The user bears provider, payment, follow-up and Evidence-return responsibility. No MGSN procurement, funds, replacement or fulfillment guarantee is implied.

Decision: **ACCEPT RECOMMENDED**

### OD-12 — Default managed route

When the user lacks a suitable external route or chooses platform capability, R2 is the default Product experience.

Decision: **ACCEPT RECOMMENDED**

### OD-13 — Matching output

The default Product presents:

```text
one Recommended Best Route
+ main reasons and constraints
+ rematch or preference request where permitted
+ external route option
```

The complete provider pool is not a constitutional user-facing requirement.

Decision: **ACCEPT RECOMMENDED**

### OD-14 — User confirmation

Ordinary MVP services require user confirmation before provider allocation.

```text
Automatic matching ≠ automatic appointment
```

Decision: **ACCEPT RECOMMENDED**

### OD-15 — Standing allocation mandate

Standing authorization is deferred as a later policy-bounded option for standardized services only.

It is not the ordinary Charter default.

Decision: **ACCEPT RECOMMENDED**

### OD-16 — Preferred provider

A user may request a preferred provider, but preference does not override:

- admission;
- qualification;
- Capability;
- conflict;
- availability;
- procurement;
- service package;
- performance and risk controls.

Decision: **ACCEPT RECOMMENDED**

### OD-17 — Provider identity disclosure

Provider identity and qualification are disclosed progressively and sufficiently for professional responsibility, conflict, client disclosure and informed confirmation.

Direct contact and procurement terms may remain stage-limited.

Decision: **ACCEPT RECOMMENDED**

### OD-18 — Primary commercial model

```text
platform wholesale network
+ managed-service packages
```

The applicable model is typed by Product edition and service package.

Decision: **ACCEPT RECOMMENDED**

### OD-19 — Pricing layers

```text
provider procurement cost
+ official fees / disbursements
+ platform service and risk cost
+ demand-side margin where permitted
= customer-facing offer
```

These layers must remain distinguishable.

Decision: **ACCEPT RECOMMENDED**

### OD-20 — Price advantage

MGSN will pursue lower or more stable price and stronger scope through aggregated demand and negotiated procurement.

This is a strategic target, not a universal lowest-price guarantee.

Decision: **ACCEPT RECOMMENDED**

### OD-21 — Routing neutrality

Margin, procurement tier and network strategy may inform matching but may not silently override:

- eligibility;
- conflict;
- professional fit;
- deadline feasibility;
- customer interest;
- unacceptable delivery or financial risk.

Decision: **ACCEPT RECOMMENDED**

### OD-22 — Funds-control principle

Platform-managed routes require typed funds collection, purpose allocation, release, settlement, refund and reconciliation controls.

Funds release must not rely only on provider assertion when consequential Evidence is available.

Decision: **ACCEPT RECOMMENDED**

### OD-23 — Legal characterization

The Charter does not universally characterize MGSN as escrow, trust account, payment institution, principal, agent or marketplace.

The legal role, funds holder and accounting model must be typed by Product edition, service and jurisdiction after specialist review.

Decision: **ACCEPT RECOMMENDED**

### OD-24 — Managed fulfillment record

The controlled baseline should evaluate an MGSN-local `Network Service Engagement` or equivalent projection for route, allocation, Provider Acceptance, milestones, funds references, incidents, replacement and Return.

It must not replace Order, Matter, Task, Workflow, Payment ledger, Execution or official outcome.

Decision: **ACCEPT RECOMMENDED**

### OD-25 — Communication model

MGSN may provide platform messaging, relay, controlled named-professional Communication and operator coordination.

Communication inside an engagement does not create unrestricted participant-to-participant network ownership.

Decision: **ACCEPT RECOMMENDED**

### OD-26 — Replacement and continuity

Managed routes may support provider replacement under governed conditions while preserving Documents, audit, funds and user notice.

External routes receive no implied replacement guarantee.

Decision: **ACCEPT RECOMMENDED**

### OD-27 — Trust model

MGSN Trust is multidimensional, source-aware, time-scoped and service-specific.

It is not one universal public score.

Decision: **ACCEPT RECOMMENDED**

### OD-28 — Operator-assisted MVP

MVP 0 is operator-assisted.

AI and automation support need typing, eligibility, comparison, recommendation and monitoring. Human operators retain admission, complex procurement, exception, funds-release review, dispute, replacement and suspension responsibilities.

Decision: **ACCEPT RECOMMENDED**

### OD-29 — Primary MVP proof

MVP 0 must prove:

```text
service need
→ defensible Recommended Best Route
→ user confirmation
→ Provider Acceptance
→ funds and milestone control
→ sourced completion Return
```

Decision: **ACCEPT RECOMMENDED**

### OD-30 — External route continuity bridge

The Product should support expected milestone reminders, manual upload, source classification, stale-state warning and conflict reconciliation for R1.

User-reported status must not become official truth by presentation.

Decision: **ACCEPT RECOMMENDED**

### OD-31 — Network non-circumvention

MGSN may use staged contact disclosure, controlled channels, contractual non-circumvention and loss-of-protection consequences.

Any restriction requires legal and commercial review and may not claim ownership of people or Organizations.

Decision: **ACCEPT RECOMMENDED**

### OD-32 — Product success evidence

Success is measured by:

- coverage;
- procurement advantage;
- route quality and acceptance;
- Provider Acceptance and timeliness;
- funds safety;
- fulfillment and Evidence;
- correction and replacement;
- user global-capability expansion;
- provider value and retention;
- sustainable margin;
- governance defects.

Provider count, page views and bidding volume alone are insufficient.

Decision: **ACCEPT RECOMMENDED**

### OD-33 — Next gate

Owner merge authorizes preparation of the controlled MGSN Product Baseline, records, journeys, scenarios, Handoffs and MVP evaluation model.

Owner merge does not authorize Chapter Map acceptance, manuscript, schema, API, payment implementation or production deployment.

Decision: **ACCEPT RECOMMENDED**

## 4. Decisions Deferred Beyond Charter

The following remain implementation or policy decisions rather than Charter blockers:

- exact contracting legal entity;
- payment-provider architecture;
- client-money and escrow characterization;
- jurisdiction-specific tax and invoicing;
- foreign-exchange and settlement mechanics;
- exact provider-identity disclosure fields;
- exact matching weights;
- precise resale corridors and margin rules;
- service-specific milestone schedules;
- detailed non-circumvention terms;
- database and API design;
- production automation level.

They must conform to OD-01–OD-33.
