## Required terminology replacement

### Demand side

Replace generic `demand-side user` where formal architecture matters with:

```text
Originating Workplace
```

The Originating Workplace owns or controls:

- its Customer relationship;
- its Order/Matter context;
- its customer-facing commercial responsibility;
- its internal pricing and margin;
- its decision and approval context;
- the authorized demand projection;
- returned Evidence and lifecycle continuity.

### Provider side

Use:

```text
Execution Provider Workplace
```

The Execution Provider Workplace owns or controls:

- provider-side operations;
- assigned professional responsibility;
- provider-side work records;
- delivery Evidence;
- qualification and conflict responsibility;
- accepted instruction execution.

### Network layer

Use:

```text
MGSN Connection / Gateway
≠ MGSN Network
```

A Workplace owns its Connection configuration and authorized projections. The platform owns or governs the MGSN Network, provider supply, procurement, routing, funds, fulfillment and platform Trust.

## Reconciled topology

```text
Customer / business need
→ Originating Workplace
→ authorized MGSN demand projection
→ MGSN matching, procurement and funds governance
→ controlled provider allocation and acceptance
→ Execution Provider Workplace
→ delivery Evidence and typed Return
→ Originating Workplace
→ customer and Matter continuity
```

## Route reconciliation

### R1 — External Self-Managed Route

The Originating Workplace may use an external provider outside MGSN. It retains coordination and payment responsibility and restores continuity through manually submitted source-qualified Evidence.

### R2 — Recommended Managed Route

MGSN selects from its platform-governed supply portfolio and presents one Recommended Route plus bounded alternatives where useful. The Originating Workplace confirms or rejects the route before ordinary allocation.

### R3 — Managed Preferred-Provider Route

The Originating Workplace may express a preference based on prior relationship history. The provider must still pass MGSN admission, capability, conflict, availability, procurement and risk gates.

## Formal authority chain

```text
Originating Workplace business sovereignty
≠ MGSN routing authority
≠ Owning Service formal-state authority
≠ Execution Provider professional responsibility
```

No layer silently acquires the others' authority.

## Handoff and Return direction

Book 07 baseline work must define:

- authorized demand projection;
- provider candidate and route recommendation;
- accountable user confirmation;
- allocation candidate;
- Provider Acceptance;
- purpose-limited Handoff;
- fulfillment observations and Evidence;
- typed Return;
- correction, replacement and unknown outcomes.

```text
projection ≠ formal instruction
recommendation ≠ allocation
allocation ≠ Provider Acceptance
Handoff ≠ ownership transfer
Return ≠ automatic formal-state mutation
```

## Participant role reconciliation

An Organization may act in demand and supply roles in different service contexts, but every managed engagement must identify one Originating Workplace and one or more authorized Execution Provider Workplaces.

Dual role does not create a peer-to-peer network edge and does not permit self-routing without affiliation disclosure and policy approval.
