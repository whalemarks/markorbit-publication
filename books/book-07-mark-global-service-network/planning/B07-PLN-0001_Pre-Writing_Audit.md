# B07-PLN-0001 — Pre-Writing Audit

## Status

```text
Draft for Owner Review
```

## Purpose

This audit establishes the starting point for Book 07 without prematurely accepting a Product Charter, controlled record baseline, Chapter Map or manuscript.

The audit corrects two opposite failure modes:

```text
Failure A
MGSN becomes an open provider marketplace
with weak control, fragmented procurement,
uncontrolled funds and platform bypass.

Failure B
MGSN becomes a passive user-owned contact manager
that cannot create platform purchasing power,
provider quality, stable fulfillment or network effects.
```

The working direction is a platform-owned, relatively closed and governed professional service network.

## Starting Proposition

Users and participating Organizations do not independently possess complete global service capability.

They obtain that capability through MGSN:

```text
local or limited professional capability
→ platform network access
→ global capability coverage
→ governed service procurement
→ funds and fulfillment protection
→ collaboration outcome
→ stronger future network capability
```

MGSN therefore creates an extended operating capability for the user, but the network resources, provider pool, procurement mechanisms, routing interfaces and settlement controls remain platform-governed resources.

## Platform Value Creation

MGSN creates value by combining:

- global provider recruitment and retention;
- capability and jurisdiction coverage;
- negotiated procurement and pricing leverage;
- repeat demand aggregation;
- routing and allocation intelligence;
- funds custody, staged release and settlement discipline;
- fulfillment monitoring;
- dispute, correction and provider suspension mechanisms;
- accumulated network evidence;
- consistent Product and API interfaces;
- continuity when an individual provider becomes unavailable.

This is not free infrastructure. Commercial sustainability is a constitutional requirement, although specific fees and revenue models remain hypotheses until accepted.

## User Value

The user receives:

- access to jurisdictions and services outside the user's own capability;
- reduced provider-search cost;
- more stable prices and clearer service scope;
- reduced risk of paying without performance;
- platform-backed provider replacement and escalation;
- standardized collaboration and reporting;
- improved confidence in unfamiliar jurisdictions;
- continuously expanding global service reach.

The user may experience this as possessing a global cooperation network, but this experience is enabled by MGSN rather than created through unrestricted ownership of the underlying supply.

## Platform Control Locks

Candidate constitutional locks:

```text
MGSN resources are platform resources.

Network access does not transfer
provider ownership or platform data ownership.

Platform procurement may aggregate demand
and negotiate supply terms.

Platform routing interfaces are controlled.

Funds and settlement may be governed
to reduce non-performance and financial risk.

Provider access may be staged,
masked or mediated where required.

Direct bypass must not silently destroy
platform economics, auditability or risk controls.

User choice remains bounded by
eligibility, policy, service model and platform controls.
```

## Closed-Network Direction

MGSN should be relatively closed in operation:

- providers enter through admission, verification or invitation;
- service demand is not publicly broadcast by default;
- provider identities and commercial terms may be disclosed progressively;
- platform-managed procurement can remain opaque where disclosure would weaken negotiation or encourage bypass;
- payments and release conditions can be controlled by the platform;
- provider performance and incidents feed platform governance;
- suspended providers can be removed from future routing;
- collaboration records remain traceable.

Closed does not mean arbitrary or unaccountable. The platform still requires explanation, audit, dispute procedures, user-facing transparency and fair provider governance.

## Relationship to the Orbit Principle

Organization autonomy remains relevant, but it must be interpreted correctly.

The Orbit principle protects:

- the user's own client relationship;
- the user's organization identity;
- the user's internal data and policy;
- the user's professional judgment;
- the user's commercial model toward its customer;
- the provider's independent professional obligations.

It does not require:

- user ownership of MGSN supply;
- unrestricted direct contact;
- unrestricted provider export;
- unrestricted disclosure of platform purchasing terms;
- user control of network ranking or routing infrastructure;
- absence of platform funds control;
- absence of platform commercial participation.

## Key Product Tension

Book 07 must design a stable balance among:

```text
Platform control
+ user trust
+ provider attractiveness
+ procurement advantage
+ financial protection
+ professional independence
+ service continuity
```

Excessive openness weakens platform control and risk management.

Excessive opacity weakens user trust and provider confidence.

The Product must reveal enough to support informed decisions while protecting network economics, procurement leverage and operational integrity.
