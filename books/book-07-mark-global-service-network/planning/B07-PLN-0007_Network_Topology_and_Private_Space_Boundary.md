# B07-PLN-0007 — Network Topology and Private-Space Boundary

## Status

```text
Owner Direction Incorporated — Draft for Review
```

## Purpose

This record fixes the network topology and clarifies the meaning of private space.

## Canonical Topology Candidate

MGSN is a platform-centered network.

```text
Demand-side Workplace / Lite
            ↓
          MGSN
            ↑
Provider Workplace / Provider Interface
```

Every demand-side participant connects to MGSN.

Every service provider connects to MGSN.

MGSN does not create or endorse a direct peer-to-peer network among demand users and service providers.

The platform remains the network hub, service interface, procurement controller, funds controller and fulfillment coordinator.

## Hub-and-Spoke Principle

```text
Demand User A ─┐
Demand User B ─┼→ MGSN ←┬─ Provider X
Demand User C ─┘         ├─ Provider Y
                          └─ Provider Z
```

Not:

```text
Demand User A ↔ Provider X
Demand User B ↔ Provider Y
Provider X ↔ Provider Y
User A ↔ User B
```

Any permitted direct communication is a controlled interaction within an MGSN-governed collaboration, not creation of an independent cross-participant network relationship.

## Meaning of Private Space

`Private` refers to the participant's own operating space:

```text
Organization Workplace
or
MarkOrbit Lite personal / small-team space
```

The private space may contain:

- customers;
- customer contacts;
- leads and opportunities;
- internal relationships;
- internal pricing and sales strategy;
- documents and working materials;
- private knowledge and memory;
- user preferences;
- internal approvals;
- service history;
- MGSN requests and returned results;
- organization-specific notes about platform services.

These records remain inside the user's Workplace or Lite boundary according to authorization and Product rules.

Private space does not mean:

- a user-owned MGSN provider pool;
- a user-owned global agent network;
- an unrestricted list of provider contacts;
- direct provider export;
- user-controlled provider routing infrastructure;
- a hidden peer-to-peer marketplace;
- ownership of MGSN procurement terms, Trust signals or platform network data.

## Provider Private Space

A provider may also operate through its own Workplace or controlled provider interface.

Provider-private information may include:

- internal team and workload;
- internal methods;
- internal cost structure;
- documents and professional work;
- private operating notes;
- internal approvals;
- evidence not approved for network display.

The provider connects these permitted projections to MGSN.

The provider does not gain direct access to demand-side private spaces.

## MGSN Exchange Model

Cross-party exchange occurs through MGSN-controlled records and interfaces:

```text
Demand-side authorized projection
→ MGSN service and collaboration context
→ provider-side permitted projection
→ provider response and delivery
→ MGSN validation, funds and fulfillment controls
→ demand-side Return
```

MGSN determines, under accepted policy and service model:

- which information is required;
- when it is disclosed;
- which participant may see it;
- whether direct communication is enabled;
- how funds move;
- which evidence proves a milestone;
- when a provider may be replaced;
- how the result returns to each private space.

## Relationship Model

The stable relationship graph is:

```text
Demand-side participant ↔ MGSN
Provider participant ↔ MGSN
```

Not:

```text
Demand-side participant ↔ Provider participant
```

A provider may perform work for a demand-side user's customer, but the network relationship remains mediated and governed by MGSN.

The platform may permit named professional interaction where necessary for service quality, conflict checking or professional responsibility. Such interaction does not create an unrestricted independent network connection.

## Why the Hub Must Remain Controlled

The platform-centered topology protects:

- procurement leverage;
- service consistency;
- provider quality;
- customer confidentiality;
- funds and settlement safety;
- milestone evidence;
- audit and reconciliation;
- provider replacement;
- non-circumvention;
- interface stability;
- accumulated platform capability;
- long-term network economics.

If participants can freely convert every MGSN interaction into an independent direct relationship, MGSN loses:

- purchasing power;
- fulfillment visibility;
- financial protection;
- quality control;
- accumulated evidence;
- ability to guarantee continuity;
- commercial sustainability.

## User Autonomy Under Hub Control

The user retains authority inside the user's private space and over defined business decisions.

Candidate user authorities include:

- customer ownership and management;
- definition of customer objective;
- approval of data projection into MGSN;
- customer-facing pricing where the Product model permits;
- acceptance of the platform offer;
- required professional or commercial approvals;
- review of outcome;
- correction and dispute request.

The user does not control:

- the provider pool;
- provider admission;
- platform procurement terms;
- routing infrastructure;
- funds-release rules;
- provider settlement;
- platform Trust records;
- provider suspension;
- network interface contracts.

## Orbit Reconciliation

The Orbit model applies at the private-space boundary:

```text
Each participant operates in its own Workplace or Lite space.
Each participant connects to MGSN through controlled projections.
MGSN coordinates the network interaction.
Participants do not merge Workplaces and do not form an uncontrolled lateral network.
```

The phrase `MGSN connects independent Workplaces` should therefore be interpreted as:

> MGSN is the controlled hub through which independent Workplaces access shared global capability.

It must not be interpreted as direct Workplace-to-Workplace connectivity.

## Charter Lock Candidate

```text
All demand-side and provider-side participants connect to MGSN.

MGSN does not support an independent peer-to-peer network among participants.

Private space means the participant's own Workplace or Lite operating boundary.

MGSN resources, provider supply, procurement, routing, funds, fulfillment and network evidence remain platform-governed.

Cross-party information and work move only through controlled MGSN interfaces and permitted collaboration states.
```
