## Purpose

This record separates platform ownership, user authority, provider responsibility and external formal authority.

It prevents both platform under-control and platform overreach.

## Three-Layer Ownership Model

### Layer 1 — Platform Network Assets

Includes candidate:

- provider pool;
- network coverage;
- capability classifications;
- admission and verification processes;
- procurement terms;
- routing intelligence;
- platform trust signals;
- funds and fulfillment infrastructure;
- aggregate performance evidence;
- service interfaces;
- network operating rules.

### Layer 2 — User Organization Assets

Includes:

- user customer relationship;
- user internal pricing and sales strategy;
- private notes and preferences;
- internal approvals;
- internal client data and documents;
- organization-specific experience;
- customer-facing goodwill.

Use of MGSN does not automatically transfer these assets to the platform beyond the authorized service purpose.

### Layer 3 — Provider Assets and Duties

Includes:

- provider organization identity;
- professional qualification;
- provider staff and internal operations;
- independent professional judgment;
- proprietary methods and internal knowledge;
- rights in submitted evidence, subject to platform use terms;
- duty to perform accepted work.

Platform participation grants controlled network use, not unrestricted ownership of the provider organization.

## Platform Control Does Not Mean Total Platform Ownership

The platform may own or control the network system while still respecting:

- personal-data rights;
- provider evidence rights;
- user customer confidentiality;
- professional privilege;
- local regulatory restrictions;
- accounting separation;
- provider correction and dispute rights;
- official authority ownership of formal outcomes.

## Interface-Control Principle

The platform should control interfaces that change network, funds or fulfillment state.

Candidate controlled interfaces include:

```text
provider admission
capability publication
routing request
candidate disclosure
commercial offer
funds collection
provider instruction
milestone evidence
funds release
settlement
incident report
replacement
suspension
```

Controlled interfaces support:

- version stability;
- authorization;
- audit;
- idempotency;
- duplicate prevention;
- service consistency;
- dispute reconstruction;
- fraud and bypass control.

## Funds-Control Principle

The purpose of funds control is not merely platform revenue.

It also reduces:

- advance-payment loss;
- provider non-performance;
- unauthorized fee changes;
- official-fee misuse;
- incomplete delivery;
- payment and invoice disputes;
- cross-border reconciliation failures;
- service abandonment;
- user misuse of provider credit;
- platform reputation risk.

The exact legal and technical funds model remains unresolved and must be reviewed by jurisdiction, regulation, accounting and payment specialists before implementation.

## Open Ownership Questions

The following remain unresolved:

- whether the platform legally owns all provider profile data or holds licensed/governed rights;
- whether user-introduced providers become general MGSN supply;
- which provider identity and contact fields may remain masked;
- whether a user may continue directly with a provider after an MGSN collaboration;
- which non-circumvention terms are commercially and legally appropriate;
- whether MGSN operates as principal, agent, marketplace operator or managed-service provider for each service model;
- which entity holds customer funds;
- when revenue, liability and provider cost are recognized;
- when the user receives provider identity and direct communication rights;
- whether provider selection is user-facing or platform-managed by service category.
