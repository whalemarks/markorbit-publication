# B07-PLN-0003 — Authority, Ownership and Control Matrix

## Status

```text
Draft for Owner Review
```

## Purpose

This record separates:

- platform ownership and control;
- demand-side private-space authority;
- provider private-space responsibility;
- external self-managed route responsibility;
- official and Owning Service authority.

It prevents platform under-control, platform overreach and ambiguity about who is responsible when work leaves the MGSN-managed route.

## Topology Lock

```text
Demand-side Workplace / Lite
            ↓
          MGSN
            ↑
Provider Workplace / Provider Interface
```

Demand-side and provider-side participants connect to MGSN.

MGSN does not create an independent peer-to-peer network among them.

An external self-managed provider route may exist outside this topology, but only a bounded projection and user-supplied evidence return into Workplace, Lite or MarkReg.

## Three-Layer Ownership Model

### Layer 1 — Platform Network Assets

Includes candidate:

- provider pool;
- network topology;
- jurisdiction and service coverage;
- capability classifications;
- admission and verification processes;
- procurement terms;
- matching and routing intelligence;
- platform Trust signals;
- funds and fulfillment infrastructure;
- aggregate performance evidence;
- service interfaces;
- network operating rules.

### Layer 2 — Demand-Side Private-Space Assets

Includes:

- customer relationship;
- customer contacts;
- leads and Opportunities;
- internal relationships;
- customer-facing pricing and sales strategy;
- private notes and preferences;
- internal approvals;
- client data and Documents;
- organization-specific experience;
- customer-facing goodwill;
- MGSN requests and returned results inside private space;
- declared external routes and uploaded evidence.

This layer does not include ownership of the MGSN provider pool or routing infrastructure.

### Layer 3 — Provider Private-Space Assets and Duties

Includes:

- provider organization identity;
- professional qualification;
- provider people and operations;
- independent professional judgment;
- proprietary methods and Knowledge;
- rights in submitted Evidence, subject to platform use terms;
- duty to perform accepted work.

Platform participation grants governed connection to MGSN, not unrestricted access to demand-side participants.

## Platform Control Does Not Mean Total Platform Ownership

The platform may own and control MGSN while respecting:

- personal-data rights;
- provider Evidence rights;
- customer confidentiality;
- professional privilege;
- provider professional independence;
- local regulatory restrictions;
- accounting separation;
- correction and dispute rights;
- official authority over formal outcomes.

## Interface-Control Principle

The platform should control every interface that changes managed network, funds or fulfillment state:

```text
participant connection
provider admission
capability publication
routing request
recommended route
user confirmation
provider allocation
candidate disclosure
commercial offer
Communication enablement
funds collection
provider instruction
milestone evidence
funds release
settlement
incident
replacement
suspension
Return to private space
```

External-route manual entry remains a separate projection interface and must not mutate MGSN provider or fulfillment state.

Controlled interfaces support:

- authorization;
- audit;
- version stability;
- idempotency;
- duplicate prevention;
- fraud and bypass control;
- service consistency;
- dispute reconstruction;
- prevention of unauthorized lateral networking.

## Funds-Control Principle

Funds control reduces:

- advance-payment loss;
- provider non-performance;
- unauthorized fee changes;
- official-fee misuse;
- incomplete delivery;
- payment and invoice disputes;
- cross-border reconciliation failures;
- service abandonment;
- misuse of provider credit;
- platform reputation risk.

The exact legal funds model remains unresolved and requires jurisdiction, regulation, accounting and payment review.

## External Route Consequence Lock

```text
Freedom to use an external route
≠ MGSN responsibility for that route

Manual evidence upload
≠ provider-confirmed status

User-reported status
≠ official truth

Same provider identity
≠ same service route or guarantee
```

## Open Ownership Questions

The Charter Candidate must still determine:

- legal rights in provider profile and Evidence data;
- whether a user-suggested provider may become general MGSN supply;
- provider identity and contact disclosure stages;
- permitted Communication inside managed collaboration;
- non-circumvention terms;
- platform legal role by service model;
- funds holder and settlement entity;
- revenue, liability and provider-cost recognition;
- provider-selection visibility by service category;
- whether standing user authorization may permit platform allocation without per-service confirmation;
- minimum evidence required to reconnect an external route to MarkReg or Workplace continuity;
- whether the user may withhold external provider identity completely.

## Gate

This matrix may support the Book 07 Charter Candidate after Owner acceptance.

It does not authorize implementation, payment custody, autonomous allocation, provider appointment or production deployment.
