## Purpose

`B07-PLN-0006` preserved the broad question set identified during the Pre-Writing Audit. Subsequent Owner discussion and Product/Design review resolved a material portion of that set.

This record is the current open-question register for the Network and Product Charter Candidate.

Where this record conflicts with an unresolved label in `B07-PLN-0006`, this record controls.

## A. Questions Now Resolved for Charter Drafting

The following directions should no longer be treated as open:

1. Organization is the stable participant; demand and supply are contextual roles.
2. An Organization may request and provide services simultaneously.
3. MGSN remains a central platform hub; dual roles do not create lateral networking.
4. MGSN should maintain multiple providers for material jurisdiction-and-service combinations.
5. Default presentation is one Recommended Route plus up to two materially differentiated alternatives.
6. Routine standardized services may present one managed package when provider choice has little user value.
7. Provider identity and contacts use staged disclosure.
8. Professional eligibility, conflict, availability and risk precede price and margin.
9. Demand and provider participants may introduce existing counterparties.
10. Introduced parties complete independent onboarding and admission.
11. Introductions create Relationship Provenance, not automatic ownership or permanent routing rights.
12. Initial invitation incentives should be bounded, event-based credits, discounts or bonuses rather than lifetime commissions.
13. Relationship provenance receives a default 12-month attribution period, renewable through successful managed cooperation.
14. Genuine pre-existing business may remain outside MGSN.
15. MGSN-originated demand, negotiated terms, managed Matters and platform-protected fulfillment remain inside MGSN.
16. After independent admission, introduced demand Organizations may access the wider MGSN according to Product eligibility.
17. After full admission, introduced providers may serve other eligible demand participants.
18. Dual-role payables, receivables, permissions, conflicts and audit contexts remain separated.
19. No automatic netting of demand-side obligations and supply-side entitlements.
20. Related-party or self-routing requires disclosure and operator approval.

## B. Constitutional Questions Still Open

### CQ-01 — Platform legal and commercial role

Determine the platform role by service category, including principal, reseller, disclosed agent, managed-service provider, payment coordinator or another reviewed structure.

### CQ-02 — Formal object ownership

Determine ownership for:

- provider allocation;
- provider instruction;
- Provider Acceptance;
- collaboration status;
- delivery milestone;
- replacement;
- settlement eligibility;
- closure.

MGSN must not duplicate Order, Matter, Task, Workflow or Execution authority.

### CQ-03 — Provider profile ownership

Determine the relationship among:

- canonical Organization;
- contextual Supply Role;
- platform-owned MGSN Provider Profile;
- Capability Claim;
- verification and performance projections.

### CQ-04 — Standing managed-service mandate

Determine whether selected routine service packages may permit platform allocation without a separate provider-choice confirmation for each transaction after the user accepts a standing mandate.

### CQ-05 — Legal funds holder

Determine which entity or regulated payment partner receives, safeguards and releases funds.

### CQ-06 — Funds release and settlement model

Determine service-specific rules for:

- official-fee release;
- milestone release;
- completion release;
- provider credit;
- refunds;
- replacement;
- deductions;
- reconciliation.

### CQ-07 — Customer-facing pricing authority

Determine where MGSN provides:

- fixed managed-service packages;
- recommended resale price;
- permitted price corridor;
- user-defined markup;
- different pricing rules by Product edition or service category.

### CQ-08 — Exact disclosure by service category

Determine which categories require provider identity and responsible professional disclosure before final confirmation, and which may remain package-led until instruction.

### CQ-09 — Enforceable non-circumvention model

Determine contractual scope, duration, territorial enforceability, pre-existing relationship evidence and consequences for platform-originated business.

### CQ-10 — Initial service scope

Select the initial combination of jurisdictions and services for MVP 0.

### CQ-11 — Initial participant cohort

Select the initial demand and supply cohort, likely beginning with existing professional relationships and high-volume international trademark services.

### CQ-12 — Operator assistance boundary

Determine which activities remain operator-assisted in MVP 0:

- provider recruitment;
- procurement negotiation;
- verification;
- matching exceptions;
- urgent allocation;
- funds release;
- replacement;
- dispute handling.

### CQ-13 — Commercial Plan values

Set, outside the constitutional Charter where appropriate:

- introduction credit;
- provider bonus;
- service-fee discount;
- attribution renewal conditions;
- plan eligibility;
- transaction thresholds.

### CQ-14 — External-route continuity minimum

Determine minimum evidence for resuming lifecycle continuity after External Self-Managed work.

### CQ-15 — Related-party and self-routing policy

Define affiliation, disclosure, approval, pricing, conflict and Trust-treatment rules where the demand and supply roles involve the same Organization or related Organizations.

## C. Recommended Charter Candidate Structure

The Charter should resolve CQ-01–CQ-12 at a constitutional or bounded-policy level.

CQ-13 should remain a Commercial Plan decision unless a value affects constitutional fairness or routing neutrality.

CQ-14 and CQ-15 require explicit Charter principles and may later receive implementation specifications.
