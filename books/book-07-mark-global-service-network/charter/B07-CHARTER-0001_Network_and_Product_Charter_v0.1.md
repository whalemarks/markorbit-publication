# B07-CHARTER-0001 — Network and Product Charter v0.1

## Status

```text
Candidate — Ready for Owner Review
```

## 1. Product Identity

Mark Global Service Network (`MGSN`) is the platform-owned and platform-governed global professional service capability network through which demand-side users obtain services they cannot efficiently provide through their own direct coverage.

```text
Platform-governed supply
+ aggregated demand
+ unified procurement
+ capability and eligibility controls
+ recommended best-route matching
+ bounded user decision
+ controlled funds and fulfillment
+ provider performance evolution
= reliable global professional service capability
```

MGSN is not a free directory, peer-to-peer network, open bidding market or user-owned provider database.

## 2. Constitutional Topology

```text
Demand-side Workplace / Lite
            ↓
          MGSN
            ↑
Provider Workplace / Provider Interface
```

Demand-side users and providers connect to MGSN. They do not acquire an unrestricted network right to connect directly with one another.

Permitted professional communication may occur inside an MGSN-managed collaboration. Such communication does not transfer ownership of the provider relationship, customer relationship, platform supply, procurement terms or network graph.

## 3. Ownership and Responsibility

### Platform-owned or platform-governed

MGSN owns or governs:

- provider supply pool and network admission;
- capability and service coverage;
- provider profile projections and verification state;
- procurement terms and negotiated provider cost;
- matching, routing and allocation interfaces;
- platform Trust, performance and risk signals;
- customer-facing platform service packages where applicable;
- funds-control, milestone, release and settlement rules;
- fulfillment monitoring, escalation, replacement and suspension;
- network data, aggregate evidence and operating rules.

### Demand-side private space

The user retains:

- customers and customer relationships;
- contacts and internal relationships;
- customer-facing strategy and pricing where the service model permits;
- Documents, Knowledge, memory and internal notes;
- approvals and professional decisions;
- external self-managed provider relationships;
- route choice and final confirmation within the accepted service model.

### Provider private space

The provider retains:

- independent professional identity and qualification;
- staff, methods and internal operations;
- professional judgment and responsibility;
- private cost structure and internal Knowledge;
- rights in submitted evidence subject to network terms;
- responsibility for accepted service work.

## 4. Primary Users

The initial demand-side priority is:

```text
Chinese trademark agencies,
small international trademark teams,
independent trademark professionals,
and MarkReg / Lite users
requiring foreign service capability.
```

The initial supply-side cohort is:

```text
existing proven foreign associates
+ strategic high-volume providers
+ verified law firms or filing providers
```

The initial network is operator-assisted. Full self-service is not required for MVP 0.

## 5. Initial Service Scope

MVP 0 begins with repeatable international trademark services for selected jurisdictions:

- trademark filing through registration;
- official-fee handling and filing evidence;
- selected post-filing actions with standardized scope;
- selected urgent or complex services only where operator review is available.

MVP 0 does not promise complete global lifecycle coverage from launch.

Service scope expands only after supply, pricing, evidence, funds and exception controls are ready.

## 6. Delivery Routes

### R1 — External Self-Managed Route

A user may use an external provider without importing it into MGSN.

```text
User manages provider, price, funds and follow-up
→ platform visibility remains limited
→ user uploads status and Documents manually
→ Product resumes from source-qualified evidence
```

No MGSN procurement, funds protection, replacement, fulfillment guarantee or provider-performance responsibility applies.

The platform may provide reminders, expected milestones, upload requests and reconciliation support.

### R2 — MGSN Recommended Managed Route

This is the default route when the user lacks a suitable external route or chooses platform management.

```text
Service Need
→ eligibility and supply evaluation
→ Recommended Best Route
→ user confirmation / rejection / adjustment
→ platform allocation
→ Provider Acceptance
→ managed fulfillment
→ Return
```

MGSN normally presents one recommended route and may present one or more platform-qualified alternatives when commercially and operationally appropriate.

Provider identity may be staged. The user must receive enough information to understand service scope, qualification, key fit, price, timing, platform responsibility and material constraints.

### R3 — MGSN Managed Preferred-Provider Route

A user may request a known provider.

The preference is subject to:

- provider admission;
- qualification and capability;
- conflict and availability;
- procurement terms;
- service-package compatibility;
- performance, risk and suspension controls.

The platform may reject the preference and recommend another route.

## 7. Matching and Allocation

The platform may automatically:

- identify eligible supply;
- evaluate capability, price, availability, risk and performance;
- rank suitable routes;
- preselect a Recommended Best Route;
- prepare the platform offer.

```text
Automatic matching
≠ automatic appointment
```

Ordinary transactions require the user's final confirmation.

A future standing allocation mandate may be offered only as an explicit service setting with:

- defined service scope;
- budget and price limits;
- approved jurisdictions and service types;
- replacement policy;
- cancellation and review controls;
- auditability and revocation.

Standing authorization is not accepted as the MVP default.

## 8. Commercial Model

The primary initial model is:

```text
platform wholesale procurement
+ managed transaction service
+ optional subscription or Product entitlement
```

The platform negotiates provider cost and service conditions, then supplies a platform service offer to the demand-side user.

Pricing layers remain distinct:

```text
Provider Cost
+ Official Fees / Disbursements
+ Platform Service and Risk Cost
+ User Margin where permitted
= Customer-Facing Offer
```

The demand-side user may set or retain customer-facing margin where the Product and service model permits. The platform may also offer fixed or recommended resale prices.

Provider cost, internal margin and procurement logic are not automatically disclosed.

Paid placement must not masquerade as professional suitability. Eligibility, conflict, capability and material customer interest remain mandatory gates.

## 9. Funds and Financial Risk

MGSN-managed routes require a platform-controlled funds lifecycle.

The Charter does not assume that an unregulated platform entity may legally hold client funds in every jurisdiction.

Implementation must use the platform entity or regulated payment/settlement partner authorized for the relevant model.

Candidate lifecycle:

```text
Offer accepted
→ funds requested
→ funds received or authorized
→ purpose allocation
→ provider commitment
→ official-fee / milestone release
→ delivery evidence
→ correction, dispute or acceptance
→ provider settlement
→ reconciliation
```

Official fees and professional fees must be distinguishable.

Release is service-specific. MVP policy should normally support:

- official-fee release when required and evidenced;
- professional-fee release by accepted milestone or completion;
- hold, partial release, refund or replacement during dispute.

Payment received does not automatically mean provider-earned funds.

## 10. Collaboration Ownership

MGSN owns or governs the network collaboration projection, including:

- provider allocation reference;
- provider instruction envelope;
- Provider Acceptance;
- network milestones and evidence;
- funds and settlement state;
- exception, replacement and closure projection.

MGSN does not silently replace the Owning Service for:

- Customer;
- Opportunity;
- Order;
- Matter;
- Task;
- Workflow;
- Review and Approval;
- formal Communication send state;
- official filing or legal outcome;
- protected Execution.

MarkReg or another Owning Product retains the formal Matter and lifecycle state. MGSN returns typed network results and references.

## 11. External Route Projection

The minimum external route projection contains:

- route type: `External Self-Managed`;
- provider identity: optional;
- user-responsibility notice;
- expected milestones;
- last known status;
- source type and evidence date;
- required next evidence;
- reminder or reconciliation state.

The user may withhold external provider identity, but the system must then display reduced verification and support.

Status strength is typed:

```text
user-reported
provider-message-supported
document-supported
official-source-supported
unknown / stale / conflicting
```

Official status requires official or equivalently authoritative evidence.

## 12. Provider Admission and Governance

Admission may occur through:

- platform recruitment;
- invitation;
- verified application;
- user suggestion followed by platform review;
- strategic provider program.

Admission requires scoped identity, qualification, capability, commercial and service review.

Verification expires or becomes stale according to evidence type.

Platform Trust is multidimensional and contextual. It may include:

- qualification confidence;
- response and acceptance reliability;
- delivery quality;
- evidence completeness;
- communication;
- commercial transparency;
- problem handling;
- correction history;
- funds and settlement reliability;
- current capacity.

There is no required public universal score.

Providers receive notice, correction, response and appeal rights, except that emergency suspension may apply where active risk requires immediate control.

## 13. Identity, Communication and Non-Circumvention

Provider identity is disclosed at the level required for meaningful professional and commercial decision-making.

Direct contact details may remain masked until a permitted collaboration stage.

Managed collaboration may use platform messaging, relay email, controlled meetings or named professional communication.

Unauthorized bypass may result in:

- loss of MGSN guarantees;
- termination of managed-route support;
- provider restriction or suspension;
- contractual consequence where legally enforceable.

Non-circumvention cannot be expressed as ownership of people, organizations or every future relationship.

## 14. Product Experience Principles

```text
Service Need Before Provider Browsing
Recommended Route Before Unstructured Choice
Private Space Before Shared Projection
Evidence Before Promotion
Eligibility Before Allocation
Funds Protection Before Release
Milestone Before Completion
Explainability Before Automation
External Freedom With Explicit Responsibility
Continuity Before One-Off Matching
```

The main demand-side route experience should make three paths visible:

```text
Recommended Platform Route
Preferred Platform Provider
Use My Own External Route
```

The managed route may be visually primary because it provides stronger procurement, funds and fulfillment protection.

## 15. MVP 0

MVP 0 must prove one coherent loop:

```text
typed service need
→ platform supply and pricing
→ recommended route
→ user confirmation
→ controlled payment
→ provider acceptance
→ milestone evidence
→ delivery / exception / replacement
→ settlement
→ Return to MarkReg / Workplace / Lite
→ provider performance update
```

MVP 0 also supports a bounded External Route continuity bridge.

Operator assistance is permitted for:

- provider sourcing;
- pricing exceptions;
- matching review;
- conflict and availability confirmation;
- funds release;
- replacement;
- dispute resolution.

## 16. Success and Zero-Tolerance Conditions

Success evidence includes:

- service and jurisdiction coverage;
- eligible-route rate;
- acceptance of Recommended Best Route;
- procurement advantage and price stability;
- provider acceptance and delivery reliability;
- funds-to-delivery safety;
- milestone evidence completeness;
- replacement and dispute outcomes;
- user global-capability expansion;
- repeat managed-route use;
- sustainable platform margin after failure and support cost.

Zero-tolerance conditions include:

- fabricated qualification, status or official outcome;
- silent appointment without required authority;
- release of funds without permitted conditions;
- concealment of material conflict or suspension;
- private procurement-price leakage;
- external route represented as MGSN guaranteed;
- participant data disclosed outside permitted purpose;
- unknown external state represented as completed;
- commercial ranking overriding mandatory eligibility.

## 17. Non-Goals

MGSN is not:

- a free provider directory;
- an open bidding hall;
- a lowest-price-only marketplace;
- a peer-to-peer participant network;
- a user-owned global agent database;
- an automatic provider appointment authority;
- an unregulated escrow wallet;
- a universal public rating site;
- a replacement for MarkReg, Matter, Task, Workflow or Execution;
- a guarantee for work performed outside MGSN.

## 18. Evolution Rule

This Charter defines the durable Product identity.

Commercial prices, provider cohorts, jurisdictions, service packages, matching weights, disclosure stages and operator assistance may evolve without constitutional change if they continue to respect:

- platform-owned network resources;
- hub topology;
- participant private spaces;
- external-route distinction;
- user confirmation or explicit mandate;
- professional eligibility;
- controlled funds and fulfillment;
- Owning Service authority;
- typed evidence and unknown state.

A change that removes these boundaries requires a formal Charter revision and cross-book review.

## 19. Gate Effect

Owner merge does not authorize:

- Product implementation;
- database schema or API contracts;
- legal funds custody without specialist approval;
- production routing or provider appointment;
- Chapter Map;
