## Charter Decisions

### CD-11 — Initial legal/commercial posture

MGSN is architected as a platform wholesale and managed-transaction network. The precise legal role may vary by service and jurisdiction and must be reviewed before implementation.

The Charter does not assume one universal principal, agent, marketplace or escrow status.

### CD-12 — Provider model

`Provider` is an Organization participating in a scoped MGSN capability and service context.

MGSN owns the governed network profile, admission, capability projection, commercial status, Trust and performance projection. The provider retains its legal identity, qualification, private operations and professional responsibility.

### CD-13 — Collaboration ownership

MGSN owns the network collaboration projection, provider allocation reference, acceptance, milestone, funds, exception and settlement projection.

Existing Owning Services retain Customer, Opportunity, Order, Matter, Task, Workflow, Communication, official outcome and Execution authority.

### CD-14 — Standing allocation mandate

Standing allocation is not the MVP default.

It may later exist only as explicit, revocable, scoped authorization with budget, service, jurisdiction, replacement and audit controls.

### CD-15 — User-visible options

The default user experience presents one Recommended Best Route.

The platform may expose qualified alternatives where useful. It is not required to expose the full supply pool.

### CD-16 — Provider identity visibility

The user receives sufficient provider and service evidence for informed confirmation. Contact details and confidential procurement data may remain staged or masked.

### CD-17 — External Route Projection

External routes use a bounded Product projection with route type, responsibility, expected milestones, last known status, evidence source and next required action.

Provider identity may remain undisclosed, but reduced verification must be visible.

### CD-18 — Commercial model

The initial model combines platform wholesale procurement, managed transactions and optional Product subscription or entitlement.

The demand-side user may retain customer-facing margin where the applicable Product and service model permits.

### CD-19 — Pricing transparency

Provider procurement cost, platform margin and internal routing logic are not automatically disclosed.

The user receives the platform offer, included scope, official fees or disbursements, material exclusions and applicable customer-facing pricing controls.

### CD-20 — Funds control

MGSN-managed routes require platform-controlled funds rules.

The actual funds holder must be a legally suitable platform entity or regulated partner. The Charter does not authorize unlicensed custody.

### CD-21 — Release model

Release is service-specific. Official fees may be released when required and evidenced. Professional fees are released by accepted milestone, completion, credit policy or other governed rule.

Provider assertion alone is not sufficient when consequential evidence is available.

### CD-22 — Refund and replacement

The platform governs refund, hold, replacement and provider deduction under the accepted service terms, evidence and dispute process.

External Self-Managed Routes remain the user's responsibility.

### CD-23 — Provider admission

MVP supply prioritizes proven existing foreign associates and strategic providers. Recruitment, invitation, verified application and user suggestion are permitted intake routes.

Admission always remains a platform decision.

### CD-24 — Trust model

Trust is contextual, multidimensional and primarily platform-governed.

There is no constitutional requirement for a public universal provider score.

### CD-25 — Initial service scope

MVP 0 focuses on repeatable international trademark filing-through-registration services in selected jurisdictions, with selected lifecycle services added only when controls are ready.

### CD-26 — Initial demand-side users

Priority users are Chinese trademark agencies, small international trademark teams, independent professionals and MarkReg/Lite users needing foreign service capability.

### CD-27 — Operator assistance

MVP 0 is operator-assisted. Matching, procurement exceptions, conflict, availability, funds release, replacement and disputes may require operator action.

### CD-28 — Product experience

The route selector presents:

```text
Recommended Platform Route
Preferred Platform Provider
Use My Own External Route
```

The managed route may be visually primary because it provides stronger procurement, funds and fulfillment protection.

### CD-29 — Non-circumvention

MGSN may use staged disclosure, platform Communication, contractual restrictions and loss of guarantees to reduce bypass.

These controls do not constitute ownership of people or every future relationship.

### CD-30 — Next gate

Owner acceptance of this Charter authorizes a controlled Product Baseline only.

It does not authorize Chapter Map, manuscript, schema, API, payment implementation or production deployment.
