# B04-CH-25 — MGSN Gateway and Workplace Network Interfaces

**Status:** Draft 1  
**Chapter Map:** B04-TOC-V0.1  
**Part:** Part IV — Product Architecture and Product Embedding

## Chapter Purpose

Chapters 20–24 established:

- Product architecture principles;
- Product independence and shared foundations;
- Product embedding and cross-Product context;
- Lite as a lightweight Workplace;
- MarkReg as the flagship trademark application.

This chapter applies those principles to the Workplace-side entry into the Mark Global Service Network.

The central question is:

> How should an independent Workplace discover external capability, evaluate evidence and trust, preserve private relationships, prepare Routing and collaboration, and return outcomes without turning MGSN into an open bidding marketplace, public provider directory, forced-allocation engine, or automatic appointment authority?

Professional trademark organizations depend on external capability.

They may need:

- local filing representatives;
- opposition counsel;
- cancellation counsel;
- litigation support;
- recordal services;
- translation;
- legalization;
- evidence collection;
- watch services;
- local investigation;
- domain or customs support;
- specialist technical services.

Most organizations already have some private relationships.

They may also need:

- trusted introductions;
- new-jurisdiction coverage;
- specialist capability;
- backup providers;
- temporary capacity;
- conflict-free alternatives.

MGSN exists to connect these independent organizational Orbits through governed professional relevance.

The Workplace-side Product expression of that connection is the MGSN Gateway and related network interfaces.

The central proposition is:

```text
MGSN Gateway
=
Workplace Network Entry
+ Private Relationship Context
+ Capability Need
+ Purpose-Bound Network Identity
+ Candidate Discovery
+ Capability Evidence
+ Contextual Trust
+ Routing Preparation
+ Human Selection
+ Collaboration Preparation
+ Governed Handoff
+ Outcome Feedback
```

Its primary loop is:

```text
Service or Capability Need
→ Private Partner Check
→ Trusted Candidate Discovery
→ Evidence and Context Review
→ Routing Recommendation
→ Human Selection
→ Collaboration Preparation
→ Governed Instruction
→ Delivery
→ Review and Outcome
→ Trust Feedback
```

The constitutional distinctions are:

```text
MGSN Gateway ≠ MGSN as a whole

MGSN ≠ open bidding marketplace

Network profile ≠ full Workplace

Provider listing ≠ verified capability

Capability claim ≠ Capability evidence

Capability evidence ≠ universal quality

Trust ≠ one global score

Recommendation ≠ Routing decision

Routing decision ≠ provider appointment

Human selection ≠ provider instruction

Provider acceptance ≠ organization approval

Connection ≠ relationship ownership

Visibility ≠ availability

Availability ≠ commitment

Price visibility ≠ accepted commercial terms

Outcome feedback ≠ public reputation automatically

Private relationship ≠ network-owned relationship

Public discovery ≠ default route

Network participation ≠ mandatory data exposure
```

This chapter defines the Product and Workplace interface.

It does not define the complete network architecture.

Private networks, trusted extended networks, Capability Evidence, Routing, trust feedback, ecosystem learning, and cross-Orbit governance are developed in Part VI.

---

## 1. MGSN Gateway Is the Workplace-Side Network Product

MGSN is the network responsibility connecting independent Workplaces.

MGSN Gateway is:

> the Workplace-side Product and interface through which an organization exposes a controlled network identity, expresses Capability needs, discovers candidates, evaluates evidence, prepares Routing, selects collaboration partners, and receives network outcomes.

The Gateway is not the entire network.

It is the organization-facing entry into the network.

The distinction is:

```text
MGSN
→ shared network responsibility

MGSN Gateway
→ Workplace-side Product experience
```

---

## 2. The Primary Network Node Is the Organization Orbit

MGSN should not be built around anonymous listings or isolated individuals.

The stable network node is:

```text
Organization
→ Workplace
→ Independent Orbit
```

Professionals, offices, Capabilities, jurisdiction coverage, relationships, and outcome evidence may attach to that node.

Individuals remain identifiable and accountable.

They do not replace the organization as the primary participation boundary.

---

## 3. Network Participation Is Voluntary and Governed

A Workplace decides:

- whether to participate;
- which network mode is enabled;
- which profile data is shared;
- which Capabilities are visible;
- which evidence is contributed;
- whether public discovery is permitted;
- which relationships remain private;
- which outcome feedback may be shared.

MGSN participation is not a condition for using Workplace, Lite, or MarkReg.

Connection must remain voluntary.

---

## 4. The Gateway Must Be Private-First

The accepted network principle is:

```text
Private First
```

The first candidate set should normally begin with:

- existing partners;
- approved providers;
- previously used associates;
- organization-maintained contacts;
- relationship-specific coverage;
- private provider records.

The system should not route every need directly to public discovery.

Private relationships are organizational capital.

---

## 5. Private Relationship Is Organization-Controlled

A private provider relationship may include:

- organization;
- contacts;
- jurisdictions;
- services;
- private pricing;
- document requirements;
- response history;
- quality notes;
- problems;
- preferences;
- relationship owner.

This information belongs to the participating organization unless deliberately shared.

MGSN may support its use.

It does not own it.

---

## 6. Private First Does Not Mean Private Only

An organization may lack the required capability.

It may need:

- a new jurisdiction;
- specialist counsel;
- additional capacity;
- conflict-free alternative;
- second opinion;
- backup provider.

The Gateway may expand discovery from:

```text
Private Partner Network
→ Trusted Extended Network
→ Limited Public Discovery
```

Expansion should be deliberate.

It should not be the default for every need.

---

## 7. Trust Must Precede Exposure

The network principle is:

```text
Trust Before Exposure
```

Before sensitive data is shared, the organization should understand:

- who the candidate is;
- which organization they represent;
- what Capability they claim;
- which evidence supports the claim;
- what relationship or referral exists;
- what data is necessary;
- what confidentiality boundary applies.

Candidate discovery should begin with minimal context.

Full client information should not be exposed merely to obtain a candidate list.

---

## 8. Evidence Must Precede Ranking

The network principle is:

```text
Evidence Before Ranking
```

Candidates should not rank highly because they:

- paid for visibility;
- completed a profile;
- claim broad coverage;
- offer the lowest price;
- respond to many public requests.

Ranking should use evidence appropriate to the Capability need.

The evidence state must remain visible.

---

## 9. Human Choice Must Precede Routing Action

The network principle is:

```text
Human Choice Before Routing Action
```

MGSN may:

- discover;
- compare;
- rank;
- recommend;
- explain.

The organization selects.

Where formal Routing applies, Routing Service records the decision context.

MGSN must not silently appoint the top-ranked candidate.

---

## 10. Relationship Ownership Remains with Organizations

The network principle is:

```text
Relationship Ownership Remains with Organizations
```

MGSN may facilitate:

- discovery;
- introduction;
- Communication preparation;
- collaboration;
- outcome feedback.

It does not become the owner of:

- client relationship;
- provider relationship;
- private pricing;
- commercial goodwill;
- professional responsibility.

The platform should not place itself as the mandatory commercial intermediary unless a future explicit Product model requires that relationship.

---

## 11. MGSN Gateway Begins with a Capability Need

The Gateway should not begin with a provider directory.

It should begin with:

```text
What Capability is required?
```

A Capability Need may include:

- jurisdiction;
- service;
- subject;
- required qualification;
- language;
- time;
- urgency;
- data sensitivity;
- expected output;
- review requirement;
- commercial constraint;
- collaboration mode.

This focuses discovery on professional relevance.

---

## 12. Capability Need Is Not a Public Job Posting

A Capability Need may remain:

- private;
- visible only to approved partners;
- visible to a trusted extended network;
- available for limited discovery;
- internal until a candidate is selected.

The need should not automatically become a publicly searchable request.

Professional service needs often contain confidential context.

---

## 13. Capability Requirement Must Be Minimal

At the discovery stage, the organization may share only:

- jurisdiction;
- service category;
- required Capability;
- timing;
- language;
- broad complexity;
- eligibility requirements.

It may withhold:

- client identity;
- trademark;
- legal strategy;
- Evidence;
- full Matter history;
- private budget;
- internal provider evaluation.

More information may be shared after candidate narrowing and authorization.

---

## 14. Network Identity Is a Purpose-Limited Representation

A Workplace may expose a network identity containing:

- organization name;
- selected brand;
- location;
- professional status;
- jurisdictions;
- Capabilities;
- qualifications;
- service coverage;
- selected evidence;
- availability;
- collaboration preferences.

This is not the full Workplace.

It excludes by default:

- private clients;
- private pricing;
- internal Knowledge;
- private evaluations;
- organization memory;
- unrelated Products;
- internal roles.

---

## 15. Identity Claim Must Remain Distinct from Verification

A profile may claim:

- legal status;
- qualification;
- office location;
- jurisdiction coverage;
- language;
- service Capability;
- professional membership.

Claims may be:

- self-declared;
- document-supported;
- externally verified;
- partner-confirmed;
- expired;
- disputed;
- unknown.

The Gateway should expose verification state.

A completed profile must not appear as verified professional authority.

---

## 16. Capability Profile Must Be Scoped

A provider may have Capability in:

- one jurisdiction;
- one service;
- one language;
- one complexity range;
- one organization office;
- one professional team.

The system should avoid broad statements such as:

```text
Global trademark expert
```

when evidence supports only narrow scope.

Capability is contextual.

---

## 17. Capability Claim Is Not Evidence

A Capability claim may support discovery.

Evidence may include:

- qualification;
- official registration;
- jurisdiction practice;
- prior matters;
- anonymized work samples;
- verified outcomes;
- organization relationship history;
- peer reference;
- response and delivery history;
- current availability.

Claim and evidence must remain separate.

---

## 18. Capability Evidence Must Be Purpose-Specific

Evidence suitable for filing may differ from evidence suitable for:

- opposition;
- litigation;
- recordal;
- evidence collection;
- translation;
- investigation.

A provider may be strong in one area and unknown in another.

MGSN should not compress all professional ability into one general score.

---

## 19. Evidence Source Must Remain Visible

Capability evidence may originate from:

- provider self-declaration;
- uploaded document;
- official register;
- Workplace outcome;
- trusted referral;
- organization-private history;
- network verification;
- public source.

The source affects confidence.

A network badge should not hide the underlying evidence basis.

---

## 20. Evidence Freshness Must Be Considered

Qualifications, team members, coverage, fees, and availability may change.

Evidence should preserve:

- date;
- validity;
- reviewer;
- source;
- expiry;
- last confirmation.

Historical performance may remain useful.

It does not prove current availability or qualification.

---

## 21. Trust Is Contextual

Trust means:

> confidence in a candidate or relationship for a defined purpose, under a defined organization, Capability need, evidence set, history, and time.

Trust may differ by:

- organization;
- client;
- jurisdiction;
- service;
- contact;
- team;
- matter complexity;
- confidentiality;
- commercial terms.

There is no single universally correct trust value.

---

## 22. Trust Is Relational

An organization may trust a provider because of:

- repeated cooperation;
- reliable communication;
- accurate filings;
- problem resolution;
- transparency;
- fair billing;
- specialist expertise.

Another organization may have no relationship with the same provider.

The first organization’s trust should not automatically become universal public truth.

---

## 23. Trust Is Evidence-Based

Trust may be informed by:

- identity verification;
- Capability evidence;
- relationship history;
- response performance;
- delivery quality;
- issue handling;
- review outcomes;
- client impact;
- reversals or complaints.

Trust should not be based only on popularity.

---

## 24. Trust Is Not One Universal Score

A single number may hide:

- scope;
- source;
- organization;
- time;
- evidence quality;
- unresolved issues.

MGSN may present dimensions such as:

```text
Known Relationship

Capability Evidence

Qualification Confidence

Response Reliability

Delivery Reliability

Commercial Transparency

Current Availability

Risk or Conflict Flags
```

A composite score, if used, must remain explainable.

---

## 25. Private Trust and Network Trust Must Be Distinct

Private Trust may be based on one organization’s confidential history.

Network Trust may be based on governed shared evidence.

The Gateway may combine them in an organization-specific view.

It must not publish private Trust automatically.

---

## 26. Negative Experience Must Be Governed

A negative outcome may arise from:

- provider failure;
- client delay;
- ambiguous instruction;
- official office error;
- price dispute;
- communication failure;
- Product or Execution failure.

The system should not convert one negative record into a permanent public penalty without context and review.

Feedback requires governance.

---

## 27. Candidate Discovery Must Follow Network Tiers

A candidate search may proceed through:

```text
Tier 1:
Private Partners

Tier 2:
Trusted Extended Network

Tier 3:
Limited Public Discovery
```

The organization may configure:

- whether tiers are used;
- when expansion occurs;
- what data each tier receives;
- which candidates are excluded.

Tiered discovery preserves autonomy.

---

## 28. Private Partner Check Should Be Explicit

Before broader discovery, the Gateway should be able to answer:

- Does an approved provider already cover this need?
- Is the provider currently available?
- Is the relationship active?
- Does a conflict exist?
- Are current fees known?
- Is an alternative required?

A private partner should not be selected automatically merely because they were used before.

Current eligibility still matters.

---

## 29. Trusted Extended Network Requires a Relationship Basis

Trusted extended candidates may be supported by:

- referral;
- shared professional network;
- verified collaboration chain;
- organization affiliation;
- recognized qualification;
- validated outcome evidence.

The relationship basis should remain visible.

“Trusted” must not become an unexplained marketing label.

---

## 30. Limited Public Discovery Must Remain Limited

Public discovery may be useful when private and trusted networks do not satisfy the need.

It should avoid:

- exposing full client details;
- broadcasting confidential matters;
- unlimited bidder access;
- price-only competition;
- automatic lowest-cost selection.

Public discovery should remain a controlled capability search.

---

## 31. MGSN Is Not a Provider Directory

A directory answers:

```text
Who is listed?
```

MGSN should answer:

```text
Which eligible Capability may satisfy this specific need,
under which evidence, trust, and organizational context?
```

Static listing is insufficient.

Professional relevance and governance matter.

---

## 32. MGSN Is Not an Open Bidding Marketplace

An open bidding marketplace tends to optimize:

- price competition;
- speed of response;
- platform visibility;
- transaction volume.

Professional trademark collaboration also requires:

- qualification;
- relationship;
- confidentiality;
- conflict;
- jurisdiction;
- quality;
- accountability;
- client interest.

MGSN may support quotation comparison.

It must not reduce collaboration to lowest-price competition by default.

---

## 33. MGSN Is Not a Forced Allocation Engine

The network must not assign work because:

- a candidate has the highest score;
- the platform has a preferred provider;
- the provider bought visibility;
- the route is commercially convenient.

Recommendations support human selection.

They do not replace it.

---

## 34. Candidate Set Must Be Explainable

The Gateway should explain:

- why candidates were included;
- which tier they came from;
- what Capability matched;
- which evidence applied;
- which candidates were excluded;
- what information is missing;
- which conflicts remain;
- why ranking differs.

Explainability supports professional judgment.

---

## 35. Eligibility Must Precede Ranking

Candidates may be ineligible because of:

- jurisdiction;
- qualification;
- conflict;
- organization policy;
- client restriction;
- data boundary;
- provider suspension;
- unavailable capacity;
- missing evidence;
- Product limitation.

Only eligible candidates should enter ranking.

A highly rated but ineligible provider should not appear as the recommended option.

---

## 36. Ranking Must Be Multidimensional

Candidate comparison may consider:

- Capability fit;
- jurisdiction coverage;
- qualification;
- relationship history;
- response reliability;
- delivery quality;
- availability;
- language;
- fee;
- conflict;
- data handling;
- client preference;
- organization preference.

The system should not collapse all dimensions into price.

---

## 37. Organization Preference May Influence Ranking

A Workplace may prefer:

- approved providers;
- private partners;
- specific languages;
- specific communication styles;
- local firms;
- certain fee ranges;
- stronger Human Review;
- particular data jurisdictions.

Preferences rank eligible candidates.

They do not override qualification, conflict, Permission, or Policy.

---

## 38. Client Preference May Influence but Not Control Eligibility

A client may prefer:

- a known firm;
- a jurisdiction-specific provider;
- a fee limit;
- a language;
- a relationship.

The organization should preserve the instruction.

It must still evaluate:

- professional suitability;
- conflict;
- legal eligibility;
- confidentiality;
- engagement responsibility.

Client preference does not make an ineligible provider eligible.

---

## 39. Conflict Check Must Be a Distinct Gate

Provider discovery may occur before detailed Matter disclosure.

Before selection or instruction, the organization may need:

- conflict request;
- limited identity disclosure;
- confirmation;
- conflict expiry;
- conflict result.

Conflict status should remain distinct from general availability.

No conflict result is not proof of professional suitability.

---

## 40. Availability Must Be Time-Bound

Availability may include:

- available now;
- available after a date;
- limited capacity;
- unavailable;
- unknown;
- pending confirmation.

A profile stating “available” must not remain current indefinitely.

Availability should be refreshed before instruction.

---

## 41. Price Is Contextual and Versioned

Provider fees may depend on:

- service;
- jurisdiction;
- class count;
- item count;
- complexity;
- official fees;
- urgency;
- tax;
- disbursements;
- currency;
- relationship pricing.

A stored price is not automatically a binding quote.

The Gateway should distinguish:

```text
Historical Price

Indicative Price

Private Rate

Current Quote

Accepted Commercial Terms
```

---

## 42. Price Visibility Must Follow Data Boundaries

Private pricing may be visible only to:

- selected users;
- finance roles;
- authorized Products;
- relevant Matter participants.

It should not become network-public data by default.

Organizations retain pricing ownership.

---

## 43. Provider Comparison Is a Product Result

The Gateway may produce a provider comparison containing:

- candidate references;
- Capability match;
- evidence;
- trust context;
- price context;
- availability;
- risk;
- recommendation;
- missing information.

This is a Product result.

It is not a Routing decision or appointment.

---

## 44. Routing Recommendation Must Remain Distinct

A Routing recommendation may state:

```text
Candidate A is recommended
for the current Capability need
under the stated criteria.
```

Formal Routing may require:

- Routing request;
- candidate set;
- evaluation basis;
- human decision;
- selected target;
- override;
- review;
- audit.

The Gateway prepares the context.

Routing Service records the formal decision where required.

---

## 45. Human Selection Must Be Typed

A human may:

- shortlist;
- request a quote;
- request conflict check;
- request more evidence;
- reject;
- defer;
- select for Routing;
- approve for instruction.

These are different actions.

One “Choose Provider” button should not collapse them.

---

## 46. Selection Must Identify the Decision Basis

The record may preserve:

- selected candidate;
- alternative candidates;
- criteria;
- reviewer;
- decision-maker;
- client input;
- override;
- reason;
- time.

The organization should be able to explain why a provider was selected.

---

## 47. Provider Selection Is Not Provider Appointment

Selection may establish an internal preference.

Appointment may require:

- formal Routing decision;
- engagement terms;
- client authority;
- conflict clearance;
- fee approval;
- Communication;
- provider acceptance;
- Owning Service record.

The Gateway must preserve the gap.

---

## 48. Collaboration Preparation Is a Separate Stage

After selection, the Gateway may prepare:

- introduction;
- provider instruction draft;
- scope;
- Matter summary;
- documents;
- deadlines;
- quotation acceptance;
- confidentiality requirements;
- communication route;
- expected deliverables.

This is collaboration preparation.

It is not yet governed instruction.

---

## 49. Information Sharing Must Be Staged

The collaboration may progress through:

```text
Capability Need Summary

Candidate Evaluation Context

Conflict Check Data

Quotation Context

Selected Provider Context

Authorized Matter Package

Ongoing Collaboration Context
```

Each stage may expose more information.

Information should not be shared earlier than necessary.

---

## 50. Provider Instruction Is a Protected Action

Provider instruction may:

- disclose client data;
- create representation;
- trigger work;
- create financial obligation;
- transfer documents;
- affect deadlines.

It may require:

- Human Review;
- approval;
- Communication governance;
- Routing decision;
- Execution;
- formal service record.

The Gateway prepares.

It does not instruct by Product authority.

---

## 51. Provider Acceptance Is Not Organization Approval

A provider may accept:

- availability;
- scope;
- fee;
- instruction.

This confirms the provider side.

The organization may still need:

- internal approval;
- client approval;
- payment approval;
- formal Matter or Order state;
- conflict confirmation.

Both sides’ decisions remain distinct.

---

## 52. Collaboration Must Preserve Responsibility

Cross-organization work should identify:

- instructing organization;
- performing organization;
- responsible professionals;
- client relationship;
- service scope;
- communication authority;
- review responsibility;
- formal outcome owner.

MGSN connection does not dissolve responsibility.

---

## 53. Client Ownership Must Remain Explicit

The Gateway must not imply that the network owns the client.

The instructing organization may remain responsible for:

- client relationship;
- scope;
- advice;
- billing;
- instruction;
- communication;
- outcome reporting.

The performing provider may have separate professional obligations.

Those responsibilities must be explicit.

---

## 54. Network Communication Must Remain Governed

The Gateway may prepare:

- inquiry;
- quotation request;
- conflict request;
- instruction;
- follow-up;
- delivery acknowledgement.

Communication still requires:

- sender;
- recipient;
- purpose;
- scope;
- review;
- attachments;
- send authority.

Network convenience does not bypass Communication Service.

---

## 55. Delivery Must Remain Typed

A provider may deliver:

- quotation;
- advice;
- filing receipt;
- office notice;
- response draft;
- certificate;
- status report;
- invoice;
- Evidence.

The result should identify:

- type;
- source;
- version;
- Matter;
- authority;
- review requirement;
- external confirmation.

A delivery is not automatically accepted as correct.

---

## 56. Outcome Review Must Be Contextual

The instructing organization may review:

- timeliness;
- accuracy;
- responsiveness;
- completeness;
- transparency;
- issue handling;
- client impact;
- cost accuracy.

Review should consider:

- service scope;
- complexity;
- external-office behavior;
- organization instruction quality;
- client delay;
- exceptional events.

Outcome judgment should not be reduced to one star rating.

---

## 57. Trust Feedback Must Remain Governed

Outcome feedback may update:

- private relationship history;
- organization-specific trust;
- Capability evidence;
- network-level evidence;
- recommendation quality;
- risk signals.

Feedback does not automatically become public.

It may require:

- source verification;
- review;
- anonymization;
- organization consent;
- dispute handling.

Part VI develops this further.

---

## 58. Positive Outcome Does Not Create Universal Capability

A provider’s successful Matter may support evidence.

It does not prove universal competence across:

- jurisdictions;
- services;
- complexity;
- languages;
- teams.

Capability growth remains scoped.

---

## 59. Negative Outcome Does Not Create Universal Exclusion

A failed or disputed Matter may require investigation.

The failure may arise from:

- provider action;
- instructing organization;
- client delay;
- official office;
- unclear scope;
- system failure.

The network should not impose automatic permanent exclusion from one unexplained outcome.

---

## 60. MGSN Gateway Must Support Cross-Product Entry

A Capability Need may originate from:

- Lite;
- MarkReg;
- Workplace;
- organization-specific application;
- Task;
- Matter;
- Recommendation;
- AI Agent.

The Gateway should receive a typed, minimized context package.

It should not inherit all Product context.

---

## 61. MarkReg-to-MGSN Handoff Must Preserve Trademark Context

MarkReg may provide:

- organization;
- Matter or filing need;
- jurisdiction;
- service type;
- Capability requirement;
- deadline;
- language;
- minimum facts;
- privacy boundary;
- provider preferences;
- return route.

It should not expose all client and Evidence data during discovery.

---

## 62. Lite-to-MGSN Handoff Must Preserve Preliminary Status

Lite may identify a need before a formal Matter exists.

The Gateway should know whether the request is:

- exploratory;
- quotation only;
- conflict check;
- provider shortlist;
- formal Routing preparation;
- instruction preparation.

Preliminary interest must not become formal demand automatically.

---

## 63. MGSN Results Must Return as Typed Results

The Gateway may return:

```text
PrivatePartnerAvailable

CandidateSet

EvidenceRequired

ConflictCheckRequired

QuoteRequested

ProviderComparison

RoutingRecommendation

HumanSelectionRequired

RoutingDecisionReference

CollaborationPrepared

ProviderAccepted

DeliveryReceived

Failure
```

The source Product or Workplace must not infer more than the result type states.

---

## 64. Workplace May Aggregate Network Visibility

Workplace may show:

- provider relationships;
- active collaboration;
- pending quotes;
- conflict checks;
- delivery;
- unresolved issues;
- trust feedback;
- Capability gaps.

It may aggregate through references and summaries.

MGSN remains the network Product.

Workplace does not absorb the network.

---

## 65. Network Participation Must Respect Product Entitlement

A user may have access to:

- private partner view;
- candidate discovery;
- evidence review;
- price comparison;
- selection;
- instruction preparation;
- feedback.

These rights may differ.

Viewing providers does not authorize instruction.

Product entitlement and Permission must be re-evaluated per action.

---

## 66. AI May Assist but Not Select the Provider

AI may support:

- Capability Need extraction;
- candidate matching;
- evidence summarization;
- comparison;
- ranking explanation;
- conflict-question preparation;
- instruction drafting;
- outcome summarization.

AI must not:

- invent verification;
- hide missing evidence;
- appoint the provider;
- accept fees;
- send instruction;
- publish private feedback;
- create universal trust.

Human selection remains required.

---

## 67. MGSN Gateway Must Fail Safely

Failure may include:

- no eligible candidate;
- insufficient evidence;
- unresolved conflict;
- stale availability;
- price unavailable;
- network access denied;
- private relationship data unavailable;
- Routing rejected;
- provider declined;
- delivery failed.

The Gateway should preserve:

- what failed;
- which stage;
- whether data was shared;
- whether formal state changed;
- what alternative remains.

No candidate is safer than an invented suitable candidate.

---

## 68. MGSN Metrics Must Measure Collaboration Quality

Useful measures may include:

- private partner reuse;
- Capability gaps resolved;
- time to eligible shortlist;
- evidence completeness;
- conflict resolution time;
- response reliability;
- delivery quality;
- issue resolution;
- repeat collaboration;
- recommendation acceptance;
- false-positive matching;
- data-minimization success.

Marketplace transaction volume alone is not the primary metric.

## 71. Failure Modes This Chapter Prevents

### Open marketplace drift

Every service need becomes a public bidding request.

### Lowest-price routing

The cheapest provider automatically ranks first or receives the work.

### Platform-owned client

The network becomes the commercial owner of the organization’s client.

### Private relationship extraction

Existing partner contacts, pricing, and evaluations become platform data by default.

### Directory reduction

MGSN becomes a static list of providers.

### Profile-verification collapse

Completed provider profiles appear professionally verified.

### Universal trust score

Complex relationship and evidence context is compressed into one public number.

### Evidence-free ranking

Popularity, payment, or profile completeness replaces Capability evidence.

### Automatic appointment

The top-ranked candidate becomes provider without human selection.

### Context overexposure

Full Matter and client data are shared before candidate narrowing.

### Routing-instruction collapse

A Routing decision automatically sends provider instructions.

### Feedback punishment

One disputed outcome creates permanent public exclusion.

### Product boundary loss

Workplace, Lite, or MarkReg begins to own network relationships and Routing.

These designs may increase network activity.

They do not create a conforming professional network.
