# Part I — The Workplace as an Independent Orbit

Part I establishes the constitutional foundation of Workplace.

It explains why Workplace must exist, why every Organization Workplace is an independent Orbit, how Workplace is positioned among the surrounding MarkOrbit responsibilities, and which principles and boundaries every future Workplace design must preserve.

### B04-CH-02 — Why Workplace Exists

Explains the missing organizational layer between shared MarkOrbit foundations and concrete Product, Execution, and network use.

### B04-CH-03 — The Orbit Principle and Organizational Autonomy

Defines the principle:

> 各行其道，彼此牵引，共同演进

and explains autonomy, connection, trust, and shared evolution without central platform absorption.

### B04-CH-04 — Position Between Core, Execution, Products and Network

Defines the responsibility and authority model across Core, Execution, Workplace, Products, MGSN, Owning Services, human professionals, and AI Agents.

### B04-CH-05 — Workplace Principles

Establishes the ten durable principles that constrain every Workplace edition, Product embedding, network interface, and implementation decision.

### B04-CH-06 — Workplace Boundaries and Non-Goals

Defines what Workplace is responsible for, what it consumes, what it coordinates with, and what it must never absorb.

### B04-CH-08 — People, Roles, Permissions and Accountability

### B04-CH-10 — Private Knowledge, AI Context, Preferences and Organizational Memory

Defines how information is discovered, selected, traced, and assembled into authorized professional context.

### B04-CH-15 — Capability Discovery and Skill Selection

### B04-CH-16 — Assistant, Guide and AI Agent in the Workplace

### B04-CH-17 — Value Candidates, Recommendations and Next Best Action

### B04-CH-19 — Human Review, Approval and Owning Service Handoff

## Reading Sequence

Book 04 should be read in order.

The six parts build on one another:

```text
Part I establishes the constitutional boundary.

Part II defines the organization context inside that boundary.

Part III explains how shared knowledge and capabilities are consumed.

Part IV explains how Products operate within and across Workplaces.

Part V explains how work becomes Artifacts, delivery, and outcomes.

Part VI explains how independent Workplaces participate in the wider ecosystem.
```

Later chapters may deepen earlier concepts, but they must not redefine the authority model established in Part I.
