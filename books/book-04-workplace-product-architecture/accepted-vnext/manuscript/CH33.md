A Workplace may maintain private provider relationships, negotiated terms, performance history and internal preferences. MGSN may separately operate platform-governed shared Provider Supply, network rules, Routing assets and Trust assets.

```text
participant relationship data
≠ shared Provider Supply
≠ platform Routing assets
≠ network-wide Trust assets
```

Joining the network does not transfer the originating customer relationship, private commercial context or unrestricted access to participant records. Shared visibility is purpose-limited and governed.

## Chapter Purpose

Parts I–V established the independent Organization Workplace, authorized context, Product architecture, governed Execution, Artifact outcomes, and feedback.

Part VI explains how independent Workplaces connect.

This chapter answers:

> How can an organization use its private professional relationships, extend discovery through trusted connections, and enter limited public discovery without exposing clients, surrendering relationship ownership, or becoming platform inventory?

The accepted network progression is:

```text
Private Network first
→ Trusted Extended Network
→ Limited Public Discovery
```

The central proposition is:

```text
Professional Network Participation
=
Independent Organization Orbit
+ Organization-Controlled Relationship Context
+ Purpose-Limited Network Identity
+ Private Partner Discovery
+ Trusted Extension
+ Minimum Necessary Exposure
+ Human Selection
+ Governed Collaboration
+ Outcome and Trust Feedback
```

The constitutional principles are:

```text
Private First

Trust Before Exposure

Evidence Before Ranking

Human Choice Before Routing Action

Relationship Ownership Remains with Organizations
```

The constitutional distinctions are:

```text
Network participation ≠ platform ownership

Private relationship ≠ network-public relationship

Contact ≠ trusted provider

Referral ≠ endorsement

Visibility ≠ eligibility

Eligibility ≠ selection

Selection ≠ appointment

Connection ≠ engagement

Public discovery ≠ open bidding

Network node ≠ seller inventory
```

## 1. Organization Workplace Is the Primary Network Node

The stable network participant is:

```text
Organization / Workplace / Orbit
```

Professionals, offices, Capabilities, jurisdiction coverage, evidence, relationships, and outcomes attach to the organization.

This preserves:

- accountability;
- commercial identity;
- professional responsibility;
- client relationship;
- data boundary;
- trust history.

---

## 2. Individual Professionals Remain Identifiable

An organization node does not hide human responsibility.

The network may identify:

- responsible professional;
- qualification;
- team;
- office;
- role;
- contact.

The person participates through a governed organizational context.

---

## 3. Network Participation Is Optional

An organization may use:

- Workplace;
- Lite;
- MarkReg;
- private provider records;

without joining broader MGSN discovery.

Network participation should be enabled deliberately.

The organization decides which network functions it uses.

---

## 4. Private Network Is the First Layer

A Private Network consists of organization-controlled relationships such as:

- existing foreign associates;
- approved counsel;
- translators;
- investigators;
- filing providers;
- long-term collaborators;
- referral contacts;
- internal offices;
- affiliated organizations.

These relationships already carry valuable context.

---

## 5. Private Network Is Not Merely a Contact List

A contact list records names and addresses.

A private relationship may include:

- service scope;
- jurisdiction;
- responsible people;
- private pricing;
- history;
- trust;
- problems;
- communication style;
- document requirements;
- conflict;
- availability;
- relationship owner.

This context belongs to the organization.

---

## 6. Relationship Ownership Remains with the Organization

MGSN may help the organization:

- organize;
- search;
- compare;
- communicate;
- collaborate;
- learn.

It must not claim ownership of:

- client;
- provider;
- private pricing;
- relationship history;
- commercial goodwill;
- direct Communication;
- future business.

---

## 7. Private Relationship Does Not Guarantee Current Eligibility

A provider used before may now be:

- unavailable;
- conflicted;
- unqualified for the new service;
- outside current budget;
- suspended;
- outdated in contact or coverage.

Private First means check private partners first.

It does not mean select them automatically.

---

## 8. Relationship Scope Must Be Explicit

An organization may trust a provider for:

- routine filing;
- one jurisdiction;
- one language;
- one client type;
- one professional contact.

That trust may not extend to litigation, opposition, or strategic advice.

---

## 9. Private Pricing Must Remain Private

Relationship-specific prices may reflect:

- volume;
- history;
- reciprocal work;
- confidential agreement;
- temporary discount;
- currency;
- service package.

MGSN must not publish or normalize those terms without authority.

---

## 10. Private Notes Require Strong Access Control

Private relationship notes may contain:

- quality concerns;
- negotiation history;
- disputes;
- personal observations;
- client restrictions.

They should not be exposed to:

- candidate providers;
- unrelated users;
- public network;
- AI Agents without authorization.

---

## 11. Trusted Extended Network Is the Second Layer

A Trusted Extended Network contains candidates connected through a credible basis beyond direct relationship.

The basis may include:

- referral from trusted organization;
- verified professional association;
- shared collaboration chain;
- recognized qualification;
- validated prior outcome;
- affiliated office;
- trusted network group.

---

## 12. Trusted Extension Must Explain the Relationship Basis

A label such as `trusted` is insufficient.

The system should explain:

```text
Referred by Organization B

Verified through official register

Previously collaborated with trusted partner

Member of approved professional group
```

Trust must remain explainable.

---

## 13. Referral Is Not Appointment

A referral means:

> another participant believes the candidate may be relevant.

It does not prove:

- availability;
- conflict clearance;
- final quality;
- fee;
- current qualification;
- suitability for this Matter.

The receiving organization evaluates independently.

---

## 14. Referrer Authority Is Scoped

A referrer may know a provider for one service.

The referral should preserve:

- referrer;
- relationship;
- scope;
- time;
- confidence;
- disclosure permission.

A vague referral should not become strong network evidence.

---

## 15. Referral Privacy Must Be Respected

A referrer may permit:

- private introduction;
- anonymous relationship signal;
- named referral;
- no onward sharing.

The network should not expose relationship graphs by default.

---

## 16. Trusted Extended Network Is Not a Global Trust List

Different organizations may have different trusted extensions.

The same provider may appear:

- trusted to one organization;
- unknown to another;
- restricted for a third.

Trust remains relational.

---

## 17. Limited Public Discovery Is the Third Layer

Limited Public Discovery is used when:

- private partners do not cover the need;
- trusted extended candidates are insufficient;
- the organization deliberately expands search.

It remains a controlled Capability search.

---

## 18. Public Discovery Is Not Open Broadcasting

The organization should not automatically broadcast:

- client identity;
- trademark;
- confidential dispute;
- Evidence;
- legal strategy;
- full budget;
- private deadline detail.

Discovery can begin with a minimized Capability Need.

---

## 19. Public Discovery Is Not Open Bidding

MGSN is not designed around:

- unlimited bidder access;
- lowest-price wins;
- public race to respond;
- paid placement;
- platform-forced allocation.

Professional relevance, evidence, confidentiality, and human judgment remain primary.

---

## 20. Network Identity Is Purpose-Limited

A Workplace may expose a network representation containing:

- organization name;
- selected brand;
- jurisdiction;
- service Capability;
- qualification;
- selected evidence;
- languages;
- collaboration preference;
- availability.

It is not the full Workplace profile.

---

## 21. Network Identity May Have Several Visibility Levels

An organization may expose different information to:

- private partners;
- trusted extended network;
- limited public discovery;
- selected client;
- selected provider.

One public profile should not become the only network identity.

---

## 22. Network Participation Must Be Revocable

An organization may:

- hide a Capability;
- leave public discovery;
- revoke a profile;
- remove evidence;
- suspend invitations;
- end network participation.

Historical collaboration records may remain subject to retention.

Future exposure should stop.

---

## 23. Withdrawal Does Not Erase Other Organizations’ History

If a provider leaves MGSN, an instructing organization may retain:

- Matter history;
- Communication;
- delivery;
- invoice;
- private trust notes;
- formal records.

Network withdrawal does not rewrite legitimate relationship history.

---

## 24. Network Discovery Begins with Capability Need

The system should first identify:

- jurisdiction;
- service;
- qualification;
- timing;
- language;
- data sensitivity;
- expected deliverable;
- collaboration mode.

It should not begin with generic browsing alone.

---

## 25. Minimum Necessary Need Context Should Be Shared

Early discovery may require only:

- general service;
- jurisdiction;
- deadline range;
- language;
- broad complexity;
- qualification.

Detailed Matter context can be shared later.

---

## 26. Information Exposure Should Expand by Stage

A safe progression is:

```text
Capability Need Summary

Candidate Identity and Evidence

Conflict Check Context

Quotation Context

Selected Provider Context

Authorized Matter Package

Ongoing Collaboration Context
```

Each stage requires a new purpose and access decision.

---

## 27. Conflict Check May Require Controlled Disclosure

Conflict checking may need:

- party names;
- trademark;
- opponent;
- client identity;
- related entities.

The disclosure should be limited to the selected candidate or defined group.

Conflict data should not become public discovery content.

---

## 28. Candidate Visibility Does Not Mean Availability

A provider profile may be visible while availability is:

- unknown;
- stale;
- limited;
- service-specific;
- pending confirmation.

Availability should be checked before selection.

---

## 29. Network Membership Does Not Mean Verification

A participant may be:

- self-declared;
- identity-checked;
- qualification-verified;
- evidence-validated;
- privately known;
- network-reviewed.

The verification level should remain visible.

Membership alone must not imply endorsement.

---

## 30. Network Membership Does Not Mean Universal Eligibility

Eligibility depends on:

- service;
- jurisdiction;
- qualification;
- conflict;
- organization Policy;
- client restriction;
- data boundary;
- current status;
- availability.

The network should not produce one universal eligible-provider set.

---

## 31. Invitation and Acceptance Must Be Distinct

An organization may invite a provider to:

- join private network;
- quote;
- confirm capability;
- collaborate;
- share evidence.

The provider may accept, decline, or narrow the scope.

Invitation does not create engagement.

---

## 32. Connection Is Not Engagement

A network connection may indicate:

- known contact;
- accepted invitation;
- referral relationship;
- shared group.

Engagement requires:

- scope;
- conflict clearance;
- commercial terms;
- instruction;
- acceptance;
- responsibility.

---

## 33. Engagement Is Not Relationship Ownership Transfer

The platform may record engagement context.

The participating organizations retain:

- client responsibilities;
- professional obligations;
- commercial relationship;
- future selection rights.

---

## 34. Organization May Prefer Direct Communication

MGSN may facilitate Communication.

It should not force all professional relationships through a platform-controlled channel where direct Communication is appropriate and authorized.

Trace requirements may still apply.

---

## 35. Network Portability Supports Autonomy

An organization should be able to export, subject to rights and privacy:

- its own provider contacts;
- relationship notes;
- service coverage;
- private pricing;
- collaboration history;
- trust assessments.

Platform participation should not create lock-in through relationship captivity.

---

## 36. Portability Does Not Export Other Organizations’ Private Data

A provider may not export:

- another organization’s private evaluation;
- confidential client context;
- undisclosed referral graph;
- restricted outcome data.

Portability remains bounded.

---

## 37. Network Graph Must Not Become Surveillance

Connection data may reveal:

- client relationships;
- market strategy;
- provider dependence;
- business volume;
- regional gaps.

The system should not expose or monetize the relationship graph without authority.

---

## 38. AI May Assist Discovery Under Minimized Context

AI may:

- extract Capability Need;
- search eligible profiles;
- summarize evidence;
- explain relationship basis;
- identify gaps.

AI should not receive full Matter data merely to generate a candidate list.

---

## 39. AI Must Not Invent Trust

An AI Agent must not:

- claim a referral that does not exist;
- upgrade self-declaration to verification;
- invent collaboration history;
- infer universal trust from popularity;
- expose private notes.

Trust must be evidence-linked.

---

## 40. Product Handoffs Preserve Preliminary Status

Lite or MarkReg may hand a need into MGSN.

The handoff should state whether the request is:

- exploratory;
- shortlist;
- quotation;
- conflict check;
- Routing preparation;
- instruction preparation.

Preliminary need must not become formal engagement.

---

## 41. Network Outcome Returns to the Organization

Results may include:

- private partner found;
- trusted candidate found;
- no eligible candidate;
- quote received;
- conflict unresolved;
- provider selected;
- provider declined;
- collaboration active;
- delivery received.

Workplace and source Products may present these results.

---

## 42. No Candidate Is a Valid Outcome

A safe network may conclude:

```text
No eligible candidate under current requirements.
```

It should not fabricate suitability or weaken requirements merely to produce a match.

---

## 43. Network Growth Should Follow Real Collaboration

A trusted extended network should grow through:

- referrals;
- verified identity;
- Capability evidence;
- successful collaboration;
- governed feedback.

Growth should not be measured only by registered profiles.

---

## 44. Network Quality Depends on Relationship Preservation

A network that destroys private relationships to create public liquidity weakens the professional ecosystem.

The goal is to strengthen:

- autonomy;
- discovery;
- trust;
- collaboration;
- learning.

---

## 47. Failure Modes This Chapter Prevents

### Contact-directory reduction

Private relationship context disappears.

### Public-first discovery

Existing trusted providers are ignored.

### Referral overclaim

A referral becomes universal endorsement.

### Relationship-graph exposure

Private commercial networks become platform data.

### Open-bidding drift

Confidential needs become public price competition.

### Membership-verification collapse

Profile registration appears as professional validation.

### Connection-engagement collapse

Network acceptance creates service obligation.

### Platform relationship captivity

Organizations cannot export or continue direct relationships.

These designs may increase platform activity.

They violate the Orbit model.

## 48. Minimum Conformance Rule

A conforming network participation architecture must preserve:

```text
Organization Workplace remains
the primary network node.

Participation remains voluntary.

Private relationships remain
organization-controlled and private by default.

Private discovery precedes
trusted extension and limited public discovery.

Trusted extension preserves
referral basis, scope, time,
and disclosure permission.

Public discovery uses minimum
necessary Capability context
and does not become open bidding.

Network identity remains
a purpose-limited Workplace representation.

Membership, identity claim,
verification, eligibility,
availability, selection,
appointment, and engagement
remain distinct.

Information exposure expands by stage.

Relationship graphs,
private pricing, private Trust,
and client context remain protected.

Organizations retain relationship ownership,
direct Communication options,
portability, and professional responsibility.

No candidate remains a valid safe outcome.
```

## 50. Chapter Conclusion

Independent organizations need connection without absorption.

The correct progression is:

```text
Private relationships
→ trusted extension
→ limited public discovery
→ evidence-based evaluation
→ human selection
→ governed collaboration
```

The constitutional outcome is:

```text
Organizations remain independent.

Relationships remain organization-owned.

Trust controls exposure.

Capability creates relevance.

The network enables discovery.

Humans choose.

Collaboration creates outcomes.

The ecosystem grows through governed trust.
```

> How should Capability claims become scoped evidence, how should Routing compare eligible candidates, and how should human selection remain authoritative before provider appointment or instruction?
