# B04-PUB-0003 — Glossary

**Status:** Release Candidate 1

This glossary is a Book 04 reader aid. Frozen Book 02 definitions and accepted Book 03 Execution definitions prevail where a term is inherited. Book 04 adds context but does not redefine upstream meaning.

| Term | Book 04 meaning | Primary chapters | Authority |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **AI Agent** | A governed runtime participant that may perform bounded operations through declared Capabilities, Skills, context, permissions, and review dependencies. It does not inherit professional authority. | CH16, CH18–CH19 | Book 03 / Book 04 |
| **Approval** | A Human Review decision that permits an exact reviewed version to proceed for a stated consequence and under stated conditions. Approval is not execution. | CH18–CH19, CH30–CH32 | Book 03 |
| **Artifact** | A usable, versioned business outcome assembled from content, data, Knowledge, Assets, and context, with purpose, provenance, Review, representation, and lineage. It is not automatically a Core Object or Document. | CH28–CH32, CH39 | Book 04 |
| **Asset** | An organization-controlled reusable resource such as a logo, template, image, component, media file, or reference material, with identity, rights, version, and usage scope. | CH28, CH30 | Book 04 |
| **Assistant** | A user-facing support role that helps retrieve, explain, organize, and prepare without independently governing a professional process. | CH16, CH23 | Book 04 |
| **Authorization** | Current Permission and Policy authority to perform an action. It is distinct from professional Approval. | CH08, CH18–CH19, CH37 | Book 03 |
| **Authorized Context** | The minimum sufficient, purpose-bound, permission-filtered context supplied to an eligible consumer for current work. | CH07, CH12–CH13, CH22 | Book 04 |
| **Brain** | The logical governed Knowledge-consumption boundary through which Workplace and Products retrieve and use validated Knowledge. It is not one mandatory service or central memory store. | CH14 | Book 04 |
| **Candidate** | A proposed value, Knowledge item, recommendation, Task, Opportunity, Capability claim, or output that has not yet passed the authority and governance required for canonical or formal status. | CH14, CH17, CH23, CH30 | Book 04 |
| **Candidate Before Canonical** | The rule that derived or proposed material remains a candidate until the appropriate validation, Review, Policy, or Owning Service accepts it. | CH05, CH10, CH14, CH17, CH38–CH39 | Book 04 |
| **Canonical** | Accepted shared meaning or governed formal status under the responsible authority. Completeness, confidence, or presentation quality alone does not make material canonical. | CH04–CH06, CH14, CH38 | Book 02 / Book 04 |
| **Capability** | A governed statement of what an actor, organization, Product, AI Agent, or provider can do under defined conditions. | CH15, CH25, CH34 | Book 02 / Book 04 |
| **Capability Evidence** | Scoped evidence supporting a Capability claim, including source, subject, jurisdiction, service, time, validation state, and limitations. | CH34–CH36 | Book 04 |
| **Client** | A relationship role within an organization’s professional service context. Client identity, instruction, confidentiality, and commercial relationship remain organization-controlled. | CH09, CH23–CH24 | Book 04 |
| **Communication** | A governed message package with content, participants, recipients, attachments, purpose, channel, version, and lifecycle. | CH11, CH19, CH31 | Book 02 / Book 03 |
| **Content** | Semantic expression such as text, image, audio, video, or structured material before or within an Artifact. Content is not automatically an Artifact, Document, or approved output. | CH28–CH31 | Book 04 |
| **Context Assembly** | The process of selecting and combining identity, purpose, sources, provenance, current facts, Knowledge, preferences, and constraints into authorized context. | CH13, CH16–CH18 | Book 04 |
| **Core** | The shared specification foundation that defines reusable professional meaning, contracts, controlled values, and authoritative boundaries. | CH04–CH06, CH20–CH22 | Book 02 |
| **Delivery** | A targeted transfer or availability outcome for defined recipients through a defined channel. Delivery is distinct from send, receipt, acceptance, and business outcome. | CH31–CH32 | Book 03 / Book 04 |
| **Distillery** | A governed process that turns sources and observed material into candidates for Knowledge, Capability Evidence, or other reusable value. Distillery output is not automatically approved Knowledge. | CH10, CH14–CH15, CH36 | Book 04 |
| **Document** | A formal professional object or record under a defined domain contract. A file or Artifact becomes a Document only through the appropriate formal boundary. | CH19, CH29–CH32 | Book 02 / Book 04 |
| **Edit** | A post-render or content change. Technical Edit and material Edit differ; material Edit may require a new Artifact version and renewed Review. | CH30–CH31 | Book 04 |
| **Execution** | The coordination layer that governs progression from valid context and approved intent through Workflows, Tasks, Review, protected action, Owning Services, Events, failure, retry, and trace. | CH04, CH11, CH18–CH19 | Book 03 |
| **Formalization** | The governed transition by which an authorized output is accepted by the responsible Owning Service as a formal object, decision, Communication, or business fact. | CH29, CH32 | Book 04 |
| **Guide** | A bounded user-facing interaction that gathers the minimum missing information and prepares a typed result. A Guide is not automatically a Workflow or AI Agent. | CH16, CH23–CH24 | Book 04 |
| **Handoff** | A typed transfer of purpose, references, source, version, missing information, review state, intended consequence, and return route between Products or layers. | CH18–CH19, CH22, CH25, CH27 | Book 04 |
| **Human Review** | Accountable examination by an eligible person of an exact subject, version, scope, sources, and intended consequence. | CH08, CH11, CH18–CH19, CH30 | Book 03 |
| **Information** | Available source material or observed data whose provenance, freshness, authority, and relevance must remain visible. Information is not automatically Knowledge. | CH13–CH14 | Book 04 |
| **Knowledge** | Governed reusable understanding with provenance, scope, validation, version, and limitations. It supports judgment but does not replace Policy or professional responsibility. | CH10, CH13–CH14, CH36 | Book 02 / Book 04 |
| **Lite** | A lightweight Workplace Product for independent professionals and small teams, centered on attention, private context, recommendation, preparation, Artifact production, and governed Product handoffs. | CH23 | Book 04 profile |
| **Local Vault** | A local or organization-controlled storage and retrieval boundary for private files, data, Knowledge, metadata, and derived value, subject to identity, rights, and synchronization rules. | CH10, CH12, CH23 | Book 04 |
| **MarkReg** | The flagship full-lifecycle international trademark application Product. It composes the journey but does not own shared semantics, Execution authority, provider acceptance, or formal mutations. | CH24 | Book 04 profile |
| **MGSN** | Mark Global Service Network, the capability- and trust-based connection layer among independent Organization Orbits. It is not an open bidding marketplace. | CH25, CH33–CH37 | Book 04 |
| **MGSN Gateway** | The Workplace-facing Product interface for capability discovery, relationship-aware network access, staged disclosure, Routing preparation, and collaboration visibility. | CH25 | Book 04 profile |
| **Next Best Action** | A contextual recommendation for the most valuable permitted next step. It remains a recommendation until a human or governed service formalizes the relevant consequence. | CH17, CH23 | Book 04 |
| **Opportunity Candidate** | A possible commercial opportunity derived from signals or analysis. It is not a formal Opportunity until accepted by the Opportunity Service. | CH09, CH17, CH23 | Book 04 |
| **Orbit** | The independent operating path and organizational boundary through which a professional organization retains identity, clients, data, Knowledge, rules, relationships, commercial autonomy, and responsibility. | CH02–CH07, CH33–CH39 | Book 04 |
| **Organization** | The real legal, commercial, professional, and accountable actor represented operationally through Workplace. | CH03, CH07–CH09 | Book 04 |
| **Organizational Memory** | Private, time-aware, source-aware memory of decisions, patterns, preferences, relationships, and outcomes. It is distinct from shared canonical Knowledge. | CH10, CH12, CH36 | Book 04 |
| **Owning Service** | The Service responsible for validating and performing an authoritative mutation and returning the governed result and applicable Event references. | CH04, CH18–CH19, CH32 | Book 02 / Book 03 |
| **Permission** | Actor-specific authority to access information or request an action. Permission is evaluated with Policy and current context. | CH08, CH12–CH13, CH18 | Book 02 / Book 03 |
| **Policy** | The governing rule set constraining data use, action, Review, retention, security, and other behavior. | CH08, CH12, CH18–CH19, CH37 | Book 02 / Book 03 |
| **Prepared Action** | A versioned, purpose-bound representation of a proposed consequential action, including subject, sources, intended consequence, review dependency, validity, and return route. It is not execution. | CH17–CH19, CH23–CH25 | Book 04 |
| **Private First** | The network principle that an organization first uses its private relationships, then a trusted extended network, and only then limited public discovery where necessary. | CH25, CH33, CH37–CH39 | Book 04 |
| **Product** | A focused user-facing journey with Product-owned experience and state that consumes shared Core, Execution, Workplace context, Knowledge, and Capabilities without absorbing their authority. | CH20–CH27 | Book 04 |
| **Product Loop First** | The discipline of validating a complete Product value loop before extracting repeated responsibilities into shared platform foundations. | CH20–CH21, CH38–CH39 | Book 04 |
| **Publish** | Distribution of approved material to a broader or public audience under defined channel, rights, identity, and confirmation rules. Publish is not merely Render or Delivery. | CH31–CH32 | Book 04 |
| **Recommendation** | A scored, ranked, or explained candidate for human consideration. It is not a decision, Task, Opportunity, Routing decision, or protected action. | CH17, CH23–CH25, CH34 | Book 04 |
| **Render** | Transformation of structured Artifact content, templates, Assets, and data into a usable representation. Render does not create approval. | CH30–CH31 | Book 04 |
| **Review** | A general governed assessment. `Human Review` is the accountable professional form defined by Execution; not every check is Human Review. | CH08, CH11, CH18–CH19 | Book 03 / Book 04 |
| **Routing** | The governed process of identifying and directing a need toward an eligible Capability or provider while preserving evidence, context, selection, approval, instruction, and acceptance boundaries. | CH25, CH34–CH35 | Book 02 / Book 04 |
| **Routing Decision** | The formal organization decision selecting a route or provider candidate. It is distinct from ranking, appointment, instruction, and provider acceptance. | CH19, CH25, CH34 | Book 02 / Book 04 |
| **Skill** | A replaceable implementation of a Capability through a tool, model, prompt, service, or procedure. Skill identity does not define Product or professional meaning. | CH15–CH16, CH20, CH23 | Book 04 |
| **Task Suggestion** | A candidate proposal that work should be created. It is not an active Task until the Task Service accepts it under the applicable contract. | CH11, CH17, CH23 | Book 04 |
| **Trust** | Contextual confidence grounded in relationship, evidence, service, jurisdiction, people, history, confidentiality, and time. It is not one permanent universal score. | CH33–CH37 | Book 04 |
| **Trusted Extended Network** | The relationship-aware network beyond an organization’s private partners but before unrestricted public discovery. | CH25, CH33 | Book 04 |
| **Value Candidate** | A proposed reusable or actionable value derived from Information, Knowledge, patterns, or outcomes. It remains scoped and non-canonical until governed acceptance. | CH10, CH13, CH17, CH23, CH36 | Book 04 |
| **Workplace** | The architecture and platform shell of an independent Organization Orbit. It provides authorized organization context, continuity, operating surfaces, private memory, Product access, and governed handoffs. It is not one Product or tenant account. | CH02–CH07, CH20–CH27 | Book 04 |
| **Workplace Context** | The active authorized frame containing organization, actor, purpose, references, constraints, source classes, preferences, and current work state. | CH07, CH12–CH13, CH22 | Book 04 |
| **Workplace Edition** | An organization-type-specific expression or application of Workplace that preserves the constitutional model while varying features, defaults, and operating surfaces. | CH26 | Book 04 |
