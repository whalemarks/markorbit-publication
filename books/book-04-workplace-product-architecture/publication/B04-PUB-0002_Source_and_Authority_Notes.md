## 4. Historical Naming and Classification Drift

Earlier Book 01–03 planning and manuscript records sometimes use historical sequence labels such as `Book 03 — Workplace Framework`, `Book 04 — Product System`, or describe Workplace as a `product surface` from an Execution-facing viewpoint.

Book 04 establishes the accepted classification:

> Workplace is the architecture and platform shell of an independent Organization Orbit. It may be expressed through user-facing surfaces, but it is not itself reduced to one Product or centrally owned tenant account.

These historical phrases do not require a Book 02 semantic amendment or a Book 03 Execution amendment. They are recorded as non-blocking editorial errata for future upstream publication maintenance. Book 04 must not silently rewrite frozen or owner-accepted upstream publications.

## 5. Source Classes Inside the Manuscript

Book 04 distinguishes:

- canonical architecture statements;
- accepted upstream semantics;
- Book 04 architectural interpretation;
- conceptual models;
- illustrative professional examples;
- future-facing Product profiles;
- non-binding implementation possibilities.

Examples involving trademark filing, client service, providers, fees, communications, evidence, or official outcomes illustrate architecture. They are not jurisdiction-specific legal advice, a filing instruction, a price quotation, or authorization to perform protected action.

## 6. AI and Editorial Provenance

AI-assisted drafting and editing remain subject to human owner review and the Book 04 governance boundary. AI assistance does not create architecture authority, professional approval, or publication approval. Private chain-of-thought is not part of publication provenance; the relevant record is the task, source set, changed material, review, and decision.
