## 1. Purpose

INTEGRATION-01 established deterministic chapter discovery, provenance and accepted correction routing. INTEGRATION-02A converts those routed controls into editorial instructions that can be woven into the actual argument of CH00–CH12.

The objective is not to increase the quantity of governance text. It is to remove obsolete implications, eliminate duplication and make the accepted authority model read as the book's native architecture.

## 2. Batch boundary

```text
Front Matter: CH00–CH01
Part I: CH02–CH06
Part II foundations: CH07–CH12
```

This batch is coherent because the chapters collectively establish the meaning, sovereignty, authority, identity, permission and data boundary of Workplace.

## 3. Editorial operations

Each chapter receives one or more of the following operations:

- **RETAIN** — keep the RC1 thesis or explanation;
- **REPLACE** — remove an interpretation contradicted by accepted vNext authority;
- **MERGE** — combine an RC1 explanation with accepted amendment language;
- **INSERT** — add a missing distinction at the point where the reader first needs it;
- **MOVE** — relocate a concept to avoid repetition or premature detail;
- **NORMALIZE** — align canonical terms and responsibility verbs.

## 4. Governing locks

The edited candidate must preserve these locks:

1. Workplace is an Organization-scoped business sovereignty and operating boundary.
2. Organization and Workplace are related but not identical.
3. Core owns semantic authority, not commercial or formal-state authority.
4. Owning Services alone control formal lifecycle mutation.
5. Workplace carries business authority, accountability, permissions and Product-operation context within governed limits.
6. Physical custody does not determine logical ownership or source authority.
7. Legal or official sources remain authoritative for facts they govern.
8. AI inherits the initiating user, Workplace, Product and purpose scope and cannot enlarge it.
9. Product visibility, Projection and editable presentation do not confer ownership or formal mutation rights.
10. Platform administration does not create general access to participant data.

## 7. Review gates

### Gate A — Coverage

All thirteen chapters have a weave module and provenance reference.

### Gate B — Semantic authority

No chapter implies that Workplace, Product, platform administration, data storage or AI can assume Core, Owning Service, legal-source or professional authority.

### Gate C — Editorial coherence

Corrections must be placed where the concept first matters and must replace conflicting implications rather than coexist with them.

### Gate D — Historical protection

No file under the RC1 manuscript directory may change.
