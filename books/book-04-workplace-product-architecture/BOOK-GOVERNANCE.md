# Book 04 Governance

## 1. Purpose

Book 04 governs the Workplace and Product Architecture chapter map, manuscript boundary, review baseline, and publication progression. It explains how independent professional organizations consume MarkOrbit Core, Execution, Knowledge, Intelligence, Capabilities, Products, Artifact infrastructure, and network services through their Workplaces.

## 2. Source Precedence

```text
1. MarkOrbit Orbital Architecture Canon vNext
2. Frozen Book 02 Core Specification Baseline
3. Owner-accepted Book 03 Execution System
4. Existing Book 01 Operating System principles
5. Owner-confirmed Book 04 direction and accepted reviews
6. Future architecture specifications and Product charters
7. Implementation ADRs and repository code
8. Research and POCs
```

Lower levels must not silently redefine higher-level semantics.

## 3. Constitutional Boundary

Core defines shared semantics.

Execution governs coordinated work.

Workplace provides authorized organization context.

Products provide focused user experience and domain composition.

MGSN connects independent organizational Orbits.

Owning Services change and record formal business facts.

Humans remain professionally accountable.

These statements allocate responsibility. They do not silently modify Book 02 or Book 03 terminology.

## 4. Workplace Authority Boundary

Workplace may provide organization identity, client context, preferences, Knowledge, permissions, accountability, operating surfaces, Product access, private relationships, and organizational memory. The organization remains the real professional, legal, commercial, and accountable actor. Workplace must not be treated as a centrally owned platform account and must not override Core or Execution authority.

## 5. Product Architecture Boundary

Products may compose user experience, domain journeys, and cross-Product context over authorized Workplace, Core, and Execution foundations. Book 04 may describe Product consumption and composition but must not become a Product feature catalog or PRD.

## 6. Information, Knowledge, and Capability Boundary

Information, Knowledge, Intelligence, Capability, and Skill are consumed through governed boundaries. Distillery outputs and other derived records remain candidates until professional validation and governance accept, restrict, or reject them.

## 7. AI and Agent Boundary

Assistants, Guides, and AI Agents may prepare, assemble, recommend, summarize, compare, draft, and support review within authorized context. They may not approve, send, file, submit, pay, instruct providers, mutate protected state, or replace Human Review.

## 8. Artifact and Delivery Boundary

Book 04 may distinguish Asset, Content, Artifact, Document, Human Review, Render, Edit, Delivery, Publish, and Outcome Feedback. It must not treat Artifact as identical to Document or define implementation schemas in the manuscript.

## 9. MGSN and Network Boundary

MGSN connects independent organizational Orbits through Capability, Routing, Evidence, Trust, and governed collaboration. It is not an open bidding marketplace. Network participation must preserve privacy, staged disclosure, human selection, relationship ownership, and professional responsibility.

- Preserve Book 02 frozen semantics and Book 03 Execution authority.
