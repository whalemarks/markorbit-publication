# Book 04 Governance

## 1. Purpose

Book 04 governs the Workplace and Product Architecture scaffold. It explains how independent professional organizations consume MarkOrbit Core, Execution, knowledge, intelligence, capabilities, products and network services through their Workplaces.

## 2. Source Precedence

```text
1. MarkOrbit Orbital Architecture Canon vNext
2. Frozen Book 02 Core Specification Baseline
3. Owner-accepted Book 03 Execution System
4. Existing Book 01 Operating System principles
5. Owner-confirmed Book 04 direction
6. Future architecture specifications and product charters
7. Implementation ADRs and repository code
8. Research and POCs
```

Lower levels must not silently redefine higher-level semantics.

## 3. Constitutional Boundary

Core defines shared semantics.
Execution governs coordinated work.
Workplace provides authorized organization context.
Products provide user experience and domain composition.
MGSN connects independent organizational Orbits.
Owning Services change formal business facts.
Humans remain professionally accountable.

These statements explain responsibility. They do not silently modify Book 02 or Book 03 terminology.

## 4. Workplace Authority Boundary

Workplace may provide organization identity, client context, preferences, knowledge, permissions, accountability and operating surfaces. Workplace must not be treated as a centrally owned platform account and must not override Core or Execution authority.

## 5. Product Architecture Boundary

Products may compose user experience, domain journeys and cross-product context over authorized Workplace, Core and Execution foundations. Book 04 may describe product consumption and composition but must not become a product feature catalog or PRD.

## 6. Information, Knowledge and Capability Boundary

Information, Knowledge, Intelligence, Capability and Skill are consumed through governed boundaries. Distillery candidates remain candidates until professional validation and governance accept them.

## 7. AI and Agent Boundary

Assistants, Guides and AI Agents may prepare, assemble, recommend, summarize and draft within authorized context. They may not approve, send, file, submit, pay, instruct providers, mutate protected state or replace Human Review.

## 8. Artifact and Delivery Boundary

Book 04 may distinguish Asset, Content, Artifact, Document, Delivery, Render, Edit and Publish responsibilities. It must not treat Artifact as identical to Document or define implementation schemas in this scaffold.

## 9. MGSN and Network Boundary

MGSN connects independent organizational Orbits through capability, routing, evidence and trust. It is not an open bidding marketplace, and network participation must preserve privacy, human selection and professional responsibility.

## 10. Editorial Rules

- Use the canonical title: Book 04 — MarkOrbit Workplace and Product Architecture.
- Preserve Book 02 frozen semantics and Book 03 Execution authority.
- Do not write manuscript prose until a later approved drafting task.
- Keep the TOC provisional until owner review.
- Avoid product marketing, PRD language, implementation schemas and deployment architecture.

## 11. Change Control

Changes to Book 04 scope, chapter order, chapter count, governance locks or downstream publication boundaries require an explicit future task. Any proposed Book 02 semantic change must use the Book 02 Change Proposal process.

## 12. Codex Boundary
