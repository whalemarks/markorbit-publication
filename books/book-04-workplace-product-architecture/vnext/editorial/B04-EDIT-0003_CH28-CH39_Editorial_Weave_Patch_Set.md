## CH28 — Content, Artifact and Document

**RETAIN** — Content may be prepared, structured and transformed into durable deliverables.

**SUPERSEDE** — Any implication that Content, Artifact, Document and formal business record are interchangeable.

**WEAVE TEXT**

Content is meaning under preparation. An Artifact is a versioned, provenance-bearing output produced or received through governed work. A Document is an Artifact represented in a document form. None of these becomes an authoritative Customer, Matter, Order, Payment, filing or official-result fact merely because it is stored or displayed in a Workplace.

```text
Content
≠ Artifact
≠ Document
≠ formal business fact
```

An Artifact must retain source, creator or receiver, version identity, purpose, related record references, review state, permissions and retention conditions. Where an Artifact is evidence of an official event, the applicable Owning Service still determines whether and how that evidence changes formal state.

**PLACEMENT** — Insert after the first definition sequence; merge duplicate identity explanations into this passage.

**AUTHORITY** — WP-B authority boundary; WP-C Product Projection boundary; Book 03 Artifact and Execution model.

**CONTINUITY** — Prepares CH29 version and Render distinctions.

## CH29 — Artifact Version, Render and Evidence Lineage

**RETAIN** — Artifacts may have multiple representations and versions.

**SUPERSEDE** — Any implication that a Render is the Artifact itself or that rendering creates a new approved version automatically.

**WEAVE TEXT**

A Render is a representation of an exact Artifact version for a defined channel or audience. PDF, image, portal preview, print view and message attachment may all render the same Artifact version without changing its substantive content.

```text
Artifact version
→ Render
Render
≠ material Edit
≠ new approved version
```

Every Render must remain traceable to its source Artifact version and rendering conditions. A rendering defect may require correction, but a substantive change creates a new Artifact version and re-enters the applicable review path.

**PLACEMENT** — Replace broad statements that treat file generation as completion; insert before Edit discussion.

**AUTHORITY** — WP-C Projection mechanics; accepted Artifact/version controls.

**CONTINUITY** — Leads directly to CH30 material Edit and re-review.

## CH30 — Edit, Review and Exact-Version Control

**RETAIN** — Human professionals may revise prepared outputs before delivery.

**SUPERSEDE** — Any implication that approval of one version covers later edited versions.

**WEAVE TEXT**

An Edit is material when it changes meaning, scope, legal effect, recipient understanding, commercial terms, evidence or actionability. A material Edit creates a new version and invalidates reliance on prior version-specific review.

```text
approved version A
+ material Edit
= version B requiring applicable re-review
```

Review and approval must identify the exact version examined. Cosmetic rendering changes may follow a lighter control path only where policy defines them as non-material and preserves evidence of the determination.

**PLACEMENT** — Integrate into the chapter’s Edit/Review sequence and remove any generalized approval language.

**AUTHORITY** — Book 03 exact-version Human Review; WP-B formal-state boundary.

**CONTINUITY** — Establishes the precondition for CH31 Delivery and Publish.

## CH31 — Delivery, Publish and External Communication

**RETAIN** — Work may be delivered privately or published to an audience.

**SUPERSEDE** — Any implication that Delivery, Publish, filing, submission and formal recordal are a single action.

**WEAVE TEXT**

Delivery makes an approved Artifact available to an authorized recipient. Publish exposes approved Content or an Artifact to a defined audience through an authorized surface. Filing, official submission, payment and provider instruction are distinct External Protected Actions governed by their own authority and evidence paths.

```text
Review
→ Approval
→ Delivery or Publish authorization
→ governed external action
→ evidence
→ applicable Owning Service result
```

```text
Delivery
≠ Publish
≠ filing
≠ official acceptance
≠ formal-state mutation
```

Each action must preserve exact version, actor, recipient or audience, channel, time, purpose and resulting evidence.

**PLACEMENT** — Replace any linear chain that collapses Delivery, Publish and formalization.

**AUTHORITY** — Book 03 protected-action controls; WP-B Owning Service authority; WP-C Projection boundary.

**CONTINUITY** — Leads to CH32 reconciliation with formal records.

## CH32 — Formal Records, Reconciliation and Return

**RETAIN** — Outcomes and received evidence must reconnect to Workplace context.

**SUPERSEDE** — Any implication that receipt or display of an outcome automatically changes formal state.

**WEAVE TEXT**

A received Artifact, acknowledgement, decision, certificate, invoice or Return is evidence requiring provenance and acceptance. It may be visible immediately while remaining unaccepted as a formal business fact.

```text
received
≠ verified
≠ accepted
≠ reconciled
≠ formal-state mutation
```

The applicable authority path must verify identity, scope, source, version, completeness and unresolved conditions. Only the relevant Owning Service may record the resulting Customer, Matter, Order, Payment, filing or official-status change. Cross-Product or cross-Workplace Return remains subject to originating acceptance.

**PLACEMENT** — Insert before the chapter conclusion and merge with outcome-reconciliation discussion.

**AUTHORITY** — WP-B formal-state authority; WP-C Handoff/Return distinction; WP-E originating acceptance.

**CONTINUITY** — Bridges Artifact completion into CH33 network participation.

## CH34 — Routing, Human Selection and Appointment

**RETAIN** — Routing may surface appropriate provider candidates.

**SUPERSEDE** — Any implication that ranking, matching or availability appoints a Provider.

**WEAVE TEXT**

Routing evaluates governed candidate information against a defined need and purpose. It may rank, filter or explain candidates, but it cannot create appointment, instruction or protected-action authority.

```text
candidate surfaced
≠ candidate selected
≠ Provider appointed
≠ Provider instructed
≠ External Protected Action authorized
```

Authorized human or governed business acceptance must confirm identity, scope, conflicts, commercial terms, responsibility and required credentials. Selection evidence must remain traceable, including reasons for overriding or rejecting recommendations.

**PLACEMENT** — Replace any automatic-matching language and place before appointment mechanics.

**AUTHORITY** — WP-D Routing and appointment; WP-E collaboration acceptance.

**RETAIN** — Outcomes may improve future decisions and network quality.

**WEAVE TEXT**

Trust is an evidence set interpreted for a particular purpose, service type, jurisdiction, time and evaluator context. It may include credentials, acceptance history, timeliness, quality evidence, disputes, corrections, non-performance and verified outcomes.

Trust evidence

Every Trust assertion must preserve source, scope, evaluator, time, confidence and challenge status. Negative evidence cannot be silently removed merely because a relationship ends or a later positive outcome exists. Aggregation must respect privacy, contractual and legal limits.

## CH37 — Governance Across Originating and Provider Workplaces

**RETAIN** — Independent Workplaces may collaborate across an orbital ecosystem.

**SUPERSEDE** — Any implication that collaboration creates a shared authority domain.

**WEAVE TEXT**

Cross-Workplace collaboration is a typed, bounded, accepted and auditable exchange between sovereign operating boundaries. The Originating Workplace retains customer, commercial, instruction and approval context unless an explicit lawful transfer occurs. The Execution Provider Workplace receives only the context required to evaluate or perform the accepted work and remains responsible for provider-side execution and evidence.

```text
Originating Workplace
→ scoped authorization
→ typed Handoff
→ destination acceptance
→ governed execution
→ typed Return
→ originating acceptance
```

```text
relationship
≠ access
access
≠ authority
Handoff sent
≠ accepted
Return received
≠ accepted as truth
```

Permissions must define purpose, record and Artifact scope, allowed actions, expiry, revocation and audit obligations.

**PLACEMENT** — Replace generalized collaboration language with the full acceptance sequence.

**AUTHORITY** — WP-B authority separation; WP-E collaboration model.

**CONTINUITY** — Leads to CH38 exit, revocation and portability.

## CH38 — Portability, Exit and Revocation

**RETAIN** — Organizations should be able to preserve continuity when changing Products, deployments or platform relationships.

**SUPERSEDE** — Any implication that every platform or network asset moves with a Workplace.

**WEAVE TEXT**

Workplace portability covers organization-controlled configuration, membership, permissions, Customers, Orders, Matters, Payments, private Knowledge, audit history and permitted Artifacts, subject to law, source restrictions, retention, privacy and minimization requirements.

Conditionally portable materials include licensed content, third-party records, provider evidence, derived intelligence and shared collaboration records. Their transfer depends on authority, contract and provenance.

Platform-owned shared Provider Supply, global Routing assets and models, network-wide Trust aggregates, fraud intelligence, security controls and other participants’ private data do not move with a Workplace.

```text
Workplace portability
≠ portability of the platform network
```

Exit and revocation stop future access, Projection, Handoff and credentials while preserving authoritative records, legal retention and audit evidence. Export copy, source deletion, authority transfer and migration completion are separate events.

**PLACEMENT** — Insert as the central portability classification and replace unconditional export language.

**AUTHORITY** — WP-E portability and exit model; WP-C Installation suspension/revocation.

**CONTINUITY** — Supplies the durable boundary summarized in CH39.
