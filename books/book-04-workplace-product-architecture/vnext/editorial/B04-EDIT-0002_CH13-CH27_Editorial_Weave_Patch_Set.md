## Shared editorial rule

```text
Information informs.
Knowledge explains.
Intelligence evaluates.
AI prepares and recommends.
Human professionals review and decide.
Execution coordinates accepted work.
Owning Services record formal business facts.
```

Neither visibility, convenience, recommendation quality nor Product presentation changes this authority chain.

## CH13 — Information Sources and Governed Consumption

**RETAIN:** Workplace consumes external, platform, organizational and matter-specific information to support professional work.

**REPLACE:** Any implication that ingestion converts external information into organization-owned truth.

**INSERT after the source-class introduction:**

> Information enters the Workplace with a source class, provenance, permitted purpose, retention rule and confidence state. Physical storage or indexing does not replace the originating source's legal or evidentiary authority. The Workplace may organize and use information within its authorized context, but it must preserve where the information came from, what it may support and what remains unresolved.

**NORMALIZE:** Use `source information`, `accepted organizational Knowledge`, `derived intelligence` and `formal record` as distinct terms.

**Authority:** WP-B source-authority and custody controls.

**Continuity:** Establishes the evidence basis for CH14 Knowledge formation.

## CH14 — Knowledge Formation and Organizational Context

**RETAIN:** Knowledge makes information reusable within the organization's operating context.

**REPLACE:** Any wording that treats all indexed content as accepted Knowledge.

**MERGE into the Knowledge lifecycle:**

> Knowledge is formed through governed interpretation, not through ingestion alone. A candidate Knowledge item must retain its supporting sources, authorship or derivation, scope, review state and intended use. Organization-private Knowledge remains inside the Workplace boundary unless a purpose-bound sharing path is authorized. Shared or licensed Knowledge remains subject to its source and licence conditions even when projected inside a Product.

**INSERT distinction:**

```text
stored content
≠ accepted Knowledge
≠ formal business fact
```

**Authority:** WP-B private Knowledge, provenance and source-class controls.

**Continuity:** Provides the governed inputs used by CH15 Intelligence.

## CH15 — Intelligence and Derived Evaluation

**RETAIN:** Intelligence derives patterns, comparisons and evaluations from governed inputs.

**REPLACE:** Any implication that derived intelligence owns or replaces the underlying record.

**INSERT after the Intelligence definition:**

> Intelligence is a derived and contextual evaluation. It must identify the inputs, method, time, assumptions, confidence and authorized audience that make the evaluation meaningful. It may guide attention or judgment, but it does not become the source authority for the Customer, Matter, Order, Payment, filing or official outcome from which it was derived.

**NORMALIZE:** Replace absolute scoring language with contextual evidence and bounded evaluation.

**Authority:** WP-B formal-state/source-authority separation.

**Continuity:** Prepares CH16 AI use and CH17 recommendations.

## CH17 — Recommendations and Next Best Action

**RETAIN:** Recommendations and Next Best Action help the organization identify useful work.

**REPLACE:** Any sequence that collapses recommendation, selection, approval and execution.

**MERGE into the recommendation lifecycle:**

```text
signal
→ candidate recommendation
→ contextual explanation
→ authorized human selection
→ required review or approval
→ governed execution
→ Owning Service result
```

> A recommendation must expose its purpose, evidence, assumptions, confidence, urgency and consequences. Ranking improves attention; it does not create duty, appointment, approval or authority. Dismissal, deferral and rejection remain valid governed outcomes.

**Authority:** WP-B authority chain and WP-D routing distinction by analogy.

**Continuity:** Leads from intelligence to reusable capability in CH18.

## CH18 — Capability, Skill, Assistant and Guide Placement

**RETAIN:** Capabilities, Skills, Assistants and Guides make professional work reusable.

**REPLACE:** Any wording that locates authority inside the reusable component itself.

**INSERT after component definitions:**

> A Capability describes what governed work can be supported. A Skill packages a reusable method. An Assistant composes interaction and preparation. A Guide communicates a controlled path. None independently owns the Customer, Matter or formal outcome. Their effective use is determined by the Workplace, Product Installation, initiating principal, purpose, applicable policy and the Owning Service that controls any resulting state change.

**NORMALIZE:** Components `support`, `prepare`, `recommend` and `invoke governed contracts`; they do not `approve`, `appoint`, `file`, `pay` or `record official truth` by themselves.

**Authority:** WP-B and WP-C permission/installation controls.

**Continuity:** Hands prepared work to CH19 Human Review and Owning Service handoff.

## CH19 — Human Review, Approval and Owning Service Handoff

**RETAIN:** Human Review is the professional decision boundary before protected or formal action.

**REPLACE:** Any implication that approval itself mutates formal state.

**INSERT at the decision chain:**

```text
prepared candidate
≠ reviewed candidate
≠ approved candidate
≠ execution completion
≠ formal-state mutation
```

> Human Review records who reviewed what exact version, against which evidence and with what decision. Approval authorizes the next governed step within its scope; it does not itself create the formal Customer, Matter, Order, Payment, filing or official result. The applicable Owning Service validates the typed command, records the formal mutation and returns a typed result. Rejection, expiry or material Edit invalidates any approval that no longer matches the exact candidate.

**Authority:** WP-B approval/formal-mutation boundary and WP-C typed Handoff/Return model.

**Continuity:** Establishes the authority chain consumed by Product architecture in CH20.

## CH22 — Product Installation and Workplace Participation

**RETAIN:** A Workplace can adopt and configure Products for its organization.

**REPLACE:** Tenant-style language that treats installation as a copy of the Product or a universal access grant.

**INSERT as the controlling definition:**

> A Product Installation is the Workplace-scoped governed participation relationship through which a Product is entitled, activated, configured and authorized for use. It records the installation identity, Workplace, edition or plan, authorized users, permitted record scope, capabilities, configuration, purpose, Handoff and Return contracts, audit requirements, suspension and revocation state.

```text
entitlement
≠ activation
≠ authorized user
≠ authorized record scope
≠ formal-state authority
```

> Disabling or revoking an installation stops future access, projection and Handoff within that installation. It does not erase authoritative records, completed work, audit history or legal retention duties.

**Authority:** WP-C Installation model.

**Continuity:** Enables contextual projection in CH23.

## CH27 — Cross-Product Handoff and Lifecycle Continuity

**RETAIN:** Work may continue across Products without losing professional context.

**REPLACE:** Seamless-transition language that implies shared authority or unrestricted shared state.

**INSERT as the continuity contract:**

```text
Projection
≠ Handoff
≠ Return
```

> Cross-Product continuity preserves stable record references, originating Workplace, Product Installation identity, authority source, purpose, permissions, provenance and Artifact lineage. A typed Handoff transfers only the context required for the destination Product or Workplace to evaluate or perform the stated purpose. Destination receipt is not acceptance; acceptance is not completion. A typed Return carries outcome and evidence back to the origin, where acceptance is still required before any local formal-state mutation.

```text
Handoff sent
≠ destination accepted
≠ action completed
Return received
≠ accepted as truth
≠ formal-state mutation
```

**Authority:** WP-C and WP-E Handoff/Return controls.

**Continuity:** Ends Part IV with a controlled bridge to CH28 Content, Artifact and Delivery.
