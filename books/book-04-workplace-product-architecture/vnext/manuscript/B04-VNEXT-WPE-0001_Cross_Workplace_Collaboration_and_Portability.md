# B04-VNEXT-WPE-0001 — Cross-Workplace Collaboration and Portability

**Status:** vNext amendment candidate — WP-E  
**Applies to:** CH12, CH27, CH32–CH37  
**Historical baseline:** Book 04 RC1 remains unchanged.

## 1. Collaboration Preserves Independent Workplaces

Cross-Workplace collaboration connects independent business-sovereignty boundaries. It does not create one shared super-Workplace.

```text
access grant ≠ ownership transfer
shared visibility ≠ shared authority
collaboration ≠ boundary collapse
```

Every collaboration must identify the Originating Workplace, destination Workplace, purpose, authorized objects, permitted actions, duration, responsible users and audit requirements.

## 2. Typed Handoff and Acceptance

A Handoff is a governed transfer of scoped context for a stated purpose.

```text
Handoff prepared
≠ Handoff sent
≠ destination received
≠ destination accepted
≠ work authorized
```

Destination acceptance must be explicit. The Execution Provider Workplace may reject, narrow or request clarification. Acceptance does not transfer the Originating Workplace's customer relationship or commercial sovereignty.

## 3. Typed Return and Originating Acceptance

A Return carries results, evidence, status, Artifacts or unresolved issues back to the Originating Workplace.

```text
Return received
≠ accepted as reliable
≠ accepted into local context
≠ formal-state mutation
```

The Originating Workplace must validate provenance, scope and completion state. An Owning Service performs any formal-state mutation under its own rules.

## 4. Purpose-Limited Access

Cross-Workplace access must be minimum necessary, role-bounded, time-bounded, revocable and auditable. A Provider receiving one Matter does not receive general access to the originating customer's portfolio, private Knowledge or unrelated Work.

Revocation stops future access. It does not erase valid historical evidence, completed obligations, legal retention records or accepted Returns.

## 5. CH12 — Permissions Across Boundaries

CH12 must clarify that Workplace permission does not automatically propagate to another Workplace. Cross-boundary permission requires an explicit collaboration contract or Handoff scope.

AI, Products and users inherit the narrower of source authorization, destination acceptance and purpose scope.

## 6. CH27 — Product Handoffs

CH27 must distinguish Product-to-Product context continuity from Workplace-to-Workplace authority transfer. A Product Installation may prepare a Handoff, but the relevant Workplace and Owning Service boundaries govern sending, acceptance and state mutation.

## 7. CH32 — Data Portability

Portability means the ability to obtain and move authorized records without losing provenance. It does not mean every visible or derived asset belongs to the Workplace.

Portable organization-controlled assets may include:

- organization identity and configuration;
- organization-controlled customer and relationship records;
- private Knowledge and organization-authored templates;
- authorized Artifacts;
- Product Installation configuration exports;
- audit and activity exports allowed by policy.

```text
export copy ≠ source deletion
export copy ≠ authority transfer
export completion ≠ migration completion
```

## 8. CH33 — Private and Trusted Networks

Private network membership does not change the ownership of each participant's customer relationships or private Knowledge. Shared network records require defined joint or platform authority and participant visibility rules.

## 9. CH34 — Routing Assets

Platform shared Provider Supply, routing logic, ranking models and network-wide matching intelligence are platform-owned network assets. A Workplace may export its own instructions, selection history and transaction records where authorized, but cannot export the network itself as Workplace property.

```text
participant selection history
≠ global Routing model
```

## 10. CH35 — Trust Evidence

Participant-specific evidence, completed-work history and accepted feedback may be exportable subject to confidentiality, law and network policy. Network-wide Trust assets, anti-fraud signals, confidential peer evidence and platform-derived global scores are not automatically portable.

```text
visible trust signal ≠ owned trust dataset
```

## 11. CH36 — Exit and Migration

Exit requires a controlled plan covering export scope, unresolved obligations, active Handoffs, pending Returns, retention, revocation, credential closure and post-exit access.

Migration must preserve stable references, provenance, audit history, acceptance states and responsibility for open Work. It must not silently convert candidate or shared network information into organization-owned facts.

## 12. CH37 — Governance and Disputes

Disputes must be evaluated by authority dimension: business sovereignty, Core semantics, formal-state authority, custody and legal/source authority. A storage operator, platform administrator or network governor does not gain customer relationship ownership merely by operating infrastructure.

## 13. Portability Classification

### Portable
Organization-controlled records, configuration, private Knowledge, authorized templates, owned Artifacts and permitted audit exports.

### Conditionally portable
Licensed materials, third-party records, collaboration evidence, Provider evidence, derived intelligence and shared records, subject to contract, law, confidentiality and provenance.

### Non-portable as Workplace-owned assets
Platform shared Provider Supply, global Routing models, network-wide Trust assets, security controls, fraud intelligence and other participants' private data.

## 14. Controlling Summary

```text
Workplace portability
≠ portability of the platform network

participant relationship data
≠ shared Provider Supply
≠ global Routing assets
≠ network-wide Trust assets

Handoff sent
≠ destination accepted

Return received
≠ originating acceptance
```

This amendment controls the assigned WP-E interpretations after Owner merge. It does not authorize production export, deletion, migration or automatic cross-Workplace action.
