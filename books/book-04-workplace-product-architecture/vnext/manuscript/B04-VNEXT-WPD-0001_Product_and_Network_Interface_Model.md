# B04-VNEXT-WPD-0001 — Product and Network Interface Model

## Status
Controlled Book 04 vNext amendment candidate for WP-D. RC1 remains immutable.

## 1. Interface rule
A Product may provide a focused journey, but it does not become the Workplace, the Organization, the Owning Service or the legal/professional authority merely because it is the visible interface.

```text
Product experience
≠ Workplace sovereignty
≠ formal-state authority
≠ legal or professional responsibility
```

## 2. MarkReg interface
MarkReg is the trademark lifecycle Product. It may be installed in an existing Workplace and consume authorized organization, client and Matter context.

Where MarkReg itself operates a direct customer-facing trademark business, the accountable operator must have an isolated Workplace. The self-operated business is not a hidden Product tenant and does not share another participant's private commercial context.

```text
MarkReg Product
≠ MarkReg self-operated Workplace
```

The self-operated Workplace controls its own customer relationships, commercial terms, private Knowledge, permissions and approval rules. MarkReg still relies on Core semantics and Owning Services for formal-state authority.

## 3. Lite interface
Lite is a lightweight Workplace Product for independent professionals and small teams. It may compose Today, recommendations, tasks, communications and lightweight client projections.

Lite does not own projected facts. A client-facing Lite surface is a Product Projection governed by the relevant Product Installation, purpose, permission and source authority.

```text
Lite surface
≠ Workplace
Lite projection
≠ Customer or Matter authority
```

## 4. Sites interface
Sites is the public-brand and content Projection Product. It may publish approved public pages, resources, campaigns, intake forms and contact surfaces.

A visitor action may create a candidate intake or Handoff. It does not automatically create an accepted Customer, Order, Matter, engagement or Payment fact.

```text
public submission
→ candidate intake
→ governed acceptance
→ applicable Owning Service mutation
```

Sites does not become the authority over Customer, Order, Matter, Payment or professional-service records.

## 5. MGSN interface
MGSN Connection / Gateway is the Workplace-scoped interface for discovery, evidence review, candidate routing, selection and collaboration.

MGSN Network is the platform-owned and platform-governed managed professional-service network, including network rules, shared supply, routing assets, network Trust assets and governance.

```text
MGSN Connection / Gateway
≠ MGSN Network
participant relationship
≠ platform-owned customer relationship
network supply
≠ portable participant asset
```

## 6. Originating and Execution Provider Workplaces
The Originating Workplace retains originating customer, commercial, instruction, approval and relationship context.

The Execution Provider Workplace receives only the purpose-limited context needed to evaluate or perform the accepted work. It carries provider-side execution, professional, evidence and Return responsibilities.

```text
Originating Workplace
→ typed Handoff
→ Execution Provider Workplace acceptance
→ governed execution
→ typed Return
→ Originating Workplace acceptance
```

A Handoff does not transfer the customer relationship. Provider acceptance does not grant access beyond the accepted purpose. A Return does not mutate the originating formal state until accepted through the applicable authority path.

## 7. Routing and appointment
Routing, ranking, matching and Trust evidence support decision-making. They do not appoint a Provider.

```text
candidate surfaced
≠ Provider appointed
Provider available
≠ Provider authorized
Trust signal
≠ universal score
```

Appointment requires authorized human or governed business acceptance, with disclosed scope, commercial terms and responsibility.

## 8. Collaboration outcomes and Trust
Outcomes may generate evidence and Trust feedback. The source, scope, evaluator and context must remain traceable. Network-level learning may use governed abstractions, but private relationship history and participant-owned commercial context remain protected.

## 9. Chapter application
- CH23: Lite is a lightweight Workplace Product with optional projections.
- CH24: MarkReg self-operated business requires an isolated Workplace.
- CH25: MGSN Gateway is a Workplace-scoped connection, not the Network itself.
- CH33: private participant relationships remain distinct from platform-owned network supply.
- CH34: routing supports human selection and never auto-appoints.
- CH35: outcomes and Trust remain contextual, governed and provenance-bearing.

## 10. Supersession
Upon Owner merge, this amendment controls WP-D interpretations for the chapters listed above. Unaffected RC1 meaning and accepted WP-B/WP-C controls remain intact.
