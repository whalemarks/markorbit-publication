## Constitutional authority model

Workplace is the **Organization-scoped business-sovereignty, accountability, permission, Product-operation and projection boundary**.

That statement establishes one authority dimension. It does not grant every kind of authority. MarkOrbit distinguishes five dimensions:

| Dimension | Controlled responsibility |
| --- | --- |
| Workplace business sovereignty | customer relationship, private commercial context, internal Knowledge, approvals, organization policy and accountable business operation |
| Core semantic authority | controlled meaning, identifiers, contracts, state vocabulary and shared semantic constraints |
| Owning Service formal-state authority | creation or mutation of the applicable formal business fact |
| Physical custody | storage, transmission, processing, backup and infrastructure administration |
| Legal / source authority | legal rights, official-source authority, evidentiary authority and professional responsibility |

The same record or Artifact may involve all five dimensions at once. They remain distinct.

```text
Visibility ≠ authority
Custody ≠ ownership
Administration ≠ business sovereignty
Approval ≠ formal-state mutation
Professional responsibility ≠ platform control
```

# Chapter correction modules

## CH02 — Why Workplace Exists

The chapter’s definition of Workplace as an organizational operating boundary is strengthened as follows:

> Workplace exists because an Organization requires a durable boundary within which it retains its customer relationships, private commercial context, internal Knowledge, approvals, permissions and accountable business operation while consuming shared MarkOrbit capabilities.

Workplace is not merely the place where organizational context converges. It is the boundary through which the Organization exercises business sovereignty. That sovereignty is bounded: it does not redefine Core semantics, mutate formal facts owned by another service, convert infrastructure custody into legal authority or remove professional obligations.

The minimum constitutional formula is:

```text
Organization
→ operates through its Workplace
→ retains business sovereignty and accountability
→ consumes shared semantics and governed services
→ without absorbing their authority
```

A tenant partition may implement isolation, but tenancy does not define the authority model. A Product interface may expose work, but an interface does not own the organization context it displays.

## CH03 — Orbit Principle and Organizational Autonomy

Organizational autonomy means the right and responsibility to govern the Organization’s own business context. It is not absolute independence from shared contracts, official sources or professional rules.

```text
Autonomy within the Workplace
+ semantic conformance to Core
+ formal mutation through Owning Services
+ legal and professional constraints
= governed orbital participation
```

“Each in its own orbit” therefore protects business sovereignty without creating isolated truth systems. The Organization may decide whom it serves, how it packages services, which private Knowledge it uses and who may approve work. It may not privately redefine a shared Core state or treat a local screen action as an official filing, payment, registration or recordal.

## CH04 — Position Between Core, Execution, Products and Network

The responsibility model is replaced for vNext by the five-authority view:

```text
Core
→ semantic authority

Owning Service
→ formal-state authority

Workplace
→ business sovereignty, accountability and permission boundary

Execution
→ governed coordination under accepted contracts

Product
→ focused journey operating through a Workplace-scoped installation

Infrastructure
→ physical custody and processing

Official source / qualified professional
→ applicable legal, evidentiary and professional authority
```

Execution coordinates work but does not become the owner of every business fact it touches. Product presents and composes journeys but does not acquire the Organization’s customer relationship. Workplace can approve and initiate governed action but cannot bypass the Owning Service that records the formal result.

## CH05 — Workplace Principles

The durable Workplace principles include the following authority locks:

1. **Organization scope:** every Workplace belongs to one accountable Organization context.
2. **Business sovereignty:** the Workplace retains customer, commercial, Knowledge, approval and accountability context.
3. **Semantic conformance:** local business policy cannot silently redefine Core meaning.
4. **Formal-state restraint:** Workplace interactions do not themselves mutate formal facts unless the Workplace is invoking the applicable Owning Service under an authorized contract.
5. **Custody neutrality:** hosting, backup, support and platform administration do not confer business or legal authority.
6. **Source respect:** official records, evidence and professional determinations retain their own authority.
7. **Least-authority access:** every human, Product, AI Agent and administrator receives only the minimum scope required.
8. **Purpose limitation:** access and sharing are constrained by the stated purpose.
9. **Auditability:** exceptional access, approval, Handoff and formal mutation must be attributable.
10. **Reversibility:** support access, temporary grants and synchronized projections must be revocable where the governing contract permits.

## CH06 — Workplace Boundaries and Non-Goals

Workplace is responsible for:

- Organization identity and accountable operating context;
- users, teams, roles and permission policy;
- customer relationship and private commercial context;
- internal Knowledge and organizational memory;
- approval and escalation policy;
- Product availability and projection policy;
- organization-side acceptance of Handoffs and Returns;
- audit context for organization-controlled decisions.

Workplace is not automatically responsible for:

- defining Core semantics;
- recording every formal Matter, payment, filing or official outcome;
- owning platform-wide provider supply or Trust assets;
- acquiring rights merely because data is hosted by the platform;
- giving administrators unrestricted access to Partner data;
- transferring professional responsibility to AI or infrastructure operators.

The existence of a record inside a Workplace view means that the record is available in an authorized context. It does not prove that Workplace is the record’s formal owner.

## CH07 — Organization Identity and Workplace Context

Organization and Workplace are related but not interchangeable.

```text
Organization
= accountable participant identity

Workplace
= Organization-scoped operating, permission and projection boundary
```

An Organization may have more than one controlled Workplace when legal separation, operating isolation or product configuration requires it. A Workplace may not merge unrelated Organizations merely for interface convenience.

Organization identity must remain stable across Product installations, projections and governed collaboration. Workplace context adds operating policy, users, permissions, private commercial context and enabled Products; it does not replace the Organization identity.

## CH08 — People, Roles, Permissions and Accountability

A role grants bounded permission inside an identified Workplace. It does not create authority outside that Workplace or beyond the role’s purpose.

Permission evaluation must consider:

```text
Organization
+ Workplace
+ user or service identity
+ role
+ Product Installation
+ object or context
+ purpose
+ requested action
+ time and risk conditions
```

Professional accountability remains attached to the responsible person or Organization. Delegation to a team member, service provider or AI Agent does not erase that accountability. A platform administrator is not a Partner administrator by default. Support personnel may access Partner data only through an explicit, purpose-limited, time-bounded and auditable support grant.

## CH09 — Clients, Relationships, Services and Business Rules

The Originating Workplace retains the customer relationship and originating commercial context unless a disclosed legal or commercial arrangement explicitly provides otherwise.

This includes:

- customer identity and relationship history;
- engagement and approval context;
- private pricing and margin policy;
- organization-specific service packaging;
- internal provider preferences;
- communication policy;
- business risk and escalation rules.

A shared platform may process, project or synchronize parts of this context. Such processing does not convert the customer into a platform-owned relationship. Network participation, referral, provider execution and payment routing do not silently transfer originating business sovereignty.

## CH10 — Private Knowledge, AI Context and Organizational Memory

Private Knowledge remains governed by the Workplace that owns or lawfully controls it. Shared Knowledge and Core semantics may be combined with private context only under an authorized purpose.

AI context assembly must preserve source class:

```text
shared controlled Knowledge
private Workplace Knowledge
customer-provided information
official source material
AI-generated candidate
```

These sources must not be flattened into one undifferentiated memory. AI-generated material is a candidate, not an official source or approved organizational decision. Cross-customer or cross-Workplace reuse is prohibited unless a governing policy explicitly permits a de-identified or otherwise lawful transformation.

Organizational memory must support correction, provenance and access withdrawal. Physical storage by a platform does not make the platform the business owner of that memory.

## CH11 — Work, Review, Communication and Operating Surfaces

A Workplace surface is a projection of authorized context. The surface does not inherit the full authority of the underlying record.

```text
Displayed ≠ owned
Editable field ≠ formal mutation right
Prepared communication ≠ sent communication
Approved action ≠ completed external action
```

Where a screen allows the user to request an action, the request must invoke the applicable governed contract. The relevant Owning Service remains responsible for recording the formal state. Communication send truth, payment truth, official filing truth and lifecycle truth remain with their applicable authorities.

## CH12 — Data Boundaries, Local Vault and Authorized Synchronization

Data boundary design must distinguish logical authority from physical location.

A record may be physically stored in cloud infrastructure while remaining under Workplace business sovereignty. A local copy may exist without becoming the formal source of truth. A synchronized projection may support work without granting unrestricted reuse.

Every synchronized data flow must identify:

- source authority;
- receiving Workplace and Product Installation;
- purpose;
- permitted fields;
- sensitivity;
- retention and deletion policy;
- update direction;
- conflict rule;
- audit requirement;
- whether the copy is authoritative, cached, projected or evidentiary.

Local Vault is a future custody and synchronization mechanism. It does not alter semantic authority, formal-state authority or legal rights by itself.

Platform administration versus Partner-data access is governed by this lock:

```text
Infrastructure administration
≠ permission to inspect Partner content
```

Exceptional access requires explicit authorization, a stated purpose, minimum scope, time limit, logging and review.

## CH16 — Assistant, Guide and AI Agent in the Workplace

Every Assistant, Guide and AI Agent inherits the full scope constraints of the initiating context.

```text
AI authority
≤ initiating user authority
≤ Workplace permission policy
≤ Product Installation scope
≤ purpose and review policy
```

AI may receive less authority than the initiating user. It may never receive more merely because the platform can technically access additional data.

An AI Agent must not:

- inspect another Workplace without an accepted Handoff or projection;
- combine unrelated customer contexts;
- treat platform administration access as business permission;
- mutate formal state outside the Owning Service contract;
- present generated content as official evidence;
- perform External Protected Action without explicit authorization and required Human Review.

## CH19 — Human Review, Approval and Owning Service Handoff

Human approval and formal-state mutation are separate events.

```text
Preparation
→ Human Review
→ approval within Workplace authority
→ governed invocation of Owning Service
→ formal-state mutation or recorded outcome
→ Return / projection to Workplace
```

Approval means that the authorized person accepts the proposed action within the Organization’s policy. It does not prove that an external filing was submitted, a payment was settled, a message was sent or an official record changed.

The Owning Service must return a typed result, including acceptance, rejection, pending state, evidence or error. Workplace must not infer completion from dispatch alone.

## CH37 — Governance, Privacy and Professional Responsibility Across Orbits

Cross-Workplace collaboration does not create a shared unrestricted data domain.

Each exchange must identify:

- Originating Workplace;
- receiving or Execution Provider Workplace;
- purpose;
- authorized context;
- professional responsibility;
- retention limits;
- permitted onward disclosure;
- expected Return;
- audit and revocation rules.

The Originating Workplace retains the originating customer relationship and commercial context. The Execution Provider Workplace retains provider-side operational judgment and professional responsibility. Platform governance may enforce network rules without absorbing either Workplace’s business sovereignty.

Trust, provider performance and network operating data may have platform-governed forms. They must not expose private Partner or customer context beyond the accepted purpose.

## WP-B conformance checklist

A future integrated Book 04 version conforms to WP-B only when it preserves all of the following:

```text
Workplace is Organization-scoped.
Business sovereignty is distinct from semantic authority.
Formal-state mutation remains with the applicable Owning Service.
Custody creates no implied business or legal authority.
Official and professional authority remain source-specific.
Administrator access is not automatic Partner-data access.
AI inherits Workplace, Product, user and purpose scope.
Cross-Workplace access is typed, limited and auditable.
```
