### 5.2 Canonical responsibility language

CH04 was aligned with the canonical responsibility lock so that short verbs do not understate authority or imply that Execution merely coordinates without governance.

### 5.3 Artifact preview, final render, material Edit and re-review

CH30 and CH31 were clarified so that preview rendering may precede Review, final rendering follows approval for a defined use, and material post-review Edit requires Review reconsideration.

The conceptual sequence now preserves:

```text
Preparation
→ Preview Render Where Useful
→ Review
→ Approval for Defined Use
→ Final Render
→ Optional Edit
→ Re-Review Where Material
→ Delivery or Publish
```

This does not create one universal Artifact lifecycle or status enumeration.

### 5.4 Formalization boundary

CH32 was revised so that an Artifact may become input to a formal governed record or domain fact without being described as automatically becoming a generic Core record.

The chapter now distinguishes:

```text
Approval ≠ formalization
Formalization ≠ Delivery
Formalization ≠ Publish
Internal formal result ≠ external outcome
Artifact ≠ formal domain object automatically
```

Formalization operations and execution or outcome operations remain typed separately.

### 5.5 Routing progression

CH34’s opening chain was completed to preserve the sequence after human selection:

```text
human selection
→ Routing decision
→ appointment preparation
→ approval where required
→ governed provider instruction
→ provider acceptance
```

The review preserves:

```text
Ranking ≠ Routing decision
Human selection ≠ appointment
Appointment ≠ instruction
Provider instruction ≠ provider acceptance
```

AI and platform ranking do not make the final appointment.

### 5.6 Artifact outcome and lineage

CH39 now states that an Artifact preserves a usable, versioned business outcome and its lineage, not merely an undifferentiated outcome.

## 6. Cross-Part Findings

### Part I

Part I establishes the constitutional authority model and prevents Workplace, Products, MGSN, AI, or implementation convenience from absorbing Core, Execution, Owning Service, or human professional responsibility.

### Part II

Part II supplies organization identity, accountability, relationships, private Knowledge, operating surfaces, and data boundaries without becoming a second Core or one centralized organizational database.

### Part III

Part III preserves progression from authorized Information and Knowledge consumption through Capability selection, AI assistance, recommendation, Prepared Action, Human Review, approval, governed Execution, and Owning Service handoff.

### Part IV

Part IV keeps Products independent while preserving shared foundations, authorized Workplace context, Product-specific state, typed Handoffs, cross-Product continuity, and formal authority outside Product experience.

### Part V

Part V distinguishes Asset, Content, Artifact, Document, file representation, Render, Edit, Delivery, Publish, formalization, outcome, and feedback. It preserves version-specific Review and Owning Service authority.

### Part VI

Part VI preserves Private First, Trust Before Exposure, Evidence Before Ranking, Human Choice Before Routing Action, organization-owned relationships, provider acceptance, cross-Orbit authority separation, and decentralized learning.
